# Interview Questions — Article Mapping

Goal: Each article answers one or more of these questions naturally, without looking like a Q&A. Hiring managers read the articles and understand how Andrew thinks, behaves, decides, relates, and leads before the interview begins.

---

## Resume-Specific Questions

### Performance & Results
1. You were ranked #13 nationally with a 48.6% closing ratio. Walk me through how you got there.
2. Your perfect-score call rate was nearly 3x the company average. What were you doing differently than everyone else?
3. You drove 50-100% annual revenue growth against a 5% industry average. What was the mechanism?
4. Your personal production was roughly 50% of total local market sales. How do you sustain that without burning out?

### Leadership & Team Development
5. You were selected to build onboarding training from scratch. Why you, and what did you build?
6. You identified that the core onboarding barrier was identity, not skill. Explain that diagnosis and what you did with it.
7. You moved a siloed senior team to peer collaboration without persuasion. How?
8. Reps you trained went on to close deals 10x the standard size. What did you teach them that others weren't teaching?

### Coaching & Human Development
9. You are a certified coach with a sales career. How do those two things connect?
10. What does it mean to surface the real issue beneath the stated problem? Give me an example.
11. You say you treat communication as a precision discipline. What does that mean practically?

### Career & Background
12. Your career spans financial services, media, nonprofit, coaching, and consulting. How does that tell a coherent story?
13. You served in the military. How did that shape the way you perform and lead?
14. You ran an independent coaching practice. What did owning something teach you that a corporate role couldn't?
15. You repositioned 40+ small businesses for 30% average revenue growth. What was your diagnostic process?

## Most Common Interview Questions

16. Tell me about yourself.
17. What is your greatest strength?
18. What is your greatest weakness?
19. Why are you leaving your current role?
20. Where do you see yourself in five years?
21. Describe a time you failed and what you learned from it.
22. Tell me about a time you led without formal authority.
23. How do you handle conflict?
24. What motivates you?
25. Describe your leadership style.
26. Tell me about a time you had to make a difficult decision with incomplete information.
27. How do you handle pressure or competing priorities?
28. What makes you different from other candidates?
29. Tell me about a time you had to deliver difficult feedback.
30. Why do you want this role?
