# We Are Not Losing Our Methodology — We Are Losing Our Nerve

**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Status:** Draft
**Audience:** Sales, Training, Coaching professionals

---

Leadership called it "World Class."

The training program didn't come close.

That gap exists in most organizations. The standard gets stated from the top. The investment doesn't follow. Most people accept that as the job description.

I didn't.

Not because I had resources. I had none. Not because I was asked. I wasn't. Because I valued the people sitting across from me and I couldn't reconcile teaching them a diminished version of what they were capable of becoming.

So I built the missing piece — in hours, not days.

What I learned is that skill training delivered into an identity vacuum doesn't stick. Wilson Learning research confirms it: 67% of organizations report less than 40% of learning sustains into long-term job performance. The methodology isn't broken. The sequence is.

Belonging has to come before internalization. People do not adopt a standard they feel excluded from. Before you teach anyone what to do, they need to feel they are already part of something worth becoming.

I sequenced identity first. I used real stories — client moments, agent wins — to make the standard visible and human, not theoretical. I used the language leadership was already using and built a model around it so the words meant something concrete.

Where I fell short: I needed more voices doing the same thing across more touchpoints. One trainer is not a culture. That part required coordination I didn't fully build.

The result was 30% more perfect client satisfaction scores than every prior class.

Here is what this actually requires: not a budget. Commitment. And the leadership courage to take a stand for something good when the organization has quietly stopped expecting it.

We are not losing our training methodology.

We are losing our nerve to treat people like they matter — and calling it fiscal discipline.

*What is it actually costing you to continue?*
