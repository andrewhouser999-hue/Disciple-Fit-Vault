# Stu Mittleman — Alkalizing Diet & Exercise Protocol

## Core Logic

Sugar-burning produces lactic acid; fat-burning does not. The goal is to shift metabolism so permanently toward fat oxidation that metabolic acids are rarely generated in the first place. Diet and training work together — neither alone is sufficient.

**The acid-fat cycle:** Acid is quarantined in fat, making it harder to burn fat for fuel, which forces more sugar-burning, which produces more acid. Breaking the cycle requires removing dietary acid sources and training the body to stay in aerobic fat-burning zones.

## Part 1 — Master Food Reference

### Eat Freely (Alkaline-Forming / Fat-Burning)

| Category | Specific Foods |
|---|---|
| **Leafy greens** | Spinach, kale, arugula, romaine, Swiss chard |
| **Cruciferous veg** | Broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, Brussels sprouts, bok choy |
| **Other vegetables** | Zucchini, cucumber, celery, bell peppers, asparagus, onions, tomatoes, carrots, beets |
| **Root veg (moderate)** | Sweet potato, parsnip |
| **Fats** | Avocado, olive oil, coconut oil/cream, flaxseed oil |
| **Nuts & seeds** | Almonds (Mittleman's trail food), walnuts, macadamia, pumpkin seeds, chia, sesame |
| **Protein** | Wild-caught salmon, sardines, other cold-water fish; whole eggs; grass-fed beef (moderate) |
| **Herbs/spices** | Ginger, turmeric, garlic, lemon juice, apple cider vinegar |
| **Beverages** | Water (½ bodyweight in oz/day), herbal tea, vegetable juice, coffee (black, 1 cup max) |

### Eat Sparingly (Borderline)

| Category | Notes |
|---|---|
| Legumes | Lentils, chickpeas, black beans — fiber/alkaline but some carb load |
| Whole grains | Quinoa, brown rice, oats — allow after 2-week reset, in moderation |
| Low-sugar fruit | Berries, green apple, kiwi, watermelon — limit concentrated fruit sugar |
| Natural cheeses | Hard cheeses, sour cream, butter — use sparingly |

### Eliminate (Acid-Forming / Sugar-Burning)

| Category | What's Out |
|---|---|
| **All sugar** | Table sugar, honey, syrup, agave, candy, desserts, sports gels |
| **Grains (first 2 weeks)** | Bread, pasta, rice, corn, crackers, cereals |
| **Concentrated fruit** | Fruit juices, dried fruit, most tropical fruits |
| **Processed meats** | Deli meat, sausage, hot dogs |
| **Industrial oils** | Vegetable oil, canola, soybean, corn oil |
| **Dairy (most)** | Regular milk, low-fat dairy, flavored yogurt |
| **Sports nutrition** | All commercial sports drinks, gels, bars with sugar |
| **Caffeine excess** | More than 1 cup coffee; energy drinks |
| **Alcohol** | Beer and sweet wine; dry red wine borderline |

#### High-Value Additions (Beyond Kitchen Spices)

| Herb | Form | Why It Fits This Protocol |
|---|---|---|
| **Ashwagandha** | Capsule or powder | Adaptogen; regulates cortisol — directly supports the adrenal recovery piece of Mittleman's protocol (adrenals buffer acid; chronic stress depletes them) |
| **Dandelion root** | Tea or capsule | Natural diuretic; flushes acid-forming waste; improves kidney and liver function |
| **Burdock root** | Tea or capsule | Eliminates toxins, balances blood sugar, reduces inflammation |

#### Practical Rules

- **Turmeric + black pepper together always** — piperine in black pepper increases curcumin absorption by up to 2,000%
- **Ginger daily** — add a thumb-sized piece to smoothies or steep as tea; strongest anti-acid, anti-inflammatory kitchen herb
- **Garlic raw when possible** — cooking reduces allicin content; crush and let sit 10 minutes before heating to preserve some activity
- **Fresh > dried when available** — higher enzyme and mineral content; more alkalizing effect
- **Use liberally** — these are not trace additions; load them into every meal as a baseline strategy

## Part 3 — Daily Non-Negotiables

1. **Water first** — 8–12 oz upon waking before anything else
2. **Greens supplement daily** — Mittleman calls this the "alkaline seatbelt" against modern life stress
3. **No sugar pre-workout** — eat fat/protein or fast before training; never carb-load
4. **Eat within 1 hour of waking** — prevents cortisol spike and acid cascade (Maffetone rule)
5. **Continuous sipping** — hit ½ bodyweight (lbs) in oz of water throughout the day; not in large doses
6. **Almonds as default trail food** — carry them; replace sports bars and gels entirely

## Part 4 — Exercise Protocol (Heart Rate Zones)

### Zone Calculation

**Step 1:** Start with 180 − your age = MEP upper limit  
**Step 2:** Adjust:
- Subtract 10 if recovering from illness, inconsistent training, or high stress
- No adjustment if training consistently 1+ year without issues
- Add 5 if training 2+ years with consistently improving fitness

**Step 3:** Build your three zones (each ~20 bpm wide):

| Zone | Name | Range | Feel | Fuel Used |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **MAP** | Mostly Aerobic Pace | MEP − 20 bpm to MEP | Easy, conversational, relaxed | Fat |
| **MEP** | Most Efficient Pace | MEP to MEP + 20 bpm | Productive, sustainable, no strain | Fat/mixed |
| **SAP** | Speedy Anaerobic Pace | MEP + 20 to MEP + 40 bpm | Strained, urgent, loud breathing | Sugar → Acid |

**Example (age 40):** 180 − 40 = 140 (MEP ceiling). MAP = 120–140. MEP = 140–160. SAP = 160–180.

### Talk/Breath Test (No Heart Rate Monitor Needed)

- **MAP:** breathing low, slow, quiet — full conversation easy
- **MEP:** breathing fuller but controlled — short sentences possible
- **SAP:** breathing rapid and noisy — talking is broken or impossible

If you can't pass the talk test, you're in SAP. Slow down.

### Training Distribution

| Zone | % of Weekly Volume | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| MAP | 50% | Fat-burning adaptation; mitochondrial growth |
| MEP | 33% | Efficiency and sustainable performance |
| SAP | 17% or less | Speed work only; acid accumulates here |

### Warmup Rule

Spend **7–15 minutes in MAP before increasing intensity.** This is the window needed to mobilize fat stores. Starting hard skips fat mobilization and forces immediate sugar-burning and acid production.

### Metabolic Adaptation Over Time

Training in MAP consistently produces:
- Increased mitochondrial size and number (fat-burning capacity)
- Improved insulin sensitivity
- Adrenal recovery (adrenals buffer acid via cortisol; chronic SAP training depletes them)
- Digestive health improvement (MAP training supports gut function; Mittleman links digestive health directly to lower-body strength)

## Part 5 — Maffetone 2-Week Reset (Recommended Start)

Run this before beginning the 7-day plan to accelerate the fat-burning shift:

**For 14 days, eat only from the "Eat Freely" column.** Eliminate all grains, legumes, and fruit.  

After 2 weeks, reintroduce one food group at a time (berries first, then legumes, then whole grains) and observe energy, digestion, and cravings. This resets insulin sensitivity and forces the metabolic shift the Mittleman system depends on.

**Signs the reset is working:** reduced cravings for sugar, steadier energy, less bloating, clearer breathing during low-intensity exercise.

## Part 6 — Supplement Protocol

Organized in three tiers: Foundation (everyone, daily), Acid Buffering (exercise performance), and Fat-Burning Support (optional upgrades). Tier 1 is non-negotiable; add Tier 2 once training consistently; add Tier 3 for enhanced adaptation.

### Tier 1 — Foundation (Daily)

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Greens powder** | Per label | Morning with water | Mittleman's "alkaline seatbelt" — neutralizes daily acid load from diet and stress |
| **Fish oil (EPA/DHA)** | 2–3g EPA+DHA | With a fat-containing meal | Maffetone: most people can't get adequate EPA/DHA from food alone; reduces inflammation, improves metabolic efficiency, reduces glycogen reliance |
| **Magnesium glycinate or malate** | 300–400mg | Evening / before bed | Essential for ATP production and muscle relaxation; optimizes energy metabolism; reduces lactate accumulation; most people are deficient |
| **Blueberries (food-as-supplement)** | ½ cup daily | Any meal | Maffetone recommendation: combats oxidative stress from exercise; natural antioxidant load |

### Tier 2 — Acid Buffering (Exercise Performance)

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sodium bicarbonate** | 300mg per kg bodyweight | 1–2.5 hours pre-workout | Neutralizes hydrogen ions in muscle; delays acidification and fatigue during higher-intensity efforts; caution — can cause GI distress; start with a lower dose to test tolerance |
| **Beta-alanine** | 4–6g/day for 4–6 weeks | Split across meals | Raises muscle carnosine, which buffers hydrogen ions during exercise; harmless skin tingling (paresthesia) is normal; most effective after 4+ weeks of consistent use |
| **Potassium + magnesium citrate (electrolytes)** | Per label | Post-workout or with meals | Alkaline mineral salts that buffer acid in tissues; replaces minerals lost in sweat; supports adrenal recovery |

### Tier 3 — Fat-Burning Support (Optional Upgrades)

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CoQ10** | 100–200mg | With a fat-containing meal | Supports mitochondrial ATP production; reduces lactate generation; fat-soluble — must be taken with dietary fat to absorb |
| **Acetyl-L-Carnitine (ALCAR)** | 500–1000mg | Morning or pre-workout | Transports fatty acids into mitochondria for oxidation; directly enhances the aerobic fat-burning system Mittleman trains toward |
| **Coconut oil / MCT oil (food-as-supplement)** | 1–2 tbsp | Pre-workout or in smoothie | Medium-chain triglycerides convert to ketones rapidly, providing quick aerobic fuel without triggering sugar-burning or insulin spike |

### Supplement Stack by Day Part

| Time | What |
|---|---|
| **On waking (before food)** | 8–12 oz water |
| **Morning (with breakfast)** | Greens powder + fish oil + beta-alanine (1st split dose) |
| **Pre-workout (1–2 hrs before)** | Sodium bicarbonate (if training at MEP or higher) + ALCAR or MCT oil |
| **With lunch or afternoon meal** | Beta-alanine (2nd split dose) + CoQ10 (with fat) |
| **Post-workout** | Potassium/magnesium electrolytes + water |
| **Evening (with dinner or before bed)** | Magnesium glycinate |

### Notes
- **Start with Tier 1 only** for the first 2–4 weeks. Add Tier 2 once MAP training is consistent. Add Tier 3 as needed.
- **Sodium bicarbonate** is the most powerful acid buffer but the most GI-sensitive. Test with a smaller dose (half) before a low-stakes workout first.
- **Beta-alanine** takes 4–6 weeks to meaningfully raise carnosine levels — it is not a pre-workout stimulant; consistency matters more than timing.
- **CoQ10** absorption requires fat — take it with salmon, avocado, or coconut oil, not on an empty stomach.
