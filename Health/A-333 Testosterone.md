# A-333 Testosterone — Holistic, Naturopathic & Functional Medicine Protocol

---

## UNDERSTANDING THE TERRAIN FIRST

Testosterone is not primarily a supplement problem — it's a systemic optimization problem. The functional medicine framing:

- Low T = signaling failure (LH/FSH), conversion loss (aromatization), precursor depletion (pregnenolone steal), or receptor insensitivity
- Root causes: chronic stress (cortisol dominance), sleep deprivation, excess body fat (aromatase), xenoestrogen load, micronutrient deficiency
- Supplements amplify an optimized system — they don't replace one

---

## TIER 1 — FOUNDATION (Non-Negotiables)

### Sleep Architecture

**Target:** 7-9 hours, consistent wake time 7 days/week

- Wake at the same time daily — even weekends. This is the anchor.
- Lights out within 30 min of target bedtime
- Room: 65-68°F, completely dark, no screens
- No alcohol within 3 hours of bed
- No caffeine after 1pm
- Magnesium glycinate 400mg taken 30-60 min before bed

**Why this ranks #1:** 5 hours of sleep for one week reduces T by ~10-15%. No supplement stack can outrun bad sleep.

---

### Morning Anchor Routine (Circadian Reset)

**First 30 minutes after waking:**

1. Get outside — 10-20 min of natural light exposure (no sunglasses). Resets cortisol awakening response (CAR), which sets hormonal rhythm for the day.
2. Delay caffeine 90-120 min after waking — let your natural cortisol peak do its job first.
3. Cold exposure (optional but powerful) — cold shower, 2-3 min. Stimulates LH release and Leydig cell activity acutely.

---

### Resistance Training

**Structure:**
- 3-4 days/week
- Compound, multi-joint, heavy lifts: squat, hip hinge, horizontal press, vertical pull, overhead press

**Loading parameters for T response:**
- 70-85% of 1RM
- 3-5 sets per movement
- 6-10 rep range
- Rest 2-3 minutes between sets

**What to avoid:**
- Chronic cardio overload — elevates cortisol and suppresses T
- Overtraining — recovery IS the adaptation
- Daily high-intensity cardio

---

### Body Composition Target

- Goal: 10-20% body fat
- Visceral fat is the primary aromatase site — converts T to estrogen
- Every pound of visceral fat lost = less estrogen conversion = more free T

**Approach:**
- Modest caloric deficit (300-500 cal/day) — aggressive restriction suppresses T acutely
- Protein: 0.8-1g per pound of bodyweight
- Minimum 25-30% of calories from fat (direct hormonal precursor)

## TIER 2 — NUTRITION PROTOCOL

### Macronutrient Framework

| Macro | Target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Protein | 0.8-1g/lb bodyweight | Muscle synthesis, satiety |
| Fat | 25-35% of calories | Cholesterol → pregnenolone → testosterone (direct pathway) |
| Carbs | Fill remainder | Training performance, cortisol management |

Don't go ultra-low carb long-term if training hard — chronically low carbs elevate cortisol, which suppresses T.

---

### Testosterone-Supportive Foods

**Prioritize:**
- Eggs (whole — yolk contains cholesterol precursors)
- Beef (grass-fed) — higher CLA, omega-3 ratio
- Oysters — highest bioavailable zinc source
- Sardines / salmon — D3, omega-3s
- Avocado — monounsaturated fat, supports steroid hormone synthesis
- Olive oil — same mechanism; direct correlation to T in Mediterranean diet studies
- Brazil nuts — selenium (testicular function)
- Pumpkin seeds — zinc + magnesium
- Cruciferous vegetables — DIM precursors, estrogen clearance
- Pomegranate — aromatase inhibitor properties, improves free T
- Ginger — LH stimulation, Leydig cell support, anti-inflammatory

**Minimize or eliminate:**
- Alcohol — suppresses T synthesis directly, impairs liver estrogen clearance
- Processed seed oils (soybean, canola, corn) — inflammatory
- Soy in large quantities — phytoestrogen load
- Highly processed foods — endocrine disruption
- Flaxseed in large amounts — lignans have estrogen-mimicking properties

---

### Key Micronutrients

**Vitamin D3**
- T tracks closely with D levels in studies
- Optimal range: 50-80 ng/mL
- Dose: 2,000-5,000 IU/day with fat
- Always pair with K2 (MK-7, 100-200mcg)

**Zinc**
- Rate-limiting cofactor for testosterone synthesis
- Food first: oysters, red meat, pumpkin seeds
- Supplement: zinc bisglycinate or zinc picolinate, 15-30mg/day
- Don't megadose — competes with copper. If supplementing >30mg, add 1-2mg copper

**Magnesium**
- Reduces SHBG; improves sleep architecture
- Form: Magnesium glycinate (sleep, absorption), not oxide
- Dose: 300-500mg/day, taken at night

**Boron**
- Reduces SHBG and estradiol within days; increases free testosterone
- Dose: 3-10mg/day
- Sources: supplement, prunes, raisins, avocado

## TIER 3 — ESTROGEN MANAGEMENT

High estrogen suppresses T production directly. This is the most overlooked lever.

### Estrogen Clearance Protocol

**DIM (Diindolylmethane)**
- Promotes healthy estrogen metabolism
- Dose: 200-400mg/day with food
- Note: Very high doses (600mg+) can paradoxically increase estrogen — stay in range

**Calcium D-Glucarate**
- Supports liver phase II detoxification of excess estrogen
- Prevents reabsorption in the gut (glucuronidation pathway)
- Dose: 500-1000mg/day

**Liver Support**
- Milk thistle (silymarin) — 200-400mg/day
- NAC (N-acetyl cysteine) — 600mg 1-2x/day
- Cruciferous vegetables daily

### Xenoestrogen Reduction

- BPA/BPS plastics — never heat food in plastic; use glass/stainless
- Non-stick (Teflon) cookware — use cast iron, stainless steel, carbon steel
- Conventional meat with added hormones — grass-fed/pasture-raised
- Synthetic fragrance — swap to fragrance-free or natural
- Conventional produce with high pesticide load — wash thoroughly or buy organic
- Tap water — filtered (Berkey, reverse osmosis) removes atrazine and pharmaceutical estrogens

## TIER 4 — CORTISOL / STRESS PROTOCOL

**Cortisol is T's direct antagonist.** They share precursors (pregnenolone) — when the body prioritizes cortisol under stress, T production gets downregulated ("pregnenolone steal").

### Physiological Interventions

**Breathwork (Acute Cortisol Control)**
- Box breathing: 4 count in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold. 5-10 rounds.
- 4-7-8 breath: activates parasympathetic
- Physiological sigh: double inhale through nose, long exhale — fastest known method to downregulate acute stress

**Cold Exposure**
- Cold shower 2-3 min at end of warm shower, daily
- Increases norepinephrine, stimulates LH, reduces cortisol chronically

**Heat Exposure (Sauna)**
- 3-4x/week, 15-20 min at 170-190°F
- Increases growth hormone acutely (up to 200-300%)
- Post-sauna cold plunge amplifies the effect

**Nature Exposure**
- 20-30 min in green space measurably lowers stress hormones
- Grounding (bare feet on grass/earth) — reduces cortisol, improves HRV

**Prayer / Contemplative Practice**
- Regular practice measurably reduces HPA axis reactivity
- 10-15 min daily outperforms occasional hour-long sessions

**Reduce Stimulant Dependency**
- Chronic caffeine overuse keeps cortisol elevated throughout the day
- Target: 1-2 cups before noon only

## TIER 5 — HERBAL / SUPPLEMENT STACK

### Tier 5A — Best Evidenced

**Ashwagandha (KSM-66 or Sensoril)**
- Mechanism: Lowers cortisol; T and cortisol are inversely linked
- Studies show 10-22% T increase, improved LH, better sperm parameters
- Dose: 300-600mg/day with food
- Cycling: 8-12 weeks on, 2-4 weeks off

**Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia, 200:1 extract)**
- Reduces SHBG; increases free T; stimulates steroidogenesis
- Dose: 200-400mg/day standardized extract (Physta® or equivalent)
- Cycling: 5 days on, 2 days off, or 8 weeks on / 2 weeks off

**Shilajit (purified fulvic acid resin)**
- Increases total T, FSH, and LH in clinical trials
- Dose: 250mg 2x/day of purified resin
- Source: Himalayan or Altai mountain resin — heavy metal testing is critical

**Fadogia Agrestis**
- Stimulates LH (upstream signal to produce T)
- Dose: 400-600mg/day
- Cycling: 8 weeks on, 4 weeks off minimum
- Caution: Long-term continuous use not studied

### Tier 5B — Supportive / Synergistic

**Nettle Root**
- Binds SHBG → increases free testosterone
- Dose: 300-600mg/day
- Synergistic with Tongkat Ali

**Pine Pollen Tincture**
- Contains phytoandrogens (DHEA, androstenedione, testosterone in small amounts)
- Alcohol tincture, sublingual
- Dose: 1-2 dropperfuls daily, hold 60 seconds sublingual

**Maca Root (gelatinized)**
- Libido, stamina, mood — not a direct T booster
- Dose: 1.5-3g/day gelatinized

**Ginger**
- LH stimulation, Leydig cell support, anti-inflammatory
- Dose: 500mg-1g extract, or liberal food use

**Rhodiola Rosea**
- Cortisol regulation, fatigue reduction
- Dose: 200-400mg standardized extract (3% rosavins, 1% salidrosides), AM only
- Cycling: pairs well with Ashwagandha

### Tier 5C — Cortisol Adaptogen Stack

Compoundable combination:
- Ashwagandha KSM-66 (300mg) + Rhodiola (200mg) + Holy Basil (300mg)
- Taken AM and mid-day
- 8-12 weeks on / 2-4 weeks off

## TIER 6 — DHEA & HORMONAL PRECURSORS

**DHEA (Dehydroepiandrosterone)**
- Adrenal androgen; precursor to both T and estrogen
- Declines with age starting at ~25
- Dose: 25-50mg/day for men over 35
- Do not use without baseline DHEA-S lab

**Pregnenolone**
- Master precursor — upstream of DHEA, cortisol, progesterone, and testosterone
- 10-30mg/day; best used under practitioner guidance
- Restores balance when pregnenolone is shunted toward cortisol under chronic stress

## TIER 7 — FUNCTIONAL MEDICINE LAB WORK

### Core Panel

| Test | Why |
|---|---|
| Total Testosterone | Baseline |
| Free Testosterone | The bioavailable fraction — what actually matters |
| SHBG | If high, free T is low even with normal total T |
| Estradiol (E2) | Estrogen dominance suppresses T |
| LH + FSH | Hypothalamic vs. testicular origin |
| DHEA-S | Adrenal androgen precursor pool |
| Cortisol (4-point saliva or DUTCH) | Daily cortisol curve |
| Thyroid panel (TSH, Free T3, Free T4) | Thyroid dysfunction mimics low T |
| Vitamin D (25-OH) | Tracks with T |
| Zinc, Magnesium RBC | RBC levels more accurate than serum |
| CBC, CMP, Lipids | General metabolic picture |

**DUTCH Test** — gold standard. Shows T, free T, estrogen metabolites, cortisol curve, DHEA all in one.

### Optimal Ranges (Functional, not just "normal")

| Marker | Lab Normal | Functional Optimal |
|---|---|---|
| Total T (men) | 300-1000 ng/dL | 600-900 ng/dL |
| Free T | 35-155 pg/mL | 15-25 ng/dL |
| Estradiol | <39 pg/mL | 20-30 pg/mL |
| SHBG | 10-57 nmol/L | 20-35 nmol/L |
| Vitamin D | 30-100 ng/mL | 50-80 ng/mL |

## IMPLEMENTATION ORDER

**Week 1-2:** Sleep, morning light, consistent wake time, cold shower, remove alcohol, magnesium glycinate

**Week 3-4:** Add D3/K2, zinc, resistance training structure, dietary fat correction

**Week 5-6:** Add Ashwagandha, Tongkat Ali, boron, DIM

**Week 7-8:** Add Shilajit, Nettle Root, Calcium D-Glucarate, liver support

**Week 9-10:** Assess. Get labs. Add Fadogia if warranted. Consider DUTCH test if still symptomatic.

**Ongoing:** 12-week cycles with 2-4 week breaks from adaptogens. Quarterly labs if optimizing seriously.
