### Venous Assessment
- **Ankle-Brachial Index (ABI)** — non-invasive ratio of ankle to arm blood pressure. Below 0.9 = peripheral arterial disease present. Below 0.6 = compression contraindicated entirely. **Compression socks do not enter this protocol until this test is done.** A vascular specialist or primary care provider can order it.
- **Stemmer's Sign** — can be assessed now at home. Attempt to pinch and lift the skin on the top of the second toe. If you cannot lift a skin fold, it is positive — indicates a lymphedema component is present.
- **Duplex Ultrasound (Venous Doppler)** — confirms CVI, valve reflux, and vessel integrity. Order through vascular specialist or imaging center.

### Sleep Apnea Screening
Home sleep test (available without a specialist through companies like Lofta, Sleep.com, or via primary care referral). Round belly + 10-hour sleep need + fatigue on waking = high-probability obstructive sleep apnea. Undiagnosed OSA generates nocturnal cortisol spikes that directly catabolize muscle tissue — working against the entire protein and sarcopenia protocol. This is not optional.

### Dietary Corrections (Cost: Zero)

**Remove honey immediately.**
His hypoglycemia lineage combined with central adiposity means his glucose regulatory system is under chronic strain. Honey is glucose. Every dose is a spike-and-crash trigger regardless of the cinnamon it accompanies. Remove the vehicle. Keep the cinnamon.

**Ceylon cinnamon — 1/2 tsp daily with a meal.**
Ceylon cinnamon (not cassia, which contains liver-toxic coumarin at high doses) has legitimate mild blood sugar buffering and insulin-sensitizing properties. This is the legitimate half of his current honey-and-cinnamon habit. Keep it.

**Protein targets — non-negotiable.**
- Minimum 30–40g protein per meal — not spread thin across the day in small amounts
- Target 1.2–1.6g per kg of body weight daily total
- Best sources: eggs (whole), fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel), beef, chicken thighs, organ meats if tolerated
- At 80, anabolic resistance means the muscle protein synthesis threshold is higher than in younger adults. Distributing 15g of protein across multiple small meals results in all of it going to maintenance and tissue turnover — none to muscle repair or growth. The bolus matters.

**Increase healthy fats.**
Extra virgin olive oil 2–4 tablespoons daily on food. Fatty fish 3x/week. Avocado when available. These support fat-soluble vitamin absorption (D, E, K — all require dietary fat), provide oleocanthal (natural COX inhibitor), and slow glucose absorption after carbohydrate-containing meals.

**Carbohydrate quality.**
Permitted: sweet potato, lentils, black beans, whole oats, root vegetables. Remove or minimize: white bread, white rice, pasta, crackers, refined flour products. His hypoglycemia lineage means refined carbs directly trigger the fatigue-without-food pattern he already experiences.

**Hydration.**
Minimum 6–8 glasses water daily. Thirst mechanism degrades with age and is not a reliable signal at 80. Add a pinch of Himalayan salt to 1–2 glasses daily. Dehydration worsens joint pain, concentrates inflammatory markers, impairs lymphatic fluid viscosity, and degrades kidney filtration.

### Tier 1 Supplements

**Betaine HCl with Pepsin**
- Dose: 650mg mid-meal with each protein-containing meal. Start at one capsule; increase to two if no warmth sensation
- Benefit: Restores stomach acid required to activate pepsin and begin protein digestion. At 80, gastric acid production is significantly reduced. A person can eat adequate protein and still be functionally protein-deficient at the cellular level if this step is broken. This is upstream of every other intervention.
- Flag: If warmth or burning occurs in the stomach, reduce dose. Do not use if active gastric ulcer is present.

**Broad-Spectrum Digestive Enzymes**
- Dose: 1–2 capsules with each meal
- Contains: protease, lipase, amylase, bromelain, papain
- Benefit: Supports complete macronutrient breakdown. Reduces post-meal heaviness that causes elderly patients to subconsciously reduce protein portions over time. Slowed gastric motility at this age means food sits longer and digests incompletely without enzymatic support.

**Vitamin K2 (MK-7 form)**
- Dose: 100–200mcg daily with a fatty meal
- Benefit: Directs calcium to bone and teeth rather than arterial walls. He is already taking calcium and D3 without K2. This is an active ongoing risk being created daily — supplemental calcium without K2 deposits in soft tissue and arterial walls. This correction starts immediately, not after testing.
- Form specificity: MK-7 (menaquinone-7) has a half-life of 3 days vs. MK-4's 4 hours. Take once daily.

**Magnesium Glycinate**
- Dose: 300–400mg elemental magnesium, before bed
- Form specificity: Glycinate for sleep and muscle relaxation. If malate form is chosen instead, take in the morning — malate supports energy production and is mildly stimulating.
- Benefit: Muscle relaxation (directly relevant to joint aching, stiffness, and sleep quality), blood sugar regulation, heart rhythm stability, reduces inflammatory cytokines, promotes deep sleep. Magnesium deficiency is associated with insulin resistance, poor sleep, muscle cramping, and systemic inflammation — he has markers of all four.

**Vitamin B12 (Sublingual Methylcobalamin)**
- Dose: 1,000–2,000mcg dissolved under the tongue daily
- Upgrade from standard B12 capsule: requires adequate stomach acid to cleave from carrier proteins before absorption. At 80 with reduced gastric acid, oral B12 absorption is unreliable. Sublingual delivery bypasses the gastric acid dependency entirely.
- Benefit: Nerve function, energy production, red blood cell formation. B12 deficiency mimics neurological decline and is frequently attributed to "normal aging."

**Vitamin D3**
- Starting dose: 4,000–5,000 IU daily with a fatty meal
- Pair with: K2 — non-negotiable pairing
- Calibrate after: 25-OH Vitamin D bloodwork. Functional target: 60–80 ng/mL
- Benefit: Muscle fiber activation (receptors are present in muscle tissue — deficiency directly causes the proximal weakness pattern in sarcopenia), immune function, mood, sleep quality, bone density. Near-universal deficiency at this age.

**Omega-3 Fish Oil (EPA/DHA)**
- Dose: 2–3g EPA/DHA combined daily. Read the label — total fish oil weight is not EPA/DHA content.
- Form: Triglyceride form absorbs better than ethyl ester. Enteric-coated reduces fish aftertaste.
- Timing: With the largest meal of the day
- Benefit: Reduces systemic inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-6) that are driving both sarcopenia and joint deterioration simultaneously. Supports venous wall integrity, cardiovascular function, triglyceride reduction, joint lubrication.
- Blood-thinning stack note: He takes saw palmetto (mild antiplatelet). He is now adding omega-3 (antiplatelet at doses above 3g EPA/DHA) and will add vitamin E (antiplatelet above 400 IU) and nattokinase (fibrinolytic, Tier 2). Keep omega-3 at or below 3g EPA/DHA total. Monitor vitamin E dose — if above 400 IU, reduce. No medications means no drug interactions, but the combined supplemental antiplatelet effect across this stack is real.

## TIER 2 — ADD AFTER 2–3 WEEKS, OR BEHIND SPECIFIC BLOODWORK

*Introduce after Tier 1 is tolerated. Some entries are gated by specific lab results.*

---

**Probiotics (Multi-strain)**
- Dose: 25–50 billion CFU daily
- Timing: Morning on empty stomach or before bed
- Strains: Lactobacillus acidophilus, Bifidobacterium longum, Bifidobacterium bifidum
- Benefit: Restores gut microbiome, improves nutrient absorption for the entire supplement stack, reduces systemic inflammation via gut-immune axis, supports short-chain fatty acid production which feeds gut lining integrity.

**Curcumin with Piperine (or Phospholipid-Bound Form)**
- Dose: 500–1,000mg curcumin extract daily
- Form: Must include BioPerine (piperine/black pepper extract) or be phospholipid-bound (Meriva, Theracumin). Plain curcumin without these has near-zero bioavailability.
- Timing: With a fatty meal
- Benefit: Inhibits NF-kB (the master inflammatory switch), reduces joint inflammation, inhibits cartilage-degrading enzymes, reduces morning stiffness. 15 years of joint aching indicates chronic low-grade inflammatory load — curcumin addresses the mechanism, not just the symptom.

**CoQ10 (Ubiquinol Form)**
- Dose: 200mg daily with a fatty meal
- Form: Ubiquinol only — the active, reduced form. At 80, conversion from ubiquinone (cheaper, more common) is significantly impaired. Use the active form directly.
- Benefit: Powers mitochondrial ATP production. Heart muscle is particularly dependent on CoQ10. Reduces post-exertion fatigue and recovery time. His sleep requirement and slow recovery from activity are partly mitochondrial in origin.

**Horse Chestnut Extract (Aescin)**
- Dose: 300mg standardized to 50mg aescin, twice daily — morning and evening with food
- Benefit: Most evidence-backed herbal for CVI. Reduces vascular permeability (stops protein-rich fluid leaking into surrounding tissue), strengthens vein wall tone, reduces inflammatory mediators in venous tissue. Multiple trials confirm reduction in lower leg edema.
- Note: Oral supplement — not subject to the PAD/compression contraindication. Safe to start without ABI test.

**Diosmin + Hesperidin**
- Dose: 500mg diosmin / 100mg hesperidin daily, or a combined 600mg formula with food
- Benefit: Venotonic — increases venous tone, decreases capillary permeability, reduces venous inflammation and white blood cell adhesion to vessel walls (a key driver of CVI progression). Works through a different mechanism than horse chestnut — complementary, not redundant.

**Butcher's Broom (Ruscus Aculeatus)**
- Dose: 150–300mg daily with food
- Benefit: Alpha-adrenergic activity — causes mild venous constriction, improving blood return from the legs. Reduces edema, heaviness, and nighttime leg cramps associated with venous insufficiency.

**Nattokinase**
- Dose: 2,000–4,000 FU daily on an empty stomach
- Form: Enteric-coated preferred, or take 30–45 minutes before a meal
- Benefit: Fibrinolytic enzyme. Breaks down fibrin — the structural component of fibrotic tissue making the ankles firm. Reduces blood viscosity, supports microcirculation. Long-term intervention — allow 3–6 months before expecting measurable tissue change.
- Blood-thinning stack reminder: Fourth mildly antiplatelet/fibrinolytic compound in the stack alongside saw palmetto, omega-3, and vitamin E. If unusual bruising develops, reduce dose.

**Berberine — GATED BY BLOODWORK**
- Do not start until fasting glucose and fasting insulin results are reviewed.
- If results confirm elevated fasting glucose (above 100 mg/dL), elevated HbA1c (above 5.7%), or elevated fasting insulin → berberine is appropriate and high-value.
- If results show normal or low fasting glucose with low fasting insulin → hypoglycemia is the direction; berberine is contraindicated.
- Dose when cleared: 500mg with meals, 2–3x daily. Start at once daily for 1–2 weeks to allow GI adjustment.
- Benefit when appropriate: Activates AMPK (same cellular pathway as metformin), improves insulin sensitivity, reduces fasting glucose, supports blood sugar stability between meals, mild lipid-lowering and anti-inflammatory secondary effects.

**Zinc Bisglycinate**
- Dose: 25–30mg with dinner
- Benefit: Immune function, wound healing, T3 receptor binding (thyroid), testosterone maintenance, connective tissue repair. Absorption decreases with age.
- Long-term note: If taken daily beyond 3 months, balance with 2–3mg copper — zinc at high doses depletes copper over time.

**Selenium (L-Selenomethionine)**
- Dose: 200mcg daily with food
- Benefit: Required cofactor for the deiodinase enzyme that converts T4 to active T3. Also antioxidant, immune support, and thyroid protective.

## TIER 3 — TESTING-DEPENDENT ONLY

*Nothing in this tier starts without a specific test result.*

---

**Thyroid Protocol (Full)**
Gates: Full thyroid panel results reviewed

*If TSH above 2.5 and/or Free T3 low and/or Reverse T3 elevated:*

- **Ashwagandha (KSM-66 extract)**: 300–600mg daily. Clinical trials confirm significant improvement in T3, T4, and TSH reduction. Also reduces cortisol — elevated cortisol blocks T4-to-T3 conversion and is directly catabolic to muscle.
- **L-Tyrosine**: 500mg on empty stomach. Amino acid precursor to thyroid hormone synthesis.
- **Iodine** — CONDITIONAL ONLY: Start only if (a) urinary iodine confirms deficiency AND (b) TPO antibodies are negative. If TPO antibodies are positive, high-dose iodine is contraindicated — it accelerates autoimmune destruction of the thyroid in Hashimoto's patients. This is a binary gate, not a caution.

**Vitamin D3 Dose Adjustment**
Gates: 25-OH Vitamin D test result

| Result | Action |
|---|---|
| Below 30 ng/mL | Increase to 8,000–10,000 IU temporarily, retest in 90 days |
| 30–60 ng/mL | 5,000 IU holds |
| Above 60 ng/mL | 4,000 IU holds or reduce to 2,000 IU |

Functional target: 60–80 ng/mL

**Compression Socks**
Gates: ABI test result

| ABI Result | Action |
|---|---|
| 0.9–1.3 (normal) | 20–30 mmHg graduated compression socks appropriate |
| Below 0.9 | PAD present — consult vascular specialist before any compression |

**Gotu Kola (Centella Asiatica)**
Gates: Duplex Ultrasound confirms CVI; Tier 2 venous herbs tolerated

- Dose: 60–120mg triterpenes daily
- Benefit: Stimulates collagen synthesis in vascular walls, supports lymphatic vessel repair, reduces fibrosis in venous tissue. While nattokinase breaks down old fibrotic tissue, gotu kola rebuilds healthy connective tissue in venous and lymphatic walls. Works as a long-term pairing with nattokinase.

**Leucine Supplementation**
Gates: Assessment of whether per-meal protein targets (30–40g) are being consistently met through food

- If targets are being met: not needed
- If not: Add 2–3g free-form leucine powder to the meal that falls short. Leucine directly activates mTOR (the muscle protein synthesis trigger). At 80, the threshold to activate synthesis is higher — leucine powder tips that threshold without requiring more food volume.

## MECHANICAL & MOVEMENT PROTOCOL

**Daily Leg Elevation**
- 20–30 minutes, legs elevated above heart level, twice daily
- Lying flat with legs on pillows or propped against a wall
- First session: morning before prolonged upright time. Second session: evening before bed.
- Benefit: Uses gravity to drain venous and lymphatic fluid from lower extremities. Only free intervention that directly reduces lower leg pressure.

**Seated Ankle Pumps**
- 20–30 repetitions, 3–4 times daily, especially after prolonged sitting or before standing
- Motion: flex foot up (dorsiflex), point foot down (plantarflex), rotate in circles both directions
- Benefit: Activates the calf muscle pump — the primary mechanical driver of venous return from the lower legs — without requiring standing balance or weight bearing.
- Note: Replaces rebounding. Until hernia status and gait stability are assessed, rebounding carries fall risk and intra-abdominal pressure risk. Ankle pumps replicate the circulatory benefit safely.

**Walking — Structured**
- 15–20 minutes minimum daily at a consistent pace
- Benefit: Every step contracts the calf and pushes blood upward through venous valves. Directly counters venous pooling, maintains quadriceps and calf muscle mass, improves insulin sensitivity, stimulates appetite.
- If joint pain limits walking: pool walking eliminates impact load while maintaining the calf pump and muscle activation benefits.

**Morning Sun Exposure**
- 10–20 minutes direct skin exposure before 10am
- Benefit: Supports vitamin D synthesis, regulates circadian rhythm, stimulates the morning cortisol peak that drives energy and alertness throughout the day.
