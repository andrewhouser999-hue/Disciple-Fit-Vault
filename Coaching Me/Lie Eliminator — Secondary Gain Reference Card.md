# Lie Eliminator — Secondary Gain Reference Card

Use alongside the **[[Lie Eliminator - Personal]]** when working through the *What does that lie give you?* field. Most people can name the lie but cannot initially name the payoff. This list is for observation — surface it as resonance, not diagnosis.

**In session:** Read through the clusters silently. Reflect back what you observe. Let the person confirm or correct.

---

## Protection

1. **Avoiding vulnerability** — if the lie is true, I never have to risk being truly known or loved
2. **Avoiding disappointment** — low expectations protect against grief when God or others don't show up as I expect
3. **Avoiding grief** — the lie substitutes for mourning what was actually lost (a good father, a safe childhood, a fair start)
4. **Avoiding intimacy** — the lie keeps people at a manageable distance; *I'm rejectable* becomes a controlled exit before they can leave

## Control

5. **Performance as substitute for surrender** — if God is a demanding authority, I can manage him through behavior rather than trust
6. **Predictability** — the lie is known territory; transformation is not. Familiar pain is safer than uncertain healing
7. **Avoiding responsibility** — if I'm not enough, I can't be held accountable for outcomes; the lie redistributes blame

## Narrative

8. **Explanation for pain** — the lie makes suffering make sense; cognitive closure is more comfortable than unanswered mystery
9. **Moral grievance** — the lie positions me as wronged, which carries a kind of power; the wound becomes a place of standing
10. **Relational leverage** — *I'm broken* pulls sympathy, attention, or care from others; the lie earns connection

## Identity

11. **Self-concept anchor** — the lie has been held so long it has become part of who I am; releasing it threatens identity itself
12. **Pseudo-humility** — believing *I'm not enough* feels like appropriate Christian lowliness; it is spiritualized self-protection

## Agency

13. **Permission to avoid risk** — if I'll fail, I never have to step out and face actual failure; the lie removes the demand
14. **Permission to stay small** — the lie allows opt-out from a calling or purpose that feels too large or frightening
15. **Excuse to not change** — the lie is a reason why transformation isn't possible for me specifically, which relieves the pressure

---

## Practitioner Notes

**Clusters that surface most often:** Protection and Control.

**Hardest to name:** Identity anchor and pseudo-humility — most spiritually disguised and most internally defended.

**Most socially reinforced:** Narrative payoffs (grievance, relational leverage) — others have often confirmed these without realizing it.

**Follow-on question after naming the payoff:**
> *"What would you have to trust God with to stop needing that?"*

This question converts the payoff into a surrender target. The declaration that follows should address it directly — not just the lie itself.
