# Holy Spirit Working Document — Practices

A growing collection of personal practices for partnering with the Holy Spirit in specific, named areas of need. Each practice is self-contained: what it's for, how to run it, and the exact wording to use. Add new practices as their own numbered sections below; do not renumber existing ones as the collection grows.

## How to use this document

Before starting any practice below, name the "thing" out loud and specifically — the actual mental, emotional, psychological, or spiritual condition you are bringing to God (e.g., "this anxiety about the client meeting," "this shame over my failure with my son," "this insomnia"). Every place a practice below says **'thing'**, substitute that specific name. Vague or general prayer produces vague, general engagement; naming the thing precisely is what lets the declaration land on the actual target.

---

## Practice 1 — Speak the Word Over What Needs Healing

**Purpose:** To bring a specific area of mental, emotional, psychological, or spiritual affliction under the authority of Christ's finished work, by speaking the relevant Scripture directly over it and then declaring its outcome — rather than only describing the problem to God in prayer. Speaking Scripture *over* the thing (as opposed to *about* it) treats the Word as active and effective in the moment, not just as background truth.

**Designed version:** [Speak the Word Over What Needs Healing](https://claude.ai/code/artifact/e3b79342-b974-446b-9f0b-f64dbff3d27e) — interactive page with a live fill-in for the named 'thing' (and the specific outcome in verse 4), so the declarations read as your actual spoken words rather than a template.

**How to run it:**
1. Name the specific 'thing' out loud (see "How to use this document" above).
2. Speak each of the four verses below, in order, addressing it to the 'thing' itself — not as background reading, but as a direct statement over the condition.
3. Immediately after each verse, speak the paired declaration, inserting the named 'thing' where indicated.
4. Move through all four before stopping. The sequence builds: verse 1 grounds the healing in what Christ already did; verse 2 asks the Holy Spirit to make that finished work active/alive in the body now; verse 3 is a direct refusal addressed to the enemy, refusing to receive the affliction as something still owed; verse 4 hands the outcome and its timing to the Lord.

### 1. 1 Peter 2:24
> "He himself bore our sins in his body on the tree, that we might die to sin and live to righteousness. By his wounds you have been healed."

**Declaration:** Say directly to the 'thing' — *"By His stripes, you [the 'thing'] are healed."*
*(This declares the healing as an already-accomplished fact of the cross, not a future hope — the wound of Christ is the legal and spiritual basis for the thing's healing.)*

### 2. Romans 8:11
> "If the Spirit of him who raised Jesus from the dead dwells in you, he who raised Christ Jesus from the dead will also give life to your mortal bodies through his Spirit who dwells in you."

**Declaration:** *"Holy Spirit, You quicken and You make alive [the 'thing']."*
*("Quicken" here means to make alive / bring to life — asking the same Spirit that raised Jesus from death to actively animate resurrection life into this specific mortal, present-tense condition, not merely to acknowledge that He could.)*

### 3. Matthew 8:17
> "This was to fulfill what was spoken by the prophet Isaiah: 'He took our illnesses and bore our diseases.'"

**Declaration:** *"Therefore, devil, we are not taking this [the 'thing'] no more, because it was already carried on the cross."*
*(This is addressed to the enemy, not to God — a direct refusal to keep agreeing with or hosting the affliction, on the grounds that Christ already took it. It functions as a renunciation, not a request.)*

### 4. Psalm 138:8
> "The LORD will fulfill his purpose for me; your steadfast love, O LORD, endures forever. Do not forsake the work of your hands."

**Declaration:** *"Lord, You will perfect [the 'thing'] in [this specific way — name the outcome you're asking for, e.g., 'full restoration of sleep' or 'complete freedom from this shame'], in the name of Jesus."*
*(This last declaration hands the completion and the specific shape of the outcome to God's ongoing purpose and steadfast love, rather than treating the practice itself as the mechanism that finishes the healing.)*
