# Secured in Father — Entry Guide

Use this before opening [[Secured in Father — Attachment Formation & Encounter Preparation]]. It tells you where to begin given what you are carrying today. Two steps.

---

## Step 1 — Identify Your Pattern

Run the Self-Diagnostic in the main document if you have not already. Which pattern is active today?

- **Anxious / Preoccupied** — You approach God with effort, checking, signal-escalation. Silence feels threatening. Comfort rarely fully lands.
- **Dismissing-Avoidant** — You prefer the theological to the relational. Prayer stays efficient and controlled. Intimacy feels unnecessary.
- **Fearful-Avoidant** — You want to be near God and something pulls back at the same time. Both are true at once and neither resolves. *If the oscillation doesn't fit — if approach to God feels blank, impossible, or frozen rather than conflicted — that is the disorganized form of this pattern. Use the Fearful-Avoidant routing below and proceed to the* Disorganized *encounter prompts in the main document.*

---

## Step 2 — Identify Your Emotion

Choose the closest match to what you are carrying right now:

- **Fear** — apprehension, dread, or anxiety about approaching God or what lies ahead
- **Shame** — self-contempt, a sense of unworthiness, or the fear that what is seen will disqualify
- **Anger** — frustration, resentment, or protest — including anger directed at God
- **Grief** — sadness, loss, or sorrow that needs to be received, not managed
- **Despair** — hopelessness, collapse, or numbness where engagement used to be
- **Longing** — an ache for closeness, to be known, or for something not yet fully named

*If no emotion is clearly identifiable, begin with §1 Safe regardless of pattern. Emotional inaccessibility is itself a signal worth bringing. Note it in the Nuance Notes section of the Theological Anchor.*

*If two emotions are present, work the one with the stronger somatic signal first — the one felt most in the body.*
