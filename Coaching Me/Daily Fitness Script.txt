DAILY FITNESS
Lies vs. Truth in Scripture (ESV)


WHAT IS ALL OF ONE'S HEART AND MIND?

The Hebrew word lev, meaning heart, encompasses intellect, will, emotions, and conscience, the entire inner person. Deuteronomy 6:5, the Shema, defines the scope: heart, meaning inner being, soul, meaning life force, and might, meaning resources, energy, and capacity. All means no compartmentalization, no withheld domain, no competing loyalties. It is totality without exception.

WHAT DOES SURRENDER HAVE TO DO WITH THIS?

Surrender is the mechanism of all. You cannot give all while retaining control. Romans 12:1 says to present your bodies as a living sacrifice. That is the New Testament parallel. Surrender is not a one time act but a posture. Luke 9:23 says to deny himself and take up his cross daily. It is continuous.

BESEECHING IN PRAYER

The Greek word deesis in Philippians 4:6 means earnest supplication. It implies approaching God from a position of acknowledged need and dependence. It is not casual asking. It is the prayer of someone who knows they cannot solve this themselves.

LEANING ON THE LORD'S UNDERSTANDING

Proverbs 3:5 gives a negative command: do not lean on your own understanding. That assumes a default tendency to do exactly that. Active trust means deliberately deferring your own analysis when it conflicts with what God has declared.

MEDITATING AND MURMURING HIS WORD

The Hebrew word hagah, found in Psalm 1:2 and Joshua 1:8, literally means to mutter or murmur. It is vocal rehearsal of Scripture, not silent mental activity. It is the act of speaking the Word back to yourself until it displaces competing thoughts. It is not passive. It is aggressive cognitive replacement.


THE LIE OF HOPELESSNESS
There is no future, no way out, no point.

The Truth

Jeremiah 29:11
For I know the plans I have for you, declares the LORD, plans for welfare and not for evil, to give you a future and a hope.

2 Chronicles 7:14
If my people who are called by my name humble themselves, and pray and seek my face and turn from their wicked ways, then I will hear from heaven and will forgive their sin and heal their land.

Luke 11:22
But when one stronger than he attacks him and overcomes him, he takes away his armor in which he trusted and divides his spoil.

Romans 15:13
May the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace in believing, so that by the power of the Holy Spirit you may abound in hope.

Lamentations 3:22 through 23
The steadfast love of the LORD never ceases; his mercies never come to an end; they are new every morning; great is your faithfulness.


THE LIE OF SCARCITY
There is not enough, of provision, of grace, of time, of opportunity.

The Truth

Psalm 23:1
The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want.

Philippians 4:19
And my God will supply every need of yours according to his riches in glory in Christ Jesus.

2 Corinthians 9:8
And God is able to make all grace abound to you, so that having all sufficiency in all things at all times, you may abound in every good work.

Matthew 6:31 through 33
Therefore do not be anxious, saying, What shall we eat? But seek first the kingdom of God and his righteousness, and all these things will be added to you.

John 10:10
I came that they may have life and have it abundantly.


THE LIE OF GIVING UP
It is not worth continuing. I am too tired. Nothing is changing.

The Truth

Luke 21:14 through 15
Settle it therefore in your minds not to meditate beforehand how to answer, for I will give you a mouth and wisdom, which none of your adversaries will be able to withstand or contradict.

Proverbs 3:5 through 6
Trust in the LORD with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding. In all your ways acknowledge him, and he will make straight your paths.

Galatians 6:9
And let us not grow weary of doing good, for in due season we will reap, if we do not give up.

Isaiah 40:31
But they who wait for the LORD shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings like eagles; they shall run and not be weary; they shall walk and not faint.

Hebrews 12:1 through 2
Let us run with endurance the race that is set before us, looking to Jesus, the founder and perfecter of our faith.


THE LIE OF FALSE RISK
If I step out, I will fail. The risk is too great. God will not cover me.

The Truth

Isaiah 41:10
Fear not, for I am with you; be not dismayed, for I am your God; I will strengthen you, I will help you, I will uphold you with my righteous right hand.

Romans 8:31
What then shall we say to these things? If God is for us, who can be against us?

Proverbs 16:3
Commit your work to the LORD, and your plans will be established.

2 Timothy 1:7
For God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control.

Philippians 4:13
I can do all things through him who strengthens me.


THE LIE OF BEING ABANDONED BY GOD
God has left. He does not see me. He has moved on.

The Truth

Psalm 91:1
He who dwells in the shelter of the Most High will abide in the shadow of the Almighty.

Hebrews 13:5
I will never leave you nor forsake you.

Isaiah 49:15 through 16
Even these may forget, yet I will not forget you. Behold, I have engraved you on the palms of my hands.

Romans 8:38 through 39
For I am sure that neither death nor life, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord.

Deuteronomy 31:6
Be strong and courageous. Do not fear or be in dread of them, for it is the LORD your God who goes with you. He will not leave you or forsake you.


THE LIE THAT IT CANNOT OR WILL NOT BE DONE THROUGH ME
God might do it through someone else, but not me. This is beyond my capacity.

The Truth

Zechariah 4:6
Not by might, nor by power, but by my Spirit, says the LORD of hosts.

2 Corinthians 12:9
But he said to me, My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness. Therefore I will boast all the more gladly of my weaknesses, so that the power of Christ may rest upon me.

John 15:5
I am the vine; you are the branches. Whoever abides in me and I in him, he it is that bears much fruit, for apart from me you can do nothing.

Ephesians 3:20
Now to him who is able to do far more abundantly than all that we ask or think, according to the power at work within us.


THE LIE THAT I AM NOT ENOUGH OR NOT THE RIGHT PERSON
God needs someone more qualified, more holy, more gifted. Not me.

The Truth

Isaiah 1:18
Come now, let us reason together, says the LORD: though your sins are like scarlet, they shall be as white as snow; though they are red like crimson, they shall become like wool.

Luke 15:24
For this my son was dead, and is alive again; he was lost, and is found. And they began to celebrate.

Psalm 31:24
Be strong, and let your heart take courage, all you who wait for the LORD.

1 Corinthians 1:27 through 29
But God chose what is foolish in the world to shame the wise; God chose what is weak in the world to shame the strong; God chose what is low and despised in the world, even things that are not, to bring to nothing things that are, so that no human being might boast in the presence of God.

2 Corinthians 3:5
Not that we are sufficient in ourselves to claim anything as coming from us, but our sufficiency is from God.


THE LIE OF BEING TOO CONNECTED TO SINFUL HERITAGE AND SIN
My family's sins, my own patterns, I am too wired into this. It cannot be broken.

The Truth

Romans 8:1
There is therefore now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus.

2 Corinthians 5:17
Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old has passed away; behold, the new has come.

Ezekiel 18:20
The son shall not suffer for the iniquity of the father, nor the father suffer for the iniquity of the son.

Galatians 3:13
Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us.

Colossians 1:13 through 14
He has delivered us from the domain of darkness and transferred us to the kingdom of his beloved Son, in whom we have redemption, the forgiveness of sins.


THE LIE OF BEING REJECTABLE
If people really knew me, they would reject me. God himself will eventually turn away.

The Truth

John 6:37
All that the Father gives me will come to me, and whoever comes to me I will never cast out.

Zephaniah 3:17
The LORD your God is in your midst, a mighty one who will save; he will rejoice over you with gladness; he will quiet you by his love; he will exult over you with loud singing.

Ephesians 1:4 through 6
Even as he chose us in him before the foundation of the world, he predestined us for adoption to himself as sons through Jesus Christ, to the praise of his glorious grace, with which he has blessed us in the Beloved.

Psalm 27:10
For my father and my mother have forsaken me, but the LORD will take me in.


THE LIE OF DISGUST TOWARD OTHERS IN GROSS SIN
These people are beyond reach, beyond mercy, unworthy of compassion.

The Truth

Proverbs 3:3
Let not steadfast love and faithfulness forsake you; bind them around your neck; write them on the tablet of your heart.

Romans 5:8
But God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us.

Matthew 9:12 through 13
Those who are well have no need of a physician, but those who are sick. For I came not to call the righteous, but sinners.

Galatians 6:1
Brothers, if anyone is caught in any transgression, you who are spiritual should restore him in a spirit of gentleness. Keep watch on yourself, lest you too be tempted.

Jude 22 through 23
And have mercy on those who doubt; save others by snatching them out of the fire; to others show mercy with fear, hating even the garment stained by sin.


THE LIES BORN FROM LAWFUL THINKING, EGO, RESENTMENT, ANGER, DISGUST, AND FRUSTRATION
I am right. They are wrong. Justice demands my response.

The Truth

Proverbs 3:5 through 7
Trust in the LORD with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding. In all your ways acknowledge him. Be not wise in your own eyes.

James 1:19 through 20
Let every person be quick to hear, slow to speak, slow to anger; for the anger of man does not produce the righteousness of God.

Ephesians 4:31 through 32
Let all bitterness and wrath and anger and clamor and slander be put away from you, along with all malice. Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you.

Matthew 7:3 through 5
Why do you see the speck that is in your brother's eye, but do not notice the log that is in your own eye?

Romans 12:19
Beloved, never avenge yourselves, but leave it to the wrath of God, for it is written, Vengeance is mine, I will repay, says the Lord.

Matthew 5:44
But I say to you, Love your enemies and pray for those who persecute you.


A FINAL WORD

Many of these lies share a common root: self as the reference point. The lies of inadequacy, of rejection, of disgust, of scarcity, of legalism, all assume the self is the measure. Scripture consistently reorients the reference point to God's character, God's word, God's power, and God's record. That shift is where all your heart begins.
