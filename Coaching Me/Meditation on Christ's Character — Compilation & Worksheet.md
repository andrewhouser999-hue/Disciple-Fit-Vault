# Meditation on Christ's Character — Compilation & Worksheet

This is a personal design-lab tool (see [[Lie Eliminator - Personal]] / [[Identity Installer - Personal]] pattern — working document, not yet mirrored to a client version).

**What this is:** A compiled reference of every major biblical "list" of character and virtue — Philippians 4:8 and ten others — paired with a repeatable worksheet card. For each attribute you select, you ground it in the actual words of Scripture, locate it concretely in Christ's character, and name a specific, current opportunity in your own life to exercise it.

**A note on the word "meditate":** Biblical meditation (Hebrew *hagah* — Josh 1:8, Ps 1:2) is not silent abstract thought. It literally means to mutter, growl, or murmur low in the throat — the same word used of a lion growling over its prey. It's audible, repetitive, physical rehearsal of words until they settle into you, not a single mental glance. That's the practice this worksheet is built around: read the phrase, say it low, repeat it, return to it again later the same day. (This is a different word and concept from the *negative* "murmuring" of Israel in the wilderness — Exod 15–17, 1 Cor 10:10 — which is complaint against God, not devotion to Him. Worth keeping the two apart in your own head as you use this.)

## How to Use This Worksheet

1. **Select** — Choose one attribute below that names something you want to grow toward, or something you're currently being tested in.
2. **Ground in the Word** — Read the reference. Say the phrase itself quietly, more than once. Let it settle before you rush to application.
3. **See in Christ** — Ask: where do I see this precisely embodied in Jesus? Name an actual scene from the Gospels, not an abstraction.
4. **Name the Opportunity** — Identify one real, current situation — this week, this relationship, this decision — where this attribute is being asked of you.
5. **Rehearse** — Return to the phrase two or three times that day (morning / midday / evening). Speak it low and repeat it rather than reading it silently once and moving on.

## Compiled Reference

Each list below has a different internal logic — some are things to *receive* (fruit), some to *put on* (deliberate action), some to *think about* (cognitive filter), some to *grow into* (sequential). Keeping that distinction in view keeps you from treating every item the same way.

### Mind to Fix — Philippians 4:8
*(cognitive filter — what you keep "taking into account," Gk. *logizesthe*, ongoing accounting language)*

- **True** — ἀληθῆ, in accordance with reality
- **Honorable** — σεμνά, worthy of respect, dignified
- **Just** — δίκαια, right-standing
- **Pure** — ἁγνά, morally unmixed
- **Lovely** — προσφιλῆ, pleasing, amiable
- **Commendable** — εὔφημα, well-spoken-of
- **Excellent** — ἀρετή, moral excellence
- **Praiseworthy** — ἔπαινος

### Fruit to Receive — Galatians 5:22–23
*(organic — produced by the Spirit, not manufactured by effort)*

Love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control.

### Ladder to Grow Into — 2 Peter 1:5–7
*(sequential — each quality supplementing, not replacing, the one before)*

Faith → virtue → knowledge → self-control → steadfastness → godliness → brotherly affection → love.

### Garment to Put On — Colossians 3:12–14
*(deliberate action — "put on," a clothing metaphor)*

Compassionate hearts, kindness, humility, meekness, patience, bearing with one another, forgiving each other — "and above all these put on love, which binds everything together in perfect harmony."

### Love Defined — 1 Corinthians 13:4–7
*(marks of love in action, stated both positively and negatively)*

Patient, kind; not envious, not boastful, not arrogant, not rude; not irritable or resentful; does not rejoice at wrongdoing but rejoices with the truth; bears all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things.

### Posture Under Pressure — Matthew 5:3–12 (the Beatitudes)
*(Kingdom character revealed under reversal, not general virtue)*

Poor in spirit, mourning, meek, hungering and thirsting for righteousness, merciful, pure in heart, peacemaker, persecuted for righteousness.

### Wisdom from Above — James 3:17
*(contrasted explicitly with "earthly" wisdom)*

Pure, peaceable, gentle, open to reason, full of mercy and good fruits, impartial, sincere.

### Prophetic Summary — Micah 6:8

Do justice, love kindness, walk humbly with your God.

### Messianic Template — Isaiah 11:2
*(the Spirit resting on Christ himself — the template every other list is fulfilled in)*

Wisdom, understanding, counsel, might, knowledge, the fear of the LORD.

### Love in Community — Romans 12:9–21 (selected)
*(longer, situational — love enacted among people, including enemies)*

Love without hypocrisy; abhor evil, hold fast to what is good; brotherly affection; fervent in spirit; patient in tribulation; constant in prayer; contribute to the needs of others; bless those who persecute you; live in harmony, do not be haughty; repay no one evil for evil; live peaceably with all; overcome evil with good.

### Entrance Liturgy — Psalm 15 / Psalm 24:3–4
*(worship-oriented — who may dwell with God / ascend his hill)*

Walks blamelessly, does what is right, speaks truth in his heart, does not slander, does no evil to his neighbor, honors those who fear the LORD, keeps his oath even when it hurts, does not lend at interest or take a bribe against the innocent, has clean hands and a pure heart, does not lift up his soul to what is false.
