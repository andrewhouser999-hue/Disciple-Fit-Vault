# Lie Eliminator & Identity Installer — Adaptation Over Time (Design Proposal)

**Status:** Design proposal only. Not yet built into any document. Saved for future development — review and filter before building.

## 1. The redesign already contains a built-in test for "has shift occurred"

Step 1 of "Holding Both" — *"Locate the lie: bring the lie back to mind. Where does it register in the body?"* — is, without anyone designing it as such, a **diagnostic for reconsolidation**. Ecker's framework is specific: when a belief has been genuinely reconsolidated (not suppressed), reactivating it produces *nothing* — no charge, no body location, sometimes outright bafflement that you ever believed it. That's structurally different from extinction, where the old response is still latent and can return under stress.

So the first answer to "how should this adapt over time" is: **the exercise already tells you when it's time to adapt, every time you run it.** If step 1 keeps coming up empty for a given lie/identity in its original activating context, that's the signal — not a separate check-in process.

## 2. What "shift" actually looks like, per the established sources

- **Ecker** — the old response becomes *unavailable*, not suppressed. You can't "try" to feel the old way even if you wanted to.
- **Gendlin** (felt sense) — the felt shift isn't a recurring dramatic event. Once integrated, what needed addressing becomes simply *how things are now* — it stops being noteworthy. Expecting another "loosening, exhale, warmth" moment every time is expecting the wrong signal.
- **Fosha** — there's a second-order transformational affect available here too: *processing the change itself* ("I can't believe how different this feels now" / relief at the absence of the old reaction) is itself integrative. This gives you a legitimate thing to do with an integrated entry that isn't just repetition.

## 3. The risk of running the full sequence indefinitely

Three concrete failure modes if "Holding Both" / full archaeology continues unchanged after shift:

1. **Manufactured activation.** Step 1 instructs you to find tension. If you go looking for it in a place that's actually quiet now, demand characteristics can produce a faint "something" that isn't real — you're now rehearsing a search-for-threat pattern, not a resolved belief.
2. **Vigilance-as-identity.** Continuing to "fight" a defeated lie daily risks installing a *new* implicit belief: "I am someone permanently under siege from this." That's a subtle drift back toward the lie-shaped self-concept the tool exists to dismantle.
3. **Positive-thinking drift** — the exact failure mode "How This Document Works" already names. A declaration repeated past the point of relevance becomes words running parallel to reality rather than words *doing* anything — Steele's "second belief running parallel" problem, just relocated to the truth side instead of the lie side.

## 4. Proposed adaptation: a three-phase structure (design synthesis)

Built on Prochaska's Transtheoretical Model (Maintenance stage, relapse-as-information loop) — not yet in the Source Library, but Established-tier and directly relevant to "what comes after Action."

**Phase 1 — Active** (current full sequence, unchanged): Archaeology → Agreement Audit → Stronghold Anatomy → Secondary Gain → Talking Back → Holding Both → Declaration. Used while step 1 of Holding Both still produces a real signal.

**Phase 2 — Integrating** (Prochaska's "Action," first weeks after shift): Spot-check, don't rehearse. Re-run step 1 in the *original* activating context only. Two outcomes:
- **Quiet** → log it. This is where the long-underused "**Who Has Witnessed This Truth**" and "**My Evidence**" fields finally get used — not as homework, but as the natural record of "this didn't fire when it used to."
- **Still some charge** → that's a *different strand* of the Stronghold Anatomy (argument vs. evidence vs. feeling vs. habit), not a failed reconsolidation. Run the full sequence again, but targeted at that strand specifically — not the whole lie from scratch.

**Phase 3 — Integrated** (Prochaska's "Maintenance"): Three changes to the entry itself:
- **Declaration shifts tense** — present-tense combat ("I am not...") becomes past-tense testimony ("I used to believe X. Here's what's true now, and here's how I know."). This is McAdams' redemptive-sequence narrative.
- **Holding Both is repurposed**, not retired — see §5.
- **"Today I Will" shifts from rehearsal to generalization** — see §6.

A relapse (the lie fires again later, in a new context) isn't a failure of Phase 3 — per Prochaska, it's information that a *new* activating context exists. That context gets its own "This activates when" entry and re-enters Phase 1 for that context only. The original entry stays Integrated.

## 5. How "Holding Both" specifically adapts in Phase 3

Instead of *lie-location vs. truth-location* (contest), it becomes *old-pattern-location (now quiet) vs. new-identity-location (now active)* — a consolidation/testimony practice, not a confrontation. One hand on the place that used to brace, one hand on where the new identity now lives, and the noticing is "this used to be a fight, and now it isn't" — Fosha's second-order transformational affect, given somewhere to land. Keeps the body-based practice without re-triggering anything.

## 6. Generalization is the actual "next step" — not repetition

Schema Therapy (Young, Tier 1) is explicit that schema change which isn't deliberately generalized stays context-bound — state-dependent learning means a belief can be dismantled in one context and remain fully intact in an adjacent one. ACT (Hayes) frames the same thing positively: once values-based action is no longer a struggle in context A, the next move is *extending* it into context B, not perfecting it further in A (diminishing returns — Hebb's Law has a ceiling; once a pathway is strongly potentiated, additional rehearsal in the same context yields little).

Practically: Phase 3's "Today I Will" stops being "practice this identity" and becomes "find the adjacent context where the old pattern might still be running, and act from the new identity there for the first time." This also generates the next "This activates when" entry organically — the document grows by generalization, not by repetition.

## 7. Direct answer: "declaration + supporting truths only, once shift has occurred?"

Partially — but with two changes, not a straight narrowing:
- **Yes**, the heavy excavation work (archaeology, agreement audit, secondary gain) drops away — that work is specifically for *reactivating* a belief that's still live, and there's nothing left to reactivate.
- **But** the declaration itself needs to **change form** (present-tense fight → past-tense testimony), and a **new field** (generalization target) becomes more important than the old declaration. Pure repetition of the original declaration, unchanged, is the positive-thinking failure mode — not a safe default.

## 8. If building — five candidate pieces

1. A **Status/Phase marker** per entry (Active / Integrating / Integrated) — lightweight, just a line under "This activates when"
2. A **Testimony** field (replaces/supplements "Who Has Witnessed This Truth") for Phase 3 entries
3. A **Generalization target** field replacing "Today I Will" in Phase 3
4. A short new section in "How This Document Works" / Identity Installer's psychology section explaining the phase logic and the relapse-as-information reframe
5. Possibly add **Prochaska's Transtheoretical Model** to the Source Library (Domain 5) as the grounding for the Maintenance/relapse framing

**Recommendation:** hold off on retrofitting the existing 14+23+6 entries until the phase structure has been tested against how change has actually played out for at least one or two entries in practice — this is more design judgment than the "Holding Both" addition was, and there's a real risk of over-engineering a maintenance system for entries that haven't shifted yet.
