# Identity Installer — Beatitudes
*Nine identity entries built from the Beatitude formation sequence — Matthew 5:3–12*

**Format:** Ephesians 4:22–24 — Put Off → Renew → Put On
**Basis:** Each Beatitude describes a character posture. Each entry installs that posture as identity — not behavior to perform, but person to inhabit.

**How to use:** Read daily during the season you are working a particular Beatitude. Do not rush through all nine. Work the one that is your growing edge. Return to earlier entries when the lie resurfaces.

**The sequence matters.** Each entry assumes the prior ones are operative. B3 (Meekness) is not accessible without B1 (Poverty of Spirit) having done its work. Work in order.

---

## Before You Begin

The Spirit is the active agent here — not you. What you are doing is creating conditions: presenting truth, engaging memory, opening the will. What you cannot do is cause truth to move from comprehension to transformation. That is illumination, and it is the Spirit's work.

*Holy Spirit, illuminate what I am about to declare. Make it true not just in my mind but in my depths. In Jesus' name.*

Speak declarations aloud. Close each one in Jesus' name — not as formula, but as acknowledgment that the authority is Christ's and the transformation is the Spirit's.

## The Sequence as a Daily Practice

These nine entries are not a checklist to complete. They are a formation system to inhabit over time. One way to use them:

**Current growing edge:** Identify which Beatitude is your active growing edge — the one where the lie is most active, where the posture is least natural, where the Holy Spirit seems to be pressing. Work that entry daily until it moves from intellectual to embodied.

**Under attack:** When a specific lie activates under pressure, return to the corresponding entry. The felt sense prompt is particularly important under pressure — cognitive truth accessed in calm does not always hold under stress. Somatically anchored truth does.

**Sequential review:** Move through all nine entries monthly as a character audit. Which ones feel solid? Which ones feel hollow? Which one produced something in the body when you read it? That is your current entry point.
