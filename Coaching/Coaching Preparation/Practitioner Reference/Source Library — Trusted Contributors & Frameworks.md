# Source Library — Trusted Contributors & Frameworks

This document records every major contributor, researcher, theologian, and thought leader drawn upon in the Disciple Fit / Covenant Identity Coaching practice. It serves three purposes: (1) enabling intelligent conversation with clients about what informs the work, (2) providing a defensible foundation for critics and peer review, and (3) maintaining authorship integrity by keeping the practitioner accountable to understanding, not just possessing, the sources.

**Structure:** Contributors are organized by domain and tier. Each entry includes primary works, specific contribution to this practice, evidence level, scope boundary, honest shortcomings, and which documents they inform.

**Evidence levels:** Established (meta-analyses, clinical consensus, replicated findings) / Supported (solid studies, emerging consensus) / Theoretical (plausible, not yet well-studied) / Expert opinion (respected voice, limited empirical backing)

**Updated:** 2026-05-13

---

## How to Use

- **For clients:** Cite the contributor name and the plain-language version of their contribution. Avoid jargon. Frame as "the practice draws on research by..."
- **For critics:** The full entry — works, evidence level, scope boundary, and shortcomings — is the defensible account.
- **For authorship:** The integrity test at the bottom of Tier 1 entries asks: can you explain this contributor's contribution without reading the entry? If not, that is the gap to close.

## Domain 1 — Theological Foundation

### Tier 1 — Structurally Load-Bearing

---

#### Walter Brueggemann
**Primary works:** *The Covenanted Self* (1999); *The Prophetic Imagination* (1978); *Theology of the Old Testament* (1997)
**Contribution:** Establishes covenant as ontologically prior to selfhood — not a doctrine the self adopts but the relational structure in which selfhood is constituted. Provides the biblical case that identity is covenantal at its root, not psychological. His reading of the historical prologue (grace before obligation) grounds the "already true" framing of covenant identity. Provides the structural account of lament: not a failure of faith but a covenant right — complaint, petition, and protest *within* the relationship, not against it.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (biblical scholarship, not empirical research)
**Scope:** Provides covenant structure and lament theology. Does *not* provide a psychological account of how change happens, therapeutic methodology, or clinical guidance. Does not address the New Covenant with the same depth as Reformed covenant theologians.
**Shortcomings:** Brueggemann is not a Reformed theologian. His covenant framework is more dialogical and bilateral than classical Reformed forensic accounts. His OT theological method (rhetorical-critical) is not universally accepted among evangelical scholars. Import him for the covenantal structure of identity and lament; do not import his broader hermeneutical method or his political theology without separate scrutiny. Some of his broader OT theology would be contested by evangelical exegetes.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; Covenant Identity — Discovery Call Guide; Secured in Father — Attachment Formation & Encounter Preparation

---

#### J. Todd Billings
**Primary works:** *Union with Christ* (2011); *Rejoicing in Lament* (2015)
**Contribution:** Most precise theological account of union with Christ as the load-bearing hinge of covenant identity. Grounds all identity statements in covenantal union with Christ rather than behavioral or emotional change — "already true" is a union reality, not an aspiration. *Rejoicing in Lament* is the most exegetically careful treatment of lament as covenant practice for contemporary readers.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (Reformed systematic theology)
**Scope:** Union with Christ as theological category and lament as covenantal practice. Does *not* address psychological mechanism, formation methodology, or therapeutic application.
**Shortcomings:** Academically rigorous; less practically applicative for session work without translation. His treatment assumes familiarity with Reformed systematic categories.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; Covenant Identity — Integration Rationale

---

#### Sinclair Ferguson
**Primary works:** *Children of the Living God* (1989)
**Contribution:** Adoption as the central operative category for covenant identity in daily life. Sonship/daughtership as the relational register for all Christian living — not a metaphor but the actual relationship. Provides the Father-child framing for encounter and prayer preparation.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (Reformed systematic theology)
**Scope:** Adoption as identity category. Does not address psychological formation or clinical application.
**Shortcomings:** Pastoral register; not academic. Assumes Reformed soteriology.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; Secured in Father — Attachment Formation & Encounter Preparation

---

#### N.T. Wright
**Primary works:** *After You Believe* (2010); *The Day the Revolution Began* (2016); *Paul and the Faithfulness of God* (2013)
**Contribution:** New covenant participation as the foundation for virtue formation and character change. Behavioral extension of covenant identity — "putting on" as formation rather than willpower. Strongest NT scholar for grounding why covenant identity produces character change through participation, not effort.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (NT scholarship)
**Scope:** New covenant anthropology and virtue formation. Does not address psychological mechanism or inner healing directly.
**Shortcomings:** N.T. Wright's broader New Perspective on Paul is contested by Reformed scholars on justification. Import his virtue formation framework and covenant participation framework; hold his justification framework with appropriate awareness of the debate.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference

---

#### Michael Horton
**Primary works:** *Introducing Covenant Theology* (2006); *The Christian Faith* (2011)
**Contribution:** Systematic theological account of covenant structure across Scripture — Covenant of Works, Covenant of Grace, the progressive covenants. Provides the theological argument for why salvation places a client in covenant (not merely relationship) and why covenant promises are structurally binding rather than aspirational.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (Reformed systematic theology)
**Scope:** Systematic covenant theology. Does not address identity formation, psychological mechanism, or session methodology.
**Shortcomings:** Strongly Reformed confessional — his framework assumes Westminster Standards positions. Not all evangelical traditions will share his precise covenant structure. Use for systematic grounding; hold denominational specifics with awareness.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference

---

#### O. Palmer Robertson
**Primary works:** *The Christ of the Covenants* (1980)
**Contribution:** Classic Reformed treatment of covenant unity across Scripture — the one covenant of grace progressively administered. Provides historical-exegetical grounding for the claim that the God of both Testaments is the same covenant-keeping God, which anchors counter-narrative work against distorted God-images.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (Reformed biblical theology)
**Scope:** Biblical-theological account of covenant across Scripture. Not a psychological or formation resource.
**Shortcomings:** Dated (1980); reflects pre-New Perspective scholarship. Valuable for classic Reformed covenant structure; less useful for engaging contemporary OT scholarship.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference

### Tier 2 — Theological Voice and Credibility

---

#### Gordon Fee
**Primary works:** *God's Empowering Presence* (1994)
**Contribution:** Most academically rigorous treatment of the Spirit in Paul's letters. Fee establishes that for Paul, the Spirit is the *experienced reality* of new covenant living — not peripheral, not occasional, but the defining mark of new covenant participation. Provides exegetical grounding for why Holy Spirit partnership is not optional in the coaching model.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (NT scholarship)
**Scope:** Pauline pneumatology. Does not address formation methodology or psychological application.
**Shortcomings:** 900+ pages; requires significant reading investment. Fee's own tradition (Pentecostal) shapes his reading at points, though his exegesis is widely respected across traditions.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; Covenant Identity — Integration Rationale

---

#### Cornelius Plantinga Jr.
**Primary works:** *Not the Way It's Supposed to Be* (1995)
**Contribution:** Shalom as the covenantal frame for what is broken in human identity and what restoration looks like. Provides theological vocabulary for the diagnostic layer — what is "not the way it's supposed to be" in a client's inner world.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (theology)
**Scope:** Theological account of sin as disruption of shalom. Does not address formation methodology.
**Shortcomings:** More useful for framing than for session deployment. Academic register.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference

---

#### Stanley Hauerwas
**Primary works:** *A Community of Character* (1981)
**Contribution:** Theological account of the church as identity-forming community — identity is constituted by the practices of a particular people, not by individual decision. Grounds the communal dimension of covenant identity formation.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (ethics/ecclesiology)
**Scope:** Community as formation context. Does not address individual psychology or session methodology.
**Shortcomings:** Hauerwas is not evangelical and his ecclesiology is more Catholic-sympathetic than most Protestant frameworks. Import his community-as-formation insight; hold his broader ecclesiology with appropriate distance.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference

---

#### Ed Welch
**Primary works:** *When People Are Big and God Is Small* (1997); *Shame Interrupted* (2012)
**Contribution:** Fear of man as the diagnostic root beneath most presenting lies. Provides the fastest practical heuristic for locating the category of lie armed in a client — most lies are fear-of-man lies in structure, even when the content varies. *Shame Interrupted* provides a biblical counseling account of shame that is directly applicable to session work.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (biblical counseling)
**Scope:** Lie-root diagnosis; fear-of-man structure; shame framework. Does not provide psychological mechanism or clinical methodology.
**Shortcomings:** CCEF / biblical counseling framework. Welch and the CCEF model are skeptical of integrating psychological frameworks — this practice takes a different integration posture, importing his diagnostic insight while not adopting his broader anti-integration stance.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic

---

#### Larry Crabb
**Primary works:** *Inside Out* (1988); *Connecting* (1997); *Soul Talk* (2003); *Shattered Dreams* (2001)
**Contribution:** Depth-psychological account of what the soul is protecting beneath presenting behavior. The "beneath-the-surface" diagnostic posture. *Connecting* establishes that the deepest healing happens through soul-to-soul connection — the coaching relationship itself is formative. Ache beneath dysfunction as covenant-shaped hunger, not merely psychological need.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (Christian psychology / biblical counseling)
**Scope:** Beneath-surface diagnosis; relational connection as formation; soul longing. Does not provide empirically validated intervention methodology.
**Shortcomings:** Crabb has shifted his framework significantly across his career (*Inside Out* → *Connecting* → later mystical-contemplative direction). The *Inside Out* and *Connecting* frameworks are most directly applicable here. His later work becomes more contemplative and less structured — useful but requires discernment. Not empirically grounded.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; Covenant Identity — Whole-Person Identity Framework

---

#### Paul David Tripp
**Primary works:** *Instruments in the Redeemer's Hands* (2002); *New Morning Mercies* (2014)
**Contribution:** Gospel-centered behavioral change — how daily belief connects to behavioral enactment of identity. "Therefore I Will" as a coaching tool. Highly practical session applicability for the behavioral extension layer of covenant identity work.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (biblical counseling / pastoral)
**Scope:** Gospel-to-behavior connection; daily application of identity. Does not provide psychological mechanism.
**Shortcomings:** CCEF framework; same integration caution as Welch. Pastoral register rather than clinical.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; session tools

---

#### Dietrich Bonhoeffer
**Primary works:** *Life Together* (1954)
**Contribution:** Real vs. idealized community as a diagnostic distinction. The "dream of community" concept — when clients' relational wounds are layered with idealization, naming the distinction is the diagnostic entry point. Most penetrating account of this dynamic available.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (theology / pastoral)
**Scope:** Community idealization and real community. Not a psychological or session methodology resource.
**Shortcomings:** Historical context (German church struggle) shapes some of his framing. Short text — high return.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Author-Framework Mapping; session dialogue

## Domain 2 — Attachment Theory and Neuroscience

### Tier 1 — Structurally Load-Bearing

---

#### John Bowlby
**Primary works:** *Attachment and Loss, Vol. 1: Attachment* (1969); *A Secure Base* (1988)
**Contribution:** Foundational attachment theory. The Internal Working Model (IWM) as the cognitive-emotional schema through which a person organizes experience of self and others — answers "Am I worthy of love?" and "Are others available and trustworthy?" The drive to seek proximity to a protective figure as evolutionarily embedded, not preference. Four attachment criteria (proximity maintenance, safe haven, secure base, separation distress) applied to relationship with God via Kirkpatrick.
**Evidence level:** Established (foundational; decades of replicated research)
**Scope:** Attachment as a relational system organizing self-other experience. Does not address spiritual formation or therapeutic intervention directly.
**Shortcomings:** Bowlby's original framework was built on ethological and psychoanalytic foundations that have been refined by subsequent researchers (Ainsworth, Main, Siegel). Import the IWM concept and the four criteria; note that the specific attachment classification system was developed by Ainsworth, not Bowlby.
**Informs:** Secured in Father — Attachment Formation & Encounter Preparation; We Become Who We Are Loved By; Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference

---

#### Mary Ainsworth
**Primary works:** *Patterns of Attachment* (Ainsworth, Blehar, Waters, Wall, 1978)
**Contribution:** Identification of three primary attachment styles (secure, anxious/preoccupied, avoidant/dismissing) through the Strange Situation methodology. The empirical classification system that operationalized Bowlby's theory. Foundation for the three-pattern framework used throughout the practice.
**Evidence level:** Established (extensively replicated; cross-cultural validation)
**Scope:** Attachment classification in infancy; foundational for adult attachment research. Does not directly address adult attachment or God-representation.
**Shortcomings:** Strange Situation methodology is an infant-observation tool; adult attachment application draws on later work (Main, Hazan & Shaver, Fraley). The disorganized/fearful-avoidant category was added by Main — not in Ainsworth's original three.
**Informs:** Secured in Father — Attachment Formation & Encounter Preparation; Attachment-Informed Coaching

---

#### Daniel Siegel
**Primary works:** *The Developing Mind* (1999); *Mindsight* (2010)
**Contribution:** Interpersonal neurobiology (IPNB) as the neuroscientific account of how attachment shapes neural development. "The mind is the embodied and relational process that regulates the flow of energy and information." Earned security — adults with insecure attachment histories can develop secure attachment through new relational experiences. Narrative coherence as a predictor of attachment security. The child's nervous system organized through co-regulation with the caregiver's nervous system.
**Evidence level:** Supported (IPNB is emerging consensus, not fully established; earned security is well-supported)
**Scope:** Neural basis of attachment; earned security; narrative coherence. Does not address theological framework or spiritual formation.
**Shortcomings:** Siegel's telos — integration as the goal of human development — is a secular framework. The Disciple Fit model imports his mechanism (how the nervous system organizes through relationship) but not his telos (integration as ultimate goal). His framework also does not account for the Spirit's role in formation, which is the primary mechanism here. Do not import his integration-as-ultimate-good framing.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Integration Rationale; Covenant Identity — Whole-Person Identity Framework; Secured in Father

---

#### Mary Main
**Primary works:** Adult Attachment Interview (AAI); *Attachment Theory: Eighteen Points* (1999)
**Contribution:** The Adult Attachment Interview as the gold-standard assessment tool for adult attachment classification. Most replicated finding: a parent's narrative coherence about their own childhood predicts the child's attachment classification with approximately 75% accuracy — regardless of the content of the childhood experience. Introduced the disorganized/fearful-avoidant category. Intergenerational transmission of attachment through narrative.
**Evidence level:** Established (AAI has extensive replication and validity research)
**Scope:** Adult attachment assessment; intergenerational transmission via narrative. Does not address intervention.
**Shortcomings:** The AAI is a research/clinical tool — not directly applicable in coaching contexts. The insight about narrative coherence is what the practice imports: how a person tells their story matters more than what happened. This is a coaching-applicable principle; the AAI itself is not coach-deployed.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Author-Framework Mapping; We Become Who We Are Loved By

---

#### Curt Thompson
**Primary works:** *Anatomy of the Soul* (2010); *The Soul of Shame* (2015); *The Soul of Desire* (2021)
**Contribution:** The most directly applicable integration of IPNB and Christian theology for this practice. Shame as a disruption of covenant belonging — not merely a feeling but a neural-relational state in which the person experiences isolation from the community of knowing. Healing through being fully known — by another human and ultimately by God. The God-representation as encoded implicitly in the nervous system, distinct from theological belief. The integrated person as one whose neural circuits — left-right, top-bottom — are coherent. Attunement as the relational mechanism that repairs insecure attachment and shame.
**Evidence level:** Supported (draws on established IPNB research; theological integration is expert opinion)
**Scope:** IPNB-informed theology of shame, identity, and formation. The most bridge-building source between neuroscience and covenant theology in the practice.
**Shortcomings:** Thompson's telos language — being "known" as the ultimate goal — draws from Siegel's integration framework and requires theological reframing. "Being known by God" is correct as the ultimate frame, but Thompson's account of what "knowing" accomplishes is more IPNB-driven than theologically grounded. His therapeutic methodology is implicitly relational psychotherapy — this practice imports his diagnostic framework (shame, attunement, implicit memory) while using coaching methodology for intervention. He is not a systematic theologian; his scriptural use is pastoral rather than exegetical.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Whole-Person Identity Framework; Covenant Identity — Integration Rationale; Covenant Identity — Affections Discovery — In-Session Protocol; Secured in Father; We Become Who We Are Loved By

---

#### Lee Kirkpatrick
**Primary works:** *Attachment, Evolution, and the Psychology of Religion* (2005)
**Contribution:** Empirical bridge between attachment theory and the psychology of religion. The Correspondence Hypothesis: a person's attachment style with humans corresponds to their functional relationship with God. The Compensation Hypothesis: for those with insecure human attachment, God may function as a substitute attachment figure. Applied Bowlby's four attachment criteria directly to God — God as attachment figure meeting proximity maintenance, safe haven, secure base, and separation distress functions.
**Evidence level:** Supported (multiple studies; replicated correlation between attachment style and God-representation)
**Scope:** Attachment patterns as they function in relationship with God. Does not address theological or clinical intervention.
**Shortcomings:** Kirkpatrick's framework is psychology of religion — empirical, not theological. The correspondence finding is descriptive (people relate to God the way they relate to humans) but does not prescribe theological categories. Import the empirical finding and the four criteria; the theological account of why this is true and what to do about it is supplied by other contributors.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — God-Representation — Research Basis & Synthesis; We Become Who We Are Loved By; Secured in Father

---

#### Todd W. Hall
**Primary works:** *Relational Spirituality* (with S. Hall, 2021); *Attachment to God* research (with J.P. Moreland, 2009)
**Contribution:** Empirical distinction between implicit and explicit spiritual functioning. Attachment patterns with humans correspond to the *implicit* God-image (how God is emotionally experienced) but not to explicit theological belief. New relational experiences — not new information — are required to change the implicit God-image. The relational template as neurologically encoded expectation that operates below conscious processing.
**Evidence level:** Supported (empirical research program; convergent with IPNB findings)
**Scope:** Implicit vs. explicit God-image; the gap between theological belief and functional experience. Does not address coaching methodology.
**Shortcomings:** Hall's work is empirical psychology — the theological interpretation of his findings is supplied by other contributors. His research supports why information-delivery alone doesn't change the client's experienced relationship with God; the theological account of what does change it is here supplied by Thompson, Benner, and the covenant framework.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — God-Representation — Research Basis & Synthesis; Covenant Identity — Integration Rationale

---

### Tier 2 — Complementary and Specialized

---

#### Ana-Maria Rizzuto
**Primary works:** *The Birth of the Living God* (1979)
**Contribution:** The God-representation as a distinct psychological entity — an internal working model of how a person *imagines* God to be, formed from early relational experience with primary caregivers. The God-representation is not the theologian's God but the "living God" — the internal psychological reality that governs how a person actually relates to God. The Static-Yet-Mature Problem: the God-representation can remain unchanged from childhood even as theological understanding matures. Transformation of God-representation requires new relational experience, not cognitive assent.
**Evidence level:** Theoretical / Expert opinion (psychoanalytic framework; limited empirical validation)
**Scope:** Formation and structure of the God-representation. Does not address intervention methodology or theological framework.
**Shortcomings:** Rizzuto works within an object-relations psychoanalytic framework and treats God as a "transitional object" — a psychological category that does not presuppose God's actual existence. The practice imports her descriptive account (how the representation is formed and why it is resistant to change) while operating from a theological framework that treats the living God as real, not illusory. Her framework is descriptive, not prescriptive — it explains the problem; the covenant framework provides the solution.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — God-Representation — Research Basis & Synthesis

---

#### David Benner
**Primary works:** *Surrender to Love* (2003); *The Gift of Being Yourself* (2004); *Sacred Companions* (2002)
**Contribution:** The gap between the believed-in God and the experienced God as the primary blockage in spiritual formation. Nothing can be transcended until it is first accepted. The pathway — Acknowledgment → Encounter → Surrender — as the formation sequence. Prayer as communion with God, not communication about God. Bridges depth psychology and Christian spiritual direction with high practical applicability for coaching.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (spiritual direction / depth psychology integration)
**Scope:** God-representation gap; surrender as transformation condition; spiritual direction framework. Does not provide empirical research.
**Shortcomings:** Benner's integration draws from depth psychology (not specifically CBT or behavioral approaches) and contemplative spirituality. His framework is experiential and relational — which fits the model — but requires more structure for coaching deployment than he provides. He does not provide session methodology; he provides the theological-psychological account of why encounter matters.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — God-Representation — Research Basis & Synthesis; Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference

## Domain 3 — Christian Psychology and Integration

### Tier 1 — Structurally Load-Bearing

---

#### Eric L. Johnson
**Primary works:** *Foundations for Soul Care* (2007); *Christianity and Psychology: Four Views* (ed., 2010)
**Contribution:** The primary integration rationale for this practice. Johnson develops "Christian psychology" as a distinct position — Scripture as governing framework, psychology as a tool that contributes mechanism and vocabulary where Scripture does not directly address. Distinguishes Christian psychology (Scripture governs) from integration (equal dialogue) and biblical counseling (skeptical of psychology). Provides the theoretical justification for importing Thompson, Allender, Siegel, and others under scriptural governance. The most systematic account of why and how psychological frameworks can be used without theological compromise.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (systematic; academic-level framework)
**Scope:** Integration methodology and theoretical rationale. Does not provide session methodology or therapeutic technique.
**Shortcomings:** "Christian psychology" as a formal position is debated within evangelical academic circles — it is contested by both pure integration advocates and biblical counseling advocates. Johnson's framework is more systematic than any single denomination would endorse in full. The practice uses his governing position (Scripture governs; psychology contributes) without adopting all of his specific proposals. His framework is the *rationale* for the integration; it does not resolve every specific integration question.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Integration Rationale; Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference

---

#### Dan Allender
**Primary works:** *The Wounded Heart* (1990); *To Be Told* (2005); *The Healing Path* (1999); *Bold Love* (1992)
**Contribution:** Wound archaeology at the lie level — tracing how specific wounds produce specific lie structures. Identity formed through reading one's own story with God as the covenantal author. Narrative as the therapeutic medium: the client's story is the diagnostic data. Most wounds are relational wounds; most healing is relational healing. The Healing Path provides a structured diagnostic for detecting "agreements with lies" and "first morsel of doubt" — where the wound's lie was first accepted. The wounded heart as the deepest diagnostic category.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (clinically informed; not empirically validated as a distinct framework)
**Scope:** Story-based wound archaeology; lie-structure detection; narrative identity work. Does not provide empirically validated intervention protocols.
**Shortcomings:** Allender's story-reading approach can drift toward treating subjective narrative and emotional experience as authoritative — which requires theological guardrails in this practice. The story is diagnostic data; Scripture governs interpretation of what the story means and where it leads. His framework can also implicitly encourage revisiting trauma content in ways that approach clinical territory — coaching deployment requires appropriate scope awareness. Allender's own therapeutic context is clinical; his methodology is adapted for coaching use here with scope boundaries intact.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool; Covenant Identity — Affections Discovery — In-Session Protocol; Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic; Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference

## Domain 4 — Spiritual Formation

### Tier 1 — Structurally Load-Bearing

---

#### Dallas Willard
**Primary works:** *Renovation of the Heart* (2002); *Hearing God* (1999); *The Spirit of the Disciplines* (1988)
**Contribution:** The most rigorous philosophical and theological account of Spirit-cooperative transformation. *Renovation of the Heart* maps how the Spirit works across every dimension of the person — mind, will, body, emotions, social world, soul — providing the anthropological architecture for whole-person formation. *The Spirit of the Disciplines* establishes spiritual disciplines as the means by which we place ourselves in the path of transformation we cannot produce by willpower. *Hearing God* is the most carefully reasoned treatment of cultivating the interactive voice of God in daily life.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (philosophical theology)
**Scope:** Spirit-cooperative transformation; spiritual disciplines as formation means; hearing God. Does not address psychological mechanism or clinical application.
**Shortcomings:** Willard works at a philosophical level — his framework is architecturally indispensable but requires translation for session deployment. He also does not address the attachment system directly — the psychological account of *why* disciplines do or do not work for particular clients is supplied by Thompson, Siegel, and Kirkpatrick.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; Covenant Identity — Integration Rationale

---

#### Richard Foster
**Primary works:** *Celebration of Discipline* (1978); *Prayer: Finding the Heart's True Home* (1992)
**Contribution:** Practical taxonomy of spiritual disciplines — inward, outward, corporate — as Spirit-access channels. The most accessible and widely used framework for mapping between-session practices. *Prayer* provides the most comprehensive pastoral account of prayer forms and their function.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (pastoral / devotional)
**Scope:** Spiritual disciplines as practices; prayer forms. Does not provide psychological mechanism or empirical grounding.
**Shortcomings:** Foster draws from a broad contemplative tradition (including some Catholic and Eastern Orthodox sources) without always marking the theological distance. His framework requires theological discernment in selection — not all disciplines are equally applicable to all clients. Some of his contemplative practices (centering prayer) are contested by evangelical critics. Treat as a disciplinary taxonomy with pastoral wisdom; evaluate specific practices theologically before recommending to clients.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; between-session practice recommendations

---

#### Ruth Haley Barton
**Primary works:** *Sacred Rhythms* (2006); *Strengthening the Soul of Your Leader* (2008); *Invitation to Solitude and Silence* (2004)
**Contribution:** Coach soul preparation as an essential prerequisite — what the coach carries into the session shapes what the client can encounter. Discernment framework as a coaching diagnostic method: what is the Spirit already doing in this person? Solitude and silence as foundational disciplines for the coach's own formation. The most practically applicable source for coaching posture and pre-session preparation.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (spiritual direction / leadership formation)
**Scope:** Coach formation and pre-session preparation; discernment as diagnostic posture. Not a client-facing methodology.
**Shortcomings:** Barton's framework is for leaders and spiritual directors — application to life coaching requires some translation. Her framework is formation-focused rather than session-structured.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; coach preparation protocols

---

#### Henri Nouwen
**Primary works:** *The Wounded Healer* (1972); *Life of the Beloved* (1992); *The Return of the Prodigal Son* (1992)
**Contribution:** The formation arc — Beloved → Broken → Given — as the pattern of covenant identity development. Being formed as beloved sons and daughters through relational encounter first, before behavioral extension. The wounded healer framework: the coach's own wounds, rightly held, are the most powerful resource in the coaching relationship.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (pastoral / contemplative)
**Scope:** Belovedness as identity foundation; the wounded healer posture; formation arc. Not a session methodology.
**Shortcomings:** Nouwen's theology is Catholic and contemplative — not all of his framework maps to an evangelical Protestant context. Import his identity-as-beloved framework and the formation arc; hold his specific sacramental and contemplative theology with awareness of the distance.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; Secured in Father

---

#### Leanne Payne
**Primary works:** *The Healing Presence* (1989); *Restoring the Christian Soul* (1991)
**Contribution:** The most rigorous practitioner account of healing prayer and Spirit agency in identity and wound work. Spirit-led lie renunciation and identity installation as the active mechanism of session work. Her model is the closest structural parallel to the Disciple Fit session architecture — she identifies the same sequence (wound → lie → renunciation → truth installation) that structures this practice.
**Evidence level:** Expert opinion (healing prayer practitioner)
**Scope:** Spirit-active healing prayer as session methodology; lie renunciation; identity installation. Does not provide empirically validated protocols.
**Shortcomings:** Payne works within a charismatic healing prayer context that is more directional and practitioner-active than the coaching model. Her framework assumes a practitioner with developed healing prayer gifts — the coaching deployment uses her sequencing while maintaining coaching posture (client as agent, Spirit as primary mover, coach as witness). Some of her methodology around demonic influence and generational strongholds requires theological discernment before deployment.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; session tool architecture

## Domain 5 — Behavioral Science and Coaching Methodology

### Tier 1 — Structurally Load-Bearing

---

#### Jeffrey Young
**Primary works:** *Reinventing Your Life* (with Klosko, 1994); *Schema Therapy: A Practitioner's Guide* (2003)
**Contribution:** Early maladaptive schemas as deeply-held patterns formed in childhood through chronic unmet emotional needs. 18 schemas organized in five domains. Schema formation through four primary channels: toxic frustration, traumatization, overindulgence, and selective internalization. Coping modes: surrender (live as if the schema is true), avoidance (escape what activates it), overcompensation (fight against it). The Young Schema Questionnaire (YSQ) as a psychometrically validated identification tool.
**Evidence level:** Established (schema therapy has strong empirical support; see Clark & Beck meta-analyses)
**Scope:** Schema identification and the cognitive-behavioral mechanism of early wound-driven patterns. Does not address spiritual formation, theological framework, or healing prayer.
**Shortcomings:** Schema therapy is a secular clinical framework. The Disciple Fit model imports schema identification (schemas as the cognitive-behavioral expression of wound-driven lies) while grounding the change mechanism in covenant truth and Spirit agency rather than pure cognitive restructuring. Schema therapy's change mechanism (mode work, imagery rescripting, limited reparenting) is clinical; the coaching deployment is more limited and appropriately scoped.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; diagnostic intake; pattern recognition

#### Robert Kegan and Lisa Lahey
**Primary works:** *Immunity to Change* (2009); *How the Way We Talk Can Change the Way We Work* (2001)
**Contribution:** The Immunity to Change (ITC) as the explanation for why capable, motivated people fail to follow through on sincere commitments. Non-follow-through is a competing commitment problem, not a motivation deficit — the person has a hidden commitment that is protected by a big assumption (an operational lie formed in wound and held as reality). The four-column map: Goal → Doing/Not Doing → Hidden Commitment → Big Assumption. Kegan's Orders of Mind as developmental framework for what kind of accountability is accessible at each stage.
**Evidence level:** Supported (substantial practitioner evidence; empirical research in development)
**Scope:** Competing commitments as the diagnostic for non-follow-through; accountability coaching; developmental stage. Does not address theological framework or spiritual formation.
**Shortcomings:** Kegan and Lahey's framework is secular developmental psychology. The big assumption maps closely to what this practice calls the "lie" — but the origin and resolution are different. ITC locates the big assumption in developmental experience and resolves it through collaborative investigation; this practice locates the lie in wound and resolves it through covenant truth and Spirit agency. Import the diagnostic tool (the four-column map); interpret findings through the covenant lens.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; accountability coaching; session diagnostics

#### Edward Deci and Richard Ryan
**Primary works:** *Self-Determination Theory* (1985–2000, research program); *Intrinsic Motivation and Self-Determination in Human Behavior* (1985)
**Contribution:** Three basic psychological needs: Autonomy (self-direction), Competence (effectiveness), Relatedness (connection). Motivation continuum from amotivation through controlled (external, introjected) to autonomous (identified, integrated, intrinsic) motivation. Autonomous motivation produces sustained behavior change; controlled motivation (including shame and ego-driven motivation) does not. One of the most extensively validated frameworks in motivational psychology.
**Evidence level:** Established (thousands of studies across cultures, domains, and populations)
**Scope:** Motivation quality and basic psychological needs. Does not address spiritual formation or theological framework.
**Shortcomings:** SDT is a secular framework. The three needs (autonomy, competence, relatedness) have partial theological analogues — but the theological account of what these needs are *for* is supplied by the covenant framework. Autonomy in SDT is self-direction; the covenant framework grounds genuine agency in Spirit-led freedom (Galatians 5:1), not autonomous self-determination. Import the motivation quality distinction (autonomous vs. controlled) and the needs diagnostic; hold the teleology with theological awareness.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; accountability coaching; motivation assessment

#### Aaron Beck
**Primary works:** *Cognitive Therapy of Depression* (1979); *Cognitive Therapy and the Emotional Disorders* (1976)
**Contribution:** The CBT model as the foundational behavioral-change mechanism underlying much of the session work. Automatic thoughts, intermediate beliefs, and core beliefs as the three-layer cognitive structure. The ABC model (Activating event → Belief → Consequence) as the entry point for identifying and restructuring cognitive patterns. Cognitive restructuring as the mechanism by which false beliefs are replaced with accurate ones — maps directly to the lie-nuke → covenant truth sequence.
**Evidence level:** Established (CBT is among the most replicated psychotherapeutic frameworks)
**Scope:** Cognitive restructuring as change mechanism. Does not address spiritual formation, relational healing, or theological framework.
**Shortcomings:** CBT is a clinical modality. This practice deploys CBT-informed tools within coaching scope — not clinical CBT therapy. The distinction matters: coaching use identifies cognitive patterns and introduces restructuring tools; it does not diagnose, treat, or provide therapy for clinical presentations. When CBT tools are insufficient because the block is implicit memory and not accessible to conscious restructuring, the practice shifts to relational/attentive response (Stage 4b in the Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic).
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic; CBC session skills

#### Richard Schwartz
**Primary works:** *Internal Family Systems Therapy* (1995); *No Bad Parts* (2021)
**Contribution:** IFS as a framework for understanding the multiplicity of inner voices — parts that protect, exile, and manage. The Self (capital S) as the undamaged, untraumatized core capable of leading the system. Protector parts (managers and firefighters) and exiles as functional categories for understanding defensive patterns in session. Theological mapping: the Self has analogues in imago Dei; the old self/new creation distinction; and the coaching goal of leading from the Spirit-renewed self rather than from wounded parts.
**Evidence level:** Supported (growing empirical base; meta-analyses in progress)
**Scope:** Internal system mapping; parts language; Self-leadership. Does not address theological framework directly.
**Shortcomings:** IFS is a secular psychotherapy. Schwartz's framework has been enthusiastically adopted in Christian circles but requires theological translation. Specifically: the Self in IFS is not the same as the new creation self in Christ — IFS treats the Self as a pre-existing undamaged core (implying sufficiency without redemption), while the biblical account locates the renewed self in union with Christ and the Spirit's indwelling. Import the parts language for diagnostic purposes and the Self-leadership concept as a practical category; hold the theological claim about the Self's sufficiency with care.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; parts-language in session dialogue

#### Gabriele Oettingen
**Primary works:** *Rethinking Positive Thinking* (2014); WOOP framework and MCII research program (30+ years)
**Contribution:** Mental Contrasting with Implementation Intentions (MCII) as an empirically validated behavior-change framework. Positive visualization alone is associated with *worse* goal attainment — it produces a premature sense of achievement that reduces the energy needed for actual change. Mental contrasting (vivid positive future + realistic obstacle) combined with implementation intentions (if-then behavioral pre-programming) significantly improves follow-through. WOOP (Wish, Outcome, Obstacle, Plan) as the practical delivery tool.
**Evidence level:** Established (30+ years of replication; cross-cultural; multiple populations)
**Scope:** Goal attainment and behavior change methodology. Does not address theological framework or spiritual formation.
**Shortcomings:** WOOP is a secular behavior-change tool. The Disciple Fit deployment pairs it with covenant identity framing — the wish is grounded in calling, the plan is an implementation intention anchored in identity rather than willpower. Import as a practical tool; frame theologically.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; between-session action tools

#### Dan McAdams
**Primary works:** *The Redemptive Self* (2006); *The Stories We Live By* (1993)
**Contribution:** Narrative identity theory — humans construct identity through an internalized, evolving story. Narrative contamination (stories dominated by decline, victimhood, shame) vs. redemptive narrative (movement through suffering toward meaning). The biblical meta-narrative as the original redemptive narrative — clients whose stories are contaminated need a different narrative, not just different content.
**Evidence level:** Supported (narrative identity has solid empirical base; specific claims about redemptive narrative are emerging)
**Scope:** Narrative as identity construction. Does not provide session methodology.
**Shortcomings:** McAdams works from a secular narrative psychology framework. His redemptive narrative concept is useful but the source of redemption differs: he locates it in the person's resilience and meaning-making; this practice locates it in God's covenantal action in Christ. Import the narrative framework and contamination/redemption distinction; reground the source of redemption theologically.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Full Model Reference; story-reading in session

#### Rachel Yehuda
**Primary works:** Epigenetic transmission research (multiple studies, 1990s–present); Holocaust survivor descendant studies
**Contribution:** Empirical evidence for epigenetic transmission of trauma responses — descendants of trauma survivors show altered cortisol profiles and stress-response gene modification. Provides scientific grounding for why generational patterns are not merely behavioral or narrative but may be physiologically transmitted.
**Evidence level:** Supported (compelling findings; replication ongoing; field developing)
**Scope:** Biological transmission mechanism of intergenerational trauma. Does not address intervention.
**Shortcomings:** Epigenetic transmission research is a developing field with some contested findings. Import as supporting evidence for the intergenerational layer; do not overstate the certainty of specific mechanisms.
**Informs:** Covenant Identity — Author-Framework Mapping; intergenerational pattern work

#### Murray Bowen

## Domain 6 — Forgiveness and Relational Repair

---

#### Robert Enright
**Primary works:** *Forgiveness Is a Choice* (2001); *Moving Forward* (2015)
**Contribution:** The empirically strongest forgiveness framework. Forgiveness defined as "willingness to abandon one's right to resentment, negative judgment, and indifferent behavior toward one who unjustly hurt us." The four-phase process (Uncovering → Decision → Work → Deepening) as the structured protocol. Self-forgiveness as a distinct mechanism requiring a different process from other-forgiveness.
**Evidence level:** Established (the strongest empirical base among forgiveness researchers)
**Scope:** Forgiveness as a structured process. Does not address theological framework beyond surface integration.
**Shortcomings:** Enright's framework is primarily psychological — the theological grounding (forgiveness as an act of grace, rooted in having been forgiven, not as a therapeutic technique) is supplied here by the covenant framework. Import the structure and empirical evidence; ground the motivation theologically.
**Informs:** Forgiveness Coaching — Enright & Worthington

---

#### Everett Worthington
**Primary works:** *Forgiving and Reconciling* (2003); REACH Model research program
**Contribution:** The REACH Model (Recall → Empathize → Altruistic Gift → Commit → Hold) as a complementary forgiveness protocol. Decisive distinction between decisional forgiveness (a choice to release the debt) and emotional forgiveness (the full resolution of resentment and its replacement with positive emotion). Empathy as the primary active mechanism in emotional forgiveness. Clearest treatment of the forgiveness/reconciliation distinction — forgiveness is unilateral; reconciliation requires mutual change.
**Evidence level:** Established (extensive empirical research program)
**Scope:** Forgiveness process; decisional vs. emotional forgiveness; reconciliation distinction. Does not provide theological framework.
**Shortcomings:** Same import note as Enright. Worthington is a Christian who integrates faith language, but the theological grounding is pastoral rather than systematic. The reconciliation distinction is particularly important for coaching: forgiveness can be coached; reconciliation may require pastoral or clinical support.
**Informs:** Forgiveness Coaching — Enright & Worthington
