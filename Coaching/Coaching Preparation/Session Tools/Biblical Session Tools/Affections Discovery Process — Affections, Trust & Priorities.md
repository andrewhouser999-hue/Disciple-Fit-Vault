## Purpose

This process surfaces what a client is prioritizing, trusting, and relying on — the actual objects of their affections — as the first diagnostic layer beneath their presenting story. Affections reveal functional identity: who a person lives from, not just what they believe. Trust is not separate from affections; it is integral to them. What a person depends on for security, significance, or identity is what they functionally worship.

This is not a discrete session event. It is a continuous diagnostic stance — initiated in the intake, opened in the discovery call, and deepened across early sessions. The coach holds this frame throughout the engagement.

This process is internal coaching practice. It does not appear in external marketing or client-facing descriptions of the model.

## Theological Foundation

**Matthew 6:21** — "Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also." Affections follow investment. What a person gives time, energy, emotional weight, and attention to reveals what they actually treasure — regardless of what they confess.

**Matthew 22:37** — "Love the Lord your God with heart, soul, mind, and strength." The whole person is ordered around what it loves. Heart = affections and desires. Soul = life and being. Mind = thought and understanding. Strength = will and action. Displacement of God in any of these is a diagnostic signal.

**Proverbs 4:23** — "Guard your heart, for from it flow the springs of life." The heart — the seat of affections, trust, and priorities — is the source from which life flows. Change at the affections level produces change at the life level.

**Matthew 6:33** — "But seek first the kingdom of God and his righteousness, and all these things will be added to you." Anxious pursuit of provision functions as evidence of a competing trust object (Matt 6:24, "you cannot serve God and money"). What a person seeks first — not what they say they value — names their functional god in the provision/security domain. Diagnostic only when anxiety is disproportionate to actual circumstance; a client facing genuine material scarcity is not exhibiting disordered affections but a legitimate threat, and this text is not a substitute for acknowledging that.

**Trust as worship:** What a person trusts for security, significance, and identity is their functional god. This is a diagnostic frame, not an accusation. The coach does not lead with "you are worshiping X." The coach holds the lens and uses it to locate the false identity the affections are maintaining.

## Model Position

Affections discovery is the first layer of the diagnostic sequence:

> **Affections Discovery → False Identity Named → Covenant Truth Spoken → Stage 4a (CBT tools, if cognitive distortion blocks reception) / Stage 4b (Relational-Attentive Response, if implicit memory blocks reception)**

The affections reveal what the client trusts. That trust reveals what they believe they need to be okay. That belief reveals the identity they are living from. Once named, covenant truth can be brought against it directly. Full stage-by-stage entry and transition criteria: [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]].

## The Five Layers — Coach-Facing

### Layer 1 — Surface Inquiry
*What does the client say they want and value?*

This is the presenting layer. The coach is listening, not leading. Stated values and priorities are the starting point — rarely the diagnostic target. Note what is emphasized, minimized, and absent.

**Listen for:** Ratio of external vs. internal attribution. Whether stated priorities match the behaviors described. Absence of God in priority language.

---

### Layer 2 — Pattern Surfacing
*What does the client's actual life — time, energy, attention, anxiety — reflect they value?*

This is where the gap between stated and functional values begins to appear. The client is usually unaware of it. The coach surfaces it through behavioral questions, not confrontation.

**Listen for:** What generates the most anxiety when threatened. What the client emotionally orbits. Where perfectionism or avoidance is concentrated. What they've sacrificed most consistently.

---

### Layer 3 — Dependency Probe (Trust)
*What is the client trusting and relying on for security, significance, or identity?*

Trust is the core of this layer. The coach follows the dependency chain: what does this person need in order to feel okay? The CBT laddering technique is coach-safe here — follow the belief chain downward: *"And if that were true, what would that mean about you?"* Repeat until the core assumption surfaces.

**Listen for:** Conditional worth language ("I'm okay when... / I'm not okay when..."). Sources of security that don't include God. What the client cannot imagine losing. Statements that reveal the structure of their functional safety.

---

### Layer 4 — God-Exclusion Map
*Where is God absent from what the client is depending on?*
*(Coach-internal frame — may or may not be stated directly to client)*

The coach locates where the client's trust structure excludes or functionally displaces God. This may surface as a direct question ("Where is God in this picture?") or remain internal as the coach's organizing diagnostic lens. Follow the client's lead on when to name it aloud.

**Listen for:** Absence of God in explanations of what helps, provides relief, or gives meaning. Self-sufficiency language. God described functionally as distant, demanding, or irrelevant to the area of stuckness.

> **Full Layer 4 reference:** For the eight-domain framework (security, significance, belonging, control, comfort, purpose, identity, future), complete in-session protocol, question bank, and diagnostic signals, see [[Covenant Identity — God-Exclusion Map]].

---

### Layer 5 — Identity Revelation
*What false identity do the affections reveal?*

This is the handoff point. Once the pattern of affections, trust, and dependencies is clear, the coach names the functional identity they reveal — specifically, not generally.

Examples:
- "I am only safe when I am accepted."
- "I am only significant when I am performing at a high level."
- "I need to be in control in order to feel safe."
- "I am only secure when I've secured it myself."

**Listen for:** Behavioral consistency across contexts (same dependency in multiple domains). The client naming their own pattern without the identity framing yet. Core-belief language surfacing spontaneously.

**Transition:** When the coach can name the false identity specifically, move to Covenant Truth — Stage 3 of the Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic.

**Secondary diagnostic layer:** Once the affections picture from Layers 1–5 is specific, [[Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool]] can deepen the false-identity naming further by identifying which of the three character wound types (Warrior, Hermit, False Noble) is operating underneath it. Deploy after this process, not before.

## Question Bank by Layer

Select 2–3 questions per layer based on what the client has surfaced. These are tools, not a sequence. The client's responses determine direction.

### Layer 1 — Surface Inquiry
- "What matters most to you right now?"
- "What are you most hoping changes?"
- "What would feel like winning in your life at this point?"
- "If you could describe what thriving looks like for you — not just okay, but thriving — what comes to mind?"

### Layer 2 — Pattern Surfacing
- "If I watched how you spent your time and energy this week — not what you intended, but what actually happened — what would I conclude you care most about?"
- "What do you find yourself thinking about most when you're not focused on anything in particular?"
- "What's the thing you find hardest to let go of?"
- "Where does most of your emotional energy go in a typical week?"
- "What would feel most threatening to lose right now — not just inconvenient, but actually threatening?"

### Layer 3 — Dependency Probe (Trust)
- "What would need to be true for you to feel okay — really okay — about yourself?"
- "What do you feel like you can't do without?"
- "When you're anxious or struggling, who or what do you reach for first?"
- "What gives you the most relief when things feel unstable?"
- "What do you need from [person / outcome / situation] to feel settled about yourself?"
- "Is there something you believe you have to hold onto — or hold together — in order for things to be okay?"
- *(Laddering — follow any answer downward):* "And if that were true, what would that mean about you?"

### Layer 4 — God-Exclusion Map
*(Use selectively — hold as internal frame first; surface when it can land without defensiveness)*
- "Where is God in this picture you're describing?"
- "What would trusting God here look like — specifically, not in general?"
- "Is there any part of this area of your life where you find yourself reaching for something other than God first? What is it?"

> **Scriptural mirror (provision/security — coach-internal frame, not necessarily verbalized):** Matt 6:25-33 functions as an internal lens, not a script: anxious seeking, without reference to a Father who already knows the need. Hold silently rather than paraphrasing aloud — verbalized mid-session, it risks landing as correction rather than invitation. Use only when Layer 3-4 answers cluster in provision/security, and only after ruling out genuine material scarcity (see Theological Foundation). If it produces identity language, move to Layer 5.

*Full question bank by domain, naming moves, and diagnostic signals: [[Covenant Identity — God-Exclusion Map]]*

### Layer 5 — Identity Revelation
- "What does this pattern say you believe about yourself?"
- "What do you believe you need to be okay — at the deepest level?"
- "Who does your life say you are when no one's watching?"
- *(Reflection):* "What I'm hearing is that a lot of what you're describing circles around needing [X] to feel okay. Does that land?"
- "Is there a belief about yourself underneath all of this — something you haven't quite said out loud yet?"

## Diagnostic Signals — What to Listen For

| Signal | What It Suggests |
|---|---|
| Conditional worth statements ("I'm okay when...") | Performance or approval dependency — significance tied to output or relational standing |
| God described as distant or functionally absent in hard times | Anxious or avoidant God-attachment; God-representation divergent from declared theology |
| Anxiety concentrated in one domain (approval, control, provision, achievement) | Locates the functional god — the object being trusted for security |
| Can state theological identity but can't feel it as operative | Covenant identity gap is live; indicative/imperative split present |
| Self-sufficiency or control language | Control/provision functional identity — "I must manage this myself" |
| Worth tied to what others think or need | Approval or belonging functional identity |
| Avoidance of a specific domain or conversation | Secondary gain or competing commitment maintaining the false identity |
| Same dependency appearing across multiple life domains | Structural false identity, not situational — root-level work indicated |

## Client-Facing: Reflection Component

*Included in Tier 2 intake (Covenant Identity Intake). May also be used as in-session reflection prompts when appropriate. Written in accessible language — no model vocabulary required.*

**Instructions (as given to client):**
> Answer honestly. There are no right answers. These questions are designed to help you know yourself better — and to help me know where you actually are before we work together.

---

1. What are you spending the most time and energy on right now?

2. What would need to change for you to feel like your life is genuinely going well?

3. When you're anxious, overwhelmed, or struggling — who or what do you turn to first?

4. What are you most afraid of losing?

5. Where do you feel the most pressure to perform, prove yourself, or meet a standard?

6. What do you feel like you depend on most to feel okay about yourself?

7. If you had to simplify your life significantly, what would you give up last?

---

### Coach Annotation Guide

| Q | What to Read For |
|---|---|
| 1 | Stated vs. functional priority gap — does what they describe match the life they've described elsewhere? |
| 2 | Dependency structure — what condition must be met for life to work; names the functional need |
| 3 | Trust object — the first reach reveals what they're actually relying on; absence of God is diagnostic |
| 4 | What they're holding most tightly — the thing they can't lose without losing themselves |
| 5 | Performance or shame structure — where contingent worth is operating |
| 6 | Core dependency probe — what the functional god is; the most direct affections question |
| 7 | Values clarification under scarcity — what survives the cut is what actually matters |

## Integration Notes

**Intake (Tier 2):** Client-facing reflection questions (above) are added to the Covenant Identity Intake. Coach reviews responses using the annotation guide before Session 1 and before the discovery call review.

**Discovery Call:** Affections discovery is initiated in Movement 3 (Identity Gap Indicators) and threaded through Movement 2 (Behavioral Staging) of the Discovery Call Guide. The coach holds the affections diagnostic lens throughout the entire call and notes the emerging picture in the post-call protocol.

**In-Session Tool:** [[Covenant Identity — Affections Discovery — In-Session Protocol]] adapts this document's question bank and annotation framework for structured in-session use, with session-specific pacing, stay/move signals, and somatic observation layered in. This document remains the theological and theoretical foundation that tool draws from.

**Alternative narrative entry:** If a client responds better to a story frame than direct affections questions, [[Covenant Identity — Prodigal Son Diagnostic Narrative Framework]] offers a client-facing self-locating alternative (Far Country / Returning Son / Older Brother) that transitions into this same Stage 1 work.

**Receptivity precondition:** [[Covenant Identity — Isaiah 66 2b — Practitioner Exegetical Reference]] grounds the posture (humble, contrite, trembling at the word) that determines whether affections work will actually surface anything — useful when a client is intellectually engaged with the questions above but not softening.

**Periodic re-assessment:** [[Covenant Identity — Arc Location Diagnostic]] gives a fillable self-assessment for locating current position on the arc, derived from the Transition Logic — useful alongside this continuous diagnostic stance at intervals through the engagement, not only at intake.

**Transition Logic:** See *Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic* for how affections discovery hands off to false identity naming, covenant truth, and Stage 4a/4b deployment.
