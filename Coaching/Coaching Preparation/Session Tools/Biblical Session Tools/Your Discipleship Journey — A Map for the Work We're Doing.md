# Your Discipleship Journey — A Map for the Work We're Doing

## Why a Map?

Most Christians know they're supposed to grow. What's usually missing isn't desire — it's direction. You've probably had seasons of trying harder, reading more, praying better, only to find yourself back in the same place wondering what went wrong.

That's rarely a character problem. It's a structure problem. Growth has an order, and when you don't know the order, real effort gets wasted on the wrong things at the wrong time.

What follows is the map. It won't tell you everything about where you're going — only God can do that. But it will tell you how the journey works, where you are right now, and what the next step actually is.

---

## Three Layers of the Journey

Every person's walk with God moves through three layers — not once, but repeatedly, at greater depth each time.

---

### Layer 1: First Things
*What your life is organized around*

There are some things God says come first. Not first as in "do these before breakfast" — first as in "everything else in your life depends on getting this right."

Jesus was direct about it: *"Love the Lord your God with all your heart, soul, mind, and strength"* — and *"Love your neighbor as yourself."* He called these the greatest commandments. Everything else hangs on them.

If your life isn't organized around these things, it doesn't matter how disciplined you are or how much you know. You're building on the wrong foundation.

Some of the other First Things Jesus named:
- Seek first the kingdom of God — Matthew 6:33
- Deny yourself, take up your cross, follow me — Matthew 16:24
- Repent and believe the gospel — Mark 1:15
- Abide in me — John 15:4

**The question this layer keeps asking:** *What is your life actually organized around?*

---

### Layer 2: Daily Rhythms
*How you actually live it out*

Knowing what comes first isn't the same as living from it. This is where first things become daily realities.

These are the practices and postures that keep you connected to God and shaped by him — things like:

- Praying — not as a ritual but as real conversation
- Reading and sitting with Scripture
- Living in honest community with other believers
- Walking in step with the Holy Spirit
- Forgiving — not once but as a way of life
- Serving others at cost to yourself
- Sitting in silence long enough to actually hear

These aren't performance items on a checklist. They're the means God uses to form you. You don't do them to earn anything. You do them because they're how a branch stays connected to the vine — and a branch that stays connected bears fruit without straining for it.

**The question this layer keeps asking:** *What rhythms are actually shaping you right now?*

---

### Layer 3: Honest Questions
*Where are you in the process?*

Growth that can't be assessed can't be guided. This layer is about taking honest stock — not to condemn yourself, but to locate yourself.

The Bible gives us some powerful mirrors:

- **The Parable of the Soils** — What kind of ground is the Word landing on in your life? What's blocking it from taking root?
- **The Beatitudes** — Where are you on the formation arc Jesus describes? Poverty of spirit? Mourning? Hungering and thirsting?
- **The Fruit of the Spirit** — Love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control. What is your life actually producing?
- **The Prodigal Son** — Are you in the far country, on your way home, or standing outside resenting those who got in?
- **The Rich Young Ruler question** — What is the one thing you still cannot surrender?

None of these are weapons. They're compasses. They tell you where you are so you can figure out what's next.

**The question this layer keeps asking:** *Where am I, honestly — and what does that reveal?*

---

## How the Layers Work Together

The layers don't replace each other — they build on each other, and you return to all three your entire life.

You never graduate past First Things. The most mature disciples are the most deeply rooted in the basics — they haven't moved on from the Greatest Commandment, they've moved deeper into it.

Daily rhythms are cumulative. You don't swap out prayer for serving others — you add to what's already there. The foundation stays; the building grows.

The honest questions deepen over time. The same mirror will show you something different at year five than it did at year one. The goal isn't to find new questions — it's to go further into the ones that matter.

---

## The Four Stages of the Journey

The journey moves through four broad stages. These aren't rigid boxes — they're markers that help you know roughly where you are and what the work of this season looks like.

**Starting Out**
You're finding the foundation. Repentance, faith, the Greatest Commandment as the organizing center of your life. You're beginning the basic rhythms: prayer, Scripture, community. You're figuring out what kind of soil you are — what's blocking the Word from really landing.

**Building the Foundation**
You're learning what it actually means to seek God first — not just in principle but in daily choices. The cost of following Jesus is becoming real, not theoretical. Denying yourself isn't a concept anymore; it's showing up in your actual life. The Beatitudes are starting to describe your experience.

**Growing**
You're bearing fruit. The hard things — suffering, forgiveness, serving at real cost — are forming you rather than just testing you. The question shifts from *Am I growing?* to *Who am I becoming?* You're living from your identity in Christ more than you're striving toward it.

**Maturing**
You're carrying others. Making disciples isn't something you volunteer for — it's how you live. You know how God has wired you and you're using it. The deepest question — *What am I still holding back from God?* — can finally be asked honestly and sat with.

## What This Means for Our Sessions

In our coaching sessions together, we'll move through a simple sequence:

1. **Orient** — What does God say this situation is really about? What is the first-things view of what you're facing?
2. **Locate** — Where are you honestly right now? What does a clear-eyed look reveal?
3. **Equip** — What is the one practice or posture that, if you actually did it this week, would move the needle?
4. **Return** — Why does this matter? How does it connect back to what your life is for?

The goal is never behavior management. It is formation — becoming who God says you already are, from the inside out, one honest conversation at a time.
