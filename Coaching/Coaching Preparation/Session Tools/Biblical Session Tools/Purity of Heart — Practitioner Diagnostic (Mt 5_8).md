## Purpose

This is a practitioner reference for in-session diagnostic use when a client's inner integrity — whether their professed values and actual operative values run on one track or two — is a live question. Andrew uses it during session to select and sequence diagnostic probes. It is not a scoring rubric and is not shown to clients. It belongs in the diagnostic layer of the coaching mechanism: after the relationship has created a safe container, before covenant truth application.

**Read "Relationship to Psalm 24," below, before using this alongside late-stage declaration work.** This tool and [[Covenant Identity — Psalm 24 Redemptive-Historical Telos Frame]] both engage "pure heart" language but do different jobs — conflating them risks reopening a question that document says must never sit unanswered in the room.

*Part of a series covering all nine Beatitudes (Mt 5:3–12) — see [[_Beatitude Diagnostics — Quick-Select Index]] for the presenting-signal lookup and cross-reference map across all eight diagnostics.*

## Governing Reframe

**This tool diagnoses where a client's inner life runs on two tracks — not whether they're sincere enough.**

James 4:8 names purity's precise opposite: not vague impurity, but being *dipsychos* — double-souled, double-minded. A divided inner life, professed values running separately from what's actually operative. This gives a sharp diagnostic target: not "is this person good," but "is there a gap between what they say governs them and what actually does."

Purity of heart is something God creates (Psalm 51:10 — David's prayer after his worst failure: "*create* in me a clean heart," the same verb as Genesis 1:1), not something self-manufactured through more careful effort. This produces an ironic trap specific to this beatitude, worth naming directly: **performing sincerity to prove you aren't duplicitous is itself a form of duplicity** — a managed presentation of unity rather than the real thing. Do not use this tool to instruct a client to "just be more genuine" or "stop being two-faced." That's willpower applied to authenticity, which cannot produce what only the Spirit creates, and it recruits the very mechanism (image management) this beatitude's counterfeit runs on.

## Relationship to Psalm 24

Both this document and the Psalm 24 Redemptive-Historical Telos Frame engage "clean hands, and a pure heart" — but they answer different questions, at different points in the engagement, for different purposes. Hold this distinction clearly; do not let the two blend in a single client's case.

**This document — sanctification question, ongoing, coach-internal:** Having already been given standing before God by grace, is this already-justified client's inner life becoming more integrated, or is there a gap between profession and operation that needs surfacing? This is diagnostic work for the coach's own tracking, usable across the engagement, not a question ever put to the client as a qualification test.

**The Psalm 24 document — justification question, late-stage, explicitly declarative:** How does someone who cannot meet the "clean hands, pure heart" standard get any standing before God at all? Answered once, decisively, by Christ's righteousness received as gift — and that document is explicit that this question must never be left in the room unanswered.

**The rule this creates:** if this diagnostic's material surfaces in a session — a client's professed values gapped against their operative ones — that is sanctification-stage material to name, explore, and bring covenant truth to (the ordinary diagnostic-to-Stage-3 movement). It is never, under any circumstance, to be treated as reopening the "do you qualify to ascend" question. That question is closed. This tool works entirely downstream of it being closed.

## Entry Signal

Deploy when one or more of the following appears in session:

- A client's stated values and actual choices show a recurring, specific gap the client hasn't named themselves
- The client presents as unusually composed, sincere-sounding, or "put together" in a way that doesn't match what surfaces elsewhere in their story
- Affections or Character Wound work has surfaced a managed public self that differs from what the client discloses privately
- The client raises the question themselves — "I don't know if I'm actually being honest with myself" — or something close to it

## Contraindication

**Do not treat trauma-driven internal fragmentation as moral double-mindedness.** A client with dissociative features, complex trauma, or a genuinely "parts"-based internal experience (see [[IFS — Internal Family Systems Framework]]) may describe feeling divided or having conflicting internal voices for clinical, not moral, reasons. Probing this as duplicity risks pathologizing a trauma adaptation. If the "division" being described sounds like distinct internal parts with their own memories, ages, or protective functions — rather than a gap between stated values and actual behavior — this is IFS/clinical territory, not this tool's target. Distinguish carefully before proceeding.

**Do not use this to manufacture scrupulosity.** No one has a perfectly undivided heart at every moment; ordinary human complexity and ambivalence are not the target. This tool is for a recurring, namable pattern, not a search for any trace of inconsistency.

## Diagnostic Probes

Double-mindedness, like meekness and mercy, resists direct self-report. Probes surface a specific, concrete gap rather than asking abstractly whether someone is sincere.

---

### Layer 1 — The Named Gap (Biblical Counseling diagnostic)

#### The Value-Choice Question
*Source: Matthew 15:18-19 — Jesus locates defilement in the heart, naming specific content (evil thoughts, deceit, slander) rather than leaving "impurity" abstract.*

**Session-ready:** "Name something you'd say genuinely matters to you — a value you'd defend if someone challenged it. Now think of a recent, specific choice or reaction of yours. Does that choice actually reflect that value, or was something else actually driving it in that moment?"

**Why this works:** Asking for a specific instance, not a self-assessment, surfaces the gap directly rather than asking the client to grade their own sincerity — which a genuinely divided heart is poorly positioned to do accurately.

**Listen for:** A client who can locate a real gap without defensiveness, and who seems more curious than ashamed about it, is showing exactly the kind of self-access this beatitude describes as growing. A client who can't locate any gap at all, ever, on request, may be showing the False Noble pattern (see Layer 2) rather than genuine integration.

---

### Layer 2 — Counterfeit Mapping (Biblical Counseling / Spiritual Formation diagnostic)

Duplicity has three shapes, not one — matching the pattern already established for meekness and mercy in this series.

| Counterfeit | Wound type | What it looks like | What's actually driving it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Righteous-looking performance | Warrior | Zealous, morally assertive, visibly "on the right side" | Self-vindication or control, not genuine devotion — impure motive under correct-looking behavior |
| Untested purity | Hermit | Appears calm, non-conflicted, "above" the mess | Avoidance — withdrawal keeps the divided parts of the self from ever being tested or revealed |
| Managed image | False Noble | Composed, sincere-sounding, consistent public presentation | Active gap-management between the presented self and the actual inner reality — the direct James 4:8 pattern |

**Why this works:** Only False Noble is a direct verbal/behavioral match for "double-minded." The other two are easy to miss precisely because they don't look divided — Warrior's zeal looks like devotion, Hermit's calm looks like integration. Both require probing the *motive* or *the untested quality* of the calm, not just its presence.

**Listen for:** With Warrior-pattern presentations, ask what the moral stance is actually protecting or securing for the client. With Hermit-pattern presentations, notice how much of the "calm" depends on staying out of situations that would actually test it.

## What to Do with What Surfaces

**A specific, namable gap surfaces and the client engages it without defensiveness** — this is the beatitude's own diagnostic work happening in real time; move toward Affections Discovery or covenant truth application on the specific gap named, not a general integrity overhaul.

**Warrior-pattern impure motive surfaces** — the moral performance is protecting something (being right, being in control, being seen as righteous). Route toward Affections Discovery to locate it.

**Hermit-pattern untested calm surfaces** — name gently that the calm hasn't been tested, not that it's false; this is Character Wound territory, approached carefully.

**False Noble-pattern managed image surfaces** — this is the closest match to James 4:8's double-mindedness directly. For deeper, structured work on the confessed-vs-functional gap specifically, see [[Covenant Identity — Functional vs. Confessional Theology — 20 Diagnostic Contrasts]] — this tool identifies that the gap exists; that document provides the fuller 20-contrast diagnostic framework for working it.

**Trauma-driven fragmentation surfaces instead of moral duplicity** — stop this tool's line of inquiry. See Contraindication.

## What This Tool Is Not

- Not a scoring rubric. There is no quota of detected division that constitutes sufficient purity.
- Not a re-opening of the qualification question Psalm 24 answers definitively. See Relationship to Psalm 24, above.
- Not a tool for trauma-driven internal fragmentation or dissociative presentations. See Contraindication.

## Purity of Heart in Life Experience

### What It Is

An inner life running on one track instead of two — where what a person says actually matters to them is, in practice, what's actually driving their choices, especially when no one is checking. It's recognized less by the absence of any inconsistency (which no one has) and more by whether a person can locate and own their own gaps without needing to manage how the moment looks.

### Not to Be Mistaken For

- **Naivety or lack of self-awareness** — genuine purity of heart includes accurate self-perception, not an inability to see one's own contradictions.
- **Moral rigidity or scrupulosity** — a search for any trace of inconsistency as evidence of failure; see Contraindication, above.
- **Composure or even temperament** — a naturally calm or steady personality is not the same as an integrated inner life; see Layer 2's Hermit-pattern mapping.
- **Zealous or visibly moral behavior** — outward righteousness can be motivated by self-vindication or control rather than genuine devotion; see Layer 2's Warrior-pattern mapping.
- **Dissociative or trauma-driven internal fragmentation** — a clinical presentation, not a moral one; see Contraindication.
