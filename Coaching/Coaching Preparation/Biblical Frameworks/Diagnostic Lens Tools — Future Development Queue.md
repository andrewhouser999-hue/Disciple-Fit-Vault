## Purpose

The governing document's [[1-Biblical Framework Governing Order — Organizing Philosophy & First Things#C- Diagnostic Lenses — Catalog (Top 15)|C- Diagnostic Lenses catalog]] lists fifteen (now sixteen) diagnostic lenses for reading a client's formation, most of which are not yet built as deployable practitioner tools. This document records the queue, the build order, the design pattern to follow, and the existing vault documents that must be checked before building — so a future build session does not start from zero or duplicate work already in the vault.

## The Queue

1. **Love-in-Action diagnostic** (catalog #16, new addition) — an alternative to the Five Love Languages, oriented around *giving* love rather than a preferred way to *receive* it. Diagnoses how well a client is actually practicing neighbor-love in observable behavior (Matthew 22:39; 1 Corinthians 13:4–7; 1 John 3:18 — "let us not love in word or talk but in deed and in truth"), paired with a follow-up plan that targets the specific gap areas the diagnostic surfaces.
2. **Parable of the Soils diagnostic test** (catalog #3) — a receptivity instrument answering "what is currently blocking the Word from taking root," distinct from the existing flagship framework document (see Vault-Check Flags below).
3. **Prodigal / Elder Brother diagnostic test** (catalog #4) — locates a client relative to the Father: far country, returning, or resentful-at-home (elder brother).
4. **Remaining catalog items**, in no fixed order, to be sequenced when the queue above is complete: Love Chapter diagnostic (#5), Sermon on the Mount interior-life diagnostic (#6), Greatest Commandment self-audit (#7), Old Man/New Man identity diagnostic (#8), Counterfeit Kingdom diagnostic (#9), Pride vs. Humility diagnostic (#10), Vine/Fruit test (#11), Rich Young Ruler test (#12), Spiritual gifts inventory (#13), Holistic formation diagnostic (#15).

**Already built, not part of this queue:** the Beatitudes (#1 — nine practitioner diagnostics complete, see [[project_beatitude_diagnostics|Beatitude Practitioner Diagnostics]] memory) and Comprehensive Discipleship Markers (#14 — see `_Index/Comprehensive Discipleship Markers.md`). Fruit of the Spirit (#2) is catalogued but not yet queued.

## Design Pattern to Follow

The nine Beatitude Practitioner Diagnostics (`Session Tools/Biblical Session Tools/`) are the proven template for this kind of build. Each one carries:

- An entry signal (when to deploy this diagnostic in a live session)
- A governing reframe (the correct target, stated against the most common misreading)
- Mechanism-layer probes tied to specific texts, not generic self-report questions
- Session-ready question forms, phrased for direct use in conversation
- A contraindication section — including, where the lens touches anything that could be confused with tolerating real harm (abuse, unsafe relationships), a lead safety contraindication checked *before* probes deploy, not a standard closing paragraph
- Response scaffolding and handoff logic to the next tool

**Process steps that produced this template and should be repeated:** run `/bible` for the text(s) first, then `/scrutinize` the plan before building, then build. Before any of these four builds starts, vault-check the specific text and theme (see below) — the Beatitude series caught two real duplication risks this way (Mt 5:3 vs. the CIC Isaiah 66:2b document; Mt 5:8 vs. the Psalm 24 document) and one required a scope change (companion document instead of full parallel build).

**Where these should live when built:** `Session Tools/Biblical Session Tools/`, following the `[Concept] — Practitioner Diagnostic ([Reference]).md` naming convention already established by the Beatitude series.

## Vault-Check Flags — Read Before Building

- **Love-in-Action diagnostic vs. [[C-Greatest Commandment — Diagnostic Overlay]]:** the existing overlay already probes neighbor-love in its Relational Domain (Question 5) and treats relational and interior love as co-constitutive (the 1 John circularity note). The new diagnostic must be clearly differentiated as an outward-practice measurement instrument with a structured follow-up growth plan — not a restatement of Question 5. Read the existing overlay in full before scoping this build; it may need to become a companion to the overlay rather than a standalone document, per the Mt 5:3 pattern.
- **Parable of the Soils diagnostic test vs. [[A-Parable of the Soils — Kingdom Coaching Deep Framework]]:** this is the flagship A-tier document integrating somatic, CBT, schema, psychodynamic, ACT, narrative, attachment, and spiritual-warfare frameworks. Check whether a receptivity diagnostic instrument already lives inside it before building a new freestanding one — if it does, the queue item may be "extract and format as a standalone practitioner tool" rather than "build from scratch."
- **Prodigal / Elder Brother diagnostic test:** no existing vault document found on this text at time of flagging (2026-07-19) — confirm this is still true before building, since the vault has grown since this note was written.
