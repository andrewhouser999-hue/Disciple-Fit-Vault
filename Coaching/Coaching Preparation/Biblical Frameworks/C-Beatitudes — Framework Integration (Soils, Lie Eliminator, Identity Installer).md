# Beatitudes — Framework Integration
*How the Beatitudes map onto and unify the Soils Framework, Lie Eliminator, and Identity Installer*

---

## The Core Insight

These four frameworks are not parallel systems competing for space. They are layers of a single system, each doing a distinct job:

| Framework | Function | Question It Answers |
|---|---|---|
| **Soils** | Diagnoses the ground | What condition is the client in? |
| **Lie Eliminator** | Defends against attack | What false belief is blocking movement? |
| **Identity Installer** | Constructs the new self | Who is the client becoming? |
| **Beatitudes** | Formation sequence | In what direction and order does transformation move? |

**The Beatitudes are the content that runs through all three existing frameworks.** The Soils tell you where the client is. The Lie Eliminator tells you what is blocking them. The Identity Installer tells you what to build. The Beatitudes tell you the direction and sequence of the whole movement.

## Session Sequence Distillation — The Three Essential Moves

*Each Beatitude has a full 6–9 step session sequence in the B-file. This distills each to the three moves that do the most work in most presentations. Use as an in-session reference. Return to the full sequence in the B-file when a presentation requires more precision.*

---

**B1 — Poverty of Spirit**
1. **Surface the self-sufficiency narrative** (MI) — Elicit without confrontation. *"What does it look like for you to have it together spiritually? What does managing your own standing before God cost you?"*
2. **Reframe the posture** (CBT) — Accurate self-assessment vs. defeat. Empty-handed ≠ worthless. *"Jesus doesn't say blessed are the spiritually strong. He says blessed are the ones who know they have nothing. That's not the bottom — that's the entry point."*
3. **Install the identity and activate** (Identity + SDT) — *"The kingdom belongs to the empty-handed — now, not when you earn it. What do you want to do from that place — not to earn anything, but because you're already accepted?"*

---

**B2 — Mourning**
1. **Assess safety and surface the block** (CFT + Self-Talk) — Can this client grieve without collapsing? If markedly depleted or clinical presentation present — refer before proceeding. If safe: *"What internal rule is telling you not to feel this? Where did that rule come from?"*
2. **Externalize the anti-grief story and create space** (Narrative Therapy + ACT) — *"Who taught you that grief was spiritually dangerous? What would it look like to make room for this — not to fix it, not to fight it, just to be honest about it in God's presence?"*
3. **Anchor the promise** (Theological) — *"Jesus wept at Lazarus's tomb knowing he was about to raise him. He wasn't confused. He was honest. The comfort he promises comes to the one who mourns — not to the one who manages grief away."*

---

**B3 — Meekness**
1. **Identify the pattern and find the core belief** (CBT) — Where does the client over-assert, control, or defend? *"Walk me through what you were thinking right before that response. What did you tell yourself was at stake? What would have happened if you had yielded?"*
2. **Build the soothing system** (CFT) — The threat system driving aggression cannot be argued down — it must be settled. *"What would it feel like to be in that same situation and not feel threatened? What would have to be true about you for that to be possible?"*
3. **Install the identity** (Identity + Theological) — *"Moses confronted Pharaoh. Jesus cleared the temple. Both are called meek. Meekness isn't the absence of strength — it's strength that doesn't need to prove itself. The inheritance isn't seized. It's given to the one who stops fighting for it."*

---

**B4 — Hunger and Thirst for Righteousness**
1. **Surface the desire and diagnose motivation structure** (SDT + Approach/Avoidance) — *"What do you actually want — not what you should want? Are you moving toward something or away from failure? Is there a difference?"*
2. **Find what extinguished the hunger OR name the competing desire** (MI + WOOP) — If desire is flat: *"You've wanted this before. What happened to it? When did it go quiet?"* If redirected: *"What wins the daily contest against righteousness — specifically, not generally?"*
3. **Anchor the promise and install identity** (Identity + Theological) — *"This desire was created by the same one who promises to fill it — completely. Who are you as someone who hungers? Not what do you produce — who are you?"*

---

**B5 — Mercy**
1. **Assess the resource** (CFT) — *"When you fail — what does your internal response look like? The mercy you can give others maxes out at the mercy you can give yourself. Let's work on the source before the output."* If compassion fatigue is present — replenish before asking for more output.
2. **Surface the distortion and resolve ambivalence** (CBT + MI) — *"What story are you telling yourself about this person's motives?"* Then: *"Part of you knows mercy is the right move. Another part feels like extending it would be wrong or dangerous. Tell me about both."* Don't argue for mercy — elicit it.
3. **Anchor in received mercy** (Theological) — *"You're right that they don't deserve it. That's the definition. The question is whether you've received any mercy you didn't deserve. Because that's what you're being asked to pass on."*

---

**B6 — Purity of Heart**
1. **Name both masters** (CBT + ACT) — Surface the competing commitment explicitly and specifically. *"You've named two things that both feel important. When they conflict — which one actually wins? Is the one that wins the one you most want to be defined by?"*
2. **Map the cost and find the hidden assumption** (CBT / Immunity to Change) — *"Where is the double-mindedness producing instability in your life? What do you believe would happen if you fully released the competing thing? What are you afraid would go wrong?"*
3. **Anchor the promise** (ACT + Theological) — *"The undivided heart sees God — not as a reward for being good, as a perceptual consequence of being whole. This week: when the competing master appears — don't fight it, don't follow it. Name it. Choose the primary one. One vote."*

---

**B7 — Peacemaking**
1. **Diagnose the posture and surface the blocking belief** (CBT) — *"Are you keeping peace or making peace? What belief is blocking you from engaging — hopelessness, mind-reading, all-or-nothing standard? What would partial restoration look like — not perfect peace, but movement?"*
2. **Defuse from the enemy narrative and clarify shalom** (ACT + Values) — *"You're having the thought 'they don't want peace.' Can you hold it loosely enough to test it? What would flourishing look like for everyone in this situation — not for you to win, but for everyone to flourish?"*
3. **Anchor the identity** (Theological) — *"The peacemaker carries the clearest family resemblance to God of any posture in this list. This is what God looks like in a relationship. Peacemakers don't wait for the other person to deserve peace. They move first."*

---

**B8 — Persecuted for Righteousness**
1. **Accurate diagnosis first** (CBT) — This must happen before anything else. *"Would this opposition exist if you stopped doing the righteous thing?"* If no — name it as persecution for righteousness. If yes — this is a different kind of suffering requiring a different response. Do not apply B8 framing until confirmed.
2. **Name the meaning and defuse from the failure narrative** (Logotherapy + ACT) — *"Suffering with meaning is categorically different from suffering that feels random. What does this opposition tell you about the direction you're walking? The automatic thought is 'something is wrong.' The accurate thought: this opposition is the cost of what you're building. It confirms the direction."*
3. **Build the evidence base and anchor the promise** (Self-Efficacy + Theological) — *"Tell me about a time you endured something hard that you didn't think you could endure. What did that prove about you?"* Then: *"The kingdom is present tense. Not when this ends — now. The same promise given to the empty-handed in B1 is given to the emptied-further in B8."*

---

**B9 — Falsely Accused**
1. **Stabilize first** (CFT) — Compassion before framework. Do not rush to reframe. *"What is this like for you right now — not what to do about it, what is it like to be you in this?"* The soothing system must be activated before anything else can land.
2. **Separate accusation from identity and build the true story** (ACT Defusion + Narrative Therapy) — *"Someone has written a story about you. Let's look at it carefully. What does it claim? What does it leave out? What does it need to be true in order to work — and is that actually true?"* Then: *"Who are you actually — not the defensive version, the accurate one?"* Thicken the preferred story with specific evidence.
3. **Relocate identity and anchor the promise** (Identity + Theological) — *"What has the accusation not taken? Who are you when reputation is removed and no one can be convinced? That person — is still intact."* Then: *"God did not clear the prophets' names in real time. What he gave instead was presence, promise, and company. Your reward is great. The company you are in is not the company of the defeated."*

## Training Prerequisites by Tool

*The 14 psychological modality tools used in this framework vary significantly in what they require before safe deployment with a client. Familiarity with a concept is not the same as competence with the tool. This reference consolidates training requirements in one place.*

**Three tiers:**
- **Low** — Self-study and personal application are sufficient. These tools carry low clinical risk and do not require formal training to use in a coaching context.
- **Moderate** — Self-study provides conceptual access, but formal training (workshop, course, or supervised practice) is strongly recommended before structured deployment. These tools can cause harm or produce poor results when used without adequate preparation.
- **High** — Formal training and/or supervision required before deployment. These are clinical modalities where untrained use risks client harm or scope violation.

**The rule that applies to all tiers:** Do not use any tool with a client that you have not first applied to yourself. Personal practice is the minimum prerequisite for every tool, regardless of tier.

## The Interpretive Bridge: Why These Two Frameworks Belong Together

### What Is Definitive

The Soils (Mark 4 / Luke 8) and the Beatitudes (Matthew 5) share three things that justify treating them as a unified diagnostic-formational system:

**1. The same teacher.**
Both come from Jesus. The theology is continuous. When Jesus describes the conditions under which kingdom seed fails to take root (Soils) and the character of those in whom it has (Beatitudes), he is describing the same human transformation process from two angles — one diagnostic, one formational.

**2. The same center: the heart.**
Both frameworks treat the *kardia* — the heart — as the decisive variable. Not circumstances. Not the quality of what is being offered. Not even the effort of the person. The heart's condition is what determines whether kingdom reality lands, roots, grows, and bears fruit. This is not incidental overlap — it is the theological load-bearing beam that connects the two.

**3. The same kingdom.**
Both are kingdom teachings. The Soils describes how the word of the kingdom (Matthew 13:19) is received. The Beatitudes describe the character of kingdom citizens. They are describing different stages of the same kingdom-formation reality.

---

### What Is a Strong Theological Inference

**The diagnostic-formational axis.**
The Soils and the Beatitudes operate on different axes of the same person:

- The **Soils** describe the *current receptivity condition* of the heart — the ground as it presently exists.
- The **Beatitudes** describe the *character formation trajectory* of a heart opening to the kingdom — the direction and shape of the growth.

These axes are orthogonal, not sequential. A person is simultaneously a soil type AND somewhere in the Beatitude formation sequence. The soil is the condition of the ground *while* the Beatitude formation is being attempted. They are not steps — they are concurrent realities. The soil does not resolve before the Beatitudes begin. The person brings their soil condition with them to every Beatitude.

This is the most important formulation of the relationship: **the heart of the soil is always present and always active at every point in the Beatitude formation sequence.** The soil condition either hinders or opens each Beatitude posture. Rocky soil does not disappear during B3 work — it is the condition the B3 work must penetrate. Thorny soil does not clear before B6 — B6 IS the thorn-clearing work.

**The reciprocal relationship.**
The influence runs in both directions — and this is where the framework gains its depth:

*Soil → Beatitudes:* The soil condition determines how available each Beatitude posture is. Hard path soil cannot receive B1 because the hardness IS the defense against the poverty of spirit B1 requires. Rocky soil accesses early Beatitudes but cannot sustain deeper ones because there is no root to hold them under pressure. Thorny soil hits its diagnostic wall at B6 — the competing masters that thorny soil refuses to release are exactly what purity of heart names and requires releasing. Good soil can sustain the costly relational work of B7–B9 because depth has been built.

*Beatitudes → Soil:* This is the move that is often missed. **Working the Beatitudes is soil preparation.** Formation in the Beatitudes actively changes the soil condition — not as a side effect, but as its primary mechanism.

| Beatitude | Soil Work It Does |
|---|---|
| B1 — Poverty of spirit | Breaks up path-soil hardness — the self-sufficiency that made the ground impermeable |
| B2 — Mourning | Creates depth where rocky shallowness existed — honest grief builds root space |
| B3 — Meekness | Loosens the defensive compaction of ego-protection that keeps soil shallow |
| B6 — Purity of heart | Is the thorn-pulling work itself — naming and releasing competing masters IS soil preparation |
| B7–B9 | Are fruit stages — they appear in soil that B1–B6 has progressively prepared |

The implication: you do not fix the soil and then begin Beatitude formation. Working the Beatitudes **is** how the soil is changed. They are the same process. The coaching sequence — diagnose soil, address the soil's specific blockage, apply the corresponding Beatitude work — is not three steps. It is one continuous movement.

---

### What Is Our Interpretive Construction

The specific mapping of soil types to Beatitude accessibility — which soil can reach which Beatitude, where the diagnostic walls are — is a **hermeneutical synthesis**, not an exegetical claim. Jesus never connected these two frameworks. We are connecting them, drawing on the theological logic of each, the shared heart-focus, and pastoral observation of what formation actually looks like in human lives.

This kind of synthesis is legitimate biblical theology. The test is whether the connection is coherent with both texts, honest about its constructed nature, and productive in practice. It passes all three. But it requires transparency: **this framework makes an interpretive claim, not an exegetical one.**

Two things follow:
1. The specific soil-to-Beatitude mappings are directional guides, not deterministic rules. The Holy Spirit is not bound to them. Neither is the client.
2. Soil type is not a fixed label. Soil changes. The whole point of formation is that it does. Use soil type as a diagnostic read of the current condition — not a permanent category.

---

### The Psychological Substrate

The Soils and Beatitudes describe the same psychological reality using biblical language. The overlap is not incidental:

| Soil Type | Psychological Parallel |
|---|---|
| Hard path | Avoidant attachment / self-sufficiency schema / no internal access to need |
| Rocky | Anxious / no regulatory depth / emotional reactivity without root |
| Thorny | Ambivalent / values conflict / competing attachment loyalties |
| Good soil | Secure attachment / differentiated self / integrated values |

The Beatitudes then map to what psychology calls identity formation, virtue development, and schema change. The coach who understands both the theological language and the psychological substrate can move fluidly between them — using whichever frame is most accessible to the client without losing the connection to the other.

## Integration Layer 1: Beatitudes × Soils

### The Structural Insight

**Note on sequence:** The Beatitude formation sequence used in this framework is a **pastoral heuristic, not a biblical mandate.** NT scholarship does not uniformly read the Beatitudes as developmental — the dominant readings treat them as eschatological descriptions of kingdom citizens or character portraits. The sequential framing is a practical organizing principle. The Holy Spirit is not bound to it. Use it as a directional guide, not a prerequisite gate. For the full rationale for connecting the Soils and Beatitudes frameworks, see *The Interpretive Bridge* section above.

**Soil type often indicates which Beatitude postures are most accessible and which are currently out of reach.**

| Soil Type     | Beatitude Blockage                | Why                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ------------- | --------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Path**      | Before B1                         | Cannot enter poverty of spirit — the armor exists to prevent exactly that openness. Hardness IS the protection against B1.                                              |
| **Rocky**     | B1–B2 accessible, B4 doesn't hold | Passion without roots. Can land on hunger (B4) emotionally but the filling doesn't integrate. Mourning (B2) dissipates rather than processes.                           |
| **Thorny**    | B1–B3 partially, B6 is the wall   | Gets through early Beatitudes but double-mindedness (B6) IS the thorny condition — competing masters are the thorns. B4's hunger has been redirected toward the thorns. |
| **Good Soil** | B7–B9 accessible                  | Can sustain peacemaking, endure persecution, rejoice under false accusation — requires the depth only good soil has built.                                              |

---

### Diagnostic Use by Soil Type

**Path soil client:**
The entry work is B1 — not as a teaching, as a posture. The path soil client cannot be poor in spirit before a God who has already disappointed them. The soil work and the B1 work are the same work. The Soils diagnostic question applies directly: *"Who is God to you right now, in your actual life?"*

**Rocky soil client:**
The B2 work is critical and is the work the rocky soil client most resists. Rocky clients have emotional experiences that dissipate — they do not mourn in the *penthountes* sense. The in-session sequence principle — "go one layer deeper than the client presented" — applied here means: when the emotional resonance rises, don't ride it. Go beneath it. The root isn't found in the peak experience. It's found in B2 — the honest grief that goes down rather than out.

**Thorny soil client:**
B6 is the diagnostic center. The thorns ARE competing masters. Double-mindedness is the thorny soil condition named theologically. The Soils secondary gain question — *"If this changed, what would you lose?"* — is the B6 question: what is the competing master the client isn't willing to name? The thorny soil diagnostic and the pure heart diagnostic are running the same investigation from different angles.

**Good soil client:**
The work moves to B7–B9. Peacemaking, endurance under persecution, and rejoicing under false accusation are not accessible to shallow soil. The good soil client who is not yet bearing full fruit is usually stuck between B6 and B7 — the heart is largely unified but the costly relational engagement of peacemaking is still being avoided.

### Spiritual Warfare Overlay — Soils × Beatitudes

The Soils framework maps spiritual warfare by soil type. The Beatitudes add specificity to what is being targeted:

| Soil | Enemy Strategy | Beatitude Being Targeted |
|---|---|---|
| Path | Confirms existing disappointment | B1 — every new wound confirms the cost of openness |
| Rocky | Attacks experience to destabilize identity | B2 and B4 — dry seasons weaponized against the roots that mourning builds |
| Thorny | Waters the thorns subtly | B6 — competing masters never presented as competing. They feel like responsibilities. |
| Good | Targets pruning and self-reliance | B8–B9 — persecution reframed as evidence of failure; fruitfulness reframed as self-generated |

## Integration Layer 2: Beatitudes × Lie Eliminator

### The 12 Existing Lies Mapped to Beatitudes

Each lie in the Lie Eliminator blocks a specific Beatitude posture. Knowing which Beatitude a client is working toward tells you which lie to excavate:

| Lie | Beatitude Blocked | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **God as Disconnected Authoritarian** | B1 | Cannot be poor in spirit before a God who is adversarial. Poverty of spirit requires trust that the kingdom will be given — not withheld. |
| **I Cannot Do It** | B4, B7 | Extinguishes hunger (B4) and blocks peacemaking engagement (B7). The one who believes they cannot stops reaching. |
| **Hopelessness** | B2, B8 | Mourning (B2) requires the comfort to be real. B8's endurance requires a future. Hopelessness eliminates both. |
| **Scarcity** | B4 | Redirects hunger from righteousness toward provision and survival. You cannot hunger for righteousness in survival mode. |
| **Giving Up** | B8 | "Nothing is changing" is the primary lie blocking endurance under persecution. |
| **False Risk / I Will Fail** | B7 | Won't engage in peacemaking if failure is the anticipated outcome. The cost feels unjustifiable when failure is expected. |
| **Abandoned by God** | B2, B9 | B2: cannot trust the comfort promised if God has left. B9: cannot rejoice under false accusation if God is not present in it. |
| **Cannot Be Done Through Me** | B7 | Peacemaking requires believing your engagement can contribute something. This lie removes that belief. |
| **I Am Not Enough / Imposter** | B1 | Distorts poverty of spirit into shame. Converts "I have nothing to bring" from liberation into condemnation. Critical distinction. |
| **Too Connected to Sinful Heritage** | B1, B2 | B1: disqualification prevents receiving the kingdom. B2: cannot mourn what is broken if the brokenness feels permanent and inherited. |
| **Rejectable** | B1, B5, B9 | B1: poverty of spirit exposes the self — the rejectable client can't afford that exposure. B5: mercy risks relationship. B9: false accusation feels like the ultimate rejection confirmation. |
| **Disgust of Others in Gross Sin** | B5, B7 | Mercy (B5) requires releasing disgust. Peacemaking (B7) is impossible when contempt is active. |
| **Lawful Thinking / Resentment / Anger** | B3, B5, B7 | Resentment blocks meekness (B3). Demand for deserving blocks mercy (B5). Zero-sum thinking blocks peacemaking (B7). |

### Six New Lies the Beatitudes Expose

### The Lie Eliminator Methodology Applied to Beatitude Work

When a client cannot move from one Beatitude posture to the next — when they intellectually understand a Beatitude but cannot inhabit it — the Lie Eliminator's excavation methodology applies directly:

**Lie Archaeology:**
*"When did you first believe that [the lie blocking this Beatitude]? How old were you? What was happening? Who taught you that — not intentionally, but who lived it in front of you? What would have had to be true about you for that to be a logical conclusion?"*

**The Agreement Audit:**
*"There was a moment when you stopped just hearing that and started believing it — when you made an agreement with it. Can you locate that moment?"*

**The Stronghold Anatomy (2 Corinthians 10:4–5):**
The lie blocking the Beatitude has four layers — all requiring intervention:
1. The belief (the argument itself)
2. The evidence (accumulated experience confirming it)
3. The emotional backing (formed in a moment with real feeling)
4. The behavioral reinforcement (living as though it's true has created habits)

Lie work that only addresses the belief doesn't touch the evidence, emotion, or behavior.

**Somatic Anchoring of Truth:**
When the accurate truth lands — when the lie is named and renounced, when the Beatitude posture becomes real — slow the moment, locate it physically, amplify it, name it with language, seal it. Cognitive-only truth is accessible when the client is calm. Somatically anchored truth is accessible when they are under the pressure that ordinarily overwhelms their framework.

> ⚠ **Scope Gate — Somatic Work:** Full somatic intervention (body-based trauma processing, nervous system regulation protocols, somatic experiencing) requires specialized clinical training and is outside coaching scope. The anchoring move described here — slowing a moment of insight, locating it physically, naming it — is a light attentional practice, not a clinical somatic protocol. If a client shows significant somatic activation (dissociation, flooding, freeze response), pause and refer to a licensed clinician trained in somatic or trauma modalities. Do not attempt to work through high somatic activation in a coaching context.

## Evidence-Generation Protocol — "My Evidence"

*The "My Evidence" section is the psychological load-bearing element of every Identity Installer entry. Affirmations without personal evidence stay abstract — the brain rejects claims it has no experience to support. This protocol is for when the client draws a blank, has thin evidence, or needs help excavating what they already have.*

---

### What Counts as Evidence

Evidence is broader than a dramatic moment of clarity. Coach the client toward any of these:

| Type | Description | Example prompt |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct experience** | A moment when the identity statement was clearly true | *"When did you know, even briefly, that this was real?"* |
| **Behavioral evidence** | An action that implied the identity even without conscious awareness | *"When have you acted like this person — even without feeling like them?"* |
| **Others' testimony** | What someone has observed or named in you | *"Has anyone ever said something to you that pointed at this quality?"* |
| **Survival evidence** | You came through something that proves this is true about you | *"You're still here. What does that tell you about who you are?"* |
| **Thin evidence** | A fleeting moment, a partial experience, a hint | *"What's the closest you've come to this — even for a minute?"* |

---

### Excavation Questions — When the Client Draws a Blank

Work through these sequentially. Stop when one produces something:

1. *"Has there ever been a moment — even briefly — when this felt true?"*
2. *"When were you most like the person this statement describes?"*
3. *"What's the closest thing you have to this experience — even if it's imperfect?"*
4. *"Has anyone ever reflected this back to you — said something that pointed at it?"*
5. *"You came through something hard to get here. What did that take? What does that tell you about who you are?"*
6. *"What would it look like if this were 10% true about you right now? Is there any evidence for that 10%?"*

If all six produce nothing — do not force it. The entry is not ready. Move to the Watching Posture below.

---

### The Scaling Approach

| Evidence Level | What It Looks Like | Coach Move |
|---|---|---|
| **Strong** | Clear, specific memory; the client can describe it in detail | Write it immediately. Ask: *"What did that moment prove about you?"* |
| **Moderate** | A vague sense it has been true; partial memory | Thicken it: *"Tell me more. Where were you? What did it feel like? What happened next?"* |
| **Thin** | One small moment, barely qualifies | Accept it. Write it. It is enough to begin. *"That counts. That's the first brick."* |
| **None** | Client cannot identify any experience | Move to the Watching Posture — do not skip, do not fake |

---

### The Watching Posture — When There Is No Evidence Yet

Do not let the client skip "My Evidence." Do not let them write nothing.

Instead, write: **"I am watching for this."**

Then do two things:

**1. Identify specifically what to watch for this week:**
*"What would it look like if this were true about you, even in a small way? What would you notice?"* Give the client one specific, concrete thing to watch for between sessions.

**2. Assign the evidence homework:**
*"Before our next session: watch for one moment when this is true — even briefly, even partially. Write it down. One sentence. Bring it."*

At the next session: begin by asking for the evidence. If found — write it in. If not — repeat the watching posture one more week before reassessing whether this entry is premature for this client.

---

### What to Do With Counter-Evidence

Sometimes the client generates evidence that contradicts the identity statement — a moment proving the opposite. This is not a problem. It is a diagnostic.

- Do not argue with it or minimize it
- Name it as useful: *"That's actually helpful — it shows us exactly where the formation work needs to happen."*
- Pair it with the corresponding lie in the Lie Eliminator: counter-evidence is usually the lie's evidence base
- Do the lie work. Return to the identity entry after.

Counter-evidence before lie work = the cart before the horse.

### Pathway 4 — Via Soil Type

When the primary diagnostic is soil type and no specific lie or wound is presenting:

**Path soil client:**
The God-representation distortion is the entry point. Start with entries that address who God actually is before addressing identity.

Full range accessible. Use the presenting wound or Beatitude sequence pathway. The question is not what's accessible — it is what the Spirit is pressing on right now.

### Rules for Entry Use

1. **One entry at a time.** Work it for 1–4 weeks before moving. Depth over breadth.
2. **Evidence first.** If "My Evidence" cannot be completed even partially, the entry is not ready. Do not force it.
3. **Lie work before installation.** When a lie is active, clear it first with the Lie Eliminator. Do not install identity on top of an unrenounced lie — the lie will undermine the declaration.
4. **Speak it aloud.** The entry does not work silently. Faith comes by hearing — including the client hearing themselves say it.
5. **Return and update.** "My Evidence" is meant to grow. A return visit to a completed entry to add new evidence is often more powerful than beginning a new one.
