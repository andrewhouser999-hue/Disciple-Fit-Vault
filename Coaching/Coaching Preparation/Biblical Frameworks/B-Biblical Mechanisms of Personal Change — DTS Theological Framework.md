# Biblical Mechanisms of Personal Change — DTS Theological Framework

> A comparison of general Scripture-mapped mechanisms of change alongside the specific theological grid of Dallas Theological Seminary (DTS). Useful for coaching framework development, session prep, and theological grounding.

---

## The DTS Theological Grid

Before the list, understand the framework that shapes it:

1. **Positional vs. Experiential Sanctification** — You ARE sanctified (positional, permanent, Hebrews 10:10). You are BECOMING sanctified (experiential, progressive, 2 Cor 3:18). Most change problems are diagnosed as failing to live from your position.
2. **Grace-dependency, not self-effort** — Any mechanism that sounds like human striving is suspect. Change is God's work; humans yield and cooperate.
3. **The Filling of the Spirit is the central mechanism** — Ephesians 5:18 is the engine. Everything else is upstream or downstream of it.
4. **Historical cessationism** — Cautious about any mechanism that sounds like second-blessing, mysticism, or charismatic crisis experience.
5. **Doctrine-first sequencing** — Romans 1–11 (who you are) must precede Romans 12–16 (how you live). Ephesians 1–3 before Ephesians 4–6. You can't change behavior without first anchoring identity.

## The DTS Mechanism List

### Mechanism 1: Positional Truth as Foundation
*Change flows from knowing who you already are, not from trying to become something*

- **Romans 6:3-5** — "Don't you know that all of us who were baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into his death?"
- **Romans 6:6** — "Our old self was crucified with him so that the body ruled by sin might be done away with."
- **Ephesians 1:3-14** — The full "in Christ" inventory *(prerequisite reading before any sanctification framework)*
- **Colossians 2:10** — "In Christ you have been brought to fullness."
- **2 Corinthians 5:17** — "If anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: the old has gone, the new is here."

> **DTS emphasis:** Most Christians fail to change because they're trying to become what they already are. The mechanism is *recognition*, not acquisition.

---

### Mechanism 2: Reckoning (Counting Positional Truth as Real)
*The cognitive act of applying what is true positionally to present experience*

- **Romans 6:11** — "Count yourselves dead to sin but alive to God in Christ Jesus." *(Greek "logizomai" = reckon, consider, count as fact)*
- **Romans 6:12-13** — "Do not let sin reign... do not offer any part of yourself to sin."
- **Galatians 2:20** — "I have been crucified with Christ... the life I now live in the body, I live by faith."

> **DTS framing:** "Reckon" is not wishful thinking — it's agreeing with what God has declared. Change begins when you stop arguing with your position.

---

### Mechanism 3: Yielding / Presenting (Volitional Surrender)
*The human's primary role in the change process — not striving, but surrendering*

- **Romans 12:1** — "Offer your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and pleasing to God — this is your true and proper worship." *(hinge verse of all practical sanctification)*
- **Romans 6:13** — "Offer every part of yourself to him as an instrument of righteousness."
- **Romans 6:16** — "Don't you know that when you offer yourselves to someone as obedient slaves, you are slaves of the one you obey?"
- **James 4:7** — "Submit yourselves, then, to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you."

> **DTS framing:** The human mechanism is yield, not achieve. God does the transforming; humans do the presenting.

---

### Mechanism 4: Filling of the Spirit (THE Central Mechanism)
*The ongoing, repeatable act of being controlled/empowered by the Spirit*

- **Ephesians 5:18** — "Do not get drunk on wine... instead, be filled with the Spirit." *(command, present tense, continuous — be being filled)*
- **Galatians 5:16** — "Walk by the Spirit and you will not gratify the desires of the flesh."
- **Galatians 5:25** — "Since we live by the Spirit, let us keep in step with the Spirit."
- **Romans 8:4-5** — "Who do not live according to the flesh but according to the Spirit."
- **Acts 4:31** — "They were all filled with the Holy Spirit and spoke the word of God boldly."

> **DTS framing:** Not a one-time crisis experience. It's a continuous posture of yielding control to the Spirit. The fruit of Galatians 5 is produced by the Spirit living through you, not by you trying harder.

---

### Mechanism 5: Confession and Restoration of Fellowship
*The mechanism for restoring operational access to Spirit-filled living after sin*

- **1 John 1:9** — "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just and will forgive us our sins and purify us from all unrighteousness." *(fellowship restoration, not salvation)*
- **Psalm 51:10-12** — "Create in me a pure heart... restore to me the joy of your salvation."
- **Proverbs 28:13** — "Whoever conceals their sins does not prosper, but the one who confesses and renounces them finds mercy."

> **DTS framing:** Unconfessed sin breaks fellowship (not relationship) with God, cutting off the power supply for change. Confession is the circuit breaker reset.

---

### Mechanism 6: Renewing the Mind (with Doctrinal Content)
*The Word — especially doctrinal truth — restructures thinking which drives behavior*

- **Romans 12:2** — "Be transformed by the renewing of your mind."
- **John 17:17** — "Sanctify them by the truth; your word is truth."
- **2 Timothy 3:16-17** — "All Scripture is God-breathed and useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness."
- **Colossians 3:16** — "Let the message of Christ dwell among you richly."
- **Psalm 119:11** — "I have hidden your word in my heart that I might not sin against you."
- **Hebrews 4:12** — "The word of God is alive and active... it judges the thoughts and attitudes of the heart."

> **DTS emphasis:** Not generic positivity — specifically doctrinal content saturating the mind. Scripture memorization + exposition as primary sanctification tools (Hendricks, Swindoll).

---

### Mechanism 7: Abiding in Christ
*Ongoing relational connection as the source of fruit — not effort, but attachment*

- **John 15:4-5** — "Remain in me, as I also remain in you... apart from me you can do nothing."
- **John 15:7** — "If you remain in me and my words remain in you, ask whatever you wish, and it will be done for you."
- **1 John 2:6** — "Whoever claims to live in him must live as Jesus did."
- **Colossians 3:3-4** — "Your life is now hidden with Christ in God."

---

### Mechanism 8: Faith as the Operating System
*Change is engaged through active trust, not passive waiting*

- **Hebrews 11:6** — "Without faith it is impossible to please God."
- **Romans 1:17** — "The righteous will live by faith."
- **Galatians 3:11** — "The righteous will live by faith."
- **2 Corinthians 5:7** — "We live by faith, not by sight."

---

### Mechanism 9: Prayer
*Direct communication with God as a change-producing activity*

- **Philippians 4:6-7** — "By prayer and petition... the peace of God... will guard your hearts and minds."
- **Matthew 26:41** — "Watch and pray so that you will not fall into temptation."
- **James 5:16** — "The prayer of a righteous person is powerful and effective."

## What DTS Reframes from Standard Sanctification Lists

| Mechanism | Standard List | DTS Reframe |
|---|---|---|
| **Suffering** | Active sanctifying agent | God's sovereign tool — not a mechanism you employ; you receive it, not pursue it |
| **Community/accountability** | Legitimate mechanism | Helpful but secondary; the Spirit, not other people, is the primary agent |
| **Beholding (2 Cor 3:18)** | Contemplative practice | Yes, but framed as Word-saturated meditation, not mystical gazing |
| **Mortification** | Active killing of sin | Retained, but strictly Spirit-empowered (Romans 8:13) — not asceticism or self-discipline alone |
| **Repentance as mechanism** | Primary ongoing engine | Entry point, not the ongoing engine — confession (1 John 1:9) does that work |

---

## What DTS Adds That Standard Lists Miss

| Mechanism | Verses | Why DTS Emphasizes It |
|---|---|---|
| **Reckoning positional truth** | Romans 6:11 | The "logizomai" act — agree with God about your position |
| **Filling of the Spirit** | Ephesians 5:18 | Their primary mechanism — surprisingly absent from most change lists |
| **Doctrine-first sequencing** | Romans 1–11 → 12–16 | Behavior change without doctrinal foundation is moralism, not sanctification |
| **Fellowship restoration** | 1 John 1:9 | Specific mechanism for getting back "online" after sin breaks the connection |

## The DTS Change Model in One Sentence

> **Know your position → Reckon it as real → Yield yourself → Be filled with the Spirit → Walk.**

This sequence is Lewis Sperry Chafer's core framework (*He That Is Spiritual*), refined by Charles Ryrie (*Balancing the Christian Life*) and Chuck Swindoll, and remains the operative model at DTS.
