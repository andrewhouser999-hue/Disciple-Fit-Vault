# DTS Change Sequence — Know, Reckon, Yield, Walk

> The Dallas Theological Seminary framework for personal transformation. Four steps that must operate in order. Skipping or reversing any step produces moralism, instability, or spiritual paralysis.

**The sequence in one sentence:**
> Know your position → Reckon it as real → Yield yourself → Walk in the Spirit.

---

## The Full Sequence — Quick Reference

| Step | What You're Doing | What It Produces | Key Verses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Know** (Eph 1) | Establishing the foundation | Settled conviction about who you are | Eph 1:3-14; Rom 6:3-6; 2 Cor 5:17; Col 2:10 |
| **Reckon** (Rom 6:11) | Agreeing with your position | Choosing position over experience | Rom 6:11-13; Gal 2:20; Rom 8:6 |
| **Yield** (Rom 12:1) | Placing yourself at disposal | Availability for God to work | Rom 12:1; Rom 6:13, 16; James 4:7 |
| **Walk** (Gal 5:16) | Moving in Spirit-empowered direction | Actual behavioral change | Gal 5:16, 25; Eph 5:18; Rom 8:4-5, 13 |

## Step 1: Know What Is True — Ephesians 1:3-14

> "Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us in the heavenly realms with every spiritual blessing in Christ." — Ephesians 1:3

The word "know" here is not intellectual awareness. Paul uses **oida** — settled clarity, perceived reality, the kind of knowing that structures decisions. Not "I've heard this is true" but "this is the fixed reality I operate from."

### Three Layers of Knowing

**Layer 1: Propositional Knowledge**
You must know the actual content — what each item in the inventory is, what it means, why it's irrevocable. You cannot reckon what you don't know.

**Layer 2: Personal Appropriation**
Moving from "this is true of Christians" to "this is true of me." Paul makes it personal throughout — "he chose *us*," "he adopted *us*," "we have redemption." The inventory is not a theological category. It's a personal deed of ownership.

**Layer 3: Settled Conviction**
Not "I think this is true" but "this is the fixed reality I operate from." The difference between a man who knows he owns a house and a man who is fully settled in it — furniture arranged, pictures on the wall, acting like it's his.

### What Blocks Step 1

- **Feelings that contradict position** — feelings report based on experience, not legal reality. They are louder and more immediate, which is why most people defer to them.
- **Deeply held lies** — lies occupying the same space as positional truth. A person who grew up with an absent father has an emotional category for "chosen and adopted by a loving father" — and it's loaded with pain, not security. The doctrine may be intellectually accepted while being emotionally rejected simultaneously.

> Vague theological knowledge doesn't displace specific lies. You need specific truth occupying specific territory.

### The "In Christ" Inventory — Ephesians 1:3-14

In the original Greek, verses 3–14 are a **single sentence** — one unbroken doxology. This is the content of what must be known. Each item is already true, already accomplished, irrevocable.

---

#### v.3 — Blessed with Every Spiritual Blessing
> "...who has blessed us in the heavenly realms with every spiritual blessing in Christ."

- Not some blessings. Not most. **Every** spiritual blessing.
- Past tense — already done. Not "will bless" but "has blessed."
- Located in the heavenly realms — a dimension circumstances cannot touch.

**Coaching implication:** A client living in lack, defeat, or shame is not living from their actual position. The gap between position and experience is where coaching operates.

---

#### v.4 — Chosen Before the Foundation of the World
> "He chose us in him before the creation of the world to be holy and blameless in his sight."

- The choosing happened before you existed — before performance or failure was possible.
- "Holy and blameless" is your declared status, not a behavioral target you're working toward.
- Dismantles performance-based identity at the root.

**Coaching implication:** The lie "I have to earn my place" has no ground to stand on. The choosing was unconditional and pre-creation.

---

#### v.5 — Adopted as Sons
> "He predestined us for adoption to sonship through Jesus Christ, in accordance with his pleasure and will."

- In Roman law, adoption was irrevocable — full legal standing, inheritance rights, new name, old debts cancelled.
- "His pleasure and will" — the motivation is entirely internal to God. His delight, not your worthiness.

**Coaching implication:** Orphan-spirit clients — striving, performing, fear of rejection — are operating from a false identity. They have a legal standing they haven't inhabited.

---

#### v.6 — Freely Given Grace
> "...his glorious grace, which he has freely given us in the One he loves."

- Greek *charitoō* — same root as grace (*charis*). Graced, lavished with grace.
- Used only one other place in the NT: Luke 1:28 — "Greetings, you who are highly favored" (spoken to Mary).

---

#### v.7 — Redeemed and Forgiven
> "In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins, in accordance with the riches of his grace."

- **Redemption** — Greek *apolutrōsis* — the purchase price paid to free a slave. Bought out of slavery.
- **Forgiveness** — Greek *aphesis* — to send away, to release. The debt is not just paid; it's dismissed.
- "Riches of his grace" — not the minimum required, but according to His riches.

---

#### v.8 — Wisdom and Understanding Lavished
> "...that he lavished on us with all wisdom and understanding."

- "Lavished" — not rationed, not dripped. Lavished.
- Wisdom and understanding are included in the gift — access to God's own perspective on your situation.

---

#### v.9-10 — Mystery of His Will Made Known
> "He made known to us the mystery of his will... to bring unity to all things in Christ."

- You are an insider to God's cosmic plan, not a bystander.
- The ultimate trajectory of history is Christ's lordship — and you are positioned within that story.

---

#### v.11 — Predestined and Given an Inheritance
> "In him we were also chosen, having been predestined according to the plan of him who works out everything in conformity with the purpose of his will."

- "Works out everything" — God is not reacting to your failures. He is actively working all things toward a purpose that includes you.
- You have an inheritance — not earned, received as a son.

---

#### v.12 — Purposed for His Glory
> "...in order that we... might be for the praise of his glory."

- Your existence has a built-in purpose: to display God's glory.
- The identity question is already answered. You don't manufacture meaning — it was assigned.

---

#### v.13 — Sealed with the Holy Spirit
> "When you believed, you were marked in him with a seal, the promised Holy Spirit."

- A seal = ownership mark, authenticity stamp, protection from tampering.
- The Holy Spirit is the seal — not a feeling, not an experience, but a permanent marker of belonging.

---

#### v.14 — The Spirit as Down Payment
> "...who is a deposit guaranteeing our inheritance until the redemption of those who are God's possession."

- Greek *arrabon* — a legal down payment that obligates the payer to complete the transaction.
- God has put the Holy Spirit in you as a legal guarantee He will finish what He started.
- The Spirit is the contract. Your full inheritance is locked in.

## Step 2: Reckon It As Real — Romans 6:11

> "In the same way, count yourselves dead to sin but alive to God in Christ Jesus."

The Greek word is **logizomai** — an accounting term meaning to enter something into the ledger as fact. To count it, calculate it, credit it to an account.

It is **not:**
- Positive thinking
- Convincing yourself of something uncertain
- Pretending a feeling is true
- Willpower or mental effort

It **is:**
- Treating an established legal fact as operative in your present situation
- The same move an accountant makes when recording a transaction that already happened

### The Best Analogy

Imagine a wealthy relative died and left you $10 million. The will has been probated. The money is in an account with your name on it. It is legally, irrevocably yours.

But you keep living like you're broke — anxious about bills, making decisions from scarcity.

**Reckoning it as real** means opening the account and spending from it. Not hoping the money exists. Not trying to believe hard enough. The transaction already happened. Reckoning is simply **acting in accordance with what is already true.**

### Why "In the Same Way" Matters

Paul says "in the same way" — pointing back to Romans 6:3-10, where he just described the historical, objective fact of your co-crucifixion and co-resurrection with Christ.

He's saying: *that thing I just described — apply it to yourself. Enter it into your ledger.*

The sin nature has no legal claim on you. You died to it. That is a fact established at conversion. Reckoning is choosing to **operate from that fact** rather than from the feeling that sin still has power over you.

### Why This Is Hard in Practice

Your **experience** often contradicts your **position:**

- You feel ashamed → position: forgiven and blameless (Eph 1:4)
- You feel rejected → position: adopted and chosen (Eph 1:5)
- You feel powerless over sin → position: dead to it (Romans 6:11)

Reckoning is the act of **siding with your position over your experience** — not denying the experience, but refusing to let it be the final word.

It is closer to a legal declaration than an emotional state. A judge doesn't feel innocent — he declares it. Reckoning is agreeing with God's declaration about you.

### The Practical Move

1. **Identify the lie or feeling** — "I'm still bound by this. I can't change. This is just who I am."
2. **Locate the positional truth** — "Romans 6:6 says my old self was crucified. That happened."
3. **Make the declaration** — "I count that as true right now, in this situation."
4. **Act from the declaration** — not waiting to feel free before acting free

The feeling may or may not follow. You walk by faith, not by feeling.

## Step 3: Yield Yourself — Romans 12:1

> "Therefore, I urge you, brothers and sisters, in view of God's mercy, to offer your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and pleasing to God — this is your true and proper worship."

### The Word "Therefore" Is Load-Bearing

Romans 12:1 opens with "therefore" — which means Romans 1–11 is the prerequisite. Paul spent 11 chapters establishing justification, death to sin, the Spirit's role, and God's sovereign purposes. **Then** he says "therefore — yield."

Yield is only possible after knowing and reckoning. Without Romans 1–11, Romans 12:1 is just religious willpower.

### What "Offer" Actually Means

The Greek is **paristēmi** — to place beside, to present, to put at someone's disposal. A military and legal term. A soldier presenting himself to his commanding officer. It carries:

- **Availability** — I am here, ready to be used
- **Surrender of agenda** — I am not directing this
- **Active placement** — a deliberate act, not passive drift

Like a surgeon's assistant who has scrubbed in, positioned at the table, fully present and responsive — not passive, but actively available.

### "Living Sacrifice" — The Tension

In the Old Testament, a sacrifice was dead. Done. No more agenda. Paul deliberately uses **living** sacrifice — which creates a problem he names in Romans 6:13: the living sacrifice keeps crawling off the altar.

This is why yielding is not a one-time event. It's a continuous, renewable act. **Yielding is a posture, not a transaction.**

Practically:
- **Morning yield** — presenting the day before it begins
- **Situational yield** — presenting yourself in a specific moment of temptation or decision
- **Crisis yield** — returning to the altar after crawling off it

### "In View of God's Mercy" — The Motivation Is Critical

Paul doesn't say yield out of fear, guilt, duty, or to earn favor. He says yield **in view of God's mercy** — the entire inventory of Romans 1–11.

The motivation is **gratitude responding to grace**, not performance responding to demand.

A client yielding from fear looks identical to a client yielding from gratitude on the surface. Only one produces lasting change. Fear-based yielding collapses under pressure. Gratitude-based yielding deepens under pressure.

### "Your Bodies" — Why Physical?

Paul doesn't say "offer your minds." He says **bodies** — because that's where behavior lives. You don't sin in the abstract. You sin with hands, eyes, mouth, feet.

Offering your body means:
- Where your feet go
- What your eyes stay on
- What your hands do
- What your mouth says
- What your schedule contains

This prevents spiritual bypass — a very yielded inner life while actual physical behaviors remain unchanged.

### What Blocks Yielding

**1. Unresolved Steps 1 and 2**
If you don't know your position and haven't reckoned it, yielding feels like jumping off a cliff with no landing. You're surrendering to a God you're not sure is for you.

**2. Secondary gain**
The old self often has payoffs — control, self-protection, identity, familiar comfort. Yielding requires releasing those payoffs. The body knows what it's been getting from the old pattern and doesn't release it without processing.

**3. Confusion between yielding and passivity**
Yielding does not mean doing nothing and waiting for God to move you like a puppet. It's the active placement of yourself at God's disposal — **fully present and responsive**, not checked out.

### The Practical Move

1. **Acknowledge what you're holding** — what you're still directing, controlling, protecting
2. **Name the cost of releasing it** — what feels like it's at stake
3. **Make the declaration** — "I place myself at your disposal. This situation, this decision, this body, this day."
4. **Follow through in the next concrete choice** — the yield is confirmed in action, not just in prayer

## Step 4: Walk in the Spirit — Galatians 5:16

> "So I say, walk by the Spirit, and you will not gratify the desires of the flesh."

### The Word "Walk" Is Deliberate

The Greek is **peripateō** — to walk around, to conduct oneself, to order one's life. A lifestyle word, not a crisis word. Continuous present tense — keep walking, go on walking.

Paul could have said "be empowered" or "be transformed." He said walk — because transformation happens in the accumulated weight of ordinary daily steps, not in singular mountaintop moments.

### The Structure of the Verse Is Backwards From How We Read It

Most people read it as a command with a condition:
> "Walk by the Spirit *so that* you don't gratify the flesh."

The Greek construction is actually a **promise with a guarantee:**
> "Walk by the Spirit *and you will not* — you simply will not — gratify the desires of the flesh."

The "will not" is **ou mē** — the most emphatic negation in Greek. The outcome is guaranteed if the walking is real.

DTS's point: the solution to sin is not fighting sin harder. It's walking in the Spirit more fully. You don't defeat darkness by attacking it — you turn on a light.

### What "By the Spirit" Means

The preposition is **pneumati** — dative case, meaning "by means of" or "in the sphere of."

Walking by the Spirit means:
- **In dependence on** the Spirit as your power source
- **In responsiveness to** the Spirit's prompting and direction
- **In the realm of** the Spirit's influence rather than the flesh's

Available to every believer, continuously, as a way of moving through ordinary life. Not a special experience reserved for mature Christians.

### The Flesh vs. Spirit Conflict — Galatians 5:17

> "For the flesh desires what is contrary to the Spirit, and the Spirit what is contrary to the flesh. They are in conflict with each other."

Two competing forces with opposing agendas are both present in the believer. But this is not a coin-flip battle of equal powers. The issue is not power — it's which one you're walking in.

You are not a passive battleground. You are an active participant choosing which principle to operate from.

### Keep in Step — Galatians 5:25

> "Since we live by the Spirit, let us keep in step with the Spirit."

"Keep in step" — Greek **stoicheō** — to walk in a line, to follow in order, to march in rank. A military formation word.

Walking by the Spirit is not wandering in a generally spiritual direction. It's **following a specific lead** — moment by moment responsiveness.

Practically:
- A pause before a reaction — "what is the Spirit's direction here?"
- A check before a decision — "am I moving from flesh or from Spirit right now?"
- A return after a drift — not condemnation, but reorientation

### The Fruit Test — Galatians 5:22-23

> "But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, forbearance, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control."

Two critical observations:

**1. Singular — "fruit," not "fruits"**
One cluster, not nine separate achievements. They come together as organic output of a life genuinely walking in the Spirit. Manufacturing individual fruit through willpower is not what Paul has in mind.

**2. Produced, not performed**
An apple tree doesn't strain to produce apples. Apples are the natural output of a tree living in its proper environment. The fruit of the Spirit naturally emerges from a life connected to and walking in the Spirit. If fruit is absent, the question is not "try harder" — it's "where has the walking broken down?"

### How Walking Breaks Down

| Breakdown Point | What Happens to Walking |
|---|---|
| **Know** collapses | Walking becomes performance — trying to earn what you think you don't have |
| **Reckon** collapses | Walking becomes unstable — feelings override position and you drift back to flesh-patterns |
| **Yield** collapses | Walking stalls — you're still directing your own life, Spirit has no operational access |
| **Walk** itself | Drifting through inattention, not outright rebellion — the most common failure mode |

## The Feedback Loop

This is not a four-step linear process you complete once. It is a continuous loop:

```
Know → Reckon → Yield → Walk
  ↑                        ↓
  ←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←
```

Walking produces deeper experience of positional truth, which deepens reckoning, which makes yielding more natural, which produces more consistent walking.

Sin breaks the loop operationally — not permanently, not at the salvation level. **1 John 1:9 (confession) is the re-entry point** back into the sequence after a break.
