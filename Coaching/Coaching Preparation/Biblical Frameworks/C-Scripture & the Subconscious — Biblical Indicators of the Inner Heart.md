# Scripture & the Subconscious — Biblical Indicators of the Inner Heart

Scripture never uses the term "subconscious" — that's modern psychology — but seven categories of verses describe what we'd recognize as subconscious functioning. This reference supports the CBC (CBT + Coaching + Biblical) framework by grounding depth-level inner work in Scripture. Cross-reference with the Soils Framework (lie nukes, wound awareness) and the Life Coaching Tool Reference.

## Key Synthesis — CBC Framework Application

The Hebrew concept of **lev** (heart) = the combined cognitive/emotional/volitional core — mostly operating below conscious awareness. Scripture consistently treats it as:

- The **source** of automatic behavior (Matt 12:34)
- **Inaccessible** to the person without divine intervention (Jer 17:9, Ps 139:23)
- **Reprogrammable** through renewal, not willpower (Rom 12:2, Eph 4:22–24)
- The place where **lies root** and **wounds store** (Prov 4:23, Mark 7:21)

That's the biblical warrant for working beneath the surface. CBT-coaching-Scripture integration isn't a compromise — it's convergence. The subconscious is not a secular concept imposed onto Scripture; it is a modern name for what Scripture has always called the deep heart.
