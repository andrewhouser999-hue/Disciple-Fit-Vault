# Client-Facing Questions — Diagnostic Intake Form

*Category labels are for coach reference only — client never sees them.*

*Related foundational file: [[Comprehensive Discipleship Markers]]*

---
What Makes This Compelling

  It shifts the intake away from "what do you want to work on" toward "who are you actually." Most clients come in with
  presenting problems (anxiety, marriage conflict, career stuck). This framework pushes beneath that into identity,
  belief, and spiritual formation — which is exactly where your CBC model operates.

  It functions as a diagnostic map, not just a checklist. If a client scores high on Obedience but low on Identity in
  Christ, that's a specific profile — likely behavior-based faith, performance orientation, possible shame driver
  underneath. That's actionable on day one.

  It creates client ownership before session one. Self-reflection on these categories primes the client for depth work.
  They arrive already thinking below the surface.
---

## Coach Framing (deliver before client completes form)

> "Before we meet, I want to understand where you actually are — not where you think you should be. These questions aren't a spiritual checklist. There are no right answers and I'm not grading anything. The more honest you are, the more useful our time together will be. Take your time with each one."

## Written Pre-Session Questions
*Client completes these before Session 1. Coach reviews before the session.*

---

### Identity in Christ

**1.** When you fail or disappoint someone important to you, what's the first thing you tell yourself?

**2.** Where in your life do you feel most like you have to earn something — from God, others, or yourself?

**3.** On a scale of 1–10, how settled do you feel in who you are regardless of your performance? What would a 10 feel like for you?

*Coach lens: Shame narrative, performance orientation, functional identity source.*

---

### Emotional & Psychological Integration

**4.** Is there an area of your life where you know the right answer but still can't seem to feel it?

**5.** When something painful happens, what do you usually do with it?

*Coach lens: Belief-emotion gap, suppression vs. processing, interior/exterior match.*

---

### Suffering & Perseverance

**6.** Describe a time when your faith was genuinely hard to hold onto. What happened to it?

**7.** Is there anything that happened to you that still affects how you see God?

*Coach lens: Wound-to-God-image distortion, tested vs. untested faith, abandonment belief.*

---

### Faith & Trust

**8.** What's the area of your life where trusting God is hardest right now?

**9.** On a scale of 1–10, how confident are you that God is actively working in your life right now? What's keeping it from being higher?

*Coach lens: Domain-specific practical atheism, stated vs. behavioral theology.*

---

### Humility & Character

**10.** Is there an area of your life where you suspect you have a blind spot but haven't fully looked at it yet?

---

### Open Close

**11.** Is there anything you want your coach to know before your first session that the questions didn't cover?

---

## Verbal / In-Session Questions
*Reserve these for Session 1 debrief and Sessions 2–6 as trust builds.*

---

### Repentance & Sanctification

**12.** After a significant failure or sin, how long does it typically take to feel right with God again — and what does that process look like for you?

**13.** Is there something you keep bringing back to God that never feels fully resolved?

*Coach lens: Shame cycle depth, functional belief in forgiveness, grace vs. earning posture.*

---

### Faith & Trust

**14.** When you're anxious or afraid, what do you actually do — not what you think you should do?

*Coach lens: Behavioral tell for where trust actually lands.*

---

### Love & Relationships

**15.** Is there anyone in your life you find it genuinely hard to wish well?

**16.** When conflict arises in an important relationship, what's your first instinct — address it, withdraw, or something else?

**17.** Is there a relationship that feels unresolved or stuck that affects you more than you'd like?

*Coach lens: Unforgiveness depth, conflict posture, relational wound terrain.*

---

### Humility & Character

**18.** When someone gives you critical feedback, what usually happens inside you first — before you respond?

*Coach lens: Defensiveness structure, shame-driven pride, teachability ceiling.*

---

## Coach Observation Notes
*What to watch for when reviewing written responses and during verbal questioning:*

- Questions they answered quickly vs. where they paused, hedged, or gave short answers
- Scaling scores that are surprisingly high or low
- Questions they reframed or avoided answering directly
- Emotional tone shifts — those mark the live terrain
- The gap between stated belief and described behavior

## Diagnostic Sequence (Root to Fruit)

> Identity → Integration → Repentance pattern → Suffering history → Trust gap → Relational patterns → Teachability

---

## Highest-Diagnostic-Value Categories

*Criteria: Reveals underlying structure (lies, wounds, identity distortions) — not just behavior. Hard to perform consistently. Maps directly to coachable root issues.*

---

### Tier 1 — Root System (Highest Yield)

**Identity in Christ**
Almost every other category is downstream of this one. How a person defines themselves under pressure — in failure, conflict, shame — tells you where their actual foundation is. Performance orientation, shame cycles, approval-seeking all surface here. This is the first thing you need to know.

**Emotional & Psychological Integration**
Interior/exterior match is one of the most revealing diagnostics available. Does their stated belief match their emotional experience? Are wounds suppressed or being processed? Suppression looks like stability until it doesn't. This maps directly to the CBC model and reveals what's actually happening beneath the presenting problem.

---

### Tier 2 — Structural Reveals (High Yield)

**Repentance & Sanctification**
Reveals the shame vs. grace relationship more than it reveals holiness. Shame-driven clients spiral after failure. Grace-thin clients minimize sin. Both distortions are visible here. The pattern of repentance — not just whether they repent — is the diagnostic.

**Suffering & Perseverance**
Past suffering and how they processed it is the most reliable predictor of wound depth and God-image distortion. Bitterness, abandonment beliefs, collapse under pressure — all surface when you ask about this. Also reveals whether their faith has been tested at all.

**Faith & Trust**
The gap between stated theology and behavioral trust under pressure. A person can confess "God is sovereign" and functionally live as a practical atheist when circumstances deteriorate. That gap is where the lie lives.

---

### Tier 3 — Relational & Character Reveals (Strong Yield)

**Love & Relationships**
Loving difficult people, forgiveness patterns, and reconciliation behavior reveal heart condition in ways that private spiritual disciplines don't. Avoidance of conflict, chronic unforgiveness, or relational isolation all point to deeper wound terrain.

**Humility & Character**
Teachability and response to correction reveal defensiveness structures, shame-driven pride, and self-protective systems. How someone receives feedback — from the coach, from God, from others — will determine how much movement is possible.

---

### Why the Others Drop in Priority

| Category | Why Lower |
|---|---|
| Word & Truth | Easily performed; behavior doesn't confirm transformation |
| Prayer | Highly private; self-report unreliable without probing |
| Obedience | Downstream of identity and trust — fix those first |
| Community | Compliance is performable; isolation is the real flag to catch elsewhere |
| Service | Easiest category to perform |
| Mission | Downstream of identity and love |
| Spiritual Warfare | Overclaimed or underclaimed; rarely the entry point |
| Stewardship | Situational; high value only for specific presenting issues |
