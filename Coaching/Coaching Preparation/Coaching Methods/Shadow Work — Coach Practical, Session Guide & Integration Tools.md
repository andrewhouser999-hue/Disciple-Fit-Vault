# Shadow Work — Coach Practical: Session Guide & Integration Tools

**Purpose:** Practical guide for applying Jungian shadow concepts in Disciple Fit coaching sessions. Integrates with Parable of the Soils, Identity Installer, and Lie Eliminator. Theory and sourcing in [[Jungian Shadow Work — Integration Theory for Biblical Coaching]].

---

## Scope & Limits

**Use this when:** Client shows projection patterns, persistent triggered reactions, strong idealizations, inability to receive identity, or acting-out episodes they cannot explain.

**Do not use this for:** Active trauma processing, clients in crisis, or clients with dissociative patterns. Shadow awareness in coaching stays at the level of identity and self-concept — not deep unconscious excavation.

---

## Diagnostic Signals — Recognize Shadow Activity

| Signal | What It Sounds Like | Shadow Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| High-charge judgment | *"I can't stand people who are so needy"* (with heat) | Projection of disowned need |
| Unexplained self-sabotage | *"I don't know why I did that — it's not like me"* | Shadow acting out |
| Intense idealization | *"She's just so fearless — I could never be like that"* | Golden shadow projection |
| Identity rejection | *"People say I'm gifted at this but I just don't see it"* | Golden shadow / imago Dei not owned |
| Moral rigidity | *"I would NEVER do something like that"* (said with force) | Shadow compensatory posture |
| Repetitive relational pattern | Same conflict across multiple relationships, different people | Shadow projected onto successive targets |
| Disproportionate reaction | *"I know it's small but I just lost it"* | Shadow material was contacted |

**Diagnostic rule:** Emotional charge disproportionate to the situation = shadow proximity. The hotter the reaction, the closer the shadow.

## Soils x Shadow Map

The Parable of the Soils describes the internal terrain that determines receptivity. Shadow dynamics operate differently in each soil type.

### Hard Soil (Path)
**Shadow pattern:** Maximum suppression. The hardened surface has formed over years of trampling down rejected material — the crust *is* the shadow defense.
**What it looks like:** Heavy, rigid, defended projection. Moral certainty. Quick to judge others. Will not be confronted.
**Coach move:** Don't attack the surface. Ask what the hardness is protecting.
> *"When did trusting someone with this part of you stop feeling safe?"*

The softening must come before the shadow work. Hard-soil clients read confrontation as attack — entry is through relationship, not confrontation.

---

### Rocky Soil (Shallow Depth)
**Shadow pattern:** Material is close to the surface but unrooted — no integration depth. Enthusiasm is high; depth is avoided because it's threatening.
**What it looks like:** Strong idealizations (golden shadow heavy). Quick emotional responses that don't last. Inspiration-to-crash cycles.
**Coach move:** The golden shadow is the entry point. The rocky-soil client *wants* to be the person they admire — use that pull.
> *"What is it about them that you most want for yourself? What would have to be true about you for that to be possible?"*

Depth before dark-shadow confrontation. Don't excavate what the soil can't yet hold.

---

### Thorny Soil (Cares of the World)
**Shadow pattern:** Shadow drives are in active operation — they *are* the thorns. Worry, status-seeking, approval-need, financial anxiety = shadow desires running while the conscious self claims spiritual priorities.
**What it looks like:** Competing motivations the client can't understand. *"I want to trust God but I can't stop worrying."* The worry is shadow. What's being protected by the worry (control, approval, safety) is the disowned desire.
**Coach move:** Name the thorn as a driver, not just a problem.
> *"What does the worry do for you? What would be true if you weren't worried? What would you have to risk?"*

The shadow desire comes into view through function, not confrontation.

---

### Good Soil
**Shadow pattern:** Present, but held without identity collapse. Narrative flexibility — can acknowledge shadow without losing sense of self.
**Coach move:** Deepen integration. Use the full projection inventory. Connect shadow to Kingdom stewardship. The golden shadow is particularly fruitful — what gifts has the client been projecting onto others that belong to them?

All interventions below are available here.

## Identity Installer x Shadow

The Installer's 7-part format maps directly onto shadow integration.

| Installer Component | Shadow Function |
|---|---|
| **Identity Statement** | The integrated identity — what the shadow is being integrated *toward* |
| **Anchor Verse** | Theological grounding that holds the integration |
| **My Evidence** | Golden shadow recovery — listing actual evidence disrupts the projection |
| **What I'm Putting Off** | Shadow content named explicitly — the disowned self, named and set aside |
| **What I'm Putting On** | The integration move — not suppression but conscious replacement |
| **The Felt Sense** | Somatic integration — what does it feel like in the body to own this? |
| **Today I Will** | Behavioral integration — how does owning this change what I do today? |

**In-session application:** When a client cannot receive an identity statement (golden shadow signal), walk through the Installer live. The "My Evidence" step often surfaces exactly what the client has been projecting onto others.

**Coach prompt:**
> *"You said you don't believe you're [quality]. Let's look at your evidence. Not what you think should be there — what actually is there. What have you done, said, or chosen that someone with [quality] would do?"*

The evidence breaks the projection. The golden shadow begins to be recalled.

## Lie Eliminator x Shadow

Lies the client believes about themselves almost always have a shadow origin. Three roots:

**1. Projection internalized** — What significant others saw in us, we eventually agreed with. The parent who called a child selfish created a shadow-level belief ("I am selfish") that the Lie Eliminator targets directly.

**2. Shadow material self-condemned** — The client witnessed their own darkness (rage, desire, envy) and concluded *"I am that thing"* rather than *"I contain that thing."* The lie is built from unintegrated shadow content.

**3. Golden shadow denial** — The lie *"I'm not gifted / capable / loved"* is often the dark side of refusing to claim what God placed in the client. They cannot own the good, so they build a lie to explain the absence.

**In-session connection:** When working a lie with the Eliminator, add:
> *"Where did you first learn this lie? Who taught it to you — or what experience 'proved' it? Is there a part of you that agrees with it even now? What does agreeing with it get you?"*

The answer to the last question reveals the shadow function: safety, humility-performance, avoiding the risk of being seen.

## Session Moves — Core Interventions

### 1. The Charge Test
When a client makes a charged judgment about someone:
> *"How much charge is on that — 1 to 10? [Anything over 6:] When the charge is that high, it's worth asking — is there a version of that quality in you? Not the same form — something adjacent or related?"*

### 2. The Projection Inventory
When there's a recurring conflict or persistent judgment pattern:
> *"Tell me about three people who frustrate or irritate you most. What's the quality in each one? ... Now — is there any world in which any version of that quality lives in you? Even small? Even in a form you consider more acceptable?"*

### 3. Golden Shadow Recovery
When a client idealizes someone or dismisses their own gifts:
> *"You described [person] as [quality]. What would it mean about you if you had that same quality? What evidence would you need to see to start believing it was actually in you?"*

### 4. The Shadow Behind the Lie
When working the Lie Eliminator:
> *"Before we replace this lie — let's understand it first. What does believing this lie do for you? What does it protect you from? What would you have to risk if it weren't true?"*

### 5. Naming Without Merging
When a client is flooded or reports acting out:
> *"That part of you that [acted out] — what was it trying to do? What did it need? You are not that part, but it is a part of you. What does it need to hear?"*

## Sample Session Dialogue

### Projection to Integration

**Client:** *"My coworker is just so arrogant — acts like he's the smartest person in the room and everyone else is barely worth his time."*

**Coach:** *"How much charge is on that for you — 1 to 10?"*

**Client:** *"Like an 8 or 9 honestly. It really gets to me."*

**Coach:** *"That intensity is worth paying attention to. I want to offer something — you can push back on it: is there any version of that quality — what you just described — that might exist in you? Not arrogance exactly. Maybe a private sense of being sharper than others. Impatience with people who don't get things quickly."*

**Client:** *"... I mean. Maybe. I do get frustrated when people miss what seems obvious to me."*

**Coach:** *"What's it like to say that out loud?"*

**Client:** *"Uncomfortable."*

**Coach:** *"Yeah. That discomfort is the shadow being seen. You don't have to become your coworker — owning this doesn't make you him. But what might be available to you if that part wasn't running underground?"*

---

### Golden Shadow — Identity Installer Entry

**Client:** *"My mentor is so clear on who she is. She doesn't apologize for her gifts. I've never been able to be like that."*

**Coach:** *"If you had to name the one specific quality — what is it?"*

**Client:** *"She just owns it. She knows she has something to offer and she doesn't shrink."*

**Coach:** *"Okay. I'm going to make a claim about you, and I want you to notice your reaction — not respond yet, just notice. [Pause.] You have something to offer and you are not required to shrink."*

**Client:** *"[Long pause.] I want to believe that."*

**Coach:** *"What gets in the way?"*

*[Moves into the Installer — My Evidence step to surface actual evidence against the projection.]*

---

### Shadow Behind a Lie

**Client:** *"I know I'm supposed to believe God uses me, but I just don't think I have what it takes."*

**Coach:** *"Before we go after the lie — I want to understand it first. What does believing that do for you? If you believed the opposite — that you do have what it takes — what would you have to risk?"*

**Client:** *"... I'd have to actually try. And then maybe fail for real."*

**Coach:** *"So the lie is protecting you from the risk of a real attempt. That's not weakness — that's the shadow doing its job. It thinks it's keeping you safe. What would it mean to tell that part of you: I see what you're doing, and I don't need that protection anymore?"*

## Holy Spirit Integration

Shadow work in Kingdom coaching is never only psychological — it is spiritual. Invite the Holy Spirit as the primary integration agent.

**Psalm 139:23–24 as the frame:**
> *"God, search me. Know my heart. See if there is any offensive way in me."*

This is shadow work as prayer — voluntary exposure of unconscious material to the safest possible witness.

**In-session invite:**
> *"Before we go further — would you be open to asking the Holy Spirit to surface whatever needs to come up here? Not to expose you in a painful way, but because He knows you better than you know yourself — and He's safer than anyone."*

**The Spirit's role in integration:**
- Conviction (not condemnation) — He names the shadow accurately and with mercy
- Recall — He can surface what the ego has buried (John 14:26)
- Power — integration is not willpower; it is the Spirit renewing the mind (Romans 12:2)

**The distinction to protect:** Shadow work is not deliverance ministry. Don't conflate the psychological shadow (flesh/old man) with demonic presence. If something looks like spiritual oppression rather than unconscious self-concept, adjust accordingly. Most shadow work is anthropological, not spiritual warfare.

## Multi-Session Integration Arc

| Session | Focus |
|---|---|
| **1** | Psychoeducation: normalize the shadow concept using Romans 7 and Matthew 7:3–5. Introduce without making it threatening. Gauge client's receptivity. |
| **2** | Projection inventory: identify 2–3 high-charge judgments. Run the charge test. Surface the first shadow candidate. |
| **3** | Golden shadow: identify idealization patterns. Begin evidence inventory for the quality being projected outward. |
| **4** | Lie Eliminator entry: connect a core lie to its shadow root. Run the full Installer on one identity category. |
| **5+** | Deepening: continue projection-to-integration on specific relational patterns. Reinforce the Felt Sense of integration. Connect to behavioral change. |

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't push into shadow before trust is established** — premature confrontation produces defensiveness, not integration. Relationship is the container.
- **Don't frame the shadow as sinful** — it is suppressed material, not sin itself. It may contain sinful impulses, but the dynamic is psychological before it is moral.
- **Don't skip the Holy Spirit** — psychological shadow work without the Spirit becomes therapeutic navel-gazing. He is not an add-on.
- **Don't merge the client with their shadow** — *"You have this quality"* not *"You ARE this quality."* The container matters enormously.
- **Don't stay in the shadow** — always move toward integration and identity. Shadow work is diagnostic and access work, not the destination. The destination is the Installer — the renewed identity in Christ.
