# Comprehensive Discipleship Markers

*Foundational reference for coaching diagnostics, intake evaluation, and session focus.*

---

## 1. Word & Truth
- Regular, personal Bible reading (not just sermon consumption)
- Scripture memorization and retention
- Ability to interpret Scripture in context (not proof-texting)
- Doctrine is personally owned, not just inherited
- Truth shapes decision-making, not just belief statements
- Responds to correction from Scripture without defensiveness

## 2. Prayer & Communion with God
- Consistent private prayer life (not only crisis prayer)
- Listens in prayer, not just talks
- Prays with faith and expectation
- Intercedes for others regularly
- Prayer is conversational, not formulaic
- Engages in lament without abandoning God

## 3. Identity in Christ
- Self-definition is rooted in who God says they are
- Shame and accusation lose grip over time
- Doesn't perform for God's approval
- Receives love without deflecting it
- Identity holds under pressure and failure
- Distinguishes conviction from condemnation

## 4. Repentance & Sanctification
- Pattern of genuine, specific repentance (not vague guilt)
- Sins are decreasing in frequency and duration
- Self-awareness of besetting sins grows
- Takes responsibility without self-condemnation spirals
- Repentance leads to restoration, not shame cycles
- Holiness is desired, not just obligatory

## 5. Obedience & Surrender
- Acts on what they know to be true
- Delayed obedience is recognized as disobedience
- Willingness to obey in costly areas (money, sex, relationships, ambition)
- Surrenders outcomes they cannot control
- Doesn't negotiate with clear commands

## 6. Faith & Trust
- Trusts God's character when circumstances are hard
- Faith is tested and survives — not just untested
- Theological belief translates into behavioral trust
- Anxiety decreases over time in God's care
- Waits on God without bitterness

## 7. Love & Relationships
- Love for God is affectionate, not merely dutiful
- Loves enemies (even mildly — difficult people, critics)
- Pursues reconciliation rather than avoidance
- Forgiveness is given and received, not withheld
- Does not use grace as a license or weapon
- Relationships mark them as a Christian (John 13:35)

## 8. Community & Church
- Committed to a local church body, not just Christianity in general
- Submitted to spiritual authority (not blindly, but genuinely)
- Contributes to community rather than only consuming it
- Known and knowing — not isolated
- Conflict is addressed, not avoided
- Bearing one another's burdens is practiced

## 9. Service & Sacrifice
- Serves without need for recognition
- Gives financially — and it costs something
- Uses gifts for others' benefit, not personal platform
- Willing to be inconvenienced for others
- Lays down preferences for the sake of unity

## 10. Mission & Witness
- Talks about Jesus naturally, not only in structured settings
- Prayerfully engaged with the lost
- Life provokes questions from unbelievers
- Shares the gospel, not only moral advice
- Disciple-making is a priority, not just attendance

## 11. Suffering & Perseverance
- Faith survives suffering without collapse
- Does not interpret suffering as divine abandonment
- Suffering produces growth, not just bitterness
- Endurance is visible over time, not just in crisis
- Holds eschatological hope as a live category

## 12. Humility & Character
- Receives feedback without retaliation or shutdown
- Does not overestimate or underestimate themselves
- Intellectual humility — holds opinions loosely where Scripture is silent
- Teachable posture toward mentors and peers
- Tracks with Paul's pattern: "I have learned" (Phil. 4:11)

## 13. Spiritual Warfare Awareness
- Recognizes enemy schemes without paranoia
- Uses Scripture as a weapon (not just comfort)
- Armor of God is a lived framework, not a metaphor only
- Intercedes in spiritual battles for others
- Resists the devil — doesn't just endure him

## 14. Emotional & Psychological Integration
- Emotions are processed with God, not suppressed
- Grief, fear, and anger are sanctified, not buried
- Interior life matches exterior presentation
- Wounds are being healed, not just managed
- Formed by the Spirit from the inside out, not behavior modification

## 15. Stewardship & Vocation
- Time, money, and gifts are treated as entrusted, not owned
- Work is seen as worship and mission
- Seeks to glorify God in ordinary, unglamorous contexts
- Avoids idolizing or demonizing career/money
- Fruitfulness in assigned sphere, not just in "ministry" settings

---

**Meta-marker:** The trajectory is growth. Not perfection at any point — but measurable, honest movement toward Christlikeness over time (Col. 1:28).
