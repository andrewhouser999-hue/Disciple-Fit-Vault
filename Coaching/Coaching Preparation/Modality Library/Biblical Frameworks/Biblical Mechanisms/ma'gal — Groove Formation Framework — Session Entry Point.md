# ma'gal — Groove Formation Framework
## Session Entry Point: Patterns, Schemas & Mind Renewal

**Tags:** `[BIBLE-FRAMEWORK]` `[BIBLE-SESSION]` `[SESSION-TOOL]`
**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Connects to:** Schema Therapy (Young), CBT Automatic Thoughts, Immunity to Change, ACT Defusion, Narrative Re-Authoring, Parable of the Soils, Romans 12:2, Holy Spirit as Formation Agent

---

## What This Is

A three-phase Biblical Framework for working with entrenched behavioral and cognitive patterns. Built on the Hebrew word *ma'gal* (מַעְגָּל — wagon rut), it gives clients an embodied, scripturally grounded image for understanding how patterns form, persist, and can be changed. Functions as a session entry point when a client presents with a pattern they feel stuck in, a behavior they repeat without knowing why, or a response that fires before they can think. Integrates Schema Therapy, CBT automatic thoughts, Immunity to Change, ACT defusion, and narrative re-authoring under a single biblical mechanism.

**Who it's for:** Clients who are stuck in recurring patterns — relational, emotional, behavioral, or cognitive — especially when the pattern has gone below conscious awareness ("I've always just been like this").

**How to use it:** Introduce the ma'gal image as a shared framework early in the session. Move through the three phases sequentially. The framework can span one session or be spread across two to three depending on depth.

## Biblical Foundation (Summary)

> *"Ponder the path of your feet; then all your ways will be sure."* — Proverbs 4:26 (ESV)
> *"She does not ponder the path of life; her ways wander, and she does not know it."* — Proverbs 5:6 (ESV)
> *"He leads me in paths of righteousness for his name's sake."* — Psalm 23:3 (ESV)

**ma'gal** (מַעְגָּל) — the groove worn into a road by the repeated passage of a wagon wheel. Not merely a path (*derek*) or a track (*orach*) — specifically the rut that forms *because something has passed there many times.* The groove exists as the record of a pattern. Once deep enough, the wheel no longer needs a driver — it falls into the groove and follows it.

**Three biblical movements underlying the framework:**

1. **Grooves form by repetition** (Proverbs 4:23–27) — The heart, speech, gaze, and path form a sequence. What the heart repeatedly attends to produces the groove the life follows.

2. **Grooves can go below awareness** (Proverbs 5:5–6) — *"She does not know it."* The groove has become so deep that conscious deliberation has dropped out. The rut now makes choices the person no longer consciously makes.

3. **Grooves can be re-formed under a shepherd** (Psalm 23:3) — The shepherd leads into new *ma'galot* of righteousness. Re-grooving is not willpower — it is being *led* into new tracks by deliberate, repeated practice under divine guidance.

**The critical verb — *palles* (פלס):** Proverbs 4:26 — *"Ponder/weigh/level the ma'gal of your feet."* From the root of *moznayim* (scales). The instruction is to *deliberately examine and weigh* the groove being formed. This is the biblical command for meta-cognition — the act of stepping back and observing the rut rather than simply running in it.

*Full exegesis: see A-ma'gal — Hebrew Word Study, Thought Ruts & Mind Renewal.md*

**Integration logic:** The schema is the *source* of the groove — the early maladaptive belief that first carved the track. The automatic thought is the *cognitive content* running in the groove. Naming these layers in sequence — from surface behavior to schema root to identity story — is the full diagnostic arc of Phase 2.

## THE THREE-PHASE FRAMEWORK

---

### PHASE 1 — Groove Identification
*Surface which ma'galot are running — including below-awareness ones*

**Goal:** Name the groove before examining it. Clients cannot do *palles* work on a groove they cannot yet see.

**Entry image (coach delivers before questions begin):**

> *"There's a Hebrew word in the book of Proverbs — ma'gal. It means wagon rut. Not just a path, but the groove worn into a road by a wagon wheel going the same way hundreds of times. Once a groove gets deep enough, the wheel doesn't need a driver. It just falls in and follows the track. The Bible uses that word to describe what happens with our thought patterns and habits — the same way that groove forms in the road, it forms in us. What we're going to do today is look at what grooves might be running in this area of your life."*

**Diagnostic Questions — Conscious Grooves:**
- *"When [triggering situation] happens, what do you automatically do? Feel? Tell yourself?"*
- *"How long have you been responding this way?"*
- *"Is there a time you can remember when you didn't respond this way? What was different?"*
- *"Does this feel like a choice you're making — or does it just happen?"*

**Diagnostic Questions — Below-Awareness Grooves** *(use when client says "I don't know" or "I've always been like this"):*
- *"If your closest friend were watching this pattern from the outside, what would they say you do every time?"*
- *"What's the feeling that comes right before the behavior — even if it's subtle?"*
- *"If there were a thought running in the background when this happens — a thought you might not even notice — what might it be saying?"*
- *"What does this pattern protect you from?"*
- *"What would be at risk if you stopped doing this?"*

**"She Does Not Know It" Protocol** *(Proverbs 5:6 — when the groove is fully below awareness):*

Not all clients can surface the groove in a single session. Signs the groove is below awareness:
- "I don't know why I do it" + no emotional access
- Pattern is reported by others but not felt internally
- Client defends the pattern as "just who I am"

When this is present: **Do not force Phase 2.** Instead:
1. Name what you're observing without interpretation: *"I notice that when we talk about X, [behavior/topic shift] tends to happen. I'm not saying that's wrong — I'm just curious about it."*
2. Assign between-session observation: *"This week, when you notice [triggering situation], just observe what happens — what you do, what you feel. Don't try to change it yet. Just watch."*
3. Return to Phase 1 next session with the observation data.

**Phase 1 Output:** A named groove — stated in the client's language. Something like: *"When I feel criticized, I shut down and disappear."* Or: *"Whenever I get close to someone, I start looking for the exit."* This named groove becomes the working unit for Phase 2.

### PHASE 2 — The Palles Protocol
*Deliberately weigh the groove: origin, depth, and cost*

**Goal:** Help the client examine the groove with precision — not to shame it, but to understand it. *Palles* means to weigh on scales — every dimension matters.

#### 2A — Origin: Where Was This Groove First Cut?

*"The first wagon didn't carve the rut alone. Something started this. Let's look at where this groove first formed."*

**Questions:**
- *"When do you think you first learned to respond this way?"*
- *"What was happening in your life when this pattern began to make sense — when it was the right response for that situation?"*
- *"What need was this groove originally serving? What was it trying to protect?"*
- *"If this groove could speak, what would it say it's been trying to do for you all these years?"*

**Schema integration here:** The origin question is where Early Maladaptive Schemas (Young) surface. Common schema-groove connections:

| Schema (EMS) | Typical Groove |
|---|---|
| Abandonment/Instability | Clings or preemptively exits relationships |
| Defectiveness/Shame | Hides, performs, or collapses under criticism |
| Mistrust/Abuse | Hypervigilant, withholds, tests people |
| Emotional Deprivation | Over-gives to earn care or stops expecting it |
| Subjugation | Complies externally, resents internally |
| Unrelenting Standards | Drives relentlessly, never rests or celebrates |
| Entitlement/Grandiosity | Bypasses limits, rages when blocked |

**Coach-safe scope:** Identify which schema may be the groove's root. Name it without clinical labeling. Do not do schema imagery or origin trauma processing — refer if trauma is present.

#### 2B — Depth: How Automatic Has This Become?

*"The deeper the rut, the less the wheel chooses it. Let's look at how deep this one runs."*

**Questions:**
- *"On a scale of 1–10, how much does this pattern fire before you're even aware it's happening?"*
- *"What triggers it? Is it a feeling, a situation, a specific kind of person?"*
- *"What happens in your body right before or during this pattern?"* *(somatic awareness)*
- *"How many seconds between the trigger and the response — is there any gap at all?"*

**CBT integration here:** The depth question maps directly onto automatic thought speed and automaticity. A groove running at 9–10 depth has essentially no cognitive gap between trigger and response. A groove at 4–6 has some awareness but still fires mostly automatically.

**Immunity to Change integration:** When a client scores the depth high *and* has tried to change it without success, this is the signal for the big assumption question:
- *"You've tried to stop this before. What happens — what's the pull back into the groove?"*
- *"What part of you believes you need this groove? What would happen if it were gone?"*
- The competing commitment and big assumption live here. Name it: *"It sounds like part of you is still protecting something — and that protection is keeping the groove in place."*

#### 2C — Cost: What Is This Groove Producing?

*"Every groove takes you somewhere. Let's look at where this one has been taking you."*

**Questions:**
- *"What has this pattern cost you — in relationships, in your faith, in how you see yourself?"*
- *"How does this groove interact with the person you want to become?"*
- *"If this groove ran unchallenged for the next ten years, where does it take you?"*
- *"What is God's assessment of this groove, as best you understand it?"*

**Motivational Interviewing principle here:** Developing discrepancy. The goal is not shame — it is honest acknowledgment that the groove is taking the client somewhere they don't want to go. The client must feel the cost before they can engage the re-grooving work. Do not rush past this.

**ACT defusion integration:** At this point the narrative fused with the groove often becomes audible:
- *"I'm just not a person who can ___."*
- *"This is just how I am."*
- *"People like me don't change."*

When this appears: *"I want to name something. There's a thought running here — [repeat the client's language]. That thought is itself a groove. It's been going around long enough that it feels like a fact. What if we held it as a thought for a moment rather than a truth?"*

**Phase 2 Output:** A three-dimensional picture of the groove: its origin (what schema or wound first carved it), its depth (how automatic it has become), and its cost (what it is producing that is contrary to the client's desired direction). This picture is the working material for Phase 3.

---

### PHASE 3 — The Re-Grooving Pathway
*Shepherd-led formation of new tracks*

**Goal:** Design and begin the new groove. The theological frame is critical here: the sheep do not re-groove themselves. The shepherd leads into new *ma'galot* (Psalm 23:3). Re-grooving is not willpower — it is structured, repeated practice under Spirit-led guidance. The effort is real; the agency is shared.

**Entry (coach delivers before design work):**

> *"In Psalm 23, the shepherd doesn't just forgive the sheep for being in the wrong pasture. He leads them into new paths — new grooves. The old track didn't form overnight, and the new one won't either. But a groove that used to pull toward death can be replaced by one that runs toward life. That's not a metaphor — that's how you're actually built. We're going to design the new groove and figure out what it takes to start running it."*

Note: grooves from illusionary resting grounds to the poisoned pond and back to illusionary rest to the poison pond, over and over. New paths of peace from the pond of truth & life to full resting grounds.

#### 3A — New Groove Design

**Questions:**
- *"What would the new pattern look like? When [trigger] happens, what would you want to do/think/feel instead?"*
- *"What does this new groove feel like in your body?"*
- *"Who do you know who runs a groove like this? What does it look like in them?"*
- *"What would it say about you — about who you are — if this new pattern were running in you?"*

**Narrative re-authoring integration:** The new groove requires a new identity story. White & Epston's *unique outcomes* method is useful here — looking for times the client already ran a version of the new groove:
- *"Has there ever been a moment — even a small one — when you responded this way instead? What was happening? What was different about you in that moment?"*
- *"That moment — that's not an exception. That's evidence of who you already are becoming. What do we call that version of you?"*

The new groove must be given a name and anchored to identity, not just behavior.

#### 3B — Repetition Strategy

*"A new groove only forms by going the same new way many times. The shepherd leads — but the sheep still walk."*

**Design the practice:**
- *"What specific, small thing would you do differently the next time [trigger] happens?"*
- *"What would need to be true about your environment or routine for this new response to have a chance?"*
- *"How many times per day or week does this trigger appear? That's how many reps the new groove gets."*
- *"What will you do when you fall back into the old groove — because you will, at first?"*

**Behavioral design principles:**
- New groove must be *specific* (not "be more patient" but "when I feel criticized, I will say 'give me a moment' before I respond")
- New groove must be *repeated* — frequency matters more than intensity
- New groove must be *low enough threshold* to succeed — too ambitious and it collapses; small and specific builds depth
- Expect the old groove to reassert — this is not failure, it is groove physics. The old rut will pull until the new one is deeper

**Biblical practice anchors (shepherd tools):**
- Scripture declaration: a verse that speaks the new groove's identity truth, reviewed daily
- Prayer: *"Lord, lead me in paths of righteousness"* as a daily-submitted posture
- Community: who in the client's life can reinforce the new groove? Grooves deepen in relationship

#### 3C — Shepherd Dependency Frame

*"The deepest grooves of righteousness are formed by being led, not just by trying harder."*

**Questions:**
- *"What is your current practice of being led? How are you staying connected to the shepherd who leads you in new paths?"*
- *"What would it look like to surrender this groove actively to God — to ask Him to lead you into new tracks rather than just trying to change on your own?"*
- *"What is the Holy Spirit's role in this new groove? How does that change your approach to the practice?"*

**Theological anchor:** Romans 12:2 — *"Be transformed by the renewal of your mind."* The verb *metamorphoō* is passive in construction — you are being transformed, not transforming yourself. The deliberate practice is real effort; the agency belongs to the Spirit. Both are true simultaneously.

**Phase 3 Output:** A named new groove, a specific behavioral practice for beginning to run it, and a clear shepherd-dependency frame. Client leaves with something to *do* and someone to *follow.*

## Full Session Flow

| Phase | Duration | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry** | 5–10 min | Introduce ma'gal image; identify the presenting pattern |
| **Phase 1 — Identification** | 15–20 min | Name the groove; surface below-awareness material if needed |
| **Phase 2 — Palles** | 15–20 min | Origin / Depth / Cost |
| **Phase 3 — Re-Grooving** | 10–15 min | Design new groove / Practice / Shepherd frame |
| **Close** | 5 min | Assign between-session practice; prayer if appropriate |

**Multi-session option:** Phase 2 (especially origin work) may warrant a full session on its own when schema-level material is present. Do not rush to re-grooving before the groove is fully understood — premature behavior change without root examination produces surface compliance, not formation.

## Contraindications & Scope Notes

**Stay in coaching scope:**
- Schema *identification* is coach-safe. Schema imagery, origin trauma processing, and limited reparenting work are clinical — refer if the origin work touches significant trauma.
- "She does not know it" states require patience — do not force *palles* work before the client can see the groove. Use the below-awareness protocol and return.
- If the groove traces to abuse, complex trauma, or dissociative patterns — refer. Coach can work on present behavioral new groove design without touching the wound's origin.

**Watch for:**
- Clients who intellectualize the framework without feeling the cost (Phase 2C) — the cognitive insight without emotional engagement will not produce re-grooving. Slow down; stay in the body.
- Clients who jump to re-grooving to avoid examining the groove — the shepherd frame can become a bypass. "Let God fix it" without *palles* work is not transformation; it's avoidance with religious language.
- Clients whose groove is actually schema-level rigidity — the four-column Immunity to Change map may be needed before re-grooving is possible. The big assumption must be surfaced and tested first.

| Framework | Integration Point |
|---|---|
| Parable of the Soils | Soil type shapes groove depth and re-grooving capacity. Rocky soil (shallow root) will struggle with Phase 3 practice — the new groove does not have enough depth of Spirit-connection to sustain. Thorny soil may identify the groove but be immediately choked by competing desires. |
| Greatest Commandment | The ultimate question for Phase 2C: *"Is this groove serving love of God and neighbor — or is it serving something else?"* Provides the telos against which the groove is measured. |
