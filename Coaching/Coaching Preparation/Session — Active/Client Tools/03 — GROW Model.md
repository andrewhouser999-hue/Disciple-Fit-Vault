# GROW Model
*Coach use: Session structure | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Structure a complete coaching session or conversation from goal to committed action. The backbone framework for any issue-focused session.

## Modality
Classic coaching (Whitmore; Downey)

## When to Use
Any issue requiring a structured session — direction, decision-making, action planning, calling, conflict, behavioral change. This is the default session container.

## Coach's In-Session Guide

**G — Goal**
What does the client want from this session? What do they want to achieve more broadly?
- Keep this distinct: *session goal* ("what do you want from today?") vs. *topic goal* ("what outcome do you want in this area of life?")
- Spend time here — a vague goal produces a vague session.

**R — Reality**
Where are they now? What is actually happening?
- Resist the urge to jump to options. The reality phase surfaces facts, patterns, and feelings that reframe the goal.
- Ask for specifics — scale ratings, concrete examples, what they've already tried.

**O — Options**
What could they do? Generate broadly before evaluating.
- Aim for 5–7 options minimum before assessing any of them.
- The coach's job is to keep opening, not to suggest. Ask "What else?" repeatedly.
- Only after generation: "Which of these feel most alive to you?"

**W — Way Forward**
What will they do? By when? What might get in the way?
- Must produce a specific, time-bound commitment — not an intention.
- Accountability: How will you know you did it? Who will you tell?

## Key Questions / Prompts

*Goal:* "What do you want to get from today's session?" / "What would make this a great use of our time?"

*Reality:* "What's actually happening?" / "What have you already tried?" / "On a scale of 1–10, where are you with this?"

*Options:* "What could you do?" / "What else?" / "What would you do if you knew you couldn't fail?" / "What would someone you admire do here?"

*Way Forward:* "What will you do?" / "By when, specifically?" / "What might get in the way?" / "How committed are you, 1–10? What would make it a 10?"
