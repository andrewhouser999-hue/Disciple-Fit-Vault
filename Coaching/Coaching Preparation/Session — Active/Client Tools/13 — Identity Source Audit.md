# Identity Source Audit
*Coach use: Worth-anchor diagnostic | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Map where the client's sense of worth is actually anchored — and stress-test whether those sources can hold the weight being placed on them.

## Modality
[[Biblical Theology & Discipleship Coaching]] / [[CBT & Cognitive Reframing - Evidence-Based Foundation for Performance Coaching]]

## When to Use
Performance-based identity, role-anchored identity, shame-based identity narratives, contingent self-esteem, false self work, any client whose emotional stability tracks closely with external outcomes.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. Open: "Where do you get your sense of worth? What makes you feel like you matter?"
2. List all sources the client names — don't evaluate yet
3. Common sources: achievement, role/title, relationships, appearance, productivity, approval, morality, usefulness
4. **Stress-test each source:** "What happens to your sense of worth when this fails, disappears, or is taken away?"
5. Sort: conditional sources (can be lost) vs. unconditional sources (cannot be taken)
6. **Expose the gap:** For most clients, the conditional list is long and the unconditional list is empty or thin
7. Name the covenant alternative: worth that is *given*, not earned — grounded in union with Christ, not performance for him
8. Assign a specific Scripture that speaks directly to the client's most load-bearing conditional source

*The goal is not to eliminate striving — it is to move the foundation. A person can pursue excellence from security rather than fear. That is the shift.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "When do you feel most worthless? What happened?"
- "What would you need to achieve or maintain to feel genuinely okay about yourself?"
- "When [your main source] fails, what does that mean about you?"
- "What would it change if your worth was settled regardless of all of this?"
- "What does it mean that you are loved by God with full knowledge of everything — including what you're most ashamed of?"
