# Examen Practice
*Coach use: Interior awareness development | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Develop the client's capacity to notice and interpret interior movements — consolation and desolation — as a daily practice of awareness before God.

## Modality
[[Spiritual Direction — Ignatian]]

## When to Use
Felt disconnection from God, desolation/consolation awareness, spiritual disciplines gap, calling discernment, prayer resistance or dryness, clients who want to grow in interior life.

## Coach's In-Session Guide

**Teaching the Examen (assign as a between-session practice):**

Explain: "The Examen is a 10–15 minute daily prayer — not a journaling exercise or productivity review. It is a conversation with God about the day. The goal is to develop an ear for how he speaks through your interior life."

**The Five Movements:**
1. **Presence** — Settle. Recall that you are in God's presence. Take 1–2 minutes to stop.
2. **Gratitude** — Review the day for gifts, however small. Where did you notice grace?
3. **Review** — Move through the day looking for consolations (moments of peace, love, energy, movement toward God) and desolations (heaviness, anxiety, distance, flatness, pulling away from God).
4. **One Moment** — Choose one moment — a consolation or desolation — and linger there with God. What is he saying? What do you need to say?
5. **Forward Look** — What do you anticipate about tomorrow? What do you need from him for it?

**Best practice:** Daily, before sleep or at midday. 10–15 minutes maximum. Consistency matters more than length.

**Debrief questions for next session:**
- "What did you notice when you practiced the Examen?"
- "What consolations and desolations came up most often?"
- "What do you think God is drawing your attention toward?"
- "What surprised you?"

*The Examen is not a performance assessment — it is not "did I have a good day?" It is: "Where was God today, and where was I in relation to him?"*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "When did you feel most alive and close to God today?"
- "When did you feel most distant, heavy, or pulled away?"
- "What do those movements tell you about what's happening in your soul?"
- "What is one moment from today you want to bring to God?"
