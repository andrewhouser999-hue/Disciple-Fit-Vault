# Functional Savior Audit
*Coach use: Idolatry diagnostic | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Identify what the client is functionally trusting, loving, or fearing more than God — the thing doing the work of a savior in their daily life.

## Modality
[[Biblical Theology & Discipleship Coaching]]

## When to Use
Idolatry patterns, performance-based identity, people-pleasing, entitlement, fear-based reasoning, persistent anxiety or drivenness that doesn't respond to surface-level intervention.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. Open with Keller's diagnostic question: "What is it that, if you lost it or never got it, life would feel barely worth living?"
2. Follow the energy: "What do you think about most? What drives most of your decisions this season?"
3. Surface the hidden promise: "What is this thing offering you? What does it promise to give you?"
4. Name the functional savior — approval, achievement, security, control, comfort, a relationship, reputation
5. Identify the false gospel: "This thing says: if you [perform/achieve/maintain], then [you will be okay/loved/safe/significant]"
6. Apply the actual gospel: "What does Christ say about that need specifically? What has he already secured for you?"
7. Name the practical implication: "What would it look like to hold this thing more loosely this week?"

*This is not about eliminating desire — good things become idols when they become ultimate things. The goal is reordering, not deprivation.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "What is the one thing you work hardest to protect?"
- "What would it mean about you if you failed at this?"
- "What are you most afraid other people will think or see?"
- "When you achieve what you're working toward, how long does the satisfaction last?"
- "If God gave you everything you're asking for, would it be enough?"
- "What promise is this thing making that only God can keep?"
