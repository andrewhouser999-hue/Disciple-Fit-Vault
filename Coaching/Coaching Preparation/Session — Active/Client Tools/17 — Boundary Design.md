# Boundary Design
*Coach use: Values-based limit setting | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Help a client define a values-based limit, articulate it clearly, and design a specific response for when it is crossed — grounded in identity rather than reaction.

## Modality
[[ACT — Acceptance & Commitment Coaching]] / [[Attachment-Informed Coaching]]

## When to Use
Boundaries (values-based), people-pleasing, codependency, conflict avoidance, communication habits, over-functioning, resentment building from repeated boundary violations.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. **Identify the violated value:** "What value is being compromised when this situation happens?"
2. **Define the boundary in self-terms:** "A boundary is about what *you* will do — not what others must do. How would you state it that way?"
3. **Name the consequence:** "If X happens, I will do Y." Not a threat — a stated intention the client will actually follow through on.
4. **Anticipate the resistance:** "How do you expect them to respond? What will that pull out of you?"
5. **Prepare the statement:** Keep it short, calm, specific. Practice the exact words.
6. **Debrief:** After the client attempts it — what held? What collapsed? What made the difference?

**Key distinctions:**
- Boundaries are about what *you* will do — not demands placed on others
- Boundaries protect *values*, not preferences
- Boundaries require follow-through — a stated limit not held is not a boundary
- Biblical frame: speaking truth in love (Eph 4:15); yes meaning yes, no meaning no (Matt 5:37)

*Many clients confuse boundary-setting with confrontation or rejection. Reframe: a boundary is an act of love — it tells the other person who you actually are and what you actually need, rather than silent resentment.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "What value is being violated when this happens?"
- "What do you wish you had said? How would you say that as 'I will' rather than 'you need to'?"
- "What are you afraid will happen if you actually say that?"
- "What is it costing you to keep absorbing this without naming it?"
- "What's the smallest, clearest version of this boundary?"
