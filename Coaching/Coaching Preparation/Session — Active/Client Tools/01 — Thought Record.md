# Thought Record
*Coach use: CBT cognitive restructuring | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Surface, evaluate, and restructure an automatic thought driving a disproportionate emotion or self-defeating behavior.

## Modality
[[CBT & Cognitive Reframing - Evidence-Based Foundation for Performance Coaching]]

## When to Use
Distorted thinking patterns, shame-based reasoning, fear-based reasoning, perfectionism, core beliefs about worth.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. Identify the triggering situation — what happened?
2. Name the emotion and rate intensity (0–10)
3. Surface the automatic thought — "What went through your mind in that moment?"
4. Evaluate the thought — what is the evidence for it? Against it?
5. Generate a balanced alternative thought
6. Re-rate the emotion after the reframe

*Don't rush step 4. Clients often resist examining evidence against a thought they've held for years.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "What went through your mind when that happened?"
- "What's the evidence that thought is accurate?"
- "What's the evidence against it?"
- "What would you tell a close friend who had that same thought?"
- "What's a more balanced — not more positive, more accurate — way to see this?"
- "If that thought were only 30% true instead of 100%, what would change?"

## Without Biblical Framework — Christian Client

**Verdict: Fatal as a standalone for theologically-rooted core beliefs. Fixable when biblical declarations replace secular alternative thoughts as the counter-data.**

The replacement thought has no authoritative ground for a Christian. Cognitive disputation asks, *"Where's the evidence?"* — but many core stuck beliefs are theological claims ("God is punishing me," "I'm too far gone"), and the only sufficient answer is what God has *declared*, not what empirical evidence supports. Biblical counter-data (declared identity in Christ, adoption, righteousness imputed) is authoritative regardless of evidence and cannot be supplied by secular restructuring alone.

**Fix:** Pair with [[Lie Eliminator — Client Workbook]] — its 7-part format (Who God Is → The Lie → Declaration → Scripture Arsenal) performs biblical cognitive restructuring with proper ontological grounding.
