# 19 — Felt Sense Focusing
*Between-Session Formation Practice*

**What this is:** A quiet listening practice for between sessions. It is not prayer in the usual sense — though it can become that. It is a way of slowing down and attending to what your body already knows before your mind has words for it.

Sometimes you carry more than you can name. Something is stuck. A truth you know intellectually hasn't reached the place it needs to. You can't explain why something still feels the way it does. This practice creates conditions to listen at a deeper level — and often, to experience something shift.

**Time:** 20–30 minutes. Quiet space. No agenda for outcome.

---

## The Practice

### Step 1 — Clear a Space

Sit quietly and take a breath. Let what has been pressing in on you surface, one thing at a time. For each thing: notice it, name it briefly, and set it down gently. You are not solving these things right now — you are acknowledging what is present and making room to attend to one thing.

*Optional:* as you set each one down, pray briefly: *Lord, I place this with you. I am here now.*

### Step 2 — Find the Felt Sense

Choose one thing — a situation, a place where covenant truth isn't landing, something stuck. Instead of thinking about it, turn your attention inward.

*Where in your body does the whole thing live?* Chest? Stomach? Throat?

Don't analyze. Don't explain it. Just notice: what is the physical sense of the whole situation — all of it at once, before you break it into parts? Let it form. It will be fuzzy at first. That is right. Wait for it. The felt sense takes longer than you expect.

### Step 3 — Find a Handle

When you sense something there, let a word, phrase, or image rise from it — not something you decide, something that comes.

*Heavy. Stuck. Like a wall. Like waiting for something. Like being very young.*

Hold that word gently against the felt sense. Does your body say *yes, that's it*?

### Step 4 — Check

If it fits — notice a small release? A settling? A quiet *yes, exactly*? That is your body confirming the handle.

If it doesn't quite fit, set it down and wait for a better one. No forcing.

### Step 5 — Ask

Once you have a fitting handle, sit with the felt sense and gently ask:

- *What is it about this that makes it feel like [the handle]?*
- *What does this need?*
- *What is the hardest part of this?*

Wait. Don't answer from your head. Wait for something to come from the felt sense itself — a word, a memory, an image, an unexpected knowing.

*Formation framing:* You may ask instead: *Lord, what do you want me to notice here?* or *What are you saying through this?*

### Step 6 — Receive

When something comes, receive it. Don't analyze it immediately. Don't judge whether it makes logical sense. Let it be present. Hold it gently.

---

## What to Watch For

**The felt shift:** A physical release — breath deepening, a loosening in the body, a quiet *ah, that's it.* This is the organism registering that it has been understood at a level below thought. Don't rush past it. Stay with it. This is often where change actually begins — not at the moment you understood something, but at the moment something shifted.

**Nothing happening:** Sometimes the felt sense won't form, or nothing comes in Step 5. This is not failure. The practice of simply attending builds a capacity over time. Return to Step 1 and try again, or stay longer in Step 2. Bring the experience of nothing-coming to your next session — that is information too.

**Unexpected material:** Sometimes what surfaces is surprising — an old memory, an image, an emotion you didn't anticipate. Receive it without immediately explaining it. If it becomes overwhelming, open your eyes, orient to the room, notice five things you can see, and return to your breath. Bring what surfaced to coaching.

---

## After the Practice

Write down briefly:

- Where in your body the felt sense lived
- The handle that fit
- What came in Step 5 (if anything)
- Whether anything shifted — and what that was like

Write before you analyze. The analytical mind moves fast; write the experience first, then think about it later if you want to.

---

## What to Bring to Coaching

**If something shifted:** Bring the description of the shift — not only what you discovered (the content) but what it was like for something to shift (the experience). The shift itself is formation data, not just a prelude to it.

**If you got stuck:** Bring where you got stuck. The place the felt sense forms but cannot speak is often the most important place to work together.
