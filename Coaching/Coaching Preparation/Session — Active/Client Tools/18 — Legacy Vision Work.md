# Legacy Vision Work
*Coach use: End-of-life vision to clarify present priorities | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Create a clear picture of the contribution and impact the client wants their life to represent — and use it to clarify present decisions and expose what is currently misaligned.

## Modality
[[Existential Coaching]] / [[Positive Psychology & Character Strengths — PERMA Framework]]

## When to Use
Contribution and legacy hunger, calling confusion, disillusionment, lack of direction, life stage disorientation, clients going through major transitions who need a longer horizon.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. **Set the frame:** "I want to take you to the end of your life for a few minutes — not morbidly, but to use that perspective to clarify what actually matters."
2. **The funeral question:** "What would you most want people to say at your funeral — not what you think they would say, but what you most want to be true?"
3. **Domain work:** Move through 3–4 key domains (faith, family, work/calling, community). For each: "What does success look like here when your life is done?"
4. **The gap:** "What is one thing you're doing now that is actively building toward that? What is one thing working against it?"
5. **Present connection:** "Given that legacy — what decision in front of you right now looks different?"
6. **Next step:** What is the one most important move, based on this picture?

*The legacy question is most powerful when it interrupts immediate-horizon thinking — the client who is stuck in this week's problem often needs the 30-year view to unlock the present.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "When your life is over, what do you most want to have been true?"
- "What do you want your children — or those who come after you — to say about how you lived?"
- "If your life ended today, what would be unfinished that most matters to you?"
- "What are you building that outlasts you?"
- "Given that answer, what needs to change? What needs to stay?"
