# Decisional Balance
*Coach use: Ambivalence mapping | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Map the full cost-benefit picture of changing vs. staying the same to surface ambivalence, validate resistance, and evoke the client's own motivation for change.

## Modality
[[Motivational Interviewing — CBC Application]]

## When to Use
Ambivalence, competing commitments, values-action misalignment, persistent resistance to a change the client says they want, decision paralysis.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. Draw or present the 2×2 grid
2. Complete all four quadrants — don't skip or rush "Pros of Staying the Same." This quadrant is where the real work is.
3. Reflect back the tension without judgment: *"So part of you wants X because of [pros of change], and part of you is holding on to the current situation because of [pros of staying]."*
4. Evoke change talk: "What concerns you most about staying the same?"
5. Amplify the discrepancy: "How does staying the same fit with what you said matters most to you?"
6. Let the client summarize — their own words are the intervention.

*Do not argue for change. If the coach pushes, the client defends the status quo. Evoke; don't persuade.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "What's good about changing? What would you gain?"
- "What's hard about changing? What are you afraid of losing?"
- "What's working about the way things are now? What would you miss?"
- "What's the cost of not changing? What are you paying for staying the same?"
- "If nothing changes, where are you in five years?"
- "What concerns you most about staying where you are?"
