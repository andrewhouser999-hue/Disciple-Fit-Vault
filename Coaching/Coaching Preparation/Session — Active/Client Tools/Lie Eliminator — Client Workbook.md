# Lie Eliminator — Client Workbook

---

## Before You Begin

This workbook is a **lie excavation and renunciation tool**. It works differently from devotional reading or affirmation practice. The goal is not to feel better about yourself — it is to locate the specific false belief that is structurally load-bearing in your life, name it precisely, renounce the agreement you made with it, and replace it with what is actually true.

Lies do not die by being ignored. They die by being named, exposed, and renounced in the light of what God has said.

---

## How to Use This Workbook

**In session with your coach:**
Your coach will guide you through the excavation questions. The renunciation is most powerful when spoken aloud with a witness. Come having identified the lie that is most active right now — your coach will help you go deeper.

**Between sessions:**
Work through one lie per sitting. Do not rush. The excavation questions do more work than the fill-in blanks. Take the time the reflection requires.

**After lie work:**
Pair with the *Identity Installer — Client Workbook* to install the replacement truth. Renunciation creates an opening. The Identity Installer fills it. Lie elimination alone — without identity installation — leaves a cavity. Fill it.

---

## The Excavation Questions

These questions apply to every lie in this workbook. For each one you recognize, work through these before filling in the blanks:

1. **Does this describe you?** Rate 1–5 (1 = barely / 5 = this is me completely): ____
2. **When did you first believe this?** How old were you? What was happening in your life?
3. **Who lived this in front of you?** Not who said it — whose life demonstrated it?
4. **What made it logical?** What would have had to be true about you for this conclusion to make sense?
5. **Can you locate the moment of agreement?** There was a moment you stopped just hearing it and started *believing* it — when you made an agreement with it. Can you find that moment?

The agreement is the spiritual hinge. Renouncing the agreement specifically — not just the lie generally — is categorically different in both psychological and spiritual effect. Name the agreement if you can locate it.

---

## The Spirit's Role

The declarations you will speak in this workbook are not self-help affirmations. They are true — and the Holy Spirit is the active agent working to move them from comprehension to experience.

Your part is to create conditions: read the truth, name the lie, speak the renunciation and declaration honestly. You cannot force the transformation — that is the Spirit's work, and he is already at it.

Before each session, pray:
*Holy Spirit, make what I am about to declare real in me — not just understood, but experienced. In Jesus' name.*

Speak your declarations out loud. Close each one in Jesus' name — not as formula, but as an acknowledgment that the authority is Christ's and the work is the Spirit's.

**Receptive Return vs. Affirmation Repetition**
When returning to an entry — daily, weekly, or in a moment of need — the distinction matters: affirmation repetition is cognitive rehearsal — saying the truth enough times hoping it descends. Receptive return is posture: sitting with the truth in stillness, bringing it before the Father in prayer, asking the Spirit to move it deeper. These are not the same practice, and confusing them is why between-session work often produces limited formation.

## A Final Note

A renounced lie without an installed replacement truth leaves a vacancy. The enemy does not leave vacated space empty. After lie work, move to the *Identity Installer — Client Workbook* and work the corresponding identity category. Name what is true, build your evidence, speak your declaration, and act from it.

The sequence: **Excavate → Renounce → Install → Live.**

And when it comes, it finds the house empty, swept, and put in order. Then it goes and brings with it seven other spirits more evil than itself, and they enter and dwell there."* — Matthew 12:43–45

Do not leave the house empty.
