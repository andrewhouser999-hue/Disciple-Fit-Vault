# Needs Identification
*Coach use: Underlying need excavation | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Name the core unmet emotional need driving a presenting behavior or pattern — moving from surface symptom to root dynamic.

## Modality
[[Schema Therapy — Young Framework]] / [[ACT — Acceptance & Commitment Coaching]]

## When to Use
Unmet emotional needs, codependency, people-pleasing, emotional avoidance, recurring behavioral patterns that don't respond to surface-level change, any behavior where "why do I keep doing this?" has no satisfying answer.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. Start with the behavior or emotion: "What were you feeling/doing when this happened?"
2. Dig one level: "What is that feeling trying to protect you from — or get for you?"
3. Dig again: "Underneath that, what do you need that you're not getting?"
4. Name the core need from the five categories (see below)
5. Surface the current strategy: "What are you doing to meet that need right now?"
6. Evaluate: "Is it working? What are you paying for it?"
7. Design the legitimate alternative: "What would meeting this need look like in a healthy way?"

**Five core emotional needs (schema framework):**
- **Safety / Attachment** — to be secure and connected
- **Autonomy** — to be capable and independent
- **Validation** — to be seen, understood, and valued
- **Spontaneity / Play** — to express freely
- **Realistic Limits** — to have structure and appropriate boundaries

*A key coaching distinction: many people are trying to meet a legitimate need through a destructive strategy. The need is not the problem — the strategy is. Replacing the strategy without honoring the need doesn't work.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "What were you feeling in that moment?"
- "Underneath the [anger/anxiety/withdrawal], what are you actually feeling?"
- "What is that feeling trying to protect you from or get for you?"
- "What do you need that you're not getting?"
- "What are you currently doing to meet that need? Is it working?"
- "Who or what are you asking to meet a need only God can meet?"
- "What would it look like to meet this need legitimately?"
