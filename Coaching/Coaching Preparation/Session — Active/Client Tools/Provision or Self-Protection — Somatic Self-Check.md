# Provision or Self-Protection? — A Somatic Self-Check

*A reflection for clients*

---

Sometimes what we call "being responsible" is actually faithful stewardship — doing what's ours to do and releasing the rest. Other times, it's anxiety wearing responsibility's clothes.

The mind struggles to tell the difference. The body usually doesn't.

This reflection doesn't analyze your actions. It pays attention to what happens *inside* you when you do them — or when you imagine stopping.

Work through one specific behavior at a time. Be honest. There are no wrong answers here — only information.

## Coach Notes

*Not for client distribution.*

**When to assign this tool:**
- Client is overworking, over-preparing, or over-managing while describing it as "responsibility" or "stewardship"
- Client presents anxiety as virtue (diligence, planning, care) and resists naming it as fear
- Client has reached an outcome they can't control and is escalating effort rather than releasing

**In-session use:**
Can be run as a guided verbal sequence instead of written homework. Read each prompt aloud, pause after each, and reflect back what you hear before moving to the next. The pause test (Step 3) and grip question (Step 6) are the two highest-yield stops — spend the most time there.

**What to listen for:**
- Step 3: No body awareness at all = dissociation or high defenses; explore gently
- Step 4: "Enough keeps moving" = anxiety without a floor; the issue is internal, not circumstantial
- Step 5: "I can't not do this" = compulsion dressed as obedience; worth naming directly
- Step 6: Catastrophic or blank response = the real threat is identity or safety, not the named outcome

**Theological bridge:**
The distinction between faithful provision and anxious self-securing maps to Matthew 6:25–34. The question isn't whether to act — it's *from what* the action flows. Faithful stewardship acts and releases. Anxious securing acts and grips. The body registers the difference.
