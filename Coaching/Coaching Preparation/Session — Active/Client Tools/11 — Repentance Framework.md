# Repentance Framework
*Coach use: Distinguishing remorse from repentance | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Guide a client through genuine repentance — distinguishing it from guilt management, self-punishment, or performance — and connecting it to the specific turning it requires.

## Modality
[[Biblical Theology & Discipleship Coaching]]

## When to Use
Remorse and guilt, habitual sin patterns, confessional orientation, repentance quality (remorse that doesn't lead to change), shame spirals masquerading as repentance.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. **Name it** — "Describe what happened as specifically as possible." Vague confession produces vague repentance.
2. **Acknowledge the harm** — "Who was affected? What was broken or lost?" This includes harm to the client's own soul, to others, and to God.
3. **Receive the weight** — Allow the sorrow without rushing to resolution. Distinguish: *godly grief* produces repentance and life (2 Cor 7:10); *worldly grief* produces death — shame, self-punishment, performance.
4. **Receive forgiveness** — Claim the specific promise. *"If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us."* (1 John 1:9). The client must receive, not just know.
5. **Turn** — "What specifically will be different? What are you turning *from*? What are you turning *toward*?" Repentance is not just stopping — it is reorienting.
6. **Accountability** — "Who will you tell? What structure supports the turn?"

*The key distinction: remorse is about how I feel; repentance is about where I'm pointed. A client can feel terrible and still be pointed the same direction. The question is always: what is actually changing?*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "If you described what happened as specifically as possible, what actually occurred?"
- "What was broken by this — in your relationship with God, with others, in yourself?"
- "Is what you're feeling moving you toward God or into yourself?"
- "What does it mean that he is 'faithful and just' to forgive — not just willing, but obligated by his own character?"
- "What does turning look like this week — specifically?"
- "Who needs to know about this? What accountability makes sense?"
