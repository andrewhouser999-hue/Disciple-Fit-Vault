# Calling Discernment Framework
*Coach use: Vocation mapping | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Help the client identify their unique calling through the convergence of multiple indicators — gifting, passion, burden, open doors, and community confirmation.

## Modality
[[Existential Coaching]] / [[Biblical Theology & Discipleship Coaching]]

## When to Use
Calling and vocation confusion, lack of direction, life stage disorientation, major career or ministry transition, contribution and legacy hunger.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
Work through each indicator and record the client's responses. Look for *convergence* — calling typically sits where multiple indicators point together.

1. **Gifting audit** — "What do you do better than most people? What comes naturally that others find difficult?"
2. **Passion inventory** — "What energizes you beyond reason? What would you do even if no one paid you?"
3. **Burden** — "What breaks your heart? What injustice or need makes you angry when it's ignored?"
4. **Open doors** — "What opportunities have come to you? What have others invited you into?"
5. **Community confirmation** — "What have people who know you well consistently named in you?"
6. **Scripture and prayer** — "What has God spoken in this area? What do you sense when you bring this before him?"
7. **Look for the overlap** — Map the convergence. Where do gifting, passion, burden, and invitation all point?

*Calling is usually not a thunderclap — it is a persistent convergence. The client who "doesn't know their calling" often has more data than they realize; they're waiting for certainty that won't come before obedience.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "What would you do even if no one paid you and no one was watching?"
- "What makes you angry when it's done poorly or not at all?"
- "When you imagine yourself doing meaningful work ten years from now, what is it?"
- "What do people consistently thank you for or ask your help with?"
- "What has God been saying in this area — in Scripture, through others, through circumstances?"
- "What are you waiting for permission to pursue?"
