# God-Image Audit
*Coach use: Theological diagnostic | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Surface the client's functional (operating) image of God — not what they believe theologically, but what they act as if is true — and evaluate it against Scripture.

## Modality
[[Biblical Theology & Discipleship Coaching]]

## When to Use
God-image distortions, spiritual shame, felt disconnection from God, unbelief, prayer resistance, misaligned theology of suffering, performance-based identity rooted in a punishing-God image.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. Establish the distinction: "I want to ask about the God you actually experience day to day — not the God you know you should believe in."
2. Ask the operating image questions (see below)
3. Surface the functional image — distant, demanding, disappointed, absent, punishing, conditional, indulgent
4. Trace the source: "Where did that image come from? Does God remind you of anyone?"
5. Present the biblical corrective — identify specific attributes with specific texts
6. Name the choice: "Which image will you live from this week — this one, or the true one?"
7. Assign a Scripture to meditate on that directly addresses the specific distortion

*Many clients have an accurate theology of God and a deeply distorted experience of God. Both are real and both matter. Don't dismiss the experience; redirect it toward the Person.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "When you picture God watching you right now, what expression is on his face?"
- "When you fail or sin, what do you imagine God says or feels?"
- "Do you feel like God is pleased with you? What would it take to feel that?"
- "When you pray, who do you feel like you're talking to?"
- "Has there been a season when God felt close? What was different?"
- "Does God remind you of anyone you've known?"
