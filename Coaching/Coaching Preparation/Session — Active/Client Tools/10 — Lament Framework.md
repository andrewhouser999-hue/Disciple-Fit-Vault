# Lament Framework
*Coach use: Structured grief and honest prayer before God | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Create structured space for honest grief, disappointment, and complaint directed to God — and move through it toward trust, without bypassing the weight of the pain.

## Modality
[[Grief Coaching]] / [[Biblical Theology & Discipleship Coaching]]

## When to Use
Grief and loss, disillusionment, unresolved disappointment, felt disconnection from God, anger at God, seasons of spiritual dryness, suffering without obvious meaning.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. Establish permission: "The Psalms of Lament (22, 42, 88) model complete honesty before God. You are not protecting God by sanitizing this."
2. Address: Help the client direct the lament *to* God — not just about him. This is prayer, not processing.
3. Complaint: "What do you need to say honestly? What have you been afraid to tell him?"
4. Trust: Find the thread — "Is there anything you still believe about God, even now?" Often thin; that's enough.
5. Petition: "What do you specifically need from him in this?"
6. Praise: Not manufactured — "Is there anything, however small, you can offer back?"

*The lament movement is not linear. Some clients will stay in the complaint phase for multiple sessions. Don't force the arc. Psalm 88 ends in darkness — God receives that too.*

*The lament is not therapy. It is prayer. Its power is in the address — the client speaking directly to the Person, not just processing feelings about him.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "If you could say anything to God about this — anything — what would it be?"
- "What have you been afraid to tell him? What have you been editing?"
- "Is there anything you still believe about God right now, even if you can't feel it?"
- "What do you need from him that only he can give?"
- "Is there anything, however small, you can acknowledge as gift?"
