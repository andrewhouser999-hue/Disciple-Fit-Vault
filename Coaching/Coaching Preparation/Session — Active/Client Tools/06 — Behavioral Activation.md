# Behavioral Activation
*Coach use: Breaking avoidance cycles | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Break inaction cycles by identifying avoided activities and scheduling small, meaningful steps — rebuilding momentum from the outside in.

## Modality
[[CBT & Cognitive Reframing - Evidence-Based Foundation for Performance Coaching]]

## When to Use
Avoidance behaviors, procrastination, emotional avoidance, rhythm and routine deficits, busyness masking inaction, low energy and motivation.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. Map the current activity-mood pattern: "What does a typical day look like? What is your energy/mood through the day?"
2. Identify what is being avoided — specific activities the client is putting off or has stopped doing
3. Surface the avoided feeling: "What do you think would happen if you did that? What feeling are you avoiding?"
4. Choose ONE small, specific step — not a full recovery plan
5. Schedule it: day, time, duration — specific
6. Anticipate the internal obstacle: "What will the voice in your head say when the time comes?"
7. Debrief the following session: "What did you notice about your mood/energy before and after?"

*The mechanism is behavioral — action produces mood change, not the reverse. This runs against most clients' intuition ("I'll do it when I feel like it"). The coach's job is to gently correct this assumption.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "When are you most alive and productive? What are you doing?"
- "What have you been putting off that you know matters?"
- "What's the smallest possible version of that you could do this week?"
- "What do you think would happen if you actually did it?"
- "What will you tell yourself to avoid it? And what's your response to that?"

## Without Biblical Framework — Christian Client

**Verdict: Fixable. Useful but the activation targets and feedback interpretation both need biblical grounding.**

Secular activation targets mood uplift and personal values — excluding obedience as a category. This trains the Christian to engage only what feels rewarding, which runs counter to discipleship. Spiritual disciplines (prayer, Scripture, confession, service) are commanded regardless of felt benefit, and a secular protocol will inadvertently deprioritize them. The behavior-to-mood feedback loop is also incomplete: spiritual disciplines produce effects (conviction, consolation, spiritual clarity) that the secular model cannot account for and may misread as negative outcomes.

**Fix:** Expand activation menu to include obedience-category practices. Reframe the debrief to capture spiritual effects alongside emotional ones.
