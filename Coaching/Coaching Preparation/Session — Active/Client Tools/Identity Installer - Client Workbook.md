# Identity Installer — Client Workbook

---

## Before You Begin: Read This First

This workbook is a **formation tool**, not a reading exercise. The difference matters. You can read through every page and feel nothing change. Or you can slow down, engage each section honestly, and find that something begins to shift at a level deeper than information.

Identity is not discovered in a moment — it is **installed over time** through repeated exposure to truth, honest personal reflection, spoken declaration, and consistent action. This workbook is designed to support all four.

It works. The result, increased faith, grounding, connection with the Lord, reduced anxiety, dropping the idols, freedom and much more.  Tend to the garden of your heart. Increase the fertile soil actively.  Utilize proven biblical and evidence-based work that answers how to keep the Word like meditation/murmuring, imagination, feeling fully, making verbal agreements and taking action.  These practices are what brings your heart, mind, soul & strength to life!  

Nothing is more effective than God's divine truths, as promised, altering your life according to His riches and purposes.  We achieve at far greater heights when our inner work aligns our thoughts, emotions and nervous system with His Word in seeking first the Kingdom of heaven.  When we seek we will find. When we plant seeds, God will grow.  When we sew even in tears we will come out with sheaths.  

---

## How This Workbook Is Structured

Each identity category contains seven sections. Here is what each one does and how to complete it:

---

### 1. Identity Statement
**What it is:** A single, clear declaration of who you are in Christ — written in first person, present tense.
**What to do:** Read it out loud. Do not rush past it. Let the words land before you move on. Over time, this statement should become something you can say from memory without looking at the page.

---

### 2. Anchor Verse
**What it is:** The primary scripture that supports this identity truth.
**What to do:** Read it slowly. If a word or phrase stands out, underline it. This is not a verse to collect — it is a verse to own.

---

### 3. My Evidence *(the most important section)*
**What it is:** A one or two sentence personal account of a moment, season, or experience where this identity truth became real in your life.
**What to do:** Write it in your own words. It does not need to be polished. It does not need to be dramatic. It just needs to be true and specific.

**Why this matters:** Research in psychology consistently shows that affirmations only take root when they are connected to personal evidence. Without a real story, declarations stay abstract. Your brain will reject a claim it has no evidence for. The moment you attach a personal memory to a truth — it moves from something you believe in theory to something you *know*.

**If you cannot think of an example yet:** Write "I am watching for this" and return to fill it in when God confirms it. Do not skip the section — leave it open and expectant.

---

#### What Counts as Evidence

Evidence is broader than a dramatic moment of clarity. Any of the following counts:

| Type | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Direct experience** | A moment when this identity statement was clearly true in your life |
| **Behavioral evidence** | A time you acted like this person — even without feeling like them |
| **Others' testimony** | Something someone said to you that pointed at this quality in you |
| **Survival evidence** | Something you came through that proves this is true about who you are |
| **Thin evidence** | A fleeting moment, a partial experience — it counts. Start there. |

#### If You're Drawing a Blank — Ask Yourself These

Work through these one at a time. Stop when one produces something to write:

1. Has there ever been a moment — even briefly — when this felt true?
2. When were you most like the person this statement describes?
3. What's the closest thing I have to this experience — even if it's imperfect?
4. Has anyone ever said something to me that pointed at this quality?
5. I've come through something hard to get here. What did that take? What does that tell me about who I am?
6. What would it look like if this were 10% true about me right now? Is there any evidence for that 10%?

#### How Much Evidence Is Enough

| What you have | What to do |
|---|---|
| A clear, specific memory | Write it. You have what you need. |
| A vague sense it has been true | Write what you have. Add detail as it comes. |
| One small moment that barely qualifies | Write it. One brick is enough to begin building. |
| Nothing yet | Write "I am watching for this." Then name one specific thing to watch for this week. |

---

---

### 4. What I'm Putting Off
**What it is:** A specific renunciation of the false identity this truth is replacing.
**What to do:** Complete the sentence: *"I renounce the identity of..."* Be specific. The more precisely you can name the old identity, the more clearly you can release it. Vague renunciations produce vague results.

**A note on renunciation:** This is not a ritual — it is an act of will aligned with scripture. Ephesians 4:22 says to "put off the old self." You are doing exactly that. You are naming what you are no longer willing to carry, and handing it over.

---

### 5. What I'm Putting On
**What it is:** A personal declaration of your new identity — written in your own voice, in first person, present tense.
**What to do:** Write 2–4 sentences that state what is now true about you. Use words that feel real to you. Avoid language that sounds borrowed or overly formal. This section should sound like *you* speaking about *yourself* — from truth, not from wishful thinking.

**Important:** Speak this declaration out loud when you write it, and again during your daily practice. Faith comes by hearing — including hearing yourself say it. *Romans 10:17.*

---

### 6. The Felt Sense
**What it is:** A reflective question designed to engage the emotional and experiential layer of your identity — not just the cognitive.
**What to do:** Do not answer this question quickly. Sit with it. Close your eyes if it helps. Let the question do its work before you write. What you write here is for you — it does not need to be theologically precise. It needs to be honest.

**Why this matters:** Identity is not only formed in the mind. It is stored in the body, the emotions, and the memory. If you can connect a truth to a felt experience — even a brief one — it becomes far more accessible when you need it most.

---

### 7. Today I Will
**What it is:** A single, specific behavioral commitment that enacts this identity today.
**What to do:** Keep it concrete and doable. Not "I will be more loving" — but "I will initiate a conversation with the person I have been avoiding." Identity is confirmed through action. Every time you act from your new identity instead of your old one, you reinforce the neural and spiritual reality of who you are becoming.

---

## Your Practice Rhythm

This workbook is not designed to be completed once and filed away. Use it this way:

- **Daily:** Read your Identity Statement and *Today I Will* for one category. Speak both out loud.
- **Weekly:** Work through one full category — including the Felt Sense question. Take your time.
- **Monthly:** Return to *My Evidence* in categories you have already worked through. Update it. God will have confirmed things. Write them down.
- **As needed:** When a lie attacks, use the companion document *Lie Eliminator* to respond directly. Then return here to reinforce the corresponding identity truth.

### EXAMPLE — Beloved Child of God

**Identity Statement:**
> I am a beloved child of God — chosen, accepted, and delighted in — not because of what I do, but because of who he is.

**Anchor Verse:**
> "See what great love the Father has lavished on us, that we should be called children of God! And that is what we are!" — 1 John 3:1

**My Evidence:**
> Two years ago I was in the worst season of my life — I had failed publicly and I was sure God was done with me. I opened my Bible expecting nothing and the first verse I read was Isaiah 43:1: *"Fear not, for I have redeemed you; I have called you by name, you are mine."* I sat on the floor and wept. That was the moment I knew his love was not based on my performance.

**Human Witness:**
> *(Someone whose words, presence, or response confirmed this truth in you. Name the person and moment if you can.)*

**What I'm Putting Off:**
> I renounce the identity of the orphan — the one who strives, hides, and performs to earn love that was already freely given before I did anything to deserve it.

**What I'm Putting On:**
*(Speak aloud — in Jesus' name.)*
> I am not a servant earning access to the Father's house — I live in it. God is not disappointed in me; he is delighted in me. I did not graduate into his love. I was born into it. When I came to him broken, he did not step back — he ran toward me.

**The Felt Sense:**
> When I imagine being fully at rest — no striving, no proving, nothing to earn — I feel a kind of exhale I rarely allow myself. The last time I felt that was during worship last spring when I stopped singing and just stood there and let God look at me. It lasted maybe thirty seconds, but something settled.

**Today I Will:**
> Bring the fear I've been carrying about my finances directly to God — as a child to a Father — without rehearsing it first or trying to have a solution ready.

## The Spirit's Role

The declarations you will speak in this workbook are not self-help affirmations. They are true — and the Holy Spirit is the active agent working to move them from comprehension to experience.

Your part is to create conditions: read the truth, write your evidence, speak your declaration honestly. You cannot force the transformation — that is the Spirit's work, and he is already at it.

Before each session, pray:
*Holy Spirit, make what I am about to declare real in me — not just understood, but experienced. In Jesus' name.*

Speak your declarations out loud. Close each one in Jesus' name — not as formula, but as an acknowledgment that the authority is Christ's and the work is the Spirit's.

## A Final Note

Do not wait until you believe every statement before you speak it. Speak it because it is true — and let the believing follow. This is not self-deception; it is alignment. You are bringing your mouth, your mind, and your actions into agreement with what God has already declared over you.

The goal is not to feel different by the end of this workbook. The goal is to be different — slowly, consistently, and from the inside out — as truth takes root and bears fruit in a life that keeps showing up.

> *"So then, just as you received Christ Jesus as Lord, continue to live your lives in him, rooted and built up in him, strengthened in the faith as you were taught, and overflowing with thankfulness."* — Colossians 2:6-7
