# Forgiveness Process
*Coach use: Structured forgiveness walk-through | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Walk a client through a structured forgiveness decision — releasing the offender from the debt owed — as distinct from reconciliation, minimization, or forgetting.

## Modality
[[Forgiveness Coaching — Enright & Worthington]]

## When to Use
Forgiveness orientation, anger and resentment, unresolved disappointment, bitterness affecting present relationships, unforgiveness toward God.

## Coach's In-Session Guide

**Establish key distinctions before beginning:**
- Forgiveness ≠ reconciliation (reconciliation requires changed behavior from the offender; forgiveness does not)
- Forgiveness ≠ forgetting or minimizing
- Forgiveness ≠ excusing
- Forgiveness is *unilateral* — it doesn't require the offender's participation or apology
- Forgiveness is for the one who forgives — it removes the ongoing damage of carrying the wound

**Enright's 4-Phase Process (coaching-adapted):**

*Phase 1 — Uncover*
Allow the full weight of the wound: the anger, hurt, shame, and impact. Don't rush past this.
"What actually happened? What did it cost you? What are you still carrying?"

*Phase 2 — Decide*
Present forgiveness as a choice, not a feeling.
"Forgiveness is something you do, not something you feel. Are you willing to consider choosing it — not because they deserve it, but because you want to be free?"

*Phase 3 — Work*
Move through the forgiveness itself: bearing the pain rather than passing it on; reframing the offender as a flawed, suffering human; absorbing the debt rather than demanding payment.
*Biblical frame: Matt 18:21-35; Eph 4:32 — we forgive as those who have been forgiven.*

*Phase 4 — Deepen*
Find what has been gained through forgiving. Connection to something larger than the wound.
"What has forgiving this person given you? What are you free from now?"

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "What did that cost you? Name it fully."
- "What are you still doing because of what they did?"
- "What would it mean to be free from carrying this?"
- "Forgiveness is giving what is not deserved — which is exactly what God gave you. What shifts when you see it that way?"
- "What does this person's wound or brokenness look like that led them here?"
