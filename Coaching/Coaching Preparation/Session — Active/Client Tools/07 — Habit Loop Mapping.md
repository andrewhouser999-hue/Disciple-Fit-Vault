# Habit Loop Mapping
*Coach use: Behavioral pattern redesign | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Identify the cue-routine-reward structure maintaining an unwanted habit so it can be redesigned rather than simply resisted.

## Modality
[[CBT & Cognitive Reframing - Evidence-Based Foundation for Performance Coaching]] / Behavioral coaching (Duhigg)

## When to Use
Habitual sin patterns, avoidance behaviors, self-regulation gaps, recurring behaviors the client wants to stop but can't through willpower alone.

## Coach's In-Session Guide
1. Name the routine — the specific unwanted behavior (be precise, not categorical)
2. Identify possible cues — run through the five categories: time of day, location, emotional state, preceding action, other people present
3. Identify the reward — what does the routine provide? Relief, stimulation, connection, numbing, comfort, distraction?
4. Verify the reward by experimenting: hold the cue constant, vary what follows the routine — does the craving change?
5. Design the replacement: *same cue → new routine → same reward class*
6. Implementation intention: "When [cue], I will [new routine] instead of [old routine]."
7. Track for one week — note whether the craving shifts

*The key insight (Duhigg): you cannot eliminate a habit, only replace it. The reward is the lock; find what the routine is really giving and offer it through a better path.*

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "When does this happen? What time, place, situation?"
- "What is usually happening — or what are you feeling — right before?"
- "What do you get from this? What does it give you in the moment?"
- "What need is this meeting, even if it's meeting it badly?"
- "What else could give you that same thing — that you wouldn't be ashamed of?"
