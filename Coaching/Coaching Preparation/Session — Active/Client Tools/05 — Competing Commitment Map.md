# Competing Commitment Map
*Coach use: Immunity to Change — four-column map | Client label: TBD*

## Purpose
Uncover the hidden competing commitment and protective big assumption that sustain self-defeating behavior — explaining why a client repeatedly fails to change despite genuine intent.

## Modality
[[Immunity to Change — Kegan & Lahey Framework]]

## When to Use
Self-sabotage, secondary gain, persistent failure to change despite commitment, competing commitments, procrastination rooted in fear, values-action misalignment that CBT alone hasn't moved.

## Coach's In-Session Guide

**Column 1 — Improvement Goal**
"What do you genuinely want to change or improve?"
*Write it as a positive commitment: "I want to..."*

**Column 2 — Contrary Behaviors**
"What are you doing — or not doing — that works against that goal?"
*Be specific. These are observable behaviors, not explanations.*

**Column 3 — Competing Commitment**
"What might you be protecting by doing/not doing those things?"
*Frame: "I am also committed to... [not being seen as X / not losing Y / avoiding Z]"*
*This is the hardest column. Take time. The competing commitment is usually a fear statement in disguise.*

**Column 4 — Big Assumption**
"What are you assuming would happen if you didn't protect that?"
*Frame: "I assume that if I [stopped the contrary behavior], then [feared outcome]..."*

**Behavioral Experiment**
Design a small, safe test to find out whether the big assumption is actually true.
"What is the smallest thing you could do that would test that assumption without fully dismantling the protection?"

## Key Questions / Prompts
- "You've said you want this — so why hasn't it happened?"
- "What are you protecting by staying stuck here?"
- "If you actually achieved this goal, what would you have to give up or risk?"
- "What are you most afraid would happen if this changed?"
- "What's the smallest experiment you could run to test whether that fear is true?"
