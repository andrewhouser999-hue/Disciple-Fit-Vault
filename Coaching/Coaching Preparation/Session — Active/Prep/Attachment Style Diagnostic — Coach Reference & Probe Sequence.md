# Attachment Style Diagnostic — Coach Reference & Probe Sequence

*Coach-only. Not client-facing. Originally built as a companion to [[Client-Facing Questions — Diagnostic Intake Form]] (archived 2026-07-06 — superseded by the [[Covenant Identity — Two-Tier Intake Protocol|two-tier intake system]]). This attachment-style probe reference remains active; use alongside the two-tier system's Tier 2 intake rather than the archived form.*

---

## The Four Styles — Quick Reference

| Style | Self-View | Other-View | Core Fear | Regulatory Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Secure** | Worthy | Trustworthy | None dominant | Flexible — seeks and gives support naturally |
| **Anxious/Preoccupied** | Unworthy | Trustworthy | Abandonment | Hyperactivating — amplifies distress to get response |
| **Avoidant/Dismissing** | Self-sufficient | Unreliable | Engulfment/vulnerability | Deactivating — suppresses need, compulsive independence |
| **Disorganized/Fearful** | Unworthy | Dangerous | Both simultaneously | Collapsed — approach-avoid cycles, often dissociation |

## Attachment Indicator Map (Existing Intake Questions)

These questions already surface attachment data. No new questions needed for client — just know what you're hearing.

| Q# | Question | What to Listen For |
|---|---|---|
| **Q1** | First self-talk when you fail or disappoint | *"I'm not enough / they'll leave"* = anxious; *"I'll handle it / don't need them"* = avoidant |
| **Q4** | Know the right answer but can't feel it | Gap between stated belief and felt reality = likely deactivating (avoidant) or hyperactivating (anxious) system |
| **Q5** | When something painful happens, what do you do with it? | Suppress/move on = deactivating (avoidant); ruminate/spiral/need to process with someone = hyperactivating (anxious) |
| **Q6–7** | Faith hard to hold / still affects how you see God | God as abandoning = anxious; God as distant/uninvolved = avoidant; God as dangerous/confusing = disorganized |
| **Q14** | When anxious, what do you actually do? | Isolate/handle alone = avoidant; seek reassurance/call repeatedly = anxious; reach out + can actually receive comfort = secure |
| **Q16** | Conflict instinct — address, withdraw, other? | Withdraw/go quiet = avoidant; pursue/escalate = anxious; engage calmly and stay = secure; freeze/dissociate = disorganized |
| **Q17** | Unresolved/stuck relationship | Obsessive rumination = anxious; cut-off, distant, "I'm fine" = avoidant; chaotic push-pull = disorganized |

## Attachment Probe Sequence (Coach Use — Session 1 Debrief or Session 2)

Pick 2–3 based on what the written intake already surfaced. Do not run all of them.

### Anxiety Axis (fear of abandonment)

> "When someone important to you goes quiet or pulls back — even just emotionally — what happens inside you?"

> "Is there a relationship where you feel like you're always the one working harder to keep it close?"

### Avoidance Axis (discomfort with closeness / dependency)

> "When you're going through something hard, do you naturally reach out or pull back? Which feels more like home?"

> "Is there a version of 'needing someone' that feels like weakness to you — something you try to avoid?"

### Disorganized Flag (use only when trauma history is already present)

> "Have you ever found yourself wanting to be close to someone, but then pulling away once they actually showed up?"

> "Is there a relationship — past or present — where the person who was supposed to be safe was also the source of the fear?"

### Secure Baseline Check

> "When you're really struggling, who would you actually call — and do you feel like you could fully let them in?"

## Attachment × Faith Pattern (CBC Application)

| Style | God-Image Distortion | Presenting Spiritual Problem | Coaching Entry Point |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Anxious** | God as conditional — must perform to keep His approval; fears being "too much" | Prayer as transaction, fear-based obedience, shame spiral after failure, Scripture-reading for reassurance not formation | Identity in Christ → unconditional belovedness; name and dismantle the abandonment lie directly |
| **Avoidant** | God as uninvolved or unnecessary in practical matters; "I've always figured it out myself" | Practical atheism, self-sufficiency spiritualized as stewardship or discipline, low prayer life, emotional distance from God | Break compulsive self-reliance; identify specific domain where dependency on God is blocked; re-frame vulnerability as trust, not weakness |
| **Disorganized** | God as unsafe, unpredictable, or abusive — especially if a caregiver was the source of harm | Unstable faith with intensity peaks, avoidance of spiritual intimacy, trauma responses in prayer/worship contexts | Stabilize safety before belief work; somatic regulation is prerequisite; reframe God-image slowly and carefully — do not rush identity declarations until felt safety exists |
| **Secure** | God as reliable, present, good — trusts even in ambiguity | Usually a surface presenting problem, not identity-level rupture | Tend goals directly; identity work is maintenance, not repair; watch for over-reliance on current stability |

## Earned Secure Attachment

*Important for trauma clients and adult discipleship.*

Attachment style is not fixed. Adults who form even one deeply safe relationship — a caregiver, spouse, therapist, mentor, or pastor — can move from insecure to **earned secure** over time. This is well-documented (Main & Goldwyn; Siegel). The coaching relationship itself can function as a corrective attachment experience if the coach maintains:

- Consistent attunement (shows up, follows through)
- Repair after rupture (doesn't disappear when client pushes back)
- Non-anxious presence under the client's emotional load
- Clear differentiation (coach is not a friend, not a parent, not God — but models safe attachment)

This is theologically significant: sanctification and the body of Christ are the primary mechanisms God uses to produce earned secure attachment in His people.

## Integration with Existing Diagnostic Sequence

Current sequence: **Identity → Integration → Repentance → Suffering → Trust → Relationships → Teachability**

Attachment diagnosis slots between **Integration** and **Repentance**. It provides the structural explanation for:

- *Why* the trust gap (Q8–9) exists relationally before it exists theologically
- *Why* repentance patterns (Q12–13) cycle without resolution (anxious shame spiral vs. avoidant minimization)
- *Why* relational patterns (Q15–17) look the way they do — behavior is strategy, not character

It also predicts **resistance patterns in coaching itself:**
- Avoidant client: won't be vulnerable, will minimize, may intellectualize or give short answers
- Anxious client: over-dependent on coach, may need excessive reassurance between sessions, escalates when coach is unavailable
- Disorganized client: push-pull with the coaching relationship — engages deeply then goes cold or cancels

Name these patterns to yourself early. They are diagnostic data, not problematic clients.

## Samuel Cooper — Working Attachment Hypothesis

*Hypothesis only — update after Session 1 debrief.*

| Factor | Attachment Signal |
|---|---|
| Complex early trauma (pre-adoption) | Disorganized attachment is live hypothesis — approach-avoid pattern, possible freeze under relational stress |
| Strong adoptive parent relationship | Possible **earned secure** pathway — this is the hopeful variable. Assess quality: can he receive from them, not just perform for them? |
| Pursuing marriage at 19, partner 3 years older, different country | Ambiguous — could be confident secure attachment or anxious pursuit. Watch for: who is driving the pace, how he handles her uncertainty, whether distance regulation is dysregulating him |
| No career/college foundation, low-salary job | Possible avoidant overlay — self-reliance or shame-avoidance may be operative even if relationally he's pursuing closeness |

**Watch for in session:**
- Can he receive a challenge from the coach without shutdown or collapse?
- Does he idealize his partner or can he hold complexity about her?
- How does he talk about the biological family/origin story? (Coherent narrative = earned secure. Fractured, over-controlled, or flooded = not yet resolved)
