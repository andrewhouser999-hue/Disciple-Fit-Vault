# Integrated Coaching Card: Emotional Intelligence + CBT + Biblical Framework

**Purpose:** This card develops Emotional Intelligence through the renewing of the mind (Word), the empowering of the Spirit, and evidence-based CBT tools designed for self-administration and coach facilitation.

---

## 1. REVEAL — Self-Awareness

### Phase 1: Recognize
**EQ Focus:** Recognize and understand your emotions
**Ask:** What am I feeling? Why? What am I believing?

**CBT Tools:**
- Thought records
- Emotion labeling (specific, not vague)
- Situation → Thought → Emotion → Behavior mapping

**Biblical Framework:**
- Psalm 139:23–24 — *"Search me, O God..."*
- Proverbs 4:23 — Guard your heart
- Matthew 7:3–5 — Examine yourself first
- **Command:** Examine yourself honestly
- **Warning:** The heart can be deceptive (Jeremiah 17:9)
- **Promise:** God reveals truth and insight
- **Cue:** *"Name it clearly"*

---

### Phase 2: Interpret
**EQ Focus:** Interpret emotions accurately
**Ask:** What is actually true vs. what I feel?

**CBT Tools:**
- Identify cognitive distortions
- Evidence for / against
- Replace distorted thoughts

**Biblical Framework:**
- John 8:32 — Truth sets you free
- Romans 12:2 — Renew your mind
- **Command:** Align thinking with truth
- **Warning:** Deception leads to misjudgment
- **Promise:** Truth produces freedom and clarity
- **Cue:** *"Feelings inform, not define"*

---

## 2. RESTRAIN — Self-Regulation
**EQ Focus:** Govern emotional responses
**Ask:** What response aligns with truth and self-control?

**CBT Tools:**
- Pause and response delay
- Behavioral choice vs. impulse
- Exposure vs. avoidance decisions

**Biblical Framework:**
- James 1:19 — Slow to anger
- Proverbs 25:28 — Lack of control = vulnerability
- Galatians 5:22–23 — Fruit of the Spirit (self-control)
- **Command:** Control your responses
- **Warning:** Unchecked emotion leads to destruction
- **Promise:** The Spirit enables self-control
- **Cue:** *"Slow down before acting"*

---

## 3. RELATE — Empathy / Social Awareness
**EQ Focus:** Understand others accurately
**Ask:** What might they be experiencing? What else could be true?

**CBT Tools:**
- Perspective-taking
- Alternative explanations
- Role-reversal reflection

**Biblical Framework:**
- Romans 12:15 — Enter others' emotions
- Philippians 2:3–4 — Value others
- Hebrews 4:15 — Christ's empathy
- **Command:** Consider others seriously
- **Warning:** Hardness of heart damages relationships
- **Promise:** Unity and deeper connection
- **Cue:** *"Consider their view"*

---

## 4. RESPOND — Social Skills / Relationship Management
**EQ Focus:** Engage effectively and constructively
**Ask:** What action builds rather than damages?

**CBT Tools:**
- Assertiveness training
- Communication scripts
- Conflict mapping
- Behavioral rehearsal (practice conversations)

**Biblical Framework:**
- Proverbs 15:1 — Gentle answer diffuses conflict
- Colossians 4:6 — Gracious speech
- Romans 12:18 — Live at peace when possible
- James 3 — Power of the tongue
- **Command:** Build others up in speech and action
- **Warning:** Words can destroy and divide
- **Promise:** Favor, peace, and influence
- **Cue:** *"Speak to build"*

---

## 5. REPEAT — Motivation / Discipline
**EQ Focus:** Sustain change through purpose and consistency
**Ask:** What will I practice repeatedly?

**CBT Tools:**
- Behavioral activation
- Habit stacking
- Goal setting and reinforcement

**Biblical Framework:**
- Galatians 6:9 — Do not grow weary
- Colossians 3:23 — Work as unto the Lord
- 1 Corinthians 9:24 — Run to win
- **Command:** Act with diligence and endurance
- **Warning:** Drift, inconsistency, and double-mindedness (James 1:8)
- **Promise:** Harvest, reward, and impact
- **Cue:** *"Do it again, correctly"*
