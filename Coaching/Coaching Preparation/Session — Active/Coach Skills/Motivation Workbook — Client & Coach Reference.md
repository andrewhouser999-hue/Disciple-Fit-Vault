## How to Use This Workbook

This workbook maps eight layers of your motivational life — from your biology to your identity. Each layer has tools: structured exercises that help you understand what's driving you, what's blocking you, and what to do about it.

**A few guidelines before you begin:**

- Work through the layers in order the first time. Each layer builds on the one before.
- Honesty matters more than the "right" answer. The goal is an accurate picture, not a flattering one.
- Not every tool will apply to you right now. Use the ones that catch something real.
- This is a living document — come back to it. What's true in one season changes in another.
- Some questions will be uncomfortable. That's usually the signal to slow down, not skip ahead.

**How to know a tool is working:** You feel something shift — recognition, resistance, clarity, or discomfort. Any of those is useful. Numbness or "I don't know" repeated across multiple tools is itself a diagnostic signal.

*Is your body capacitated for the life you're trying to live?*

Before motivation, strategy, or belief work can hold, the physical system must be functioning. Sleep, energy, arousal regulation, and brain chemistry determine whether your prefrontal cortex — the seat of long-term goal pursuit — is online or offline.

# LAYER 2 — Needs-Based Foundations
*Are your core psychological needs being met?*

Three needs function as non-negotiable nutrients for sustained motivation: **Autonomy** (I am the author of my actions), **Competence** (I am effective), and **Relatedness** (I am genuinely connected). Deprive any one — motivation dysregulates regardless of what else is in place.

---

# LAYER 3 — Cognitive Foundations
*What do you believe about your capability and worth?*

Your beliefs about whether you can succeed and whether success is worth the cost determine what you pursue before any action begins. Attribution — the story you tell about why things go wrong — shapes whether failure produces learning or shutdown.

---

## Tool 3.1 — Expectancy-Value Matrix

**What this is:** A two-axis diagnostic that reveals where motivation is stuck on your most important goals.

**What to expect:** Clear identification of whether the block is "I don't think I can" (expectancy) or "I'm not sure I want this enough / the cost is too high" (value).

**How to use it:** List your top 5 current goals or desired changes. Rate each on expectancy and value.

---

**Expectancy:** How confident are you that you can actually do this? (1 = no confidence, 10 = fully confident)
**Value:** How much do you genuinely want this outcome — after accounting for the cost? (1 = barely, 10 = completely)

| Goal / Desired Change | Expectancy (1–10) | Value (1–10) | Primary Block | Action Indicated |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | | |
| | | | | |
| | | | | |
| | | | | |
| | | | | |

**Interpreting your scores:**

| Pattern | What It Means | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| High Value, Low Expectancy | You want it but don't believe you can | Efficacy work — Layer 3 tools below |
| High Expectancy, Low Value | You could do it but don't really want to | Values clarification — Layer 5 |
| Low Expectancy, Low Value | Neither belief nor desire is present | Re-examine why this is on your list at all |
| Low Value due to cost | You want the outcome but not the price | Honest cost accounting and decision |
| High both | Action is the only missing element | Regulatory layer — implementation intentions |

**For your lowest-expectancy goal, answer:**

_______________________________________________

**Expected outcomes:** Precise diagnosis of where to intervene for each goal. Eliminates the generic "I need more motivation" narrative and replaces it with a specific target.

# LAYER 4 — Regulatory Foundations
*Can you follow through on what you've already decided?*

This layer is about execution. Most motivation failures are not failures of desire — they are failures of follow-through. The tools here close the gap between intention and behavior through structure, planning, and temporal design.

---

# LAYER 5 — Motivational Type
*Why are you actually doing what you do?*

Motivation exists on a spectrum from fear-driven compliance to genuine love of the work itself. The quality of your motivation — not just its quantity — determines how sustainable it is, how it feels, and what it costs you. This layer surfaces the real driver.

---

**What this is:** A recursive questioning practice that moves past surface-level motivation to the actual driver underneath. Borrowed from systems thinking and applied to the motivational layer.

**How to use it:** Take a goal or behavior you're currently pursuing and ask "why does this matter to me?" five times in sequence, each time responding to the answer before it.

Your environment and your relationships are producing your default behaviors whether you've chosen them to or not. Behavior follows the path of least resistance. The shape of your surroundings determines what that path is.

# LAYER 7 — Identity & Meaning
*Who are you, and what are you for?*

The most durable motivation is identity-level motivation. When a behavior is congruent with who you believe you are, it requires less willpower, survives failure more readily, and scales into meaning rather than just satisfaction. This layer addresses the deepest question: not what you want, but who you are becoming.

---

## Tool 7.1 — Identity Statement Development

**What this is:** The construction of explicit identity statements in domains where behavior change is desired. Identity statements shift the motivational question from "should I do this?" to "is this what someone like me does?"

**What to expect:** Statements that feel uncomfortable at first — because they describe who you are becoming, not who you currently are. That discomfort is the work.

---

**Format:** *"I am a person who ___________________."*

**Guidelines:** Each statement should be:
- Aspirational but believable (not fantasy, not your current default)
- Behavioral (what you do, not what you feel)
- Present tense
- Yours — not what someone else would want for you

**Expected outcomes:** A motivational reframe that removes the daily decision-making burden from key behaviors. Over time, the identity statement becomes true because it guides behavior which generates evidence which reinforces belief.

# LAYER 8 — Motivational Pathologies
*What is breaking the system?*

Motivational pathologies are system failures, not character flaws. Each one has a specific cause and a specific remedy. Treating them as moral failures — as laziness, weakness, or lack of discipline — produces shame that deepens the pathology. The tools below are diagnostic and corrective, not punitive.

# BEHAVIOR CHANGE READINESS
*Before deploying any tool — know what stage you're in.*

---

## Tool BC.1 — Stage of Change Assessment

**What this is:** A self-assessment based on the Transtheoretical Model (Prochaska & DiClemente) to identify where you actually are in the change process — and therefore which tools and approaches apply.

---

**Choose the behavior or change area you are working on:**
_______________________________________________

**Read each stage description and mark the one that most accurately fits you right now:**

☐ **Precontemplation** — I don't really see this as a problem. Or I've thought about it but I'm not seriously considering changing. Other people think I should change more than I do.

☐ **Contemplation** — I know something needs to change. I'm thinking about it. But I'm also unsure — part of me wants to change and part of me doesn't. I go back and forth.

☐ **Preparation** — I've decided to change. I'm getting ready. I may have already taken some small steps. I'm looking for a plan.

☐ **Action** — I am actively doing the new behavior. I've started. It's still new and requires effort.

☐ **Maintenance** — I've been doing this consistently for more than 6 months. It's more stable now but I still need to protect it.

☐ **Relapse** — I was making progress and fell back to the old pattern. I'm trying to figure out how to re-engage.

**My current stage:** _______________________________________________

**What this means for the tools I should focus on:**

| Stage | Focus Here |
|---|---|
| Precontemplation | Layer 7 (identity/meaning), Layer 5 (motivation type), conversations — not planning |
| Contemplation | Layer 5 (Five Whys, shame audit), Layer 3 (Expectancy-Value), Legacy Mapping |
| Preparation | Layer 4 (WOOP, implementation intentions, milestone architecture), Layer 3 (goal sharpening) |
| Action | Layer 1 (biological support), Layer 4 (tracking), Layer 6 (accountability, environment design) |
| Maintenance | Layer 7 (identity consolidation), Layer 6 (environmental design), relapse prevention |
| Relapse | Normalize → re-identify stage → re-enter; do not restart from precontemplation |

**Expected outcomes:** Using the right tool at the right time. The single most common cause of coaching failure is applying action-stage tools to pre-contemplation or contemplation clients — producing pressure, resistance, and the illusion of failure.
