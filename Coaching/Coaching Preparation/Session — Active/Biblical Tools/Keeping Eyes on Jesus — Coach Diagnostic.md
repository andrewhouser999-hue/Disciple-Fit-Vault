# Keeping Eyes on Jesus — Coach Diagnostic Tool

*Use before or during session to identify which mechanical gap is primary for this client. Most clients have 2-3 active gaps. Start with the most foundational one. Not every gap needs immediate attention — identify the primary gap and begin there. Secondary gaps often shift when the primary one is addressed.*

---

## Gap 1 — Turn Away From (aphorao)

*The client is trying to ADD Jesus to an already saturated attentional field rather than clearing space first.*

**Observable Indicators:**
- "I try to bring God into my day" — additive thinking, not substitutive
- Devotional life is squeezed in; time with God is never protected, always what remains
- Technology/media consumption is unexamined and constant
- Has never fasted; describes it as unrelated to spiritual attention
- Prayer is the last thing added, not the first thing protected

**Diagnostic Questions:**
- "Walk me through a typical morning. When does God first get your conscious attention?"
- "Is there anything in your life that gets more of your attention than you'd like it to?"
- "What would it feel like to sit in silence for 15 minutes with no input at all?"

**Recommended Starting Point:** Silence and Solitude — 10-15 minutes of actual silence before any other morning practice.

---

## Gap 2 — Beholding Depth (katoptrizomenoi)

*The client's engagement with God is too brief and too cognitively active to allow transformation to occur.*

**Observable Indicators:**
- "I read my Bible every day" — information-extraction framing, not encounter framing
- Reports no emotional or affective engagement in prayer; it feels transactional
- Can articulate theology clearly but shows little corresponding transformation
- Moves quickly through devotional content; no dwelling or rumination
- "I'm doing everything right but nothing is changing"

**Diagnostic Questions:**
- "When you read Scripture, how long do you typically stay with one passage?"
- "When was the last time something in prayer or Scripture moved you — actually changed your emotional state?"
- "What does your prayer actually look like? Walk me through it."

**Recommended Starting Point:** Lectio Divina — one short passage (4-6 verses), 20 minutes minimum, with explicit instruction to stay in each movement longer than feels productive.

---

## Gap 3 — Receiving the Covenant Gaze

*The client is performing for God rather than receiving from God. The gaze runs one direction only.*

**Observable Indicators:**
- Performance language: "I need to do better," "I keep failing God," "I don't deserve..."
- Difficulty receiving love without immediately deflecting to unworthiness or qualification
- Prayer feels like reporting to a supervisor, not encountering a Father
- Anxiety spikes when performance feels inadequate — "God must be disappointed in me"
- Circumstances function as evidence of whether God is for or against them

**Diagnostic Questions:**
- "When you're failing spiritually, what do you imagine God's face looks like toward you in that moment?"
- "Can you receive the idea that God is looking at you right now with complete delight — not because of your performance, but because you're His?"
- "Do you believe God loves you, or do you believe the love of God?" *(Manning's distinction — note the difference in the client's response)*

**Recommended Starting Point:** Receiving Prayer — 5-10 minutes with "Abba, I belong to you." Not as petition. As reality being received.

---

## Gap 4 — Counter-Narrative (distazō)

*The wave-narrative is dominant. The client has no practiced, automatic counter-account from Scripture ready under stress.*

**Observable Indicators:**
- Faith state tracks directly with circumstances — good circumstances, strong faith; hard circumstances, doubt
- No Scripture comes to mind under stress — anxiety and rumination fill the space instead
- Reacts to circumstances rather than responding from a prior framework
- "I know the truth in my head but I can't access it when I'm in it"
- Does not speak truth to themselves deliberately; listens to themselves passively instead

**Diagnostic Questions:**
- "When you're in the middle of a hard situation, what is the automatic narrative that runs in your head?"
- "What Scripture comes to mind when you're under stress — if any?"
- "Do you have a practice where you intentionally speak truth to yourself? What does that look like?"

**Recommended Starting Point:** Scripture Memorization — one passage fully committed before adding another. Suggested: Romans 8:38-39 or Isaiah 43:1-3.

---

## Gap 5 — Return Reflex

*The client has no practiced mechanism for returning to God-awareness throughout the day. The gaze is a single morning event, not a continuous orientation.*

**Observable Indicators:**
- "I pray in the morning and try to make it last" — single-event thinking
- No structured return practice during the day; long uninterrupted stretches without God-awareness
- Under stress, defaults to rumination, distraction, or control — not return to God
- "I know I should pray more" but has no concrete practice of how to return
- Sees prayer as a time block, not an orientation

**Diagnostic Questions:**
- "Between your morning devotion and bedtime, how many times do you consciously return to awareness of God?"
- "When you're in the middle of something hard during the day, what's your first instinct — what do you actually do?"
- "Have you ever tried to keep God in conscious awareness throughout a whole day? What happened?"

**Recommended Starting Point:** Breath Prayer — develop a personalized 6-8 syllable prayer; practice during transitions for one week before expanding.

---

## Gap 6 — Attentional Restoration

*The client is spiritually depleted because there is no structure that clears and restores the attentional field. The week accumulates without reset.*

**Observable Indicators:**
- No Sabbath practice; works or consumes content continuously through the week
- Reports chronic spiritual depletion — never restored, only continuing
- "I don't have time" for extended prayer; the idea feels impossible
- The daily devotional is squeezed; an extended retreat is unimaginable
- Spiritual life has no weekly rhythm — just daily attempts with no larger arc

**Diagnostic Questions:**
- "Do you have a day in the week where you stop? What does that actually look like?"
- "When was the last time you spent 2+ hours alone with God with no agenda?"
- "What would have to change for you to protect one morning a week for extended time with God?"

**Recommended Starting Point:** Sabbath — identify one day or half-day to structurally stop. Not a productive rest day. A stop.

---

## Gap 7 — Accountability Structure

*The client processes their spiritual life in isolation. No one has permission to name what they observe.*

**Observable Indicators:**
- "My relationship with God is personal/private"
- No one in their life knows the real state of their spiritual walk — only the polished version
- Spiritual struggles are processed alone; conclusions are reached independently
- Has not given anyone permission to speak into their spiritual life
- Community is social, not formational — people are present but not seeing

**Diagnostic Questions:**
- "Is there anyone in your life who knows where you actually are spiritually — not the presentable version?"
- "Have you ever given someone specific permission to name what they observe in your walk?"
- "Who, if anyone, would you call when you're spiritually sinking?"

**Recommended Starting Point:** Identify one relationship that could hold this role. Begin with one honest conversation about where you actually are.

---

## Diagnostic Summary

| Gap | Primary Signal | First Practice to Assign |
|---|---|---|
| Turn away from | Additive thinking; fragmented, unprotected attention | Silence and Solitude |
| Beholding depth | Information-only engagement; cognitive but not transformational | Lectio Divina |
| Receiving the gaze | Performance orientation; circumstances as evidence of God's favor | Receiving Prayer |
| Counter-narrative | Circumstances determine faith state; no Scripture under stress | Scripture Memorization |
| Return reflex | No mid-day return practice; prayer is a morning event only | Breath Prayer |
| Attentional restoration | Chronic depletion; no Sabbath or extended prayer | Sabbath |
| Accountability | Spiritual isolation; no one with permission to see | One honest conversation |
