# Positive Psychology & Character Strengths — PERMA Framework

## The Honest Assessment

Positive Psychology is the most misrepresented branch of psychology in coaching culture. The pop version — positive thinking, gratitude journals, affirmations — is a caricature that actively discredits the actual science. The real thing is rigorous: Martin Seligman's PERMA model has substantial empirical grounding, and the VIA Character Strengths framework (Peterson & Seligman) is built on cross-cultural research spanning 54 nations. These are not feel-good frameworks — they are precision instruments for understanding what makes human beings flourish.

The danger from the other direction: coaches who reject Positive Psychology entirely because the pop version is shallow. That's throwing out Seligman with the Instagram bathwater. The research on character strengths, positive emotions, meaning-making, and engagement has direct session application that is not available anywhere else in the CBC framework.

For Kingdom coaching specifically: Positive Psychology is the discipline most structurally aligned with a theology of image-bearing, calling, and flourishing. The PERMA model describes shalom in psychological language. Know this framework fluently.

## Elaborate Overview

### The Historical Shift

Traditional psychology, from Freud forward, was built on a deficit model: identify what is broken, eliminate the pathology, return to neutral. Seligman, as President of the American Psychological Association in 1998, named the gap: psychology had accumulated 50 years of knowledge about what goes wrong and almost none about what goes right. Positive Psychology is the formal investigation of flourishing — what allows people not merely to survive but to thrive.

Critical distinction: Positive Psychology does not deny suffering, pathology, or darkness. It adds a previously missing dimension. A Kingdom coaching integration does not choose between wound work and strengths work — it holds both. The Parable of the Soils framework and Positive Psychology are complementary, not competing.

### PERMA — The Five Pillars of Flourishing

Seligman's PERMA model identifies five domains that are intrinsically motivating, independently pursued, and empirically linked to subjective and objective wellbeing. Each is measurable. Each is coachable.

---

#### P — Positive Emotions

**What it is:** The experience of positive affective states — not as a goal in itself but as a predictor and enabler of flourishing. Positive emotions are not luxuries; they are functional.

**Broaden-and-Build Theory (Barbara Fredrickson, 2001):** The most important research in this domain. Positive emotions *broaden* the momentary thought-action repertoire (more options become visible, creative problem-solving increases, relational awareness expands) and *build* lasting personal resources — cognitive, social, physical, and psychological. The effects are not temporary.

**The 3:1 ratio (Fredrickson & Losada):** Initially proposed that flourishing required at least 3 positive emotional experiences for every negative one. Note: the mathematical modeling behind the exact ratio was later disputed. The directional finding — that positive emotional orientation produces real structural benefits — remains supported. Don't cite the exact ratio; cite the directional principle.

**What positive emotions are not:** Forced happiness, toxic positivity, or the suppression of negative experience. Fredrickson's research specifically distinguishes authentic positive emotion from performed positivity — only authentic positive emotion produces the broaden-and-build effect.

**Coaching application:** Positive emotion is a resource to cultivate, not a byproduct to hope for. Asking clients to notice, name, and amplify genuine positive experiences — not manufacture them — is a legitimate intervention.

---

#### E — Engagement

**What it is:** Full psychological investment in an activity — characterized by concentration, absorption, and loss of self-consciousness. Closely related to Csikszentmihalyi's *flow*.

**Flow (Csikszentmihalyi):** The state of optimal experience when challenge and skill are in balance — just hard enough to require full engagement, within the capacity to succeed. Flow is intrinsically rewarding; time distorts, self-consciousness disappears. It is the opposite of boredom (under-challenge) and anxiety (over-challenge).

**Engagement and signature strengths:** VIA research shows that engagement is highest when a person is using their top character strengths in meaningful ways. This is the connection between the E and the Strengths model below.

**Coaching application:** If a client is chronically disengaged, the diagnostic questions are: (1) Is the challenge level wrong? (2) Are they using their actual strengths? (3) Is meaning present? Engagement is not a mood — it is a structural condition. Fix the structure.

---

#### R — Relationships

**What it is:** Authentic, positive connections with others — the experience of being genuinely cared for and valued, and of genuinely caring for and valuing others.

**Social connection is not optional:** Research by John Cacioppo on loneliness shows that chronic loneliness has health consequences equivalent to smoking 15 cigarettes per day. Relationships are not peripheral to flourishing — they are foundational infrastructure.

**High-quality connections (Jane Dutton):** Even brief, positive interactions — not deep relationships — produce measurable increases in wellbeing, energy, and performance. Not all relationship impact requires intimacy; quality of micro-interactions matters.

**Coaching application:** A client's relational context is not background information — it is an active variable in their flourishing. The Wheel of Life already surfaces this; PERMA gives the theoretical basis for why relational deficit is urgent, not just uncomfortable.

---

#### M — Meaning

**What it is:** The sense of belonging to and serving something believed to be bigger than the self. Meaning is subjectively constructed and does not require objective validation — but it is strongly associated with spiritual, communal, and transcendent frameworks.

**Viktor Frankl's contribution:** Frankl's *Man's Search for Meaning*, built from Holocaust survival experience, argues that the human drive for meaning is primary — not a secondary derivative of pleasure-seeking. Logotherapy (meaning-centered therapy) predates Positive Psychology and provides its deepest theoretical anchor.

**Meaning vs. happiness:** Research consistently shows that meaning and happiness are related but not identical — and that meaning, not happiness, is more strongly associated with long-term wellbeing. Clients pursuing happiness often feel hollow; clients pursuing meaning often report suffering alongside deep satisfaction. Know this distinction. It is theologically significant.

**Coaching application:** The meaning question is not motivational language — it is a clinical diagnostic: *"To what is your life oriented that matters beyond your own comfort or achievement?"* A client without a functional answer to this question is structurally vulnerable to existential despair, regardless of their external success.

---

#### A — Accomplishment (Achievement)

**What it is:** Pursuing and achieving goals, mastery, and success — for its own sake, not only instrumentally.

**The accomplishment-autonomy connection:** SDT research shows that accomplishment only contributes to flourishing when it is pursued autonomously (not out of fear or shame). Accomplishment driven by performance-avoidance motivation depletes rather than builds wellbeing.

**Coaching application:** Helping clients accomplish things matters — but the *why* of accomplishment determines whether it builds flourishing or drains it. Review SDT and Approach/Avoidance Goals in parallel.

### VIA Character Strengths — The Applied Diagnostic

Developed by Martin Seligman and Christopher Peterson (2004), the VIA (Values in Action) Classification is a cross-cultural catalog of 24 character strengths organized under six core virtues. Unlike personality assessments (Big Five, MBTI), character strengths are explicitly value-laden and action-oriented — they describe not just how a person *is* but how they *operate best*.

**The six virtues and 24 strengths:**

| Virtue | Character Strengths |
|---|---|
| **Wisdom** | Creativity, Curiosity, Judgment, Love of Learning, Perspective |
| **Courage** | Bravery, Perseverance, Honesty, Zest |
| **Humanity** | Love, Kindness, Social Intelligence |
| **Justice** | Teamwork, Fairness, Leadership |
| **Temperance** | Forgiveness, Humility, Prudence, Self-Regulation |
| **Transcendence** | Appreciation of Beauty, Gratitude, Hope, Humor, Spirituality |

**Signature Strengths:** A person's top 5–7 strengths — the ones that feel like "the real me," energize rather than deplete when used, and come naturally. These are not skills (learned capacities) — they are core expressions of character.

**Research on signature strength use:**
- Using signature strengths in new ways for one week produced increases in happiness and decreases in depressive symptoms that lasted six months (Seligman et al., 2005)
- Strength use in work is correlated with higher engagement, life satisfaction, and lower stress
- Strengths developed during adolescence tend to remain stable across the lifespan — they are not mood states

**Underuse and overuse:** Strengths can be deployed below capacity (underuse) or pushed beyond the appropriate context (overuse). Curiosity is a strength; curiosity deployed as avoidance of commitment is overuse. Prudence is a strength; prudence deployed as refusal to act is underuse. The sweet spot is situational calibration.

**Free VIA Assessment:** viacharacter.org — 15 minutes, research-validated, free. Use it with clients.

## Tier 1: Empirical Foundation

| Finding | Evidence |
|---|---|
| PERMA predicts wellbeing across populations | Seligman (2011); Butler & Kern (2016) PERMA-Profiler validation across 18,000 participants |
| Signature strengths use predicts engagement and life satisfaction | Hone et al. (2015) — strengths use predicts wellbeing above and beyond personality traits |
| Broaden-and-Build Theory | Fredrickson (2001) *American Psychologist* — foundational paper with 1,000+ citations |
| Meaning > happiness for long-term wellbeing | Baumeister et al. (2013) *Journal of Positive Psychology* — giving vs. taking as happiness vs. meaning |
| Positive Psychology interventions effective | Sin & Lyubomirsky (2009) meta-analysis: 51 studies, PPIs significantly increase wellbeing and decrease depressive symptoms |

## What to Ignore

- The 3:1 positivity ratio as a precise prescription — the mathematical underpinning was retracted. The directional principle stands; the specific number does not.
- Positive Psychology as endorsement of toxic positivity — Seligman's own research includes negative emotion, grief, and difficulty as legitimate components of a flourishing life
- VIA as a personality test like MBTI — it is not. VIA is value-laden (strengths are explicitly moral) and explicitly tied to behavior, not just description
- Pop "gratitude hacks" disconnected from the theoretical base — gratitude is a real intervention with real RCT support; use the evidence-based protocols, not the app version
- "Positive thinking will change your brain" neuroscience claims without citation — the popular neuroscience overlay on Positive Psychology is frequently overblown

## Self-Directed Therapy/Coaching Questions

### Positive Emotions
- "When do you feel genuine delight — not relief, not satisfaction at a task completed, but actual delight? When was the last time?"
- "What activities or relationships consistently leave you more energized than you started? What drains you? What does the ratio look like in your life right now?"
- "What do you appreciate about your life right now that you rarely stop to acknowledge? What would it feel like to acknowledge it?"

### Engagement
- "When do you lose track of time in a good way — fully absorbed, self-consciousness disappears? How often does that happen in a given week?"
- "When you imagine the activities that use your best qualities — the things that feel like the real you — what are they? How much of your actual time is spent in them?"
- "What would you work on for free, just because it matters?"

### Relationships
- "Who do you feel genuinely known by — not admired, not needed, but *known*? Who knows the whole picture of you?"
- "Are your most important relationships giving you energy or draining it? What is the quality of the connection beneath the function?"
- "Is there a relationship you've been neglecting that, if restored, would change the texture of your life?"

### Meaning
- "What are you building that will matter after you're gone? What are you a part of that is larger than your own comfort?"
- "On an ordinary Tuesday — not a high moment — what gives your life its weight? What would make today worth having lived?"
- "If your faith were not a tradition you inherited but something you would choose from scratch today — would you choose it? What does your answer tell you?"

### Accomplishment
- "What have you accomplished in the last year that you are genuinely proud of? Not what others noticed — what you know was real and hard?"
- "Are you building toward something that matters to you, or executing someone else's definition of success?"

### Character Strengths
- "If you completed the VIA assessment — which strengths felt most like 'the real you'? Which surprised you?"
- "In what areas of life are you using your signature strengths consistently? Where are they being suppressed or underused?"
- "What would a week look like if you deliberately used each of your top five strengths at least once?"

## Coaching Application — In Session

**The PERMA audit:** At intake or in a reflective session, score each of the five domains 1–10. The resulting shape identifies where flourishing is blocked. Low M (meaning) is a different coaching target than low R (relationships) — and require different interventions.

**VIA Strengths in session:**
1. Have the client complete the free VIA assessment (viacharacter.org) before the session
2. Review their top 5–7 with them: *"Which of these feel like the real you?"*
3. Identify the domain where their strengths are most underused
4. Design one specific, small application: *"How could you bring [curiosity / bravery / kindness] into something you're facing this week?"*

**Broaden-and-Build in practice:** When a client is narrowed — stuck, anxious, or depleted — positive emotion broadens their aperture. Before problem-solving, ask: *"What is something genuinely good in your life right now?"* This is not bypassing the problem — it is restoring the cognitive bandwidth to address it.

**Meaning work when hollow achievement is presenting:** The client who has "accomplished everything" and feels nothing is usually suffering from M deficit. The accomplishment machine is running; the meaning engine is off. Don't add more achievement interventions. Confront the meaning gap directly: *"What is all of this for?"*

## Bottom Line

PERMA gives you the map of what a whole human life looks like. VIA tells you what each person's unique form of that wholeness is built from. Used together, they turn strengths-based coaching from a feel-good posture into a precise diagnostic and intervention system. The goal of Kingdom coaching is not to help people feel better — it is to help them flourish. Seligman named that same goal. The language differs. The target is the same.
