# Burnout — Recognition, Prevention & Recovery

*Practitioner reference. How to recognize burnout, distinguish it from stress and depression, and what is coachable vs. clinical.*

`[CURRICULUM]`

---

## The Honest Assessment

Burnout is real, well-researched, and frequently mishandled in two directions: dismissed as "just stress" or conflated with clinical depression. The Maslach model is well-established and gives the clearest coaching framework — burnout is a **person-environment fit problem**, not an individual deficiency, and that reframe matters for how you coach it. The coaching scope is real but bounded: early and moderate burnout are coachable; burnout that has developed into clinical depression or severe functional impairment is not. "Self-care" misses the mechanism — rest does not fix structural mismatches. The coaching value is in diagnosing which of Maslach's six worklife dimensions is most misaligned and working there.

## What to Ignore

- **"Self-care" as treatment** — rest rituals do not fix structural mismatches. When burnout is caused by chronic workload, lack of control, or values violation, individual wellness practices are insufficient.
- **Resilience coaching as burnout prevention** — when the cause is organizational or structural, coaching the individual to be tougher is mislabeled help. Diagnose the person-environment fit before prescribing individual interventions.
- **Hustle recovery framing** — treating burnout as a recoverable episode to power through before returning to identical conditions is not recovery; it is relapse setup.

## Coaching Application

**Step 1 — Distinguish burnout from stress and depression:**

| | Stress | Burnout | Depression |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Core experience** | Too much — overwhelm, urgency | Nothing left — emptiness, detachment | Pervasive sadness, hopelessness |
| **Engagement** | Overengaged | Disengaged | Withdrawn from all domains |
| **Domain** | Can be life-wide | Initially work-specific | All life domains |
| **Affect** | Anxious, reactive | Flat, cynical | Sad, anhedonic |
| **Reversibility** | Responds to rest | Requires structural change + recovery | Requires clinical intervention |

**Clinical scope flag:** Burnout and depression overlap — burnout can develop into depression. If exhaustion and disengagement have spread beyond work to all life domains with sustained low mood and anhedonia, screen for depression and refer before continuing coaching.

**Step 2 — Diagnose which worklife dimension is most misaligned (Maslach & Leiter):**

| Dimension | Signs of Mismatch | Coaching Direction |
|---|---|---|
| **Workload** | Chronically too much | Prioritization, boundary-setting, workload negotiation |
| **Control** | No autonomy over how work is done | Identify where agency exists; negotiate scope where possible |
| **Reward** | Effort not recognized (intrinsic or extrinsic) | Values clarification; connect work to intrinsic meaning |
| **Community** | Isolation, conflict, lack of support | Relationship audit; identify repair or support-building |
| **Fairness** | Perceived inequity | Distinguish actionable vs. must-be-accepted; reappraisal if latter |
| **Values** | Work violates personal values | Most serious mismatch — coaching may reveal the job itself needs to change |

Values mismatch is the coaching-strongest dimension — this is where CBT core belief work, SDT autonomy support, and identity-based coaching integrate most naturally.

**Step 3 — Recovery: what actually works (Sonnentag):**

Four recovery experiences, in order of coaching relevance:
1. **Psychological detachment** — mentally disengaging from work during off-hours. The strongest predictor. Checking email "just to stay on top of things" actively prevents recovery.
2. **Relaxation** — low-demand, low-arousal activities. Passive scrolling does not qualify.
3. **Mastery** — challenging, meaningful activity outside work. Rebuilds efficacy without work stakes.
4. **Control** — genuine choice about how non-work time is spent.

Assess which of these four the client is actually accessing. Many clients in burnout rest passively while remaining mentally tethered to work. The mastery dimension is most commonly neglected and most important for clients whose professional efficacy has eroded.

**Sleep as foundation:** Recovery coaching without addressing sleep is incomplete. Chronic sleep deprivation accelerates burnout. It is not a lifestyle preference to optimize around.

**Cross-references:**
- SDT: Burnout prevention maps onto need fulfillment — diagnose which need is most chronically frustrated. See [[Self-Determination Theory]].
- Perfectionism: Perfectionistic concerns (fear of mistakes, contingent worth) predict burnout via the over-functioning pathway. See [[Perfectionism — Adaptive vs. Maladaptive]].
- Self-efficacy: Burnout erodes efficacy; efficacy erosion deepens burnout. Recovery requires engineering mastery experiences that rebuild the evidence base. See [[Self-Efficacy Theory — Bandura Framework]].

**Clinical scope flags:**
- Burnout + depression features (pervasive anhedonia, hopelessness, functional impairment) — refer before coaching.
- Burnout + suicidal ideation — immediate referral; do not continue coaching.
- Severe functional impairment (cannot work, cannot manage basic self-care) — clinical territory.

---

## Bottom Line

Burnout is a person-environment fit problem, not a character flaw. The coaching value is in diagnosing which of the six worklife dimensions is most misaligned and working there — not prescribing rest. Distinguishing burnout from depression is the most critical diagnostic task before proceeding.
