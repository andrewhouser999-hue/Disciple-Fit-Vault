# Covenant Identity — Practitioner Qualities & Skills for the Work

*What it is: a narrative walkthrough of what a practitioner must be able to do, and must have become, to practice Covenant Identity Coaching well. Companion to [[Covenant Identity — Practitioner Competency Framework]], which holds the full domain-by-domain technical reference — this document is the readable orientation to it, not a replacement for it.*

*When used: initial practitioner self-assessment, supervision conversations, and as a standing check against drift — reread when a season of practice has started to feel purely technical.*

---

## The organizing principle

The Practitioner Competency Framework draws a hard distinction that has to anchor everything else: **skills that can be trained versus capacities that must be formed through the practitioner's own work.** Treating those as the same category is the most common way this practice gets performed badly — technically correct, relationally empty.

- **Acquired through training** — can be taught, studied, and supervised into place. Diagnostic frameworks, intervention tools, CBT-informed technique.
- **Formed through personal work** — cannot be performed from a manual. Presence, attunement, non-anxious holding of dark material, the practitioner's own inhabited covenant identity. These develop through the coach's own therapy, spiritual direction, supervision, and lived formation — not through reading about them.

A coach can execute every diagnostic stage correctly and still fail the client, because the explicit competencies are *necessary but not sufficient*. If the coach can't hold non-anxious presence, clients sense it and start managing their disclosure — hedging, shortening, giving the coach what won't provoke a flinch — instead of bringing the actual material the work requires. The implicit competencies aren't an advanced layer on top of the explicit ones; they're the container that makes the explicit work possible at all. **Formation precedes and sustains technique** — not the reverse.

## Domain 1 — Diagnostic Reading

**Explicit layer** (what the client says and how it's structured):
- Run Affections Discovery across all five layers and recognize when to move
- Map functional exclusion of God across the God-Exclusion Map's eight domains
- Identify character wound type (Warrior, Hermit, False Noble) from the pattern that's accumulated — not by slapping a label on early and working from the label
- Name the false identity specifically and personally ("I am only safe when I am accepted"), never generically
- Read stage position (1 through 4a/4b) accurately, and tell 4a and 4b apart — this is the single most consequential diagnostic call in the sequence, because the two wrong responses (cognitive content thrown at an implicit block, or soft presence offered to an active cognitive argument) both make things worse

**Implicit layer** (what the client isn't saying):
- Read affect that doesn't match content — the laugh that doesn't belong, the too-quick pivot, the conspicuous absence of an emotion you'd expect
- Track somatic signal — posture, voice, jaw, withdrawal, expansion — as data, not atmosphere
- Notice what gets circled repeatedly but never entered, and what themes keep reappearing in new costumes across sessions
- Recognize spiritual bypassing — theological fluency deployed to manage and avoid rather than to actually engage

**Ceiling:** telling a coaching-level implicit barrier (Stage 4b) apart from a clinical trauma presentation. Default to referral when uncertain.

## Domain 2 — In-Session Explicit Engagement (trainable)

- Deploy the Five Intervention Modalities by diagnostic fit, not by rotation or habit
- Run Stage 4a's CBT-informed toolkit inside coaching scope: Socratic questioning, laddering/downward arrow, thought record, behavioral experiment
- Apply the God-Representation Deepening Tool once Phase 1 diagnostic groundwork is laid
- Facilitate lament-to-trust without rushing the client past the complaint into premature resolution
- Assign and debrief Deep Work Worksheets, reading the client's actual response as diagnostic material rather than grading it pass/fail
- Hold covenant truth as the larger reality the false identity is being measured against — not correction, not a lecture, not reassurance

---

## Domain 3 — In-Session Implicit Engagement (formed, not trained)

This is the largest domain and the one that can't be faked:

- **Presence:** arrive already having set down your own material; sustain full attention to words, affect, energy, silence, and what's absent, all at once; stay non-anxious when shameful or dark material surfaces instead of steering the client toward relief
- **Somatic use of self:** treat your own body as a data source — notice the pull to comfort, the urge to redirect, the unexpected contraction, and read those as information about the room, not just your own reaction; observe the client's body before you interpret it; tell processing silence (protect it) from avoidance silence (gently enter it)
- **Stage 4b response specifically:** when truth is intellectually received but doesn't move anything, don't add more content — slow down, invite the client's attention inward ("what do you notice happening in you right now — not what you think, what you actually notice"), speak the truth personally rather than propositionally, hold steady presence while they sit in the stuck place, and return to the same truth across sessions rather than expecting single-session breakthrough
- **Imagination and metaphor:** use these to reach what direct analysis can't — Ignatian imaginative prayer, future-self/wisest-self framing
- **The relational field itself:** name what's happening between coach and client in real time when the dynamic in the room *is* the diagnostic material; deliver calibrated challenge — grounded in what the client already said they want, naming the pattern rather than the person, followed by curiosity rather than answers, and only from genuine care
- **Healing prayer (advanced, narrow criteria):** deployed only when covenant truth has been cognitively received across multiple sessions with no felt movement, affect is present but arrested, explicit tools have stopped working, and the client has asked for prayer unprompted. This is not trainable from a workshop — it develops through the coach's own practiced experience of healing prayer.

## Domain 4 — Between-Session Tool Assignment

- Know the full tool inventory and the specific conditions under which each is appropriate
- Check contraindications before assigning tools like *Secured in Father* — especially for clients with dissociative features or active trauma dysregulation
- Assign commitments as specific implementation intentions (when, exactly what) rather than vague aspirations
- Debrief the following session and read the client's response to the assignment as diagnostic data
- Recognize when a tool isn't landing and adjust — drop back to schema-level work, shift from declaration to lament, or increase in-session relational contact before re-assigning solo practice

---

## Domain 5 — Scope Boundary and Referral

- Know exactly where the coaching ceiling sits: implicit barriers rooted in unprocessed trauma need clinical work, not more coaching
- Recognize referral triggers precisely — somatic dysregulation, dissociative features, active clinical presentation, patterns the client can't engage at a coaching level
- Resist the pull to extend scope just because the relationship has gone deep and trust is high — the ceiling doesn't move because rapport did
- Frame referral as continuation of care, not as rejection or failure

---

## Domain 6 — Coach Formation (non-negotiable, not enrichment)

These are professional requirements, not nice-to-haves:

- **Ongoing supervision** — formal or trusted peer, as standard practice, not something sought out only after something goes wrong
- **Personal formation work** — the coach's own therapy, spiritual direction, or peer coaching, sustained as an ongoing discipline
- **Pre-session ritual** — a consistent practice of asking "what am I carrying today that is mine and not this client's?" before every single session
- **Post-session debrief** — regularly reviewing what was missed, where the coach's own material entered the room, and what challenge was swallowed instead of named
- **A lived theology of suffering** — not studied, grown. Clients in genuine suffering register immediately whether the coach is actually comfortable there or quietly anxious to move them out of it
- **The coach's own covenant identity, actually inhabited** — not just taught. A coach directing a client toward something the coach isn't themselves living into, even partially and in process, will eventually either perform the material or project their own unresolved version of it onto the client

---

## The floor claim

A coach can only take a client as deep as the coach has been willing to go themselves. Everything in Domain 6 exists because Domains 1–5 don't work reliably without it.
