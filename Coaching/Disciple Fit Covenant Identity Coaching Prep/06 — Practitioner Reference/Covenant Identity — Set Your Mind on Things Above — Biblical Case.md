# Set Your Mind on Things Above — The Biblical Case for Formation

*What this document is:* The exegetical and theological case that Covenant Identity Coaching is the structured, guided practice of what Paul commands in Colossians 3:1–2 — the active, sustained reorientation of the whole inner life toward the reality of the believer's secured identity in the risen, reigning Christ. This is not a session tool. It is the biblical grounding for why the coaching work is what it is.

---

## The Command

Colossians 3:1–2 (ESV):
> "If then you have been raised with Christ, seek the things that are above, where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God. Set your minds on things that are above, not on things that are on earth."

Two verbs carry the command. The first — **ζητεῖτε** (*zēteite*, "seek") — is a present imperative: ongoing, directional pursuit. Not a one-time adjustment or a spiritual mood. The same language Jesus used in Matthew 6:33: *"Seek first the kingdom."* The second — **φρονεῖτε** (*phroneite*, "set your mind") — carries more weight than its English translation suggests.

In Greek, **φρονέω** names the whole disposition of mind: not what you occasionally think about, but what your inner life is fundamentally *oriented toward* — the evaluative compass, the operating system that assigns weight to what is real, urgent, threatening, and worth wanting. Paul uses the same root in Philippians 2:5 (*"have this mind which was also in Christ Jesus"*) and in Romans 8:5–6, where he draws a life-or-death contrast between the *φρόνημα* of the flesh and the *φρόνημα* of the Spirit — not two different thought-topics, but two entirely different operating orientations.

To *set your mind on things above* is not to think about the right subjects. It is to reorient the whole inner compass by which you perceive and move through the world.

---

## What "Above" Actually Is

Paul does not leave the content undefined. He names it immediately: *"where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God."* This is not:

- Heaven as a future destination to cultivate detachment toward
- A category of "spiritual things" as opposed to material things (Platonic dualism)
- An escape from embodied, earthly life

It is a **person and his present reign.** The language of "right hand" draws on Psalm 110:1 — the Old Testament text cited more often in the New Testament than any other — which names the messianic king seated at God's right hand as the one exercising active, present lordship. *Above* is the realm where the resurrected Christ currently reigns: the already-inaugurated order of the Kingdom, decisive and real, though not yet fully visible.

Paul extends this in verses 3–4:
> "For you have died, and your life is hidden with Christ in God. When Christ who is your life appears, then you also will appear with him in glory."

Your true identity is not aspirational. It is not a hoped-for future version. It is **already secured** — hidden with Christ in God. Not at risk. Not something to produce or defend. Real and fixed, but not yet fully revealed. This is the reality "above" that the mind is called to dwell in: Christ's reign, and the believer's life as one who is already raised, already held, already located there.

---

## The Indicative Before the Imperative

Paul's grammar is deliberate and load-bearing: *"If then you have been raised with Christ"* — a first-class conditional in Greek, assumed true, not hypothetical — *"seek the things that are above."* The command rests on an accomplished fact.

The indicative (what is true of you in union with Christ) precedes and grounds the imperative (what you are therefore called to do). This is Paul's consistent pattern throughout his letters. Romans 6:11 names the same move: *"So you also must consider yourselves dead to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus."* The word translated "consider" — **λογίζεσθε** — means *reckon*, *account*, *align your inner life to the fact that is already true.*

Formation, for Paul, is not striving toward an identity you hope to acquire. It is learning to live from an identity already given. The direction of the movement is critical: not "become someone raised with Christ" but "you *are* raised; now set your mind accordingly."

---

## What the Mind Is Up Against

If the command is to set the mind on things above, what resists it? Paul names this directly in verses 5–9. The contrast term — "things on earth" — does not mean physical or material things as a category. It means the evaluative economy of the **old self**: covetousness (which Paul calls idolatry), anger, malice, slander, lying — a whole system of perception calibrated to scarcity, threat, status, and appetite. Romans 8:5 calls it the *φρόνημα* of the flesh: an inner orientation running on old-self rules.

These patterns do not dissolve at conversion. They have been forming for years — through wound, through repeated relational experience, through the thousand daily choices that groove a way of seeing. They feel *more real* than covenant truth. More immediate. More evidently relevant to what is happening right now. The person who has genuinely received the truth that their life is "hidden with Christ in God" may still run an entire morning on old-self categories: fear, comparison, craving, reactivity, the need to prove.

This is not because the covenant truth is not real. It is because the mind has not yet been **trained** to operate from it. Matthew 6:21 is the underlying diagnostic: *"Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also."* The *φρόνημα* follows treasure. Formation is the process of relocating the operative treasure — not by willpower, but by sustained, Spirit-enabled return to the reality of Christ and one's covenant standing in him.

---

## What the Command Actually Requires

The present tense of both imperatives — *seek* (ongoing) and *set your mind* (cultivate and maintain) — rules out a passive or once-for-all reading. Paul is not describing an experience to receive; he is describing a practice to sustain.

His contemporaries would have recognized the concept of **hexis** — the formed capacity that results from repeated practice, the settled disposition that eventually becomes second nature. The New Testament takes this up explicitly in Hebrews 5:14: *"the mature, who have their powers of discernment trained by constant practice to distinguish good from evil."* The word translated "trained" — **γεγυμνασμένα** — is from the same root as gymnasium. Maturity, for the author of Hebrews, is not accumulated insight; it is formed capacity. The mind that sets itself on things above is not the mind that has had one breakthrough; it is the mind that has been trained into a new operating reality.

This training is not technique. It is not management of thought-content by willpower. It is the repeated, intentional return of the whole inner person to the reality "above" — Christ's reign, one's own secured identity in him — until that reality begins to function as the actual evaluative ground from which life is lived. The Spirit does the transforming work (2 Cor 3:18); the believer creates the conditions by returning, attending, receiving, and declaring what is true.

---

## Why This Coaching Process Is Exactly This

Covenant Identity Coaching is not self-improvement with biblical language added. It is the structured, guided practice of what Colossians 3 commands.

**The diagnostic work** surfaces where the *φρόνημα* is actually oriented — what the inner life is treating as real, urgent, and determinative. False identity structures are not primarily moral failures; they are misoriented evaluative compasses, minds operating on "earthly" rules (performance, scarcity, threat, approval) rather than on the secured reality of who the believer already is in Christ. The affections diagnostic and false identity work are asking the Colossians 3 question at the root level: *what is your mind actually set on?*

**The covenant truth work** is the progressive installation of what is already true — not creating a new identity, but training the mind to operate from the one already given. The declaration practice is λογίζεσθε: *reckon* what is real; rehearse it until the inner life begins to run on it rather than on the old-self economy. This is not positive thinking. It is the deliberate alignment of the mind with an accomplished fact about the believer's standing before God.

**The formation disciplines assigned between sessions** are the ongoing ζητεῖτε — the active, directional seeking that keeps the inner compass pointed toward Christ's reality rather than drifting back into old calibrations. They are not technique; they are the conditions in which the Spirit does what only the Spirit can do.

**The whole arc of the engagement** — from diagnostic to covenant truth to installation to integrated practice — is the embodied answer to a single command: *set your mind on things above, where Christ is.*

The coaching work is not an addition to the spiritual life. It is the spiritual life, taken seriously enough to practice.
