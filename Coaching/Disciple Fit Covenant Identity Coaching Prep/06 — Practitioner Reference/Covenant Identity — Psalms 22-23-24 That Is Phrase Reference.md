# Covenant Identity — Psalms 22-23-24 "That Is" Phrase Reference

## What This Document Is

A practitioner reference showing each key phrase of the Psalm 22-23-24 triptych with a single-line definition — what that phrase names about the human condition, the covenant relationship, or the redemptive arc. Used for orienting the practitioner to the triptych's emotional and theological range, and as raw material for session language, formation anchors, and declaration prompts.

## Psalm 22 — The Cry and the Answer (KJV)

*The arc of Psalm 22: desolation → wound → covenant anchor → plea → suffering → vindication. Not every verse is listed — only the phrases that carry the arc.*

**My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?** — That's the desolation.

**Why art thou so far from helping me, and from the words of my roaring?** — That's felt abandonment.

**O my God, I cry in the daytime, but thou hearest not** — That's the silence of God.

**But thou art holy, O thou that inhabitest the praises of Israel** — That's the anchor held in the dark.

**Our fathers trusted in thee: they trusted, and thou didst deliver them** — That's the covenant precedent.

**I am a worm, and no man; a reproach of men, and despised of the people** — That's the wound.

**All they that see me laugh me to scorn** — That's the exposure.

**Thou art he that took me out of the womb** — That's the covenant origin.

**Be not far from me; for trouble is near; for there is none to help** — That's the plea.

**I am poured out like water, and all my bones are out of joint** — That's the depletion.

**They pierced my hands and my feet** — That's the suffering.

**They part my garments among them, and cast lots upon my vesture** — That's the stripping.

**But be not thou far from me, O LORD: O my strength, haste thee to help me** — That's the turn.

**For he hath not despised nor abhorred the affliction of the afflicted; neither hath he hidden his face from him; but when he cried unto him, he heard** — That's the answer.

**All the ends of the world shall remember and turn unto the LORD** — That's the vindication.

**They shall come, and shall declare his righteousness unto a people that shall be born, that he hath done this** — That's the testimony.

## Psalm 23 — The Shepherd's Care (KJV)

**The LORD is my shepherd** — That's a relationship.

**I shall not want** — That's supply.

**He maketh me to lie down in green pastures** — That's rest.

**He leadeth me beside the still waters** — That's refreshment.

**He restoreth my soul** — That's healing.

**He leadeth me in the paths of righteousness** — That's guidance.

**For his name's sake** — That's grace.

**Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death** — That's the trial.

**I will fear no evil** — That's courage.

**For thou art with me** — That's presence.

**Thy rod and thy staff, they comfort me** — That's protection.

**Thou preparest a table before me in the presence of mine enemies** — That's honor.

**Thou anointest my head with oil** — That's consecration.

**My cup runneth over** — That's abundance.

**Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life** — That's assurance.

**And I will dwell in the house of the LORD for ever** — That's eternity.

## Psalm 24 — The King of Glory (KJV)

**The earth is the LORD's, and the fulness thereof; the world, and they that dwell therein** — That's ownership.

**For he hath founded it upon the seas, and established it upon the floods** — That's authority.

**Who shall ascend into the hill of the LORD? or who shall stand in his holy place?** — That's the standard.

**He that hath clean hands, and a pure heart; who hath not lifted up his soul unto vanity, nor sworn deceitfully** — That's the qualification.

**He shall receive the blessing from the LORD, and righteousness from the God of his salvation** — That's the reward.

**This is the generation of them that seek him, that seek thy face, O Jacob** — That's the community.

**Lift up your heads, O ye gates; and be ye lift up, ye everlasting doors; and the King of glory shall come in** — That's the return.

**Who is this King of glory? The LORD strong and mighty, the LORD mighty in battle** — That's the Victor.

**Lift up your heads, O ye gates; even lift them up, ye everlasting doors; and the King of glory shall come in** — That's the certainty.

**Who is this King of glory? The LORD of hosts, he is the King of glory** — That's the answer.
