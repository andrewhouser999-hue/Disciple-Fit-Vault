# Covenant Identity — Matched Declaration Architecture

**What this is:** The Stage 3 deployment reference for wound-matched covenant truth. Five identity tracks. Four are matched to discrete wound categories — not because other texts don't apply, but because these texts engage the exact lie structure each wound produces. The fifth (Track 5 — The Impostor / Constructed Self, added 2026-07-17) is not wound-specific; it addresses the general false-self construction dynamic that underlies all four wound-matched tracks and that also presents on its own in diffuse-conditioning clients with no single traceable injury. Includes wound markers, the false identity statement, the ESV declaration text, exegetical rationale for the match, deployment timing, and connection to the Lie Eliminator and Identity Installer.

**When used:** Stage 3 (Covenant Truth Received). After Stage 2 has named the specific false identity, this document tells the practitioner which declaration framework to reach for and how to deploy it. Also used in Lie Eliminator development (counter-truth sourcing) and Identity Installer category work.

**Who uses it:** Practitioner only.

## How Declaration Works — Before Deploying Any Track

Declaration is not positive affirmation. Four conditions must be present for it to reach depth rather than produce cognitive assent that recedes under stress.

**Specific** — The declaration must address the named lie, not a generic alternative. "God loves me" is not declaration; it is a theological statement. "God chose me in Christ before the foundation of the world — before the failure that still defines how I see myself" is declaration. The more precisely the counter-truth addresses the specific charge, the more it can reach the implicit encoding where the lie lives.

**Anchored** — The declaration must be tied to covenant text that carries authority beyond the client's current emotional state. The text is not support material for the declaration — it *is* the declaration. The client is not generating a belief; they are agreeing with what has been spoken by God in covenant. Authority matters because the wound's lie also carries a kind of authority — internalized accusation that feels like fact. The counter-truth must carry at least equal authority.

**Embodied** — Spoken aloud, written by hand, or prayed directly — not thought. The implicit memory system that encodes the wound is not primarily a conceptual system. Cognitive acknowledgment ("I know this is true") does not reach it. The body must be engaged. Spoken declaration, written declaration, and vocal prayer each access a different registration channel. For Stage 4b presentations, the embodied dimension is non-negotiable.

**Rehearsed** — Repeated until the covenant truth becomes the default operating assumption rather than a position the client has to consciously recall. The goal is not that the client can produce the declaration when asked; it is that the declaration is what plays when the wound is triggered. This requires sustained between-session practice, not one strong session moment.

**The Stage 3 activation requirement:** Before introducing the declaration, verify that the implicit belief is emotionally live in the room — felt, not just named. Declaration spoken into cognitive acknowledgment produces extinction: temporary suppression that returns under stress. Declaration spoken into an activated implicit belief creates conditions for reconsolidation: the lie is replaced rather than overridden. If the belief cannot be activated, return to Stage 1 and deepen the affections picture before proceeding.

## Track 1 — Abandonment / Orphan

### Wound Markers

- God-image: absent, distant, personally unreliable ("there for others, not for me")
- Prayer quality: sending letters into empty space; no expectation of personal response; can pray in public settings more easily than in private
- Relational pattern: hyper-self-sufficiency presented as strength; difficulty receiving care; tests relationships before trusting them; expects to be left
- Behavioral signature: figures things out alone even when help is available; difficulty delegating; interprets silence or delay as abandonment
- In-session signal: may test the coaching relationship — arriving late, missing a session, probing how the practitioner responds to perceived failures

### False Identity Statement

*"I am alone. No one comes. God is present for others but not for me. I am the one people leave."*

### Declaration Text — Romans 8:14-17 (ESV)

> For all who are led by the Spirit of God are sons of God. For you did not receive the spirit of slavery to fall back into fear, but you have received the Spirit of adoption as sons, by whom we cry, "Abba! Father!" The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God, and if children, then heirs—heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ, provided we suffer with him in order that we may also be glorified with him.

### Why This Text for This Wound

**"Spirit of adoption"** (pneuma huiothesias — pronounced NYOO-mah hwee-oh-theh-SEE-as) — adoption is the legal opposite of abandonment. The orphan spirit's organizing experience is: "I have no permanent place in a household." Adoption places the client permanently in the household by a legal act of the father, not by the quality of the child's behavior or worthiness.

**"By whom we cry, 'Abba! Father!'"** — Abba (Aramaic, pronounced AH-bah) is intimate family address, not formal religious language. The word itself is evidence of what the wound has blocked: the orphan spirit cannot access Abba because it does not believe the father is present and safe. The text says this cry is produced by the Spirit, not generated by the client's emotional capacity. The practitioner can frame it: *"You are not trying to feel something that isn't there. The Spirit is already crying this inside you. You are learning to agree with what is already being said about you."*

**"The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit"** — the client's inner sense of abandonment does not have the final word about their status. There are two testimonies: the wound's testimony ("I am alone") and the Spirit's testimony ("you are a child of God"). The text says the Spirit is actively witnessing against the abandonment verdict.

**"Spirit of slavery to fall back into fear"** — names the presenting experience precisely. Fear and isolation are the emotional signature of the abandonment wound. The text names it and distinguishes it from the adoption reality — not to dismiss the fear, but to locate it correctly: it is the old register, not the current address.

### Exegetical Note for Practitioner

The "we cry" in verse 15 is communal — "we" not "you" or "I." The orphan spirit isolates the client into believing their aloneness is unique and permanent. The adoption positions the client inside a community of those crying Abba together. The declaration is not only "you are not alone before God" — it is "you are not alone among those crying this." This communal dimension can be introduced when the client is ready for it; at entry it may be too abstract.

### Deployment Timing

Stage 3, after Stage 1 Affections has confirmed the abandonment affection structure and Stage 2 has named the specific false identity ("I am the one people leave," "no one comes for me," etc.). Do not deploy at entry — the wound is defended by the self-sufficiency structure and the declaration will be heard as nice but impersonal. Deploy when the abandonment has been inhabited enough that the alternative is genuinely needed rather than merely pleasant.

**God-representation check before deployment:** The Absent/Distant God-image almost always accompanies this wound. If the client is receiving God as distant, the Abba declaration will misfire — it will be received as theological information about what should be true while experientially the father remains distant. Run God-Representation Deepening Tool work before or alongside Stage 3 declaration for this wound category.

### Lie Eliminator Connection

Primary entries targeted: "I am ultimately alone," "No one will come for me," "I am the one people leave," "God is not personally present to me." Romans 8:14-17 is the primary counter-truth source for each.

### Identity Installer Connection

Primary layers: **Known and Chosen** (Layer 2), **Adopted Sonship** (Layer 4), **Indwelt by Spirit** (Layer 6).

## Track 2 — Performance / Slave Identity

### Wound Markers

- God-image: transactional; God as employer, scorekeeper, or merit evaluator
- Presenting: exhaustion from sustained output with no sense of sufficiency; worth is contingent on usefulness; panic when production drops
- Prayer quality: reporting (updating God on what the client has done) or requesting (with implicit assumption that request viability depends on recent performance record)
- Behavioral signature: inability to rest without justification; spiritual practices tracked as obligations; compulsive helpfulness; difficulty receiving without reciprocating
- In-session signal: often presents as high-functioning client who is now burning out; reports spiritual dryness despite consistent practice; has extensive spiritual activity and thin encounter; calls their performance "dedication" or "faithfulness"

### False Identity Statement

*"My worth is what I produce. I earn my place. If I stop performing, I lose my position. I am hired, not beloved."*

### Declaration Text — Galatians 4:4-7 (ESV)

> But when the fullness of time had come, God sent forth his Son, born of woman, born under the law, to redeem those who were under the law, so that we might receive adoption as sons. And because you are sons, God has sent the Spirit of his Son into our hearts, crying, "Abba! Father!" So you are no longer a slave, but a son, and if a son, then an heir through God.

### Why This Text for This Wound

**"No longer a slave, but a son"** — the most direct address in the canon of the slave identity. Not "a better slave" or "a more valued employee" — a change in category. The false identity statement is "I am hired." The text names that exact category and announces a transfer out of it. The precision is the power.

**"Born under the law, to redeem those who were under the law"** — the performance system (law as the mechanism of worth) is named and addressed. Christ entered the exact system the wound has the client operating in, fulfilled it completely, and released the client from it. The performance client does not need to perform better; they need to understand that Christ's performance is the completed work from which they now draw.

**"Heir through God"** — inheritance is not earned; it is received by virtue of relationship. The heir does not generate their inheritance through output. They receive it as a child of the household. This is the sharpest possible contrast to the hired-servant model: the client is not working toward a wage; they are receiving what has already been secured for them.

**"Crying 'Abba! Father!'"** — Abba (Aramaic, pronounced AH-bah) bridges to Track 1 for blended presentations. The performance identity and the abandonment wound frequently co-occur: the client has been performing to secure the presence and approval they could not receive as a given. The Abba cry is also the Track 2 declaration — you are not hired to address the father; you are born to him.

**"The fullness of time"** — God's initiative, not the client's performance, is the trigger for redemption. The timing is God's. The act is God's. Nothing in the client's performance record advanced or delayed it. This directly counters the implicit belief that the client's effort is what makes grace available to them.

### Exegetical Note for Practitioner

The contrast is doulos (slave, pronounced DOO-los) → huios (son, pronounced hwee-OSS). The key clarification for this wound: the client is not being told to relax their standards or to stop being responsible. They are being told they are in the wrong ontological category for understanding their relationship to God. They have been relating to God as an employer and to themselves as an employee. The text announces that the category has changed. The identity statement is not "your performance is good enough" — it is "performance is not the relevant category for this relationship."

### Deployment Timing

Stage 3, after the slave identity has been named specifically in Stage 2. This wound often requires the most Stage 1 investment before Stage 3 is viable — the performance structure is the most socially reinforced wound category. The client has received approval for their productivity their entire life. The performance identity must be genuinely named and grieved — not just intellectually identified — before the declaration can be received as liberation rather than as a threat to the only identity structure they have known.

**Watch:** If the client receives "no longer a slave" as permission to stop performing rather than as a category change, the identity work is not yet specific enough. Return to Stage 2.

### Lie Eliminator Connection

Primary entries targeted: "My worth is what I produce," "I earn my place with God," "I am only valuable when useful," "If I stop performing I will lose my position." Galatians 4:4-7 is the primary counter-truth source.

### Identity Installer Connection

Primary layers: **Adopted Sonship** (Layer 4), **Co-heir with Christ** (Layer 5), **Loved as Son** (Layer 8).

## Track 3 — Shame / Unworthiness

### Wound Markers

- God-image: split — either punitive (God agrees the client is disqualified) or permissive (God doesn't actually see or care, which is experienced as absence rather than grace)
- Presenting: self-reduction language ("I'm just...," "I don't deserve..."); pre-emptive rejection (withdrawing before being rejected); deflects affirmation; hiding behavior
- Spiritual signature: convinced the invitation to draw near is for others; comparison-driven spiritual assessment ("at least I'm not as bad as..."); unworthiness operates as identity, not as humility
- Behavioral signature: difficulty with direct eye contact or sustained positive attention; minimizes their own needs; anticipates rejection in ambiguous situations
- In-session signal: the shame wound produces a visible contraction when the truth is spoken directly at the client — they want to look away from something being said to them. Watch for deflection, minimizing, or re-direction when affirmation is offered.

### False Identity Statement

*"I am too much and not enough simultaneously. I cannot be received as I am. My flaw is not what I did — it is what I am. I am disqualified."*

### Declaration Text — Ephesians 1:3-8 (ESV)

> Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us in Christ with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places, even as he chose us in him before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and blameless before him. In love he predestined us for adoption to himself as sons through Jesus Christ, according to the purpose of his will, to the praise of his glorious grace, with which he has blessed us in the Beloved. In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of our sins, according to the riches of his grace, which he lavished on us, in all wisdom and insight.

### Why This Text for This Wound

**"Chose us in him before the foundation of the world"** — the election precedes everything. Before the client's flaw existed, before the wound was inflicted, before the failure that still writes their self-understanding — chosen. The shame wound's organizing belief is "I am disqualified by what I am." Election is the exact counter: the choosing precedes any basis for disqualification.

**"Holy and blameless before him"** — this is declared status, not achieved status. The shame wound is driven by the experiential record of falling short. The text declares a standing that is not derived from the record — "before him" means in God's sight, in God's verdict, not in the client's self-assessment.

**"Blessed us in the Beloved"** — the acceptance is located in Christ, not in the client's condition. The client is not received because they have achieved blamelessness; they are received in the one who is blameless. The shame wound asks "am I acceptable?" The text reframes the question: acceptable in whom? The standing is located in the Beloved, not in the client's current condition.

**"Grace lavished on us"** — the excess language is specific to the shame wound. Shame believes grace is rationed proportionally to worthiness — dispensed carefully to those who qualify for it. "Lavished" (eperisseusen — pronounced eh-peh-RIS-syoo-sen — overflowed, poured out in abundance) directly contradicts the ration model. The shame client does not need to qualify for measured grace; they are standing in overflowing grace.

**"In love"** — the predestination is driven by love, not merit assessment. The shame wound reads its origin as liability (something is wrong with me that produced this wound or this sin). The text locates the origin of the divine initiative in love, not in a merit review.

### Exegetical Note for Practitioner

"In the Beloved" (en tō ēgapēmenō — pronounced en toh ay-gah-pay-MEH-no — v.6) is the pivotal phrase. The client's standing is located in Christ, not in the client's condition. This means the shame wound's central claim — "I cannot be received as I am" — is addressed not by arguing about whether the client's condition is actually as bad as the wound says, but by relocating the basis of reception entirely. The declaration is not "you are not actually flawed" — it is "your reception is not determined by your condition; it is determined by whose you are."

### Deployment Timing

Stage 3, but this wound requires the most careful timing of any track. Shame responds to premature declaration by intensifying — the gap between "chosen before the foundation of the world" and "I am disqualified by what I am" is wide enough that early declaration can register as mockery. The false identity must be specifically named, the shame experienced as real in the room, and the wound inhabited enough that the alternative is genuinely desired. The Stage 3 activation requirement (reconsolidation vs. extinction) is most critical for this track. Do not rush.

**God-representation check:** Shame clients split on the God-image in opposite directions. Check which image is operational before deployment — the punitive God-image requires different framing than the permissive. For the punitive image, the "in love" and "in the Beloved" language is primary. For the permissive/absent image, the "chose us" and "holy and blameless before him" language is primary.

### Lie Eliminator Connection

Primary entries targeted: "I am fundamentally flawed," "I cannot be received as I am," "My flaw disqualifies me," "I am too much and not enough." Ephesians 1:3-8 is the primary counter-truth source.

### Identity Installer Connection

Primary layers: **Imago Dei** (Layer 1), **Known and Chosen** (Layer 2), **New Creation** (Layer 3), **Loved as Son** (Layer 8).

## Track 4 — Condemnation / Accusation

### Wound Markers

- God-image: punitive; God as prosecutor holding a permanent record
- Presenting: guilt that does not resolve with confession; compulsive return to past failures; hypersensitivity to criticism; cannot receive forgiveness as permanent
- Spiritual signature: repeated confession of the same sin; does not believe they have been actually forgiven or that forgiveness covers their specific case; internal accuser is present and authoritative
- Behavioral signature: self-punishing patterns; in conflict, immediately assumes they are in the wrong; anticipates punishment in neutral situations
- Two forms: (a) explicit — "I have done something for which I cannot be forgiven"; (b) implicit — general anxiety and self-punishment without a nameable guilt source; the condemnation operates as a climate, not as a specific charge

### False Identity Statement

*"The record against me is real and permanent. I am the sum of what I have done wrong. Forgiveness is available in principle but does not actually cover me. I am still condemned."*

### Declaration Text — Romans 8:1, 31-34, 38-39 (ESV)

> There is therefore now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus.

> What then shall we say to these things? If God is for us, who can be against us? He who did not spare his own Son but gave him up for us all, how will he not also with him graciously give us all things? Who shall bring a charge against those whom God has chosen? It is God who justifies. Who is to condemn? Christ Jesus is the one who died—more than that, who was raised—who is at the right hand of God, who indeed is interceding for us.

> For I am sure that neither death nor life, nor angels nor rulers, nor things present nor things to come, nor powers, nor height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord.

### Why This Text for This Wound

**"Now no condemnation"** — the temporal marker is load-bearing. Not "eventually acquitted" or "conditionally released" — now. The condemnation wound lives in two tenses: the past ("I have done what cannot be undone") and the future ("I will eventually be exposed and found guilty"). "Now" addresses both: the verdict is present and final.

**"Who shall bring a charge?"** — the legal register is explicit and intentional. This wound operates in legal categories — guilt, accusation, record, verdict. Many pastoral responses try to move the client out of the legal frame into an emotional one ("God loves you"). Romans 8 stays in the legal frame and wins the case there. The question "who shall bring a charge?" is a courtroom challenge. The answer is: no one has standing. God is the judge, and the judge has already justified.

**"Who is to condemn? Christ Jesus is the one who died"** — the only one with standing to condemn is the one who bore the sin. That one has used his standing not to condemn but to intercede. The accuser's voice inside the client carries apparent authority. The text names who has actual authority (Christ) and what that authority is doing (interceding, not condemning).

**"Nothing shall separate"** (vv.38-39) — the condemnation wound specializes in exceptions: "God's love covers others, but not after what I've done." The list in vv.38-39 functions as an explicit exception-closer. Not a general assurance — a specific enumeration that closes every door the wound's lie can open. "Nor anything else in all creation" is the catch-all that covers whatever the client would name as the exception.

**"Interceding for us"** — Christ at the right hand is not waiting to prosecute; he is actively speaking for the client. The internal accuser's testimony is being actively countered by the one at the right hand of God. The client is not alone in the courtroom.

### Exegetical Note for Practitioner

Romans 8:1 is often received as information — "that's good to know." The condemnation wound does not need information about the verdict; it needs the verdict to land as personally applicable to the specific case. Before deploying v.1, help the client name the specific charge — not "I feel condemned" but "I believe the charge against me is ___." Then the declaration is spoken directly at the named charge: "There is now no condemnation for this. Not in principle — for this." The specificity is what allows it to reach the implicit level rather than hovering as a general theological truth.

### Deployment Timing

Stage 3, after the specific accusation structure has been named in Stage 2. The condemnation wound requires naming the specific charge before declaration work can hit the target. "You are not condemned" addressed to a vague sense of guilt produces less than "there is now no condemnation for the specific accusation you have been living under." Use Stage 2 work to get specific before deploying Stage 3 declaration.

**Implicit form note:** When the condemnation presents as climate rather than specific charge (the client cannot name what they feel condemned for), Stage 1 Layer 4 God-Exclusion Map work is needed before Stage 3 — the punitive God-image must be surfaced before declaration can address it.

### Lie Eliminator Connection

Primary entries targeted: "I am not actually forgiven for ___," "The record against me is permanent," "God's love doesn't cover this specific thing," "I am what I have done wrong." Romans 8:1, 31-34, 38-39 is the primary counter-truth source.

### Identity Installer Connection

Primary layers: **New Creation** (Layer 3), **Loved as Son** (Layer 8), **Being Conformed to Christ** (Layer 10).

## Track 5 — The Impostor / Constructed Self

**Structural note:** This track differs in kind from Tracks 1-4. Each of those names a specific accusation against the client ("you are alone," "you are hired," "you are disqualified," "you are guilty") and matches it to the scriptural framework that answers that exact charge. Track 5 does not answer a specific accusation — it addresses the operating rule underneath all four: that identity itself must be earned, built, and defended rather than received as already given. Most clients will meet Track 5 as a foundational or closing layer alongside a wound-matched track. Some — particularly diffuse-conditioning presentations with no single traceable injury (see Character Wound Diagnostic Tool, "Before Typing" section) — will meet it as the primary track.

### Wound Markers

- God-image: variable; not a single characteristic distortion the way Tracks 1-4 have one. The impostor pattern can coexist with almost any God-image because its organizing anxiety is about the self, not primarily about God.
- Presenting: identity felt as contingent on ongoing evidence — achievement, image management, being useful, being seen, being liked — rather than as a settled fact. A chronic, low-grade need to keep proving that does not resolve with success, because success only re-funds the account rather than closing the case.
- Behavioral signature: image management persists even in private, unwitnessed moments; discomfort with unstructured time that produces no visible output; difficulty answering "who are you" apart from role, function, or relationship to others' approval; often presents as high-functioning across multiple domains rather than impaired in one — the construction is diffuse, not localized to a single fear.
- In-session signal: the client can often state covenant truths accurately and still report "I know that, but it doesn't feel like the real me." Watch for language of a true self that is elsewhere, behind, or not yet arrived at, and for exhaustion from "keeping it up" without being able to name specifically what "it" is.

### False Identity Statement

*"I am who I perform, produce, or am perceived to be. I must build and defend this self because there is no self underneath it that could stand on its own. Every other version of me is safer than the real one, because I do not know if the real one is enough."*

### Declaration Text — Mark 1:9-11; Colossians 3:1-4 (ESV)

> In those days Jesus came from Nazareth of Galilee and was baptized by John in the Jordan. And when he came up out of the water, immediately he saw the heavens being torn open and the Spirit descending on him like a dove. And a voice came from heaven, "You are my beloved Son; with you I am well pleased."

> If then you have been raised with Christ, seek the things that are above, where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God. Set your minds on things that are above, not on things that are on earth. For you have died, and your life is hidden with Christ in God. When Christ who is your life appears, then you also will appear with him in glory.

### Why This Text for This Wound

**"You are my beloved Son; with you I am well pleased"** — spoken over Jesus before he preaches, heals, or performs a single recorded act of ministry. This is the direct scriptural pattern the impostor's operating rule violates: the rule says value must be built and demonstrated before belovedness can be granted; the text shows belovedness declared first, with no ministry evidence yet in view. The declaration to the client is that this is the order for every identity in Christ, not a privilege unique to Jesus.

**"You have died, and your life is hidden with Christ in God"** — the most direct address to the impostor identity available in the canon. The construction project — the self that must be built, defended, kept visible, kept evidenced — is not described as improving or eventually retiring. It is declared dead: a completed, past-tense fact, not a future goal the client is working toward.

**"Hidden... in God"** — hiddenness directly reverses the impostor's central anxiety, that safety and identity depend on being seen, verified, or proven. The text locates the true self's security in what is unseen. The client does not need to make the self visible or provable to be real; the opposite is true — the realest thing about them is currently hidden.

**"When Christ who is your life appears, then you also will appear with him in glory"** — the appearing (being fully seen, known, and vindicated) is future and already guaranteed by another's action, not a present task the client must engineer through performance. This releases the pressure to prove the self now, because the proving has already been scheduled and secured elsewhere.

### Exegetical Note for Practitioner

Where Tracks 1-4 each answer one specific charge, Track 5 names the chassis the charge is riding on. A client can resolve the Track 2 performance lie in relation to God's approval and still operate the same construction pattern socially, professionally, or internally — because Track 2 addressed one application of the impostor structure, not the structure itself. When a client has done real work in a wound-matched track but reports the underlying pattern of self-construction is unchanged, Track 5 is very likely the layer that has not yet been named.

### Nouwen's Four Movements — Diagnostic Layer

Henri Nouwen's *Life of the Beloved* (1992) supplies a mechanism this track's declaration text states but doesn't itself sequence: belovedness has to move through four movements before it functions as identity rather than sitting as a believed fact that doesn't reach the impostor structure. When a client can state the Mark 1:11 / Colossians 3:1-4 declaration accurately but reports it "doesn't feel like the real me," use these four movements to locate which stage hasn't landed yet, rather than simply re-deploying the same declaration text:

- **Taken/Chosen** — Can the client state belovedness as a given fact, prior to and independent of any selection process? If they keep locating the "why" in something they did or were chosen for, this movement hasn't landed — return to Mark 1:11's timing (spoken before any recorded ministry act).
- **Blessed** — Has belovedness actually been *spoken to* the client and received as address, not just read or affirmed as a proposition? An unspoken truth does not function as identity. This is the piece most identity-work skips, and often the fastest to fix: speak the declaration text directly at the client, by name, rather than having them read or paraphrase it.
- **Broken** — Has belovedness been tested against a real failure or suffering the client actually had, and found still standing? A client who has only held the declaration under good conditions has not yet completed this movement — the identity is a mood, not yet load-bearing. Bring a specific past failure into the room and ask whether the declaration holds there, not hypothetically but concretely.
- **Given** — Is belovedness curdling into self-focus, or has the client begun extending it outward as a source for someone else? An impostor identity that has resolved theologically but never moved toward giving is incomplete — this is often the last movement to arrive and the best confirming evidence that the earlier three have taken.

A client stuck at Taken/Blessed needs different work (naming and direct address) than a client stuck at Broken (activation against a real failure) or Given (a between-session assignment to extend the belovedness toward someone specific). Diagnosing which movement is missing is more useful than repeating the declaration text at a client for whom the text is already cognitively assented to.

### Deployment Timing

Two valid uses. (1) As a foundational or closing declaration alongside any of Tracks 1-4, once the specific wound-matched lie has been named and addressed — Track 5 names the deeper rule ("identity must be earned") that the specific wound was one expression of. (2) As the primary track when Stage 2 naming does not resolve into one of the four discrete wound categories but into a chronic, diffuse performance-identity pattern with no single traceable origin. Before treating Track 5 as sufficient on its own, cross-check against the Character Wound Diagnostic Tool's "Before Typing" section and the Wound-Led vs. Vision-Led Architecture reference to confirm a discrete injury is not being missed.

**Watch:** If the client responds to "you have died" with anxiety rather than relief — a fear of losing the only self they know how to operate as, rather than a sense of release — the constructed identity is more load-bearing than initially assessed. Slow down. Do not treat the death language in verse 3 as automatically freeing; for a client who has never operated without the construction, it can register as a threat rather than good news until the "hidden... in God" and "beloved" language has been sufficiently inhabited first.

### Lie Eliminator Connection

Primary entries targeted: "I am not enough / not the right person," "I am an imposter," "I am adequate only when proven — the pending verdict," "I must build and defend who I am." Mark 1:9-11 and Colossians 3:1-4 are the primary counter-truth sources.

### Identity Installer Connection

Primary layers: **Loved as Son** (Layer 8), **Imago Dei** (Layer 1), **New Creation** (Layer 3).

## Blended Presentations

Most clients present with more than one wound category active, though one is usually primary. Common blends and practitioner guidance:

**Performance + Abandonment:** The most common blend. The performance identity is often a secondary formation — the client learned to produce to secure the presence and approval that could not be received as a given. Start with the abandonment track (Track 1) to surface the primary wound, then work the performance structure (Track 2) as the adaptive response. If Track 2 is addressed first without addressing the abandonment beneath it, the client may stop performing without having received an alternative relational structure.

**Shame + Condemnation:** Frequently co-occur but have different primary wounds. Shame is about being ("I am disqualified by what I am"); condemnation is about doing ("the record of what I have done is permanent"). In session, they can look identical until the practitioner asks: "Is the primary accusation about who you are — or about what you've done?" The answer determines which track is primary, though both declaration frameworks will likely be needed.

**Abandonment + Condemnation:** Less common but occurs in backgrounds of parental punishment patterns. The client feels both alone (no one comes) and accused (a charge is being held). The God-image in this blend is typically Absent and Punitive simultaneously — the father is distant until the client fails, at which point he appears as judge. The Track 1 and Track 4 declarations address different dimensions of the same wound.

When blended presentations are active, work the primary track first and let the secondary declaration emerge later as the primary wound begins to move.

## The *Logizomai* Caution — Romans 6:11

The declaration practice in Stage 3 is grounded in Romans 6:11 (ESV): *"So you also must consider yourselves dead to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus."*

The Greek verb is *logizomai* (pronounced loh-GID-zoh-my) — to reckon, count, consider as established fact. It is an accounting term. To reckon something is to count it as actually true in the ledger, not to generate a feeling of certainty about it.

**What reckoning is:** Counting on what the Spirit has already made true. The client is not trying to make the declaration true by believing it hard enough. They are agreeing with a completed reality — the death and resurrection of Christ, which is already accomplished and already applied to them by the Spirit. The declaration is an act of agreement, not an act of generation.

**What reckoning is not:** Willpower applied to an identity claim. Positive thinking. The force of conviction producing a spiritual outcome. These are all forms of the performance identity applied to the declaration work — the client's certainty is what makes it effective. If this belief is operating, the effort to produce certainty becomes the new slave identity.

**The practitioner's diagnostic check:** If a client says "I keep declaring this but it doesn't feel true," the first question is not "are they declaring enough?" It is: "Do they believe their feeling of certainty is what makes it true?" If so, the declaration work has been framed as performance rather than agreement. Reframe: *"You're not trying to generate a feeling. You're counting on what is already true. The reckoning is not the cause of the truth — it is your agreement with it."*

## General Deployment Protocol

1. **Confirm Stage 2 is complete.** The specific false identity must be named before declaration can address it. "God loves me" is not a counter to a specific lie; it is a counter to nothing in particular.

2. **Activate before declaring.** Bring the implicit belief into the room emotionally before introducing the counter-truth. Ask the client to say the lie aloud, to locate it in the body, to notice what it feels like to hold it. Declaration into a live implicit belief creates conditions for reconsolidation. Declaration into a named but unactivated belief produces extinction.

3. **Match track to wound.** Use the wound marker profile above to confirm which track is primary. When in doubt, ask: "What is the accusation — is it about who you are, what you've done, whether you are received, or whether you have to keep earning your place?" The answer maps to Track 3, Track 4, Track 1, or Track 2 respectively. If the answer is none of these — the client describes the self itself as something that must be constantly built and defended, with no single accusation at the center — the primary track is Track 5.

4. **Speak the text directly.** Read or speak the declaration text aloud in the session. Do not paraphrase it into abstraction. The text carries weight that a summary does not.

5. **Assign between-session rehearsal.** Identify the specific verse or phrase from the declaration text that most directly addresses the named lie. Assign it as the between-session content-abiding practice: spoken aloud, written, prayed. This is the John 15 content-abiding layer — the words that abide in the client are being specifically targeted.

6. **Return and deepen.** One declaration session does not complete Stage 3. Return to the declaration in subsequent sessions, track whether it is holding under stress (content abiding) or receding (extinction), and deepen or adjust accordingly.

## Watch Points

**Declaration without specific lie identification is encouragement, not installation.** Generic covenant truth addressed to no particular false identity produces temporary relief but not reconsolidation. The counter-truth must be addressing something specific.

**Premature deployment intensifies shame.** Track 3 particularly. If the shame wound is not inhabited and the gap between the lie and the truth is too wide, early declaration can feel like mockery. The client experiences it as "you're telling me to feel something I can't feel" — which confirms the lie. Wait until the wound is inhabited and the alternative is genuinely needed.

**Distinguish forgiveness from the end of the self-punishing pattern.** Track 4 clients often believe they have been forgiven theologically while still maintaining the self-punishing structure. The self-punishment is not a failure to believe the forgiveness — it is the continuation of the condemnation wound underneath the theological belief. The implicit belief and the confessional belief are two different encoding layers.

**Watch for Track 2 clients performing the declaration.** The performance identity will apply its structure to the declaration practice — tracking consistency, evaluating quality, reporting results. The declaration is being executed as a production task. The practitioner should redirect: "You're not trying to perform this well enough. You're agreeing with what is already true."
