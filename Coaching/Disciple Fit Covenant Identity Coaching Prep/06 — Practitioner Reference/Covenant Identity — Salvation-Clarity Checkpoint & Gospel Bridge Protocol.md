# Covenant Identity — Salvation-Clarity Checkpoint & Gospel Bridge Protocol

*Companion to [[Covenant Identity Coaching — Practice Definition]] Dimension 3 ("Spiritual baseline required at entry") and structural parallel to [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]]. Read before your first client engagement. Return to it whenever a client's spiritual baseline is uncertain — at intake, or when it surfaces mid-arc.*

---

## A Note on What This Document Is

Covenant Identity Coaching's entire mechanism depends on a fact being true of the client before the first session: that they are already united to Christ, already possess a positional identity secured at new birth (Rom. 6:6, Col. 3:3, 2 Cor. 5:17), and the coaching work only ever *forms conscious alignment with* that already-true reality (see the Practice Definition's "Positional Identity vs. Identity Formation" section). The practice's core tools — Lie Eliminator, Identity Installer, targeted declaration — work by having the client reckon with (Rom. 6:11, *logizomai*) a status that is objectively theirs. That is what keeps the tools from functioning as manifestation-style self-persuasion.

If that status is not yet true of the client — because they have not yet trusted Christ, or their profession of faith is cultural/nominal rather than a genuine trust-response to the gospel — the entire mechanism breaks. There is no positional identity yet to reckon with. Running the identity-formation tools on such a client does not fail gently; it produces exactly the "declare it until you believe it" pattern the Practice Definition explicitly names as a predictable and damaging misreading of the practice.

This document exists to close that gap. It is not an evangelism training curriculum, and Andrew is not functioning here as a trained evangelist — it is a boundary protocol: how to test the entry condition Dimension 3 already states, what to do when the test raises doubt (at intake or mid-arc), and how to have a single clear gospel-clarifying conversation without turning CIC into something it is not.

**What this document is not:**
- Not a claim about which Scriptures the Holy Spirit "uses more often" to reach people — that is not a knowable empirical claim. The Scripture set below is selected because it has a long, well-documented track record in evangelistic practice (the "Romans Road" tradition and its close relatives), not because of any claim about the Spirit's internal workings.
- Not a decision-pressure or altar-call methodology. Nothing here instructs creating urgency, isolating the client until they respond, or treating a session's outcome as a personal success/failure metric for the coach.
- Not a substitute for the client's local church. A single gospel-clarity conversation inside a coaching relationship is not discipleship, and this document does not pretend it is.

## 1. The Checkpoint at Intake

Dimension 3 states the entry bar plainly: *"Active or genuine opening faith in Jesus Christ."* Self-report on an intake form ("Yes, I'm a Christian") does not test this — it tests cultural or family religious identity, which is common in the same population CIC serves and is not the same thing as trust in Christ.

**Add the following probes to intake or the Discovery Call** (see [[Covenant Identity — Discovery Call Guide]] for where this fits alongside the existing Entry Fork read):

1. *"If you died tonight and stood before God and He asked you, 'Why should I let you into My heaven?' — what would you say?"*
   Listen for the answer's object: Christ's finished work, or the client's own effort, goodness, church attendance, or improvement. An answer built on personal merit — even sincerely religious-sounding merit — is a signal, not a pass.

2. *"Tell me, in your own words, what it means that Jesus died on the cross. What was that for?"*
   Listen for whether the client can state the actual content of the gospel (sin, substitution, resurrection) or only associates the cross with a vague sense of goodness, tradition, or moral example.

3. *"Was there a point where you personally trusted Christ — a before-and-after, even if you can't name the exact date? Or has this always just been what your family/culture called itself?"*
   This distinguishes a genuine trust-response (even a quiet, undramatic one) from inherited religious identity with no actual point of personal reliance on Christ.

**Reading the answers:** A confident, Christ-centered answer to all three does not require further action — proceed with intake as normal. A merit-based, vague, or purely inherited answer to any of them does not disqualify the person from ever engaging CIC, but it does mean the spiritual baseline in Dimension 3 is not yet confirmed, and CIC-proper should not begin. Move to Section 3 (Gospel Bridge Conversation) before scheduling Session 1.

## 2. Mid-Arc Trigger Signals

A client can pass the intake checkpoint on a good day and still turn out, once real formation work begins, not to have genuine saving faith underneath a sincere but merit-based or inherited profession. This is not a failure of the checkpoint — it is why the boundary has to stay open through the arc, not just at the door. Treat the following as a formal addition to the existing "Resistance indicates diagnostically" list in Dimension 5, alongside "Insufficient spiritual baseline":

- **Declaration never lands, and probing reveals why.** The client goes through Lie Eliminator / Identity Installer work but the declared truths ("you are dead to sin," "you are a new creation") produce blank confusion rather than resistance-with-recognition — not "I know that's true but I don't feel it" (the normal formation gap this practice is built to address) but "I don't actually know if that's true of me." The first is a formation gap. The second is a positional-identity gap, and it is diagnostic, not a technique problem to solve with a better-worded declaration.
- **The gospel is described as something the client is working toward, not something already secured.** Language like "I'm trying to become a Christian" or "I'm working on my relationship with God" in the present tense, well into the engagement, rather than "I am in Christ and I'm learning to live like it."
- **No memory of, or resistance to the idea of, a personal trust-response to Christ**, surfacing during Phase 1 or 2 story-work — even after the client affirmed general Christian identity at intake.
- **Affections and behavior track consistently with an unregenerate pattern** (per the Character Wound Diagnostic's own framing) with no evidence of the Spirit's ordinary fruit — not a single hard season, but a sustained absence noticed across multiple sessions.

**When any of these surface:** Pause CIC-proper content for that session. Do not continue applying identity-formation tools while this question is open — doing so is the exact misuse this document exists to prevent. Move to Section 3.

## 3. The Gospel Bridge Conversation

This is a single, honest conversation — not a program. Structure, parallel to the referral-conversation structure in [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]] Section 5:

**1. Name what you're noticing, specifically and without alarm:**
*"I want to pause and be honest about something. As we've talked, I'm not sure the foundation this coaching stands on — that you're already in Christ, and we're helping you live like it — is actually settled yet. I'd rather stop and make sure of that than keep building on it."*

**2. Ask directly, without pressure:**
*"Can I ask you plainly — have you ever actually trusted Christ, personally, to save you? Not grown up around this, not tried to be good — trusted Him specifically."*

**3. Present the gospel clearly**, using the Scripture set in Section 4. Present it as content to be understood, not a script to be rushed through. Let silence sit. Answer questions honestly, including hard ones.

**4. Invite a response without manufacturing one:**
*"This isn't something I can decide for you, or talk you into. If God is making this real to you right now, you can respond to Him honestly, in your own words, right now or later. If you're not there, that's honest too, and it doesn't end our relationship."*

**5. Name what happens next**, per Section 5 below, depending on the client's response.

**Governing posture throughout:** This mirrors the practice's own Layer 1 mechanism (the Spirit is the primary agent; the coach does not produce transformation). The coach's job in this conversation is to present the gospel with clarity and stay out of the way of what only the Spirit can do — not to close a sale. If the coach notices the impulse to pressure, manufacture urgency, or treat the outcome as a personal win or loss, that is the signal to slow down, not speed up.

## 4. Scripture Set — Gospel-Clarifying Passages (ESV)

*Organized by rhetorical function, in the order they are typically used (the "Romans Road" tradition and its near-relatives). These are commonly and effectively used passages in evangelistic practice — not a claim about the Spirit's internal workings.*

**The problem — universal sin and its consequence:**
- Rom. 3:23 — *"for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God"*
- Rom. 6:23a — *"For the wages of sin is death"*
- Isa. 59:2 — *"but your iniquities have made a separation between you and your God, and your sins have hidden his face from you"*
- Eph. 2:1 — *"And you were dead in the trespasses and sins in which you once walked"*

**God's provision — the gospel itself:**
- John 3:16 — *"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life."*
- Rom. 5:8 — *"but God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."*
- 1 Cor. 15:3–4 — *"that Christ died for our sins in accordance with the Scriptures, that he was buried, that he was raised on the third day in accordance with the Scriptures"*
- 2 Cor. 5:21 — *"For our sake he made him to be sin who knew no sin, so that in him we might become the righteousness of God."*

**The response invited — faith, not works:**
- Rom. 10:9–13 — *"if you confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved... For 'everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved.'"*
- Eph. 2:8–9 — *"For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast."*
- John 1:12 — *"But to all who did receive him, who believed in his name, he gave the right to become children of God"*

**Assurance — what is true the moment faith is exercised:**
- 2 Cor. 5:17 — *"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old has passed away; behold, the new has come."*
- John 1:12–13 — adoption as God's child, "born, not of blood nor of the will of the flesh nor of the will of man, but of God"
- Rom. 8:15–16 — *"you have received the Spirit of adoption as sons, by whom we cry, 'Abba! Father!' The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God"*
- 1 John 5:11–13 — *"And this is the testimony, that God gave us eternal life, and this life is in his Son... I write these things to you who believe in the name of the Son of God that you may know that you have eternal life."*

Use as few or as many of these as the actual conversation calls for. This is not a checklist to complete in order — it is a reference so the coach isn't reaching for passages under pressure.

## 5. After the Gospel Bridge Conversation — Three Outcomes

**(a) The client professes new or renewed trust in Christ.**
Do not resume CIC-proper content in the same session, and do not treat the profession itself as the completed entry condition. Positional identity is instantaneous and real the moment genuine faith is exercised (see Practice Definition, Dimension 1) — but *confirming* that a profession reflects genuine faith, versus relief or social pressure in the moment, takes time and is not the coach's judgment to rush. Practically: warmly affirm what happened, connect the client to a local church and ordinary means of grace (baptism if not yet baptized, a church community) before resuming CIC, and revisit CIC entry at a deliberately later checkpoint — not the next scheduled session by default. This parallels Dimension 3's existing "sufficient stability for formation work" bar: a fresh profession needs a season of ordinary grounding before the identity-formation arc, which presupposes a settled positional identity, can do its work.

**(b) The client is unmoved or declines.**
CIC-proper does not begin, or pauses if already underway. This is not the end of the relationship by default, but it does mean the current container is wrong. Two honest paths, named directly to the client:
- Refer to their local church, a pastor, or a discipleship relationship suited to ongoing gospel conversation — this is squarely outside what a coaching relationship, even a well-meaning one, is built to carry long-term.
- If the relationship and context allow, continue as a distinct, non-CIC coaching or mentoring relationship with covenant-identity content withheld entirely — never diluted or implied. Do not blend. A client without confirmed positional identity does not get a "lighter version" of Identity Installer; they get none of it.

**(c) The client is uncertain and wants more time.**
Hold at the intake gate. Do not begin CIC-proper. Offer to revisit the conversation at a specific future point rather than leaving it open-ended and unspoken. Continued relationship (prayer, ordinary conversation, church connection) is appropriate; CIC's specific tools are not, until the baseline is confirmed.

## 6. What This Protocol Explicitly Does Not Do

- It does not make Andrew an evangelist by training or vocation. This is one clear conversation grounded in an existing relationship, not a program, and not a claim of specialized evangelistic competency beyond what any mature believer is called to (1 Pet. 3:15).
- It does not authorize decision-pressure tactics of any kind — no artificial urgency, no isolating the client until they respond, no treating hesitation as a problem to be overcome in the moment.
- It does not extend into ongoing evangelistic counseling or discipleship. If more than this one conversation is needed, the referral in Section 5(b) applies — that belongs to the client's pastor or church, not to an expanded coaching scope. This mirrors, in kind, the boundary the Crisis & Referral Protocol draws around clinical material: recognizing the limit and referring well is the competent move, not a failure.

## 7. Practitioner Preparation Checklist

Before your first client engagement:

- [ ] Be able to state the gospel clearly, in your own words, without notes — Section 4's passages should be familiar, not something you're reading cold in the moment.
- [ ] Identify at least one local church or pastor you can confidently refer a client to for ongoing discipleship or gospel conversation beyond a single session.
- [ ] Do not treat this as a rare edge case. Nominal or cultural Christian identity — professed faith without a personal trust-response — is common in the population CIC's marketing and intake are likely to reach; expect to use the intake checkpoint, not just keep it on file.
- [ ] Review this document alongside [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]] before first engagement — both are boundary protocols for when CIC-proper is not (yet) the right container.

## Document Provenance

**What this document is:** Practitioner protocol resolving Open Precision Gap #1 in the Practice Definition — how to test the "genuine opening faith" entry bar at intake, what mid-arc signals indicate the bar was not actually met, how to have a single gospel-clarity conversation without turning CIC into an evangelism program, and what happens next under each of the three possible outcomes.

**What informed it:** The Practice Definition's own stated entry condition and its Positional Identity vs. Identity Formation section (which supplies the theological reason the boundary is load-bearing, not optional); the Romans Road evangelistic tradition for the Scripture set's selection and sequence; the Crisis & Referral Protocol's structure, adapted from a clinical boundary to a spiritual-baseline boundary.

**Where it sits:** Block 3 (Diagnostic Architecture) alongside the Crisis & Referral Protocol — both are boundary-recognition documents read before first engagement and returned to when their respective triggers appear.

**What this document does not do:** Does not train Andrew as an evangelist or provide a general evangelism curriculum. Does not adjudicate the genuineness of any individual's profession of faith — that judgment belongs to God; the coach's responsibility is to test honestly, present the gospel clearly, and route appropriately, not to certify outcomes. Does not replace the client's local church for ongoing discipleship.
