# Covenant Identity — Category Architecture Cross-Reference

*Practitioner reference — how the system's categorization schemes relate to each other*

---

## Purpose

This document addresses a navigation problem: Covenant Identity Coaching deploys multiple categorization schemes across its tools, and without a documented relationship between them, a practitioner can feel as if they are working several competing systems simultaneously. They are not. The schemes are layered — each operates at a different level and serves a different function. This document maps those relationships so the practitioner can move between systems with clarity.

---

## The Three-Level Architecture

The categorization schemes in this model operate at three functional levels. They are not competing taxonomies; they are complementary lenses operating at different depths.

| Level | Schemes | Function | Who holds it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Diagnostic** | Character Wound types (3) · Affections Discovery (5 layers) · God-Exclusion Map (8 domains) · Whole-Person Identity Framework (5 dimensions) | Tells you *what you're working with* — the specific wound shape, what the client is trusting, where God is excluded, which dimension of the person is affected | Practitioner only |
| **Content** | Covenant Identity Layers (10 declarations) · Secured in Father (10 attachment dimensions) | Tells you *what is true* — the covenant realities the coaching installs; the attachment dimensions of the relationship with God | Practitioner and client (shared) |
| **Implementation** | Identity Installer (22 categories) · Lie Eliminator (12 lies) | Tells you *how to work it* — the client-facing tools that deliver the covenant content at the point of the lie or the gap | Client-facing |

**Key principle:** Diagnostic work precedes content delivery; content delivery precedes implementation. The practitioner does not assign the Lie Eliminator without first knowing which lie is operative (diagnostic), and does not assign the Identity Installer without knowing which layer is most severed (content). The schemes at each level are designed to feed the next.

**Secured in Father operates separately from the 10 layers:** The 10 covenant identity layers are theological declarations (what is true in Christ). The 10 Secured in Father dimensions are attachment-relational dimensions (how the relationship with God functions — Safe, Seen, Soothed, etc.). These are complementary frameworks at different levels — they do not need to be reconciled into a single taxonomy. A practitioner may work Layer 8 (Loved as the Father Loves the Son) through the Secured in Father framework's "Worth" and "Seen" dimensions. They describe the same underlying reality from different angles.

## Cross-Cutting Notes

**"Abandoned by God" (Lie #7) spans Layers 2, 6, and 8.** This is the most cross-cutting lie in the system. The abandonment lie can manifest as: loss of the sense of being known and chosen (Layer 2), loss of the sense of God's indwelling presence (Layer 6), or loss of the sense of God's personal love (Layer 8). Diagnosis matters: the practitioner should identify which layer dimension the client's experience of abandonment is rooted in before assigning Layer-specific declaration work.

**"I Am Not Enough / Imposter" (Lie #9) spans Layers 1, 4, and 9.** Worth (Layer 1), relational standing as a child (Layer 4), and sent-ness/purpose (Layer 9) all carry an "enough" dimension. The specific flavor of "not enough" tells the coach which layer to target: not enough in worth → Layer 1; not enough to belong / not deserving → Layer 4; not enough to carry the mission → Layer 9.

**Lies #3 (Hopelessness) and #5 (Giving Up) both touch Layers 7 and 10.** The distinction is directional: Hopelessness says "it cannot change" — Layer 3 (New Creation) and Layer 10 (formation process) address this. Giving Up says "I will not survive the process" — Layer 7 (standing secure) and Layer 10 both apply. When both lies are active, Layer 10 is the primary installation target.

## How to Use This Document

**During Phase 2 (Wound Mapping):** As the primary schema and false identity come into focus, use the cross-reference table to identify which covenant identity layer(s) are most directly opposed to what the client is living from. This narrows the Phase 3 installation target.

**When assigning between-session tools:** Use the layer mapping to select the most on-target Identity Installer category or Lie Eliminator lie for the specific layer being worked. Generic assignment (e.g., "work through the whole Identity Installer") is less formative than precise assignment (e.g., "this week work only category #13 — Known and Seen — because Layer 2 is where the primary gap is").

**When the Lie Eliminator and Identity Installer feel redundant:** They're not — they operate differently. The Lie Eliminator moves from false belief toward truth through renunciation and replacement. The Identity Installer moves from truth declaration toward embodiment. Use the Lie Eliminator when a specific false belief is blocking reception; use the Identity Installer when the belief is not actively blocked but is not yet felt as operative.
