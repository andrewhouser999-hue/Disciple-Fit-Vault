# Covenant Identity — Deep Work Worksheets — Design Rationale
*Practitioner Reference | Applies to: Deep Lie Work Worksheet + Deep Identity Installation Worksheet*

---

## The Problem These Worksheets Solve

The Lie Eliminator and Identity Installer are effective tools. They address cognition and behavior — the lie named, the truth declared, the identity stated, the intention set. What they do not address is the layer underneath those: the psychological infrastructure that makes lies feel self-evidently true and identity declarations feel false, abstract, or irrelevant.

That infrastructure is the schema.

A schema is a deep emotional conviction — formed early, operating automatically — about the self, others, or the world. It does not respond to cognitive counter-argument. When activated, it floods the person with emotion fast enough that rational correction arrives too late. Standard lie work challenges individual thoughts. Schema work addresses the emotional climate in which those thoughts feel like facts.

Without schema-level intervention, lie work produces declarations that don't hold and identity statements that bounce. The schema keeps generating fresh evidence for the lie and keeps making the identity claim feel like wishful thinking.

These two worksheets extend the existing tools into that layer.

---

## Theoretical Foundation

**Jeffrey Young — Schema Therapy**
Schemas maintain themselves through three behavioral responses: Surrender (living as if the schema is true), Avoidance (numbing or withdrawing from schema-activating situations), and Overcompensation (rigidly doing the opposite). All three preserve the schema while giving the illusion of managing it. The interrupt target is the gap between activation and behavioral response — that gap is where choice lives.

**Kegan & Lahey — Immunity to Change**
Every significant behavioral goal has a competing commitment operating underneath it — a hidden protective logic. Column 4 of their process is the Big Assumption: the if/then rule the person is living by. The assumption survives because it is never tested. A behavioral experiment is a small, safe test that collects real data against the assumption. The Lie and the Big Assumption often describe the same belief from two angles: the lie is the theological framing, the Big Assumption is the operational behavioral logic. Working both simultaneously is more effective than working either alone.

**Dan McAdams — Narrative Identity Theory**
Identity is a story, not a belief. People with strong, coherent self-narratives have significantly more psychological stability than those who hold abstract beliefs about themselves. Evidence listed stays abstract. Evidence narrated — told as a specific, inhabitable first-person story — is retrievable under the kind of pressure that ordinarily overwhelms a client's framework.

**Somatic Integration**
Cognitive truth is accessible when the person is calm. Somatically anchored truth — connected to a physical felt sense through repeated practice — is accessible when the person is under pressure. That is the only moment it matters. Declaration work that skips somatic anchoring produces truth that vanishes under schema activation.

*(Mechanism — Thompson, Curt, Anatomy of the Soul, 2010; The Soul of Shame, 2015: Implicit memory stores relational and somatic experience in right-hemisphere neural networks. Explicit, propositional truth is stored in left-hemisphere language systems — accessible when the person is regulated, but under activation the right hemisphere dominates and the left goes offline. Somatic anchoring creates a right-hemisphere pathway to truth: not additional doctrine but embodied knowing that survives stress. Evidence level: neuroscientifically grounded; the coaching-context application is practitioner extrapolation.)*

**Ephesians 4:22-24 — The Biblical Frame**
Put off the old self / be renewed in the spirit of your mind / put on the new self. Scripture gives a three-part formation structure (Renounce → Renew → Receive) that maps onto what psychology confirms across multiple frameworks. The worksheets are built on this same structure at greater depth.

## Why Second-Level Tools, Not Modifications

The Lie Eliminator and Identity Installer are personal tools — Andrew's own formation instruments. Modifying them for client use would muddy a clean, tested structure and conflate two different use cases. Client-facing instruments need plain language, fillable prompts, and explicit framing that self-explains without a coach present. The second-level worksheets serve a different function and appropriately live as separate documents.

---

## Design Logic — Deep Lie Work Worksheet

**Layer order (Theological → Psychological → Behavioral)**
The lie must be named precisely before the schema can be identified — the schema is what operates underneath a specific belief, not a general mood. The Big Assumption must come after the schema because the assumption is the behavioral logic the schema produces. Reversing the order produces confusion.

**Why the Big Assumption is Part 3, not Part 2**
The schema (Part 2) is the felt conviction. The Big Assumption (Part 3) is the behavioral rule the conviction generates. The client needs to name what they feel before they can see clearly what they do. If you ask for the behavioral logic first, you get behavior without the emotional root. The schema identification in Part 2 is what makes Part 3 legible.

**Why the experiment debrief is the session re-entry point**
The experiment generates concrete, lived behavioral evidence. That evidence is harder for the schema to dismiss than a declaration — the emotional system has to contend with something that actually happened. Opening the following session with the debrief (not a general check-in) puts you immediately into specifics. How the client handles the debrief — whether they minimize a disconfirming result, explain it away, or can let it count — is diagnostic information about the schema's current grip.

**Why the somatic anchor is the integration point**
Declaration work without somatic anchoring produces cognitive truth only. Cognitive truth is accessible when calm. The somatic anchor — locating where the truth lands physically, naming it, returning to it under activation — is what makes the truth retrievable under schema pressure (Thompson — right-hemisphere implicit encoding). This is the difference between a declaration that helps someone feel better in a quiet moment and a declaration that holds when the lie is loudest.

## Design Logic — Deep Identity Installation Worksheet

**Why the blocking schema rather than general resistance**
Every identity category has a specific schema that resists it. "I am beloved" has a different blocker than "I am capable." Naming the specific schema — and its specific distortion pattern for this identity category — is more useful than a general acknowledgment that "this is hard to believe." The schema reference table in Part 2 is a starting point, not an exhaustive taxonomy.

**Why evidence story rather than evidence list**
The Identity Installer already has an evidence field. The gap is format, not presence. A list is a set of data points. A story is an event with specificity, location, felt sense, and narrative arc — something the person can re-enter. Under schema activation, a story is retrievable; a list is not. Part 3 asks the client to write it in first person, past tense, with enough detail to inhabit again.

**Why identity vote rather than "Today I Will"**
"Today I Will" is a daily intention. An identity vote is a specific enacted behavior that accumulates into lived conviction. The distinction matters because identity consolidates through behavioral evidence, not through intention. The vote also generates debrief material for the following session — the same function the experiment serves in the lie worksheet.

**Why relational ecology is Part 5 (secondary)**
Relational ecology is real and important — identity is partly maintained by who reflects it back. But it is not the primary lever in most cases. Putting it secondary prevents it from hijacking the session with relationship processing before the internal work is established. Introduce it when the client has enough stability with their identity statement to look outward.

**Why the daily practice is stripped down**
The worksheet is weekly depth work. The daily practice is a different function: repetition and habituation. A long daily practice won't be done. One sentence, one verse, a thirty-second felt sense check — that will be done. The depth work and the daily practice serve different purposes and should not be collapsed into each other.

## When to Use Each Worksheet

**Deep Lie Work Worksheet** — Phases 2–3. Introduce after Wound Mapping has surfaced the operative schema. Assign one lie and one schema to work. Running multiple lies simultaneously dilutes the work and makes the experiment design too diffuse.

**Deep Identity Installation Worksheet** — Phases 3–4. Use during active identity installation work, once the Phase 2 map has identified which identity layers are most disrupted. Assign one identity category per worksheet.

**Sequencing relationship:** Lie work establishes the negative space — naming and dismantling what is false. Identity installation fills it — building what is true. Both are necessary; neither alone is sufficient. In practice they often run in parallel during Phase 3, with the coach alternating based on what is most live for the client in a given week.

## What to Watch When Refining

Client use will reveal the gaps. Specific signals to watch:

**In the Deep Lie Work Worksheet:**
- If clients return with Parts 1–2 complete but Part 3 empty or vague, the Big Assumption prompt needs clearer scaffolding — clients may need an in-session walkthrough before attempting it alone
- If experiment designs are too large or abstract, the prompt needs more constraining language — "one action, in one specific context, with one named person or situation"
- If debrief fields are left blank or filled in one-word answers, the experiment wasn't concrete enough or the client is avoiding the result
- If schema identification is consistently difficult, the reference table descriptions may need revision or the coach may need to assign the schema name rather than have the client find it

**In the Deep Identity Installation Worksheet:**
- If evidence stories are lists in paragraph form rather than narrated events, the prompt needs a stronger "tell it, don't summarize it" instruction
- If identity votes are general intentions rather than specific actions, add more constraining examples — the current examples may not span enough territory
- If clients can't name a felt sense, don't treat this as a failure — it means the somatic anchor work needs more in-session time before it can be done independently
- If the blocking schema section is consistently skipped or superficially completed, it may be too abstract without coach support — consider whether this section needs in-session introduction before between-session use

## What Is Outside the Scope of These Tools

These worksheets interrupt and redirect schema activation and build behavioral evidence for identity and truth. They are not designed to:

- Produce deep schema dissolution — that is longer-term work, often requiring professional therapeutic support
- Address group or relational dynamics beyond the relational ecology section
- Handle crisis presentations — see the Crisis & Referral Protocol
- Replace the in-session work — they extend it; the session remains the primary container

## Load-Bearing vs. Modifiable

**Load-bearing** — these elements must not be adjusted without disrupting the model's integrity:

- **Sequential layer order (Theological → Psychological → Behavioral)** in the Deep Lie Work Worksheet. The lie must be named precisely before the schema can be identified; the schema must be identified before the Big Assumption can be named. Reversing the order produces behavioral analysis without emotional root or theological grounding.
- **Lie named before schema identified.** Schemas are lie-specific — they operate underneath a particular conviction, not a general mood. Attempting schema identification before the lie is precise produces category confusion.
- **Big Assumption named in session before between-session experiment is assigned.** The Big Assumption prompt often requires in-session scaffolding before clients can complete it independently. Assigning the experiment without first identifying the assumption means the experiment has no clear testing target.
- **Somatic anchor as integration point — not optional.** The anchor is the mechanism that makes truth retrievable under schema pressure (Thompson — implicit memory). Omitting it produces cognitive-only gains that vanish when the schema activates.
- **Lie work precedes identity installation as primary sequence.** Lie work establishes the negative space (naming and dismantling the false); identity installation fills it. Running identity installation without lie work foundation means installing over an active schema that immediately rejects the installation.
- **Session remains primary container.** These worksheets extend the in-session work; they do not replace it. Assigning deep work without adequate session framing treats the worksheet as standalone therapy, which it is not.

**Modifiable** — these elements can be adjusted to fit the client:

- Schema reference table descriptions — may be adjusted as client vocabulary develops; coaches may name the schema directly rather than having the client locate it
- Evidence story prompts — can vary in form while maintaining the "first-person narrated, specific event" requirement; do not reduce to list format
- Identity vote examples — should be tailored to the client's specific domain and phase; the examples in the worksheet are starting points
- Daily practice duration — the minimum form (one sentence, one verse, brief felt-sense check) should be maintained; may expand slightly but should not become another depth worksheet
