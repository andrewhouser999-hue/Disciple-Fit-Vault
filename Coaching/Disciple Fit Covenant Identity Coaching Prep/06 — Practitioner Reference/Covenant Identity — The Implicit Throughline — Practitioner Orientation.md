# Covenant Identity — The Implicit Throughline — Practitioner Orientation
*Practitioner Reference — why the implicit/explicit distinction governs the diagnostic sequence*

**What this is:** A cross-cutting orientation to how the implicit/explicit memory distinction operates across the entire Covenant Identity diagnostic sequence — not a new mechanism, but a consolidated statement of a thread that otherwise appears only piecemeal, scoped to individual stages, across Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic, Affections Discovery, and the Stage 3/4b mechanism docs.

**When used:** Before a practitioner works through Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic for the first time, or whenever a coach is unclear on why Stage 4a and Stage 4b are treated as fundamentally different problems rather than as a severity gradient of the same problem. Read this first — it supplies the *why* that the stage-specific documents assume the reader already has.

**Who uses it:** Coach only. Not shared with clients.

---

## The Core Claim

The implicit/explicit distinction is not a side concern that surfaces occasionally in Covenant Identity Coaching — it is the organizing diagnostic question the entire stage sequence exists to answer. At each stage, the practitioner is asking some version of: *is what I'm looking at explicit — something the client can report, argue with, or consciously hold — or implicit — something the client enacts or is organized by, without being able to report or feel it directly?* The tools that work on one will not work on the other, and applying the wrong tool to the wrong layer can actively backfire (see "Why This Matters" below).

Three places this shows up in the model:

### 1. Affections are read as implicit data, not self-report

Stage 1 (Affections Discovery) does not ask the client what they believe — it asks what they depend on, inferred from where their time, anxiety, and energy actually go rather than from what they say they value. [[Affections Discovery Process — Affections, Trust & Priorities]] Layer 2 ("Pattern Surfacing") is built specifically around the gap between stated and functional priorities — a gap the client is usually unaware of, which is precisely why it has to be surfaced through behavioral questions rather than asked directly. The false identity named in Stage 2 is therefore a claim about implicit functional trust — something the client is organized by — not a belief they would produce if simply asked "what do you believe about yourself?"

### 2. Covenant Truth only produces lasting change if it reaches the implicit level

This is the load-bearing move at Stage 3, and it is why [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]] requires an activation check before Covenant Truth is spoken: does the client have emotional, body-forward contact with the false identity right now, or are they reflecting on it from a distance?

If Covenant Truth is spoken while the false identity is only explicitly (intellectually) acknowledged, the result is **extinction** — a competing statement that suppresses the lie temporarily. Under stress, the original implicit encoding returns, because it was never rewritten — only overridden.

If Covenant Truth is spoken while the implicit belief is emotionally active — felt in the room, not merely named — the result is **reconsolidation**: the underlying encoding itself is rewritten, and the lie is replaced rather than suppressed.

This distinction (drawn from Ecker and Nader & LeDoux's memory reconsolidation research — full treatment in [[Covenant Identity — What Moves Covenant Truth from Registration to Reception]]) is why "the client agrees intellectually" is treated in this model as an *insufficient* outcome, not a success marker. Explicit agreement without implicit activation produces a proposition the client holds, not a belief system that has actually changed.

### 3. Stage 4a and 4b are a direct implicit/explicit fork, not a severity gradient

When Covenant Truth doesn't land, the model does not ask "how hard is this client resisting" — it asks "which memory system is holding the false identity in place." [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]] draws this fork explicitly:

- **Stage 4a** — the block is explicit: a cognitive distortion the client actively argues against Covenant Truth with reasoning. CBT tools (Socratic questioning, laddering/downward arrow, thought record, behavioral experiment) apply here, because the block is accessible to cognitive engagement.
- **Stage 4b** — the block is implicit: the client says "I know that's true" with full sincerity and feels nothing. There is no argument to engage — cognitive tools are documented as actively counterproductive at this stage, because they deepen shame ("I've heard this many times and it still doesn't land — something must be wrong with me"). The response instead is relational presence and repetition across sessions (full skill taxonomy in [[Covenant Identity — Stage 4b Implicit Level Practitioner Reference]]), because implicit memory responds to relational experience, not to argument.

## Why This Matters — the Category Error This Prevents

[[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]] names directly what happens when Stage 4a and 4b are conflated — this is a diagnostic failure with real cost to the client, not a stylistic preference between two similar approaches:

- **4a tools applied to a 4b block:** more content, clearer doctrine, or better argument aimed at a client who already agrees and simply cannot feel it. The client experiences this as their own failure — "something must be wrong with me" — which can deepen a shame or defectiveness pattern precisely when the coach believes they are addressing it.
- **4b posture applied to a 4a block:** soft relational presence offered to a client who is actively arguing against Covenant Truth leaves the distortion undisturbed, because the client needed cognitive engagement, not warmth. A Warrior-type client in particular will read this as the coach backing down — which confirms rather than challenges the pattern the coaching is meant to interrupt.

The practical implication: before choosing a Stage 4 response, the practitioner has to determine which memory system is holding the block, not just how stuck the client appears. This read is not always clean — see the Contested Ground note in [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]] for guidance on ambiguous cases (when in doubt, the documented default is to err toward Stage 4b — less cognitive input — before adding more content).

## Document Provenance

**Why this exists:** The implicit/explicit distinction is documented piecemeal across at least five practitioner-reference documents (Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic, Affections Discovery Process, What Moves Covenant Truth from Registration to Reception, Stage 4b Implicit Level Practitioner Reference, Disordered Affections and the Diagnostic Entry — Mechanism), each treating it only within their own stage's scope. No single document previously stated the throughline as one continuous claim. A practitioner encountering Stage 4a/4b for the first time without this orientation can misread the split as an arbitrary severity gradient rather than the different-memory-system fork it actually is.

**Governing question:** Why does this model treat implicit and explicit blocks as requiring fundamentally different responses, rather than the same response applied with more or less intensity?

**Primary contributors:** See [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]] Document Provenance for the full contributor table (Allender, Thompson, Beck, Johnson, Welch, Ecker/Nader & LeDoux). This document synthesizes their conclusions as already applied in that document and in [[Affections Discovery Process — Affections, Trust & Priorities]]; it does not draw independently on the primary source material.

**Design decisions:**

- *Positioned as orientation, not a new mechanism document:* This document introduces no theoretical claim beyond what is already established in the two source documents. It exists to sequence the reading, not to add content. Reason: a practitioner who reads Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic cold, without this framing, has to reconstruct the implicit/explicit throughline themselves from signals scattered across the document; this states it up front instead.
- *Placed in 06 — Practitioner Reference, not 01 — Model Foundation:* Model Foundation documents describe what Covenant Identity Coaching is, to different audiences (Manifesto, Theory of Change, client-facing descriptions). This document is theoretical and mechanism-level, and assumes the reader already knows what the model is — it belongs alongside the other diagnostic-sequence and mechanism documents it synthesizes, not with the audience-facing description documents.

**Client summary:** This is a practitioner-only reference. It is not shared with clients.

**Contested ground:** See the Contested Ground note in [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]]. The implicit/explicit distinction underlying this entire throughline is drawn from interpersonal neurobiology (Siegel, Thompson) and memory reconsolidation research (Ecker, Nader & LeDoux) — both empirically grounded in their own fields but not independently validated in Christian coaching contexts specifically. This document inherits that same evidentiary status; it makes no empirical claim beyond what the source documents already establish.
