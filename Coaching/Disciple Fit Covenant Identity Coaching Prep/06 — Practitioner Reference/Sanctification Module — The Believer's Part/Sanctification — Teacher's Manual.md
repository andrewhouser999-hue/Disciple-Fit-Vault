# TEACHER'S MANUAL
## The Believer's Part in Sanctification
### Covenant Identity Coaching — Disciple Fit

**Module Classification:** Formation Framework Module
**Mechanism Layer:** 3 (Diagnostic) + 4 (Covenant Truth) + 5 (Between-Session Disciplines)
**Deployment Stage:** Middle sessions — after Affections Discovery has surfaced a false covenant object without defensive collapse; not at intake; not during acute crisis
**Session Arc:** Two focused sessions; ongoing integration thereafter. Session 1: framework introduction + failure mode identification + one Tier 1 practice. Session 2: practice debrief + deepening + formation track advanced. Session 3+: framework established; practice becomes the between-session anchor and ongoing thread.
**Format:** Individual (group adaptation pending)
**Governing Discipline:** Spiritual Formation (telos and posture); Biblical Counseling (diagnostic precision); Coaching (relational methodology)

---

## I. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This module addresses the most common formation impasse in this practice: theological literacy without biblical coherence, producing knowledge that does not reach the formation layer and therefore does not produce change.

The client's presenting experience: *"I know what I'm supposed to believe. I've known it for years. It isn't working."*

The module's purpose is not to add more information to an already-loaded knowledge base. It is to give the client the **integrating framework** — the organized picture of who does what in sanctification, in what order, by what mechanism — that their prior biblical education did not provide. When that coherence is received, it produces two things this client urgently needs: **relief** (the gap is not evidence of defect) and **traction** (there is a mechanism, and I can engage it).

**What this module is not:**
- A lesson on the definition of sanctification — clients already have the word
- A call to try harder — this is the Warrior failure mode
- A prescription for passive surrender — this is the Hermit failure mode
- A therapy session — high ACE prevalence is an active formation consideration, not the primary treatment target

---

## II. CLIENT PORTRAIT

**Demographics:** Ages 35–55, individual engagement
**Theological literacy:** 85% moderate, 15% moderate-light
**Integration level:** Low to low-enough across the board — the gap is causing active pain
**ACE prevalence:** High — light to moderate ACE actively present in approximately 90% of clients

### What the client knows
Doctrinal vocabulary is present: *sanctification, Holy Spirit, justification, grace, spiritual disciplines*. They can produce definitions on demand — often painfully. Key passages are familiar: Phil 2:12-13, Rom 6, Gal 5. They may have heard these texts dozens of times in sermons.

### What the client cannot do
Organize a coherent account of sanctification — who does what, in what order, by what mechanism. Ask a moderate-literacy client to explain how God's role and their role coexist without contradiction. Most cannot. They have the pieces. They do not have the picture those pieces form.

### What the client is experiencing
- **Shame:** *"I've been a Christian for 20 years and I'm still dealing with this"*
- **Confusion:** *"I do the right things and nothing changes"*
- **Performance fatigue:** many have cycled through willpower-based attempts and collapsed
- **For ACE-prevalent clients:** protective dissociation from the inner life, making genuine formation engagement feel inaccessible — not because they are unwilling but because the inner world has not been safe

### The central question the client is living with
*"If God does it, why isn't it happening? If I'm supposed to do it, why can't I?"*

This is not rhetorical. It is the presenting pain. The module's first task is to answer it with precision and without adding shame.

## III. THE CENTRAL GAP — VOCABULARY WITHOUT FRAMEWORK

This client population has been formed by fragmented, fill-in-the-blank biblical education that delivered doctrinal answers without connective tissue. They have the pieces. They do not have the integrated picture that makes the pieces intelligible.

**The result:** Theological vocabulary that is functionally inert. A definition heard 100 times without landing as a coherent whole has no formation power. The word "sanctification" in the client's mouth may be phonetically identical to that same word in a mature practitioner's mouth and functionally entirely different — one is integrated knowledge; the other is borrowed language.

**The coaching implication:** When a client says "I know what sanctification is," that is not the end of the assessment — it is the beginning. The diagnostic question is:

> *"Walk me through how you understand it to work — specifically, who does what?"*

The gap between their definition and their ability to organize the picture tells the coach where the Client Study needs to do its work and which failure mode is most active. Do not skip this question. It is the literacy-vs-integration check and it will shape everything that follows.

**The central logical connection most clients are missing:**

Philippians 2:12-13 is the hinge text. Most clients know both verses. Almost none have been shown how they connect.

> *"Work out your own salvation* with the gravity befitting one who cooperates with the living God, whose holiness and purposes are not to be handled casually — *¹"* (v. 12)
> *"for it is God who works in you, both to will and to work for his good pleasure."* (v. 13)

The connective word is *gar* — "for" / "because." God's working is not the replacement of human working. It is the **ground and power** of it. The command to effort stands *because* God is the one enabling it — not despite that fact. This one connection, received and organized, resolves the central confusion. It does not end the formation work. It makes the formation work intelligible for the first time.

## IV. THE BIBLICAL FRAMEWORK — THE INTEGRATED PICTURE

*This section presents what the coach must understand in full. The Client Study presents this same framework in plain integrated language. The Session Facilitation Guide scripts how to walk a client through it.*

### A. The Order of the Players

**1. God — Primary Agent**

God is the initiating, enabling, and sustaining agent in sanctification. He does not assist the believer's self-improvement program. He *works in* the believer at the level of desire and action (Phil 2:13). He *causes* the walking (Ezek 36:27). He gives the new heart (Ezek 36:26). He conforms to Christ's image (Rom 8:29; 2 Cor 3:18).

This is not background theology. It is the direct answer to the client's question about why their efforts have not produced change — they may have been working at the wrong layer, by the wrong means, without engaging the one whose working makes theirs possible.

**2. The Spirit — The Operative Power**

The Spirit is the person of the Trinity through whom God's sanctifying work is effected in the believer. Key functions:
- Produces fruit — character formation at the affections and disposition level (Gal 5:22-23)
- Enables the walk — moment-by-moment orientation of life (Gal 5:16, 25; Rom 8:4)
- Mortifies the deeds of the body — in cooperation with the believer's genuine agency (Rom 8:13)
- Conforms to Christ — the telos of the whole process (2 Cor 3:18; Rom 8:29)

The Spirit's work is not a later addition to the believer's effort. It is the condition without which formation does not happen regardless of effort.

**3. The Believer — Real but Empowered Agent**

The believer is a genuine agent — not a passive recipient and not an autonomous moral achiever. The NT imperatives are real commands to real persons with real wills:

- *Reckon yourselves dead to sin* (Rom 6:11) — a cognitive-spiritual act of appropriating what is true in Christ
- *Present yourselves to God* (Rom 6:13) — a decisive act of offering the whole person
- *Put to death what is earthly in you* (Col 3:5) — active, decisive, commanded
- *Put on the new self* (Col 3:12) — active reception of covenant identity
- *Walk by the Spirit* (Gal 5:16) — ongoing oriented movement
- *Abide in me* (John 15:4) — maintained relational connection as the condition of fruitbearing

The believer's acts are genuine. They are not supplemental to insufficient grace — they are the *expression* of grace at work. The capacity to act rightly is given; the act itself is genuinely the believer's own.

**4. The Means of Grace — Appointed Conditions**

The Spirit works through appointed instruments: the Word, prayer, the community of believers, the Lord's Supper, and spiritual disciplines. These are not transformation mechanisms — they do not *cause* formation. They *create conditions* in which the Spirit transforms what direct effort cannot reach.

This distinction is load-bearing for this client population. Many have been using the means as willpower levers — trying harder at the disciplines in hope of producing change at the affections level. That is not how the means work. They work by creating repeated, sustained exposure to the God who transforms. That is a different operation entirely.

### B. The Structural Logic — Asymmetric Agency

The two poles clients hold in unresolved tension:
- *"God does it"* → so my effort is either unnecessary or presumptuous
- *"I'm supposed to do something"* → so if it's not working, I am defective

The resolution is **asymmetric agency**: God is primary — prior, enabling, ultimate. The believer is real — genuine, necessary, accountable. The two do not compete. God's working is the *ground* of the believer's working, not its replacement.

The Ezekiel 36:26-27 pattern is the clearest biblical statement: God *gives* the new heart and God *causes* the walking — yet the walking is genuinely the people's walking. Divine gift produces genuine human act. This is not a paradox to manage; it is the normal structure of new covenant life.

### C. Definitive vs. Progressive Sanctification

Clients frequently collapse these, generating confusion that runs beneath everything else.

**Definitive sanctification** (Rom 6) — a once-for-all breach with sin's dominion through union with Christ in death and resurrection. Completed. Positional. The ground of all progressive work. Answers: *"Am I fundamentally different from who I was before Christ?"* Yes — in Christ, yes.

**Progressive sanctification** — the ongoing work of conforming the whole person (affections, character, behavior, body) to what is already true in Christ. Incomplete in this life. Answers: *"Am I growing?"* — the question producing shame.

The confusion: clients who know they are definitively sanctified experience progressive sanctification stalling and conclude one of three things — the positional reality is not real, God is withholding, or they are defective. The module must establish this distinction before formation work can proceed without that confusion running underneath it.

## V. THE FOUR FAILURE MODES

### Mode 1 — Warrior
**Profile:** Intense effort; disciplines as willpower; sin-identification as primary activity; cycles of striving and collapse.
**Root:** Identity tied to performance; effort is control; rest feels like failure or spiritual laziness.
**Diagnostic signal:** *"I just need to be more disciplined / more consistent / try harder."*
**Formation gap:** Mortification without vivification. Works at the behavioral layer without engaging the affections or character layers. Produces fatigue, not formation.

### Mode 2 — Hermit
**Profile:** Passivity as surrender. Avoids engaging the means. "Let go and let God" functions as a defense against genuine formation engagement.
**Root:** Engagement feels threatening. The inner world is dangerous to enter. Withdrawal is protective.
**Diagnostic signal:** *"I just need to surrender more / stop trying and let God do it."*
**Formation gap:** Neither mortification nor vivification. Disengagement from the appointed means while using spiritual language to describe it. May present as mature but is avoiding formation entirely.

### Mode 3 — False Noble
**Profile:** Performance-based formation. Growth measured by what can be shown. Effort for approval, not from love. Spirituality functions as a social register.
**Root:** Identity tied to being perceived as spiritually healthy. Exposure of stagnation produces shame rather than honest assessment.
**Diagnostic signal:** Progress language is audience-facing. Reports spiritual activity in terms of others' perception. Shame at the gap between appearance and inner reality.
**Formation gap:** Vivification as performance rather than reception. Puts on the new self as a costume rather than as covenant identity received.

### Mode 4 — Practiced Christianity Without Inner Access
**Profile:** Faithful external engagement without genuine interiority. Church, prayer, Bible reading, small group — performed consistently and sincerely — from behind a wall. The practices are real. The inner engagement is not.
**Root:** ACE-driven. The inner world has been unsafe — shamed, violated, dismissed, or punished — and protective dissociation from interiority became adaptive. The Christian life was learned as external compliance, which felt both familiar and manageable. Genuine interiority was never modeled as safe.
**Diagnostic signal:** Client describes faithfully performing spiritual practices while reporting nothing penetrates or changes anything underneath. *"I pray but I don't feel like God is there." "I read the Bible but it doesn't reach me." "I believe this is true but I can't feel it."* Often accompanied by sustained confusion — they are doing what they were taught; they do not understand why it is not working.
**Formation gap:** The practices are executed at the surface. The inner layer where formation happens is walled off. This is not a discipline problem. It is an access problem.
**Critical distinction from Mode 2:** The Hermit avoids the practices. This client faithfully performs them. The presenting symptom may look similar — nothing is changing — but the mechanism is entirely different and the coaching response is different. Assigning more practices to a Mode 4 client will deepen the failure and the shame. The work is building inner access first.
**ACE note:** This is the dominant failure mode in ACE-prevalent populations. It is not laziness or bad faith. It is protection that has outlived its original function.

## VI. WOUND TYPE AND FAILURE MODE MAPPING

| Wound Type | Primary Failure Mode | Secondary | Key Diagnostic Tell |
|---|---|---|---|
| Warrior | Mode 1 — over-effort | Mode 3 — performance | *"I need to be more disciplined"* |
| Hermit | Mode 2 — passivity | Mode 4 — no inner access | *"I just need to surrender"* |
| False Noble | Mode 3 — performance | Mode 1 — effort-for-approval | Growth language is audience-facing |
| Any + significant ACE | Mode 4 — no inner access | Overlays any of above | Faithful practices, no penetration |

**Mode 4 as overlay:** Mode 4 is a modifier that can sit on top of any wound type. A Warrior with significant ACE may appear to be in Mode 1 (hard effort) while actually being in Mode 4 — effort that never reaches the inner layer because the inner layer is walled off. The distinction matters for formation tool selection. Hard effort at the surface does not address the access problem underneath.

## VII. SOIL TYPE MAPPING

Two soil types from the Parable of the Soils (Matt 13:3-23) are primarily activated in the formation conditions this module addresses.

**Rocky Soil (Matt 13:5-6, 20-21)**
Receives the word with joy but has no root — withers under trial or opposition. The diagnostic image for this module: enthusiastic surface reception without formation depth. The client receives the integrating framework with relief in session, performs the assigned practices, but nothing penetrates to the affections or character layer and nothing holds under pressure.
Most activated by: Mode 1 (cycles of engagement and collapse) and Mode 4 (faithful practices without inner penetration).
Formation track implication: rootedness over breadth — Tier 2 abiding rhythm with attention to depth of engagement rather than volume of practice.

**Thorny Soil (Matt 13:7, 22)**
The word is present but is choked by the worries of life and the deceitfulness of wealth — not hostile to the word but crowded out by competing affections. The diagnostic image: formation is possible but is being displaced by disordered loves the client has not yet fully identified. The client genuinely wants to grow; other loves are winning the competition for the heart's center.
Most activated by: Mode 3 (False Noble — worldly concerns shape the spiritual register) and the affections diagnostic layer — what false covenant objects are crowding out the Spirit's work?
Formation track implication: Affections Discovery before formation track assignment. The Immunity to Change Map to surface the competing commitment. Formation practices assigned before the crowding affection is named will be choked.

**Note:** These two soil types are not mutually exclusive. Rocky soil describes a formation depth problem; thorny soil describes a formation competition problem. A client can present with both.

## VIII. MECHANISM NOTES — FIVE-LAYER APPLICATION

**Layer 1 — Spirit as Primary Agent**
Hold this posture throughout the entire module. The coach is not producing formation. The Spirit is the operative agent. Every tool creates conditions; nothing causes transformation. State this explicitly to the client — it is one of the primary relieving truths this module carries, particularly for clients who have been striving without change.

**Layer 2 — Coaching Relationship as Formation Context**
With ACE-prevalent clients, the coaching relationship itself is doing formation work at the character layer independent of the content discussed. The coach's regulated presence, consistent attunement, and non-reactive engagement with the client's pain creates the relational experience that Thompson identifies as necessary for implicit memory reshaping. For Mode 4 clients specifically, experiencing the coaching relationship as safe may be the prerequisite for inner access becoming possible at all. Do not rush past this layer to content delivery.

**Layer 3 — Diagnostic Work**
This module does diagnostic work at two levels simultaneously:
1. **Framework gap** — what is missing in the client's integrated picture of sanctification? (The literacy-vs-integration check)
2. **Failure mode** — which of the four patterns is primary? (Determines formation track assignment)

Both must be assessed before the Client Study is introduced or formation practices assigned.

**Layer 4 — Covenant Truth Application**
The Phil 2:12-13 connection is the primary covenant truth this module applies. The task is not cognitive understanding — the client may already be able to state it. The task is *receiving it as the answer to their specific confusion* and beginning to operate from it rather than around it. The Ezek 36:26-27 pattern reinforces the same truth from the OT foundation. The Col 3 structure (old self / covenant self) gives the identity frame within which the effort makes sense.

**Layer 5 — Between-Session Formation Disciplines**
Three tracks are available, assigned by failure mode. Assign one track only — not all three. The goal is one practice engaged with genuine interiority, not three practices performed from behind the wall.

## IX. FORMATION TOOLS — SEQUENCED FOR ACE-PREVALENT CLIENTS

**Governing principle:** Relational safety before inner access. Grounding before somatic work. Assess inner access capacity before assigning any practice that requires it. Assigning an interiority-demanding practice to a client who has no inner access does not build formation; it builds shame.

### Tier 1 — Safe for all clients regardless of ACE level
These practices have low interiority demand and can be introduced early.

- **Rom 6:11 Reckoning Practice** — Daily, brief: state aloud or in writing the specific reality the verse names. *"I am dead to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus."* Not affirmation as positive thinking — appropriation of what is true. Low threat; high repetition builds over time.
- **Phil 2:12-13 Framework Meditation** — Read both verses slowly. Notice the *gar* ("for" / "because"). Ask: what is God's working in you making possible right now? Low interiority demand; reinforces the framework.
- **Immunity to Change Map** — Intellectual engagement suitable for moderate-literacy clients. Surfaces why the client doesn't change despite knowing the truth, without requiring emotional access. The "big assumption" revealed is often the formation-layer barrier.

### Tier 2 — Appropriate after 2–3 sessions of relational safety established
- **Abiding Rhythm** — Word + prayer structured around John 15:7's two conditions: *his words remaining in you* + *asking.* Not devotional performance — relational engagement with specific attention to where the text meets the client's current formation gap.
- **ACT Defusion Exercise** — When the old self's narrative runs automatically (*"I'm not really changing / God isn't there / I can't do this"*), practice the move from fusion to observation: *"I notice I'm having the thought that..."* Creates space between the client and the thought without arguing against it.
- **Expressive Writing (Pennebaker)** — 15–20 minutes, 3–4 consecutive days, writing freely about a difficult emotional experience without editing or self-censoring. Not journaling toward a conclusion — emotional access and integration. Requires enough interiority to access emotion without flooding.

### Tier 3 — Only after inner access has been assessed as available
- **Lament Practice** — Honest grief before God using the Psalms of lament as a container (Ps 22, 88). Do not assign without scaffolding. The practice requires the client to have an inner world they can enter and a God they believe can hold what is there. Introduce with: *"The Psalms give us permission to bring the real thing to God — not the edited version."*
- **Somatic Awareness Check-In** — Brief, structured: notice body sensation in a low-stakes moment; name it without judgment; bring it to God in prayer. Requires capacity to tolerate body sensation without flooding. Introduce slowly with explicit permission to stop.
- **Narrative Entry Work** — Entering a specific life story with the coach present, looking for where God was, what was truly lost, what needs to be grieved. Requires significant relational safety, affect regulation capacity, and explicit coach judgment about readiness.

## X. SCOPE BOUNDARY — REFERRAL THRESHOLD

This module operates in a coaching container. The following signals indicate ACE presentation has exceeded coaching scope and requires clinical referral — concurrent with or prior to continued coaching:

**Refer when:**
- Client floods, dissociates, or shuts down when approaching formation content and cannot re-regulate within the session
- ACE material is actively destabilizing daily functioning — work, relationships, basic self-care
- Client reports intrusive memories, nightmares, or hypervigilance as currently active symptoms
- The inner access wall is absolute — no formation practice gains any traction after 4–6 sessions of consistent relational safety building with consistent coaching presence
- Client is in active crisis: suicidal ideation, self-harm, acute substance use

**Coaching can continue when:**
- Client can engage the formation conversation without persistent flooding
- ACE content surfaces in the context of formation work, not as the primary presenting trauma
- Client has basic affect regulation — can notice an emotion without being overwhelmed
- Client has stabilizing resources: faith community, relationships, basic self-care capacity

**Referral language:**
*"What you're carrying is real and it matters. Some of what's underneath this needs more specialized support than coaching can offer. That's not a problem with you — it's information about what you need. Coaching and therapy can work together, and I'd like to help you think through finding the right support so this work can go deeper."*

## XI. DIAGNOSTIC TOOL CONNECTIONS

**Affections Discovery Protocol**
Entry point for this module. The question *"what is sanctification functionally protecting you from?"* surfaces the false covenant object underneath the formation language. Findings feed directly into failure mode identification.

**Character Wound Diagnostic**
Identifies which wound type is producing which failure mode. Use after Affections Discovery, not before. Wound type determines formation track assignment.

**Immunity to Change Map**
Particularly well-suited for Mode 4 clients and moderate-literacy clients who can engage the intellectual structure. Surfaces the structural reason the client doesn't change despite knowing the truth and performing the practices. The "big assumption" revealed is frequently a relational wound operating as an unexamined law — and for ACE-prevalent clients, it often names the inner access problem more precisely than any other tool. See: [[Sanctification — ITC Practitioner Reference]].

**Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic**
This module operates at Stages 1–2. Stage 3 (covenant truth application) begins when the client has received the integrated framework and the failure mode has been named without defensive collapse. Stage 4a (CBT tools) activates only if cognitive distortion blocks reception after Stages 1–3 have been engaged — not before.

**Secured in Father**
The abiding component (John 15) connects directly to attachment formation. For clients whose inner access is blocked by insecure attachment to God — anxious, dismissing, or fearful — Secured in Father is the appropriate next referral rather than a more interiority-demanding formation practice.

## XII. FAILURE MODE GUARDS FOR THIS MODULE

**Legalism guard:** Every discipline is framed as condition, not cause. Phil 2:13 is kept in view throughout — the believer's effort is the *expression* of divine working, not a supplement to insufficient grace. The formation tracks are means of grace, not willpower levers.

**Insight without formation guard:** The module does not end at framework delivery. Every session use must exit with exactly one formation practice assigned, matched to the failure mode. No session closes on clarity alone.

**Tool dependence guard:** The opening frame in every document is explicit: the tools create conditions; the Spirit transforms. The coach does not present discipline tracks as mechanisms of change. The question behind every practice assigned is not "did you do it?" but "where did you notice the Spirit in it?"
