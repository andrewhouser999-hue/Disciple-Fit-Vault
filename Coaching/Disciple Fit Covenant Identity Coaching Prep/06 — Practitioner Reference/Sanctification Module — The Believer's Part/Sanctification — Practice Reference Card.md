# PRACTICE REFERENCE CARD
## The Believer's Part in Sanctification
### Covenant Identity Coaching — Disciple Fit

**Two pages. Page 1 contains Tracks A and B. Page 2 contains Track C (Immunity to Change Map). Give the client only the page(s) for their assigned track. Coach: circle or mark the assigned track before distributing.**

---

## PAGE 1

### Before You Begin — What This Card Is For

Your coach has given you one practice from the tracks below. This card is not homework. It is a formation condition — something that creates the space in which the Spirit does the work that willpower alone cannot reach.

**Do not evaluate whether it is working.** Do not measure your performance. Do not compare this week to last week. Do it. Then notice. Bring what you noticed to your next session.

**The question to carry into every practice:**
> *Where did I notice the Spirit in this?*

Bring your answer — or your absence of an answer — to your coach. Both are worth something.

---

### TRACK A — Philippians 2:13 Meditation
*Assigned for: intensity without traction / cycles of effort and collapse*

**Your assigned practice:**

Once a day — it does not need to be long — read Philippians 2:12-13 slowly.

> *"Work out your own salvation with the gravity befitting one who cooperates with the living God, whose holiness and purposes are not to be handled casually. For it is God who works in you, both to will and to work for his good pleasure."*

When you reach verse 13, **stop.**

Ask one question and hold it — do not force an answer:

> *"What is God's working in me making possible right now — not someday, right now?"*

**What this is doing:**
The goal is not productivity. The goal is to begin cooperating with what is already happening rather than trying to generate what you think should be happening. God is already at work at the level of desire — the word "will" in verse 13 means wanting. He is working on what you want before he works on what you do. This practice orients you to that reality.

**What faithful engagement looks like:**
You read both verses. You stop at verse 13. You hold the question for at least a few minutes. You do not evaluate the session. You note what, if anything, surfaces — even if it is nothing.

**What going through the motions looks like:**
Reading quickly to complete the task. Moving on without holding the question. Measuring whether you felt something.

---

### TRACK B — Romans 6:11 Reckoning Practice
*Assigned for: passivity disguised as trust / faithfully doing practices that are not penetrating*

**Your assigned practice:**

Once a day, say this aloud or write it:

> *"I am dead to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus."*

That is Romans 6:11. That is the whole practice.

**What this is not:**
This is not a positive affirmation. You are not trying to feel it. You are not trying to generate something new. You are not performing.

**What this is:**
Paul calls it *reckoning* — like entering a fact into a ledger. You are recording what is already true in Christ, not manufacturing something that is not. The act of reckoning is an act of appropriation — taking hold of what belongs to you in him.

Do it even if it feels mechanical. Do it especially if it feels mechanical — because mechanical is a place to start, and the Spirit meets you there.

**What faithful engagement looks like:**
You say it or write it once a day. You do not evaluate whether it landed. You notice whatever is present — resistance, flatness, a small movement, nothing. You bring it to your coach.

**What going through the motions looks like:**
Saying it quickly to finish. Measuring it by how it felt. Skipping it when it seems pointless.

**A note for when nothing seems to happen:**
Nothing happening is not failure. It is information. Bring it to your coach exactly as it is. The absence of felt movement does not mean the Spirit is absent — it means the access layer is still developing. That is what the coaching relationship is for.

## PAGE 2 — TRACK C
### Immunity to Change Map
*Assigned for: formation that serves audience rather than genuine inner growth*

**What this is:**
The Immunity to Change Map is a way of looking at why you do not change even when you genuinely want to. It is not a spiritual exercise in the traditional sense. It is a diagnostic — and what it surfaces tends to be more honest than most of us expect.

Your coach will walk you through the first two columns in session. The third and fourth columns are your between-session work. Bring what you find to your next session without editing it.

---

### The Four Columns

**Column 1 — My Improvement Goal**
*Complete this in session with your coach.*

What do you genuinely want to change, grow in, or become? Name it specifically — not "be a better Christian" but the specific thing underneath that.

Write it here:

_______________________________________________

_______________________________________________

**Questions to help:**
- What do you most wish were different about your inner life?
- What would you change if you knew the change would hold?

---

**Column 2 — What I Do Instead**
*Complete this in session with your coach.*

What behaviors — things you do or do not do — actually work against the goal in Column 1? Be concrete. These are not character flaws. They are patterns that are doing something.

Write them here:

_______________________________________________

_______________________________________________

_______________________________________________

**Questions to help:**
- When you fail to move toward Column 1, what are you doing instead?
- What do you consistently avoid that would serve your goal?

---

**Column 3 — What I Am Protecting**
*Complete this between sessions. Bring it to your coach.*

Look at your Column 2 behaviors. They are not random — they are protecting something. If you actually achieved your Column 1 goal, what would become threatened or uncomfortable? What would you have to give up, face, or risk?

This is not about what you should feel. It is about what you actually feel when you imagine the goal fully achieved.

Write what comes:

_______________________________________________

_______________________________________________

_______________________________________________

**Questions to help:**
- If Column 1 fully happened, what would change that you are not sure you want changed?
- What does the undermining behavior protect you from having to face?
- What feels safer about staying where you are than moving toward what you say you want?

---

**Column 4 — The Assumption Underneath**
*Complete this between sessions. Bring it to your coach.*

Column 3 is held in place by a belief — something you assume to be true that makes the protection in Column 3 feel necessary. This belief operates like an unwritten rule. You may never have put it into words. That is the point of this column.

Complete this sentence:

> *"I assume that if I [achieve Column 1], then _______________________________________________."*

Or:

> *"I believe that _______________________________________________."*

**Questions to help:**
- What would have to be true for Column 3 to feel necessary?
- What experience taught you that the risk in Column 3 is real?
- If you could name the deepest reason you stay where you are, what would it be?

---

**What to bring to your coach:**
Bring all four columns — including what you are uncertain about, what surprised you, and what you could not answer. Especially bring what you could not answer. The edges of what you can see are often where the most important things are living.

**What this is doing:**
Column 4 — the assumption — is the formation-layer barrier. It is the place where knowledge stops producing change because something underneath is operating as a law. Naming it does not dissolve it. But naming it in the presence of another person, and eventually in the presence of God, begins the work that argument alone cannot do.

---

*This card is not a measure of your performance. It is a record of your engagement. The Spirit works through the engagement — not through your assessment of how well you engaged.*
