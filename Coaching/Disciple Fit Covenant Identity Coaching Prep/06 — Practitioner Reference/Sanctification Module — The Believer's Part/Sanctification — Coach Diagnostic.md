# COACH DIAGNOSTIC
## The Believer's Part in Sanctification
### Covenant Identity Coaching — Disciple Fit

**Purpose:** Use this document in session preparation to determine deployment readiness, identify the client's failure mode and formation gap, select the correct formation track, and recognize scope boundary signals before they become a crisis.
**Not a script.** These are assessment frameworks and question banks. Coach judgment governs application.
**Routing card:** Use the Diagnostic Summary at the bottom at the end of each session to confirm your reading and plan next steps.

---

## STEP 1 — DEPLOYMENT READINESS

Do not introduce this module until all three conditions are met.

| Condition | Check |
|---|---|
| Affections Discovery has surfaced a false covenant object without defensive collapse | ☐ |
| Client is not in acute crisis (suicidal ideation, active self-harm, acute substance use, daily functioning actively destabilized) | ☐ |
| Client has basic affect regulation — can notice an emotion without being overwhelmed by it in session | ☐ |

**If any condition is unmet:** hold the module. Address the unmet condition first. Introducing the framework before readiness is established produces shame, not relief — the client will hear "here is how it works" and conclude they are failing at a mechanism they now understand.

---

## STEP 2 — LITERACY VS. INTEGRATION CHECK

**Ask this question before introducing any module content:**

> *"Walk me through how you understand sanctification to work — specifically, who does what?"*

Then listen. Do not prompt. Do not fill the silence.

### What the answer tells you

**Vocabulary-only response — integration gap confirmed:**
- Names God and the Holy Spirit without describing their specific function
- Says "I need to surrender" or "I need to work at it" without being able to explain how both are true simultaneously
- Can cite Phil 2:12-13 but cannot explain the connection between the two verses
- "God does it" — full stop. Or "I'm supposed to do something" — full stop.
- Produces a definition of sanctification they have clearly heard before, delivered without ownership

*Coach reads:* The framework gap is the primary presenting condition. The Client Study and framework introduction are the right first move. Proceed through the remaining steps to identify which failure mode is shaping how the gap is being lived.

**Partial framework response — moderate integration gap:**
- Identifies God's role generally ("the Spirit transforms me") but cannot specify what the believer's role is, or vice versa
- Knows the definitive/progressive distinction as vocabulary but uses "I'm already positionally sanctified" to explain current stagnation without apparent awareness of the conflation
- Can name practices but cannot explain what they are doing or at which layer

*Coach reads:* The framework needs filling in at specific connection points, not a full introduction from the ground up. Note which connection is missing — agency structure, definitive/progressive distinction, or the means-of-grace mechanism — and target there.

**Organized response — lower framework gap:**
- Explains Phil 2:12-13's connection without prompting
- Distinguishes definitive and progressive sanctification and applies them accurately
- Can name formation practices and explain why they work

*Coach reads:* The gap is not primarily a framework problem. The formation block lives elsewhere — likely in a failure mode, ACE-driven inner access issue, or a soil type problem. Skip or abbreviate the Client Study introduction; go directly to Step 4 (Mode 4 Screen) and Step 5 (Failure Mode Identification).

## STEP 3 — SOIL TYPE READING

These two readings run in parallel with Steps 4 and 5. They refine the formation track assignment at Step 7.

### Rocky Soil Indicators
- Receives new formation content with enthusiasm in session; engagement drops between sessions
- Reports practices done inconsistently or abandoned after initial attempt
- Pattern of spiritual excitement followed by discouragement followed by restart
- Nothing seems to hold under pressure or in ordinary life conditions

*Coach reads:* The formation depth problem. Formation track priority: sustained rootedness over breadth. Assign one practice; hold it longer than feels necessary before advancing.

### Thorny Soil Indicators
- Genuinely wants to grow; reports competing demands that consistently displace formation time and energy
- Spiritual life is crowded, not avoided — there simply is not room
- Disordered affections (anxiety, financial stress, relational conflict, status concerns) are occupying the center
- Formation practices are present but thin — squeezed in rather than inhabited

*Coach reads:* The competing affections problem. Assigning more practices will be choked by the same conditions. Run Affections Discovery first or return to it. The Immunity to Change Map is the most direct tool — it will surface the competing commitment that is winning against formation.

**Note:** Rocky and Thorny are not mutually exclusive. When both are present, address the Thorny condition first: remove what is choking before focusing on what will root.

## STEP 4 — MODE 4 SCREEN

Run this screen before failure mode identification. Mode 4 is an overlay — it can sit on top of any wound type or failure mode. Missing it means assigning formation practices that will not reach the layer where change needs to happen.

**Opening question:**

> *"Walk me through your current spiritual practices — what does a typical week look like?"*

Then:

> *"When you do those things, what happens inside you?"*

### Mode 4 Positive Indicators
- Practices are present and consistent — the client is doing them
- Interior response is flat, absent, or described in terms of going through motions
- *"I pray but I don't feel like God is there"*
- *"I read the Bible but it doesn't reach me"*
- *"I believe this is true but I can't feel it"*
- *"I've been doing this for 20 years and I'm still in the same place"*
- Confusion is present — they are doing what they were taught; they do not understand why it is not working
- Low-grade despair about spiritual life despite genuine desire for growth
- In session: engages content cognitively without affective response; observing quality rather than participating quality
- May say: *"I think I'm just not wired for this kind of thing"*

**Mode 4 vs. Mode 2 — the critical distinction:**

| | Mode 2 — Hermit | Mode 4 — No Inner Access |
|---|---|---|
| Practices present? | No — sparse or absent | Yes — consistent |
| Reason given | Spiritual framing ("trusting God," "not my own strength") | Confusion ("I do them but nothing happens") |
| Emotional signature | Flat affect, some peace | Confusion, low despair, flatness |
| In session | Lets coach carry; vague about desires | Engages cognitively; affectively distant |
| The problem | Avoidance of engagement | Engagement without inner access |

**If Mode 4 is present:** inner access must be developed before any Tier 2 or Tier 3 practice is assigned. The coaching relationship itself (Layer 2) is the primary instrument at this stage. Tier 1 practices only until inner access signals emerge.

**Inner access emergence signals:**
- Client begins referencing interior states unprompted (*"I noticed I felt..."*)
- Affective response appears in session to formation content
- Between-session practice produces some interior movement, even small
- Client can sit with an uncomfortable emotion in session for a sustained period without shutting down or deflecting

## STEP 5 — FAILURE MODE IDENTIFICATION

### Observable Indicators by Mode

**Mode 1 — Warrior**

*Language:* "I just need to be more disciplined." "I keep falling back." "I can't seem to be consistent."
*Behavior pattern:* Intense engagement followed by collapse, followed by restart. Cycles.
*Emotional signature:* Frustration, self-criticism. Spiritual self-worth tied to performance metrics.
*In session:* Takes notes. Wants action steps. Uncomfortable with sitting in ambiguity or with not having a plan.
*Body:* Forward lean, urgency, tight affect, problem-solving energy.

*Diagnostic questions:*
- *"What does it feel like when you miss a day of your practice?"*
- *"What would it mean about you spiritually if you stopped trying to grow?"*
- *"Who do you imagine is watching your spiritual progress?"*

---

**Mode 2 — Hermit**

*Language:* "I just need to surrender." "I've stopped trying to do it in my own strength." "I'm trusting God to do it."
*Behavior pattern:* Low engagement with practices. Spiritual life is primarily receptive with no structured active response.
*Emotional signature:* Flat affect around formation. A peace that may be avoidance. Difficulty naming specific desires for growth.
*In session:* Tends toward passive responses. Lets coach carry the conversation. Vague about what they actually want.
*Body:* Low energy. Reclined. Affect subdued. Comfortable with extended silence.

*Diagnostic questions:*
- *"What does surrendering look like on a Tuesday afternoon?"*
- *"What would it mean to engage this actively rather than wait?"*
- *"When did you stop trying? What happened right before that?"*

---

**Mode 3 — False Noble**

*Language:* "I've been really consistent with my quiet time." "People come to me with this kind of thing." "I led a discussion on this recently."
*Behavior pattern:* Spiritually active in visible and social domains. Private formation is thinner than the presentation suggests.
*Emotional signature:* Shame at exposure of the private gap. Defensiveness when the inner life is specifically probed.
*In session:* Presents well. Engages theological content fluently. May subtly redirect toward what they are doing well when the gap is approached.
*Body:* Composed, maintained presentation. Micro-shifts when inner life is directly probed.

*Diagnostic questions:*
- *"When you're alone and no one is watching — what does your spiritual life feel like?"*
- *"What would it mean if the people who know you spiritually could see exactly where you are right now?"*
- *"Who are you growing for?"*

---

**Mode 4 — Practiced Christianity Without Inner Access**

*(See Step 4 for full indicators. Summary:)*
*Language:* Nothing penetrates. Practices are present. Interior is flat or absent.
*Behavior pattern:* Consistent external engagement; no felt interior movement.
*Diagnostic distinction from Mode 2:* Practices are present and consistent. The problem is not avoidance — it is absence of interior response to genuine engagement.

---

### When Multiple Modes Are Present

**Primary = the mode producing the most pain, stagnation, and shame.**
**Secondary = the mode that reinforces or protects the primary.**

Assign the formation track to the primary mode. Note the secondary as a complicating factor. Do not assign two tracks.

**Common pairings and routing:**

| Pairing | Primary | Routing Logic |
|---|---|---|
| Mode 1 + Mode 3 | Mode 1 | Effort is the more accessible layer. Address effort pattern first; Mode 3 surfaces when asking who the effort is for. |
| Mode 2 + Mode 4 | Determined by practices | If practices are absent → Mode 2 primary. If practices are present but empty → Mode 4 primary. |
| Mode 1 + Mode 4 | Mode 4 | Inner access is the prerequisite. Assigning effort-based practices before access is established deepens the wall. |
| Mode 3 + Mode 4 | Mode 4 | Most defended presentation. Surface the gap carefully — the Mode 3 shame response will activate if exposure feels sudden. |

## STEP 7 — FORMATION TRACK ASSIGNMENT

Assign **one track only.** Do not adjust upward in tier before inner access signals are present in clients with Mode 4 overlay.

| Primary Failure Mode | Formation Track | Tier | Entry Practice |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mode 1 — Warrior | Phil 2:13 anchor: cooperation, not performance | Tier 1 → Tier 2 | Phil 2:13 Framework Meditation; advance to Abiding Rhythm |
| Mode 2 — Hermit | Active engagement at lowest demand | Tier 1 | Rom 6:11 Reckoning Practice |
| Mode 3 — False Noble | Surfaces who the effort is for; private formation space | Tier 1 → Tier 2 | Immunity to Change Map; advance to Expressive Writing |
| Mode 4 — No Inner Access | Relational safety first; no interiority demand | Tier 1 only until access signals emerge | Rom 6:11 Reckoning (cognitive-spiritual; low demand) |

**Practice debrief question (use at next session regardless of track):**
> *"Where did you notice the Spirit in it?"*

## STEP 8 — SCOPE BOUNDARY QUICK REFERENCE

**Stop and refer when:**
- Client floods, dissociates, or shuts down approaching formation content and cannot re-regulate within the session
- Daily functioning actively destabilized
- Active symptoms: intrusive memories, nightmares, hypervigilance currently present
- Mode 4 overlay with no traction on any Tier 1 practice after 4–6 sessions of consistent relational safety
- Active crisis: suicidal ideation, self-harm, acute substance use

**Continue when:**
- Client can engage the formation conversation without persistent flooding
- Basic affect regulation is present
- Stabilizing resources exist

*See [[Sanctification — Teacher's Manual]] Section X for referral language.*
