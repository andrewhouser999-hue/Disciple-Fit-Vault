# SESSION FACILITATION GUIDE
## The Believer's Part in Sanctification
### Covenant Identity Coaching — Disciple Fit

**Format key:**
**Say:** — scripted language, adapt naturally to your voice
**Watch for:** — what to observe in the client's response
**If [X]:** — branching logic based on client response
**Note:** — coach guidance, not scripted

---

## SESSION 1 — FRAMEWORK INTRODUCTION

### Opening the Session

**Note:** Do not open with the topic. Open with the client. Give them 5–10 minutes to land. Let them bring what is present. The framework introduction follows naturally from where they are — not from an agenda you announce.

When the moment is right — typically when the client returns to the presenting experience of stagnation, confusion, or shame around growth:

**Say:**
> *"I want to spend some time today on something that I think is underneath a lot of what you're describing. Before I do, I want to ask you something, and I just want you to answer it naturally — no right answer. Can you walk me through how you understand sanctification to work? Specifically, who does what?"*

**Watch for:** How much of the silence they can hold. Whether they reach for a definition quickly or actually think. The vocabulary they use and whether it carries ownership.

---

### Literacy-vs-Integration Check — Responding to Each Answer Type

**If vocabulary-only response** *(definition delivered without ownership; cannot explain the connection between God's role and theirs):*

**Say:**
> *"That's helpful. What you just described is actually the definition — and you have it right. What I'm noticing is that the pieces are there but they may not yet be connected into a picture that answers the question you're actually living with. Can I show you what I mean?"*

**Note:** Do not frame this as "you don't know enough." Frame it as "no one showed you how the pieces connect." The distinction matters — one produces shame, the other produces curiosity.

---

**If partial framework response** *(can name God's role or the believer's role but not both, or conflates definitive and progressive):*

**Say:**
> *"You've got real pieces of this. What I want to do is fill in the specific connection that I think is missing — because I think that gap is where a lot of the confusion is living."*

**Note:** Identify which connection is missing and target there specifically. Do not deliver the full framework if only one piece needs filling in.

---

**If organized response** *(can explain both roles and their connection):*

**Say:**
> *"You actually have a solid framework for this. So the gap isn't in what you know — it's somewhere else. Let me ask you something different: when you live from that framework, what happens? What does it actually produce?"*

**Note:** Move directly to failure mode identification. The Client Study introduction is not needed.

---

### Introducing the Framework — The Integrated Picture

**Note:** Lead with the client's question, not with the theology. The framework is the answer to what they are living with.

**Say:**
> *"Here's the question I think you're actually carrying, even if you haven't put it in these words: 'If God does this, why isn't it happening? And if I'm supposed to do something, why can't I?' Does that land?"*

**Watch for:** Whether the client recognizes themselves in the question. A yes here means the framework will be received as an answer, not as information.

---

**Say:**
> *"The reason those two things feel like they contradict each other is that no one has shown you the specific connection between them. Let me show you what I mean from a text you probably already know."*

**Present Philippians 2:12-13. Read it aloud using the paraphrased version:**

**Say:**
> *"Philippians 2:12 says, 'Work out your own salvation* with the gravity befitting one who cooperates with the living God, whose holiness and purposes are not to be handled casually. *Then verse 13 says, 'for it is God who works in you, both to will and to work for his good pleasure.'"*

**Say:**
> *"The word connecting those two verses is 'for' — or in the Greek, 'because.' Paul doesn't say: work, even though God works. He says: work, because God works. God's action in you is not the reason you don't need to act. It is the reason you can."*

**Watch for:** Whether this lands. A client who receives this well will often pause, or say something like "I've never heard it put that way." A client who deflects — immediately moving to application or to someone else this applies to — is showing you their failure mode.

---

**Continue with the order of the players:**

**Say:**
> *"So here's the picture when you organize it. God is the primary agent — he initiates this, he sustains it, and he works at a level beneath your conscious effort. The Spirit is specifically how he does that work in you — producing the fruit, enabling the movement, doing the deep change that willpower cannot reach. And you are a real agent — your choices matter, your engagement matters, your practices matter. But your agency is empowered agency, not independent agency. You are not supplementing insufficient grace. You are expressing the grace that is already at work."*

**Say:**
> *"The means of grace — things like Scripture, prayer, community, the disciplines — are not transformation machines. They do not cause change. They create conditions in which the Spirit does what only the Spirit can do. That is a different operation than most of us were taught."*

**Watch for:** How the client responds to "the disciplines are not transformation machines." Warrior clients will feel resistance here. Hermit clients may use it to justify passivity. Name what you observe.

---

### Naming the Failure Mode — Without Shaming

**Note:** Do not name the failure mode clinically. Name what you observe in plain language and let the client confirm.

**For Mode 1 indicators:**

**Say:**
> *"What I notice when you talk about this is that your instinct is to work harder. And I want to ask you something about that — what does it feel like spiritually when you miss a day of practice, or when you fall back into a pattern you were trying to break?"*

**Watch for:** Whether the client's self-worth is in the answer. If yes:

**Say:**
> *"I think one of the things that's happening is that you've been working at the wrong layer — not from bad faith, but because no one showed you a different layer existed. The disciplines you've been using as levers are actually conditions. And the difference between those two things changes everything about what effort is for."*

---

**For Mode 2 indicators:**

**Say:**
> *"I want to ask you something. When you say you're trusting God to do it — what does that look like on a Tuesday afternoon? What are you actually doing?"*

**Watch for:** Whether "surrender" has concrete content or is diffuse. If diffuse:

**Say:**
> *"I think what's happened is that the truth — that God does the deep work — has been received in a way that removed your part from the picture. But look at verse 12 again. The command is still there. 'Work out.' God's working in you is the reason you can — not the reason you don't need to."*

---

**For Mode 3 indicators:**

**Say:**
> *"I want to ask you about the private version of this. When you're alone — no one watching, no one to report back to — what does your formation look like? What's actually happening inside you?"*

**Watch for:** Whether the client can access the private register at all. If they redirect to what others see:

**Say:**
> *"I'm going to bring you back to the inside. Not what it looks like — what it feels like. When it's just you and God and no one is watching. What's there?"*

---

**For Mode 4 indicators:**

**Note:** Do not move forward with content when you see cognitive assent without affective engagement. Slow down.

**Say:**
> *"You said that makes sense. I want to stay here for a minute before we move. What is it like to hear this — not whether it's true, but what does it land like for you?"*

**Watch for:** Whether the client can access an interior response. If they return to cognitive processing:

**Say:**
> *"I hear you — and I believe you understand it. I'm asking something slightly different. Not what you think about it, but what you notice inside when you hear it. Even if the answer is 'not much' — that's worth knowing."*

**Note:** "Not much" is a Mode 4 signal. Do not push. Receive it with warmth. The relational experience of being heard without judgment is doing formation work at Layer 2 even when content is not penetrating.

---

### Practice Assignment

**Note:** Assign one practice only. Frame the practice as a condition, not a cause.

**Opening frame for all practices:**

**Say:**
> *"I want to give you something to carry between now and when we meet again. Before I do, I want to be clear about what this is and what it isn't. This practice is not going to transform you — that's the Spirit's work. What it does is create the conditions for that work to reach the layer where change needs to happen. Think of it less like pulling a lever and more like opening a window."*

---

**Tier 1, Track A — Philippians 2:13 Framework Meditation (Mode 1 primary):**

**Say:**
> *"Once a day — it doesn't need to be long — read Philippians 2:12-13 slowly. When you get to verse 13, stop. And ask one question: 'What is God's working in me making possible right now — not someday, right now?' Don't force an answer. Just hold the question. The goal is not productivity. The goal is to begin cooperating with what is already happening rather than trying to generate what you think should be happening."*

---

**Tier 1, Track B — Romans 6:11 Reckoning Practice (Mode 2 primary):**

**Say:**
> *"Once a day, say this aloud or write it: 'I am dead to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus.' That's Romans 6:11. This is not a positive affirmation — it's not about feeling it or generating it. It's an act of appropriating something that is already true. Paul calls it 'reckoning' — like a ledger entry. You are recording what is real, not trying to create something new. Do it even if it feels mechanical at first. Do it especially if it feels mechanical — because mechanical is a place to start, and the Spirit meets you there."*

---

**Tier 1, Track C — Immunity to Change Map (Mode 3 primary):**

**Say:**
> *"I want to walk you through something before you leave today, and then give you a piece of it to sit with. It's called an Immunity Map — and it's a way of looking at why we don't change even when we genuinely want to. It's not a spiritual exercise in the traditional sense. It's more like a diagnostic — and what it surfaces tends to be more honest than most of us expect."*

**Note:** Begin the Immunity to Change Map in session. Complete Columns 1 and 2 together. Columns 3 and 4 are between-session work. See [[Sanctification — ITC Practitioner Reference]] for full facilitation guidance.

---

**Tier 1, Track D — Rom 6:11 Reckoning Practice, low-demand entry (Mode 4 primary):**

**Say:**
> *"I want to give you something very simple — and I mean that literally. Once a day, read Romans 6:11 and say it aloud: 'I am dead to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus.' That's it. Don't evaluate it. Don't try to feel it. Don't try to make it land. Just say it. The goal right now is not a formation experience — it's simply showing up. We'll talk about what happens when you do."*

---

### Closing Session 1

**Say:**
> *"Before we close — what are you taking with you from today? Not what you're supposed to take — what's actually present for you right now?"*

**Watch for:** Whether the client can access an interior response to the session. This is a soft Mode 4 check and a formation posture indicator.

**Note:** Use the Coach Diagnostic routing card after the session to confirm your reading and plan Session 2.

## SESSION 2 — FRAMEWORK DEEPENING

### Opening — Practice Debrief

**Do not open with "Did you do it?" Open with:**

**Say:**
> *"Tell me — where did you notice the Spirit in what we gave you to carry this week?"*

---

**If the client engaged and noticed something:**

**Say:**
> *"Tell me more about that. What specifically happened?"*

**Note:** Let them describe it. Do not interpret it for them. The act of narrating interior movement is itself formation work.

---

**If the client engaged but noticed nothing:**

**Say:**
> *"You did it and nothing moved. That's worth knowing — and it's not a failure. It tells us something about where you are right now. Can I ask — when you did it, where were you? What was the context around it?"*

---

**If the client did not engage:**

**Say:**
> *"Okay. What got in the way?"*

**Watch for:** Whether the obstacle is circumstantial (life happened) or structural. Circumstantial → reassign and continue. Structural → the obstacle is the formation content for this session.

---

### Definitive vs. Progressive Distinction — When to Introduce

**Introduce this when:** The client has expressed some version of "I know I'm supposed to be sanctified / I know I'm in Christ — so why isn't it working?"

**Say:**
> *"There's a distinction in Scripture that I think is underneath some of what you're describing. There are actually two things happening when the Bible talks about sanctification — and they're different enough that mixing them up causes real confusion.*

> *The first is a once-for-all reality — a decisive breach that happened when you were united to Christ. Paul spends all of Romans 6 on this: the old self was crucified with him. That's done.*

> *The second is an ongoing process — the work of conforming your whole person to what is already true in Christ. That's the progressive work, and it's genuinely incomplete in this life.*

> *Here's where the confusion happens: you know the first is real, and you're experiencing the second stalling — so you conclude either the first isn't actually real, or God is withholding, or something is wrong with you. None of those are right. The first is solid. The second is where the formation work actually lives — and it has a mechanism."*

---

### Soil Type Mapping — When to Introduce

**For Rocky soil:**

**Say:**
> *"There's an image from the Parable of the Soils that I think describes what you're experiencing. Jesus talks about seed that falls on rocky ground — it springs up quickly, even with joy, and then withers because there's no root. The problem isn't the seed and it's not the response — it's the depth. What we're working on together is depth, not volume. One practice held consistently goes further than five practices tried and abandoned."*

---

**For Thorny soil:**

**Say:**
> *"Jesus also talks about seed that falls among thorns — and he names what the thorns are specifically: the worries of life, the pull of other things we're living for. The word doesn't get rejected; it gets crowded out. Before we add more practices, I want to ask: what's taking up the space where formation could be happening? What's getting the center of your attention and energy right now?"*

---

### Advancing the Formation Track

**Advance from Tier 1 to Tier 2 when:**
- Client engaged the Tier 1 practice with some degree of genuine interiority
- At least one interior observation was made during the debrief
- No flooding, dissociation, or shutdown in either session
- Mode 4 inner access signals are emerging

**Hold at Tier 1 when:**
- Practice was performed but the debrief produced flatness or confusion
- Mode 4 overlay remains strong — no inner access signals yet
- Client is still in the framework-receiving phase

**Say when advancing:**

> *"What you did this week created a small opening. I want to give you something that goes a little deeper through that opening — not more effort, just a different kind of engagement."*

---

### Closing Session 2 — Transitioning to Ongoing Thread

**Say:**
> *"Here's where we are: you have the picture now of how this actually works — who does what, and why the gap between what you know and what you're experiencing isn't evidence of defect. And you have a practice that's starting to create conditions for the Spirit to work at the layer where change needs to happen.*

> *This doesn't become a separate topic we return to every session — it becomes the thread underneath everything else we do. When you notice something isn't moving in your life, we'll look at which layer it's happening at and what the Spirit might be doing there. The framework stays active. The practice stays between sessions. And we keep reading what's emerging."*

## HANDLING RESISTANCE — BY FAILURE MODE

### Mode 1 — Warrior Resistance
*Presentation:* Immediately converts the framework into a new performance standard. Wants a more rigorous practice.

**Say:**
> *"I notice you want to take this and run with it — and I want to name what I see happening. That impulse is the pattern we're looking at, not the solution to it. The goal isn't to do this harder. The goal is to do this differently — at a different layer. Effort applied at the wrong layer produces the same result you've already been getting."*

---

### Mode 2 — Hermit Resistance
*Presentation:* Uses "God works in you" (Phil 2:13) to reinforce passivity.

**Say:**
> *"I want to bring you back to both verses — not just the one. Verse 13 is the ground of verse 12, not the replacement of it. The because is pointing forward, not backward. God's working in you is why you can engage — not why you don't need to. What would it look like to take one small step from that ground?"*

---

### Mode 3 — False Noble Resistance
*Presentation:* Processes the framework through others. "This is really helpful — I have a friend who needs to hear this."

**Say:**
> *"I want to bring you back to you for a minute. Not your friend — you. Where does this land in your own formation? What does it name that you've been living with privately?"*

**If the client redirects again:**

**Say:**
> *"I'm going to be direct — I notice we keep moving away from you when the conversation gets close to the inside. That's worth paying attention to. What happens when you try to stay there?"*

---

### Mode 4 — No Inner Access Resistance
*Presentation:* Receives the framework cognitively. Says "that makes sense." No affective response.

**Note:** This is not resistance in the traditional sense — it is the absence of access. Do not push content forward.

**Say:**
> *"I want to slow down here. You've received this really well cognitively — you understand it. What I'm sitting with is whether there's somewhere underneath the understanding where this is landing, or not landing. Can you tell me what you notice?"*

**If the client returns to cognitive processing:**

**Say:**
> *"That's okay. Sometimes the inside isn't available yet, and that's not a failure — it's information. What I want you to know is that you showing up to this conversation, even when nothing seems to land, is not nothing. The Spirit meets that."*

---

## RETURN SESSION PROTOCOL — SESSION 3 AND BEYOND

**Open with:**

**Say:**
> *"Since we last met — where have you noticed the Spirit working at the formation layer? It doesn't have to be dramatic. Even small movement counts."*

**If the client reports no movement:**

**Say:**
> *"Tell me what the week held. Not to evaluate it — to read it together. Sometimes what looks like no movement is movement that hasn't been named yet."*

**Note:** Use the Coach Diagnostic routing card at the end of each session to confirm your current reading. Modes shift as formation advances — what presented as Mode 1 at intake may reveal Mode 4 underneath once the effort-identity begins to loosen. Stay current.

## TRANSITION PROTOCOL — MOVING BEYOND THIS MODULE

### Readiness Signals

**Load-bearing signals (both required before transitioning):**

1. **Framework organized** — The client can explain who does what in sanctification without prompting. They can articulate why God's working and their working are not in competition. The Phil 2:12-13 connection is theirs, not borrowed.
2. **Interior movement in practice** — Debrief language has shifted. The client no longer reports completing the practice — they report noticing something in it. *"I noticed the Spirit in..."* rather than *"I did it."* Even small movement counts. Complete flatness after 4–6 sessions of consistent engagement is a Mode 4 signal, not a readiness signal.

**Confirming signals (strengthen the readiness reading):**

3. **Failure mode self-identified** — The client names their own pattern without the coach naming it first. Self-identification signals the formation framework is operative, not just understood.
4. **ITC Column 4 assumption named and held** — If Track C was used, the big assumption has been named, traced to its origin, and is being held without flooding. It does not need to be resolved — it needs to be known.
5. **Spirit-noticing is unprompted** — At least one session has opened with the client reporting movement from between sessions without being asked specifically. The formation posture is becoming habitual.

### What Comes Next — By Diagnostic Signal

| Primary Signal After Module Work | Next Step |
|---|---|
| False covenant object surfaced and named; client ready for Stage 2 work | Affections Discovery deepening → Diagnostic Lens Stage 2 (False Identity Named) |
| Attachment patterns blocking the abiding component; insecure God-attachment active | [[Secured in Father — Practitioner Design Notes]] → Entry Guide + Encounter Prep |
| Character wound clearly identified and driving the failure mode | [[Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool]] deepening; wound-type formation track |
| Cognitive distortion blocking reception of covenant truth after formation layer engaged | Stage 4a — CBT tools per [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]] |
| Column 4 assumption is a specific lie ready for counter-declaration | Lie Eliminator entry — build declaration from Column 4 assumption |
| Client stable in the framework; no acute diagnostic signal dominant | Continue formation practice; deepen one layer at a time; monitor for next signal |

### Handoff Language to the Client

**Say:**
> *"What we've been building together is the foundation — the framework for how formation actually works, and the practice that creates conditions for the Spirit to do what only the Spirit can do. What I'm seeing now is that you're ready to go deeper. What we've done here doesn't go away — it becomes the ground we work from. What we're adding is [specific next work]. The same question stays active: where are you noticing the Spirit? The answer is just going to get more specific."*

**If transitioning to Affections Discovery Stage 2:**
> *"We've named what you're trusting instead of God in this area. The next piece is getting precise about the identity that has been built around that trust — what the false self looks like, and what the covenant self looks like instead. That's where the identity work gets specific."*

**If transitioning to Secured in Father:**
> *"What I'm seeing is that the abiding practice keeps running into something in your relationship with God itself — a distance that isn't just about practice. There's a tool designed for exactly that. It's not more information about God — it's a structured way of encountering him in the specific places where the attachment is thin."*

**If transitioning to Lie Eliminator:**
> *"You've named the assumption that's been running underneath. That's the lie in its most honest form. What we do next is build the counter-declaration — not to argue it away, but to practice receiving what is actually true until the covenant truth becomes more real than the assumed one. That's the Lie Eliminator work."*

### Note for the Coach

The module does not end — it becomes background. The formation practice continues as the between-session anchor throughout the coaching engagement. The framework (Spirit as primary agent, empowered human agency, disciplines as conditions) is operative for all subsequent formation work. The transition is not away from this module's content but deeper into the specific layer the diagnostic signals are pointing to.
