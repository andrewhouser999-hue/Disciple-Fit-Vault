# Your Part in How Change Actually Happens
### Covenant Identity Coaching — Disciple Fit

---

## The Question You're Actually Living With

If you have been a Christian for any length of time, you have probably carried some version of this question — maybe quietly, maybe with real frustration:

*If God does this, why isn't it happening? And if I'm supposed to do something, why can't I?*

Most of us were never given a straight answer to that question. We were given pieces — verses about surrender, sermons about discipline, encouragement to pray more, study more, try harder. Some of us tried harder and burned out. Some of us stopped trying and called it trust. Most of us have spent years somewhere in between, wondering if something is wrong with us that other people have figured out.

This study is not going to give you more pieces. It is going to show you how the pieces you already have fit together — because the picture they form, when you finally see it, changes what effort is for, what rest is for, and why the gap between what you know and what you feel is not evidence that God is withholding from you.

---

## The Text Nobody Connected for You

You probably know Philippians 2:12-13. You may have heard it dozens of times. Here it is again — read it slowly:

> *"Work out your own salvation* with the gravity befitting one who cooperates with the living God, whose holiness and purposes are not to be handled casually. *For it is God who works in you, both to will and to work for his good pleasure."* ¹

The word between those two sentences is *for* — or in the original Greek, *because.*

Paul does not say: work out your salvation *even though* God works in you. He says: work out your salvation *because* God works in you.

That one word changes everything. God's action in you is not the reason you do not need to act. It is the reason you can.

Most of us received these two verses as a contradiction to manage — God's part versus our part, with the tension unresolved. Paul is not giving us a contradiction. He is giving us a foundation. The command to engage is built on the reality that God is already at work at the level where change actually happens.

---

## The Order of the Players

When you organize the biblical picture, three things are happening — not in competition, but in a specific relationship.

**God is the primary agent.**
He initiates. He sustains. He works in you at a level beneath your conscious effort. Ezekiel 36:27 names it directly: God puts his Spirit within his people and *causes* them to walk in his ways. Not enables. Causes. The change comes from him, at depths you cannot reach on your own.

This is the answer to "why isn't it happening?" — not because God is withholding, but because the means through which he works may not yet have been engaged at the right layer. More on that below.

**The Spirit is how God does that work in you.**
The Spirit produces fruit — not you (Galatians 5:22-23). The Spirit enables the moment-by-moment movement of life in a new direction (Galatians 5:16, 25). The Spirit works with you to put to death the patterns that keep reasserting themselves (Romans 8:13). The deep change — the change at the level of desire, disposition, and character — is the Spirit's work. It is not something you can produce through willpower. But it is something you can create conditions for.

**You are a real and necessary agent.**
This is where the pieces that get handed to us in fragments actually belong. The New Testament is full of commands — real commands to real people with real wills.

*Reckon yourselves dead to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus* (Romans 6:11). This is an act — a cognitive and spiritual appropriation of what is already true.

*Present yourselves to God* (Romans 6:13). A decisive offering of the whole person.

*Put to death what is earthly in you* (Colossians 3:5). Active. Commanded.

*Put on the new self* (Colossians 3:12). Active reception — not manufacture, but receiving what God has declared.

*Walk by the Spirit* (Galatians 5:16). Ongoing, oriented movement.

*Abide in me* (John 15:4). Maintained relational connection.

These commands are not decorations. They are not impossible ideals. They are real instructions to people who have been given real capacity — capacity given, not generated. The ability to act rightly is something God provides. The act itself is genuinely yours.

## What the Disciplines Are — and What They Are Not

Here is where many of us were handed a misunderstanding that has cost us years.

The spiritual disciplines — prayer, Scripture, fasting, community, Sabbath, confession — are not transformation machines. They do not cause change. Doing them harder does not produce more change. That is not how they work.

What they do is create *conditions* — the kind of soil in which the Spirit does work that direct effort alone cannot reach. Think of it this way: you cannot make a plant grow by pulling on it. But you can water it, place it in light, tend the soil. You do not cause the growth. You create the conditions in which growth becomes possible.

The disciplines are the watering and the light. The Spirit is the one who grows.

This is why intensity of effort has not always produced the depth of change you hoped for. Effort applied at the wrong layer — at the surface, at behavior, at willpower — does not reach the layer where desire and character actually live. The Spirit reaches that layer. The disciplines create the conditions for the Spirit to do so.

---

## Two Things Happening at Once

There is a distinction in Scripture that, once you see it, makes sense of a lot of confusion.

There is what theologians call *definitive sanctification* — a once-for-all reality accomplished when you were united to Christ. Romans 6 describes it: the old self was crucified with him. The power of sin over you was broken decisively. This is done. This is not the question.

And there is *progressive sanctification* — the ongoing work of conforming your whole person to what is already true in Christ. Your affections. Your character. Your desires. Your patterns. This work is real, it is incomplete, and it continues throughout your life.

Here is where the confusion lives: you know the first is real — you belong to Christ. And you experience the second stalling — the same patterns reassert, the same wounds resurface, the same distance from God persists. And in that gap, most of us quietly conclude one of three things: the first is not as real as we thought, God is withholding from us, or something is defective in us that is not defective in other people.

None of those conclusions are correct.

The first is solid. You are in Christ. That is not in question. The second is where the formation work actually lives — and it has a mechanism. That mechanism is what this study is about.

---

## Why the Gap Is Not Your Defect

The gap between what you know and what you feel — between the truth you hold and the life it hasn't yet formed — is not evidence of spiritual failure. It is evidence that the formation layer has not yet been reached.

Think about what the biblical picture shows: God works at the level of desire — Philippians 2:13 says he works in you "to will" — to want rightly — before he works in you "to work." The deepest register of change is desire. And desire is not something you can restructure through argument or willpower. It is restructured through sustained, whole-person engagement with the God who reorders what we love.

That engagement has a name in Scripture. Jesus called it abiding.

> *"Abide in me, and I in you. As the branch cannot bear fruit by itself...neither can you, unless you abide in me."* (John 15:4)

The branch does not manufacture fruit. It stays connected to the vine. The fruit is the vine's work, expressed through the branch's life. Your part is the staying — the sustained, intentional, Word-saturated, prayer-active remaining in connection with Christ. That staying is not passive. It is not easy. It is the real work. And it is the condition under which the Spirit produces what willpower cannot.

---

## What This Means for You

The question you have been living with — *if God does this, why isn't it happening? if I'm supposed to do something, why can't I?* — has an answer.

It is not happening at the depth you long for because the means through which the Spirit works at the deepest layers have not yet been engaged there. Not because God is withholding. Not because you are defective. Because formation at the affections and character layer requires conditions — sustained, honest, whole-person engagement — that most of us were never taught to create.

What you are supposed to do is real. It is not try harder at what you have already been trying. It is engage differently — at the layer where the Spirit is already working, through the means God has appointed, without demanding that the means produce what only the Spirit can.

Your part is showing up with genuine engagement. God's part is doing what only God can do. And those two things do not compete. They belong together.

---

## A Question to Sit With

Before you meet with your coach, sit with this:

*When you have tried to grow spiritually, what layer have you been working at? Behavior? Belief? Or have you been creating conditions for the Spirit to reach the layer underneath both?*

You do not need to have an answer. The question itself is the beginning of something.

---

*¹ The original text of Philippians 2:12 reads "with fear and trembling." This phrase has been paraphrased here because in a culture shaped by shame and performance, "fear" language often activates the wrong response — dread, hypervigilance, the sense that God is watching for failure. Paul's meaning is closer to the sober weight of knowing that the one you are cooperating with is the holy and living God. That weight is not terror. It is gravity — the gravity of something that actually matters.*
