# Covenant Identity — Psychological Constructs & CIC Reverse Connection Reference

*Practitioner quick reference. Entry point: a psychological construct encountered in a client's presentation, reading, or supervision. Output: the CIC diagnostic stage, engagement phase, tool, file, and mechanism that addresses it.*

*This document does not provide clinical definitions. It maps recognized psychological constructs to their CIC equivalents and connection points — so that encountering a psychological term in a client, in reading, or in supervision produces immediate orientation within the CI system.*

---

## How to Use

**Direction:** Psychological construct → CIC system (not the reverse).

When you encounter a construct in intake language, session material, reading, or supervision — find it here to locate:
- **Layer:** whether it operates explicitly (cognitive), implicitly (body/relational memory), or both — which determines the CIC track
- **Diagnostic Stage:** where in the in-session sequence it is addressed
- **Engagement Phase:** where in the four-phase arc it primarily surfaces
- **Scope:** coaching territory, extra care required, or clinical referral
- **How CIC engages:** the specific mechanism the model uses
- **Primary tools and key files** to reach for

---

## Scope Legend

● **Coaching scope** — fully within non-clinical coaching practice
◐ **Coaching with care** — can be engaged in coaching; clinical proximity requires active monitoring of referral criteria
○ **Refer** — exceeds coaching scope; clinical care required; coaching engages stable residue only, if at all

*See [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]] for referral triggers and language.*

---

## Layer Legend

**Explicit** — held consciously; accessible through language and cognition; Stage 4a track
**Implicit** — held pre-consciously in emotional memory, body, and relational patterns; Stage 4b track and formation disciplines
**Both** — operates at both levels; requires dual-track engagement across the arc

---

## Reference: Engagement Phases

- **Phase 1** — Covenant Orientation (Sessions 1–2): alliance, intake review, Gap naming, initial God-representation surfacing
- **Phase 2** — Wound Mapping (Sessions 2–4): schema excavation, ITC, lament, wound origin tracing
- **Phase 3** — Identity Installation (Sessions 4–8): declaration, schema interruption, narrative re-authoring
- **Phase 4** — Integration & Close (Sessions 8–10): consolidation, formation sustainability, Growth Report

## Reference: Diagnostic Stages

- **Stage 1** — Affections Discovery: surface what the heart is functionally trusting
- **Stage 2** — False Identity Named: name the specific lie, false covenant object, and trigger pattern
- **Stage 3** — Covenant Truth: speak covenant truth into the activated false identity
- **Stage 4a** — CBT-informed: engage the cognitive distortion blocking covenant truth reception
- **Stage 4b** — Relational-Attentive: address the implicit memory barrier when cognitive tools cannot reach it

## 1. Attachment & Relational

---

### Insecure Attachment

*Organized insecurity in early attachment — anxious (protest), avoidant (withdrawal), or disorganized (ambivalence) — producing implicit relational templates that shape identity formation and govern relational behavior non-consciously*

**Layer:** Implicit
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 1 (surfacing) → Stage 2 (naming)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 1–2
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care — disorganized attachment has highest clinical proximity

**How CIC engages:** The character wound typology is the CI structural analog for insecure attachment classification: Warrior = protest/betrayal (anxious); Hermit = terror/withdrawal (avoidant); False Noble = ambivalence (disorganized). Wound identification runs after the affections picture is specific; all subsequent tools are calibrated to wound type, making wound identification one of the highest-leverage early diagnostic moves.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 1 (disordered affections formed in attachment disruption) → Node 2 (wound type deepens the false identity picture before Stage 2 naming)
**Primary tools:** Character Wound Diagnostic Tool; Affections Discovery
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool]], [[Covenant Identity — Affections Discovery — In-Session Protocol]]

---

### Internal Working Models

*Bowlby: implicit relational templates encoding expectations about self, others, and the availability of care — formed through early attachment experience and governing relational behavior non-consciously; distorted IWMs produce predictable self-concept and relational patterns*

**Layer:** Implicit
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 1 (Affections Layers 3–4) → God-Representation surfacing
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 1–2
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care

**How CIC engages:** CI routes IWM distortion through God-representation work — the client's implicit God-image is the internal working model applied to God. Hall's implicit/explicit God-image distinction and Rizzuto's "living God" framework ground this. The God-Exclusion Map and God-Representation Deepening Tool surface the IWM distortion operating at the level of the client's experienced theology. Surfacing the implicit God-image is the CIC entry point for IWM work.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 1 (disordered affections include distorted relational expectation patterns that were formed in early attachment)
**Primary tools:** God-Representation Deepening Tool; God-Exclusion Map; Affections Discovery (Layers 3–4)
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — God-Representation — Research Basis & Synthesis]], [[Covenant Identity — God-Representation Deepening Tool]], [[Covenant Identity — God-Exclusion Map]]

---

### Object Relations Disturbance

*Disruptions in internalized representations of self and significant others — derived from formative relational experience — affecting self-concept formation, relational functioning, and the capacity for healthy attachment*

**Layer:** Implicit
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 1–2
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 1–2
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care

**How CIC engages:** CI does not import clinical object relations theory as a framework, but the Whole-Person Identity Framework accounts for how formative relational experience shapes distorted self-representation. Thompson and Allender are positioned at the mechanism level — naming how relational history creates identity distortion — without importing clinical intervention models. The Integration Rationale documents the epistemological basis for this deployment.
**Primary tools:** Character Wound Diagnostic Tool; God-Representation Deepening Tool
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Whole-Person Identity Framework]], [[Covenant Identity — Integration Rationale]], [[Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool]]

---

### Relational Trauma

*Identity-level disruption produced by chronic wounding within significant relationships — distinct from single-event trauma; embedded in implicit memory and relational patterning; often presents as persistent relational difficulty, self-protective patterns, and deep mistrust*

**Layer:** Implicit
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 2 (wound mapping) → Stage 4b (if stable and entering implicit layer)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 2 → Phase 3 if stable
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care — acute or active presentations → ○ Refer

**How CIC engages:** Wound mapping traces relational trauma as the origin story of the character wound and false identity structure. Stage 4b tools address implicit-level trauma encoding through coach presence and attunement rather than cognitive technique. The referral protocol governs the scope ceiling — when somatic dysregulation or dissociative features are present, clinical referral is triggered before continuing in Stage 4b.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 1–2 (relational trauma produces the character wounds that organize the false identity; wound type deepens the diagnostic picture)
**Primary tools:** Character Wound Diagnostic Tool; Stage 4b Implicit Level tools; Crisis & Referral Protocol
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool]], [[Covenant Identity — Stage 4b Implicit Level Practitioner Reference]], [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]]

## 2. Schema & Core Belief

---

### Early Maladaptive Schemas

*Young: deeply held, self-reinforcing cognitive-emotional patterns formed in childhood through unmet core needs — defectiveness/shame, abandonment/instability, subjugation, failure, unrelenting standards, etc.; they resist change because they are self-confirming and often unconscious*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 2 (excavation) → Stage 3 (installation); Stage 4a if cognitive; Stage 4b if implicit
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 2 → Phase 3
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope — personality-level schema entrenchment → ◐ Coaching with care

**How CIC engages:** Schema is the psychological term for what CI calls a false identity structure. The Lie Eliminator targets the schema's cognitive-verbal content (the lie); the Identity Installer installs covenant counter-truth. The ITC Protocol (Column 3–4) surfaces the schema's hidden protective function. Deep Worksheets extend the work into schema and narrative layers explicitly (Young, Kegan & Lahey, McAdams frameworks embedded in design rationale).
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 2 (the schema is the specific structure being named with diagnostic precision) → Node 5 (the schema must be emotionally activated before covenant truth can displace it rather than supplement it)
**Primary tools:** Lie Eliminator; Identity Installer; ITC Protocol; Deep Lie Work Worksheet; Deep Identity Installation Worksheet
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Deep Lie Work Worksheet]], [[Covenant Identity — Deep Identity Installation Worksheet]], [[Covenant Identity — Deep Work Worksheets — Design Rationale]], [[Sanctification — ITC Practitioner Reference]]

---

### Dysfunctional Core Beliefs

*Beck: negative beliefs about self, world, and future (the cognitive triad) operating at the explicit cognitive level and driving maladaptive emotional and behavioral responses; the client can often articulate them when asked directly*

**Layer:** Explicit
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 2 → Stage 4a (when actively blocking covenant truth reception)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 2–3
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope

**How CIC engages:** Stage 4a deploys CBT-informed tools specifically when a cognitive distortion is actively blocking covenant truth reception — the client engages the truth but pushes back through reasoning. The Lie Eliminator directly addresses the cognitive content of false core beliefs with covenant counter-truth. Stage 4a serves Stage 3; it does not replace it — the goal remains covenant truth landing, not distortion correction for its own sake.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 2 (naming the specific lie) → Node 5 (activation is required; the belief must be emotionally live before truth can land at the implicit level)
**Primary tools:** Lie Eliminator; Stage 4a CBT tools (Socratic questioning, downward arrow, thought record)
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]], [[Covenant Identity — Phase 3 Session Tools (Identity Installation)]], [[Covenant Identity — Deep Lie Work Worksheet]]

---

### Cognitive Distortions

*Beck/Burns: systematic errors in thinking — catastrophizing, mind reading, all-or-nothing thinking, personalization, etc. — that reinforce false beliefs about self and actively block the reception of corrective truth*

**Layer:** Explicit
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 4a — triggered by specific observation: client engaging covenant truth but pushing back through reasoning
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 3 — triggered as needed, not scheduled
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope

**How CIC engages:** Cognitive distortions are the specific trigger for routing to Stage 4a in the Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic. The Stage 4a/4b distinction is load-bearing: cognitive distortion produces active reasoning resistance (apply Stage 4a); implicit memory barrier produces intellectual agreement without felt movement (apply Stage 4b). Applying Stage 4a tools to a Stage 4b presentation adds more content to a client who already cognitively agrees — it deepens their sense of failure without moving the barrier.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 5 (the distortion is preventing activated belief — it must be surfaced and loosened before covenant truth can land at the implicit level)
**Primary tools:** Stage 4a tools (Socratic questioning, downward arrow, thought record, behavioral experiment); Lie Eliminator
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]], [[Covenant Identity — What Moves Covenant Truth from Registration to Reception]]

## 3. Memory Architecture

---

### Implicit/Explicit Memory Dissociation

*The gap between what a person consciously believes (explicit declarative memory) and what their emotional and procedural systems enact (implicit memory) — the primary neurological explanation for why intellectual truth does not automatically produce behavioral change; Thompson and Ecker provide the theoretical basis*

**Layer:** Both — the gap between the two systems is the construct itself
**Diagnostic Stage:** Operative throughout; the Stage 4a/4b distinction is the in-session diagnostic expression of it
**Engagement Phase:** Throughout — this is the central problem the entire CIC arc addresses
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope — this is the core mechanism CI is designed to address

**How CIC engages:** The entire CIC formation architecture is built around this gap. The Functional vs. Confessional Theology document maps 20 diagnostic contrasts between what the client confesses and what their heart functionally enacts. Formation disciplines create conditions for implicit reorganization. Stage 4b specifically targets the implicit layer when it cannot be reached through cognitive tools. The Theory of Change is an eight-node sequence for moving truth from explicit registration to implicit reorganization through Spirit-initiated reconsolidation.
**Theory of Change Node:** Nodes 1–8 — the entire arc is a system for closing the gap between explicit registration and implicit reorganization
**Primary tools:** All formation disciplines; Stage 4b tools; Declaration architecture (Lie Eliminator, Identity Installer, Matched Declaration)
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Functional vs. Confessional Theology — 20 Diagnostic Contrasts]], [[Covenant Identity Coaching — Theory of Change]], [[Covenant Identity — What Moves Covenant Truth from Registration to Reception]], [[Covenant Identity — Stage 4b Implicit Level Practitioner Reference]]

---

### Reconsolidation Failure

*When implicit memory structures are repeatedly activated but never updated — because the conditions for neural reconsolidation (juxtaposition of the emotionally active old pattern with a disconfirming new experience) are not met; the pattern holds and returns under stress even after apparent progress (Ecker, Nader & LeDoux)*

**Layer:** Implicit
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 3 (activation requirement is the in-session reconsolidation condition) → Stage 4b (when barrier is implicit); Node 7 (between-session)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 3–4
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope

**How CIC engages:** The activation requirement at Stage 3 — implicit belief must be emotionally live before covenant truth is introduced — is the in-session reconsolidation condition. Declaration spoken into cognitive acknowledgment produces extinction (parallel track, original returns under stress); declaration spoken into activated implicit belief creates conditions for reconsolidation (the encoding rewrites). Between-session formation disciplines are designed to produce receptive return — not affirmation repetition — because only receptive return meets the reconsolidation conditions in the between-session space.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 5 (activation before truth = reconsolidation condition) → Node 7 (repeated receptive return consolidates the reorganization across the engagement)
**Primary tools:** Declaration disciplines; Secured in Father; Parts & Burden Discovery Worksheet; Spiritual Disciplines Guide
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Consolidation Through Receptive Return — Mechanism]], [[Covenant Identity — What Moves Covenant Truth from Registration to Reception]], [[Covenant Identity — Parts & Burden Discovery — Session Worksheet]]

## 4. Trauma

---

### Developmental / Complex Trauma (C-PTSD)

*Cumulative relational trauma producing pervasive affect dysregulation, identity disruption, and fragmented self-concept — distinct from single-incident PTSD; typically formed through chronic exposure to threat, neglect, or relational unpredictability in childhood*

**Layer:** Implicit
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 2 (wound mapping); Stage 4b only if client is stable
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 2
**Scope:** ○ Refer for active presentations — ◐ Coaching with care only when stable and with clinical support in place

**How CIC engages:** CI names developmental trauma as the formative context for character wound formation. Wound mapping traces the origin story of the false identity. Stage 4b relational-attentive tools can attend to implicit-level encoding when a client is stable, non-crisis, and clinically supported. The engagement cannot proceed with active C-PTSD — the Crisis & Referral Protocol governs the entry decision and ongoing scope boundary.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 1 (developmental trauma produces the character wounds that organize the false identity and its disordered affections)
**Primary tools:** Crisis & Referral Protocol (entry gate); Character Wound Diagnostic; Stage 4b tools
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]], [[Sanctification — Teacher's Manual]], [[Covenant Identity — Stage 4b Implicit Level Practitioner Reference]]

---

### Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACE)

*Felitti et al.: cumulative childhood exposure to abuse, neglect, and household dysfunction — with dose-response effects on identity formation, affect regulation, relational patterns, and adult functioning; elevated ACE scores raise C-PTSD risk and require calibrated pacing*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 1–2 (intake and wound mapping)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 1–2
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care — high ACE scores raise C-PTSD risk; screen actively at intake

**How CIC engages:** The Sanctification Teacher's Manual is explicitly calibrated to an ACE-prevalent population. The four failure modes mapped to wound types account for ACE-produced patterns. Client portrait (85% moderate literacy, high ACE) is the design baseline for the sanctification module. ACE history surfaces through wound mapping and ITC diagnostics and shapes tool deployment intensity and pacing throughout the engagement.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 1 (ACE produces the disordered affections and character wounds that organize the false identity)
**Primary tools:** Character Wound Diagnostic; ITC Protocol; Crisis & Referral Protocol
**Key files:** [[Sanctification — Teacher's Manual]], [[Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool]], [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]]

---

### Unprocessed Grief

*Accumulated loss — relational, identity-level, or circumstantial — that has not moved through a grief or lament process; leaves the heart organized around wound rather than formation; often presents as chronic low-grade anger, numbness, or an inability to receive comfort or covenant truth*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 2–3 (Lament as the transition mechanism between naming and receiving)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 2–3
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care — complicated grief may warrant clinical referral

**How CIC engages:** The Felt Inadequacy mechanism (Node 3) requires the grief of release — the false covenant object must be experienced as failing before covenant truth can fill the vacancy it leaves. The Lament Practice Guide provides the four-movement container (honest address → complaint → petition → trust) and calibrates psalm selection to wound type. Lament is not grief therapy — it is the formation practice that moves grief through the relationship with God rather than around it.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 3 (felt inadequacy requires the grief of release; lament creates the vacancy that covenant truth can fill)
**Primary tools:** Lament Practice Guide; Parts & Burden Discovery; ITC Column 3–4
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Lament Practice Guide]], [[Covenant Identity — Felt Inadequacy and the Vacancy — Mechanism]]

## 5. Shame

---

### Internalized Shame

*Shame held as a core identity conclusion ("I am defective, unlovable, fundamentally flawed") rather than a situational emotion — stored implicitly in bodily experience and relational pattern, predating and outlasting any explicit cognitive knowledge of worth*

**Layer:** Implicit (primary) / Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 1 (Hermit wound markers) → Stage 2 → Stage 3 (Shame Declaration track)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 2–3
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care — deep character-level shame has clinical proximity

**How CIC engages:** The Hermit wound type (terror of exposure, withdrawal) is the primary CI presentation of internalized shame. Isaiah 66:2b (contrite in spirit) provides the biblical anchor for the formation posture available to a shame-organized self. The Matched Declaration Architecture (Ephesians 1:3–8 — Shame track) is the primary covenant truth response. Stage 4b attends to the somatic and implicit dimension that carries shame below the cognitive level.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 2 (the shame structure must be named with precision — not "I struggle with shame" but the specific lie and its trigger) → Node 3 (the grief of the shame-organized self's inadequacy) → Node 5 (activated shame + covenant truth = reconsolidation condition)
**Primary tools:** Character Wound Diagnostic (Hermit type); Matched Declaration Architecture; Lament Practice Guide; Stage 4b tools
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool]], [[Covenant Identity — Matched Declaration Architecture]], [[Covenant Identity — Isaiah 66 2b — Practitioner Exegetical Reference]]

---

### Shame-Based Identity

*The entire self-concept organized around hiddenness, performance, or pre-emptive self-protection — shame as the governing principle of identity rather than one affect among many; the identity system is structured to prevent exposure rather than to receive relationship*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 1–3 (pervasive — this is a presenting configuration of false identity across the full arc, not a single stage issue)
**Engagement Phase:** Throughout
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care

**How CIC engages:** Shame-based identity is one of the primary presentations of false identity in CIC. The full diagnostic and formation sequence addresses it: affections work surfaces the shame-organized functional dependencies; wound mapping traces the origin and formation; Why the Heart Does Not Heal names the resistance patterns specific to shame-organized clients (self-deception, refusal of suffering, not receiving). Covenant truth (adoption, acceptance, belovedness) is the specific Stage 3 counter-truth.
**Theory of Change Node:** Nodes 1–8 — shame-based identity is not one node; it is a presenting configuration the entire arc works to displace
**Primary tools:** Full CIC diagnostic and formation arc; Why the Heart Does Not Heal; Matched Declaration (Shame track)
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity Coaching — Practice Definition]], [[Covenant Identity — Why the Heart Does Not Heal — Practitioner Diagnostic]], [[Covenant Identity — Matched Declaration Architecture]]

## 6. Neurobiological Regulation

---

### Polyvagal Dysregulation

*Porges: chronic threat-based nervous system patterning — sympathetic hyperactivation (fight/flight) or dorsal vagal shutdown (freeze/collapse) — that keeps the false self structurally in place at the physiological level regardless of cognitive change; the body enforces old identity patterns even when the mind has updated*

**Layer:** Implicit
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 4b (primary track for nervous system–level engagement)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 3 (Stage 4b triggered)
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care — severe or persistent dysregulation approaches clinical boundary

**How CIC engages:** Stage 4b is the primary CIC track for nervous system–level work. Coach presence, attunement, and a regulated relational field are the vehicles — not technique. The Exceptional Coaching Practice document develops the coach's somatic and relational attunement skills. The Differentiation Document names Polyvagal-informed tools as a core differentiator; operational deployment is embedded in Stage 4b competency rather than named as a standalone track.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 4 (receptive posture has a physiological dimension — the nervous system must be sufficiently regulated to receive rather than defend) → Node 6 (the Spirit's witness is received in an open, regulated state)
**Primary tools:** Stage 4b Implicit Level tools; coach presence and regulated relational field
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Stage 4b Implicit Level Practitioner Reference]], [[Exceptional Coaching Practice — What Is Missed, Meaningful & Important]], [[Covenant Identity — Practitioner Competency Framework]]

### Somatic Encoding

*Identity-level conclusions held in the body — posture, chronic muscular tension, physiological response patterns, gut-level reactions — encoded in procedural and implicit memory; not accessible through cognitive intervention alone; the body enacts the false identity even when the mind disagrees*

**Layer:** Implicit
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 4b (primary); somatic observation embedded in Stage 1 as a diagnostic signal
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 1–2 (somatic observation as diagnostic data); Phase 3 (Stage 4b engagement)
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care

**How CIC engages:** The Affections Discovery in-session protocol (Thompson/Allender sourced) includes somatic observation as a diagnostic signal across all five layers. Stage 4b competency includes somatic tracking — noticing and naming body signals in session without immediately moving them back toward insight or resolution. The Self-Supervision Template prompts coach reflection on activation and somatic signals in both self and client as diagnostic data.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 2 (somatic signals surface what the implicit level is holding — diagnostic data, not distraction) → Node 4 (receptive posture has a somatic dimension — quieting the body's defensive response)
**Primary tools:** Affections Discovery (somatic observation layer); Stage 4b tools; Self-Supervision Template

## 7. Motivational Structure

---

### Competing Commitments / Immunity to Change

*Kegan & Lahey: the hidden motivational structure that systematically undermines stated commitments — an implicit commitment that directly competes with the stated goal, maintained to protect against a deeply held anxiety (the Big Assumption); produces the experience of sincere effort and repeated failure*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 2 (surfaces the hidden competing commitment as part of naming the false identity structure)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 2
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope

**How CIC engages:** ITC is a named and fully deployed tool in CIC. The four-column process makes the competing commitment explicit. CI routes Column 3/4 findings to deeper diagnostic work: Column 3 patterns → Affections Discovery; Column 4 (Big Assumption) → Character Wound Diagnostic, Lie Eliminator, or Secured in Father. ITC is particularly useful for surfacing what the false self is protecting and why displacement is costly — the psychological mechanism behind Node 3.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 2 (ITC surfaces the false identity structure with precision — names the specific competing commitment) → Node 3 (the competing commitment must be felt as costly, not just identified as present, before the vacancy opens)
**Primary tools:** ITC Protocol (full four-column process)
**Key files:** [[Sanctification — ITC Practitioner Reference]], [[Covenant Identity — Phase 2 Session Tools (Wound Mapping)]]

### Secondary Gain

*The hidden payoff the false identity is providing — the function it serves — that makes displacement costly and change threatening; the false self is not merely incorrect, it is working for the person in some way*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 2 (surfaces through ITC Column 4 and wound mapping)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 2
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope

**How CIC engages:** Secondary gain is the psychological term for what CI names as the protective function of false identity. The Practice Definition states it directly: "secondary gain from the false identity structure — the false self is serving a function." ITC Column 4 (Big Assumption) surfaces the specific function. Why the Heart Does Not Heal names idol attachment as the theological frame for the same phenomenon — the false covenant object provides something real, which is why release requires more than recognizing it is false.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 3 (secondary gain keeps the false covenant object viable; felt inadequacy requires the secondary gain to fail or its cost to be felt as greater than its payoff)
**Primary tools:** ITC Protocol (Column 4); Why the Heart Does Not Heal diagnostic
**Key files:** [[Sanctification — ITC Practitioner Reference]], [[Covenant Identity — Why the Heart Does Not Heal — Practitioner Diagnostic]], [[Covenant Identity Coaching — Practice Definition]]

### Avoidance Conditioning

*Behavioral and relational pattern of avoiding people, situations, or internal states that would expose or challenge the false self — reinforcing the false identity through systematic non-exposure to disconfirming experience*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 1–2 (behavioral markers in wound type diagnostic)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 1–2
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope

**How CIC engages:** The Character Wound Diagnostic maps avoidance as a wound-type–specific behavioral marker — Hermit avoids exposure; Warrior avoids vulnerability; False Noble avoids anything that disrupts the performance construct. ITC Column 1 captures avoidance as a stated positive commitment. Why the Heart Does Not Heal names avoidance of suffering as a specific barrier to heart healing. Formation disciplines between sessions create structured conditions for non-avoidant return to what was previously avoided.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 3 (avoidance keeps the false covenant object viable by preventing the experience of its failure — it is the mechanism that maintains the false structure against Node 3's work)
**Primary tools:** Character Wound Diagnostic; ITC Protocol; Why the Heart Does Not Heal
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool]], [[Sanctification — ITC Practitioner Reference]], [[Covenant Identity — Why the Heart Does Not Heal — Practitioner Diagnostic]]

## 8. Identity Architecture

---

### Identity Diffusion

*Erikson: absence of stable, coherent identity formation — the self is undefined, fragmented, or entirely contingent on external validation, with no settled internal reference point; the person cannot hold a stable sense of self under pressure or across contexts*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Pre-entry (screening, not a stage to work in)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 1 (entry evaluation only)
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care (mild, stable) → ○ Refer (severe, acute, or persistent)

**How CIC engages:** The spiritual baseline requirement at entry functions as the identity-diffusion screen — active faith, willingness to receive Scripture as authoritative, and sufficient stability for formation work. Severe identity diffusion signals wrong-fit for CIC; the formation arc requires enough structural stability to hold the work across multiple sessions. The Discovery Call Guide surfaces readiness and stability indicators. The Crisis & Referral Protocol governs the boundary.
**Primary tools:** Discovery Call Guide (entry screen); Crisis & Referral Protocol
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity — Discovery Call Guide]], [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]], [[Covenant Identity Coaching — Practice Definition]]

---

### False Self / True Self

*Winnicott: a compliance-based, constructed self built in response to environmental demands — concealing and protecting the authentic (true) self; the false self is relationally functional but existentially hollow; the true self, when found, has genuine desire and aliveness*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 1–4 (structural analog for the entire arc)
**Engagement Phase:** Throughout
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope

**How CIC engages:** Winnicott's false/true self distinction is the closest secular structural analog to CI's false identity/covenant identity framework — useful for orientation between psychological and theological language. The critical distinction: Winnicott's true self is discovered (found under the false self when it is removed); CI's covenant identity is received (given by God in Christ, not found by removing the false self — it must be spoken and received, not excavated). The entire CIC arc is the Christian formation version of this movement.
**Theory of Change Node:** Nodes 1–8 — the entire arc is the mechanism by which false self is displaced and covenant identity received
**Primary tools:** Full CIC arc
**Key files:** [[Covenant Identity Coaching — Practice Definition]], [[Covenant Identity Coaching — Full Model Reference]], [[Covenant Identity Coaching — Theory of Change]]

---

### Narcissistic Defenses

*Grandiosity, devaluation, and entitlement deployed as psychological defenses against an underlying shame-based core — the constructed superior self protects against exposure of the defectiveness it conceals*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 1–2 (False Noble wound type indicators) → Stage 3 (resistance pattern to anticipate)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 1–2
**Scope:** ◐ Coaching with care — Narcissistic Personality Disorder → ○ Refer

**How CIC engages:** The False Noble wound type (ambivalence, performance superiority, false competence) maps most closely to narcissistic defense structure. The Self-Deception barrier in Why the Heart Does Not Heal addresses the protective function of grandiosity — the person cannot see their false identity because they have constructed a self that does not need to. The coach must recognize the wound beneath the presentation and resist the False Noble's characteristic pull toward performance-focused sessions.

### Self-Verification

*Swann: the motivated tendency to seek feedback confirming the existing self-concept — even when that self-concept is negative; not passive drift but active behavioral maintenance of identity coherence; disconfirming truth is discounted, avoided, or reinterpreted to fit the existing structure rather than received as corrective*

**Layer:** Both
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 2 (false identity named with precision is the prerequisite) → Stage 3 (self-verification is the primary mechanism the coach must account for when covenant truth is introduced)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 2–3
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope

**How CIC engages:** Self-verification explains why Stage 2 must precede Stage 3 — a client whose self-concept is organized around unworthiness will filter incoming covenant truth to maintain psychological coherence rather than receive it as disconfirming evidence. This is not willpower failure; it is the motivated maintenance of identity coherence. Moving to covenant truth before the false identity is named with precision allows the self-verification mechanism to absorb the truth into the existing structure, producing the appearance of understanding without inner movement. The Felt Inadequacy mechanism (Node 3) addresses this directly: the false structure must be felt as failing — not merely identified as false — before the self-concept becomes sufficiently destabilized for disconfirming truth to land rather than be filtered.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 2 (precision in naming the false identity matters because the self-verification mechanism will absorb imprecise truth; the specific lie must be named, not the general category) → Node 3 (felt inadequacy of the false covenant object is what temporarily destabilizes the self-concept and opens it to revision) → Node 5 (the activation requirement at Stage 3 is partly a self-verification management mechanism — truth must enter an emotionally live, destabilized self-concept, not a defended one)

### Implicit God-Image Distortion

*Hall, Rizzuto: the experienced God (held implicitly, formed through relational history — especially with early attachment figures) diverging from the professed God (held cognitively through theology); the implicit image governs behavior, trust, and self-perception even when explicit theology is orthodox; no amount of correct doctrine corrects an implicit God-image distortion*

**Layer:** Implicit (by definition)
**Diagnostic Stage:** Stage 1 (surfacing: Affections Layers 3–4, God-Exclusion Map) → Stage 3 (addressed through covenant truth targeted at the implicit image)
**Engagement Phase:** Phase 1 (surfacing) → Phase 3 (addressed)
**Scope:** ● Coaching scope

**How CIC engages:** This is one of CIC's primary diagnostic tracks. Hall's implicit/explicit God-image distinction and Rizzuto's "living God vs. theologian's God" framework are the research basis. The God-Exclusion Map (Affections Layer 4) surfaces the specific domains from which God has been functionally excluded — where the implicit image predicts his absence, unreliability, or danger. The God-Representation Deepening Tool identifies which of six distortion types is operative and provides coaching approaches per type. The Functional vs. Confessional Theology document provides 20 diagnostic contrasts for practitioner reference. Covenant truth application at Stage 3 must target the implicit image specifically — addressing only the theological proposition leaves the implicit image running.
**Theory of Change Node:** Node 1 (disordered affections include distorted God-perception — the implicit God-image governs what the heart reaches for) → Node 2 (God-image distortion is a named false identity structure, not just a belief problem) → Node 5 (covenant truth must address the implicit God-image, not only the explicit theology, to meet the reconsolidation condition)
