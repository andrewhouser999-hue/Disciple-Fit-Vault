# Covenant Identity — Disordered Affections and the Diagnostic Entry — Mechanism

**What this is:** The mechanism explanation for Nodes 1 and 2 of the Theory of Change — why disordered affections produce false covenant objects and predictable false identity patterns, and why affections work is the correct diagnostic entry rather than behavior, cognition, or symptoms. This document explains *why* the Affections Discovery Process is structured the way it is and what it surfaces that other diagnostic approaches miss.

**When used:** Before deploying the Affections Discovery Process with a new client, to understand what the tool is doing and why. When affections work is producing unexpected results. When a practitioner asks why behavior change or cognitive correction alone is insufficient.

**Who uses it:** Practitioner only.

---

## The Core Claim

The presenting problem — behavior, relational pattern, emotional state, or spiritual symptom — is almost never the actual problem. It is the downstream expression of a more fundamental condition: the heart is organized around a false covenant object. Something other than God is being functionally trusted as the source of security, significance, worth, or belonging. The explicit mind may hold correct theology. The implicit level — where the real organizing is happening — runs an older, more operational architecture.

This is why the presenting problem rarely resolves when addressed directly. Behavior modification treats the symptom. Cognitive restructuring reaches the explicit belief system. Neither touches the organizing center.

---

## What Affections Are

Affections are not emotions. Emotions are affective states that rise and fall in response to circumstances. Affections are the deep motivational commitments that orient the person toward what they love, trust, fear, and reach for. They are the settled dispositions of desire — what the heart is organized around, not what the heart is feeling in a given moment.

The distinction matters diagnostically. A client can report feeling peaceful (emotion) while being affectively organized around approval (affection). The emotion is the surface; the affection is the architecture underneath.

Jonathan Edwards's *Religious Affections* grounds this: the affections are "the more vigorous and sensible exercises of the inclination and will of the soul." They are not identical to emotions because they include the will's orientation — what the person is moved toward and by, not just what they are feeling. The diagnostic entry at the affection level reaches the will's organized commitments, not the feeling state that varies with circumstance.

Augustine's *Confessions* names the same dynamic in the other direction: "our heart is restless until it rests in Thee." The heart is constitutively organized around a resting place — a center of gravity that everything else orbits. The diagnostic question is always: what is the actual resting place? What does the heart organize around when it is not under theological instruction but under pressure?

## How Disordered Affections Produce False Covenant Objects

A false covenant object is anything other than God that the heart trusts as the functional source of security, significance, worth, or belonging. The heart does not choose false covenant objects arbitrarily. It moves toward them along a predictable path:

**Step 1 — The wound disrupts the primary covenantal trust.** A betrayal, an abandonment, a shame event, or a prolonged experience of conditional reception damages the heart's ability to trust God as a reliable covenant partner for a specific need. The betrayal wound damages trust in guidance and protection. The abandonment wound damages trust in presence and belonging. The shame wound damages trust in acceptance and reception. The wound does not necessarily produce atheism — it produces practical mistrust: "God may be there for others, but not for this specific thing, for me."

**Step 2 — The heart adapts by trusting something else.** The need that God was no longer trusted to meet gets redirected. Competence, approval, control, performance, relational attachment, substance, or achievement becomes the functional provider. This is the biblical frame of Hosea 2 and Jeremiah 2:13 — the Baals (territorial gods who promise local provision) and the broken cisterns (self-constructed water systems). The heart turns to a substitute, not because the substitute is obviously adequate, but because God has been experienced as unavailable or unreliable for this specific need.

**Step 3 — The false covenant object produces the false identity.** The false identity is not random. It is coherent — the behavioral and relational system organized around serving and protecting the false covenant object. If competence is the false covenant object, the false identity is organized around producing, maintaining, and defending competence: self-sufficiency, productivity as worth, inability to tolerate failure. If approval is the false covenant object, the false identity is organized around reading and maintaining approval signals: people-pleasing, emotional management, pre-emptive rejection. The false identity makes complete sense given what the heart has organized around.

This is the predictability that Stage 1 Affections Discovery and Stage 1b Character Wound work can map: given the wound type and the false covenant object, the presenting false identity structure is largely predictable before it is explicitly confirmed.

## Why Affections Work Is the Correct Diagnostic Entry

### What other approaches miss

**Behavioral approaches** address what the client does. They can reduce the frequency or intensity of a behavioral pattern without reaching the affections structure that is producing it. The behavior changes; the false covenant object remains intact; new behaviors form to serve the same object. The client is better-functioning but not transformed at the organizing center.

**Cognitive approaches** address what the client believes — the propositions in explicit memory. They can modify the belief content without reaching the affections structure operating at the implicit level. The client can affirm "God loves me" (explicit belief) while functionally trusting approval as the arbiter of their worth (implicit affection). The explicit and implicit levels run divergent architectures, and the implicit level governs behavior under pressure. Curt Thompson's work on implicit and explicit memory is load-bearing here: the narrative self is formed through the integration of both systems, and they can diverge. Explicit change that does not reach the implicit level does not persist under threat.

**Symptom-focused approaches** address the presenting state — anxiety, depression, relational conflict, spiritual dryness. Symptom management is not wrong, but it does not answer the question "what is the heart trusting that is producing this?" The symptom is reliable diagnostic data; it is not the diagnosis itself.

### What affections work surfaces

The Affections Discovery Process (five-layer protocol) is designed to surface the actual organizational architecture of the heart — not just the explicit layer but enough of the implicit layer to name what is being functionally trusted:

**Layer 1-2 (Trust and Desire):** What does the client reach for? What do they protect? What do they fear losing? This begins to outline the affection structure — what is valued and what is trusted.

**Layer 3 (Displacement):** Where has God been crowded out — not theologically denied, but practically displaced? The client who knows God is the source of security but practically runs the security calculation through their performance record is showing a displacement at the affections level.

**Layer 4 (False Covenant Object):** The God-Exclusion Map names the specific object that has been functionally trusted in God's place. This is where the Hosea 2/Jeremiah 2:13 frame is internally operative — what is functioning as the Baal (the territorial god, the local provider) for this client's specific need?

**Layer 5 (Identity Revelation):** The affections picture becomes specific enough to name the false identity statement — the organizing self-understanding that has formed around the false covenant object.

This five-layer process reaches progressively deeper into the implicit organizing structure. The later layers are not additional facts the client reports — they are diagnostic inferences the practitioner makes from patterns that emerge across all five layers. The affections picture at Layer 5 is rarely what the client would have self-reported at the discovery call. It is what the systematic surfacing of the heart's actual organization produces.

## The Character Wound as the Explanation for Predictability

The specific false covenant object a client's heart has organized around is predictable given the character wound history. This is not determinism — it is pattern recognition from the wound's specific damage.

| Wound Type | What Was Damaged | Predictable False Covenant Object | Predictable False Identity |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Warrior (betrayal) | Trust in guidance, protection, reliable authority | Competence / Control / Independence | "I am safe when I do not need anyone; I prove my worth through what I produce" |
| Hermit (terror) | Trust in safety, belonging, proximity to others | Predictability / Isolation / Avoidance | "I am safe when I stay hidden; proximity is threat" |
| False Noble (shame/ambivalence) | Trust in acceptance, reception, being seen | Approval / Spiritual performance / Appearing acceptable | "I am acceptable when I perform well; being seen without armor is exposure" |

The character wound diagnostic is not an independent tool — it is the explanation for why the affections picture looks the way it does. The wound is the event or pattern that redirected the affections away from God as the trusted partner for a specific need. The false covenant object is what the heart trusted instead. The false identity is what organized around protecting that trust.

## The Implicit/Explicit Divergence — Why This Matters for Stage 3

The most clinically significant implication of this mechanism is the one that governs Stage 3 (Covenant Truth Received): explicit belief change and affective reorganization are not the same event, and the gap between them is precisely what "I know it in my head but not my heart" describes.

The client who has received correct theological information about God's love (explicit belief updated) while the affections are still organized around approval (implicit architecture unchanged) will behave as though approval is the truth under pressure — regardless of the theological belief. This is not hypocrisy or spiritual failure. It is the divergence between two memory systems: explicit memory holds the new proposition; implicit memory runs the old organizing architecture.

Affections work at Nodes 1 and 2 is the preparation for Stage 3 to work. The more specifically the false covenant object is named and felt — not just identified but genuinely seen as what the heart has been trusting — the more specifically the covenant truth can be addressed to the actual structure at Stage 3. And the more the implicit level has been engaged through affections work, the closer Stage 3 declaration is to the implicit encoding that needs to be displaced.

## The Biblical Frame

**Hosea 2:** Israel has gone after the Baals — territorial gods who promise local provision (rain, harvest, fertility). The Baals are not worshipped as alternative supreme beings; they are trusted as local providers for specific needs. This is the exact mechanism of the false covenant object: not theological rejection of God, but practical trust in a substitute provider for a specific need God was not believed to reliably meet.

**Jeremiah 2:13:** "For my people have committed two evils: they have forsaken me, the fountain of living waters, and hewed out cisterns for themselves, broken cisterns that can hold no water." The broken cisterns are self-constructed alternatives — they require effort, they have to be maintained, and they leak. The covenant object that requires the client to keep the affections performance going in order to maintain its provision is a broken cistern.

**Augustine:** "Thou madest us for Thyself, and our heart is restless, until it repose in Thee." The heart's restlessness is not pathology — it is the signal that the resting place has not been found. The false covenant objects produce a specific kind of restlessness: the exhaustion of having to maintain what should be received. When the false covenant object is named in Stage 2 and lament is inhabited in Stage 3, the exhaustion is acknowledged as the signal it has always been: the restlessness of a heart that has been trusting what it was never designed to trust.

## Failure Mode When This Mechanism Is Not Understood

**If the practitioner treats affections work as rapport-building or opening questions rather than as the primary diagnostic layer:** The session produces a pleasant relational encounter without a specific picture of what the heart is trusting. Stage 3 has no target. Covenant truth addressed to "general insecurity" has nothing specific to land on.

**If the practitioner moves to Stage 2 (false identity named) before the affections picture is specific:** The false identity is named at a category level ("I struggle with performance anxiety") rather than at the organizing center level ("I have been treating approval as the condition for my worth, and the Warrior wound organized my entire relational system around producing what would secure that approval"). A category-level naming does not create a specific enough hook for Stage 3 covenant truth to reach.

**If the client's affections work is primarily self-report:** The client describes what they think their affections structure is. Self-report at Layer 1-2 is reliable; self-report at Layer 4-5 is rarely accurate. The five-layer structure is designed so that the deeper layers emerge from the practitioner's diagnostic inference across the full picture, not from the client's self-description.
