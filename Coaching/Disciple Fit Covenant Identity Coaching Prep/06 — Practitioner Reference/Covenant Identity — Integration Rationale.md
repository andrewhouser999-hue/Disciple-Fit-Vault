# Covenant Identity — Integration Rationale
*Practitioner Reference — the theoretical basis for using psychological contributors alongside Scripture in this coaching model*

*Related: [[Covenant Identity — Whole-Person Identity Framework]]; [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]]; [[Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool]]*

---

## The Governing Position

This model is built on a three-discipline architecture: Spiritual Formation governs the telos (covenant renewal toward Christ-likeness), Biblical Counseling governs the diagnostic framework (how to read what has gone wrong), and Life Coaching governs the helping methodology (how the helping relationship is structured). This document addresses the more specific question that architecture raises: on what basis are psychological contributors incorporated, and under what constraints?

This coaching model does not treat psychology and theology as co-equal sources. Scripture governs — it determines what identity is, what has gone wrong, and what renewal looks like. Psychological contributors are tools: useful where they confirm what Scripture says about human nature or where they describe mechanisms Scripture assumes but does not explain at a developmental, neurological, or behavioral level.

This corresponds to what Eric L. Johnson calls *Christian psychology* — psychology developed from within Christian theological commitments, not secular psychology blended with Christian content. It is distinct from three other common approaches:

| Approach | Position |
|---|---|
| **Biblical counseling** | Scripture is sufficient; psychological observation can describe but not prescribe |
| **Classic integration** | Psychology and theology are blended as co-equal sources |
| **Levels of explanation** | Psychology and theology address the same phenomena at different levels; neither threatens the other |
| **Christian psychology** (this model's position) | Psychology is evaluated against a Christian anthropology; contributions used only where consistent with or illuminating of what Scripture says about human nature |

The practical result: psychological contributors in this model make biblical realities *more visible in the coaching room* — they do not revise, supplement, or replace the biblical account.

## The Integration Criteria

Every psychological contribution used in this model was evaluated against two criteria before inclusion:

**Criterion 1 — Confirmatory**
Psychology independently observes what Scripture states. The psychological observation confirms the biblical claim but adds no new content. This is the lowest-risk integration mode — the psychological finding is redundant to Scripture but may give the coach more specific vocabulary for what Scripture already describes.

**Criterion 2 — Additive**
Psychology describes a mechanism Scripture assumes but does not explain. Scripture addresses the phenomenon at the level of meaning, telos, or moral reality; psychology describes how it operates at the developmental, neurological, or behavioral level. This mode carries higher integration risk — it requires verifying that the mechanism description does not import a competing anthropology alongside the observation.

Contributions that neither confirm Scripture nor describe assumed mechanisms — but instead introduce competing anthropological frameworks or alternative definitions of human flourishing — are excluded, regardless of how clinically useful they may be.

## Why Each Contributor

### Ed Welch — *Blame It on the Brain*; *Shame Interrupted*

**Integration mode:** Confirmatory and additive — from within a covenantal framework that already governs the observation.

**What he contributes:**

- *Brain biology evaluated from within covenant categories* — Welch engages neuroscience findings from within biblical counseling categories rather than importing secular psychological frameworks and filtering afterward. Scripture governs the question; neuroscience describes how the brain behaves within what Scripture has already said about the heart, sin, and suffering. This is the correct governance direction — inside-out rather than outside-in.
- *Shame as signal, not root* — Scripture treats shame as a consequence of broken covenant relationship (Genesis 3; Psalm 34:5; Isaiah 61:7). Welch's *Shame Interrupted* addresses shame diagnostically without treating it as the organizing root of identity distortion — shame is a signal pointing toward what a person has loved and feared losing. This holds shame seriously while subordinating it to the covenant diagnostic underneath.
- *Fear, weakness, and suffering read through biblical categories* — *Blame It on the Brain* addresses how biological and neurological realities intersect with spiritual formation without reducing spiritual realities to biological mechanism. The contribution: how brain behavior participates in the patterns Scripture describes, without the brain becoming explanatorily primary.

**Boundary:** Welch operates within the biblical counseling tradition, which is more past-focused, sin-diagnostic, and directive in its helping posture than life coaching. His diagnostic observations are used; his model's helping relationship structure is not imported. The coaching scope — present/future, developmental, with a normally functioning client population — governs how his diagnostic work is drawn upon and how far it extends.

### Curt Thompson — *Anatomy of the Soul*; *The Soul of Shame*

**Integration mode:** Primarily additive — mechanism description only.

**What he contributes:**

- *Identity formed through relational attunement* — Scripture affirms humans are made for knowing and being known (John 17:3; 1 Corinthians 13:12); Thompson describes the developmental mechanism by which relational attunement shapes the self's architecture.
- *Implicit and explicit memory gap* — Scripture affirms the heart can be divided; what a person says and what operates in the heart can diverge (Jeremiah 17:9; Luke 6:45; Romans 7). Thompson describes the neurological mechanism — why explicit belief and functional behavior diverge, and why truth-telling alone is often insufficient to change the implicit layer.
- *Shame as a neurobiological attack on being* — Scripture treats shame as a hiding mechanism (Genesis 3) addressed through being known and restored (Isaiah 61; Romans 10:11). Thompson describes why shame resists cognitive correction — it operates below the level of conscious thought.
- *Being-known as the change mechanism* — Scripture presents being known by God and others as restorative (Psalm 139; John 4). Thompson describes the relational and neurological conditions under which implicit memory can shift.

**Boundary:** Thompson draws heavily on Dan Siegel's interpersonal neurobiology (IPNB). Siegel's observational findings — relational formation, memory systems, integration markers — are used. Siegel's framework telos — psychological integration as the definition of human flourishing — is not imported. The goal of coaching in this model is conformity to Christ (Romans 8:29), not neural integration.

Thompson also tends to treat shame as the presenting root of identity disorder. This model treats shame as a signal pointing toward the covenant object underneath — what the person has loved and feared losing. Mechanism is drawn from Thompson; the shame-as-root framing is not.

### Dan Allender — *To Be Told*; *Redeeming Heartache* (with Cathy Loerzel)

**Integration mode:** Primarily additive; some confirmatory.

**What he contributes:**

- *Story as the primary identity carrier* — Scripture establishes that the mouth speaks from the overflow of the heart (Luke 6:45); Allender describes the specific mechanism by which story structure reveals what the heart is organized around — what rises, what disappears, what repeats.
- *Three character wound types* (Warrior, Hermit, False Noble) — Scripture addresses the patterns these types describe — pride, hiding, performance — but does not give observational typology. Allender provides clinically observed categories that make these patterns diagnostically visible in session.
- *False self / true self axis* — Scripture establishes the old self / new self distinction (Ephesians 4:22-24; Colossians 3:9-10). Allender translates this into coaching-observable behavioral and narrative markers distinguishing which self is speaking in a given moment.
- *Emotions as theological language* — Scripture treats the emotional life as revealing the heart's orientation (Proverbs 4:23; the Psalms). Allender maps specific emotional patterns to specific identity belief structures, giving coaches diagnostic specificity.

**Boundary:** Allender's typology is wound-based. It describes how harm shapes the false self — which is true and important — but can underemphasize the moral agency dimension if used alone. The sin dimension (what the person has cultivated through repeated choice) must be held alongside the wound dimension in this model. Johnson's contribution (below) provides this corrective.

### Eric L. Johnson — *Foundations for Soul Care*

**Integration mode:** Foundational and architectural — governs how the other contributors are received.

**What he contributes:**

- *The theoretical justification for Christian psychology* — Johnson distinguishes Christian psychology from classic integration, providing the epistemological framework this model operates within.
- *Imago Dei as the foundational identity category* — before any psychological category, every person is an image-bearer. This is both the ontological ground of the client's dignity and the telos of the coaching work.
- *The soul's faculties as a structural identity model* — drawing on the Christian tradition (Augustine, Aquinas, the Puritans), Johnson identifies intellect, will, affections, imagination, memory, and body as interdependent aspects of the whole person — each capable of distortion, each participating in renewal. This is the Christian tradition doing its own psychology, not importing a secular framework.
- *Virtue and character as the middle-term in identity change* — identity distortions are not merely beliefs to be corrected; they are stable character dispositions formed through repeated practice. Identity change is slow because it is character change, not just cognitive change.
- *Holding wound and sin together* — Johnson insists that identity distortion has both a wound dimension (what was done to the person) and a sin dimension (what the person has cultivated). Coaching that addresses only one dimension is incomplete.

**Note:** Johnson's contribution is primarily architectural — he governs the framework within which Welch, Thompson, and Allender operate. His work determines the anthropological boundaries within which psychological contributions are received.

### Richard Schwartz — *Internal Family Systems Therapy* (1995); *No Bad Parts* (2021)

**Integration mode:** Additive — diagnostic language layer only. The full IFS clinical protocol is not imported.

**What he contributes:**

- *Parts language that maps onto the false identity structure* — IFS provides clinically observable language for why the false self resists change. The Manager (protective strategy) maintains the false identity's functional dependencies. The Exile (the wounded origin) carries the burden — the specific lie the false self is organized around. The Firefighter (reactive emergency response) explains presenting symptoms that may exceed coaching scope. This taxonomy enriches Stage 1 (Affections Discovery) diagnostically without requiring the coach to administer IFS therapy.
- *The burden as the lie at its emotional root* — Exiles carry *burdens* — acquired implicit beliefs formed through painful relational experience: *I am not enough; love is dangerous; I deserve to be alone.* The burden is not inherent to the part; it was acquired. This maps onto the lie/false identity structure with unusual precision and adds one dimension Lie Eliminator does not explicitly address: the wounded origin from which the lie emerged, and the protective strategy that developed around it.
- *Self as diagnostic language for covenant identity* — IFS distinguishes the Self (curious, calm, compassionate, courageous) from the parts that blend with and obscure it. With theological adaptation, this maps onto new self in Christ — the regenerate identity that formation progressively makes available as the false self yields.

**Boundary:** IFS is not imported as a clinical protocol. What is used is the conceptual map: parts language enriching Stage 1 diagnostic work, burden identification deepening Lie Eliminator entries, Self/parts distinction informing the covenant identity vs. false self frame. Coach-safe elements: curiosity posture, parts awareness language ("what part of you is responding right now?"), written parts dialogue as a between-session practice. Clinical threshold: significant exile activation with trauma history, inability to access Self, Firefighter activity as primary presenting issue — refer.

**Critical theological adaptation:** IFS assumes Self is inherently whole and autonomous — an unconditioned core. This is not compatible with a Christian anthropology. In this model, Self is the *new self in Christ* — Spirit-formed, not self-generated. When Self speaks to a part, it speaks from covenant identity. The source of Self's compassion and calm is the Spirit's work, not an inherent psychological faculty. Without this adaptation, IFS-informed practice teaches self-sufficiency at the exact point the client needs to be oriented toward Spirit-dependence.

### Eugene Gendlin — *Focusing* (1978); *Focusing-Oriented Psychotherapy* (1996)

**Integration mode:** Additive — mechanism access description only.

**What he contributes:**

- *The felt sense as the pre-verbal form of implicit belief* — Thompson describes the implicit memory gap: what the heart holds that explicit declarations cannot reach. Gendlin names and describes the specific form that implicit content takes in the body: the *felt sense* — a holistic, pre-verbal, meaning-carrying bodily sense of a situation before language can reach it. This is not a competing anthropology; it is phenomenological description of the level at which Thompson's implicit memory actually resides and how it presents in lived experience.
- *Focusing as the access method* — Scripture assumes the heart can be reached, not only argued with (Proverbs 4:23; Jeremiah 31:33 ESV). Gendlin provides a specific, learnable, non-clinical practice for attending to the pre-verbal level: the six-step Focusing process. The *felt shift* — the bodily release that occurs when the felt sense is accurately touched — maps directly onto the reconsolidation labile window: the implicit encoding has been destabilized and is briefly open to updating.
- *Client-teachable between-session practice* — Gendlin designed Focusing as a self-practice, explicitly non-clinical and accessible to anyone. It is the most portable implicit-level tool available for between-session coaching work — specifically for Stage 4b, where the presenting barrier is the gap between intellectual clarity and felt resonance.

**Boundary:** Gendlin's *Focusing-Oriented Psychotherapy* is a clinical approach. What is used in this model is the self-practice layer — the six-step Focusing process as described in *Focusing* (1978) — which Gendlin designed for general self-use, not clinical application. If Focusing practice surfaces trauma-level material (dissociation, escalating somatic activation), stop and refer. The Bio-Spiritual Focusing adaptation (McMahon & Campbell, 1991) provides the formation framing for client-facing deployment in this model.

## Theory of Change — The Reconsolidation Mechanism

**Integration mode:** Additive — mechanism description at the neurological level.

**The gap this fills:** The contributors above describe *what* has gone wrong in identity (false identity, implicit memory encoding, shame, wound) and *who* the person actually is (imago Dei, covenant heir, beloved). The model's tools — Declaration, Lie Eliminator, Covenant Truth installation, Stage 4b relational encounter — address these realities. What has been less explicit is the mechanism by which those tools produce structural change in the implicit belief. Why do they work when they work? And why do they fail when they fail?

The answer is memory reconsolidation (Nader & LeDoux, 2000; Ecker, Hulley & Hulley, 2012).

**The mechanism:** Long-term implicit memories — including the false identity beliefs this model targets — are encoded as structural changes at the synapse: receptor density, synaptic strength, dendritic morphology. These are not static records; they are maintained by ongoing protein synthesis. When a memory is reactivated, the encoding briefly destabilizes (the labile state), and protein synthesis is required to re-stabilize it. During this window, new information introduced into the labile encoding can be incorporated into the re-stabilized trace. The original encoding is not suppressed — it is replaced.

This is structurally distinct from extinction — the standard outcome of most formation and therapeutic approaches. Extinction produces a competing association that suppresses the original belief. Under stress or context shift, the original returns. Reconsolidation replaces the original encoding. There is no original to reinstate.

**The three conditions:**

1. **Reactivation** — the implicit belief must be emotionally live. The labile window does not open through cognitive acknowledgment of the belief.
2. **Prediction error** — the implicit belief must encounter something it did not predict: a disconfirmation that registers at the felt level, not a logical argument against it. The organism registers: *this is not what I was wired to expect.*
3. **Juxtaposition** — the disconfirming experience must be held simultaneously with the activated belief during the labile window.

**What this means for this model's tools:**

*When Declaration and Covenant Truth produce lasting change:* The lie is emotionally active (reactivated); Scripture or covenant reality arrives as a living disconfirmation (prediction error — this is not what the lie predicted); both are held simultaneously in the client's lived experience. The implicit encoding rewrites.

*When Declaration and Covenant Truth produce only temporary change:* The session stayed cognitive. The lie was named and acknowledged but not emotionally live. Counter-statements landed in explicit memory and suppressed the implicit belief temporarily — extinction, not reconsolidation. Under stress, the original encoding returned. This is not client failure; it is a practitioner cue that the implicit belief was not activated before Covenant Truth was introduced.

*What Stage 4b is at the neurological level:* The "moment of meeting" (BCPSG/Fosha) is a reconsolidation event. The relational disconfirmation — *I am not alone with this* — meets the activated implicit belief (*I am alone*) while that belief is live. The coach's regulated, accompanying presence is the juxtaposition. This is why Stage 4b cannot be replaced by tools: tools deliver propositions; reconsolidation requires lived prediction error.

**Theological correspondence:** Scripture describes the covenant promises as written not on tablets but on the heart (Jeremiah 31:33; 2 Corinthians 3:3 ESV). The mechanism described here is how that writing occurs at the neurological level — not through propositional delivery to the explicit mind, but through living encounter with truth that disconfirms the lie while the lie is active. The mechanism is consistent with, and illuminates, what Scripture assumes about how covenant truth transforms.

**Contributor:** Bruce Ecker (clinical translation — *Unlocking the Emotional Brain*, 2012); Nader, Schafe & LeDoux (neuroscience basis, 2000); Schiller et al. (human evidence, 2010). Integration mode: Additive — the neuroscience describes how mechanisms Scripture assumes but does not explain at the neurological level (the heart that resists change; truth that must be *written on* the heart, not merely heard) actually operate.

**Boundary:** Ecker's Coherence Therapy is a clinical protocol. What is used here is the mechanism description — reconsolidation conditions, lability, juxtaposition requirements — as practitioner theory explaining why existing tools work and when they fail. Practitioners do not need to administer Coherence Therapy; they need to ensure the implicit belief is activated before Covenant Truth is introduced, and that the truth arrives as lived disconfirmation, not propositional instruction.

## What Is Not Imported

Even where contributors' observations are used, their framework assumptions about human flourishing are not:

- **Siegel's telos** (via Thompson) — human flourishing defined as neural integration and psychological well-being. Not used. Scripture defines flourishing as conformity to Christ and life in right relation to God.
- **Thompson's shame-as-root** (via Thompson) — shame treated as the organizing category of identity disorder. Not used. Shame is a signal pointing toward the covenant object underneath; the model's diagnostic sequence moves through shame to what is being worshipped and feared.
- **Welch's counseling posture** (via Welch) — the biblical counseling helping relationship is more directive, more past-focused, and more sin-diagnostic than life coaching. Not imported. Welch's observations about shame, fear, and brain biology are used within coaching scope; the counseling model's helping structure is not.

## The Epistemological Basis

Why can psychology — produced by scholars who may not share Christian commitments — identify truths about human nature at all?

Because all human beings are made in the image of God. They are God's creation. Careful observation of how they function — however distorted the observer's anthropological framework — can surface real truths about how God made human beings to work. This is the common grace basis for taking psychological findings seriously without endorsing psychological frameworks wholesale.

Fallen scholars observing fallen people can observe real things. The task is not to reject those observations but to evaluate them against a Christian anthropology — to ask whether the observation describes something true about God's image-bearers, or whether it imports a competing account of what human beings are and what they are for.

The contributions in this model have been evaluated by that standard. Where they pass, they are used. Where their frameworks introduce competing anthropology, the observation is separated from the framework — and the framework is set aside.

This is not compromise. It is discernment.

## Anticipated Critiques

### Critique 1 — CCEF / Nouthetic: Scripture is sufficient; psychological integration introduces an alien anthropology

**Steelmanned form:** Scripture is sufficient for all matters of soul care (2 Peter 1:3). The sufficiency claim is not merely hermeneutical — it is pastoral. What people need for life and godliness is fully addressed in Scripture. Importing psychological observation does not simply add vocabulary; it invites a competing authority. Secular psychology was built on naturalistic assumptions — the self as self-determining, suffering as dysfunction rather than sanctification, flourishing as psychological well-being. Even where specific observations appear benign, framework assumptions travel alongside them and gradually reshape the questions asked, the answers expected, and what "better" looks like. The safer path is to use only what Scripture provides, supplemented only by what the Christian tradition has developed from within its own theological commitments.

**Response:** This critique is partially correct and has functioned as a legitimate corrective to integration approaches that do not evaluate psychological contributors against a controlling theological anthropology. The concern about framework assumptions traveling alongside observations is valid — it is precisely that concern that produces the integration criteria in this document (Criterion 2 in particular). Where this model agrees: psychological frameworks are never co-equal with Scripture; contributors whose framework assumptions compete with a Christian anthropology are not used; the helping relationship structure of any secular counseling model is not imported. Where this model diverges: the sufficiency of Scripture is properly a claim about Scripture's authority for determining what identity is, what has gone wrong, and what renewal looks like — not a claim that Scripture provides developmental descriptions of neurological mechanism or clinical observational typology of how early harm shapes the self. Scripture addresses phenomena at the level of meaning and telos; psychology can describe mechanism at the developmental, neurological, and behavioral level without revising the theological claim. The contributors in this model are not deployed as alternative authorities; they are tools evaluated against and constrained by the theological anthropology Scripture establishes.

**Remaining exposure:** The boundary between mechanism description and anthropological claim is not always clean. Psychological contributors regularly shade from observational description into evaluative claims about what is healthy, what constitutes growth, and what a person needs — and those evaluations may not align with a Christian anthropology. The integration criteria reduce that risk but do not eliminate it. Practitioners applying this model need sufficient theological formation to recognize when a contributor has crossed from describing mechanism to prescribing telos — and the capacity to stop at that boundary. A practitioner without that formation can apply these criteria nominally while framework assumptions travel undetected.

---

### Critique 2 — Common Grace Epistemology: The common grace warrant is too permissive

**Steelmanned form:** Common grace explains why fallen observers can identify *some* things correctly — it does not tell you *which* things are observed correctly and which are distorted by secular anthropological assumptions. Without a principled filtering criterion, the common grace argument functions as a permission slip that justifies importing whatever psychologists observe about human beings. The warrant is too generic; it does not do the epistemological work the argument claims.

**Response:** The critique is correct that common grace alone is insufficient as a filtering mechanism — and this model does not rely on common grace alone. Common grace explains why non-Christian scholars can observe real things about God's image-bearers; the integration criteria in this document (The Integration Criteria section) specify what makes a given observation usable within this model. Criterion 1 (confirmatory) requires that the observation confirm what Scripture already says. Criterion 2 (additive) requires both that the mechanism described is one Scripture assumes without explaining at a developmental or neurological level, and that the mechanism description does not import a competing anthropology. The "What Is Not Imported" section makes explicit what has been excluded from specific contributors even where their observations are used. Common grace is the ontological basis for why careful psychological observation can surface true things at all; the integration criteria are the epistemological filter that determines which observations are admitted.

**Remaining exposure:** Applying these criteria requires a practitioner who is both theologically and psychologically literate — capable of distinguishing confirmatory/additive mechanism from framework assumption in real-time session work. The criteria can be held nominally by a practitioner who lacks that formation; the model cannot be stronger than the practitioner's capacity to maintain the distinction in practice.

## Load-Bearing vs. Modifiable

**Load-bearing** — changing these changes what the model is:

- *The governing epistemological position* — Christian psychology (Johnson's framing), not classic integration, levels of explanation, or Scripture-alone. This position determines the direction of authority and the function of psychological contributors. If this changes, the entire contributor evaluation structure changes.
- *The two integration criteria* — Confirmatory and Additive. These are the filtering mechanism that prevents framework assumptions from traveling with observations. If weakened or replaced with a looser standard, the boundary between tool use and framework import dissolves.
- *The explicit exclusions* — Siegel's telos (neural integration as flourishing), Thompson's shame-as-root framing, the nouthetic helping relationship structure. These mark the points where the psychological contributors most commonly exceed the confirmatory/additive boundary.
- *Johnson's architectural role* — imago Dei as the foundational identity category governing all other contributors. If Johnson's framework is removed, the anthropological structure that holds the other contributors loses its governing logic.

**Modifiable** — can change without changing what the model is:

- *Specific contributor selection* — if new research produces a more precise mechanism description for any area Welch, Thompson, or Allender address — and that description meets the same integration criteria — the contributor can be updated. The criteria govern selection, not the contributors themselves.
- *The four-approach comparison table* — the governing position illustration (Biblical Counseling / Classic Integration / Levels of Explanation / Christian Psychology) is illustrative. It could be expanded or relabeled without changing the governing position.
- *The common grace formulation* — the argument's language could be refined in response to critique without changing its conclusion.
