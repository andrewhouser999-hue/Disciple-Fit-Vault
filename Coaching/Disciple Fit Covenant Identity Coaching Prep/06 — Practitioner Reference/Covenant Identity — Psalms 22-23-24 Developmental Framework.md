# Covenant Identity — Psalms 22-23-24 Developmental Framework

## What This Document Is

A practitioner reference showing how the Psalm 22-23-24 triptych maps onto the developmental arc of Covenant Identity Coaching — three positions a client can occupy, the transitions between them, and the coaching work appropriate to each. Used for session orientation and for understanding where a client is in the formation journey.

> ⚑ **Further development needed** — field-testing required before treating this as a stable protocol. Scriptural frameworks are exegetically grounded; coaching application has not yet been tested with live clients. See end of document.

---

## The Core Developmental Claim

These three psalms do not just describe three theological truths — they describe three positions a human soul can occupy, and a developmental arc that moves between them. The positions are real. The sequence is directional but not rigidly staged — a client may occupy different positions in different life domains simultaneously.

Used in Covenant Identity Coaching, the triptych functions as:
- A **diagnostic map** — where is the client actually living?
- A **formation sequence** — what is the movement from wound to covenant rest to telos?
- A **declaration architecture** — who does the client become as they move through?

**The practitioner holds this map. The client walks the path.**

---

## Psalm 22 — The Diagnostic Entry Point

### Where It Maps

This is the lament and wound layer — the presenting condition of most clients who arrive for Covenant Identity work. They may not use the language, but they are living in Psalm 22:

- *"My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?"* — abandonment, the silence of God, the sense that covenant has failed
- *"I am a worm, and no man"* — shame-based identity, the self reduced to nothing
- *"They shoot out the lip, they shake the head"* — the internalized voice of accusers
- *"All they that see me laugh me to scorn"* — exposure, humiliation, the wound that formed a false identity structure

### The Diagnostic Function

**First: it validates the lament without pathologizing it.** The client who feels forsaken is not faithless — David was here, and more importantly, Jesus was here. The cry "My God, my God" is addressed *to* God, not away from him. It is covenant language in the darkest moment. Their pain is already an act of covenant address, even when it doesn't feel like it.

**Second: it surfaces the affections underneath the wound.** What was the client trusting when the collapse came? What false covenant object — a relationship, a performance identity, an approval structure, a sense of control — functioned as "shepherd" and then failed them? The Psalm 22 cry almost always reveals a disordered affection that is now exposed. That is the Biblical Counseling diagnostic entry: not the presenting symptom but the displaced worship underneath it. *(Use Hosea 2 / Jeremiah 2:13 internally to name the false covenant object — see God-Exclusion Map, Layer 4.)*

**Third: it names the false identity structures that formed in the darkness.** "I am forsaken." "God does not hear me." "I am alone." "I am a worm." These are not just feelings — in the client's interior architecture, they have become operative identity statements. These are the structures Covenant Identity work targets.

### What Not to Do Here

Do not move a client out of Psalm 22 prematurely. The lament must be fully inhabited. The diagnostic work must be done. Clients who have been told to skip to Psalm 23 — to just trust the shepherd, just rest — have been robbed of the covenant legitimacy of their condition. Premature hope is not formation; it is spiritual bypassing.

## The Psalm 22→23 Transition — The Most Critical Movement

### What This Transition Is

This is not a transition from pain to no pain. It is a transition from the shepherd being absent to the shepherd being present in the same pain. The valley of the shadow of death still exists in Psalm 23. The enemies are still at the table. The difference is not the removal of the threat — it is the arrival of the shepherd within it.

For a client, this transition is the moment when:
- The false covenant object that failed them is named and grieved
- The cry of Psalm 22 is received as legitimate — not fixed, but heard
- The shepherd enters — not to explain the darkness but to accompany them through it

### The Coaching Work at This Transition

This is where the Affections → False Identity → Covenant Truth diagnostic sequence operates most directly:

1. **Affections work:** What was the client worshipping that failed? What disordered love drove them into the Psalm 22 condition? Name it without shame.
2. **False Identity work:** What did the Psalm 22 experience deposit in their interior architecture? What operating lie formed in the wound? These go into the Lie Eliminator frame.
3. **Initial Covenant Truth:** The first covenant truth a Psalm 22 client can receive is not yet "I shall not want." It is simpler: *"thou art with me."* Presence before provision.

## Psalm 23 — The Formation Zone

### Where It Maps

Psalm 23 is the ongoing formation zone — the primary territory of Covenant Identity work. The client is no longer in pure Psalm 22 desolation, but has not yet arrived at Psalm 24 maturity. They are in the valley — accompanied, but still walking. Most of the session investment lives here.

### The Seven Formation Realities as Developmental Markers

Each Psalm 23 provision is both a formation target and a diagnostic signal. A client who cannot receive a particular provision reveals the wound underneath it.

| Phrase | Formation Target | What Blocks It |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| He maketh me to lie down | **Rest** — capacity to stop striving | Performance identity; hypervigilance; unresolved fear |
| He leadeth me beside still waters | **Refreshment** — receiving renewal | Inability to receive; compulsive self-sufficiency |
| He restoreth my soul | **Healing** — reconstitution of the depleted self | Shame; belief that restoration is not available to me |
| He leadeth me in paths of righteousness | **Guidance** — trusting the shepherd's direction | Control; betrayal wound from authority figures |
| Thou art with me | **Presence** — secure attachment to God | Abandonment wound; experiential orphan spirit |
| Thou preparest a table before mine enemies | **Honor** — receiving dignity in exposed places | Shame; inability to be seen without collapsing |
| Goodness and mercy shall follow me | **Assurance** — living from covenant certainty | Anxiety; belief that God's favor is conditional |

**The diagnostic question for each:** *What in this client's interior architecture prevents them from receiving this particular reality?*

### Disciplines as the Formation Mechanism

Following Dallas Willard's VIM structure: Vision (Psalm 24) → Intention (Designed Alliance) → Means (disciplines).

Psalm 23 is the Means territory. Disciplines appropriate to each blocked provision:
- Cannot lie down → Sabbath, physical rest, silence
- Cannot be refreshed → Solitude, nature, Secured in Father
- Soul needs restoration → Lament, confession, Scripture Saturation
- Cannot trust guidance → Submission exercises, lectio divina
- Cannot receive presence → Ignatian imagination, prayer of examen
- Cannot receive honor → Community witness, accountability companion
- Cannot receive assurance → Reckoning Pattern (Romans 6:11), declaration practice

The disciplines are not the formation — the Spirit does the formation. The disciplines create conditions.

### The Valley Is Still There

One of the most important developmental observations: verse 4 says *"even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death."* The valley is not removed in the formation zone. A client who expects Psalm 23 to mean the elimination of threat is not yet receiving what the psalm actually offers. The formation is not from suffering to no suffering. It is from **suffering alone to suffering accompanied.** The developmental marker is not the absence of the valley — it is the presence of the shepherd in it, and the client's growing capacity to receive that presence as real.

## The Psalm 23→24 Transition — Telos Orientation

### What This Transition Is

The shift from formation in process to identity from the end. In Psalm 23, the client is learning to receive. In Psalm 24, the client is learning to declare. The movement is from receiving covenant truth to standing in it.

This transition does not mean Psalm 23 formation is complete. It means the client begins to orient toward the telos that Psalm 24 establishes, even while still in Psalm 23 territory. They learn to live **from the end** while still in the middle.

### The Coaching Work at This Transition

Declaration work becomes primary — the Lie Eliminator and Identity Installer operating at full capacity. The client is not just countering lies anymore; they are inhabiting the truth that the King of Glory has declared about who they are and what their life belongs to.

Key question at this transition: *"What does it mean for you, specifically, that the King of Glory is coming in?"* Not a generic eschatological question — a personal covenant identity question. The return of the King reframes the client's present suffering, their valley, their enemies, their formation work — all of it becomes temporary, purposeful, and held within a sovereignty that was never in doubt.

## Psalm 24 — The Telos and Declaration Layer

### Where It Maps

Psalm 24 is the identity anchor and telos destination. This is what the client is being formed toward — not an emotional state, not a theological position, but a settled covenant identity as someone who belongs to the King of Glory and lives in a world that is entirely his.

### Three Declaration Anchors

**"The earth is the LORD's, and the fulness thereof"** — That's the sovereignty frame.
For a client whose Psalm 22 condition included the sense that evil was winning, that their story was out of control — this declaration is the counter-identity. Nothing in their story, including the wound and the darkness, exists outside the ownership of YHWH. Their life is not a casualty of events beyond God's reach.

**"Who shall ascend into the hill of the LORD? He that hath clean hands and a pure heart"** — That's the qualification question answered in Christ.
Every client carries some version of the question: *Am I enough to stand before God?* Psalm 24 raises the standard and then the gospel answers it. They qualify not by their own condition but by the one who descended in Psalm 22 so that they could ascend in Psalm 24. Covenant identity declaration: *"I stand on the hill of the LORD because the King of Glory went outside the gate for me."*

**"The King of Glory shall come in"** — That's the orientation of the entire life.
The client's formation, their valley, their enemies, their incomplete sanctification — all of it exists in the space between the cross (Psalm 22) and the crown (Psalm 24). They are Psalm 23 people: living between what has been accomplished and what will be consummated. Formation is not self-improvement; it is preparation for a King who is coming.

## The "Enemies" Arc as a Developmental Marker

A client's relationship to what threatens them — shame, fear, accusation, relational wound, false identity — is one of the clearest indicators of where they are in the developmental arc.

| Stage | Psalm | The Client's Relationship to "Enemies" |
|-------|-------|----------------------------------------|
| **1** | Psalm 22 | Enemies define reality; they dominate and appear to win |
| **2** | Psalm 23 | Enemies are present, but the table is set in their sight — they are witnesses to provision, not controllers of it |
| **3** | Psalm 24 | Enemies are seen in true proportion — the King of Glory is sovereign; they have no ultimate claim |

**Diagnostic question:** *"When the threat shows up, what does it tell you about who you are?"*
- If the threat **defines** them: Psalm 22 position.
- If the threat is **present but not controlling**: Psalm 23 movement.
- If the threat is **seen in its true proportion**: Psalm 24 orientation beginning.

## Practical Session Architecture

### Phase 1 — Location (Psalm 22 Work)
*Where is the client actually living?*
- Lament validation and covenant framing
- Affections diagnostic: what false covenant object failed?
- False identity naming: what did the wound deposit?
- Initial covenant truth: *"thou art with me"* — presence before provision

### Phase 2 — Formation (Psalm 23 Work)
*Which specific realities of the shepherd is the client blocked from receiving — and why?*
- Targeted formation work on each blocked Psalm 23 provision
- Discipline assignment matched to the specific block
- Between-session practice: *"Which of the shepherd's actions do you need to receive this week?"*
- Valley reframe: formation is not from suffering to no suffering; it is from alone to accompanied

### Phase 3 — Declaration (Psalm 24 Work)
*Who is the client in Christ, and what does their life belong to?*
- Sovereignty declaration: their story is held by the King of Glory
- Qualification declaration: they stand on the hill in Christ, not in their own condition
- Telos orientation: they are Psalm 23 people living between Psalm 22 and Psalm 24
- Lie Eliminator and Identity Installer working at full capacity

## The Single Most Important Developmental Insight

The triptych teaches the coach and the client this: **you are not behind.**

A client in Psalm 22 darkness is not failing at covenant identity — they are in the same position as David, as Jesus. The formation is not broken. The developmental arc has a proven trajectory: through the cross, into the shepherd's care, toward the King of Glory's return. Their suffering is not an interruption of covenant formation. It is, when properly received, the beginning of it.
