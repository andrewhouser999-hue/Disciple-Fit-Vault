# Covenant Identity — Practitioner Competency Framework
*Practitioner Reference — what the coach must be able to do*

This document defines the competency domains required for Covenant Identity Coaching practice. It distinguishes between skills that can be acquired through training and capacities that must be formed through personal work. Both categories are requirements.

**Scope:** All competencies here operate within coach-safe practice. Where a competency approaches the clinical ceiling, this is noted. See [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]] for referral triggers.

---

## Competency Domains

---

### Domain 1 — Diagnostic Reading

**Explicit layer:**
- Conduct Affections Discovery across all five layers; recognize stay/move signals per the In-Session Protocol
- Map functional exclusion of God across the eight domains of the God-Exclusion Map
- Identify presenting character wound type (Warrior, Hermit, False Noble) from affections and behavioral pattern — not from premature label-application
- Name false identity specifically and personally, not generically
- Read the client's stage position (1–4a/4b) accurately and distinguish between them

**Implicit layer:**
- Read affect that does not match verbal content — the laugh that doesn't belong, the quick pivot, conspicuous absence of emotion
- Observe somatic signals in the client — posture shifts, voice changes, jaw tension, withdrawal, expansion — as diagnostic data
- Notice avoidance patterns: what is circled but never entered, what themes recur across sessions in new costumes
- Recognize spiritual bypassing: theological language used to manage and avoid rather than to genuinely engage

**Competency ceiling:** Distinguishing coaching-level implicit barrier (Stage 4b) from clinical trauma presentation requiring referral. When in doubt, err toward referral.

---

### Domain 2 — In-Session Explicit Engagement

Competencies in this domain are learnable through training, study, and supervised practice.

- Deploy Five Intervention Modalities with diagnostic precision — matched to what the session reveals, not rotated by default
- Execute Stage 4a CBT tools within coaching scope: Socratic questioning, laddering/downward arrow, thought record, behavioral experiment
- Apply God-Representation Deepening Tool following Phase 1 diagnostic work
- Facilitate lament-to-trust movement in session — not rushing the client from complaint to resolution
- Assign and debrief Deep Work Worksheets; read the client's response to declarations as diagnostic data, not a report card
- Hold and deploy covenant truth as the larger reality the false identity is being measured against — not as correction, lecture, or reassurance

---

### Domain 3 — In-Session Implicit Engagement

Competencies in this domain require formed capacity, not only technique. They develop through personal work, supervision, and sustained practice. They cannot be performed; they must be inhabited.

**Presence and attunement:**
- Arrive prepared and present — not cold. Pre-session self-management: set down own material before the session begins
- Sustain Level 3 attention: words, underlying affect, energy, silence, and what isn't being said, simultaneously
- Hold non-anxious presence when difficult, shameful, or dark material surfaces — not flinching, not rushing the client to resolution

**Somatic engagement:**
- Use own body as a data source in session — notice countertransference signals (pull to comfort, urge to redirect, unexpected contraction) and read them as information about what is happening in the room
- Read client body signals without projecting: observe first, ask second
- Hold silence deliberately: distinguish processing silence (protect it) from avoidance silence (gently enter it)

**Stage 4b — Relational and Attentive Response:**
When covenant truth is intellectually received but produces no felt movement, the coach does not add content — the coach changes mode:
- Slow the session; resist the impulse to restate more clearly or try a different framing — more content will not reach an implicit barrier
- Invite internal attention: *"What do you notice happening in you right now — not what you think, what you actually notice"*
- Speak covenant truth personally, not propositionally: address the person, not the distortion
- Hold regulated, attuned presence while the client sits in the stuck place — this is itself a formation input
- Return to the same covenant truth across multiple sessions; implicit memory shifts through accumulated relational experience, not single-session breakthrough

**Imagination and metaphor:**
- Deploy metaphor and imagination to reach material direct analysis cannot access
- Facilitate Ignatian imagination: entering a Gospel scene and encountering Christ as themselves, in their specific situation
- Use future-self and wisest-self framings to access perspective the client cannot reach analytically

**Relational field:**
- Name what is happening between coach and client in real time when the dynamic in the room is itself diagnostic material
- Deploy calibrated challenge: grounded in what the client has named they want, naming the specific pattern not the person's character, followed by curiosity not answers, delivered from genuine care

**Healing prayer (advanced — occasional, explicit criteria):**
- Deploy in-session healing prayer only when Stage 4b conditions are met: covenant truth cognitively received across multiple sessions with no felt movement; affect present but arrested; explicit tools not reaching the implicit level; client has expressed desire for prayer without coach prompting
- Hold the relational field as the client addresses God directly — brief, specific, targeted: naming the lie in God's presence and receiving the specific covenant truth as response; the coach does not intercede at length
- *Advanced skill requiring practitioner formation in Spirit Partnership before deployment. See Leanne Payne in [[Covenant Identity — Author & Resource Reference]] (Tier 2). Competency develops through the coach's own practiced work in healing prayer — not through training alone.*

### Domain 4 — Between-Session Tool Assignment

- Know the full between-session tool inventory and appropriate deployment conditions for each (see [[Covenant Identity — Tool Library]])
- Consult [[Secured in Father — Practitioner Design Notes]] for contraindications before assigning *Secured in Father* — particularly for clients with dissociative features or active trauma dysregulation
- Assign session commitments as specific implementation intentions (when, what, exactly) — not aspirational goals
- Debrief between-session tool use in the following session; the client's response is diagnostic data
- Recognize when a between-session tool is not holding and adjust: add schema-level work, shift from declaration to lament, increase relational contact in session before re-deploying solo practice

---

### Domain 5 — Scope Boundary and Referral

- Know the coaching ceiling: when implicit barriers indicate unprocessed trauma requiring clinical work, not more coaching
- Recognize referral triggers per [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]]: somatic dysregulation, dissociative features, active clinical presentation, patterns the client cannot engage with coaching-level tools
- Resist extending scope because progress has been made — the ceiling is the ceiling regardless of relationship depth
- Communicate referral clearly and as a continuation of care, not as failure or rejection

---

### Domain 6 — Coach Formation

These are not optional enrichments. They are professional requirements. A coach can only take a client as deep as they themselves have been willing to go.

- **Ongoing supervision** — formal (experienced coach reviewing work) or peer (trusted colleague who will tell the truth). Standard practice, not crisis response.
- **Personal formation work** — own therapy, spiritual direction, or peer coaching as ongoing discipline
- **Pre-session ritual** — consistent self-management practice before every session: what am I carrying today that is mine and not this client's?
- **Post-session debrief** — regular written or supervised review: what did I miss? Where did my own material enter the room? What challenge did I swallow?
- **Theology of suffering** — a lived, not merely studied, posture toward suffering and lament. Cannot be taught; must be grown. The client in suffering will feel immediately whether the coach is comfortable in it or anxious to move them out of it.
- **Own covenant identity inhabitation** — actively receiving and living from the covenant identity, not only teaching it. A practitioner coaching toward something they are not themselves inhabiting — even partially, even in process — will eventually either perform or project.

---

## How Competency Domains Interact

The diagnostic and explicit competencies are necessary but not sufficient. A practitioner who executes Stages 1–4 correctly but cannot hold non-anxious presence will find clients shortening, hedging, and managing their disclosure rather than bringing the real thing. The implicit competencies are not advanced refinements layered on top of the explicit ones — they are the container within which the explicit work becomes possible.

Formation precedes and sustains technique.
