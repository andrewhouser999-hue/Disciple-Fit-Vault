# Covenant Identity — Pre-Practice Readiness Action Plan
*Practitioner Reference — what to do before the first CIC client, and in what order*

**Status as of 2026-07-10:** No CIC client currently lined up. This plan governs the interval between now and first client contact — it is not the ongoing practice-development plan (see [[Covenant Identity — Practitioner Competency Framework]] and [[Covenant Identity — Self-Supervision Template]] for that).

**Governing finding:** Deliberate-practice research (Ericsson, Krampe & Tesch-Römer, 1993) and coaching-specific outcome research (de Haan, Duckworth, Birch & Jones, 2013) both point the same direction — structured practice with external feedback predicts skill development and client outcome more reliably than accumulated theoretical study alone. This plan is sequenced to get external feedback in place early and running in parallel with everything else, not gated behind "readiness."

## Priority Order

### 1. Scope-boundary supervisor — start immediately, non-negotiable
**What:** A licensed clinical counselor/therapist or ACBC/CCEF-trained biblical counselor, grace-forward in orientation (not Reformed-legalistic — see the Tripp caution in the contributor reference), willing to review actual case material rather than self-report, on a recurring cadence.
**Why first:** Closes the specific open gap named in the 2026-07-04 Crisis & Referral Protocol audit — "no verified supervision relationship." This is the one item on this list with a safety dimension, not just a skill dimension, and it has the longest lead time (vetting, scheduling, agreement) of anything here.
**How:** Source candidates through church counseling networks, ACBC's referral directory, CCEF alumni contacts, or personal relationships. Once a candidate is identified, confirm with your liability insurer (e.g., CPH & Associates) whether a documented supervision agreement affects coverage or expectations.

### 2. Coaching-craft supervisor — start immediately, in parallel with #1
**What:** An ICF-credentialed coach or MI-trained practitioner who can evaluate Facilitative Interpersonal Skills (warmth, empathic accuracy, persuasiveness, hopefulness, verbal fluency, problem-focus) and review recordings against a rubric — not just talk through sessions verbally.
**Why now, not after "internalizing the structure":** Skill drilled without a corrector risks entrenching errors rather than improving on them. Get this relationship in place before racking up solo repetitions, not after.
**How:** See the outreach draft below.

### 3. Skills practice (FIS/alliance drill deck) — begins once #2 is in place, runs continuously after
**What:** Structured, isolated-skill rehearsal (not full mock sessions) using scripted client statements, reviewed by the craft supervisor on a fixed cadence.
**Why:** This is the actual mechanism the research points to — not solo rehearsal. Directly implements the drill-deck component already scoped in the CIC Alliance & FIS Development project.
**How:** Resume the drill-deck build (paused pending review of build approach), then run it against supervisor feedback biweekly to start.

### 4. Networking — start immediately, in parallel with everything above
**What:** Two distinct tracks, not one activity:
- **Peer network** — other Christian coaches/counselors, for referral partnerships and professional community.
- **Target-market network** — actual prospective-client sources: churches, ministry leaders, referral partners who see your target client directly.
**Why now:** Pipeline relationships take months to mature. Sequencing this last, after "readiness" is achieved, adds dead time for no benefit — networking doesn't require CIC mastery to begin.

## What Not To Do

- **Don't substitute a certificate program for the supervision relationship.** CCEF/Allender/MI certificate tracks teach someone else's framework; they don't give feedback on your diagnostic judgment inside your own framework. If the goal is calibration, the supervision relationship is the mechanism — a certificate is not a proxy for it.
- **Don't let "internalize the structure" run without a stop condition.** It's the same move as reaching for one more credential — feels productive, doesn't produce the feedback loop that predicts outcome, and can run indefinitely if you set both the task and the finish line yourself.
- **Don't drill coaching skills solo for an extended period before the craft supervisor is engaged.** Unsupervised repetition can entrench uncorrected habits rather than build good ones.
- **Don't sequence networking last.** It has the longest lead time of anything on this list without a safety component — start it now, not after "readiness."
- **Don't let peer-coach networking stand in for target-market networking.** Coach-to-coach relationships are valuable but don't fill a calendar; make sure real prospective-client/referral-source contact is also happening.
- **Don't accept a supervisor, in either role, who will only take your self-report.** Self-rated alliance correlates poorly with client-rated alliance (de Haan et al., 2013) — the same failure mode applies to self-reported case summaries. Insist on recordings or transcripts.
