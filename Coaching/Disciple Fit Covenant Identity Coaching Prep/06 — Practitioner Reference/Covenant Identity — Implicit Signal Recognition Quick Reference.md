# Covenant Identity — Implicit Signal Recognition Quick Reference

**What this is:** A condensed, channel-organized companion to the full Stage 4b Implicit Level Practitioner Reference. That document teaches the mechanism and the skill taxonomy; this document exists only to make the *observable signals* fast to scan for reflection.

**How to use it — read the guard below first:** Per the source document's own Failure Mode Guards (Section IX), this is for use *after* a session, in reflective review or peer consultation — not as a during-session checklist. A coach reading down a list mid-session to classify the client has already broken the presence the classification depends on. Use this to sharpen what you noticed in hindsight, so recognition gets faster and quieter over time — not to perform tracking in the room.

**What this does not cover:** Category 2 (Being) and Category 3 (Responding) skills from the source document — presence, regulation, restraint — are formed capacities, not recognizable signals, and are deliberately excluded here. This document only condenses Category 1 (Tracking): what to notice, not what to do about it.

---

## The Single Clearest Marker

Before scanning any channel below, check for this first — it is the source document's primary entry signal and will resolve most cases on its own:

> The client articulates covenant truth clearly, may even say it sounds right — but reports no change in felt experience. **"I know it in my head but not my heart."**

If this split is present with no counter-argument, you are almost certainly looking at a **Stage 4b** entry, not Stage 4a. If instead the client is actively arguing, reframing, or minimizing the truth rather than failing to feel it, that is **Stage 4a** — a cognitive distortion, not an implicit block.

---

## Channel 1 — Verbal / Language

| Signal | What it indicates |
|---|---|
| "I know this but..." that does not resolve with more information or more truth | Stage 4b — the barrier isn't missing information |
| Arguments, reframes, or minimizing language directed at the covenant truth | Stage 4a — active cognitive resistance |
| Language organized around an anticipated relational outcome (being seen, left, judged, used) | Fork → Route A (relational) |
| Interior, undifferentiated language — "it's just there," "I don't know what it is" | Fork → Route B (body/felt-sense) |

## Channel 2 — Paraverbal (how it's said)

| Signal | What it indicates |
|---|---|
| Shifts in tone, rhythm, pace, or volume — especially the *change itself*, not the baseline | Primary tracking signal (Schore: nonverbal channels carry more relational information than verbal content) |
| A subtle pull-away, distancing, or shutdown in response to covenant truth — not argument | Stage 4b entry signal |

## Channel 3 — Nonverbal / Body

| Signal | What it indicates |
|---|---|
| Face, posture, breath, gesture — read together with paraverbal shifts, not alone | Category 1 tracking channel |
| Physical stilling | Transformation marker (State 4 approaching) |
| Tears that shift quality — from managed to authentic | Transformation marker (moving through State 2 into State 3) |
| Facial softening or vulnerability; breath shifts; somatic activation | Core affective experience (State 2) |
| Controlled presentation, flat or absent affect, rapid topic shifts, intellectualizing | Defensive processing (State 1) — not yet at the implicit material |

## Channel 4 — The Coach's Own Body (data, not noise)

| Signal | What it indicates |
|---|---|
| Your own somatic state shifting in the room — tension, unease, pulled-toward-fixing, flatness | Countertransference as neurobiological signal (Schore) — read it as data about what the client is transmitting, not as something to suppress |
| A felt urge in yourself to fill silence, reassure, or rescue | Often marks the exact moment implicit material has surfaced — the urge itself is diagnostic |

## Channel 5 — Timing / Process

| Signal | What it indicates |
|---|---|
| The normal coaching frame breaks — something unexpected and emotionally charged is live, and neither party knows what happens next | A now moment — cannot be scripted, only recognized |
| Client repeatedly returns to the same stuck place despite intellectual clarity and genuine desire to move | Stage 4b entry signal |
| The stuck place visibly shifts, tightens, or guards depending on what you do | Fork → Route A (tracks with relational moves) |
| The block doesn't loosen or tighten based on relational offering | Fork → Route B (doesn't track with relational moves) |

## Channel 6 — Enactment (relational, in the room right now)

| Signal | What it indicates |
|---|---|
| You notice you've been recruited into moving away, rescuing, or staying safely unavailable | An enactment — the client's implicit relational pattern is being enacted *with you*, not just described |
| Connection with you feels stable while something else is clearly live | Points away from enactment, toward Route B |

---

## Decision Flow

1. **Is the Single Clearest Marker present** ("knows it, doesn't feel it," no counter-argument)? → If yes, proceed as Stage 4b. If the client is instead arguing or reframing, this is Stage 4a — stop here, apply CBT-informed tools per the Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic.
2. **In Stage 4b, is there an enactment** — is the pattern happening with you, right now? → **Route A.**
3. **If the client's connection to you feels stable but something else is clearly live and interior** → **Route B.**
4. **If signals are mixed or unclear** → default to Route A (source document: missing a live enactment is the costlier error).
