# Covenant Identity — Self-Supervision Template

*Post-session coach reflection. Distinct from [[Covenant Identity — Session Growth Notes Template]], which tracks the client. This document tracks the coach — what entered the room from your side, what you avoided, what the work is doing in your own formation.*

*Use within 24 hours of session. 10–15 minutes. Do not perform this review — let it be honest.*

---

## Governing Posture

> *"A coach can only take a client as deep as they themselves have been willing to go."*
> — [[Covenant Identity — Practitioner Competency Framework]], Domain 6

Countertransference is not contamination to suppress. It is neurobiological signal — data about what the client is transmitting that the coach's body received before the coach's mind processed it (Schore). The goal of this review is not to eliminate the coach's reactions but to read them accurately and keep them from running the session without being named.

Formation precedes and sustains technique. This document is not a performance review. It is a formation instrument.

## 5. Wound Pattern Intersection

*Your own wound type shows up in your coaching. Not as a failure — as a signal.*

**Your wound pattern calibration:**

| Type | Avoidance signature | Countertransference signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Warrior** | Pushes through client resistance rather than sitting with it; urge to solve what needs to be held; impatience with client's grief or stuckness | Frustration, restlessness, tightening in chest or jaw; pull to confront when client needs to be received |
| **Hermit** | Accommodates client's avoidance rather than naming it; under-confronts; excessive caution about going to the wound | Anxiety, hypervigilance, pull to protect client from what they actually need to face; feels like care, functions as avoidance |
| **False Noble** | Executes technique correctly while being emotionally external; performs presence rather than inhabiting it; over-relies on structure | Detachment presenting as competence; smooth sessions with low felt-sense engagement; reviews feel clear and thin |
