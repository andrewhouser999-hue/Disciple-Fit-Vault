# Covenant Identity — From Vague to Specific — Condition 1 Practitioner Technique
*Practitioner Reference — how a coach actually produces the specificity Condition 1 requires*

**What this is:** A compressed, practitioner-facing walkthrough of how specificity is actually produced in session — not by asking the client to "be more specific," but by a five-move sequence that excavates the specific claim, its trigger context, and the false covenant object it serves.

**When used:** Whenever Condition 1 of Stage 3 formation ([[Covenant Identity — What Moves Covenant Truth from Registration to Reception]]) is not yet met — the practitioner has a general sense of the pattern ("insecurity," "control," "perfectionism") but not yet the specific claim, its triggers, and what it is protecting. This is Stage 1 (Affections Discovery) work.

**Who uses it:** Practitioner only.

## Why "Be More Specific" Doesn't Work

Direct belief questions — *"What do you believe about yourself?"* — produce analyzed, distanced answers. The client reports on the pattern from outside it rather than naming it from felt recognition. Condition 1 is not met by asking for specificity; it is met by a sequence of increasingly narrow behavioral and dependency questions that arrive at specificity as a byproduct.

**Vague:** *"I struggle with insecurity."*
**Specific:** *"When I fail publicly, I believe I am fundamentally disqualified — and that belief is working to protect my need for approval, which I have been treating as the condition for my worth."*

The second statement did not come from asking better questions about insecurity. It came from five distinct moves.

---

## The Five Moves

### 1. Start with behavior, not belief

Layer 1 asks what the client *says* they want (*"What matters most to you right now?"*). Layer 2 asks what their actual time, energy, and anxiety reveal (*"If I watched how you spent your time this past week — not what you intended, what actually happened — what would I conclude you care most about?"*). The gap between the two answers is where the real structure lives, and because the question is descriptive rather than evaluative, it surfaces without triggering defensiveness.

### 2. Ladder the dependency down to the core assumption

Layer 3 asks what the client is trusting for security, significance, or identity (*"When you're anxious, who or what do you reach for first?"*), then follows any answer downward with one repeated question: *"And if that were true, what would that mean about you?"* Each pass strips a layer of abstraction:

*"I need to succeed"* → *and if you didn't?* → *"I'd have let everyone down"* → *and if that were true?* → *"I am fundamentally disqualified."*

This is the Kegan-Lahey laddering/downward-arrow technique. It is how the specific claim is excavated rather than asked for directly.

### 3. Locate the God-exclusion in parallel

Layer 4 asks where God is functionally absent from what the client is managing on their own (*"Is there any part of this you find yourself handling without much thought of God?"*). This is what turns *"I need approval"* into *"...which I have been treating as the condition for my worth"* — it names what the felt need has actually been substituted for.

### 4. Offer the naming tentatively — let the client arrive first

Layer 5's move is reflection, not diagnosis: *"What I'm noticing is that a lot of what you're describing circles back to needing [X] to feel okay. Does that land?"* A client naming their own pattern lands differently than receiving it from the coach. The coach's job is to narrow the field until the client can say it — not to hand them the finished sentence.

### 5. Know when to stop — three conditions, not a feeling

Move to naming only when all three are present:
- The trust object is specific — the practitioner can name what the client is depending on for security, significance, or identity
- The God-exclusion is locatable
- The false identity statement is forming

Condition 1's own completion test (in [[Covenant Identity — What Moves Covenant Truth from Registration to Reception]]) is the same check from the other side: can the practitioner answer *what is the lie, what does it serve, what triggers it* — all three, with specificity, not just one.

## Additional Examples Across Common Presenting Issues

The insecurity example above is one instance of a repeatable pattern. The same five-move sequence produces the same quality of specificity regardless of which affections domain the client's dependency is organized around. These are worked examples across the presenting complaints most common in session — useful for pattern-matching quickly when a new client's vague complaint doesn't immediately suggest which domain is live.

| Presenting Complaint (Vague) | Specific Claim | False Covenant Object |
|---|---|---|
| *"I struggle with insecurity."* | *"When I fail publicly, I believe I am fundamentally disqualified — and that belief is protecting my need for approval, which I have been treating as the condition for my worth."* | Approval / Significance |
| *"I have trust issues."* | *"When I'm not the one managing the details, I believe things will fall apart — and that belief is protecting my need for control, which I have been treating as the only thing standing between me and chaos."* | Control |
| *"I'm a perfectionist."* | *"When my work isn't excellent, I believe I've proven I don't measure up — and that belief is protecting my need for significance, which I have been treating as something only flawless performance can secure."* | Achievement / Significance |
| *"I care too much what people think."* | *"When someone is disappointed in me, I believe I've failed at the one thing that makes me acceptable — and that belief is protecting my need for belonging, which I have been treating as conditional on managing everyone's experience of me."* | Approval / Belonging |
| *"I get anxious in relationships."* | *"When someone I love pulls away even slightly, I believe I'm about to be left again — and that belief is protecting my need for security, which I have been treating as dependent on being needed enough that no one leaves."* | Security |

**Reading the table diagnostically:** Each specific claim follows the same internal shape — *trigger context* → *the fundamental belief the trigger activates* → *what the belief is protecting* → *the false covenant object standing in for God*. A practitioner who can fill in that four-part shape for a client's presenting complaint has reached Condition 1, whatever the presenting language happened to be.
