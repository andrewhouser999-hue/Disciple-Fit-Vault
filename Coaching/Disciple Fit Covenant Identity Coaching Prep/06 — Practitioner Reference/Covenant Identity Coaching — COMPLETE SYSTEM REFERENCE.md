# Covenant Identity Coaching — **COMPLETE SYSTEM REFERENCE**

## What This Document Is

A single practitioner reference showing the complete diagnostic and treatment architecture of Covenant Identity Coaching — the sequence, the tools that populate each stage, the biblical frameworks that ground them, and the parallel streams that run across all phases. Every other document in the system elaborates one component; this document shows how all components connect.

---

## Governing Architecture

**Three disciplines in ordered relationship:**
- **Spiritual Formation** — telos and transformation mechanism; the Spirit is the primary agent
- **Biblical Counseling** — diagnostic precision; surfaces affections, wounds, and false identity
- **Life Coaching** — relational methodology; creates the formation context and structures forward movement
- **CBT** — subordinate descriptor only; applied at Stage 4a when cognitive distortion is the primary block

**Structural spine — indicative before imperative:**
Covenant grammar is the organizing logic of the entire model. The indicative (what God has declared to be true — identity given) always precedes the imperative (the call to live from it). The client's deepest problem is almost never behavioral — it is identity: a failure to inhabit the covenant reality already established by grace. Legalism reverses this grammar (perform → then receive identity); Covenant Identity Coaching restores it.

**Transformation mechanism:**
Spirit as primary agent → coaching relationship creates formation context → diagnostic work surfaces false identity → covenant truth received and applied → between-session disciplines sustain conditions for the Spirit's work

**Practitioner's background orientation (invisible to client):**
Psalm 22-23-24 triptych runs continuously beneath all session work. Psalm 22 = where the client is living (lament, desolation, wound). Psalm 23 = the formation zone (shepherd present, valley still real). Psalm 24 = the telos orientation (the King of Glory returning; the client's story held by sovereign love). This locates the client in the redemptive-historical arc and tells the practitioner what kind of work is appropriate. *(Practitioner reference document: to be built — see queue.)*

| **Stage 3**  | Covenant truth received                         | Identity Installer; Reckoning Pattern (Rom 6:11)                                                              | Matched by wound — see Declaration Architecture below                                                                                             |
| **Stage 4a** | CBT-informed (if cognitive distortion)          | Big Assumption experiment; schema interruption; Socratic questioning; thought record                          | Colossians 3:1-2; Romans 12:2                                                                                                                     |
| **Stage 4b** | Relational-attentive (if implicit memory block) | Parts & Burden Discovery (in-session); What the Part Carries (between-session); silence; Ignatian imagination | Spirit as primary agent; moments of meeting                                                                                                       |

**Transition logic:** Affections picture must be specific before moving to Stage 2. False identity must be named before covenant truth lands. Stage 4a vs. 4b is determined by whether the block is primarily cognitive (client argues against truth) or pre-verbal/implicit (client agrees but cannot feel it).

**Stage 3 activation requirement — load-bearing:** Before introducing covenant truth, verify the implicit belief is emotionally live in the room — felt, not just named. Declaration spoken into cognitive acknowledgment produces *extinction*: temporary suppression that returns under stress because the implicit encoding was never changed. Declaration spoken into an activated implicit belief creates conditions for *reconsolidation*: the lie is replaced, not overridden. If the belief cannot be activated, return to Stage 1 and deepen the felt sense before proceeding.

**Stage 4a vs. 4b distinction — critical:** Applying CBT tools to an implicit memory barrier will not move it and leaves the client feeling like they are failing. Applying relational presence to a cognitive distortion leaves the distortion undisturbed. Identify which is operating before choosing a response.

## Parallel Diagnostic Streams

These run across all phases simultaneously with the stage sequence above. They are not stages — they are continuous diagnostic lenses.

**God-Representation (all phases):**
The client's functional God-image — distinct from theological belief — shapes how every stage lands. A client may affirm God's love while experientially operating from an image of God as absent, punitive, transactional, or overwhelming. Surfaced first in Phase 1 (Discovery Call and intake), then deepened through all four phases. Five primary types: Punitive, Absent/Distant, Permissive/Safe, Transactional, Overwhelming/Consuming. Has its own between-session practice library calibrated by type and phase.
*Tools:* God-Representation Deepening Tool; God-Representation Between-Session Practices; God-Representation Research Basis

**Parable of the Soils (parallel frame, all phases):**
Runs alongside Stages 1–4 as a receptivity diagnostic — answers the readiness question (how truth lands, pressure response, soil condition) while the stage sequence answers the content question (what the false identity is, what covenant truth addresses it). Hard/Rocky/Thorny/Good soil assessment shapes pacing, container-building, and formation track assignment. Soil type does not replace Stage 1 affections work; it frames the container within which all stages operate.
*Tool:* Parable of the Soils — Kingdom Coaching Deep Framework

## When Movement Stalls

When progress stops at any phase, consult `Covenant Identity — Why the Heart Does Not Heal — Practitioner Diagnostic.md` before reaching for a different stage tool.

Eight diagnostic barriers to heart healing, each with a participation move and coaching signal:

| Barrier | Signal |
|---------|--------|
| Self-deception | Client describes the problem but doesn't own it |
| Wrong source | Seeking healing from people, circumstances, or coaching itself |
| Idol attachment | Named false identity is still being protected |
| Wrong treatment level | Addressing symptoms without reaching the organizing center |
| Hardening | Repeated exposure without movement; defense has solidified |
| Will not engaged | Understands and agrees but refuses the cost of change |
| Refusing suffering | Resisting the formation that comes through the valley |
| Not receiving the gift | Cannot accept what is freely given; performing for what is offered |

**Governing frame:** God sanctifies; the client participates through three acts — honesty, covenant openness, and choosing to trust. Stall is almost always located in one of these three.

*Full reference:* `Covenant Identity — Why the Heart Does Not Heal — Practitioner Diagnostic.md` (routing table) + `Covenant Identity — Why the Heart Does Not Heal — Biblical Research.md` (exegetical basis)

## Core Intervention Modalities

Five modalities available across all phases. Selected based on what is blocking reception at each stage.

| Modality | Function | When |
|----------|----------|------|
| **Targeted Declaration** | Specific counter-truth spoken against a named lie; embodied (aloud, written), anchored in covenant text, rehearsed until the new identity becomes the default operating system | Stage 3; after false identity is named and specific lie structure is clear |
| **Lament-to-Trust Movement** | Structured honest complaint before God — not bypassing the gap but bringing the full self into covenant. Address → Complaint → Petition → Confession of trust → Praise. Required for spiritual bypassing, hollow prayer, spiritual dryness; not optional where grief is present | Phase 2 entry; whenever emotional processing is suppressed through theological performance |
| **Schema Interruption** | Real-time practice of catching schema activation (somatic signal first) and inserting a pause before the automatic behavioral sequence completes | When the false identity runs beneath cognitive level — fast, automatic, felt as fact |
| **Narrative Re-authoring** | Externalizing the problem narrative; finding and thickening exception events; constructing an alternative story grounded in Scripture and observed evidence | When the self-narrative is problem-saturated and counter-evidence is being discounted |
| **Big Assumption Experiment** | Small, low-risk behavioral test designed to gather data about whether the Column 4 Big Assumption is actually true | After competing commitment and Big Assumption are named; when behavioral evidence is needed to crack the assumption |

**Lament note:** The Psalms are approximately 40% lament. Lament is the practice of remaining in covenant while honest about the distance between covenant truth and lived experience — the structural opposite of spiritual bypassing. When a client presents with spiritual dryness, disconnection from God, or prayer that feels hollow, the first intervention is almost always lament, not encouragement or theological correction.

## Formation Architecture

**Formation posture (what the client does):**
John 15 — "Abide." Not striving (the branch does not produce the fruit) and not passive waiting (the branch remains deliberately connected). Between-session question: *What did abiding look like this week — and where did you disconnect?* *(Practitioner reference: to be built — see queue.)*

**Formation provision (what the shepherd does while the client abides):**
Psalm 23 — rest, refreshment, healing, guidance, presence, honor, assurance. Each blocked provision is a diagnostic signal pointing to the specific wound or false identity underneath it. *(Formation provision reference: to be built — see queue.)*

**Formation mechanism (VIM — Willard):**
- **Vision** — Psalm 24 (redemptive-historical telos: the King of Glory returning); Interior Life as Telos (9 qualities: Settled, Undivided, Free, Moved Without Being Swept, Unhurried, Loved and Knowing It, Truthful Without Cruelty, Purposeful Without Striving, Available)
- **Intention** — Designed Alliance; client's explicit covenant to pursue formation
- **Means** — Spiritual disciplines matched to wound type; between-session library; rule of life (Phase 4)

**Primary between-session formation discipline:**
*Secured in Father — Attachment Formation & Encounter Preparation* is the primary between-session formation tool. Assign after Phase 1 diagnostic work is specific enough to name the attachment pattern. Eight components organized across ten dimensions with pattern-specific language (Anxious/Dismissing/Fearful). Consult `Secured in Father — Practitioner Design Notes.md` for absolute and conditional contraindications before assigning — do not assign during active trauma dysregulation or dissociative presentation.

**Disciplines calibrated by wound type:**

| Wound Type | Most Formative | Co-optation Risk |
|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Warrior | Lament; Confession; Sabbath | Productivity disguised as discipline |
| Hermit | Scripture Saturation; Accountability Companion | Isolation disguised as solitude |
| False Noble | Silence; Examen; Ignatian Imagination | Performance disguised as spiritual effort |

## Formation Modules

Deployable multi-document modules that combine practitioner reference, diagnostic tools, session facilitation, and client-facing materials.

**Sanctification Module — The Believer's Part**
*Deployment:* Middle sessions — after Affections Discovery has surfaced a false covenant object without defensive collapse. Not at intake. Not during acute crisis.
*Target client:* Moderate theological literacy with low integration; presenting with the gap between knowing and experiencing.
*Four failure modes:* Warrior (performance-driven striving), Hermit (withdrawal and isolation from formation), False Noble (spiritual performance masking the gap), Mode 4 — no inner access (cannot identify internal experience; requires pre-module stabilization).
*Core documents:* Teacher's Manual, Coach Diagnostic (8-step), Session Facilitation Guide (scripted language), Client Study, Practice Reference Card (Tracks A/B/C), ITC Practitioner Reference.
*Governing text:* Philippians 2:12-13 — God works; therefore work out. The order of the players is load-bearing.

## Declaration Architecture

**Ten-layer covenant identity architecture:** Declaration work is not generic. The full map is the ten-layer covenant identity architecture (Full Model Reference, Section V) — each layer corresponds to a specific schema it counters (Imago Dei → Defectiveness/Shame; Adopted Sonship → Subjugation/Conditional Worth; Indwelt by Spirit → Abandonment/Isolation; etc.). The four wound-category table below is a working subset. For full schema-to-declaration mapping, see `Covenant Identity — New Covenant Promises — Comprehensive Reference.md` and `Covenant Identity — Category Architecture Cross-Reference.md`.

**Declaration matched to wound category:**

| Wound Category | False Identity Statement | Declaration Framework |
|---------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Abandonment / orphan | "God is far; I am alone; no one comes" | Romans 8:14-17 — adoption; Abba, Father; Spirit bearing witness |
| Performance / slave identity | "I earn my place; worth = production" | Galatians 4:4-7 — no longer slave but son, heir |
| Shame / unworthiness | "I am too much / not enough to be received" | Ephesians 1:3-8 — chosen, accepted in the Beloved, sealed |
| Condemnation / accusation | "The record against me is real and permanent" | Romans 8:1, 31-39 — no condemnation; nothing separates |

**Declaration tools:** Lie Eliminator (7-part format), Identity Installer (23 categories), Reckoning Pattern (Romans 6:11), New Covenant Promise Map.

**Declaration mechanism — how it works:** Declaration is not positive affirmation. For it to reach depth it must be: **specific** (addressing the named lie, not generic "I am loved"), **anchored** (tied to covenant text carrying authority), **embodied** (spoken aloud, written by hand — not just thought), and **rehearsed** (repeated until the new identity becomes the default operating system rather than requiring conscious effort). Cognitive assent alone does not produce transformation.

**Critical distinction:** The reckoning in Romans 6:11 (*logizomai*) is counting on a completed reality, not willpower applied to identity. If this distinction is not held, declaration work drifts into the legalism failure mode.

| **3 — Identity Installation**    | 4-8      | Targeted declaration, schema interruption, narrative re-authoring, implicit level work, Sanctification Module (if indicated)                                                                 | Lie Eliminator, Identity Installer, Reckoning Pattern, Phase 3 Session Tools, Stage 4b Reference, Sanctification Module |

|                                  |          |                                                                                                                                                                                              |                                                                                                                         |

**Phase 4 note:** The final phase is not only integration and closure — it is outward sending. Covenant identity is not kept; it is deployed. The client is not waiting to become someone. They are already sent as an ambassador of the King of Glory. Telos work (*what specific corner of the world are you sent to?*) and sustainable renewal rhythms (prayer, community, Sabbath, lament, Scripture engagement) are the primary Phase 4 deliverables alongside the Growth Report.

## Three Failure Modes (Diagnostic & Practitioner Watch)

1. **Legalism** — disciplines deployed as willpower applied to sin identification; Spirit's agency collapsed into human effort
2. **Insight without formation** — false identity clearly named; no between-session practice; understanding without embodiment
3. **Tool dependence** — tools treated as causes of transformation rather than conditions for it; "the tool worked" rather than "the Spirit formed"
