# Covenant Identity — Psalms 22-23-24 Triptych Practitioner Orientation Frame

**What this is:** The practitioner's internal compass for locating a client within the redemptive-historical arc of Psalms 22, 23, and 24. Used in every session to orient the practitioner's listening and response choices. Never taught to the client or named as a framework during a session.

**When used:** Continuously — before a session (as a preparatory orientation), during a session (as a listening lens), and after a session (as a reflective diagnostic). This is not a stage tool that deploys at a particular sequence point. It is always running.

**Who uses it:** The practitioner only.

---

> **The governing distinction:** The Developmental Framework (`Covenant Identity — Psalms 22-23-24 Developmental Framework.md`) describes *what coaching work belongs to each position.* This document describes *how the practitioner holds and uses the triptych as an internal instrument* — what they listen for, what it tells them, and how it shapes their moment-to-moment decisions without becoming session content.

---

## The Frame

The Psalm 22-23-24 triptych is the practitioner's redemptive-historical map of the client's soul. It runs invisibly beneath all session work. The client never needs to know it exists.

Three positions on the map:

| Psalm | Position | Redemptive Moment |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| **22** | Lament / wound / desolation | The cross — God forsaken; the cry that is still covenant address |
| **23** | Formation / shepherd accompaniment | The present age — the valley is real; the shepherd is present; the client is learning to receive |
| **24** | Telos / declaration / kingship | The return — the King of Glory; nothing stands outside his ownership |

**What the practitioner holds:**
- Where is this client living *right now* — in this session, in this domain of life?
- What does their position tell me about what kind of work is appropriate?
- Is the Psalm 23 provision they most need rest, presence, healing, guidance, honor, refreshment, or assurance?

The practitioner does not impose the sequence. The triptych is a map for reading the client's location, not a curriculum that moves them through three chapters.

## Locating the Client — What to Listen For

### Psalm 22 Signals — The Client Is in Desolation

These are not always presented as crisis. Many clients in Psalm 22 territory present as exhausted, disconnected, numb, or performing spiritual language while experiencing none of it.

**Listen for:**
- *Distance language:* "God feels far," "I know he's there but I can't feel it," "I've been praying and nothing changes"
- *Self-reduction:* language that shrinks identity — "I'm just..." "I don't have what it takes" "Maybe I'm not the kind of person who..."
- *Internalized accusation:* the voice they describe speaking *about* them — shame, condemnation, or the judgment of others operating as interior fact
- *Covenant doubt framed as theological question:* "Does God actually care about my specific situation?" — this is often Psalm 22 language, not a doctrinal inquiry
- *Exposure fear:* distress about being seen, judged, or found lacking — particularly in contexts that previously promised safety

**What the position indicates:**
The client is not ready to receive Psalm 23 provision. The primary work is lament legitimation, affections diagnostic (what failed covenant object is exposed?), and presence — not instruction. The shepherd must be brought into the desolation first, not positioned beyond it.

### Psalm 23 Signals — The Client Is in the Formation Zone

The client is not in acute desolation but is not yet standing in the telos. They are in the valley — accompanied, but still walking. This is where the majority of the engagement's formation investment lives.

**Listen for:**
- Language of uneven reception: "I know this is true but I don't feel it in this area" — the shepherd's provision is present in some domains but blocked in others
- Resistance or confusion that is specific, not global: not "God is absent" but "I can't seem to rest" or "I keep striving even when I know I don't have to"
- Partial movement: progress that stalls at a recognizable provision (rest, refreshment, presence, guidance, honor, assurance)
- The valley acknowledged but not controlling: the client can name the threat without being consumed by it

**What the position indicates:**
Identify which Psalm 23 provision is blocked and work that specific gap. The discipline assignment and between-session practice should be calibrated to what the shepherd is *doing that the client cannot yet receive* — not the full formation agenda at once.

### Psalm 24 Signals — The Client Is Beginning to Stand in the Telos

This position does not mean formation is complete. It means the client has begun to inhabit their covenant identity from the end — they are orienting from the King's return rather than living purely in the middle.

**Listen for:**
- Sovereignty framing arising organically: "Even this belongs to God" — without coaching prompting
- Declaration language emerging without effort: they are beginning to say covenant truth rather than receive it as information
- Enemy perspective shifting: the things that formerly defined them are being seen in their true proportion
- Outward movement: their attention is beginning to move from their own formation to what they are sent to do with it

**What the position indicates:**
Declaration work is primary. The Lie Eliminator and Identity Installer are functioning at capacity. Phase 4 (Mission and Fruitfulness) work — ambassador framing, telos inquiry, rule of life — is now available.

## He→You: The Intimacy Signal

Psalm 23 contains one of the most significant pronoun shifts in the Psalter — and in a session, its equivalent is one of the most significant diagnostic events a practitioner can observe.

**The text:**

Verses 1-3: *"The LORD is my shepherd... He maketh me to lie down... He leadeth me... He restoreth my soul... He leadeth me in the paths of righteousness."*

Verse 4: *"Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil: for **thou** art with me; **thy** rod and **thy** staff they comfort me."*

Verse 5: *"**Thou** preparest a table before me... **thou** anointest my head with oil."*

**What happened:**

At the most dangerous moment of the psalm — the valley, the shadow, the threat — the speaker's language shifts from third-person description ("He") to second-person direct address ("Thou"). Distance collapses precisely in the dark. The shepherd, previously described, is now addressed. This is not rhetorical variation. It marks the arrival of presence.

**The session equivalent:**

Watch for a client making the same shift — from theological language about God to direct address to God. "God is faithful" is third-person. "You were with me in that" is second-person. The shift from He to You in a client's speech is not incidental. It marks the moment the shepherd has become present rather than conceptual.

**The practitioner response:**

When this shift occurs, do not intervene with more diagnostic or conceptual content. Do not ask a question. Hold the space. The encounter is happening. The practitioner's job at this moment is to protect the space, not fill it.

A client in Psalm 23 territory who cannot make this shift — who can only speak about God, never to him — is still living in Psalm 22 at the level of relational access, even if they are receiving some provision. The He→You distance is itself a diagnostic signal: what is blocking direct address?

## The Enemies Arc as Developmental Marker

The practitioner does not ask "Does this client have enemies?" — every client does. The diagnostic question is: *What authority does the enemy have in this client's interior architecture right now?*

| Stage | Psalm | The Client's Relationship to What Threatens |
|-------|-------|---------------------------------------------|
| **1** | Psalm 22 | The threat defines reality — it appears to win; it writes the interior identity statement |
| **2** | Psalm 23 | The threat is present but demoted — the table is set *in the presence of enemies*; they witness provision but do not control it |
| **3** | Psalm 24 | The threat is seen in true proportion — the King of Glory is sovereign; no enemy survives the return |

**In-session diagnostic question (internal to practitioner):**

*When the threat shows up in this client's narrative, what does it say to them — and do they believe it?*

- If the threat **writes their identity** ("I am what the wound says I am"): Psalm 22 position.
- If the threat **is present but losing authority** ("That's what I used to believe about myself"): Psalm 23 movement.
- If the threat is **seen without power** ("That has no claim on me"): Psalm 24 orientation.

This is one of the most reliable developmental markers available in session. It does not require a formal assessment — it surfaces naturally in how the client narrates pressure, conflict, and self-perception.

## Practitioner Internal Questions by Session Moment

These are the questions the practitioner carries internally. They are not asked of the client.

**At the opening of a session:**
- Where does this client appear to be living today — 22, 23, or 24?
- Has their position shifted since the last session? Toward the telos or back toward desolation?
- Which domain of life are they most in 22 territory right now, even if they have 23 movement elsewhere?

**During a session:**
- Is what I'm hearing lament that needs to be inhabited, or has it been inhabited — and is movement toward provision now appropriate?
- Which specific Psalm 23 provision is blocked in this moment?
- Have I heard a He→You shift? If not, what is maintaining the distance?
- What does the enemy's authority level tell me about where the client is developmentally?

**When considering a response or intervention:**
- Does the work I'm considering belong to this position — or am I moving the client before the lament has been fully inhabited, or before the provision has been partially received?
- Am I responding to what the client needs in their actual position, or where I want them to be?

**At the close of a session:**
- What psalm is the client walking into this week?
- Is the between-session practice matched to their actual formation position?

## Relationship to the Stage 1–4b Diagnostic Sequence

The triptych and the stage sequence are parallel instruments. They are not competing, and the triptych does not replace the stages.

| Instrument | What It Answers |
|-----------|----------------|
| Stage 1–4b sequence | *What* is the content of the work — what affections, wounds, false identity, or cognitive/implicit block is operating? |
| Psalm 22-23-24 triptych | *Where* is the client in the arc — and what kind of work is appropriate to that location? |

A client can be in Stage 1 (Affections diagnostic) while living in Psalm 22 territory — or while showing first signs of Psalm 23 movement. The stage tells the practitioner what to surface; the triptych tells the practitioner how to hold what surfaces and what register of work is appropriate (lament vs. formation vs. declaration).

When the two instruments conflict — when the stage sequence says "move to covenant truth" but the triptych reads "this person is still in Psalm 22 desolation" — trust the triptych. Diagnostic accuracy is not transformation. A client who intellectually reaches Stage 2 but is experientially still in desolation cannot receive what Stage 3 offers. Return to Psalm 22 work: inhabit the lament, validate the cry, let the shepherd enter before declaring provision.

## Watch Points

**Do not name the triptych in session.** If a client asks what framework you are using, describe your approach in plain language. The triptych is practitioner scaffolding, not client content. A client who is holding the map is not walking the path.

**Do not move from 22 to 23 because the practitioner is uncomfortable with desolation.** The most common practitioner error is premature movement — introducing provision before the lament has been inhabited, because the suffering is difficult to hold. Premature Psalm 23 application is not formation; it is spiritual bypassing with a shepherd. The diagnostic check: has the false covenant object been named? Has the resulting false identity been surfaced? If not, stay in 22.

**Do not assume sequence is linear across domains.** A client can be in Psalm 23 movement regarding their identity but Psalm 22 regarding their marriage, their work, or their relationship with God in prayer. Locate the client domain by domain, not globally.

**He→You shift in the wrong direction is also diagnostic.** If a client who was previously making direct address reverts to third-person God-language, that is not rhetorical. It usually marks renewed distance — grief, re-traumatization, or an unexamined barrier that has solidified. Investigate rather than ignore.
