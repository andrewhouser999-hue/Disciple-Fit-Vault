# Covenant Identity Coaching — Practice Descriptions (Three Forms)

*Authored in Dallas Willard's voice. All three forms share the same spine: the false self governing from below, covenant truth meeting it, the Spirit forming the true self into being. Each form is an expansion of the one before it.*

---

## One Sentence

Covenant Identity Coaching surfaces the false self a disciple has been living from, meets it with the covenant truth of who God says they are in Christ, and creates — through structured formation disciplines — the conditions in which the Spirit forms the true self into being.

---

## A Few Sentences

Many sincere disciples carry a persistent gap between the life they know they are called to and the life they are actually living — not for lack of effort, but because the self they are working with is not the self they were made to live from. The false identity shaped by wounds, failures, and the world's long verdict keeps governing their affections and behavior from below, unmoved by the truths they sincerely hold above. Covenant Identity Coaching works at this root — surfacing the false self through diagnostic precision, applying the covenant truth of who God says they are in Christ, and creating the conditions in which the Spirit forms the true self into being.

---

## One-Minute Pitch

The most common assumption in helping Christians change is that they need more — more knowledge, more discipline, more accountability. We give them more. The gap remains. Not because the help was wrong, but because it was aimed at the wrong level.

The problem is not what they are doing. It is what they are living from. Beneath the repeated failures and the persistent distance from God is a false self — an identity formed by wounds, history, and the world's long verdict — that keeps governing their affections and behavior from below, unmoved by everything applied above.

Covenant Identity Coaching works at this level. It surfaces the false self through diagnostic precision, meets it with the covenant truth of who God says they are in Christ, and creates — through structured formation disciplines — the conditions in which the Spirit forms the true self into being.

That is not a better version of what already exists. It is a different kind of work entirely.
