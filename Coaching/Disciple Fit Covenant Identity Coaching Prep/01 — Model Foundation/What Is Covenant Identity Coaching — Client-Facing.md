# What Is Covenant Identity Coaching?

---

## The Problem Most People Can't Name

Most people I work with aren't struggling because they lack information.

They know the verses. They've been to the conferences, read the books, gone through the Bible studies. They can tell you what the Bible says about who they are in Christ. And yet — something isn't working. The same patterns keep showing up. The same wall. The same gap between who they know they're supposed to be and how they actually live, feel, and show up day to day.

They're not lazy. They're not faithless. They're not lacking discipline or devotion.

They're living from the wrong identity.

That's a different problem — and it requires a different kind of work.

---

## Why Other Approaches Haven't Fixed It

Most approaches — in church, in counseling, in Christian self-help — try to solve this from the outside in.

Better habits. More accountability. More Scripture memory. Better thinking patterns. Clearer goals. More discipline. More prayer.

These things matter. None of them are wrong. But they don't fix the root problem, and most people who've tried them know that — because the change works for a while, and then it stops. Or it never fully started.

Here's why: most people are trying to *perform* their way into a self they've never actually received.

Deep down, underneath the theology, there's a belief running — formed long before adulthood — that says something like: *I'm not enough. I'm fundamentally flawed. I'll be abandoned if I'm truly known. I have to earn my place.* These beliefs don't feel like beliefs. They feel like facts. They shape every relationship, every decision, every moment of failure and every moment of success. And they run whether or not a person consciously agrees with them.

You can memorize every verse about your identity in Christ and still live entirely from those deeper operational beliefs. Most people do.

The work isn't more information. The work is closing the gap between what you know and what actually feels true — in the dark, in the moment of failure, in the quiet.

---

## What This Coaching Is Built On

God's design has a specific shape: **He declares who you are first. Identity is given before it's grown. Belonging comes before behavior.**

This is the pattern throughout Scripture. He doesn't say: "Perform well enough and I'll accept you." He says: "You are mine — now live like it." The obligation always comes *after* the declaration of who you are to Him. Always.

The problem is that most of us received a different message early. From family. From failure. From pain. From the way the world responded to us. We formed deep beliefs about ourselves in those early experiences — beliefs that became the lens through which we read everything, including God.

So we end up with this: a covenant God who has declared our identity in Christ, and a lived experience that feels nothing like that declaration. Not because the declaration is false. Because something is in the way.

That gap — between what you believe about God with your mind and what you actually live from in your gut — is the primary terrain of this coaching.

---

## What This Coaching Actually Does

This is not therapy. I'm not treating wounds or diagnosing disorders. If that's what you need, I'll help you find it — but it's not what I do.

This is coaching — which means you drive. You set the agenda. You determine what matters and what you want to work toward. My job is to ask the questions you haven't thought to ask yourself, hold you to what you say matters to you, and refuse to let you settle for less than who you actually are.

What makes this different from most coaching is that we have a reference point beyond your own preferences and goals: who God has already said you are. That's not a ceiling. It's a floor. Everything builds on it.

**Across our work together, we typically:**

- Name the story you've been telling about yourself — where it came from, what it's costing you, and whether it's true
- Surface what's actually keeping you stuck (it's usually not what you think — and it's almost never a lack of willpower)
- Work directly in the gap between your lived experience and your God-given identity
- Bring your real self — the frustrated, doubting, grieving, angry self — into the conversation instead of presenting only the polished version
- Build practices that move truth from your head into how you actually live, feel, and relate
- Identify where you've been trying to earn an identity that's already been given to you — and what it would look like to receive it instead

This is slow work. It's honest work. It doesn't produce a 30-day transformation. What it tends to produce is change that holds — because it goes to the root rather than managing the symptoms.

---

## What You Can Expect

**Sessions** are focused conversations — typically 50-60 minutes. You bring what's most alive for you. We work it together. I'll ask hard questions. I'll name things I notice. I'll hold you accountable to what you say matters. I won't give you a sermon, and I won't let you off the hook.

**Between sessions**, you'll carry something — a commitment, a practice, something to notice, something to try. Not a to-do list. Something that keeps the work moving in real life.

**Over time**, most people find that they understand themselves differently — not just behaviorally, but at depth. They start catching themselves living from the old story. They have language for what's happening when they get stuck. They experience, often for the first time, what it actually feels like to live from who God says they are rather than performing toward it.

---

## Is This for You?

This is likely a good fit if:

- You're a follower of Jesus who knows the right answers but keeps hitting the same walls
- You feel the gap between who God says you are and how you actually experience yourself — and you're tired of it
- You've tried behavior-modification approaches and found them insufficient
- You're willing to be honest — about what you actually feel, what you actually believe in your gut, what you actually want
- You want to understand yourself at depth, not just manage yourself better
- You're not currently in crisis that requires clinical care

This is probably not a fit if:

- You want someone to tell you what to do
- You're looking for a spiritual accountability partner or discipleship relationship
- You want practical tips without the deeper work underneath
- You're dealing with clinical-level depression, trauma, or mental health conditions that need a therapist first — in that case, I'd encourage you to pursue that, and coaching may be a strong complement afterward

---

## A Note on What This Is Not

This is not a shortcut to spiritual maturity. It's not a replacement for church, community, prayer, or Scripture. It's not therapy — and it's not discipleship in the traditional sense.

It's a focused, structured relationship designed to help you close the gap between who God has declared you to be and how you actually live — using real questions, honest conversation, and practices that go beneath the surface.

The work of becoming who you already are in Christ is the work of a lifetime. This coaching is one concentrated season of that work.
