**WHAT PHASE 4 IS**

Phase 4 has three jobs: consolidate, sustain, and close.

**Consolidate** — Gather the evidence of what actually changed. Not what the client hopes changed — what the record shows. The Session Growth Notes contain this evidence. The Growth Report synthesizes it. Phase 4 brings it before the client explicitly, in their own words, so they cannot argue with it.

**Sustain** — Build the forward structure that holds the work after the coaching container is gone. The schema will activate again. The old story will return under pressure. The competing commitment will reassert itself. Phase 4 does not pretend otherwise. It gives the client a named setback protocol, a relational ecology audit, and a specific practice design so the formation continues after the engagement ends.

**Close** — End the engagement with honesty and weight. The coaching relationship held real material. Ending it is not routine. Phase 4 names the grief, names the gratitude, names what remains, and closes with the Growth Report — read aloud, together, in full.

**Phase 4 has two distinct session types:**

1. **Consolidation & Forward Structure Session** — Examines the evidence of growth, designs the forward structure (practice, relational ecology, setback protocol), and assigns the Letter to Beginning Self.

2. **Growth Report Session (Final Session)** — The Growth Report is read aloud in full. Space is held for the client's response. The Letter to Beginning Self is read if the client has written it. The engagement is closed with honesty and honor.

---

**WHAT PHASE 4 IS NOT**

Phase 4 is not new excavation. When clients bring new material in Phase 4 — new memories, wounds, schema content — the phase has not changed. Name it explicitly and decide together: extend or refer. Do not open Phase 2 terrain in a Phase 4 session and then close the engagement two sessions later.

Phase 4 is not a victory lap. Generic celebration ("look how far you've come!") cheapens the work. The Growth Report does the work of naming growth — the coach does not need to supplement it with enthusiasm.

Phase 4 is not the beginning of an ongoing dependent relationship. The ethics of non-dependency require that Phase 4 actively builds the client's independence. The client should leave capable of continuing the work without requiring the coach as container.

**Sustainability and forward structure:**

- Sanctification as ongoing and eschatological — the engagement is a chapter, not the whole story; what continues after the engagement is not evidence the coaching failed — it is the condition of all formation in the already/not-yet

**FORMAT**

The document must include these seven components, in order:

1. **Phase Job Description** — What Phase 4 is for and what it is not for (consolidate, sustain, close). What happens if Phase 4 is entered before Phase 3 is complete. Why non-dependency is a clinical and ethical requirement, not a preference.

2. **Session Structure** — Two distinct session types, each with full structure guidance:
   - **Consolidation & Forward Structure Session**: Opening (naming the transition), consolidation conversation, forward structure design (practice, relational ecology, setback protocol), Letter to Beginning Self prompt. Include time allocations and specific guidance for each component.
   - **Growth Report Session (Final Session)**: Preparation requirements, how to open the session, how to read the report (pace, handling silence, what to do when the client responds mid-reading), the post-reading open space, the Letter to Beginning Self if written, and closing the engagement. This session type must be treated as a distinct event with its own guidance — not a generic session close.

3. **Primary Question Library** — Questions organized by function: consolidation, setback protocol design, relational ecology, forward structure, Growth Report session, and closing the engagement. Each question should have a brief coach note. Minimum 25 questions.

4. **Coach Observation Guide** — Phase 4-specific: how to read the emotional climate of Phase 4 (grief, pride, fear, relief, ambivalence — often simultaneously); how to distinguish legitimate extension need from relational dependency driving extension; what to watch for when the Growth Report is being read (client responses to specific sections, minimizing, tears, flatness); non-dependency indicators vs. dependency markers.

5. **Common Pitfalls** — 6-8 specific Phase 4 errors with navigation. Prioritize: premature close, reopening excavation, the Growth Report as debrief rather than event, extending out of relationship rather than clinical necessity, skipping the setback protocol, failing to name the relational ecology.

6. **Integration Notes** — What feeds into Phase 4 (specific documents and fields). What Phase 4 produces (Growth Report delivered, Letter to Beginning Self, forward structure named).

7. **Transition Marker / Engagement Close** — Specific indicators the engagement is complete. What an incomplete close looks like. What to do when close is complicated — active crisis, genuinely unfinished Phase 3 work, new urgent material in Phase 4. The final closing language the coach uses to seal the engagement.
