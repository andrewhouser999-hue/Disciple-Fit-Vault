## Purpose

Build `Covenant Identity — Formation Mechanism Guide.md` — a practitioner reference with embedded client-facing psychoeducation guide explaining how spiritual formation actually works, why it is slow, and why resistance is expected and normal. Deployed in Phase 1 (Covenant Orientation), after the Gap has been named and before wound work begins. Designed to be re-issued when formation resistance surfaces in later phases.

This is not a tool that opens wound content or assigns practice. It is orientation — it gives the client a frame for understanding what is happening in their formation before frustration and misinterpretation undermine engagement.

## Expert Framework Stack

Listed in order of governing authority, not contribution volume.

### Governing Theological Frame

- **2 Corinthians 3:18 (ESV)** — "And we all, with unveiled face, beholding the glory of the Lord, are being transformed into the same image from one degree of glory to another. For this comes from the Lord who is the Spirit." Primary mechanism: transformation comes from beholding, not from effort or information. The Spirit is the agent of transformation.
- **Psalm 1:2–3 (ESV)** — the tree whose roots go deep through sustained meditative saturation. Fruit is produced by rootedness, not effort. Formation is slow because rootedness is slow.
- **Joshua 1:8 (ESV)** — day-and-night meditation producing way-making. The same *hagah* rootedness frame: formation happens through sustained return, not single corrective events.
- **Philippians 1:6 (ESV)** — "And I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ." Formation is God's project. The client cooperates — they do not manage it.
- **Romans 12:2 (ESV)** — "Do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewal of your mind, that by testing you may discern what is the will of God, what is good and acceptable and perfect." Transformation is a renewal process across time, not a moment of correction.

### Mechanism Layer

Draw from **Curt Thompson** (*Anatomy of the Soul*; *The Soul of Shame*) for the two-layer mechanism. Translate into accessible, plain-language description throughout the client-facing section — do not use clinical vocabulary. The phenomenon to describe: every person operates with two layers simultaneously — what they consciously believe, and what they actually expect and function from at a deeper level. The deeper layer was encoded through relational experience before language existed. It does not update by receiving new information. It updates through new relational experience over time — specifically, being known differently than what the deeper layer has learned to expect. These two layers can contradict each other, and that contradiction is not a spiritual failure. It is how human beings are made.

Draw from **Dallas Willard** (*Renovation of the Heart*; *The Spirit of the Disciplines*) for the training vs. trying distinction. The spiritual disciplines do not produce transformation. The Spirit produces transformation through the conditions the disciplines create. Resistance and slowness are not signs of insufficient effort — they are part of the mechanism. The client is training the whole person to receive what the Spirit is offering, not performing holiness.

### Character Wound Types

Draw from **Dan Allender** (*To Be Told*; *Redeeming Heartache*) for Warrior, Hermit, and False Noble resistance patterns. Each type misreads slow formation in a characteristic way. These patterns are named in the client-facing section.

## Document Structure

### Section I — Practitioner Reference (coach-facing)

Standard format: brief functional notes, no elaboration beyond what the coach needs to deploy.

**Deployment:** Phase 1, Session 1 or early Session 2 — after the Gap has been named, before wound work begins. Give before the client experiences resistance, not after. The frame is most useful when received before frustration, not as a corrective after frustration has already been interpreted as failure.

**Re-issuable trigger:** if the client hits a stretch where formation feels stalled, progress feels absent, or resistance is being misread as failure, direct them to re-read this document. The re-issuable note is embedded in the client-facing section. The coach does not reassign — they direct the client back to what they already have.

**Coach deployment notes per wound type:**
- *Warrior:* may receive this document as information to master rather than orientation to inhabit. Watch for the client using the framework to explain their formation rather than cooperate with it. If they can articulate the mechanism fluently but remain defended in session, the document has been managed, not received.
- *Hermit:* may use this document to disengage — "I understand why it's slow, therefore I don't need to do anything yet." The mechanism description can become permission to wait indefinitely. In session, surface whether the Hermit is staying present to what is emerging or using the frame to justify withdrawal.
- *False Noble:* will read this document to assess whether they are doing formation correctly. They will assess their own resistance patterns against the wound-type descriptions and conclude whether they are on track. The question to ask in session: *"What would it mean to just receive this, rather than evaluate it?"*

**What this document does not do:** it does not excavate wound content, introduce lament, assign practices, or open therapeutic material. It orients. If the client raises questions that move toward wound content, receive them and bridge to session — do not let the document become a session in the client's hands.

### Section II — Client-Facing Guide

Four parts. Readable in under 10 minutes. Conversational, pastoral, no clinical vocabulary. Biblical texts quoted in full (ESV) with reference.

---

#### Part I — How Formation Actually Works

Open with the governing claim: transformation is not primarily a cognitive event. It does not happen because a person learns true things, decides to be different, or tries harder. It comes from *beholding* — from sustained, repeated encounter with God that reaches the whole person, not just the mind. Quote 2 Corinthians 3:18 (ESV) in full.

Describe the two-layer phenomenon in plain language — no clinical vocabulary. Name it as: what a person consciously believes, and what they actually expect and function from at a deeper level. These are not the same thing, and they can contradict each other. A person can believe (consciously, genuinely) that God is unconditionally loving and still function from a place where that love is experienced as conditional or distant. This is not weakness of faith. It is how human beings are made — we carry what we have experienced, not only what we have concluded.

Name that coaching addresses the deeper layer, not only the conscious beliefs. Information reaches the mind. Formation reaches the whole person.

---

#### Part II — Why It Is Slow

Formation is not slow because something is wrong. It is slow because the mechanism is rootedness, not event. Quote Psalm 1:2–3 or Joshua 1:8 (ESV). The tree's roots go down before fruit appears. The image is not of effort producing growth — it is of sustained presence near the water producing a life that bears fruit in its season.

Describe how the deeper layer of expectation and functioning was laid down over years — through relational experiences, formative environments, repeated patterns absorbed before conscious awareness. It was not formed in a single event, and it does not reshape in a single event. It reshapes through new experience over time. If the formation of the wound took years, the renovation of the wound also moves through time. This is not a failure of faith or effort. It is the shape of how God works in persons.

Name the implication plainly: the coaching engagement will not complete the formation. It opens what God is doing and creates conditions for the Spirit's ongoing work. The client will leave this engagement different — and still in process.

---

#### Part III — Why Resistance Is Expected

Resistance is not a sign that formation is failing. It is often a sign that something is being reached. When the deeper layer is being approached — through declaration, through honest reflection, through encounter with God in prayer — the patterns that were built to protect against being known will activate. This is expected. It is not backsliding, weakness, or evidence that coaching isn't working.

Each wound type has a characteristic way of misreading resistance. Name them by type — the client knows their type:

**The Warrior** tends to read slow formation as weakness — as evidence that they should be further along, that they aren't pushing hard enough, that something is wrong with them or with the process. The Warrior's armor was built to prevent exactly the kind of exposed, not-yet-arrived position that formation requires. When the armor activates, it feels like protection. It is also the thing standing between the Warrior and what they most need. Resistance for the Warrior usually feels like impatience, frustration, or the impulse to take the process over.

**The Hermit** tends to read slow formation as confirmation — as evidence that change of this kind may not be possible for them specifically, that the process is asking too much, that staying in it will only expose what they have spent years concealing. The Hermit's withdrawal pattern is triggered by anything that feels like exposure or demand. Resistance for the Hermit usually feels like wanting to disappear from the process, a sense that nothing is happening, or the quiet conclusion that they are the exception to how this works.

**The False Noble** tends to read slow formation as not measuring up — as evidence that others are progressing faster, that they are not doing this well enough, that they have somehow failed the process. The False Noble's performance pattern turns formation into a metric to manage. Resistance for the False Noble often looks like increasing effort — trying harder to feel what they are supposed to feel, producing entries and reflection that demonstrate progress, monitoring themselves for signs of change.

Each of these patterns is the wound's response to the formation process — not evidence that the process is failing.

---

#### Part IV — What to Do With This

The posture is cooperation, not management. The Spirit is doing something — the coaching, the practices, the declaration work, the honest reflection — these create conditions for what the Spirit is already doing. The client's role is to stay present to the process, return to the practices when they drift, and resist the move to assess progress before it is time to assess.

Quote Philippians 1:6 (ESV) in full as the closing anchor. Name what it means: God who began this work will complete it. Formation belongs to Him. The client is not responsible for manufacturing their own transformation — they are responsible for remaining in the conditions where the Spirit works.

Close with the re-issuable note, set off visually:

> *If you hit a stretch where progress feels absent, resistance feels like failure, or the process seems to have stalled — return to this document before drawing a conclusion. The frame does not change across the engagement. Only the depth at which it applies.*
