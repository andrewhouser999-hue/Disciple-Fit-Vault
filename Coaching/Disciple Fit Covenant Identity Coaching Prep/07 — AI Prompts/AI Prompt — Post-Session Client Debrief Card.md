## Purpose

Build `Covenant Identity — Post-Session Client Debrief Card.md` — an immediate-capture tool completed within an hour or two of each session, before the integration window fully opens. Three questions only. Distinct from the Post-Session Integration Guide (what to *do* in the 24-48 hours after a heavy session) and the Phase-Specific Reflection Guide (ongoing between-session noticing across the week).

---

## Design Decisions

- **Three questions only.** Not a journaling prompt — an immediate capture. What landed, what was costly, what needs continuity.
- **Completed within an hour or two of session ending.** Before sleep, before the day resumes and the content disperses. Timing is the tool.
- **No wound-type calibration on the card itself** — too short for it. Wound-type watch-fors belong in the practitioner reference section only.
- **Phase 4 note:** answers in Phase 4 feed the Growth Report. Flag this for the client explicitly — what they write in Phase 4 debriefs will be the raw material the coach draws from when building the Growth Report.
- **Spirit-invitation posture at the top of the card** — consistent with other tools. Brief, orienting, not formulaic.
- **Distinct from adjacent tools — make the distinction explicit:**
  - Post-Session Integration Guide = what to do in the 24-48 hours after a heavy session (practices, body care, prayer)
  - Phase-Specific Reflection Guide = ongoing noticing across the week (journaling, not immediate capture)
  - This card = what happened in the session, captured while it is still warm

---

## Three Questions

1. *What is the one thing I am carrying out of today's session — the thing that landed, shifted, or stayed with me?*
2. *What was hardest about today's session?*
3. *What do I want to bring back into the next session?*

These cover three functions: what landed (formative content to hold), what was costly (honesty about the weight of the work), and what needs continuity (thread-keeping between sessions).

---

## Document Structure

### Practitioner Reference

Very brief. Cover:
- Deployment: alongside the Phase-Specific Reflection Guide; give before Phase 2 begins
- Timing: within an hour or two of session ending; before sleep
- Distinction from adjacent tools (brief, one sentence each)
- Phase 4 note: debrief answers in Phase 4 are primary Growth Report material — tell the client this explicitly when Phase 4 begins
- Wound-type watch-fors (practitioner-only, one line each):
  - *Warrior:* will answer Question 1 with a conclusion about what the session produced, not what it left unresolved. Watch for answers that manage rather than notice.
  - *Hermit:* may not complete the card at all in Phase 2 — the session opened something and the card asks them to name it again. Do not enforce; ask in the next session what it was like to try.
  - *False Noble:* will answer all three questions well and with self-awareness. Ask in session: *"What did you leave out?"*

### Client-Facing Card

**Short — this is a card, not a document.** Introduction: two to three sentences explaining what it is and when to use it. Then the Spirit-invitation. Then the three questions, clearly labeled, with brief framing for each. Then the Phase 4 note.
