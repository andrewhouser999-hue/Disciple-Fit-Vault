## Purpose

Build `Covenant Identity — Biblical Foundations Index.md` — a single practitioner-reference document that answers one question: **what Scripture does each major structural or methodological decision in the Covenant Identity Coaching model actually rest on, and where does the full exegesis live?**

This is a **navigation index, not new exegesis**. It does not re-argue any passage. For each design decision, it names the primary text(s), states in one or two lines what those text(s) establish *for that decision specifically*, points to the document where the full exegetical case is made, and marks whether the grounding is developed, assumed, contested, or currently a gap.

The model's exegetical work is scattered across ~12 documents written at different times for different immediate purposes. No document currently answers "what does the model's *architecture* — as opposed to its individual lie/identity content — rest on." This index is that document.

This index treats the ten-layer architecture *as a whole* (one entry) plus a compact per-layer anchor table — it does not re-walk each promise's full case.

## New Research Inputs (2026-06-11 — not yet written into any vault document)

The following findings came from a `/bible` research pass on 2026-06-11 answering "what Scripture backs the Lie Eliminator and Identity Installer's overall methodology." They are not yet in any source document — pull them in directly for the Implementation Layer section (and flag, per "Save Location & Follow-Up" below, that some may warrant a permanent home elsewhere).

- **Romans 6:11** (*logizesthe* — "consider/reckon yourselves dead to sin and alive to God") links to **Romans 4:3-22**'s justification-reckoning language (*logizomai*). Candidate master verb for the Identity Installer's "declare what is already true" move — declaration-as-reckoning has the same grammatical shape as the reckoning that constitutes justification. **Flag:** *logizesthe* in Rom 6:11 is frequently mis-cited as license for self-talk/positive-thinking; the text's force is "you are dead to sin — therefore stop transacting with it," not "speak it until it becomes true." The Identity Installer's framing should be checked against this distinction.
- **Isaiah 28:14-18** — "you have made a covenant with death... we have made lies our refuge... your covenant with death shall be annulled." New candidate textual parallel for the Lie Eliminator's Agreement Audit (locating and renouncing the moment of agreement with a lie). Original referent is Judah's political alliance with Egypt against Assyria — analogical extension to personal cognitive agreements, same caveat as 2 Cor 10:3-5 below.
- **James 1:22-24** (*paralogizomenoi* — "deceiving themselves") — hearing-without-doing as self-deception. Stronger, less-contested anchor than 2 Cor 10:3-5 for "a person can hold a belief and not have it formationally land" — i.e., the gap the Lie Eliminator and Identity Installer both address.
- **2 Corinthians 10:3-5** ("arguments and every lofty opinion raised against the knowledge of God... take every thought captive") — already cited in Lie Eliminator's stronghold structure. **Flag (contested):** the original referent is Paul's apostolic authority against rival teachers in Corinth (Harris, Barnett, Thrall, Garland), not personal cognitive lies. The Lie Eliminator's use is an analogical extension. This should be stated as such, not smoothed over — the *structure* (arguments/strongholds set against true knowledge) transfers; the *referent* does not.
- **Ephesians 4:22-24 / Colossians 3:9-10** (put off / be renewed / put on) — "put off" and "put on" are aorist (a decisive, definitive act); "being renewed" is present tense (ongoing). Mainstream commentators (O'Brien, Moo, Wright, Lincoln) read this as both/and: the old self is decisively put off at conversion *and* the renewal is ongoing. This is the textual basis for holding the Identity Installer's "already true" framing (aorist — definitive) together with the Lie Eliminator/declaration practice's ongoing renewal (present) without contradiction. Cross-reference [[Sanctification — Client Study]]'s definitive-vs-progressive frame — same distinction, different text.
- **Deuteronomy 6:4-9 (Shema)** and **8:11-18** (commanded "remember," against the self-sufficiency lie "my own power... produced this wealth") — commanded rehearsal/declaration as a biblical pattern predating any psychological "spacing effect" rationale. Relevant to *why spoken, repeated declaration is itself warranted* — not just borrowed from CBT.
- **Psalm 42, Psalm 103, Lamentations 3:21-24** — biblical pattern of self-address (the psalmist preaches to his own soul). Same relevance as Deut 6/8: textual precedent for the Identity Installer's and Lie Eliminator's declaration-spoken-to-self structure.

These last three (Deut 6/8, Ps 42/103, Lam 3:21-24) currently have **no home document** at all — they support a decision (#13 below) that isn't documented anywhere yet. Treat this as a Gap entry even though candidate texts exist; the gap is the absence of a developed case, not the absence of texts.
