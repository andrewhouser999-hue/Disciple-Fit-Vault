**WHAT PHASE 3 IS**

Phase 3 is the delivery. Phase 1 built the container. Phase 2 built the map. Phase 3 uses the map.

The primary work: the Gap begins to close. Not through understanding (Phase 1) or excavation (Phase 2), but through sustained engagement — the whole person making direct contact with a specific covenant truth at the precise point of its absence. The word *installation* is intentional: what is being installed is not a new belief to be performed but a new operating reality.

**The five primary installation tools used in Phase 3:**

1. **Targeted Declaration Work** — Not affirmation practice. The declaration is a probe: it finds the resistance (the wound belief on the other side) and the coach follows the resistance rather than the affirmation. Somatic tracking is essential — installation has not happened until the declaration has produced a shift in the body, not just cognitive acknowledgment.

2. **Schema Interruption Practice** — Building the capacity to catch the schema activating before it runs the client. The activation sequence (trigger → somatic signal → automatic thought → behavior) is mapped and practiced in session. The earliest intervention point is somatic. The specific lie and its specific covenant counter-statement are named. Identity votes between sessions practice the interruption in real life.

3. **Narrative Re-authoring** — Phase 2 externalized the story (the problem is not the person, the person has been living the story). Phase 3 authors the new story. Return to B2 (the present-story statement from intake). Work toward a re-authored present-story statement using the client's own language — not the coach's framing.

4. **Lament-to-Trust Movement** — For clients who entered lament in Phase 2: Phase 3 stays present in Brueggemann's arc. New orientation is not the return to pre-wound naivety — it is harder, more honest trust that has come through the disorientation rather than around it. The coach does not rush this arc.

5. **Big Assumption Experiment** — The Immunity to Change Big Assumption (from Phase 2) is tested through designed experiments: small, specific actions that generate observable data about whether the assumption is actually true. Framed as observation, not performance. Debriefed at the next session.
