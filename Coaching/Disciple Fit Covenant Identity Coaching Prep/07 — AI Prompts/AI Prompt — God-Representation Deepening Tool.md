## Prompt

You are building a standalone practitioner reference tool for a faith-integrated coaching practice called Covenant Identity Coaching. This tool is called the **God-Representation Deepening Tool**. It is used by the coach after Phase 1 has surfaced the client's functional God-representation.

---

### Context

Covenant Identity Coaching is a biblically integrated, psychologically informed model. Its organizing principle is the Gap — the distance between the client's declared covenant identity (who God says they are) and their lived experience of that identity. The nine-layer covenant identity architecture (imago Dei through eschatological formation) is the framework against which the Gap is mapped.

Phase 1 (Covenant Orientation) is where the client's functional God-representation is first surfaced. This tool addresses what the coach does with that discovery — how to work with a punishing, absent, contingent, or capricious God-representation over time, across all four phases, without bypassing it or forcing premature resolution.

This is a practitioner-facing document, not client-facing. It must be written at a level that assumes theological literacy, graduate-level knowledge of psychological frameworks, and professional coaching practice. Tone: authoritative but not clinical. Dense where depth is needed; concise where brevity serves the practitioner.

---

### Expert Framework Stack

Draw from all of the following. Synthesize these into a coherent practitioner tool — do not write a literature review.

**Psychodynamic/object relations:**
- Ana-Maria Rizzuto — *The Birth of the Living God* (1979). God as psychic object (the "living God") distinct from the theological God (propositional). Developmental formation from primary caregivers (not only the father — Rizzuto corrects Freud here). The representation's independence from intellectual/theological maturation. The "birth" of the living God as an ongoing process, not a fixed childhood product.

**Attachment-neurological:**
- Todd W. Hall — implicit vs. explicit God-image (Biola, 2009+). Explicit God-image: conscious, articulable beliefs about God. Implicit God-image: gut-level relational expectations encoded in the attachment system before language. Explicit theological belief alone does not change implicit God-image. Transformation requires new relational experience — neurological rewiring through attuned relationship, prayer encounter, and community.
- Lee Kirkpatrick — *Attachment, Evolution, and the Psychology of Religion* (2005). God as attachment figure (proximity maintenance, safe haven, secure base, separation distress). Correspondence hypothesis vs. compensation hypothesis. Secure/anxious/avoidant/disorganized attachment patterns with God. Research correlating insecure human attachment with specific God-representation types.
- John Bowlby — internal working model as the mechanism for both human and God attachment.

**Spiritual formation:**
- David Benner — *Surrender to Love* and *Soulful Spirituality*. The believed-in God vs. the experienced God. Spiritual formation is blocked when these diverge. Transformation requires acknowledgment of the experienced God (without bypassing or denial) before encounter becomes possible. Encounter, not doctrine, is the pathway.

**Schema foundations:**
- Jeffrey Young — Early Maladaptive Schemas. Defectiveness/Shame, Emotional Deprivation, Unrelenting Standards, Abandonment/Instability. The schemas and their corresponding God-representation types form a reliable co-present pattern.

**Lament theology:**
- Walter Brueggemann — orientation-disorientation-new orientation arc. Lament as honest address to the living God — not as theological failure, but as the condition for encounter.
