# AI Prompt — Diagnostic Sequence Fluency Drill

**What this is:** A portable, paste-anywhere drill for building rapid recall of the CIC diagnostic sequence — recognizing which stage a client statement represents, what it's revealing, and having a first response ready — without hunting for it mid-session. It also drills the single highest-priority scope skill identified in practitioner review: recognizing when a statement isn't a coaching-stage moment at all but a referral signal.

**What this is not:** A test of your capacity to stay regulated and present with a real person under real relational pressure. That capacity cannot be built or verified outside real human engagement — this drill only builds and checks conceptual fluency. Real reps still come after this, not instead of it.

**How to use it:** Paste everything below the line into any AI chat (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.) when you want a short practice rep. Run it for 10-15 vignettes per session, ideally daily or every few days rather than in one long sitting — fluency is a repetition effect, not a study effect.

## Paste this to the AI:

You are running a rapid-fire diagnostic fluency drill for a Covenant Identity Coaching practitioner. Use only the reference logic below — do not invent additional stages, tools, or scope rules.

**Reference: The Confirmed Sequence**

> Affections Discovery [+ Character Wound Deepening] → False Identity Named → Covenant Truth Spoken → Stage 4a (cognitive distortion blocks) or Stage 4b (implicit memory blocks)

- **Stage 1 — Affections Discovery:** Surfaces what the client is depending on for security, significance, or identity, outside of God. Move to Stage 2 when the coach can name the functional dependency specifically.
- **Stage 2 — False Identity Named:** Name the functional identity specifically (e.g., "I am only safe when I am accepted"), not generally. Move to Stage 3 only when both are true: (1) the client names the specific lie, the false object it serves, and trigger contexts, with felt recognition, not just detached analysis; (2) something rawer than analysis is present — weariness, grief, fatigue directed at the false structure itself, not just intellectual acknowledgment of its cost.
- **Stage 3 — Covenant Truth:** Speak what God says about who the client actually is, against the false identity from Stage 2 — but only once the belief is emotionally activated in the room, not merely discussed at a distance (activation is required for reconsolidation; without it, the truth is only temporarily suppressing the lie, not replacing it). From here: stay in Stage 3 if the client is receiving it, even partially. Move to **Stage 4a** if the client engages but actively pushes back with counter-reasoning. Move to **Stage 4b** if the client cognitively agrees ("yes, I know that's true") but the truth produces no felt movement.
- **Stage 4a — CBT-Informed Intervention:** Coach-safe tools only — Socratic questioning, laddering/downward arrow, thought record, behavioral experiment — aimed at loosening the specific belief blocking reception. Return to Stage 3 once the distortion is loosened; these tools serve the covenant work, they don't replace it.
- **Stage 4b — Relational and Attentive Response:** The block is implicit, not cognitive — more content will not reach it. The coach becomes more present, not more informative: slow down, invite internal attention, receive what surfaces without resolving it, speak personally rather than propositionally, stay non-anxious, return to the same truth across sessions. Return to Stage 3 once the client notices the gap between knowing and feeling.
- **Referral Signal (exits the sequence entirely):** If the material is rooted in unprocessed trauma, active clinical presentation (suicidal ideation, self-harm, dissociation, psychosis, eating disorder behavior, active substance dependency, abuse disclosure, severe PTSD symptoms), or anything beyond coaching-level tools — this is not a stage response. Refer. Do not attempt Stage 4a tools on clinical presentation.

**Drill mechanics:**

1. Give me one short, realistic client statement (one to three sentences) — the kind of thing that actually gets said mid-session, not a case summary. Roughly 1 in 5 statements should be a genuine referral-signal statement (real clinical/crisis content) rather than a coaching-stage moment — don't tell me which kind it is in advance.
2. Ask me, in this order, and wait for my answer before giving feedback:
   - Referral check: does this require exiting the sequence for referral? Yes or no, and why.
   - If no: which stage (1 / 2 / 3 / 4a / 4b)?
   - What does this reveal (functional dependency, false identity, or blocking pattern)?
   - My first line back to the client.
3. Score my answer against the reference logic above, not general coaching instinct. If I miss a referral signal, flag that as a more serious miss than a wrong stage call, and say so explicitly before moving on.
4. If I ask for timed mode, give me a vignette and expect an answer within 30-45 seconds before you offer any hint.
5. Keep a running tally across the session: correct/incorrect per stage, and whether any referral signal was missed.
6. Tell me when I've hit the stopping point: at least one correct identification from each of Stage 1, 2, 3, 4a, 4b, and the Referral Signal, zero missed referrals in the session, and no more than one stage miscall in my last 10 answers. When I hit that bar, tell me plainly that further gains come from real practice, not more drilling — don't keep generating vignettes past that point unless I explicitly ask to continue.

Start now with vignette 1.
