**CONTEXT**

You are building two practitioner session tool documents for Andrew Houser's Covenant Identity Coaching model, operated under Disciple Fit Coaching. This is a faith-based coaching practice serving Christians whose felt, lived identity has not caught up to their declared theological identity.

The model's organizing principle: **the indicative precedes the imperative.** Who the client is in Christ is already declared. The coaching engagement works in the Gap — the distance between what is declared true about the client and what they actually experience as true in their body, relationships, and daily functioning.

The **covenant identity architecture** has 10 layers:
1. I bear the image of God — my worth is not earned
2. God sees everything about me and chose me anyway
3. I am a new creation — the old is genuinely gone
4. I am an adopted child with full inheritance rights
5. I am a co-heir with Christ — the full inheritance is shared with me
6. God's presence lives in me — I am not alone
7. My standing before God is secure right now
8. I am loved the way the Father loves the Son
9. I am sent — my life carries specific purpose
10. I am in a divinely-overseen formation process

**FORMAT FOR EACH DOCUMENT**

Each document must include these seven components, in order:

1. **Phase Job Description** — What this phase is for and what it is not for. What it produces. When it is complete. What happens if a coach skips it or rushes it.

2. **Session Structure** — A sequence guide, not a script. Where a phase spans multiple sessions with meaningfully different content (e.g., session 1 vs. session 2 in Phase 1), provide distinct structure guidance for each. Include approximate time allocation per section. Include notes on what to do when the client pulls the session in a different direction than the structure intends.

3. **Primary Question Library** — Questions organized by function: opening/orienting questions, primary excavation questions, deepening questions, landing questions. Each question should have a brief coach note on what it is designed to surface and how to read the response. 15-25 questions per phase minimum.

4. **Coach Observation Guide** — What to track in the client's body, voice, affect, and language during this phase. What specific responses likely indicate and what to do with them. Include what activation looks like vs. genuine engagement vs. spiritual bypassing vs. performed response.

5. **Common Pitfalls** — 5-8 specific ways coaches get stuck, go wrong, or cause harm in this phase, with navigation for each. Be specific and honest — not a list of generic coaching errors.

6. **Integration Notes** — What document or information feeds into this phase (and where to find it). What this phase produces that feeds the next document in the system. Specific field references (e.g., "The A5 field from the Intake Addendum is the baseline for the God-representation conversation in session 2").

7. **Transition Marker** — How the coach knows the phase is complete. What the client looks like, sounds like, and what has been established. What readiness for the next phase looks like — and what it looks like when a client is not yet ready.
