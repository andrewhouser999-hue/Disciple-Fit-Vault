# AI Prompt — Formation Self-Assessment

*Generation prompt for [[Covenant Identity — Formation Self-Assessment]]. Saved for re-generation, revision, and adaptation.*

---

## Purpose

Build `Covenant Identity — Formation Self-Assessment.md` — a periodic, client-autonomous instrument used at phase transitions and monthly within a phase. Tracks formation movement over time through trend comparison across four observable areas. Client completes without the coach present; shares results at their discretion.

---

## Design Decisions

- **Four areas, not dimension labels from the model.** The organizing structure is what the client can actually observe and report on in plain language — not clinical anthropology or diagnostic sequence stages. Four areas: Identity Experience, Wound-Pattern Activity, God-Relationship, Formation Engagement.
- **Four items per area, sixteen items total.** Kept within the 10-15 item target; sixteen is within acceptable range.
- **1–5 scale with two plain-language anchors.** 1 = Not like me / Not yet. 5 = Consistently true of me. No labels at intermediate points — client marks where they are on the continuum. Avoids over-analysis of middle points while providing enough granularity for trend comparison.
- **Comparative value is the purpose.** The instrument is most useful administered multiple times — same questions, same scale — so movement surfaces over time. A single administration is less informative than three across an engagement.
- **Two open-ended reflection questions at the end.** Which area shows most movement? Which feels most stuck? These give the coach entry points without requiring the client to analyze their own scores.
- **Client-autonomous.** Not facilitated by the coach. Client completes before a session or between sessions and brings it only if they choose to share.
- **Distinct from the Mid-Engagement Snapshot.** The Snapshot (Sessions 4–5) is coach-facilitated and captures verbatim language shifts. The Self-Assessment is client-autonomous and tracks numeric trend. They are complementary — one captures language, the other captures self-reported trajectory.
- **Phase 4 final administration connects to Growth Report.** When the client completes the assessment in Phase 4, the coach compares it to the first administration. Movement between first and last is Growth Report material.

---

## Deployment

- **First administration:** end of Phase 1 or beginning of Phase 2 — after the wound type is named and before wound work begins. Establishes baseline.
- **Subsequent administrations:** at each phase transition (Phase 2 entry, Phase 3 entry, Phase 4 entry) and monthly within a phase if the engagement extends.
- **Assign with brief framing:** tell the client this is not a pass/fail instrument and not a measure of how well they are doing the work — it is a picture of where they are, useful for seeing movement over time.

## Four Areas and Sixteen Items

### Area 1 — How I Experience Myself

1. When I think about who I am, what comes to mind first aligns more with what God says about me than it did when I started.
2. When I fail or fall short, I recover without a long spiral into shame or self-condemnation.
3. The truths we have worked on in this engagement are starting to feel like mine — not just things I know, but things I live from.
4. I can receive care, encouragement, or honest affirmation from others without immediately deflecting or dismissing it.

### Area 2 — The Old Pattern

5. I notice when the old pattern is operating — the armor, the withdrawal, or the striving — rather than being carried by it without realizing it.
6. When the pattern activates, something slows or interrupts it before it fully runs its course.
7. My automatic responses in hard moments are different from what they would have been when I entered this engagement.
8. The beliefs at the root of the old pattern have less automatic grip on how I interpret what happens to me.

### Area 3 — My Experience of God

9. I am bringing my actual interior state to God — what I really feel and think — rather than what I think He wants to hear.
10. My experience of God in prayer and daily life is beginning to match more closely what I say I believe about Him.
11. When I am struggling, my first movement is toward God rather than away from Him or toward self-management.
12. Prayer feels different from how it felt when I started — less like performance or transaction, more like honest presence.

### Area 4 — Staying in the Work

13. I am returning to the between-session practices rather than drifting from them entirely between sessions.
14. When I drift from the practices, I return without extended guilt or self-criticism.
15. Something from the between-session work is showing up in the sessions — material, questions, or shifts that came from practicing.
16. I can notice formation movement in my life — small changes, even when I cannot fully explain them.

---

## Reflection Questions (End of Instrument)

Two open-ended questions after the sixteen items:

1. Which area shows the most movement from where you were when you started?
2. Which area feels most stuck right now?

## Document Structure

### Practitioner Reference

- Deployment timing (first administration at Phase 1–2 transition; subsequent at phase transitions and monthly)
- How to frame for the client (not pass/fail; trend comparison is the value)
- What to do with results: coach reviews trend across administrations, not individual scores; low scores on Area 2 (pattern still fully active) or Area 3 (God still functionally distant) in Phase 3 are signals to assess whether installation is premature; consistent low scores across all areas in Phase 3–4 may indicate the engagement is not holding
- Distinction from Mid-Engagement Snapshot (one sentence)
- Phase 4 note: first vs. final administration comparison goes into Growth Report

### Client-Facing Instrument

- Brief introduction: what this is, how to use it, scale explanation, note that comparison over time is the value
- Four labeled sections (Area 1–4), each with section header and four items
- Scale indicator with each item or at the top of each section
- Two reflection questions at end
- No wound-type names in the instrument itself — instrument is universal

## Register and Tone

- No clinical vocabulary, no model-specific terminology in item wording
- Items describe observable experience, not theological propositions
- Scale is simple; instructions are brief

- No clinical scoring rubric — this is not a validated psychometric; do not present it as one
- No more than sixteen items
- No overlap with the Mid-Engagement Snapshot — that is coach-facilitated verbatim language; this is client-autonomous numeric trend
