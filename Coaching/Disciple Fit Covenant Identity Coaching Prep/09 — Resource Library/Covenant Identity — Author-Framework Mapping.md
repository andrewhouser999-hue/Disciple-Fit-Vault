# Covenant Identity — Author-Framework Mapping, Transmission Mechanisms & Community Diagnostic

*Maps the resource library to specific components of the Disciple Fit Covenant Identity framework. Also contains: a five-mechanism clinical framework for intergenerational lie transmission (with coaching protocols), and a six-limiter diagnostic and coachable sequence for community coaching.*

> **Practitioner note — Tripp:** Paul David Tripp appears multiple times in the mapping tables below. He has a perceived legalistic tendency in video and popular content — behavioral application can shade into striving framing. Prefer Powlison when available. Pair with grace-forward sources (Fitzpatrick, Bridges, Keller, Miller/Serge) to balance the application layer wherever Tripp is cited.

## Three-Part Session Prep

### Part 1 — Locate Lie Nukes

**Primary:**
- **Curt Thompson — *The Soul of Shame*** — shame is his diagnostic engine. Maps directly to "lies ready for circumstantial detonation" — Thompson shows how shame is always primed to fire under relational or performative stress. His IPNB frame explains *why* some lies are structurally load-bearing and others aren't.
- **Ed Welch — *When People Are Big and God is Small*** — fear of man as the root lie beneath most presenting lies. Gives a diagnostic lens for quickly locating what category of nuke is armed: identity-from-approval, safety-from-people, worth-from-performance.
- **Dan Allender — *The Healing Path*** — wound archaeology at the lie level. He traces how specific wounds produce specific lie structures. Directly supports the "agreements with lies" and "first morsel of doubt" detection work.

**Supporting:**
- Larry Crabb — *Inside Out* — beneath-surface diagnosis of what the client is actually protecting

---

### Part 2 — Eternal Foundation

**Primary:**
- **Walter Brueggemann — *The Covenanted Self*** — covenant as the ontological prior. His argument is that selfhood is *constituted* by covenant — not something you add to; it's what you stand in. Directly strengthens the "confirm salvation" step: salvation isn't just doctrinal assent, it's covenant entrance.
- **J. Todd Billings — *Union with Christ*** — the theological load-bearer the Identity Installer already assumes (Gal 3:27 is the hinge; Billings is the full argument behind that hinge). Grounds the "already true" framing with rigor.
- **Sinclair Ferguson — *Children of the Living God*** — adoption as the covenant identity most directly tied to daily scaffolding. Father-child as the relational structure of the eternal foundation.

**Supporting:**
- Michael Horton — *Introducing Covenant Theology* — narrative arc of covenant across Scripture; useful for helping clients understand *why* salvation places them in covenant, not just *that* it does
- O. Palmer Robertson — theological depth layer if you need to anchor any specific covenant claim

---

### Part 3 — Wound Awareness

**Primary:**
- **Curt Thompson — *Anatomy of the Soul*** — IPNB explains why wound terrain is inhospitable. Specifically: unintegrated memory, attachment disruption, and right-brain/left-brain fragmentation create the internal conditions that make truth hard to receive. This is the scientific layer for why wound work is prerequisite, not optional.
- **Dan Allender — *To Be Told*** — wound as embedded in story. The terrain isn't just emotional — it's narrative. Clients live out of a story they've constructed from the wound. Allender gives a method for reading that story diagnostically.
- **Larry Crabb — *Shattered Dreams*** — specifically addresses the wound beneath hope structures — the place where false scaffolding collapses. Maps to "internal terrain inhospitable to God-connection."

**Supporting:**
- Miroslav Volf — *Exclusion and Embrace* — for clients whose wound is relational exclusion, rejection, or betrayal; gives theological traction

## Structural Framework — Ephesians 4:22-24 (Renounce → Renew → Receive)

> *"...to put off your old self, which belongs to your former manner of life and is corrupt through deceitful desires, and to be renewed in the spirit of your minds, and to put on the new self, created after the likeness of God in true righteousness and holiness."* — Ephesians 4:22-24 (ESV)

This passage gives both documents their underlying three-movement shape: naming and laying down what's false (**Renounce**), encountering what is true (**Renew**), and embodying it — in the body and in action (**Receive**). The author-to-element mappings below address *which contributor grounds each individual element*; this section addresses *how the elements group* into that three-part movement.

## Intergenerational Transmission — Framework Mapping

### How Lies Transmit — Five Mechanisms

**Mechanism 1: Modeled Belief (The Absorbed Lie)**
The parent never says "you are unworthy of love." They live as if it's true about themselves. The child watches and concludes: this is what love looks like, this is what I can expect, this is how life works. The lie is never stated — it is *demonstrated*. Schema therapy calls these "early maladaptive schemas formed through vicarious learning."

Jeffrey Young's research shows that a parent carrying an *emotional deprivation schema* (no one will ever meet my deep emotional needs) will deprive the child emotionally — not out of malice but because the schema drives behavior. The child then forms their own emotional deprivation schema — confirmed by lived experience, not just inherited abstractly.

**Mechanism 2: The Narrative Transmission (The Family Story)**
Families carry master narratives — stories about who they are, what they can expect, what they deserve. "People like us don't get breaks." "The Smiths always struggle with money." "You know how the men in our family are." Dan McAdams' research (*The Redemptive Self*) shows these narratives function as identity frameworks the child inherits and lives into. The lie is encoded in story form.

**Mechanism 3: Relational Rules (The Implicit Contract)**
Unspoken family rules that function as lies: "Don't outshine the parent." "Loyalty means not changing." "Your emotions are a burden." "Conflict means abandonment." John Gottman's research documents how family emotional cultures are transmitted without ever being articulated — children absorb the rules of what emotions are safe, what love looks like, and what to expect from relationship.

**Mechanism 4: Attachment Transmission (The Physiological Pattern)**
Mary Main's Adult Attachment Interview research — the most replicated finding in attachment science — shows that a parent's narrative coherence about their own childhood predicts their child's attachment classification with 75% accuracy. What this means: if a parent has an unresolved, incoherent narrative about their own wounds, they will dysregulate the child's attachment system — not because they intend to, but because attuned co-regulation requires the parent to be regulated themselves.

Dan Siegel's IPNB work extends this: the child's nervous system is literally organized around the caregiver's nervous system. Physiological stress-response patterns — fight/flight thresholds, emotional dysregulation patterns — are transmitted through relational co-regulation before any cognitive lies are formed.

**Mechanism 5: Epigenetic Transmission (The Biological Encoding)**
Rachel Yehuda's research on Holocaust survivor descendants is the most cited: children of survivors show altered cortisol profiles — lower baseline cortisol and heightened stress reactivity — matching PTSD patterns, even without direct trauma exposure. The proposed mechanism: epigenetic modification of stress-response genes passed at conception.

This is contested in terms of precise mechanism, but the data on intergenerational physiological trauma transmission is robust enough to take seriously. It means some clients are carrying physiological stress-response patterns shaped by trauma they never experienced.

### What This Means for Lie Work

The Lie Archaeology question — *"Who taught you that — not intentionally, but who lived it in front of you?"* — already reaches the first generation. The intergenerational lens extends this:

**Extended Archaeology Questions:**
- "Did someone in your family before your parents carry this lie? What do you know about your grandparents' emotional world?"
- "Is this lie something your parent believed about themselves, or something they communicated to you specifically?"
- "When you imagine your parent at the age you are now, carrying what you know they carried — what lie were they living in?"

This matters because it does two things:
1. It **de-personalizes** the lie — the client realizes they received something, they didn't generate it. This loosens the identification.
2. It **activates compassion** — understanding the parent's wound changes the emotional charge of the received lie. Forgiveness becomes more accessible when the client sees the transmission mechanism.

**The Generational Agreement:**
The Agreement Audit asks clients to locate the moment of agreement. The generational extension: "Did you inherit this agreement, or did you make it yourself?" Some agreements were made by a grandparent and were simply operating as truth before the client was old enough to question them. Renouncing an inherited agreement is different from renouncing one you consciously made.

> **Agreement Audit — status resolved (2026-05-21):** The Agreement Audit is embedded within the [[Lie Eliminator — Client Workbook]] as Question 5 ("Can you locate the moment of agreement?") plus the renunciation note that follows it. No standalone document exists or is needed. The generational extension referenced above ("Did you inherit this agreement, or did you make it yourself?") is a deepening probe the coach adds verbally in Phase 2 — it does not require a separate document.

**The Spiritual Dimension:**
Neil Anderson (*The Bondage Breaker*) and Charles Kraft (*Deep Wounds, Deep Healing*) both address generational spiritual strongholds — footholds established by ancestors that create vulnerability in descendants. This isn't fatalism — Ezekiel 18 is clear that covenant responsibility is individual. But the pattern-as-foothold is real and worth addressing in prayer.

> **Contested framework:** The Anderson/Kraft generational strongholds model is contested by cessationists and many Reformed practitioners who object to the underlying spiritual warfare ontology (the mechanism by which ancestral sin creates spiritual footholds) and who question whether the prayer-based renunciation protocol assumes a charismatic theology of spiritual authority. This framework is included here as a clinical-pastoral option for practitioners who are operating within a tradition that affirms this kind of spiritual warfare practice — it is not a doctrinal commitment of the Covenant Identity model. Practitioners in cessationist or strictly Reformed contexts may omit the generational strongholds element entirely without disrupting the coaching sequence; the psychological mechanisms (Mechanisms 1–4) do not depend on it.

The gospel's claim here is specific: Romans 5 argues that just as Adam's one act of sin transmitted condemnation to all, Christ's one act of righteousness transmits life to all who are in him. The covenant solution to generational transmission is covenantal solidarity with Christ — which is why union with Christ (Billings, Ferguson) is load-bearing for this work, not decorative.

**Chain-Breaking:**
Siegel's concept of "earned security" — adults who have *processed* their attachment history can interrupt the cycle even if they had insecure attachment. They don't pass it to their children at the same rate. This is directly hopeful for clients who are parents: the coaching work isn't just for them. The healing they do now changes what they transmit.

> **Coaching-scope qualification:** Siegel's earned security is clinically established in the context of psychotherapy-level processing and narrative coherence work. The claim that coaching-level processing produces the same degree of transmission interruption is plausible and hopeful but not clinically established. Frame this to clients as a directionally grounded hope — the direction of the claim is true; the degree to which coaching-level work achieves the full clinical effect is not confirmed. Overclaiming here does clients a disservice.

## Community Coaching — Framework Mapping

### What Typically Limits Client Commitment

**The internal barriers — what coaching owns:**

1. **Shame-based avoidance** — being fully known in community triggers the same shame that drives lie structures. The client doesn't avoid community in general; they avoid being *known* in community. This is directly coachable via the existing lie work.

2. **Attachment pattern replay** — the client's attachment history (anxious: enmeshment fear or people-pleasing spiral; avoidant: self-sufficiency as identity; disorganized: community feels simultaneously necessary and dangerous) is running in their communal relationships. Naming the pattern is the first coachable move.

3. **Idealized community expectation** — Bonhoeffer (*Life Together*) identifies this as the primary destroyer of real Christian community. The client is waiting for a community that matches their vision of what community should be, and they disengage before real belonging can form. His line is directly coachable: *"He who loves his dream of a community more than the Christian community itself becomes a destroyer of the latter."* Ask the client: what are you waiting for before you commit?

4. **Previous wound** — church hurt, betrayal, abandonment. The lie installed by that wound ("community isn't safe," "I will be rejected," "my needs will be dismissed") is running the avoidance. This is Lie Eliminator work applied to community specifically.

5. **Identity incongruence** — who the client presents in community doesn't feel congruent with who they actually are. They wear a mask and then feel isolated despite attending. The coachable question: what would it cost to show up as yourself? What lie is protecting the mask?

6. **Theology of individualism** — many clients have absorbed a Christianity that is entirely vertical (me and God) with community as optional add-on. The covenant corrective (Fee, Ferguson, Hauerwas) reframes community as intrinsic to new-covenant identity, not peripheral. This is coachable as a belief-level intervention.

### The Coachable Sequence

1. **Diagnose the limiter** — which of the six above is primary? (Lie? Attachment? Wound? Idealization? Incongruence? Theology?)
2. **Name the covenant stake** — community isn't optional; it's part of what it means to be in Christ. Not as guilt but as invitation: there's an identity category you're not inhabiting.
3. **Reduce the threshold** — "What's the smallest real commitment?" — not full membership, not leading a small group, just one honest conversation.
4. **Process the previous wound if present** — the wound is blocking re-entry; address it directly before expecting the client to risk again.
5. **Evidence-build** — every small belonging moment is evidence for the identity claim "I am someone who belongs." This feeds the My Evidence section of Identity Installer.
