# Covenantal Identity — Author & Resource Reference

*Books and learning material connecting biblical covenantal truths to personal identity for everyday living.*

---

## Reading Priority Guide

| Tier | Criteria | Authors |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Read First** | Direct session impact; most framework-critical | Thompson, Willard, Allender, Barton, Anderson (*Victory*), Bonhoeffer, Foster |
| **Tier 2 — Read Next** | High value; builds depth and range | Brueggemann, Billings, Ferguson, Crabb, Welch, Tripp, Benner, Murray, Wright, Siegel, Thomas, Tozer, Cloud/Townsend, Payne |
| **Reference Only** | Depth when needed; academic or specialized | Robertson, Horton, Volf, Fee, Peterson, Young, Bowen, Anderson (*Bondage Breaker*), Yehuda, C. Wright, Plantinga, Hauerwas |

---

## Most Directly On-Target

**Walter Brueggemann — *The Covenanted Self: Explorations in Law and Covenant***
The closest match to your exact question. Argues covenant relationship is the constitutive foundation of selfhood — not a doctrine to believe but a relational reality to inhabit.
*Priority: Tier 2*

**Neil Anderson — *Victory Over the Darkness***
Practical, widely used. Lists identity statements rooted in union with Christ. Less theological depth, high everyday accessibility. Widely used in discipleship contexts.
*Priority: Tier 1*

**Curt Thompson — *Anatomy of the Soul* and *The Soul of Shame***
Neuroscience + interpersonal neurobiology + theology of shame/identity. Directly relevant to the CBC framework. Shame as a disruption of covenant belonging; healing through attunement and truth. Very strong fit for this work.
*Priority: Tier 1*

---

## Strong Covenant-Identity Connections

**N.T. Wright — *After You Believe* and *The Day the Revolution Began***
Wright ties new covenant participation to virtue formation and everyday character — not just positional identity but the formation of a new-covenant humanity. Academic but readable.
*Priority: Tier 2*

**Dan Allender — *To Be Told* and *The Healing Path***
Narrative + Scripture + soul wound work. Identity formed through reading your own story with God as the covenantal author. Very compatible with the session prep framework.
*Priority: Tier 1*

**Larry Crabb — *Inside Out*, *Soul Talk*, *Shattered Dreams***
Deep soul work rooted in covenant longing. Argues that the ache underneath dysfunction is a covenant-shaped hunger. Strong integration of psychology and theology.
*Priority: Tier 2*

**J. Todd Billings — *Union with Christ***
Scholarly but accessible. Grounds identity transformation in covenantal union — not primarily behavioral but participatory. Strong Reformed framework.
*Priority: Tier 2*

**Sinclair Ferguson — *Children of the Living God***
Adoption as the central covenant identity — sonship/daughtership as the frame for all Christian living. Practical and pastoral.
*Priority: Tier 2*

---

## Everyday Application (Less Explicitly Covenant, High Practical Value)

**Paul David Tripp — *Instruments in the Redeemer's Hands* and *New Morning Mercies***
Gospel-centered change. Connects how people think about themselves to what they believe about God's covenant faithfulness. Highly practical for coaching.
*Priority: Tier 2*

**Ed Welch — *When People Are Big and God is Small***
Identity distortion rooted in fear of man vs. covenant fear of God. Useful for clients whose identity is other-defined.
*Priority: Tier 2*

**Tim Chester — *You Can Change***
Accessible. Connects gospel (covenant promise) to behavioral and identity change. Good for clients new to this framework.
*Priority: Tier 2*

---

## Academic Foundations (For Framework Development)

**O. Palmer Robertson — *The Christ of the Covenants***
Classic Reformed treatment of the covenant structure across Scripture. Dense but foundational if you want to anchor the framework theologically.
*Priority: Reference Only*

**Michael Horton — *Introducing Covenant Theology***
More accessible than Robertson. Good for understanding how covenant structures the whole biblical narrative and what that means for belonging and identity.
*Priority: Reference Only*

**Miroslav Volf — *Exclusion and Embrace***
Identity, otherness, and belonging through a covenantal/trinitarian lens. More theological-philosophical, but rich for understanding identity formation under pressure.
*Priority: Reference Only*

## Sharpest Trio for This Framework

**Brueggemann** (covenant = selfhood) + **Curt Thompson** (neuroscience of shame + covenant belonging) + **Dan Allender** (narrative + wound + God as covenantal author). These three together build the theological-psychological-pastoral framework most directly.

## Holy Spirit Partnership — Premium Authors

**Dallas Willard — *Renovation of the Heart*, *Hearing God*, *The Spirit of the Disciplines***
The most rigorous thinker on Spirit-cooperative transformation available. *Renovation* is his framework for how the Spirit works across every dimension of the person — mind, will, body, emotions, social world, soul. Not charismatic fluff; philosophically careful, exegetically grounded. *Hearing God* is specifically on cultivating the interactive voice of God in daily life. This is the anchor author for HS partnership in this system.
*Priority: Tier 1*

**Gordon Fee — *God's Empowering Presence***
The most academically rigorous treatment of the Spirit in Paul's letters. Fee's argument: for Paul, the Spirit is not peripheral to Christian life — the Spirit *is* the experienced reality of new covenant living. Every other author builds on what Fee establishes exegetically. If you want the theological grounding for why HS partnership isn't optional, this is it.
*Priority: Reference Only*

**Ruth Haley Barton — *Sacred Rhythms*, *Strengthening the Soul of Your Leader*, *Invitation to Solitude and Silence***
Barton is the clearest practitioner-writer on discernment and Spirit-receptive living. Her work on solitude directly addresses the pre-session preparation of the coach's own soul. Her discernment framework (what is the Spirit already doing in this person?) is a coaching diagnostic method in its own right.
*Priority: Tier 1*

**Richard Foster — *Celebration of Discipline*, *Prayer: Finding the Heart's True Home***
Foster is the entry point to spiritual disciplines as Spirit-access channels. Directly relevant to "prayerful work as standard minimum" — he provides the practical architecture. His categories (inward, outward, corporate disciplines) map cleanly to the framework layers.
*Priority: Tier 1*

**David Benner — *Surrender to Love*, *The Gift of Being Yourself*, *Sacred Companions***
Benner bridges depth psychology and Christian spiritual direction better than almost anyone. *The Gift of Being Yourself* directly addresses identity and Spirit-presence in a way that complements the Identity Installer. His concept of *surrender as the condition for transformation* maps to the "putting off" element. *Sacred Companions* is on Spirit-directed spiritual friendship — relevant to the community gap.
*Priority: Tier 2*

**Leanne Payne — *The Healing Presence*, *Restoring the Christian Soul***
Payne is the most rigorous practitioner on healing prayer and Spirit agency in identity and wound work. More charismatic than Willard, but not shallow — she's theologically careful and clinically experienced. Her work specifically addresses Spirit-led lie renunciation and identity installation. The most direct practitioner equivalent to what is being built here.
*Priority: Tier 2*

> [!important] **Key Decision Point — Leanne Payne**
> Her tier depends on a practice decision you haven't made yet: **Is healing prayer a standard session element or an occasional one?**
> - If occasional → Tier 2 is correct, read when ready
> - If standard → move her to Tier 1 and read before you formalize your session structure
> This decision shapes how you design the Declaration step in the Lie Eliminator and whether the Spirit Partnership layer has a distinct healing prayer protocol.

**Andrew Murray — *Absolute Surrender*, *The Spirit of Christ*, *Abide in Christ***
Murray's central contribution: surrender is the *mechanism* of Spirit fullness. The client who still controls their narrative, their lie, their wound, is not yet positioned for Spirit work. Murray makes this coachable. His abiding framework (John 15) is covenant participation in practice — vine-branch as daily operating posture.
*Priority: Tier 2*

**A.W. Tozer — *The Pursuit of God*, *The Divine Conquest***
*The Divine Conquest* (also titled *He Dwells Within*) is specifically on how the Spirit displaces the self-life. Tozer names self-will as the root that the Spirit must conquer — this is the spiritual layer beneath schema and lie work. Not a system, but prophetic clarity that sharpens how to articulate what's happening in deep transformation.
*Priority: Tier 2 — both books are under 150 pages; high return on short investment*

**Gary Thomas — *Sacred Pathways***
Thomas identifies 9 spiritual temperaments — the distinct pathways through which different people most naturally encounter God (naturalist, sensate, traditionalist, ascetic, activist, caregiver, enthusiast, contemplative, intellectual). Directly diagnostic for coaching: clients don't all access the Spirit through the same door. Mismatched practice recommendations are one reason spiritual disciplines don't stick.
*Priority: Tier 2*

**Eugene Peterson — *Working the Angles*, *The Contemplative Pastor***
Peterson's contribution is attentiveness — the Spirit-responsive life as counter to the distracted life. Not a systematic Spirit theology, but practical wisdom on cultivating the kind of interior quiet that makes Spirit discernment possible.
*Priority: Reference Only*
