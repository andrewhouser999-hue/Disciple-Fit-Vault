# Covenant Identity — New Covenant Promises — Comprehensive Reference

*Practitioner reference — covenant promises organized by the ten covenant identity layers*

---

## Purpose

This document serves a single diagnostic function: given a known severed layer, which covenant promises speak most directly to the schema that layer counters?

The [[Covenant Identity — New Covenant Promise Map 1]] organizes promises by felt lie class — the client's entry point. This document organizes the same promise territory by the ten covenant identity layers — the practitioner's diagnostic entry point. Use it in session prep or during schema excavation when you know which layer is most severed and need targeted promise material.

**Companion routing:** Each layer entry includes a routing note pointing to the Promise Map 1 category where the client receives the full worksheet treatment of this promise material between sessions.

**Promise format (per entry):**
- **(a) Text** — exact reference and brief quote (ESV)
- **(b) Covenant source** — which covenant; New Covenant original or fulfilled/transferred
- **(c) Mechanism and schema connection** — how this promise addresses the specific schema this layer counters

## Layer 1 — Image-Bearer
*Declaration: I bear the image of God; my worth is not earned*
*Schema countered: Defectiveness/Shame — "I am fundamentally flawed"*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a)** Genesis 1:26–27 — *"Let us make man in our image... So God created man in his own image, in the image of God he created him."*
**(b)** Pre-covenantal — God's creational act prior to the covenant structure. Applied under the New Covenant because Christ is the image of God (Col 1:15) and believers are being renewed into that image (Col 3:10).
**(c)** The defectiveness schema roots worth in behavioral evidence. The image declaration roots worth in origin — what was true before performance existed. The New Covenant restores and renews the image rather than replacing it, meaning the "image-bearer" assessment is not canceled by the fall but is the target of redemptive restoration.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a)** 2 Corinthians 3:18 — *"We all, with unveiled face, beholding the glory of the Lord, are being transformed into the same image from one degree of glory to another."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original. Transformation into the image of Christ has no Old Covenant parallel at this level of individual access and Spirit-driven process.
**(c)** The shame schema treats the person as a fixed, defective quantity. This promise names an ongoing transformation toward the image the person was originally designed to bear. The direction of movement counters the schema's verdict of permanence.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a)** Psalm 139:13–14 — *"For you formed my inward parts; you knitted me together in my mother's womb. I praise you, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made."*
**(b)** Mosaic-era wisdom — cross-covenantal; the Creator's direct involvement in formation holds across covenant transitions.
**(c)** The defectiveness schema attacks the person's fundamental construction. The Creator's own verdict — "fearfully and wonderfully made" — was pronounced over the same person the schema says is fundamentally flawed. These are competing verdicts from different sources; only one has authority.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a)** Colossians 3:10 — *"Put on the new self, which is being renewed in knowledge after the image of its creator."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; renewal in the image of the creator is Spirit-mediated.
**(c)** The shame schema says the flaw is constitutional. Colossians 3:10 names the image as the *goal* of the Spirit's renewal — not a foreign ideal but the person's own created design being restored. The schema fights against what God is actively working toward.

---

## Layer 2 — Known and Chosen
*Declaration: God sees everything about me and chose me anyway*
*Schema countered: Shame + Abandonment — "If people really knew me, they would leave"*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a)** Psalm 139:1–4 — *"O LORD, you have searched me and known me... you discern my thoughts from afar... you are acquainted with all my ways."*
**(b)** Mosaic-era — applied under the New Covenant through Christ, who knows persons fully (John 2:25) and through whom believers are fully known to the Father.
**(c)** The abandonment schema operates on a conditional premise: *if you knew me, you would leave.* This promise removes the conditionality — the knowing is complete, not partial. The schema's feared disclosure has already occurred, and the response is continued presence, not departure.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a)** Ephesians 1:4 — *"He chose us in him before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and blameless before him."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original. The election in Christ precedes creation; it is not a response to anything the person did or failed to do.
**(c)** The schema's logic requires being known *before* being chosen, then discovering the choice was a mistake. Ephesians 1:4 inverts this: the choosing occurred before creation, before knowledge of performance. There is nothing to discover that could retroactively invalidate it.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a)** John 15:16 — *"You did not choose me, but I chose you."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original, spoken by Jesus at the covenant's inauguration.
**(c)** The abandonment schema attributes the relationship's continuation to the client's effort — which means performance failure could reverse it. This promise locates initiation entirely in Christ. The relationship cannot be undone by the client's failure to maintain what the client did not originate.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a)** Jeremiah 31:3 — *"I have loved you with an everlasting love; therefore I have continued my faithfulness to you."*
**(b)** New Covenant — spoken in the Jeremiah 31 context that explicitly announces the New Covenant (vv.31–34); the everlasting love is the covenant's emotional ground.
**(c)** *Everlasting* forecloses the schema's anticipated ending. The love that encompasses complete knowledge is also the love that does not terminate — by covenantal definition.

---

*Route client to Promise Map 1 → **Category 1 (Identity)** primarily; **Category 2 (Access)** for the fear-of-approach dimension.*

## Layer 3 — New Creation
*Declaration: I am a new creation; the old is genuinely gone*
*Schema countered: Failure/Permanent Defectiveness — "I'll never really change"*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a)** 2 Corinthians 5:17 — *"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old has passed away; behold, the new has come."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original. This language has no prior covenant parallel.
**(c)** The permanent defectiveness schema locates the problem in the person's unchangeable constitution. This promise names a category change — not moral improvement of the old self but a new beginning of the same order as the original creation. The schema is contesting the wrong verdict.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a)** Romans 6:6 — *"We know that our old self was crucified with him in order that the body of sin might be brought to nothing, so that we would no longer be enslaved to sin."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; union with Christ in his death is the operative mechanism.
**(c)** The schema treats the sin-pattern as the person's permanent identity. Romans 6:6 names the old self's death as an accomplished fact — *was crucified*, past tense. Ongoing struggle does not contradict this; it occurs in someone whose old identity has already died, not someone constitutionally enslaved.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a)** Philippians 1:6 — *"I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ."*
**(b)** New Covenant — fulfills the Abrahamic covenant-faithfulness principle through Christ; the one who initiates is obligated by his character to complete.
**(c)** The "I'll never change" schema attacks trajectory — the absence of visible progress as evidence of permanent stasis. Completion is a covenantal certainty, not a performance-dependent outcome. The agent of change is God, not the client's will, which means the schema's evidence base (the client's failed efforts) is measuring the wrong thing.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a)** 1 John 3:2 — *"We know that when he appears we shall be like him, because we shall see him as he is."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; eschatological conformity to Christ is unique to New Covenant promise.
**(c)** The defectiveness schema stakes everything on present evidence. This promise grounds certainty in a future reality already secured by Christ's resurrection as firstfruits. The person who "will never change" will, at the appointed time, be fully conformed to the image of Christ — by sight, by the Spirit, by resurrection.

---

## Layer 4 — Adopted Child
*Declaration: I am an adopted child with full inheritance rights*
*Schema countered: Subjugation/Conditional Worth — "I have to earn my place; my needs don't matter"*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a)** Galatians 4:4–7 — *"God sent forth his Son... to redeem those who were under the law, so that we might receive adoption as sons... So you are no longer a slave, but a son, and if a son, then an heir through God."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; redemption from slavery-to-law and the granting of sonship is what Christ's coming specifically accomplished.
**(c)** The subjugation schema operates through earned standing. Galatians 4:7 names the opposite movement: out of the slave-structure and into sonship. The inheritance is held by right of adoption, not performance of duty.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a)** Romans 8:15 — *"You have received the Spirit of adoption as sons, by whom we cry, 'Abba! Father!'"*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; the Spirit of adoption given at Pentecost enables the *Abba* cry as the Spirit's own testimony to the believer's status.
**(c)** *Abba* is the language of a child addressing a Father from whom they expect to be heard and cared for. The Spirit who enables this cry is countering the "my needs don't matter" schema directly at the level of how the person relates to God — with access, not performance.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a)** 1 John 3:1 — *"See what kind of love the Father has given us, that we should be called children of God; and so we are."*
**(b)** New Covenant — the Father's declaration of the believer's status; original.
**(c)** "I have to earn my place" is the subjugation schema's defining statement. *"And so we are"* is the Father's counter-declaration — not aspirational, not conditional, not earned. The title is given. The earning project is directed at a standing that already exists.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a)** Ephesians 1:5 — *"He predestined us for adoption to himself as sons through Jesus Christ, according to the purpose of his will."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; the adoption was predestined before creation.
**(c)** The schema ties worth to what the person does. Predestination to adoption ties worth to what God decided before performance was possible. The schema has no purchase on a standing that was settled in eternity.

---

*Route client to Promise Map 1 → **Category 1 (Identity)** for the sonship treatment; **Category 2 (Access)** for the "needs don't matter / can't approach" dimension.*

## Layer 5 — Co-heir with Christ
*Declaration: The full inheritance is shared with me*
*Schema countered: Deprivation/Scarcity — "There's never enough; I'll be left without"*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a)** Romans 8:17 — *"And if children, then heirs — heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ."*
**(b)** New Covenant — fulfills and exceeds the Abrahamic inheritance promise; the inheritance is co-heirship with the Son, not a portion of a limited estate.
**(c)** The scarcity schema operates on a fixed-resource model — what exists could run out. Co-heirship with Christ is sharing what is inexhaustible by definition. The schema has no failure mode to invoke.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a)** Romans 8:32 — *"He who did not spare his own Son but gave him up for us all, how will he not also with him graciously give us all things?"*
**(b)** New Covenant — echoes the Abrahamic covenant (Genesis 22:16); Paul moves from the greater gift to guarantee the lesser.
**(c)** The deprivation schema anticipates withholding. This promise moves in the opposite direction: the hardest thing to give has already been given freely. The character that gave the Son cannot become stingy on lesser things. The schema's anticipation contradicts the demonstrated nature of the Giver.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a)** Ephesians 1:11 — *"In him we have obtained an inheritance, having been predestined according to the purpose of him who works all things according to the counsel of his will."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; the inheritance is obtained in Christ, secured by predestination.
**(c)** The scarcity schema fears that the inheritance might not materialize. An inheritance secured by God's predestined purpose and "the counsel of his will" cannot be blocked by any circumstance the schema anticipates.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a)** 2 Corinthians 9:8 — *"And God is able to make all grace abound to you, so that having all sufficiency in all things at all times, you may abound in every good work."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; provision through grace, not law-keeping.
**(c)** *"All grace... all sufficiency... all things at all times"* — the repetition directly addresses the schema's generalization. Not some grace, not sufficient sometimes, not most things. The promise is structured to leave no gap for the "but not enough for me" move.

---

*Route client to Promise Map 1 → **Category 3 (Provision)** for the supply and sufficiency treatment; **Category 7 (Future)** for the inheritance and secured-deposit dimension.*

## Layer 6 — Indwelt
*Declaration: God's presence lives in me; I am not alone*
*Schema countered: Abandonment/Isolation — "I am ultimately alone"*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a)** John 14:16–17 — *"I will ask the Father, and he will give you another Helper, to be with you forever... He dwells with you and will be in you."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original. The permanent, personal indwelling of the Spirit is unique to the New Covenant; under the Old Covenant the Spirit came upon specific people for specific seasons.
**(c)** The isolation schema's defining feature is *ultimate* aloneness — the conviction that no connection is truly durable. *Forever* and *in you* are the specific counters: the presence is permanent and internal, not seasonal or merely adjacent. The schema cannot locate a gap where the isolation is finally realized.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a)** John 14:18 — *"I will not leave you as orphans; I will come to you."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original, spoken at the covenant's inauguration.
**(c)** *Orphan* is the abandonment schema's experiential territory — the one left without a parent, without belonging. Jesus speaks directly into that experience and names the opposite. The Spirit (vv.23–26) is the fulfillment, not a lesser substitute.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a)** Romans 8:9 — *"You... are not in the flesh but in the Spirit, if in fact the Spirit of God dwells in you."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; the Spirit's indwelling defines the New Covenant believer.
**(c)** The isolation schema treats the person's experience of aloneness as the accurate report on reality. This promise names a concurrent reality that experience may not register: the Spirit dwells in the person. The felt isolation is not the complete description of the person's condition.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a)** Matthew 28:20 — *"I am with you always, to the end of the age."*
**(b)** New Covenant — the Great Commission's closing promise; perpetual presence through the Spirit.
**(c)** *Always* and *to the end of the age* address the schema's expectation that presence will eventually fail. There is no season, no failure, no spiritual condition in which the promise becomes inoperative. The isolation schema is predicting an outcome the promise forecloses.

---

*Route client to Promise Map 1 → **Category 2 (Access)** for the presence and approach treatment; **Category 4 (Protection)** for the security dimension of God's ongoing presence.*

## Layer 7 — Standing Secure
*Declaration: My standing before God is secure right now*
*Schema countered: Vulnerability/Instability — "The ground could disappear at any moment"*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a)** John 10:28–29 — *"I give them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand. My Father, who has given them to me, is greater than all, and no one is able to snatch them out of the Father's hand."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original.
**(c)** The instability schema expects a failure mode — a condition under which the ground gives way. This promise enumerates the security in two directions (Son's hand, Father's hand) and names the operative standard: *no one.* Not a list of exceptions but an exhaustive closure. The schema's feared scenario has no mechanism to execute.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a)** Romans 8:38–39 — *"Neither death nor life, nor angels nor rulers, nor things present nor things to come, nor powers, nor height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; Paul exhausts every conceivable category of threat.
**(c)** The vulnerability schema operates by identifying what might finally break through. Paul's list is structured to eliminate every candidate the schema could produce. *Will be able* is legal language: the separation is not merely unlikely but impossible.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a)** Hebrews 7:25 — *"He is able to save to the uttermost those who draw near to God through him, since he always lives to make intercession for them."*
**(b)** New Covenant — supersedes the Levitical priesthood; Christ's intercession is perpetual, not seasonal.
**(c)** The instability schema fears that maintenance of standing requires the client's continuous faithfulness. Christ's continuous intercession removes that requirement: the standing is being actively maintained by someone who cannot fail. The client's performance is not what the standing depends on.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a)** Jude 24 — *"Now to him who is able to keep you from stumbling and to present you blameless before the presence of his glory with great joy."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; God's active keeping toward the final presentation.
**(c)** *Able to keep* — the capacity is God's, not the client's. The vulnerability schema projects the client's own incapacity onto the security structure. The one maintaining the security is not the client; the schema is assigning responsibility to the wrong person.

---

## Layer 8 — Loved as the Father Loves the Son
*Declaration: The quality of God's love for me equals his love for the Son*
*Schema countered: Emotional Deprivation — "No one will ever truly see or meet my needs"*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a)** John 17:23 — *"You have loved them even as you loved me."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original, spoken in the high-priestly prayer at the covenant's inauguration.
**(c)** The deprivation schema is about the quality and completeness of love received. *"Even as you loved me"* sets the measure at the highest possible point: the Father's love for the eternal Son. There is no higher standard, no greater completeness the schema could point to as what the client is missing.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a)** John 17:26 — *"The love with which you have loved me may be in them, and I in them."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; the Trinitarian love is not merely aimed at the believer but enters and indwells them.
**(c)** The deprivation schema locates the problem in inadequate love from outside — no one reaches far enough in. This promise moves the love inside: the love with which the Father loved the Son is *in them.* The schema's deficit is addressed not by better external provision but by internal presence of the love itself.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a)** Romans 5:5 — *"God's love has been poured into our hearts through the Holy Spirit who has been given to us."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; the Spirit's indwelling is the mechanism of love's delivery.
**(c)** The emotional deprivation schema is a felt deficit — not only an intellectual conclusion but an experienced absence. *Poured into our hearts* names the delivery channel as the heart — the same level at which the deprivation is experienced. The Spirit meets the schema at the level where it operates.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a)** 1 John 4:9–10 — *"In this is love, not that we have loved God but that he loved us and sent his Son to be the propitiation for our sins."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; love is defined by the cross, not by the client's affective experience of it.
**(c)** The deprivation schema measures love by felt reception — the absence of felt love is taken as evidence of actual absence. This promise defines love by the most costly act in history. The love is not contingent on being felt to be real; it is established by what was done.

---

*Route client to Promise Map 1 → **Category 1 (Identity)** for the being-known dimension; **Category 2 (Access)** for the Father's attentiveness and presence.*

## Layer 9 — Sent
*Declaration: I am sent; my life carries specific purpose*
*Schema countered: Meaninglessness/Purposelessness — "My life doesn't matter; nothing I do counts"*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a)** Ephesians 2:10 — *"For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand, that we should walk in them."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; the good works were prepared *beforehand*, in eternity, as part of the new creation reality.
**(c)** The purposelessness schema locates meaning in self-construction — the client must generate significance. This promise locates purpose in prior preparation: the works were ready before the client was. The schema's conclusion would require God to have prepared works for someone whose life doesn't matter, which is a contradiction.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a)** 2 Corinthians 5:20 — *"We are ambassadors for Christ, God making his appeal through us."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; the ambassador role belongs to the New Covenant age of gospel proclamation.
**(c)** *God making his appeal through us* — the client is not manufacturing significance; they are the vehicle through which God himself speaks. The meaninglessness schema fights against a purpose that originates outside the client and is maintained by the One who delegated it.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a)** 1 Corinthians 12:7 — *"To each is given the manifestation of the Spirit for the common good."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; universal Spirit gifting at Pentecost, fulfilling Joel 2:28–29.
**(c)** *To each* closes the schema's favorite loophole: "everyone else was given something, but I wasn't." The distribution is universal, the Spirit's judgment (not the client's self-assessment) determines what was given, and the body's common good depends on what each member carries.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a)** Acts 1:8 — *"You will be my witnesses in Jerusalem and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the end of the earth."*
**(b)** New Covenant — Spirit-empowered witness is the first act of the New Covenant community's life.
**(c)** *You will be my witnesses* is a declaration, not a conditional offer. The schema says the client's contribution doesn't count; this promise assigns a specific vocational identity — witness — that is not contingent on the client's assessment of their own significance.

---

*Route client to Promise Map 1 → **Category 5 (Purpose)** for the full calling and gifting treatment.*

## Layer 10 — Formation Process
*Declaration: I am in a divinely-overseen formation process*
*Schema countered: Failure/Impatience with growth — "I should be further along; I'm a disappointment"*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a)** Philippians 1:6 — *"I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ."*
**(b)** New Covenant — fulfills the Abrahamic covenant-faithfulness principle through Christ; the one who initiates is obligated by his character to complete.
**(c)** The schema measures formation against an expected pace and finds the client behind. This promise transfers the measure to God's own completion timeline and names the agent of completion as God, not the client. The schema is grading a test the client was never responsible for passing.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a)** 2 Corinthians 3:18 — *"We all, with unveiled face, beholding the glory of the Lord, are being transformed into the same image from one degree of glory to another. For this comes from the Lord who is the Spirit."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; Spirit-driven progressive transformation is unique to the New Covenant.
**(c)** *From one degree of glory to another* names formation as incremental by design — not a failure of pace but the Spirit's own architecture. The impatience schema treats gradual progress as inadequacy. This promise names gradual progress as the intended mode.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a)** Hebrews 12:2 — *"Looking to Jesus, the founder and perfecter of our faith."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; Christ as the one who both initiates and completes faith.
**(c)** *Perfecter* — the one who brings faith to its complete form is not the client. The schema holds the client responsible for completing what only Christ can complete. The client is a participant in the formation, not its primary agent.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a)** Romans 8:28–30 — *"For those whom he foreknew he also predestined to be conformed to the image of his Son... And those whom he predestined he also called... those whom he called he also justified... those whom he justified he also glorified."*
**(b)** New Covenant — original; the golden chain from predestination to glorification.
**(c)** The schema anticipates that the process might stall or be reversed. The golden chain forecloses that scenario: every link holds, and the final link (glorification) is spoken in the past tense — it is that certain. The formation will arrive at its destination because predestination guarantees the full chain.

---

*Route client to Promise Map 1 → **Category 4 (Protection)** for the preserving-power dimension; **Category 7 (Future)** for the eschatological completion and trajectory.*
