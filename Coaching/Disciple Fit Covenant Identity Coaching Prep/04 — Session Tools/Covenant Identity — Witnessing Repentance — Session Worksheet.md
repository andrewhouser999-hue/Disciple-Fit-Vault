# Covenant Identity — Witnessing Repentance — Session Worksheet

*Coach-facilitated in-session tool — read aloud and prayed together with the client present throughout. Not a take-home worksheet; the client does not process this alone. Section II is short by design: one guided arc, roughly 10-15 minutes inside a session, not a standalone document.*

## Section I — Practitioner Facilitation Guide

### Deployment Gate

Use only when all of the following are true:
- The client is at **Stage 3 or later** — a false covenant object or false identity has already been named (Affections Discovery, and Character Wound Tool if applicable, have already run). This tool distinguishes act-level from state-level sin *inside* material that's already been diagnosed. It is not an entry point and does not replace that funnel.
- Alliance trust is established. This is not a Session 1-3 tool.
- A specific behavior or pattern has surfaced that the client is already moralizing globally ("I'm disgusting," "I'll never be free") rather than naming specifically.

**Do not deploy** if the client's presentation is closer to lament or unexplained distance from God than to a known, nameable pattern — route to [[Covenant Identity — Lament Practice Guide]] instead, for the same reason the [[Covenant Identity — What Blocks Hearing God's Voice]] guide flags this distinction. Do not deploy on a first pass with a strongly Hermit-typed client until the coach has independently confirmed the client can tolerate this content without global collapse — go slower, split across two sessions if needed.

**Referral check:** if the disclosed pattern is compulsive or escalating, check against [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]] and the Character Wound Tool's clinical triggers before proceeding as a purely affections-diagnostic case.

### What This Tool Does and Does Not Do

**Does:** creates one in-session moment of invited witnessing followed by confession and a directional turn — operationalizing the Mechanism document's core sequence (witness before confess; turn toward, not just away from) as something the client actually prays, not just something the coach understands.

**Does not:** diagnose the client's wound type to their face, categorize their sin for them, replace ongoing declaration or covenant-truth work, or function as a homework assignment. If wound-pattern material surfaces here, bring it back into the ongoing diagnostic work — this tool does not resolve it on the spot.

### What Stays Coach-Side — Do Not Hand the Client

- The wound-type sin anchor table (Warrior/Hermit/False Noble) from the Mechanism doc. Naming a client's type to their face in this context risks exactly the identity-collapse this tool exists to prevent.
- The Judas/Peter contrast. Too easily over-identified with by a shame-prone client without the coach's real-time narrative control.
- Romans 7 exegesis. Read by clients either as fatalism ("even Paul couldn't stop") or as an excuse ("so it's not my fault") without careful framing. The coach holds this; the client does not need the argument, only its fruit.

### The In-Session Redirect (keep this live in your head throughout)

If the client's language goes global ("I'm disgusting," "I'm just broken") at any point in Section II, pause the sequence and say: *"That moved from what you did to who you are — I want to come back to the specific thing. What's the actual, specific thing — not the verdict on you, the thing itself?"* Do not proceed to the next movement until the client can name something bounded. This is not a formality — the rest of the sequence does not work if run on top of global shame language.

### Per-Type Coach Notes

**Warrior** may move through this quickly and competently, treating it as a task to complete well. Watch for confession delivered like a status report. Slow the pace deliberately; don't let fluency substitute for actual exposure.

**Hermit** is the highest-risk type for this tool — most likely to let the witnessing prayer slide into global self-condemnation rather than specific naming. Watch closely during Movement 2. If shame language appears, use the redirect above immediately rather than letting the prayer continue.

**False Noble** may pray this beautifully and mean it least. Watch for polished language that performs vulnerability rather than risking it. If the language is notably eloquent and the affect is flat, ask directly: *"Say that again, plainer — what actually happened?"*

## Section II — In-Session Guide

*Coach: read the italicized lines aloud or in your own words. Client-facing Scripture is set in blockquotes — read these together, slowly.*

### Opening

*"What we're about to do isn't about producing the right feeling or arriving somewhere by the end of this. God already sees what we're about to look at together — this is just us catching up to what's already true and already known. The turning, if it happens, is His to bring. Our job is just to look honestly."*

### Movement 1 — Naming the Specific Thing

Ask: *"Name the actual thing — not the verdict on yourself, the specific thing."* Wait for a bounded answer (a behavior, an instance, a pattern with a name) before continuing. If the answer is global, use the redirect from Section I before moving on.

### Movement 2 — The Prayer of Witnessing

Read together, slowly:

> *"Search me, O God, and know my heart! Try me and know my thoughts! And see if there be any grievous way in me, and lead me in the way everlasting!"*
> — Psalm 139:23-24 (ESV)

*"This is a prayer, not an inventory — you're not producing the answer yourself, you're asking to be shown. Sit for a moment. What comes?"*

Coach: if global shame language surfaces here, this is where it most often happens — pause immediately, use the redirect, return to specific naming before continuing.

### Movement 3 — The Cistern and the Fountain

Read together:

> *"My people have committed two evils: they have forsaken me, the fountain of living waters, and hewed out cisterns for themselves, broken cisterns that can hold no water."*
> — Jeremiah 2:13 (ESV)

*"This isn't first about the thing you named being bad. It's about what you were actually reaching for when you reached for it — a real need, aimed at something that can't hold it. What were you actually needing, underneath?"*

### Movement 4 — Confession and Assurance

Read together:

> *"If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness."*
> — 1 John 1:9 (ESV)

*"Say the specific thing to God now, in your own words — not the global verdict, the specific thing from Movement 1."*

### Movement 5 — The Turn

Read together:

> *"But when he came to himself, he said... 'I will arise and go to my father.'"*
> — Luke 15:17-18 (ESV)

*"Notice the order — he saw clearly first, and what came next wasn't punishment, it was walking toward. Not just away from what you named — toward what you actually need. Can you say, in one line, what you're turning toward, not just what you're turning from?"*

### Closing

*"This is one moment, not a finished transaction — the turning continues past this room. What we do with what surfaced today is keep working it in session, not carry it alone."*

Bridge back into the session's ongoing work — do not treat this as a closing ritual for the day unless it naturally is one.
