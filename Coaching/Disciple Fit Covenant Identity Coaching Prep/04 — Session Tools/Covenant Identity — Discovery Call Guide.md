# Covenant Identity — Discovery Call Guide
*Practitioner-Facing Conversation Guide — Reusable, Not Per-Client*

*Generated from: AI Prompt — Discovery Call Guide.md*
*Related: Two-Tier Intake Protocol, Affections Discovery Process, Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic*

---

## Section 1 — Purpose and Design Principles

The discovery call has one primary job: **fit assessment** — determining whether this client and this model are the right match for each other. Everything else in the call serves that job.

This call is not a free coaching session. It is not a sales pitch. It is not a diagnostic intake. It is a structured, relational conversation that answers one question for both parties: *Is this the right fit?*

Change readiness in this model means **readiness to receive** — not readiness to achieve. The question is not "How motivated is this person to generate change?" but "How open is this person to receiving what God has already declared about them?" The change is God's. The readiness is the client's.

The call surfaces patterns. It does not diagnose. The depth ceiling is real — stay above it.

## Section 2 — Before the Call

### Review Tier 1 Pre-Discovery Screen Responses

Read the completed form within 24 hours of receiving it. Arrive at the call with an initial read — hold it loosely. Note:

| What to Read For | Where It Shows |
|---|---|
| Ratio of external vs. internal attribution in presenting problem | Q1 |
| Duration + verb used to describe attempts ("tried" vs. "prayed about" vs. "worked on") | Q2 |
| Season description — movement vs. circling — agency language present or absent | Q3 |
| God-relationship tone — flat/formulaic vs. textured and honest; close vs. distant | Q4 |
| Identity gap score — low (1–3), moderate (4–6), higher (7–9), aspirational (10) | Q5 |
| Motivation quality — intrinsic/identified vs. introjected/external; precipitating event | Q6 |
| Clinical flag — if YES on Q7, prepare the discernment conversation | Q7 |
| What they chose to bring when given total freedom | Q8 |

**Affections Layer — Read Across All Responses:**
As a separate diagnostic frame, read the completed form for affections signals. Ask: *What is this person depending on — and where is God absent from it?*

- What do they orbit around emotionally in Q1 and Q3? What are they not mentioning?
- In Q4, is God present in the functional texture of their day, or described in abstract/theological terms only?
- In Q6, what is driving the urgency? What are they afraid of losing or needing to fix?
- In Q8, what did they bring when given total freedom — and what does that choice reveal about priority?

This is a pre-call read only. You are orienting your listening, not forming conclusions.

### Prepare Your Intention, Not a Script

The call has structure but not a script. Based on the Tier 1 read, know which behavioral areas you want to enter. Have two or three behavioral questions ready for the territory that seems most active.

### Know the Go/No-Go Criteria

Before the call, review the five go/no-go criteria from the Two-Tier Intake Protocol. You are listening for evidence relevant to each throughout the conversation.

### Logistics Check

- 15-minute reminder sent
- Video link confirmed
- Tier 1 submitted (if not, have the pre-screen questions ready to ask verbally)
- Coaching agreement link ready to send if fit is confirmed

## Section 3 — Call Structure: Five Movements

Not a rigid script. Movements. Know where you are and what each movement is for.

---

### Movement 1 — Opening (2–3 min)

**Purpose:** Establish tone. The client is nervous. They don't know what this call is. Create genuine warmth and set expectations clearly so the conversation can be honest.

**Language guidance:**
- Warm and direct — no performative enthusiasm
- Name what the call is: *"This is a conversation for both of us to figure out if this is the right fit. I'll ask you some questions, you'll have space to ask me anything, and by the end we'll both have a clear sense of whether to move forward."*
- Set permission for honesty: *"There's no right answer here. The most useful thing you can bring is what's actually true."*

---

### Movement 2 — Behavioral Staging (10–12 min)

**Purpose:** Locate the client's change readiness through behavioral questions. Listen for agency language, pattern description, and what the problem has actually cost them.

**Primary territory:** Presenting problem and narrative arc at behavioral level.

**Draw from:** Domain 1 (Pattern and Cost) and Domain 2 (Narrative and Agency) in the Question Bank — select 2–3 based on Tier 1 read.

**What you're assessing:** Are they in pre-contemplation (external attribution only), contemplation (internal dimension named, ambivalent), or preparation (specific, ready, resourced)?

**Affections layer — hold this frame throughout Movement 2:**
While staging, the coach is also beginning to build an affections picture. Note what the client is orbiting around. What do they keep returning to? What generates the most emotional energy? What they've tried and failed at reveals what they've been trusting.

**Red flags for wrong fit:** Cannot name any internal contribution to the problem; attributes everything to others or circumstances; wants strategy not formation.

---

### Movement 3 — Identity Gap Indicators (10–12 min)

**Purpose:** Surface behavioral evidence of divergent identity operation — the gap between theological identity and functional identity.

**Primary territory:** God-relationship under pressure, failure response, and where the gap shows up in daily life.

**Draw from:** Domains 3, 4, 5, and 7 in the Question Bank — select 2–3.

**What you're assessing:**
- Is there a live gap? Is the client aware of it?
- Can they feel its cost?
- Is the gap structural (maintained by competing commitments) or situational (triggered by a recent event)?

**Affections focus in this movement:** This is where affections discovery enters most directly. Use Layer 3 (Dependency Probe) questions from the Affections Discovery Process to surface what the client is trusting for security, significance, and identity. Hold the God-Exclusion Map as a coach-internal diagnostic frame — surface it verbally only when it can land without defensiveness.

**Distinguish:** A client who says *"I struggle with shame"* is reporting. A client who says *"Every time I fail, I spend three days performing my way back into feeling okay with God before I can actually be present"* is revealing the gap. Listen for the behavior, not the self-assessment.

---

### Movement 4 — Model Introduction and Agreement (7–9 min)

**Purpose:** Introduce the Covenant Identity coaching framework at accessible depth — enough for the client to know what they're saying yes to — and reach two explicit points of agreement before fit is declared: what the work will specifically target for this client, and what the client themselves said they want, confirmed in their own words. A model explained is not the same as a goal agreed to; this movement does both, not just the first.

**Structure:**
1. **Name the gap:** *"What I'm hearing is a distance between who you know yourself to be in Christ and who you actually experience yourself to be — especially under pressure. That gap has a name and it has a structure."*
2. **Name the work:** *"This coaching works at the root of that gap — not just behavior, not just goals. We're working at the level of identity: what do you actually believe about yourself, about God, about what's safe? And how does that belief show up in how you live?"*
3. **Name the grammar:** *"The theological foundation is that your identity in Christ is already declared — it's not something you build, earn, or perform toward. The coaching work is closing the distance between what has already been declared and what you actually live from."*
4. **Name the pace:** *"This is formation, not productivity. It moves at the pace of real change — which is slower than most people expect and more lasting than most approaches produce."*
5. **Invite response:** *"Does that resonate with what you're carrying? What questions do you have about what this actually looks like?"*
6. **Individualize the task:** Replace the generic four-phase description with what phase one would specifically target for this client, using what actually surfaced in Movements 2–3 — not the model in the abstract. *"Based on what you've described about [the specific pattern, gap, or dependency named earlier in the call], the early work would focus on [specific territory] — that's what this looks like for you, not just the four phases in general."* Skipping this step and only ever describing the model generically is a signal the call stayed diagnostic and never reached task agreement.
7. **Confirm the goal, in their own words:** Reflect back what the client said they wanted — using their own language from Domain 6, not the coach's theological reframing of it — and ask for explicit confirmation before moving to Movement 5. *"Earlier you said [quote or close paraphrase of their own words about what they want to be different]. Is that still the right way to put it, or would you say it differently now?"* The point of this step is client-owned goal language that the client has explicitly confirmed — not coach-approved goal language that the client has merely heard and not objected to. If the client cannot restate or affirm a goal in their own words here, that is itself diagnostic information about readiness, not a step to skip past.

**Tone:** Plain. Not a pitch. Not a theological lecture. Offer it and listen to what lands.

**What to avoid:** Theological vocabulary the client hasn't used themselves; overselling outcomes; making it sound like a program rather than a relational process; filling silence after the introduction; moving to Movement 5 on the strength of the client's silence or polite agreement rather than an explicit, individualized goal confirmation.

---

### Movement 5 — Fit Assessment and Next Steps (5–7 min)

**Purpose:** Make the fit determination transparent, mutual, and clear. Do not hedge. Do not over-sell.

**If fit is confirmed:**
*"Based on what you've shared, I think this is a real match. What I'm hearing maps directly to what this coaching is built for."*
Name next steps: coaching agreement, Tier 2 intake, Session 1 date. Do not over-explain.

**If fit is not confirmed:**
*"I want to be straight with you. Based on our conversation, I don't think this specific model is what you need right now."*
Name the specific reason. Offer a referral or alternative. Do not soften into ambiguity — an unclear close is worse than a clean no.

**If more information is needed:**
*"I want to take a day to sit with what you've shared before I give you a clear answer. I'll be back to you within 24 hours."*
Use this only when genuinely needed — not as a soft no.

## Section 4 — Behavioral Question Bank

Organized by domain. For each call, select 2–3 questions per movement — not all of them. This is a conversation, not a questionnaire. The client's responses determine which direction to go.

---

### Domain 1 — Pattern and Cost *(Movement 2 — Behavioral Staging)*

- "When did you first notice this pattern? Walk me through the earliest time you can remember it showing up."
- "What has staying in this pattern actually cost you? Not in general — specifically. What relationships, what opportunities, what version of yourself?"
- "The last time you tried to address this — walk me through what happened. Not just that it didn't work, but where specifically it broke down."
- "What are you doing today — not what you wish you were doing — that you suspect is keeping this in place?"

**What these reveal:** Agency level, schema or ITC depth, whether change attempts have been behavioral (surface) or identity-level (root), pattern duration.

---

### Domain 2 — Narrative and Agency *(Movement 2 — Behavioral Staging)*

- "If you had to describe this season of your life in a sentence or two — not a résumé, just what it's actually felt like — what would you say?"
- "In the story of how you got here — are you more the person things happened to, or the person who made choices that led here? Or somewhere between?"
- "Is there a version of yourself you can see but can't seem to inhabit? Describe what that person looks like."

**What these reveal:** Narrative contamination level, agency language, identity aspirational language as a marker of contemplation or preparation readiness.

---

### Domain 3 — God-Relationship Under Pressure *(Movement 3 — Identity Gap)*

- "On a hard week — not a crisis, just a genuinely difficult week — what actually happens to your faith? Not what you hope happens. What does."
- "What does it look like when you and God are actually close — as opposed to when you're going through the motions? What's the difference?"
- "When was the last time you heard something from God that you didn't want to hear? What did you do with it?"
- "When life gets hard, do you move toward God or away from him? Be honest."

**What these reveal:** God-attachment pattern, lament capacity, spiritual bypassing level, God-representation under pressure vs. in ordinary life.

---

### Domain 4 — Failure Response *(Movement 3 — Identity Gap)*

- "When you fail at something that matters — morally, relationally, at work — what do you actually do in the next 24 hours? Walk me through it."
- "After a significant failure, how long does it typically take to feel right with God again? What does that process look like?"
- "When you make a mistake in front of someone you respect, what happens inside you first — before you respond?"

**What these reveal:** Shame-cycle depth, indicative/imperative reversal severity, performance-recovery pattern, whether failure triggers identity collapse or situational correction.

---

### Domain 5 — Identity Gap Specificity *(Movement 3 — Identity Gap)*

- "If I asked you to name one thing God says about who you are — not what you've achieved, but who you are — what would you say? And does it feel true?"
- "Is there a version of what God says about you that you believe in theory but can't seem to feel in your actual life? What is it?"
- "Where in your life does the gap between who you believe you are and how you actually live feel the widest?"

**What these reveal:** Covenant identity layer integration level, specific gap territory, whether the gap is diffuse or located in a specific domain.

---

### Domain 6 — Motivation and Urgency *(Movement 2 or 3)*

- "What's the real thing that made you reach out now — not six months ago, not a year from now, but now?"
- "What would it mean about you if nothing in this area changed in the next year?"
- "If this coaching worked exactly as it should — what would be different? Not just in your behavior. In who you experience yourself to be."

**What these reveal:** SDT motivation quality (intrinsic vs. introjected vs. external), precipitating event, identity-level aspiration, readiness for the depth of work.

**Carry forward:** Note the client's exact words here, not a paraphrase. This is the language reflected back in Movement 4, Step 7 (Confirm the goal) — the goal-agreement step depends on the client hearing their own words, not the coach's summary of them.

---

### Domain 7 — Affections, Trust & Dependencies *(Movement 3 — Identity Gap)*

Use these to surface the dependency structure beneath the presenting problem. Hold the affections frame as a coach-internal diagnostic lens. Surface verbally when it can land without defensiveness.

- "What would need to be true for you to feel okay — really okay — about yourself?"
- "When you're anxious or struggling, who or what do you reach for first?"
- "What gives you the most relief when things feel unstable?"
- "What do you feel like you can't do without?"
- *(Laddering — follow any answer downward):* "And if that were true, what would that mean about you?"
- "Is there something you believe you have to hold onto — or hold together — in order for things to be okay?"
- "Where is God in this picture you're describing?"

**What these reveal:** The functional god — what the client is trusting for security, significance, and identity. Where God is absent from the dependency structure. The false identity the affections are maintaining. This feeds directly into the Affections Discovery Process and the Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic.

## Section 5 — Stage-Readiness Indicators

What to listen for — not what to ask directly. These are behavioral signals that locate the client's stage throughout the conversation.

**Pre-contemplation — not ready (referral or pause):**
- Every answer attributes the problem to external factors
- Cannot name any pattern they are contributing to
- Spiritual life described in terms of activity with no experiential texture
- Shows no emotional weight when the identity gap is named
- Wants practical strategies, accountability, or goal-setting help — not formation

**Contemplation — minimum threshold for fit:**
- Names an internal dimension to the problem, even tentatively
- Shows emotional weight when talking about who they "should" be vs. who they are
- Has tried to change and can describe where it broke down behaviorally
- Feels the gap between theological and lived identity, even without language for it
- Ambivalent about what addressing it will cost, but genuinely willing to look

**Preparation — strong fit:**
- Came with specific questions about the model or what coaching involves
- Can name exactly what they want to be different — not just the behavior but the underlying thing
- Has support in place (community, therapy if needed, time)
- Energy when talking about what becomes possible if the gap closes

**Action/Maintenance — assess for wrong fit or wrong level:**
- Already doing significant identity work — may not need this specific model
- OR: Describes impressive spiritual discipline with no change in the underlying gap — this is actually a Contemplation/Preparation client addressing the wrong level. Strong fit for this model.

## Section 6 — Divergent Identity Behavioral Markers

**Present and coachable — right fit:**
- Theology and behavior diverge consistently under pressure (can describe both)
- Failure response involves shame cycling, performance recovery, or extended disconnection from God
- Identity language is external (role, achievement, relational position) — covenant identity is known but not felt as operative
- Duration of pattern is significant; change attempts have been behavioral, not root-level
- Client can feel the gap when it's named and is curious rather than defensive

**Absent — wrong fit (may need a different coaching model):**
- Gap is genuinely small; behavior reflects integrated covenant identity
- Issue is primarily practical (career direction, decision-making, skill development) without identity-level underpinning
- Client is looking for accountability or performance coaching — clear mismatch

**Present but clinical — referral indicated:**
- Gap is producing functional impairment (significant depression, inability to function in key life domains)
- Trauma is actively destabilizing the ability to engage reflectively
- Identity collapse is acute and crisis-level — needs clinical stabilization before coaching
- Client is describing symptoms of clinical-level mood disorder, dissociation, or psychosis
- See Crisis & Referral Protocol for referral pathways and language

## Section 7 — Model Introduction: Accessible Language

Use as a template, not a script. Adapt to what the client actually said.

**The one-minute version (when time is tight):**
*"The coaching I do works in the gap between who you know yourself to be in Christ and who you actually experience yourself to be in daily life — especially under pressure. Most growth approaches work on what you do. This one works on who you live from. The foundation is that your identity in Christ is already given — not something you build or earn. The work is closing the distance between that declaration and how it actually operates in your life."*

**The three-minute version (standard):**
1. *"What I'm hearing you describe is a distance between [specific thing they said about their theological identity] and [specific behavior or experience they described]. That gap is real and it has a structure."*
2. *"This coaching is built on the idea that your identity in Christ is already declared — it's the given. The work isn't to build it. It's to close the distance between what's already declared and what you actually live from. The gap is maintained by specific formation material: early messages you believed, vows you made, competing commitments that make change feel dangerous even when you genuinely want it."*
3. *"We work through that gap in four phases — getting the baseline, mapping the specific wounds and lies underneath, doing targeted installation work, and then integrating and closing. It's not fast. It moves at the pace of real formation. But what changes, changes at a depth that holds."*
4. *"Does that match what you're actually carrying? What questions does that raise?"*

## Section 8 — Transition Language

**Confirmed fit — moving forward:**
*"Based on what you've shared, I think this is the right work for where you are. What I'm hearing maps directly to what this coaching is designed for. Here's what happens next: I'll send you the coaching agreement and a fuller intake form to complete before our first session. Once those are in, we'll schedule Session 1. Any questions before we close?"*

**No fit — graceful and honest:**
*"I want to be straight with you — I don't think this specific model is the right fit for you right now. [Name the specific reason.] That's not a judgment; it's precision about what this model is built for. What I'd suggest instead is [referral / alternative resource]. I'm happy to help point you in the right direction."*

**Pause — clinical or readiness concern:**
*"There's something important I want to make sure we address before moving forward. [Name the specific concern.] I don't want to rush past that. Let's [specific next step]. I'll reach out on [specific date]."*

**Unclear — need to think:**
*"I want to sit with what you've shared before I give you a clear answer. I'll be back to you within 24 hours by email."*
*(Use only when genuinely needed — not as a soft no.)*

## Section 9 — Post-Call Protocol

Complete within one hour of the call ending, while the conversation is fresh.

**1. Note the stage:** Which Stages of Change stage did behavioral signals indicate? What was the strongest evidence?

**2. Note the identity gap territory:** Where specifically did divergent identity operation show up? Which domain — failure response, God-relationship, narrative, specific covenant identity layer?

**3. Note the affections picture:** What is this person trusting for security, significance, or identity? What functional dependency emerged? Where was God absent from the dependency structure? What false identity did the affections begin to reveal? (This feeds into the Affections Discovery Process and the diagnostic sequence for Session 1.)

**4. Note any flags:** Clinical concerns, scope questions, referral considerations. Note whether Q7 from Tier 1 was discussed and what the outcome was.

**5. Decision record:** Did you send Tier 2? Yes — on what basis? No — what was the specific reason?

**6. Note alliance formation:** What did the client explicitly confirm as their own goal, in their own words (Movement 4, Step 7)? What specific task did you name for phase one, and did they confirm it (Movement 4, Step 6)? What evidence — not inference — indicated bond and trust were established, beyond the client simply granting diagnostic access? If any of these three are missing, note it as a gap to close in Session 1, not something to assume happened because the call went well.

**7. Follow-through:** If you said you'd do something (send the agreement, follow up in a week, provide a referral), do it within 24 hours.
