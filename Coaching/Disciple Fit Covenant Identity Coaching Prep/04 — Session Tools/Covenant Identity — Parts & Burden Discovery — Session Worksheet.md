# Covenant Identity — Parts & Burden Discovery — Session Worksheet

`[SESSION-TOOL]` `[BIBLE-SESSION]`

**What this is:** A coach-facilitated discovery process for Stage 4b work — used when the client knows their covenant truth intellectually but it is not landing at the felt level. The coach guides each section in-session through dialogue; the client writes; the completed worksheet is handed to the client at the end for between-session formation practice.

**Prerequisite:** Stage 2 complete (false identity named). Explicit covenant truth has been introduced but is not producing felt shift — either declared but not experienced as true, or known but not changing behavior at the pattern level.

**See also:** [[Covenant Identity — Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic]] (Stage 4b entry criteria), [[IFS — Internal Family Systems Framework]] (parts model), [[Memory Reconsolidation — Ecker & Neuroscience Basis]] (activation requirement), Tool 19 (Felt Sense Focusing — activation step)

---

## Coach Instructions — Read Before Beginning

> **[COACH] What you are doing:** You are facilitating structured discovery — not informing, not correcting. Your job in each section is to ask the question, wait for the client's genuine answer, and write what emerges. The client does the work; you hold the frame.

> **[COACH] Activation requirement (non-optional):** The pattern must be emotionally live before discovery begins. Mapping a remembered pattern from a calm cognitive state produces conceptual output — which cannot reconsolidate an implicit belief. Before beginning Section 1, confirm the pattern is activated: a recent specific memory, a felt-body response, some present-tense emotional access. If not, use Tool 19 (Felt Sense Focusing) to locate the bodily sense first. Do not skip this.

> **[COACH] Posture:** You are getting curious with the client about a part of them — not confronting a sin pattern. The protective behavior is not the enemy. It formed to serve a purpose, and it served that purpose well. Curiosity and compassion before any truth. A part that feels attacked defends; a part that feels seen opens.

> **[COACH] Scope boundary:** This process can surface early relational material. Monitor throughout: if the client cannot maintain any observer distance (they are the part, not watching it), if dissociation or escalating distress appears, or if early childhood relational trauma activates with high emotional charge — stop, stabilize, and consider referral before continuing. The eight Cs of Self-energy (curiosity, calm, clarity) are your green-light signals. Their absence is a stop signal.

> **[COACH] On writing:** Write for the client in this session. You are scribing what emerges from discovery so the client has it in their own words on paper. Ask clarifying questions to get to specificity. Vague names produce vague declarations; specific names produce formation.

## Section 1 — Activate

> **[COACH]:** Before naming anything, locate the pattern in the present moment. Ask the client to bring to mind a recent, clear example — not the most painful memory, just a specific recent instance. Then:
> *"Let yourself sit with that memory for a moment. Where do you feel it in your body right now? What does that feel like — not what you think about it, just the physical quality?"*
> Wait. The bodily sense is what makes this work. Do not move on until the client has some felt contact with the pattern.

## Section 2 — Name the Pattern

> **[COACH]:** You want a behavioral name, not a trait label. *"I'm a perfectionist"* is a trait. *"I keep working past the point of good enough, then feel shame when I submit it anyway"* is a behavior in context. The specificity of the name determines the specificity of everything downstream. Probe with: *"What specifically are you doing — or not doing — when this pattern is running?"*

## Section 3 — What Is the Pattern Protecting?

> **[COACH]:** This is the heart of the discovery. Work through the question sequence slowly — one question at a time, genuine answer before the next. You are not evaluating the pattern; you are finding out what it is afraid would happen without it.

> **[COACH] Sequence:**
> 1. *"What does this pattern help you avoid or prevent?"*
> 2. *"And if that happened — what would that mean?"*
> 3. *"What are you most afraid would happen if you just stopped doing this completely?"*
> 4. *"And if the worst of that happened — what would that mean about you?"*
>
> Question 4 is the one that belongs on paper. Let the client sit with it.

**This pattern helps me avoid:**

_______________________________________________________________________________

**If I stopped doing it entirely, I'm afraid:**

_______________________________________________________________________________

**If that worst thing happened, it would mean about me that:**

_______________________________________________________________________________

> **[COACH]:** The answer to the last question is likely close to the burden — the implicit belief the false identity is organized around. Hold it. You will confirm and sharpen it in Section 4.

## Section 4 — Meet the Part

> **[COACH]:** You are now shifting from describing the pattern to getting curious about the part that is running it. Introduce the framing:
> *"It sounds like a part of you has been doing this job for a long time. I'd like to get curious about that part — not to fix it, not to argue with it, just to understand what it has been trying to do for you. Can we do that?"*
>
> Use the language *"a part of you"* throughout — not "you." This is the observer-distance shift that is itself formative.

> **[COACH] Questions:**
> 1. *"How long has this part been doing this job?"*
> 2. *"What does this part believe would happen to you if it stopped?"*
> 3. *"What does this part most need you to know? What has it been trying to tell you all this time?"*
> 4. *"What is this part most afraid of — about you, about others, about God?"*

## Section 5 — What God Says

> **[COACH]:** This is the juxtaposition step. The truth introduced here must be specific enough to genuinely collide with the specific lie named above. A generic truth (*"God loves me"*) placed against a specific lie (*"I am only valuable when I perform"*) produces insight, not reconsolidation. The disconfirmation must be precise.
>
> Before naming the truth, invite the client to receive it as a question — this keeps the door open rather than triggering the part to defend:
> *"What would it mean — about you, and about God — if the opposite of this belief were true? Not asking whether you believe it yet. Just: if it were true, what would change?"*
>
> Let that question do work before writing anything.

## Section 6 — The Declaration

> **[COACH]:** The Declaration is addressed to the part, spoken from the new self in Christ. This is not affirmation — the client is not trying to convince themselves. They are speaking to a part of themselves from a more grounded place: the Spirit-formed identity that is already true in covenant, however unfelt.
>
> The source of this grounded place is not the client's willpower or insight. It is the Spirit's work. Frame it explicitly:
> *"From who you are in Christ — not from effort or performance — what do you want to say to this part? What does it need to hear from the new you?"*
>
> Declarations are: first-person, present-tense, specific to the lie, addressed to the part. Do not settle for generic affirmations — press toward the specific.

**Declaration from the new self in Christ, to the part:**

> *I see you. I know what you have been carrying and how long you have carried it.*

> *But I no longer have to live from the belief that ________________________________.*

## Between-Session Formation Practice

*The coach completes the worksheet with the client in session, then hands it over. These instructions are for the client.*

---

You have just done something important. A part of you that has been running a protective job got seen — with curiosity and without condemnation. What happens in the next days determines whether this stays a session insight or becomes something that forms you.

**Daily, for the next seven days:**

**Morning (2 minutes):** Read your Declaration aloud — slowly, once. Let the body hear it. Do not rush past it. Notice what tightens or settles.

**When the pattern activates:** Pause before defaulting. Say internally: *"I notice [name the part] is up. I see you. But I am [your covenant truth]."* Then choose from that grounded place — even imperfectly.

**Evening (one sentence):** Write in a notebook or journal: *"Today I noticed the pattern when _______. What I did was _______."* No evaluation — just observation.

**Weekly:** Bring what you are noticing to coaching. What is landing? What is the part still resisting? Where is the Declaration feeling true and where does it still feel like a statement about someone else?
