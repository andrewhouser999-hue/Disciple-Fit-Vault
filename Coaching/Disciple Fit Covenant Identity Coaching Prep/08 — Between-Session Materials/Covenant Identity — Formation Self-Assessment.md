# Covenant Identity — Formation Self-Assessment

*Practitioner reference with embedded client-facing instrument. Administered at phase transitions and monthly within a phase. Client-autonomous — completed without the coach present, shared at client's discretion.*

---

## Section I — Practitioner Reference

### Deployment

**First administration:** end of Phase 1 or beginning of Phase 2 — after the wound type has been named and before wound work begins. This establishes the baseline all subsequent administrations are compared against.

**Subsequent administrations:** at each phase transition (Phase 2 → 3, Phase 3 → 4) and monthly if the engagement extends within a phase.

**How to frame it for the client:** "This is not a test and there is no passing score. It is a picture of where you are right now — useful because the same picture taken again in six weeks shows you movement you would not otherwise be able to see. Answer quickly; first response is most accurate."

### What to Do with Results

Review trend across administrations, not individual item scores. Signal patterns to watch:

- **Low scores on Area 2 (wound pattern still fully active) or Area 3 (God still functionally distant) in Phase 3:** assess whether identity installation is premature — the wound and God-rep may need more Phase 2 work before installation will hold.
- **Consistent low scores across all areas entering Phase 4:** the engagement may not be holding at the depth needed. Do not close prematurely. Extend Phase 3 or reassess what is blocking movement.
- **High scores across all areas in Phase 2:** watch for the False Noble performing the assessment. High scores at the beginning of wound work are a signal, not a celebration.

### Distinction from Adjacent Tools

The **Mid-Engagement Snapshot** (Sessions 4–5) is coach-facilitated, uses verbatim language, and captures qualitative shift in how the client describes themselves. The Self-Assessment is client-autonomous, uses numeric ratings, and tracks self-reported trajectory over time. They are complementary: one captures language, the other captures trend.

### Phase 4 Note

When you administer the assessment in Phase 4, place it beside the first administration. The movement between them — in the client's own ratings — is primary Growth Report material. If the client shares their scores, use their language about what moved and what stayed stuck as the vocabulary for the report.

## Section II — Client-Facing Instrument

*Complete this on your own — no coach present needed. It takes about ten minutes. Your coach will tell you when to complete it; you decide whether to share your results.*

**What this is:** sixteen short statements about your experience. For each one, mark where you are on the scale below. Answer quickly — your first response is usually the most honest.

**The scale:**

> **1** — Not like me / Not yet
> **2** — Beginning to be true / Occasionally
> **3** — Sometimes true
> **4** — Often true
> **5** — Consistently true of me

Mark a number (1–5) next to each item. There is no passing score. The value is in comparing this picture to the same picture taken later in the engagement.
