# What the Part Carries — An Independent Formation Practice

`[CLIENT-TOOL]`

---

## What This Is

Most of us have experienced the frustrating gap between knowing something true and feeling it. You know God loves you, that you are not defined by your performance, that you are secure in Christ — and it is true. But in the moments that count, something older and faster takes over. The anxiety spikes. The pattern runs. The truth you know stays somewhere behind glass.

This is not a failure of faith. It is a feature of how human beings are made. The beliefs that govern our nervous system, our snap reactions, and our deepest sense of who we are were not formed by information. They were formed by experience — often early, often relational, often wordless. And they do not change through information alone.

This practice is designed for exactly that gap. It is not a way to think harder or try more. It is a guided process for finding the belief that is actually running — the one underneath the behavior you keep returning to — and bringing it into contact with a truth that genuinely contradicts it.

The Spirit does not need you to be perfectly composed to work. But He does work through real encounter: with your own history, with specific truth, and with the living Christ who speaks to parts of you that sermons haven't reached.

**Work through one pattern at a time.** Do not try to cover your whole history. One pattern, engaged honestly, is worth more than ten patterns surveyed.

**This is not therapy.** If what emerges feels unmanageable or too large for this format, stop and bring it to your coach.

## Before You Begin — Activation Step

**This step is not optional.** Skip it and the rest of the document will produce thoughts, not formation.

The pattern you are working with must be emotionally present before you begin writing. If you approach this from a calm, reflective distance — analyzing a pattern you remember — you will produce good analysis. But the implicit belief you are trying to reach does not live in analysis. It lives in the felt, immediate experience of being in the pattern.

**Use Tool 19 (Felt Sense Focusing) before continuing.** If you do not have that document:

1. Find a quiet place where you will not be interrupted. Sit comfortably.
2. Bring to mind a specific recent moment when the pattern you are working with showed up. Not the worst time — just a clear, recent one. Make it concrete: where were you, who was present, what happened?
3. Let the memory be present without analyzing it. Just be with it.
4. Turn attention to your body. Where do you feel something in response to this memory? Chest, throat, stomach, shoulders — somewhere there is a physical quality associated with this. Give it your attention.
5. Do not try to name what it "means." Just stay with the physical sensation for 60–90 seconds. Let it be there.
6. When you have some felt contact with the pattern — a physical sense, however vague — you are ready to begin.

**If you cannot locate any bodily response:** The pattern may not be sufficiently activated. Try a more recent or more emotionally vivid example. If you are still unable to find any felt contact, come back to this practice after a session where the pattern is live in the room.

## Safety — Read This First

This practice can surface material that is older and heavier than expected. Most of the time, that is good — it means you are reaching what has been driving the pattern. But occasionally it surfaces more than is wise to hold alone.

**Stop and bring to coaching if:**

- You begin to access a specific memory from childhood that carries significant emotional weight and you feel yourself inside it rather than observing it
- You feel flooded — the emotional intensity is escalating rather than settling as you stay with it
- You feel like you are losing your footing — unclear where you are, difficulty orienting to the present room
- The practice feels destabilizing rather than grounding

**This is not failure.** It means you have found something real and important that deserves more support than a document can provide. Mark where you stopped and bring it to your next session. Your coach can hold the container for that material with you.

**If you feel distress after stopping:** Put your feet flat on the floor. Notice five things you can see in the room around you. Take three slow breaths. The present moment is safe.

## PART 3 — The Part and What It Carries

**What you are doing here:** Getting curious about the part of you that is running the pattern. Not attacking it, not trying to fix it — just seeing it. This shift, from *being* the pattern to *noticing a part of you that runs it*, is not a small one. It is the difference between being trapped inside a belief and having enough distance to examine it.

A part of you formed this protective response. It probably formed when you were young and did not have better options. It was smart. It served you. It may have protected you from something real. The fact that it is now costing you more than it is providing does not make it foolish — it makes it ready to be met with something better.

## PART 5 — The Declaration and the Invitation

**What you are doing here:** Speaking. Not to yourself in general — to the part specifically, from the new self in Christ. And then inviting the part into a different relationship with you going forward.

The source of what you are about to say is not willpower or confidence. It is the Spirit's work — the new creation that is already true in covenant even when unfelt. You are speaking from who you are in Christ, not from how you feel right now. That is not denial; it is declaration of what is already real.

## Formation Practice — The Next Seven Days

What you have done in this document will only become formation if it extends beyond this sitting. The belief you named formed over years of lived experience. It does not dissolve in one session. But it can begin to loosen through sustained, attentive practice.

---

**Daily (5–10 minutes):**

**Morning:** Read your Declaration aloud — once, slowly. Not as performance. As a real address to the part of you that is still learning. Notice what tightens or settles in your body as you say it.

**When the pattern activates during the day:** You will notice it more now that you have named it. When it shows up, pause before defaulting. You do not have to do anything dramatic. Just say internally: *"I notice [name the part] is up. I see you. But I am [one phrase from your Declaration]."* Then choose — even imperfectly — from that grounded place.

**Evening:** One sentence in a journal: *"I noticed the pattern when _______. I responded by _______."* No evaluation. Just honest observation.

---

**Bring to coaching:**

- What shifted in the Declaration — where it lands and where it still feels like a statement about someone else
- Where the pattern is loosening and where it still has grip
- Anything that surfaced in the Part 3 dialogue that felt bigger than expected

## Stuck-Point Guide

If you get stuck in this practice, here are the most common places and what to do:

---

**"I can't find any felt sense in my body."**

You may be too cognitively active. Try a more recent, more vivid example of the pattern — one that happened today or this week. Or try speaking the fear out loud (not silently) while holding the memory. Spoken words engage the body differently than silent reading.

If you regularly have difficulty accessing any felt sense, this is worth exploring with your coach. It is not a character flaw — it is a pattern of its own with its own story.

---

**"The Part 3 dialogue just sounds like me analyzing myself."**

That is common. The voice you want is younger and more immediate than analysis. Try writing faster — do not stop to edit or evaluate. Let whatever surfaces come out without approval. The analytical voice is not wrong; it is just not where the burden lives.

Another approach: Write with your non-dominant hand. It is awkward and forces you to slow down in a way that bypasses the editorial layer.

---

**"The lie I wrote in Part 3 feels too obvious — I already knew that."**

Two possibilities. First: you may have named the secondary lie, not the primary one. The primary burden is usually the one that feels most exposing to write. Push the Question 4 sequence one level deeper: *"And if that were true about me — what would that mean?"*

Second: there is sometimes a difference between knowing a lie and having it emotionally named. If your body responded when you wrote it — if something in your chest or stomach registered — it is doing real work even if the content feels familiar.

---

**"The Declaration feels hollow or performative."**

This is usually an activation problem — the lie was not emotionally present when you wrote the Declaration. Go back to the Part 3 burden. Re-read it. Let it land again. Then write the Declaration with that belief still in front of you, not after you have moved past it.

A Declaration written against a felt lie is different from one written in the absence of the lie. The disconfirmation only does its work when both are present simultaneously.

---

**"Something came up that feels too big to hold."**

Stop. Mark the page. Do not try to complete the practice in this sitting. What surfaced is real and important — it just needs more support than a document can provide.

Put your feet on the floor. Breathe slowly. Notice what is around you in the room.

Bring what you found to your next session. Your coach can hold the container for this material with you. What surfaced is not a problem — it is something real that has been waiting to be seen.
