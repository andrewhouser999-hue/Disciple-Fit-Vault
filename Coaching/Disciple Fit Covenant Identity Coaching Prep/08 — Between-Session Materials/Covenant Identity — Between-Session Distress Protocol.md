# Covenant Identity — Between-Session Distress Protocol

*Practitioner reference with embedded client-facing content. Read Section 1–5 before assigning. Give the CLIENT CARD section to the client when deploying.*

---

## 1. What This Document Is

Covenant Identity Coaching works with significant formation material — wound history, false identity beliefs, and the schemas that maintain the Gap between declared and lived covenant truth. This work sometimes opens material that does not fully settle before the next session. A client left without a protocol for that moment is left to manage something the engagement helped activate without any framework for what to do with it.

This document is a care responsibility, not a service enhancement. It defines what qualifies as between-session distress (distinct from normal post-session processing and from clinical crisis), gives the client a specific sequence of actions, and gives the coach a response protocol when notified.

**What this document is not:** A substitute for the [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]]. That document governs in-session crisis, referral triggers, and clinical presentations. This document governs the middle ground — elevated but not crisis-level distress that surfaces between sessions. When in doubt whether something is distress or crisis, treat it as crisis until you can assess.

---

## 2. The Three-Tier Distinction

The coach and client both need a shared framework for distinguishing between three states. Without this framework, clients either over-contact (treating normal processing as distress) or under-contact (treating genuine distress as something they should manage alone).

### Tier 1 — Normal Post-Session Processing
**What it looks like:** Emotional heaviness, tiredness, a sense of something having been opened. The client is aware of the material but it is not overwhelming. Sleep may be slightly disrupted the night of a significant session. The session content stays present in their thoughts.

**How long:** Typically 24–48 hours. Gradually settles.

**What to do:** Standard between-session practices (Post-Session Integration Guide, between-session commitments). No coach contact needed.

### Tier 2 — Between-Session Distress
**What it looks like:** Elevated activation that is not self-settling after 48 hours, or an unexpected trigger event that has activated the primary schema at unusual intensity. The client may be functioning but is noticeably destabilized. Sleep disrupted. Intrusive thoughts related to session content. Spiritual disorientation (God feeling more distant, not more present). The material is present and heavy but the client is not in danger.

**How long:** Does not self-settle within the expected window. May be triggered by a life event (conversation with a parent, relational rupture, significant failure) intersecting with the wound terrain being worked.

**What to do:** This protocol. Coach contact is appropriate.

### Tier 3 — Crisis
**What it looks like:** Thoughts of ending life or harming self. Inability to care for self or dependents. Uncontrollable panic that grounding does not settle. Feeling unsafe.

**What to do:** Immediately. See the client-facing escalation section below and [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]].

---

## 3. Deployment Conditions

**When to give this document to the client:**

Give the client-facing card at or before the first Phase 2 session — before excavation begins. Phase 1 does not typically produce Tier 2 distress. Phase 2 does. The client should have the protocol before they need it, not after.

Specific conditions that warrant pre-assignment:
- Client history includes complex family-of-origin wound material
- Client endorsed significant lament incapacity (Q7 at intake) — unprocessed loss material is more likely to surface acutely
- Client shows high activation response to early Phase 1 God-representation work
- Coach's clinical instinct says this client needs a safety net

**Contraindications:**
- Do not assign in Phase 1 unless one of the above indicators is present — assigning it too early can prime the client to expect distress before they have experienced the work.
- Do not assign to clients currently in active crisis who are being held by this protocol rather than being referred — that is scope creep.

## 4. Coach Response Protocol

### When the Client Makes Contact

**Response window:** Acknowledge within [COACH SPECIFIES: 24 hours / same business day / etc.]. This should be specified in the coaching agreement and reiterated when the card is assigned. The client needs to know the window before they need it, not at the moment of contact.

**Form of acknowledgment:** Brief. Do not conduct a session via text or email. The acknowledgment communicates: *I received this, I'm present, we will address it properly.*

*Example:* "I received your message. What you're describing makes complete sense given the work we've been doing. I want to address this properly — can we schedule a brief call [today / tomorrow] or would you like to move up our next session?"

### Decision Tree — What to Do After Acknowledging

**Option 1 — Scheduled session is within 72 hours:** Hold it for the session. Send the client back to the grounding practices in the Client Card with a brief note that you've received the message and will open the next session with it.

**Option 2 — Scheduled session is more than 72 hours away:** Offer a brief bridge call (15–20 minutes). The bridge call is not a coaching session — it is containment. Its job is to stabilize the client in the present, confirm Tier 2 (not Tier 3), and give them a frame for what is happening. Do not excavate. Do not process. Do not open new material.

**Bridge call structure:**
1. *"Tell me what happened — briefly. What triggered this and when did it start?"* (2–3 minutes)
2. *"On a scale of 1–10, how destabilized are you right now? Are you safe?"* — If 8+ or any safety question, move to Crisis Protocol.
3. *"What you're experiencing is [name it clearly: activation from the wound material we've been working / the schema responding to the session content / normal processing that's heavier than expected]. This is not a sign something has gone wrong."*
4. *"Here's what I want you to do between now and our session: [one or two specific practices from the Client Card]."*
5. Confirm session time. End call.

**Option 3 — Escalation to Crisis Protocol:** If, at any point in the assessment, what the client is describing sounds like Tier 3 — suicidal ideation at any level, inability to function, a safety concern — move immediately to [[Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol]] Section 3 and 4. Do not attempt to hold a crisis with a Tier 2 protocol.

### After the Bridge Call

Document it briefly: date, what the client reported, your assessment (Tier 2 confirmed / escalated to Tier 3), what you recommended, client's state at end of call. Add a note to the Session Growth Notes for the next session flagging that between-session distress occurred and documenting what was activated.

### What the Coach Does Not Do

- Does not conduct ongoing emotional support via text or email between sessions. The Distress Protocol is for acute situations, not a standing support channel.
- Does not interpret or excavate the material in the acknowledgment message or bridge call. That work belongs in session.
- Does not minimize what the client is reporting in order to avoid scheduling a bridge call. If the client said it was significant, treat it as significant.
- Does not hold a client in Tier 2 protocol when what they describe is Tier 3.

---

## 5. Scope Boundary

**What this protocol holds:** Between-session activation that is elevated but not crisis-level, where the client retains the capacity to use the grounding practices, make contact with the coach, and function in daily life while destabilized.

**What this protocol does not hold:**
- Active suicidal ideation at any level
- Self-harm (current or newly disclosed)
- Uncontrollable panic or severe dissociation the client cannot interrupt
- Clients who require between-session emotional support regularly — if this is happening consistently, the wound material is exceeding the coaching container (see Crisis Protocol, Section 2, Scope Creep signals). That is a referral conversation, not a protocol adjustment.

**If the Distress Protocol is being used frequently:** A client who contacts the coach between sessions two or more times in a month is showing the coach that the engagement is moving faster than their window of tolerance, or that the wound material is clinical in depth. Slow down Phase 2. Consider whether concurrent therapy is indicated. Do not simply optimize the protocol — read what the frequency of use is saying.

# CLIENT CARD
## When Something Hard Surfaces Between Sessions

*This section is for you. Keep it somewhere accessible — not filed away.*

---

The work we are doing together sometimes opens material that doesn't fully settle before we meet again. That is not a sign something has gone wrong. It is often a sign the work is touching the right place.

This card tells you what to do when something surfaces between sessions that feels too heavy to carry alone until we meet.

---

## Step 1 — Name What Is Happening

Before anything else, take a breath and answer this question:

**Am I in distress, or am I in danger?**

**Distress** looks like: activation that isn't settling, intrusive thoughts from our session, something triggered by a conversation or event, emotional heaviness heavier than normal, God feeling more distant. You are functioning but destabilized. **→ Stay with this card.**

**Danger** looks like: thoughts of ending your life or harming yourself, inability to care for yourself, panic that won't settle no matter what you try, feeling unsafe. **→ Go immediately to the Crisis section at the end of this card.**

If you are not sure, treat it as danger and go to the Crisis section. You can always come back.

---

## Step 2 — What to Do Right Now

Do these in order. Don't skip to the next step until you've done the one you're on.

**1. Get your body first.**
Before you think about what it means, before you call anyone — get your body into the present.

Feet flat on the floor. Both hands on your thighs. Feel the chair.

Then: *Name five things you can see. Four things you can touch right now. Three things you can hear. Two things you can smell. One thing you can taste.*

Stay with each one. Don't rush through it.

**2. Say what is true out loud.**
Speak one of these sentences — whichever one you can get your mouth around:

- *"I am here. I am not in danger. This is hard, but I am not undone."*
- *"God, you see me right now. You are with me in this."*
- *"This is activation from real work on real wounds. It is not evidence I am broken."*

Say it again. Slower.

**3. Write it down.**
Not to process it — to externalize it. Three to five sentences. What happened, when it happened, what you're feeling in your body right now. Writing moves the material from inside your nervous system to somewhere you can see it. You do not have to figure it out. Just write what is true.

**4. Do not be alone in your head with this.**
Call or text one safe person. Not to talk about the coaching work — just to not be isolated. *"I'm having a hard day and I just needed to hear a voice."* That is enough.

---

## Step 3 — Contact Your Coach

After you have done the steps above, contact your coach.

**How:** [COACH SPECIFIES — email / voicemail / text]
**Response window:** [COACH SPECIFIES — e.g., within 24 hours on business days]

**What to say:** You don't have to explain everything. A brief message is enough:

*"Something has surfaced between sessions that I'm having trouble settling. I've used the card and I'm okay right now, but I'd like to connect before our next session if that's possible."*

If your next session is within 72 hours, your coach will most likely address it there. If it's further out, your coach may schedule a brief call to help you contain it until then.

**What to expect from your coach:** A brief acknowledgment first. Then a decision together about what kind of support makes sense — addressing it in your next session, or a short bridge call. The bridge call is not a coaching session. It is containment. Its job is to help you stay regulated until we can do the real work properly.

---

## Step 4 — While You Wait

These are not for excavating the wound. They are for staying regulated.

**Pray the lament.** If you have been working with the lament practices, this is the time. Bring what is happening to God honestly. Not the cleaned-up version. The raw one.

*"God, I don't understand what is happening right now. I am overwhelmed. I am naming this before you and I am trusting you are here even though I cannot feel it."*

**Read one of these passages slowly — once:**
- Psalm 46:1–3, 10–11 — *God is our refuge and strength...*
- Psalm 62:1–2, 5–8 — *My soul waits in silence...*
- Isaiah 43:1–3a — *Fear not, for I have redeemed you...*
- Romans 8:38–39 — *Neither death nor life... shall separate us...*

Don't study it. Don't extract a lesson. Read it once, let it sit, and breathe.

**Limit excavation.** Do not spend the next two hours trying to figure out what everything means. What surfaced in the work is real and worth understanding — in session, with your coach, when there is a container for it. By yourself between sessions is not the time. Write it down so you don't lose it, then set it down.

---

## Wound-Type Notes

*Your coach will have told you which of the following patterns fits you. Read the one that applies.*

**If you are a Warrior type:**
Your first instinct when activated is to push through, shame yourself for being destabilized, or armor up. The protocol asks the opposite: stop, get the body, be honest with your coach. You are not weak for using this card. You are honest. This is what the Warrior pattern needs to learn to do.

**If you are a Hermit type:**
Your first instinct when activated is to go silent, not contact anyone, and manage this alone. The card's most important instruction for you is Step 4 — *do not be alone in your head with this.* Contact one person. And contact your coach. The silence that feels protective is the pattern — not wisdom.

**If you are a False Noble type:**
Your first instinct when activated is to perform okayness, tell yourself you're fine, or turn the distress into productivity (research the wound, make a plan, journal extensively). None of that is what this card is for. The card asks you to do the simple things: feet on the floor, say what is true, write three sentences, contact your coach. Stop at three sentences.

---

## If This Feels Like a Crisis

If at any point you are having thoughts of ending your life or harming yourself — stop and use one of these resources now. Do not wait to contact your coach first.

- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline:** Call or text **988** (U.S. — available 24/7)
- **Crisis Text Line:** Text **HOME** to **741741**
- **Emergency services:** **911**
- **Nearest emergency room**

After you have contacted a crisis resource and you are safe, let your coach know what happened.

Your coach cares about your wellbeing. Part of that care is being honest about what this coaching engagement can hold and what requires more than it can give. If what you are experiencing is crisis-level, you deserve crisis-level support. That is not a failure of the coaching work — it is the work taking you seriously.

*You are not alone in this. The formation work is real, the wounds are real, and the God who meets you in them is real. Take it one step at a time.*
