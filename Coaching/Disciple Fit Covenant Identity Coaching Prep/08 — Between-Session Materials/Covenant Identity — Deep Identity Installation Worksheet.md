# Covenant Identity — Deep Identity Installation Worksheet
*Between-Session Practice | Phases 3–4*

---

## How to Use This Worksheet

The Identity Installer gives you the what — a declared identity, anchored in Scripture, tied to behavioral intention. This worksheet addresses why it doesn't land and how to make it consolidate.

Three gaps the Installer doesn't address on its own:

- **The blocking schema** — every identity category has a corresponding psychological structure that resists it. The schema doesn't argue against the identity statement; it creates an emotional climate where the statement feels false. Naming what's blocking reception is as important as naming what's true.
- **The evidence story** — lists of evidence stay abstract. Identity consolidates when the evidence becomes a narrated story — something specific that happened, that you can inhabit again. A story is retrievable under pressure in a way a list is not.
- **The identity vote** — identity isn't only declared, it's enacted. Each time you act from your stated identity — especially when the blocking schema is pushing against it — you cast a vote for who you are. Those votes accumulate into lived conviction. Without them, the identity statement stays in the mind and never moves into the body's sense of self.

**How to work through this:**
1. Complete Parts 1–3 in one sitting (30–45 minutes). Don't rush Part 3 — the evidence story is where most of the work happens.
2. Design and run the identity vote in Part 4 during the week.
3. Fill in the Part 4 debrief after the vote.
4. Bring the completed sheet to your next session.

## Part 4 — The Identity Vote (Consolidation Layer)

Identity isn't only declared — it's enacted. Each time you act from your stated identity, especially when the blocking schema is pushing against it, you cast a vote for who you are. Those votes accumulate into lived conviction that no amount of declaration alone can produce.

An **identity vote** is a specific action taken from the stated identity — not a performance, not proof, but an act of alignment. Small votes count. The schema loses ground through accumulation, not through dramatic breakthroughs.

## Part 7 — Reflection Prompts *(bring to your next session)*

1. When did the blocking schema show up this week in relation to this identity? What triggered it?
2. Did you return to the evidence story? Was it retrievable? Did it feel different the second or third time?
3. What happened with the identity vote? What did the schema do with the result?
4. Is the identity statement landing differently than when you first wrote it?
5. What is the blocking schema still saying? What evidence is it still using?

### Coach Note — Opening the Following Session

Open with the identity vote debrief — not the declaration, not the felt sense, not a general check-in. The vote generated lived behavioral evidence. That is the highest-leverage entry point.

Opening questions:
- "What did you do?"
- "What happened?"
- "What did the blocking schema do with it?"

That last question matters as much as the result. Watch for the client minimizing a confirming outcome ("it worked out, but it was probably just luck"), dismissing evidence the schema couldn't predict, or struggling to let the vote count. That response pattern is the blocking schema defending the old identity — and naming it in the moment is the coaching move.

The evidence story debrief is the secondary entry point: "Did you go back to the story you wrote? Did it feel any different?" Over time the story should become more inhabitable, less effortful to access. If it's still bouncing off, the blocking schema is still running — and that's where the work is.
