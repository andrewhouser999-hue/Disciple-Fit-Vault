# Covenant Identity — Post-Session Integration Guide

*A client-facing guide for the 24-48 hours after a significant session*

> **Coach Note — Deployment condition (remove before giving to client):** Do not give this guide before the client's wound type has been identified. Wound type determines which section of "What You May Experience" the client receives — assigning the guide before wound typing produces either the wrong section or no relevant section. This guide is appropriate beginning in Phase 2, after the Character Wound Diagnostic Tool has been completed. Before handing to the client, mark or lightly personalize the relevant wound-type paragraph in "What You May Experience" so the client does not have to read all three and self-identify.

---

## What Just Happened

Something real happened in that session. You may not be able to name it yet — and you don't need to. What you are holding is active. The work that began in the session does not stop when you walk out; the Spirit continues what was opened. The next 24-48 hours are not empty time between sessions. They are a formation window.

This guide gives you language for what you may experience, and simple practices to receive what the Spirit is doing rather than manage it away.

When deep material surfaces in a session — a wound, a lie, a grief, a longing — it enters conscious awareness. Theologically, what was hidden is being brought to light (*"Everything exposed by the light becomes visible"* — Ephesians 5:13, ESV). Your mind and body are still processing what was opened. Both realities are true at once.

What you feel in the next 24-48 hours is not malfunction. It is the signature of real work.

---

## What You May Experience

*Read what resonates — don't label yourself.*

**If you tend toward the Warrior pattern (betrayal / weakness):**
You may feel exposed. The inner critic may activate: *I said too much. I looked weak. What does my coach think now?* You may feel the pull to immediately solve or fix what surfaced — to not just let it sit. The urge to close what was opened is not wisdom. It is the old protection mechanism. Resist it.

**If you tend toward the Hermit pattern (terror / overwhelm):**
You may feel flooded and want to withdraw entirely. Isolation will feel safe. Some quiet is good — but if you find yourself going completely dark, that is the pattern protecting itself, not you. The Spirit does His work in hiddenness, but not in shutdown. Stay gently connected to one safe person if you can.

**If you tend toward the False Noble pattern (ambivalence / not enough):**
You may feel two things at once: relief that something true surfaced, and anxiety about what it means. You may be tempted to explain it away, to manage the narrative (*it wasn't that significant*), or to move quickly to conclusions. Hold the ambivalence. Don't resolve it prematurely.

## What This Window Is For

Scripture has a consistent pattern: significant encounter requires a receiving period before the next thing comes. Not analysis. Not performance. Receiving.

**Elijah after Carmel (1 Kings 19:1–8)**
After his defining confrontation on Carmel, Elijah crashed. He ran, sat under a broom tree, and asked to die. What did God send? Not a sermon. Not next steps. An angel touched him and said, *"Arise and eat, for the journey is too great for you"* (1 Kings 19:7, ESV). Rest and food before revelation. Your session may have been that kind of journey. Body care before the next thing is not weakness — it is what God prescribed.

**Moses descending Sinai (Exodus 34:29–30)**
When Moses came down from the mountain, his face shone — and *he did not know it*. Others could see what had happened before he could. The session may have done more than you know. You are not always the best judge of what just occurred. Don't force an assessment yet.

**Jacob at Peniel (Genesis 32:24–30)**
Jacob wrestled with God and walked away limping. He named the place Peniel: *"I have seen God face to face, and yet my life has been delivered"* (Genesis 32:30, ESV). He was changed and marked. The limp was not a failure — it was proof of contact. If you feel marked by what happened, that is not a problem to fix.

**The disciples at the Transfiguration (Matthew 17:6–7)**
They fell on their faces, overwhelmed by what they had seen and heard. Jesus came to them, touched them, and said, *"Rise, and have no fear"* (Matthew 17:7, ESV). The touch came before the rising. You may need to be touched — by presence, by rest, by the Word — before you can stand back up. That is the sequence, not a detour.

## Practices for the Next 24-48 Hours

Work through these in order. Start at the top before moving lower. The order matters: each practice creates the conditions for the next one. Body care creates the conditions for receptive prayer. Receptive prayer creates the conditions for the Word to land. Brief naming creates the conditions for honest connection. If you skip ahead to what feels most comfortable, you are likely skipping what your pattern most needs to do first — Warriors tend to skip to analysis, Hermits to isolation, False Nobles to resolution. The order is the formation.

**1. Body care first.**
Rest. Drink water. Eat a real meal. Walk outside if you can. This is Elijah precedent, not self-indulgence. The journey is sometimes too great, and the body needs care before more can be received. Don't skip this step by going straight to spiritual practice.

**2. Receptive prayer — not petition.**
Rather than asking God for things or explanations, simply be present. A posture, not a request:

> *Lord, I'm here. I don't fully understand what happened. I'm open to what You want to do with it.*

You are not directing; you are receiving. Don't try to make something happen in prayer today.

**3. One scripture anchor.**
Not a study — one verse, held gently. Return to it throughout the day. Don't explain it or extract a lesson. Let it settle.

If you don't have one:
- *"He who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ."* (Philippians 1:6, ESV)
- *"Fear not, for I have redeemed you; I have called you by name, you are mine."* (Isaiah 43:1, ESV)
- *"Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."* (Matthew 11:28, ESV)

**4. Brief naming — not processing.**
If you journal, keep it short. This is not the time to analyze or conclude. Just name:
- What did I feel in that session?
- What surprised me?
- What felt true, even if I couldn't hold it?

You are marking the ground, not mapping the territory.

**5. One safe person.**
You don't need to explain or replay the session. But staying connected — a brief conversation about ordinary things — keeps isolation from shutting down what the Spirit is doing. You are not alone in this.

*If you cannot name a safe person right now:* That is worth bringing to your next session — it is itself significant data. For this window, the alternative is brief, low-demand human contact: a text exchange, a short phone call, sitting in a coffee shop. You are not trying to process; you are interrupting complete isolation. The goal is the same — don't be entirely alone with what the session opened.

## What to Hold Lightly

- **Don't try to conclude what the session meant.** The meaning often comes later, sometimes days later.
- **Don't share the session's content broadly before it has settled.** What is tender needs a protected space to become clear.
- **Don't perform normalcy if you aren't.** Give yourself permission to be quieter, slower, and less productive today. This is not avoidance; it is stewardship of what just happened.
- **Don't analyze your coach's responses** or wonder what they thought. The relationship is designed to hold what was said — you don't have to manage it.
- **Don't try to resolve it faster than it resolves.** The Spirit is not in a hurry. The formation window has its own pace.

---

## Closing Anchor

The Spirit who opened what was opened in that session is still at work. You don't carry this alone, and you don't have to manage it into resolution. The work belongs to Him.

> *"And I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ."*
> — Philippians 1:6, ESV

Receive. Rest. Let Him finish what He started.
