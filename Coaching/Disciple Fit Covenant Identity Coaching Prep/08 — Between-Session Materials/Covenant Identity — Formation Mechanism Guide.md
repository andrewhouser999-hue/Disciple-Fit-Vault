# Covenant Identity — Formation Mechanism Guide

*Practitioner reference with embedded client-facing psychoeducation guide. Section II is given to the client in Phase 1, Session 1 or early Session 2, after the Gap has been named. Designed to orient before formation resistance appears — not as a corrective after.*

---

## Section I — Practitioner Reference

### Deployment

Give in Phase 1, Session 1 or early Session 2 — after the Gap has been named and before wound work begins. The goal is to give the client a frame before they experience the frustration of slow formation, not after frustration has already been interpreted as failure. Assign the full client-facing section (Section II). No formal debrief is required — but if the client returns with questions that move toward wound content, receive them and bridge to session rather than encouraging solo processing.

### Re-Issuable Trigger

This document is designed to be re-read, not re-assigned. If the client hits a stretch in Phase 2, 3, or 4 where formation feels stalled, resistance feels like failure, or non-movement is being misread as evidence the process is not working — direct them back to this document. Do not rebuild the frame from scratch in session. Say: *"Do you still have the Formation Mechanism Guide? Before we draw any conclusions, I want you to re-read Part III."*

### Coach Deployment Notes by Wound Type

**Warrior:** may receive this document as information to master rather than orientation to inhabit. The Warrior will often be able to articulate the mechanism fluently within a session — and remain defended. If the client can explain the frame accurately but is still armored in session, the document has been managed, not received. In session, ask: *"What would it mean to stay in this without resolving it?"*

**Hermit:** may use the mechanism description as permission to wait indefinitely — *"I understand why it's slow, so I don't need to push yet."* Watch for the client using the frame to explain absence of engagement rather than stay present to what is emerging. In session, ask: *"What is actually present right now — not what you understand, but what you feel?"*

**False Noble:** will read this document evaluatively — assessing whether they are doing formation correctly, comparing their pattern against the descriptions, concluding whether they are on track. This is the performance pattern at work. In session, ask: *"What would it mean to receive this rather than evaluate it?"*

### What This Document Does Not Do

It does not excavate wound content, introduce lament, assign practices, or substitute for session. It orients. If the client raises questions during the week that move toward wound material, receive them at the next session.

## Section II — Client-Facing Guide

*Read this in full when your coach gives it to you. It takes about ten minutes. Keep it — you may be directed back to it later in the engagement.*

---

### Part I — How Formation Actually Works

Most of us carry an assumption about how change happens: you learn something true, you decide to be different, and over time — if you try hard enough — you become that. It is a reasonable assumption. It is also not quite how formation works.

The Scripture that governs this engagement is this:

> *"And we all, with unveiled face, beholding the glory of the Lord, are being transformed into the same image from one degree of glory to another. For this comes from the Lord who is the Spirit."*
> — 2 Corinthians 3:18 (ESV)

Transformation comes from *beholding* — from sustained, repeated encounter with God that reaches the whole person, not just the mind. Not from learning more. Not from deciding more firmly. Not from effort. The Spirit is the agent of transformation. The coaching, the practices, the honest reflection — these create conditions for what the Spirit is already doing. They do not produce the transformation themselves.

There is something else to understand. Every person operates on two levels at once: what they consciously believe, and what they actually expect and function from at a deeper level. These are not the same thing. You can believe — genuinely, consciously — that God loves you unconditionally, and still experience that love as conditional most of the time. You can believe you are not defined by your performance, and still feel the ground give way when you fail.

This is not a spiritual failure. It is how human beings are made. We carry what we have experienced, not only what we have concluded. Formation addresses both levels — not just what you believe, but what you actually live from.

---

### Part II — Why It Is Slow

Formation is not slow because something is wrong.

> *"Blessed is the man who walks not in the counsel of the wicked, nor stands in the way of sinners, nor sits in the seat of scoffers; but his delight is in the law of the Lord, and on his law he meditates day and night. He is like a tree planted by streams of water that yields its fruit in its season, and its leaf does not wither. In all that he does, he prospers."*
> — Psalm 1:1–3 (ESV)

The image is a tree — not a project completed, not a problem solved. A tree, planted near water, roots going down before fruit appears. The fruit arrives in its season — not on demand, not in proportion to how hard the tree is trying.

The deeper layer of how you actually function — what you expect from God, from yourself, from relationships — was not formed in a single event. It was laid down through years of relational experience, through patterns absorbed before you had words for them, through what was consistently true in your formative environment. It does not reshape by a moment of insight. It reshapes through new experience over time — through being known differently than you learned to expect.

What that means for this engagement: the coaching will not complete the formation. It opens what God is doing and creates conditions for the Spirit's ongoing work. You will leave this engagement different — and still in process. Both are true. Neither cancels the other.

---

### Part III — Why Resistance Is Expected

At some point in this process, you will hit resistance. A pull back toward old patterns. A sense of non-movement — that nothing is changing, or that you are the exception to how this works. A kind of internal pushback when something true is getting close.

This is not a sign that formation is failing. It is often a sign that something is being reached. When the deeper layer of how you actually function is being approached — through honest reflection, through declaration, through prayer — the patterns that were built to protect you will activate. That is what they were built to do. The resistance is not the enemy of the process. It is part of it.

Each wound type has a characteristic way of misreading this resistance. Yours is worth knowing:

**If you are a Warrior:** your armor was built to prevent the exposed, not-yet-arrived position that formation requires. Slow formation will tend to feel like weakness — as if you should be further along, as if you are not pushing hard enough, as if the process is not working or you are not working it correctly. The Warrior's resistance often feels like impatience, frustration, or the impulse to take the process over and accelerate it. It is the armor activating. It is not evidence that you are failing.

**If you are a Hermit:** your withdrawal pattern is triggered by anything that feels like exposure or demand. Slow formation will tend to feel like confirmation — as if change of this depth may not be possible for you specifically, as if the process is asking more than it has a right to, as if staying in it will only expose what you have spent years concealing. The Hermit's resistance often feels like a quiet conclusion that you are the exception, that nothing is happening, that it would be better to step back. It is the withdrawal pattern activating. It is not evidence that you are failing.

**If you are a False Noble:** your performance pattern turns everything into a metric. Slow formation will tend to feel like not measuring up — as if others are progressing faster, as if you are not doing this well enough, as if you should be further along by now. The False Noble's resistance often looks like increasing effort: trying harder to feel what you are supposed to feel, monitoring yourself for signs of change, producing more to demonstrate that the process is working. It is the performance pattern activating. It is not evidence that you are failing.

Each of these is the wound's response to the formation process — not evidence that the process is failing.

---

### Part IV — What to Do With This

The posture is cooperation, not management.

You are not responsible for producing your own transformation. You are responsible for staying present to what the Spirit is doing and returning to the practices when you drift. The formation belongs to God:

> *"And I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ."*
> — Philippians 1:6 (ESV)

He who *began* it will *complete* it. This is not passive — it requires that you show up, that you remain honest, that you do not manage the process away. But it is also not yours to carry. God is not waiting for you to figure out how to finish what He started.

When formation feels slow, resist the move to assess it before it is time to assess. Return to the practices. Return to honest prayer. Return to what is true, not what is felt in the moment.
