# Covenant Identity — Post-Session Client Debrief Card

*Practitioner reference with embedded client-facing card. Give alongside the Phase-Specific Reflection Guide before Phase 2 begins. Completed by the client within an hour or two of each session — before sleep, while the content is still warm.*

---

## Section I — Practitioner Reference

### Deployment

Give before Phase 2 begins, alongside the Phase-Specific Reflection Guide. The two tools serve different functions and work together — the Reflection Guide is ongoing noticing across the week; the Debrief Card is immediate capture right after the session ends.

**Timing is the tool.** The value of this card is in completing it while the session content is still present — within an hour or two, before sleep, before the noise of the day resumes. A debrief completed the following morning is useful but not the same thing.

### Distinction from Adjacent Tools

- **Post-Session Integration Guide** — what to *do* in the 24-48 hours after a heavy session (body care, receptive prayer, Scripture anchor). Assigned when a session is particularly heavy. The Debrief Card is used after every session.
- **Phase-Specific Reflection Guide** — ongoing between-session noticing across the week. Prompts are for after the immediate content has settled. The Debrief Card captures before it settles.

### Phase 4 Note

In Phase 4, tell the client explicitly: the answers they write in these debriefs are the primary raw material for the Growth Report. Their own language — how they describe what landed, what was hard, what they want to carry forward — is what the Growth Report is built from. The debrief card in Phase 4 is not optional.

### Wound-Type Watch-Fors (Coach Only)

**Warrior:** Question 1 answers tend to be conclusions about what the session produced rather than honest noticing of what it left unresolved. If the answer reads like a summary of insight, ask in the next session: *"What was still unresolved when you left?"*

**Hermit:** May not complete the card in Phase 2 — the session opened something and the card requires naming it again while it is still raw. Do not enforce. Ask in the next session: *"What was it like to try?"* The resistance is the material.

**False Noble:** Will complete all three questions with evident self-awareness and accuracy. The answers will be honest — and curated. Ask in session: *"What did you leave out?"*

## Section II — Client-Facing Debrief Card

*Complete this within an hour or two of each session — before sleep, before the day resumes. Ten minutes. Three questions.*

This is not a journal entry and not a reflection exercise. It is a capture — while the session content is still present, before it disperses. The Post-Session Integration Guide gives you practices for the hours after a heavy session. The Reflection Guide gives you prompts for noticing across the week. This card is different: it asks what just happened, while it is still warm.

---

*Before you begin, take thirty seconds:*
*"Holy Spirit, what do you want me to hold from today? I'm listening."*

---

**Question 1**
What is the one thing I am carrying out of today's session — the thing that landed, shifted, or stayed with me?

*One or two sentences. Not a summary of the session — the one thing that is most present right now.*

---

**Question 2**
What was hardest about today's session?

*Name it plainly. Not what was most significant — what was hardest.*

---

**Question 3**
What do I want to bring back into the next session?

*A thread that was opened and not closed. A question that stayed with you. Something you held back. One sentence.*

---

*If you are in Phase 4 of the engagement: your coach will use what you write here to build your Growth Report. Write honestly — your own words are what make the report yours.*
