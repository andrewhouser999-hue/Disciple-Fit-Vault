# Covenant Identity — Pre-Session Preparation Guide

*Practitioner reference with embedded client-facing practice card. Give to the client before Session 2. Universal across all four phases — assign once, keep through the engagement.*

---

## Section I — Practitioner Reference

### Deployment

Give before Session 2 — after the designed alliance and Gap have been named in Session 1, and before the wound work begins in Session 2. The client has enough context to use the practice by this point. Do not give in Session 1, which is typically intake and orientation.

Assign once. This is not rotated or replaced as phases progress. The posture does not change across phases. The content the client brings does.

### What This Is Not

Not a journaling prompt, not a between-session reflection tool, not a therapeutic warm-up, and not a practice for the coach — those are separate tools. This is five minutes of arriving intentionally rather than walking in from wherever the day left them.

### Coach Notes by Wound Type

**Warrior:** will arrive having constructed a session agenda — what needs solving, what the coach should address, what they want to cover. The pre-session practice asks them to check whether the agenda is replacing what is actually present beneath it. If the Warrior skips the orientation step or arrives reporting that nothing is present, they are in armor. In session: *"Before we get to what you prepared — what is actually here right now?"*

**Hermit:** the orientation step is the most likely point of resistance. Naming what they are bringing — even to themselves — carries exposure risk before the session has even begun. Do not reinforce the skip. If the Hermit reports having nothing to bring, or that they could not do the practice, ask what it was like to try: the resistance is the material.

**False Noble:** will arrive having prepared a thorough answer to the orientation question — a summary of the week, progress since last session, what they want the coach to know. The practice is not preparation for the coach. If a False Noble hands you a written summary at the start of a session, receive it warmly, then ask: *"What is present right now, underneath this?"*

## Section II — Client-Facing Practice Card

*Your coach will give you this before your second session. Keep it. Use it before every session from here forward.*

---

### Why This Matters

The sessions in this engagement open real content — things that are alive in you, things that are changing, things that are resisting change. What you bring into the room matters. Walking in directly from work, from a difficult conversation, from the noise of the day means you are arriving divided rather than present. Five minutes changes that.

This is not a ritual. It is not a formula. It is a decision to arrive.

> *"But I have calmed and quieted my soul, like a weaned child with its mother; like a weaned child is my soul within me."*
> — Psalm 131:2 (ESV)

---

### The Three Movements

**Five minutes. Before every session. Three steps.**

---

#### Move 1 — Arrive

Put down what you are carrying from the day.

Sit somewhere quiet — in your car, at your desk, wherever you are before the session begins. Slow your breathing. Let what just happened stay outside the conversation for now. You are not managing your emotional state or achieving calm — you are simply stopping. One to two minutes.

---

#### Move 2 — Orient

One question. One sentence answer. To yourself — not for your coach.

**What am I bringing into this session?**

Not an agenda for what you think should be covered. Not a summary of your week. Not what you want the coach to know. What is actually present right now — what is heaviest, most alive, most unresolved. Name it in one sentence and hold it.

You are not required to share this answer. It is yours. The point is arriving with honest internal access rather than arriving with a prepared presentation.

---

#### Move 3 — Open

A brief invitation. Not a formula — find your own words. Something like:

*"Holy Spirit, I'm here. Open what matters. I'm listening."*

Thirty seconds. Then go in.

---

### A Note for Your Wound Type

Each wound type has a characteristic way of shortcutting this practice. Knowing yours helps you catch it.

**If you are a Warrior:** you will likely arrive with an agenda — what needs to be solved, what you've decided should be addressed, what you want the session to accomplish. The agenda is not the problem. The problem is when the agenda replaces what is actually present beneath it. Before you go in, the orientation question is: *"What is here, underneath what I've prepared?"* The session can address your agenda. First, check what the armor may have organized away.

**If you are a Hermit:** the orientation step will be the hardest. Naming what you are bringing — even to yourself — feels like exposure before the session has even begun. You may be tempted to skip it, or to decide that nothing is particularly present. The practice is not disclosure. It is one honest sentence, to yourself, never required to share. If you find that nothing comes, notice what it is like to try. That is where the practice is.

**If you are a False Noble:** you will be tempted to prepare a thorough answer — a summary of the week, what you noticed, what you want your coach to know. This is not preparation for your coach. It is one sentence, for yourself, naming what is actually present before the prepared version takes over. The practice is not performing arrival. It is arriving.

---

*Five minutes. Arrive, orient, open. Then go in.*
