## How to Use This Document

This map serves two purposes simultaneously.

**For the client:** Read the lie class at the top of each category. If you recognize yourself, write your specific version of that lie in the field provided. Then read the promises in that category — they are addressed directly to you. Carry the ones that land hardest into your Deep Lie Work Worksheet as your *Who God Is*, *Who I Am*, *His Promise*, and *Scripture Arsenal* fields.

**For the coach:** The Coach Reference at the bottom of each category identifies which phase of the session model this category primarily serves and what client language signals it should be surfaced.

## 1. Identity
*Who I am in covenant — child, heir, priest, new creation, friend*

---

**The Lie Class This Category Addresses**

People who struggle here have absorbed the conclusion that they are defined by what they have done, what has been done to them, or what they have failed to become. The internal logic runs: *I know what I am — the evidence is my history.* This belief produces either relentless self-improvement effort (trying to become someone worth being) or resignation (accepting the damaged version as final). Either way, the true identity declared by God feels abstract, unearned, and too good to apply to the specific person looking back in the mirror.

**Your Lie in This Category:**
> *(Write the specific version of this lie that is most active for you. Be precise — vague lies produce vague work.)*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a) Text:** 2 Corinthians 5:17 — *"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old has passed away; behold, the new has come."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. This language does not appear in any prior covenant. It emerges from Christ's death and resurrection as the inaugurator of the new age.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Union with Christ. The believer is incorporated into Christ's death and resurrection (Rom 6:3–5), dying to the old self and rising in the new. The new creation is not moral improvement of the old — it is a new beginning in the same category as the original creation.

**(d) What This Means for You:** You are not a reformed version of who you were. The person shaped by failure, shame, and inherited pattern — that person died with Christ. What God sees when he looks at you is not your history; it is his Son's resurrection life at work in you. The old has passed. Not dimmed — passed.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a) Text:** Romans 8:15 — *"You have received the Spirit of adoption as sons, by whom we cry, 'Abba! Father!'"*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — fulfills and exceeds the Abrahamic *"I will be your God and you will be my people."* The covenantal relationship reaches its highest expression through adoption into the Son.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Spirit of adoption. The Holy Spirit bears witness with the believer's spirit (v.16), not as an argument to be accepted but as an internal testimony — the Spirit who knows the Father cries *Abba* through you.

**(d) What This Means for You:** You are not a servant earning favor or a distant follower hoping to qualify. You are a child — not by effort or spiritual achievement but by the Spirit's own witness. The same Spirit who raised Christ from the dead lives in you and addresses the Father on your behalf. You belong to this family.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a) Text:** 1 Peter 2:9 — *"You are a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for his own possession."*

**(b) Covenant source:** Transferred from the Mosaic covenant (Exodus 19:6 — *"a kingdom of priests and a holy nation"*) through Christ's high-priestly fulfillment. Under the Mosaic covenant this status was national and mediated through the Levitical priesthood. Under the New Covenant, it belongs to all believers through union with the one true Priest.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Christ's high-priestly office. He is the mediator of the New Covenant (Heb 8:6), and believers participate in his royal-priestly identity through union with him — not by lineage or appointment, but by incorporation.

**(d) What This Means for You:** You are not on the outside of what God is doing, watching others who seem more qualified. You have been brought inside — into the priestly family, with access to the presence and the vocation. Your prayers are not uncertain attempts. They are the offerings of a priest who belongs in the presence of God.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a) Text:** John 15:15 — *"No longer do I call you servants... I have called you friends, for all that I have heard from my Father I have made known to you."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. No prior covenant grants this relational standing. This is something Jesus offers, in the upper room, on the night of the covenant's inauguration, as one of its defining features.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** The Son's own disclosure. Jesus transfers to his disciples the intimacy and shared purpose that belongs to covenant friendship — including knowledge of the Father's plans. This is not earned; it is given by the one who has the authority to give it.

**(d) What This Means for You:** The God of the universe does not merely tolerate you or deploy you. He calls you friend — which means he has made his purposes known to you, included you in his counsel, and chosen to be *with* you rather than merely above you. That is not a metaphor. It is what he said.

---

**Promise 5**
**(a) Text:** Romans 8:17 — *"And if children, then heirs — heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — fulfills and exceeds the Abrahamic covenant. Galatians 3:29: *"If you are Christ's, then you are Abraham's offspring, heirs according to promise."* The inheritance is no longer land or national blessing — it is co-inheritance with the Son of everything the Father has.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Adoption through the Spirit. As a child of God by adoption, the believer inherits alongside the natural Son — not a diminished portion, but a co-heir's portion.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Everything that belongs to Christ by right belongs to you by grace. You are not a spiritual beggar hoping for scraps. You are an heir of the living God, and your inheritance is secured in a place where nothing in creation can diminish it. That is the status you carry right now — in this season, in this struggle, in this body.

---

**— Coach Reference —** *(not client-facing)*

**Session model fit:** Primarily (2) Address eternal foundation — establishing who the client is before God as the load-bearing ground of all other work. Also serves (1) Locate Lie Nukes when the identity lie is actively detonating under circumstantial pressure (job loss, relational rejection, moral failure).

**Coach trigger:** Client language: *"I'm just not that kind of person," "after everything I've done," "I don't think I'm who God wants to use," "I know who I really am."* Any statement that locates the client's identity in the past or in behavioral evidence rather than in covenantal declaration.

## 2. Access
*My standing before God — boldness at the throne, no condemnation*

---

**The Lie Class This Category Addresses**

People who struggle here believe that access to God must be earned — that they need to clean themselves up, resolve the current failure, or reach a minimum spiritual condition before they can genuinely approach God. The internal logic runs: *God is available to the faithful and the cleaned-up — but not to me, not right now, not in this condition.* This produces hesitation, delayed prayer, distance in the spiritual life, and a pattern of waiting to feel worthy before bringing the real thing.

**Your Lie in This Category:**
> *(Write the specific version of this lie that is most active for you. Be precise — vague lies produce vague work.)*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a) Text:** Hebrews 4:16 — *"Let us then with confidence draw near to the throne of grace, that we may receive mercy and find grace to help in time of need."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. Under the Mosaic covenant, the Most Holy Place was accessible only to the High Priest, once per year, with blood. Direct access for ordinary believers was not possible. This promise belongs entirely to the New Covenant.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Christ's high-priestly intercession. He passed through the heavens (v.14) as the believer's forerunner and now intercedes at the right hand of the Father (Heb 7:25), making their access perpetually open and guaranteed.

**(d) What This Means for You:** You do not have to wait until you feel worthy to come. The invitation is not to the cleaned-up — it is to the weak, the struggling, and the sin-sick. The throne you are invited to approach is described as a throne of *grace* — which means the entire design of it is to give you what you need, not to evaluate whether you deserve to be there.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a) Text:** Romans 8:1 — *"There is therefore now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. Under the Mosaic covenant, condemnation for covenant violation was the operative verdict for the unfaithful. This promise is Christ's fulfillment of the law's demands on behalf of the believer — absorbing the condemnation so the verdict can be reversed.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Justification. The legal verdict of *not guilty* is declared over the believer through faith in Christ's righteousness imputed to them (Rom 3:21–26). The courtroom is not ongoing — the verdict has been rendered and it stands.

**(d) What This Means for You:** The courtroom is closed. The verdict has been delivered — not guilty — and it was not based on your performance. Every internal voice and every external accusation that tells you God is against you, that you are condemned, that your failures have canceled your standing is lying. The Judge has spoken. The case is closed.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a) Text:** Hebrews 10:19–22 — *"We have confidence to enter the holy places by the blood of Jesus, by the new and living way that he opened for us through the curtain... let us draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — fulfills and terminates the Mosaic sacrificial system. The veil of the temple was torn at Christ's death (Matthew 27:51), signaling the end of the mediated-access system and the opening of the direct way.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Christ's body as the new and living way. He is the path through which the believer enters the Most Holy Place — not a path to a location but a path to the Father's presence, opened by his death and kept open by his resurrection.

**(d) What This Means for You:** The veil that kept ordinary people out of God's presence was torn when Christ died. You are not on the outside looking in, hoping for a special dispensation. The path is open — not someday, not after more growth, not after the current failure is resolved. Now. Jesus opened it and it cannot be shut.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a) Text:** 1 John 2:1 — *"If anyone does sin, we have an advocate with the Father, Jesus Christ the righteous."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. The concept of a righteous advocate who speaks for the believer before the Father after they sin is unique to the New Covenant.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Christ's ongoing mediatorial role. He speaks on the believer's behalf not as an apologist but as one who has already satisfied the legal requirement — his righteousness is the ground of his advocacy.

**(d) What This Means for You:** When you sin, you do not lose your standing before God and have to rebuild it. You have an Advocate who rises to speak for you. Not to excuse what you did but to present his own righteousness in your defense. You are not abandoned when you fail. You are defended.

---

**— Coach Reference —** *(not client-facing)*

**Session model fit:** Primarily (2) Address eternal foundation — establishing the unconditional nature of the client's access as the ground on which any other work can happen. Critical for clients whose God-representation is Punitive or Transactional, where access is felt as conditional.

**Coach trigger:** Client language: *"I haven't been praying because...," "I feel like God and I are distant right now," "I need to get things right before...," "I know I should come to God but..."* Any deferred approach to God tied to a self-evaluation of unworthiness.

## 3. Provision
*God's material and spiritual supply*

---

**The Lie Class This Category Addresses**

People who struggle here are living under a scarcity conviction — the felt certainty that there will not be enough. Enough money, enough grace, enough time, enough strength, enough opportunity. The lie is often specific to a domain (financial, emotional, relational), but its logic applies everywhere: *my situation is too precarious, too specific, or too far gone for God's provision to reach.* It produces hoarding, anxiety-driven striving, withholding, and a chronic low-grade fear of lack that does not respond to evidence of current sufficiency.

**Your Lie in This Category:**
> *(Write the specific version of this lie that is most active for you. Be precise — vague lies produce vague work.)*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a) Text:** Philippians 4:19 — *"And my God will supply every need of yours according to his riches in glory in Christ Jesus."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — fulfills the Abrahamic promise of blessing through Christ. The phrase *"in Christ Jesus"* locates the supply within the new covenant provision, not in Mosaic law-keeping or Abrahamic national identity.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Union with Christ. The supply is measured by what Christ has secured — *"according to his riches in glory"* — not by what the believer has earned or demonstrated.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Every need — not most needs, not your spiritual ones — every need you have is within the scope of what God has committed to meet. He is not stretched by your situation. His supply is not a fixed resource that might run short before he reaches you. It is measured by what he has in Christ, and that is inexhaustible.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a) Text:** Matthew 6:31–33 — *"Do not be anxious... your heavenly Father knows that you need them all. But seek first the kingdom of God and his righteousness, and all these things will be added to you."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant teaching — Jesus, as the inaugurator of the kingdom, speaks this within his covenant community. It fulfills the Mosaic provision of manna in the wilderness (God knowing and meeting daily need) but reframes it around the kingdom's priorities rather than law-keeping.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Kingdom priority. As the believer seeks God's reign first, provision follows as a covenant commitment — not a reward for performance but a design feature of life ordered rightly.

**(d) What This Means for You:** The anxiety about what you will have is not your burden to carry forward. God already knows what you need before you ask. The invitation is not to stop caring about your circumstances — it is to stop letting provision be the thing you are driving toward. Let it follow the right priority, and it will follow.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a) Text:** Romans 8:32 — *"He who did not spare his own Son but gave him up for us all, how will he not also with him graciously give us all things?"*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — echoes the Abrahamic covenant (as God did not withhold Isaac, so he did not withhold his Son; Genesis 22:16). Paul uses the greater gift to guarantee the lesser.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** The logic of gift. If God gave the hardest thing to give freely, the lesser things follow by covenantal necessity. The argument moves from the cross to all other provision: the character that gave one cannot withhold the other.

**(d) What This Means for You:** He already gave you the thing hardest to give. If he held nothing back then — if the cross is what it is — he will not become stingy now. Whatever you are afraid he will withhold: bring that fear back to what he has already given. Let the cross answer the scarcity.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a) Text:** 2 Corinthians 9:8 — *"And God is able to make all grace abound to you, so that having all sufficiency in all things at all times, you may abound in every good work."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. The Mosaic covenant linked material blessing to obedience (Deuteronomy 28). This promise locates abundance in grace, not law-keeping — an entirely different mechanism.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Grace. The supply is grace — unearned, not contingent on faithfulness, and not diminished by failure. *"All grace... all sufficiency... all things at all times"* — the repetition is deliberate.

**(d) What This Means for You:** The supply of grace does not run out when you do. You are not going to exhaust God's capacity to meet your need — in material things, in strength, in wisdom, in patience, in the grace to do the next right thing. He has more than enough. And it is given freely.

---

**Promise 5**
**(a) Text:** James 1:5 — *"If any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask God, who gives generously to all without reproach, and it will be given him."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. In the Old Testament, wisdom was mediated through Torah, the sages, and specific leaders. In the New Covenant, wisdom is given directly to any believer who asks — universally accessible, with no reproach attached.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Direct gift by God to the asking believer. The Spirit is the agent (1 Cor 2:10–13); the requirement is asking in faith.

**(d) What This Means for You:** You do not have to figure this out alone and you do not have to feel foolish for needing wisdom. The decision in front of you, the relationship you cannot navigate, the next step you cannot see — wisdom for all of it is available. Ask. God gives it without making you feel like you should have known.

---

**— Coach Reference —** *(not client-facing)*

**Session model fit:** Primarily (3) Build wound awareness — scarcity often signals a deeper wound around deprivation, abandonment, or distrust of God's goodness. Also serves (1) Locate Lie Nukes when a financial or circumstantial crisis has detonated the scarcity lie, making it acutely active.

**Coach trigger:** Client language: *"I'm afraid there won't be enough," "I can't afford to...," "I don't have the capacity for...," "I don't know where it's going to come from."* Also watch for hoarding, withholding generosity, and constant financial anxiety as behavioral indicators even when language is vague.

## 4. Protection
*God's covenantal preservation and security*

---

**The Lie Class This Category Addresses**

People who struggle here carry a fragile, performance-dependent security — the felt conviction that their standing with God could be lost if they fail badly enough, wander long enough, or prove faithless enough times. The internal logic runs: *I know how faithless I can be — and God's patience has a limit I might be approaching.* This produces either exhausting spiritual vigilance (trying to maintain their own security) or a creeping despair when they cannot maintain it. The security is conditional in feeling even when they affirm it is unconditional in doctrine.

**Your Lie in This Category:**
> *(Write the specific version of this lie that is most active for you. Be precise — vague lies produce vague work.)*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a) Text:** John 10:28–29 — *"I give them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand. My Father, who has given them to me, is greater than all, and no one is able to snatch them out of the Father's hand."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. The Good Shepherd imagery fulfills the Davidic shepherd-king typology, but David could not guarantee this. Jesus can — and does.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** The Father's sovereign grip. The believer is held by both the Son and the Father simultaneously. The security is not the believer's grip on God but God's grip on the believer — and no created force can override both.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Your security does not depend on how tightly you are holding on. It depends on how tightly he is holding. No failure, no wandering, no spiritual crisis, no external force, no internal collapse can reach into the Father's hand and remove you. You are held — not by your faithfulness but by his.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a) Text:** Romans 8:38–39 — *"For I am sure that neither death nor life, nor angels nor rulers, nor things present nor things to come, nor powers, nor height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. Paul names every conceivable category of threat — cosmic, circumstantial, temporal, spatial, supernatural — and places the love of God in Christ above all of them.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** The love secured in Christ. The inseparability is covenantally guaranteed by Christ's own love, which is stronger than every force Paul can name.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Name your worst fear — the thing you are most afraid could finally separate you from God. Paul has already named it. And the answer is: not that. Not any version of that. The love of God in Christ Jesus is not breakable by anything in creation. You cannot fall far enough, fail badly enough, or wander long enough to land outside it.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a) Text:** Hebrews 7:25 — *"He is able to save to the uttermost those who draw near to God through him, since he always lives to make intercession for them."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — supersedes the Aaronic and Levitical priesthood, which was temporary and required continuous sacrifice. Christ's priesthood is eternal, his sacrifice is once for all, and his intercession is perpetual.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Christ's perpetual high-priestly intercession. He is not a past Savior whose work is finished but a present intercessor who continuously mediates the believer's standing before the Father.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Right now, in this moment, Christ is before the Father making intercession for you. Not occasionally. Not when you remember to pray. Continuously. You are not left to maintain your own standing before God — someone who cannot fail is maintaining it for you.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a) Text:** Psalm 91:1–2 — *"He who dwells in the shelter of the Most High will abide in the shadow of the Almighty."* (New Covenant application)

**(b) Covenant source:** Originally a Mosaic-era wisdom psalm. Applied under the New Covenant through Christ, who is the true fulfiller of Psalm 91 (Satan quotes it in the wilderness — Matthew 4:6 — but Jesus is the one who actually abides in the shelter of the Most High). Believers share in his protection through union with him. *Misuse alert: this psalm has been taken to promise physical invulnerability, financial protection, or immunity from illness for believers who "claim" it in faith (common in health-and-wealth and word-of-faith contexts). That reading requires removing the psalm from its framework of covenantal relationship — in which the sheltered one still faces "the pestilence" and "the arrow" but is not ultimately destroyed by them — and turning it into a conditional transaction. Paul, who prays three times for a thorn to be removed and is answered "my grace is sufficient," is a New Covenant corrective. The shelter is real; the invulnerability from suffering is not promised.*

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Union with the One who truly abides. The protection described is not a formula for invulnerability — it is the description of life sheltered in the One who is invulnerable. Believers share Christ's protected position with the Father.

**(d) What This Means for You:** The shelter described here does not mean God protects you from all suffering — Paul suffered, the apostles were martyred, and the faithful in Hebrews 11 did not always receive the promised deliverance in this life. What it means is that you are in the safest possible position — hidden in the One no created force can ultimately overwhelm. You are not exposed. You are sheltered. And what you endure while sheltered is never without him.

---

**Promise 5**
**(a) Text:** Philippians 1:6 — *"And I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — fulfills the Abrahamic *"I will be your God"* through God's covenant faithfulness. The one who initiates is obligated by his own character to complete.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** God's own covenant commitment. The work of salvation is his work. Its completion is his responsibility — not a collaborative project the believer must uphold their end of.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Your spiritual journey is not going to stall or fail because you could not maintain it. God started this. The one who called you is faithful — not faithful *if you perform*, but faithful because that is who he is. He will finish what he began. In you.

---

**Coach trigger:** Client language: *"What if I've gone too far?," "I'm afraid I'm losing my faith," "I don't know if I'm really saved," "what if God gives up on me."* Also: any pattern of spiritual crisis following moral failure, where the failure is interpreted as evidence that the security has been lost.

## 5. Purpose
*Covenant calling and gifting*

---

**The Lie Class This Category Addresses**

People who struggle here have concluded that they don't have a real purpose — or that whatever purpose existed has been forfeited by their failures, wrong turns, or wasted years. The internal logic runs: *Other people are called; I am just surviving. My life is too ordinary, too damaged, or too late to matter to what God is doing.* It produces passivity, comparison, paralysis, and a resigned shrinking from any sense of calling. Sometimes it produces frantic striving as a counter-move — trying to manufacture meaning rather than receive what has been given.

**Your Lie in This Category:**
> *(Write the specific version of this lie that is most active for you. Be precise — vague lies produce vague work.)*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a) Text:** Ephesians 2:10 — *"For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand, that we should walk in them."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. The language of new creation (*"created in Christ Jesus"*) places this promise entirely within what Christ's work makes possible. The good works were prepared *beforehand* — before the believer existed, let alone performed.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** New creation. The believer's purpose is embedded in their recreation in Christ. The works were prepared by God before they walked in them — purpose precedes performance; the believer walks into what is already prepared, not constructs it by striving.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Your purpose was not forfeited when you failed. It was not canceled by your wrong turns. It was prepared before you drew your first breath, in eternity, by God. You are not constructing meaning — you are walking into what he already laid out. The question is not whether purpose exists for you. It is whether you will show up for it.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a) Text:** 1 Peter 2:9 — *"You are a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for his own possession, that you may proclaim the excellencies of him who called you out of darkness into his marvelous light."*

**(b) Covenant source:** Transferred from Mosaic covenant (Exodus 19:6) through Christ's fulfillment. Under the Mosaic covenant, the priestly calling was restricted to the tribe of Levi. Under the New Covenant, it belongs to every believer through union with the one true Priest and King.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Christ's high-priestly and royal mediation — all believers are incorporated into his office and share his priestly-royal vocation of declaration and intercession.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Your calling is not incidental or conditional on your level of spiritual development. You were chosen — not because of what you bring but because of who chose you. The work of declaring God's excellencies is not reserved for the theologically trained or the publicly visible. It is the covenant vocation of every member of this people, including you.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a) Text:** 1 Corinthians 12:7 — *"To each is given the manifestation of the Spirit for the common good."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. Spirit gifting poured out universally at Pentecost (Acts 2), fulfilling Joel 2:28–29. Under the Old Covenant the Spirit rested on specific leaders for specific seasons — prophets, kings, judges. Under the New Covenant the Spirit is given to all.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** The outpouring of the Spirit. Every believer receives a manifestation of the Spirit — not as a spiritual reward but as a design feature of the new covenant community. The gifts are distributed as the Spirit determines (v.11), not earned or achieved.

**(d) What This Means for You:** You were not overlooked when gifts were distributed. The Spirit gave you something specific — something the body of Christ genuinely needs and cannot fully function without. You are not filling a seat. You are filling a role that was designed with you in view. The body is incomplete without what you carry.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a) Text:** Romans 12:6 — *"Having gifts that differ according to the grace given to us, let us use them."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. Gifts are distributed according to grace, not merit or spiritual seniority.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Grace distribution. The specific gifting each believer carries is a grace-allocation — given by the Spirit to serve the body in a way no one else can serve it in exactly the same way.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Your specific gifting is not an accident and it is not a consolation prize compared to what others carry. It was grace-designed — given to you precisely so you can serve in the way only you can serve. Comparison with others who appear more gifted misunderstands the design. The body needs what you carry, not a copy of what someone else carries.

---

**Promise 5**
**(a) Text:** Jeremiah 29:11 — *"For I know the plans I have for you, declares the LORD, plans for welfare and not for evil, to give you a future and a hope."* (New Covenant application)

**(b) Covenant source:** Originally spoken to Israel in Babylonian exile — a Mosaic covenant promise of restoration to a specific nation in a specific historical crisis. *The common exegetical objection is that lifting this promise for individual Christian application is irresponsible: it was addressed to exilic Israel, not to individuals in a different century and context, and applying it out of that setting flattens the covenantal meaning.* The response is not to ignore the original context but to trace the promise through its covenantal fulfillment: the promise to Israel finds its ultimate referent in Christ, the true Israel (see Hosea 11:1 / Matthew 2:15; Isaiah 49 / the servant), who is himself the one returning from exile and entering the inheritance. Those in Christ inherit what was promised to Israel — in Christ (Gal 3:16, 29). This is not an evasion of the exegetical concern; it is the New Testament's own approach to OT promise.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Union with Christ as the true Israel. The covenant promises to the nation are fulfilled in Christ and applied to all who are incorporated into him (Gal 3:16, 29).

**(d) What This Means for You:** God's intention toward you is not harm. The season of confusion, loss, wasted years, or stagnation is not the final word on your story. God spoke future and hope into a people sitting in rubble in Babylon. He speaks it into you — wherever you are sitting right now.

---

**— Coach Reference —** *(not client-facing)*

**Session model fit:** Primarily (1) Locate Lie Nukes — purpose lies are frequently primed by job loss, ministry failure, comparison with peers, or mid-life transitions that strip visible markers of significance. Also serves (2) Address eternal foundation when purpose has never been established as a covenantal reality rather than a performance-contingent one.

**Coach trigger:** Client language: *"I don't know what I'm here for," "I feel like I've wasted my life," "I'm not like [other person]," "I missed my window," "I don't really have any gifts."* Also: sustained passivity, refusal to step into opportunities, or frantic overwork as compensation for felt purposelessness.

## 6. Community
*Life within the covenant people*

---

**The Lie Class This Category Addresses**

People who struggle here have concluded that they do not truly belong — that the community around them is either a performance-based structure they must maintain or a group that would reject them if they knew the truth. The internal logic runs: *Real belonging requires me to be a version of myself I haven't become yet.* This produces surface-level participation, withheld need, performed togetherness, and a pattern of being physically present while remaining existentially alone. It can look like faithful church attendance while being completely unknown.

**Your Lie in This Category:**
> *(Write the specific version of this lie that is most active for you. Be precise — vague lies produce vague work.)*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a) Text:** Hebrews 10:24–25 — *"And let us consider how to stir up one another to love and good works, not neglecting to meet together, as is the habit of some, but encouraging one another."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. The gathering of believers is a new covenant institution with no direct Mosaic parallel. It is designed by the Spirit who indwells the community.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** The shared Spirit. The community is possible and necessary because the same Spirit dwells in every believer, creating a common life that is more than organizational membership.

**(d) What This Means for You:** The instruction to gather is not a religious obligation imposed from outside. It is a covenant design — you were built to need the people of God and to be needed by them. Not gathering is not a neutral choice. It is refusing what God designed specifically for your formation and theirs.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a) Text:** 1 Corinthians 12:12–13 — *"For just as the body is one and has many members... so it is with Christ. For in one Spirit we were all baptized into one body."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. The body metaphor for the community of believers is unique to the New Covenant. Under the Old Covenant the people of God were organized nationally; under the New they are organically united by the Spirit who baptizes all into one body.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Spirit baptism into one body. The unity is not manufactured by organizational effort or shared doctrine alone — it is created by the Spirit who incorporates every believer into the same body, transcending background, status, and culture.

**(d) What This Means for You:** You are not an optional attachment to the body of Christ. You are a member — which means your absence is felt and your presence is necessary. The belonging you are looking for is not conditional on your performance. It is covenantally established by the Spirit who placed you here.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a) Text:** Ephesians 4:15–16 — *"Speaking the truth in love, we are to grow up in every way into him who is the head, into Christ, from whom the whole body... makes the body grow so that it builds itself up in love."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** The Spirit's distribution of gifts within the body — each part contributing causes growth; growth is not possible without every part working properly.

**(d) What This Means for You:** When you withhold yourself from the community — your gift, your honest need, your real experience — the whole body is diminished by exactly what you are holding back. Your participation is not a favor you offer when you feel ready. It is a function the body requires. The growth of the people around you is connected to whether you show up fully.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a) Text:** Galatians 6:2 — *"Bear one another's burdens, and so fulfill the law of Christ."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — the *"law of Christ"* (v.2) exceeds the Mosaic neighbor ethic (love your neighbor as yourself) by adding the posture of gentleness and the goal of restoration (v.1).

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** The Spirit of sonship — as members of the same family, believers carry what family carries. The mutual burden-bearing is an expression of the shared life created by the Spirit.

**(d) What This Means for You:** You are allowed to need. The burden you are carrying — the one you have not told anyone, the one you have decided is yours alone to manage — is exactly what this community was designed to share. Carrying it alone is not faithfulness. It is refusing the covenant design that says you were never meant to.

---

**Promise 5**
**(a) Text:** John 13:34–35 — *"A new commandment I give to you, that you love one another: just as I have loved you... By this all people will know that you are my disciples."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. Jesus calls this a *"new commandment"* to distinguish it from the Mosaic standard of loving your neighbor as yourself. The standard is now Christ's own sacrificial love — a categorically higher and more costly measure.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Christ's love poured out through the Spirit. The love that is commanded is not self-generated; it is sourced in the Spirit who distributes the love of God into the believer's heart (Romans 5:5). It is given before it is commanded.

**(d) What This Means for You:** The community God has placed you in is not a social arrangement — it is a covenant witness. When you love the people around you with the self-giving kind of love Christ showed you, something happens that the watching world has no category for. This is not optional conduct for the spiritually advanced. It is the mark of the covenant people. It is the mark of you.

---

**— Coach Reference —** *(not client-facing)*

**Session model fit:** Primarily (3) Build wound awareness — community avoidance almost always has a wound underneath: prior rejection, betrayal, relational trauma, or the learned conclusion that being known leads to being hurt. Also serves (1) Locate Lie Nukes when a recent relational rupture within the community has reactivated the belonging lie.

**Coach trigger:** Client language: *"I don't really fit in," "I go but I don't really connect," "people wouldn't understand," "I'm not close to anyone at church," "I've been burned before."* Also: social isolation framed as introversion or busyness; consistent pattern of not asking for help.

## 7. Future
*Inheritance, resurrection, new creation*

---

**The Lie Class This Category Addresses**

People who struggle here have lost the weight of the future — or it has never had weight to lose. The internal logic runs: *This is all there is. The suffering is permanent, or at least longer than I can bear. God's promises about eternity are too distant and too abstract to matter to what I am facing right now.* It produces despair, short-term collapse, the inability to endure present suffering because the future has become invisible, and a spiritual tunnel vision that can only see the present season. Sometimes it presents not as overt hopelessness but as a functional detachment from eternity — believing the promises intellectually while living as if this is all there is.

**Your Lie in This Category:**
> *(Write the specific version of this lie that is most active for you. Be precise — vague lies produce vague work.)*

---

**Promise 1**
**(a) Text:** Romans 8:18 — *"For I consider that the sufferings of this present time are not worth comparing with the glory that is to be revealed to us."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. Paul frames the comparison between present suffering and coming glory within the new creation horizon inaugurated by Christ's resurrection. This perspective is unavailable outside of the New Covenant.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Christ's resurrection as firstfruits. His resurrection is the beginning of the new creation (1 Cor 15:20); the believer's resurrection and glorification are guaranteed by the same Spirit who raised him (Rom 8:11).

**(d) What This Means for You:** What you are carrying right now is real. The suffering is not minimized. But Paul, writing from prison, says the coming glory is so far beyond what you are enduring that the comparison does not hold. You are not at the end of the story. You are in the middle of it, and the middle is not the measure of where it ends.

---

**Promise 2**
**(a) Text:** 1 Peter 1:3–5 — *"An inheritance that is imperishable, undefiled, and unfading, kept in heaven for you, who by God's power are being guarded through faith for a salvation ready to be revealed."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — fulfills and exceeds the Abrahamic land promise and the Davidic kingdom promise. The inheritance is not a geographic region but an eternal kingdom, secured in heaven and kept there by God's own power.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Resurrection and divine preservation. The inheritance is grounded in the living hope created by Christ's resurrection from the dead (v.3), and it is kept by God — not maintained by the believer's faithfulness.

**(d) What This Means for You:** The inheritance waiting for you cannot be stolen, devalued, destroyed, or diminished by anything happening in your life right now. It is being kept — not by you but by God — in a place where none of the forces working against you can reach it. You have not lost it. You cannot lose it. It is as secure as the One keeping it.

---

**Promise 3**
**(a) Text:** Revelation 21:3–4 — *"Behold, the dwelling place of God is with man... He will dwell with them, and they will be his people... He will wipe away every tear from their eyes, and death shall be no more."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant consummation — the fulfillment of the recurring covenant promise *"I will be your God and you will be my people"* (first stated to Abraham, formalized at Sinai, promised through the prophets) reaches its final, permanent, unmediated form in the New Jerusalem.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Direct divine dwelling. The tabernacle and temple were anticipations — God's presence mediated through structure, priesthood, and sacrifice. The New Jerusalem is the reality: God's presence unmediated, permanent, and filling everything.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Everything the temple was pointing toward — God fully present, unfiltered, without distance — is coming. The gap you sometimes feel between yourself and God right now is not the permanent condition. There is a day coming when he will wipe every tear from your eyes with his own hand. Not a metaphor. His hand. That day is as certain as his covenant.

---

**Promise 4**
**(a) Text:** Romans 8:23 — *"We ourselves, who have the firstfruits of the Spirit, groan inwardly as we wait eagerly for adoption as sons, the redemption of our bodies."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. Bodily resurrection is a new covenant reality established by Christ's bodily resurrection as firstfruits (1 Cor 15:20). The Old Testament pointed toward it; Christ established it.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** The Spirit as deposit and firstfruits. The Spirit given to the believer now is the down payment guaranteeing the full redemption of the body to come (2 Cor 1:22, 5:5). The guarantee is not hope-as-wish but hope-as-certainty.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Your body's limitations, pain, and decay are not the final word on who you are. The same Spirit who raised Christ from the dead will raise your body — this body, transformed — into the life of the age to come. What you are groaning under right now is a birth pain, not an ending.

---

**Promise 5**
**(a) Text:** 2 Corinthians 4:17 — *"For this light momentary affliction is preparing for us an eternal weight of glory beyond all comparison."*

**(b) Covenant source:** New Covenant — original. Paul places temporal suffering within the eschatological horizon of new creation, arguing not just that the glory outweighs the suffering but that the suffering is actively *preparing* the glory.

**(c) New Covenant mechanism:** Eschatological reversal and productive suffering. The verb *katergazetai* (preparing, working out) is active — the suffering is not passive endurance but is being used by God to produce something of incomparably greater weight. The mechanism is not explained, but the apostolic guarantee is unqualified.

**(d) What This Means for You:** Your suffering is not pointless. Something is being produced in you through what you are enduring — something of such eternal weight that it will feel, looking back, like the suffering was light and brief. You cannot see the weight yet. But it is being prepared. Nothing you are going through right now is being wasted.

---

**— Coach Reference —** *(not client-facing)*

**Session model fit:** Primarily (2) Address eternal foundation — the weight of the future is part of the load-bearing ground of faith; a faith with no future weight cannot sustain present suffering. Also serves (3) Build wound awareness when present suffering has collapsed the client's eschatological horizon, making them functionally hopeless even if they affirm the doctrine.

**Coach trigger:** Client language: *"I just don't see a way forward," "what's the point," "it's always going to be like this," "I can't imagine things being different."* Also: spiritual resignation, loss of motivation for growth, or a faith that is entirely present-tense with no reference to what is coming.

## Document Provenance

**What this document is:** A practitioner reference and client worksheet that maps New Covenant promises by lie class — seven categories, each with 4–5 promises traced to their covenantal source, a client-facing lie field, client-facing application language, and a coach reference section. Designed for direct client use between sessions and as a coach diagnostic tool in-session.

**Hermeneutical methodology:**
This document operates from a progressive covenantalist framework (Gentry & Wellum, *Kingdom Through Covenant*, 2012; Wright, *The Mission of God*, 2006). The governing assumption is that the Abrahamic, Mosaic, and Davidic covenants form a unified covenantal story that reaches its telos in the New Covenant inaugurated by Christ. Old Testament promises are not extracted from their original context and applied directly to individual believers. Instead, the covenantal trajectory is traced through its fulfillment in Christ, and participation in that fulfillment is located in union with him.

The primary exegetical mechanism is union with Christ (Gal 3:16, 29): Christ is the singular seed in whom the covenant promises find their ultimate referent; believers are incorporated into him and therefore inherit what was promised to him. This is the apostolic hermeneutic — Paul's handling of Abrahamic promises in Galatians 3–4, his treatment of Mosaic covenant fulfillment in Romans and 2 Corinthians, and his application of inheritance promises in Romans 8 and Ephesians 1 all employ this logic. The document follows that exegetical pattern consistently: each (b) field names the covenant of origin, traces the trajectory, and locates the New Covenant application through union with Christ rather than through simple extraction.

**Acknowledged hermeneutical position:**
Progressive covenantalism is a specific position within evangelical hermeneutics, distinct from both classical dispensationalism and traditional covenant theology. Classical dispensationalism (and to a lesser degree progressive dispensationalism) objects to some OT-to-NC promise applications on the grounds that certain promises belong to national Israel in a future dispensation and are not transferable to the church through union with Christ. This document does not adjudicate that debate. It operates from the progressive covenantalist position as its working framework, names the contested points where they arise (see Jeremiah 29:11 and Psalm 91 entries), and provides the exegetical rationale for the position taken. Practitioners operating from a dispensationalist framework should flag those entries and substitute exegetically grounded alternatives.

**Primary sources:**
- Scripture (ESV) — primary source for all promise texts
- Gentry & Wellum, *Kingdom Through Covenant* (2012) — governing hermeneutical architecture; progressive covenantalism as the interpretive framework for OT-to-NC promise continuity
- Wright, *The Mission of God* (2006) — missional-covenantal methodology; the way OT covenant promises carry forward through the mission of God into Christ and the New Covenant community

**What this document does not do:**
- Does not provide exhaustive exegetical commentary on individual texts — each (b)/(c) entry is practitioner-level, not scholarly
- Does not resolve dispensationalist objections — flags them and gives the working response; not a full theological defense
- Does not generate additional promises beyond the mapped categories — use the [[New Covenant Promise Map — Generation Prompt]] for category-specific expansion
