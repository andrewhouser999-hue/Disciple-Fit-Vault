# Covenant Identity Coaching — System Document Master List
*A complete inventory of all documents in the Covenant Identity Coaching system, organized by category and roughly chronological order of use within an engagement. Status: Done or Suggested.*

*Last updated: May 2026*

## 1. Model Foundation
*The documents that define what Covenant Identity Coaching is. These are not phase-specific — they are the conceptual and theological ground the entire system stands on.*

---

**✅ What Is Covenant Identity Coaching — Client-Facing.md**
Plain-language explanation of the model for prospective clients. No jargon. Describes the problem being addressed, what the coaching does, what to expect, and who it is for. The first document a prospective client sees.

**✅ Covenant Identity Coaching — Full Model Reference.md**
Comprehensive 16-section practitioner reference. All frameworks defined with jargon explained inline. The coach's complete model document — theological architecture, psychological integration, how the model differs from therapy and generic coaching. The canonical source for all model language.

**✅ Biblically Integrated Covenant Identity Coaching — Framework.md**
*(Located in Coaching Business folder)*
Original 14-section framework document. The conceptual origin of the model. Predates the Full Model Reference. Overlap is intentional — this is the development record.

**🔲 Biblical Foundation Reference — The 9 Layers**
Each of the nine covenant identity layers mapped to their specific biblical texts with exegetical depth. Not a devotional — a practitioner reference giving the coach textual and theological substance behind each layer. Supports grounded declaration work and prevents the layers from functioning as slogans rather than rooted truth.

## 2. Business & Client Acquisition
*Documents used before a client commits to an engagement. Not yet built.*

---

**🔲 Discovery Call Framework**
Structure for a 30-45 minute discovery call with a prospective client. How to present the model accessibly, assess fit (theologically, psychologically, motivationally), and invite commitment. Includes questions to ask, what to listen for, and how to handle common objections or hesitations.

**🔲 Pricing & Engagement Structure Document**
How the engagement is packaged and priced. What a 6-session vs. 10-session engagement includes. What the Growth Report represents as a deliverable. How to handle session extension requests. Internal reference document.

## 3. Client Onboarding
*Documents a client receives after committing, before the first session.*

---

**✅ Disciple Fit Coaching — Engagement Onboarding Document.md**
Client-facing narrative document sent when the client commits, before session 1. Covers: the model's organizing principle (the Gap), phase structure in plain language (4 phases with plain-language descriptions), what sessions are like, the faith dimension (prayer, Scripture, the coach is not a pastor or therapist), what the growth documents are and why they exist, what is expected of the client, and what the coaching is not. Closes with a reference to the Coaching Agreement they will be asked to sign.

**✅ Disciple Fit Coaching — Coaching Agreement.md**
Formal client agreement. 12 sections: what coaching is and is not (scope of practice); faith integration disclosure (explicitly Christian, ICF-aligned); engagement structure and session logistics; fees and payment (package fee with $145 intake component named); cancellation policy (24 hours, reschedule within 3 days or next session); refund and early termination policy (tiered: full refund pre-intake, minus $145 after intake, non-refundable after session 1 with 12-month credit for unused sessions); growth documentation consent; confidentiality and its limits; not a crisis service (988, 741741, 911 listed); referral acknowledgment; acknowledgment and consent; signature block. Placeholder brackets for fee, session medium, frequency, and contact details.

## 4. Intake System
*Completed before and around session 1. Establishes the documented baseline against which all growth is measured.*

---

**✅ Covenant Identity Intake — Design Basis & Annotation.md**
Medium-agnostic master reference for the intake form. **✅ Covenant Identity Intake — Coach Annotation Reference.md**
Standalone quick-reference for reviewing a completed intake form before session 1. Clean per-question format: diagnostic target, how to read answers, follow-up questions to prepare, referral watch signals. Working hypothesis template at the end.

Coach-completed document (not client-facing). Part A: 12 verbatim language fields pulled from the Tally form. Part B: 4 supplemental fields gathered verbally in session 1 — felt-sense description, present-story statement, covenant identity self-assessment, coach synthesis statement. Summary Baseline table. The foundational "before" record for the entire engagement.

## 5. Phase 1 — Covenant Orientation (Sessions 1–2)
*Establishes the theological frame, surfaces the God-representation, names the Gap, and builds the alliance sufficient for Phase 2.*

---

**✅ Covenant Identity — Phase 1 Session Tools (Covenant Orientation).md**
Full practitioner session tool document. Includes: phase job description, session structure (sessions 1 and 2 in detail), primary question library (20+ questions with coach notes), coach observation guide, 7 common pitfalls with navigation, integration notes (what feeds in, what feeds out), and transition marker.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Affections Discovery — In-Session Protocol.md**
Coach-facing in-session execution of Stage 1. Five-layer protocol (Surface Inquiry, Pattern Surfacing, Dependency Probe/Trust, God-Exclusion Map, Identity Revelation) with in-session pacing, stay/move signals at each layer, pre-question observation stance (somatic and relational — Thompson/Allender), story-reading and somatic observation layers, diagnostic signals quick reference, transition logic to Stage 2, and connection to the Whole-Person Identity Framework. Open during early sessions; Stage 1 reference throughout the engagement. Companion to the Affections Discovery Process (Coaching Preparation folder) which holds the full theological foundation and question bank.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Session Prep Template.md**
5-part pre-session readiness tool. Part 1: coach self-check (what the coach is carrying into the room, countertransference, posture). Part 2: client review (last session one-sentence summary, commitment follow-up, schema trend, operative language). Part 3: phase-specific review (shifts by phase — pulls specific Intake Addendum fields and Phase Session Tool checkpoints). Part 4: session intention (primary job, question to hold, what to resist). Part 5: final identity check. Copyable template block per session with coach notes on each part.

**✅ Covenant Identity — God-Representation — Research Basis & Synthesis.md**
Raw research and cross-theorist synthesis underlying the God-Representation Deepening Tool. Four full theorist summaries: Rizzuto (developmental formation, the living God vs. theologian's God, transformation mechanisms), Hall (implicit vs. explicit God-image, empirical basis, neurological rewiring), Benner (believed-in vs. experienced God, encounter and surrender pathway), Kirkpatrick (compensation vs. correspondence hypotheses, God as attachment figure, attachment patterns under stress). Closes with a cross-theorist synthesis identifying shared findings, areas of tension, and open research questions. Saved for further research and adaptation.

**✅ Covenant Identity — God-Representation Deepening Tool.md**
Practitioner reference for working with the surfaced God-representation across all four phases. Theoretical frame synthesizing Rizzuto (living God vs. theologian's God), Hall (implicit vs. explicit God-image), Benner (believed-in vs. experienced God), and Kirkpatrick (God as attachment figure). Four God-representation types with relational origins, coaching presentation, attachment pattern, and schema correlation: Punishing, Absent/Distant, Contingent/Transactional, Capricious/Unsafe. Three practitioner errors (theological correction, premature resolution, conflating the two layers). The Deepening Process across all four phases. Type-specific coaching approaches (core task, specific approaches, what to avoid). Full question library (30+ questions organized by phase use). Integration notes with schema correlation table.

## 6. Phase 2 — Wound Mapping (Sessions 2–4)
*Excavates the formation material maintaining the Gap. Produces the map Phase 3 uses.*

---

**✅ Covenant Identity — Phase 2 Session Tools (Wound Mapping).md**
Full practitioner session tool document. Includes: phase job description (with Phase Handoff Note from Phase 1), session structure, primary question library (25+ questions with coach notes), somatic tracking and schema activation observation guide, 7 common pitfalls with navigation, integration notes, and transition marker.

**🔲 Schema-Specific Intervention Guides**
One guide per primary schema: Defectiveness/Shame, Unrelenting Standards, Emotional Deprivation, Abandonment/Instability. Each guide covers: how the schema presents in a coaching context, its specific maintenance behaviors (surrender/avoidance/overcompensation), its relationship to covenant identity (which layer it most directly severs), and targeted intervention questions and approaches within the covenant frame.

**✅ Immunity to Change — Big Assumption Work** *(integrated into Deep Lie Work Worksheet)*
The Big Assumption (Kegan & Lahey column 4) is fully built into the Deep Lie Work Worksheet as Part 3 (stated commitment, competing behaviors, the worry underneath, the Big Assumption named, origin, schema connection) and Part 4 (behavioral experiment design and debrief). Covenant identity framing throughout. A standalone four-column ITC worksheet in its pure form is not built — the integrated format is more appropriate for this context, where the Big Assumption is always worked in relationship to a named lie and schema.

**🔲 Lament Protocol**
A standalone tool giving lament its full treatment. Theological frame (Brueggemann's arc, the lament psalms as permission structure), specific prompts for introducing lament in session, what to do when the client's lament includes anger at God, how to distinguish genuine lament from emotional venting, and how lament connects to covenant identity movement.

## 9. Growth Tracking System
*Spans the full engagement. Captures evidence of growth in real time and synthesizes it at two key points.*

---

**✅ Covenant Identity — Session Growth Notes Template.md**
Post-session coach documentation (5-10 minutes). 8 fields: follow-up from last session, growth moments, identity votes, language evolution (before/after table), schema activity, spiritual formation markers, coach observations, next session intentions. One-sentence session summary. The raw material for both the Mid-Engagement Snapshot and the Growth Report.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Mid-Engagement Snapshot Template.md**
Client-facing document presented in session 4-5. Read aloud together. Two-column then/now language comparison table, Q9 score comparison, 4-5 named milestones with dates, what others noticed, coach observations, and where the second half is going. Coach preparation notes section (removed before presenting).

**✅ Covenant Identity — Growth Report Template.md**
End-of-engagement client-facing document. 12 sections: Where You Were (verbatim intake language), What We Worked Through (narrative arc), What Was Hard, Specific Milestones, Identity Votes, What Others Noticed, What the Coach Noticed, Where You Are Now (verbatim final session language), The Gap Closed (side-by-side table), Covenant Identity Layer Movement table, What Continues, Letter to Beginning Self. Signed and dated by the coach.

## 10. Practitioner Reference & Tools
*Background reference documents and standalone tools not specific to a single phase.*

---

**✅ Covenant Identity — Practitioner Reference Index.md**
Navigation hub for the diagnostic/theoretical cluster. Document map with prescribed read-order (Integration Rationale → Whole-Person Identity Framework → Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic → Affections Discovery In-Session Protocol → Character Wound Diagnostic Tool), diagnostic sequence diagram, upstream reference table, and scope boundary notes. Entry point for any practitioner new to the model or returning after time away.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Integration Rationale.md**
Theoretical justification for using Thompson, Allender, Johnson, and Collins together within a biblically governed frame. Governing position: Christian psychology (not classic integration or secular synthesis). Two integration criteria (confirmatory vs. additive). Per-contributor justification: what each contributes, the limits on what is imported, and what is explicitly excluded — Siegel's telos, Erikson's normative identity framework, CBT as sufficient for change, humanistic telos. Common grace epistemological basis. Read once for theoretical grounding; consult when integration questions arise in practice.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Whole-Person Identity Framework.md**
Survey-depth practitioner anthropology reference. Governing framework: imago Dei (origin, distortion, telos), soul's faculties (mind, will, affections), and five-dimension whole-person structure (cognitive, affective, volitional, relational, somatic). Mechanism layer (Thompson — neurological/relational formation), diagnostic layer (Allender — character wound; Collins — clinical boundary). Scope boundary and sources/boundary notes. Scripture as governing plumbline. The conceptual frame for what the coach is looking at in every session. Contributors: Thompson, Allender, Johnson, Collins.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Character Wound Diagnostic Tool.md**
Secondary diagnostic layer; used after the affections picture is specific enough to inform a type hypothesis — not before. Three types: Warrior (core wound: betrayal and weakness), Hermit (core wound: terror and too much), False Noble (core wound: ambivalence and not enough). Per type: wound + sin framing, behavioral markers, story patterns, emotional signature, God-image distortion, false self vs. refined false self distinguishing marker, true self emergence signals, coaching implications, and clinical referral triggers. Additional sections: Warrior/False Noble early-session distinguishing tells, blended presentations, and type-to-affections probe connection. Works in conjunction with the Affections Discovery In-Session Protocol.

**✅ Exceptional Coaching Practice — What Is Missed, Meaningful & Important.md**
28-section comprehensive reference on what makes coaching exceptional. Covers: quality of presence, silence, body tracking, naming what isn't said, calibrated challenge, not solving, resistance, metaphor, pacing, reframing, managing the coaching relationship, faith-specific dimensions, and the expanded question library. The practitioner's formation document.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Crisis & Referral Protocol.md**
9-section safety and liability reference. Covers: scope of practice definition; non-crisis referral triggers (clinical presentations, scope creep signals, faith-specific situations); crisis categories with C-SSRS severity triage table (suicidal ideation, self-harm, acute trauma response, abuse disclosure, psychotic presentation); 8-step in-session crisis protocol (scannable under pressure); referral conversation scripts with common faith-based resistance responses; concurrent coaching and therapy guidance; documentation requirements; post-crisis coach care; and a pre-engagement preparation checklist including QPR training requirement.

**✅ Secured in Father — Practitioner Design Notes.md**
Practitioner reference layer behind *Secured in Father — Attachment Formation & Encounter Preparation* (located in Coaching Me/). Seven sections: (I) Component Evidence Map — 8 components documented with empirical basis, mechanism, design-for-effect, evidence level; (II) Sequencing Rationale — 5-zone architectural logic for the 10 dimensions (threshold conditions, active relational capacities, identity consolidation, demanding terrain, return mechanism), load-bearing vs. flexible sequence constraints; (III) Anticipated Critiques — 5 domains (clinical psychology, biblical counseling, confessional Reformed, cessationism, coaching ethics) with specific scholars, steelmanned arguments, responses, and remaining exposure; (IV) Outcome Markers — cross-pattern markers, per-pattern progress trajectories, practitioner observation signals, what progress is not, temporal expectations; (V) Contraindications — 3 absolute, 4 conditional with routing table; (VI) Load-Bearing vs. Modifiable — 13 load-bearing elements with mechanism-break explanations, modifiable elements table with governing constraints, three critical distinctions; (VII) Integration Map — system position diagram, four connection maps (Entry Guide, Affections Discovery, Character Wound Tool, Diagnostic Lens Transition Logic) with information flow, integration logic, and category errors to prevent. 26-source log with evidence levels.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Deep Work Worksheets — Design Rationale.md**
Practitioner reference covering the theoretical foundation and design logic behind both the Deep Lie Work Worksheet and the Deep Identity Installation Worksheet. Sections: the problem the worksheets solve (schema layer gap in standard lie/identity work); theoretical foundation (Young — schema therapy and the three maintenance modes; Kegan & Lahey — Immunity to Change and the Big Assumption; McAdams — narrative identity theory; somatic integration; Ephesians 4:22-24 as the biblical frame); why second-level tools rather than modifications; design logic for each worksheet (layer order, key section rationale, why the debrief is the session re-entry point); sequencing relationship between the two worksheets; what to watch when refining (specific client signals per worksheet); and what is outside the scope of these tools. The document to consult when revising either worksheet after client use.

**✅ Session Prep Template**

**🔲 Self-Supervision Template**
A structured post-session debrief the coach does on their own. Distinct from Session Growth Notes (which tracks the client) — this tracks the coach: places of avoidance, countertransference, where the coach felt pulled or stuck, the coach's own formation markers. Supports ongoing coach development and prevents pattern blindness.

## 11. Between-Session Client Materials
*What the client uses between sessions to extend and reinforce the session work.*

---

**✅ Covenant Identity — Between-Session Support Materials.md**
Practitioner reference with client-facing practice guides. Coach preamble (how to use, precondition framework, theological framing of practices as encounter rather than self-help). Universal base set for all clients: between-session commitment, identity vote log, weekly reflection prompts. Type-specific practice profiles for all four God-representation types: what works, what is contraindicated, specific practices with client-facing instructions. Critical contraindication documented: Absent/Distant (avoidant) type should not receive extended solitary contemplative practice before relational grounding is established — research evidence cited. Written practice templates: lament template (Brueggemann structure), identity vote log, weekly reflection guide, type-specific prayer prompts. Session debrief guide for the following session, including how to handle non-follow-through. Phase guidance table (Phase 1–4 practice escalation).

**✅ Covenant Identity — Deep Lie Work Worksheet.md**
Client-facing fillable worksheet for between-session use in Phases 2–3. Works a single lie at three simultaneous layers: (1) Theological — the lie's claim about God and identity, addressed by declaration and Scripture; (2) Psychological — the schema operating beneath the lie, addressed by activation signature mapping, origin excavation, and the pause practice; (3) Behavioral — the Big Assumption (Kegan & Lahey Immunity to Change framework), addressed by a structured behavioral experiment that generates real-world counter-evidence. Integration point in Part 5 anchors the declaration somatically so truth is retrievable under activation pressure. Part 6 sets a coordinated weekly practice tying all three layers together. Introduced after Wound Mapping surfaces the operative schema. Coach assigns one lie and one schema — not all. Experiment debrief is primary material for the following session. Fills the Immunity to Change standalone worksheet gap (Phase 2 suggested item).

**✅ Covenant Identity — Deep Identity Installation Worksheet.md**
Client-facing fillable worksheet for between-session use in Phases 3–4. Extends the Identity Installer by addressing three gaps the 7-part format doesn't reach: (1) The blocking schema — the psychological structure actively resisting each identity category landing, with schema reference table, distortion pattern field, and a per-category interruption practice; (2) The evidence story — moves the evidence field from list to narrated first-person story, making it retrievable under activation pressure; (3) The identity vote — a specific behavioral enactment of the stated identity with a structured debrief, generating lived counter-evidence against the blocking schema. Secondary section on relational ecology (who reflects this identity accurately vs. distorts it). Includes a distilled daily practice (one sentence, one verse, felt sense check) separate from the weekly worksheet work. Coach assigns one identity category per worksheet. Identity vote debrief and evidence story are primary material for the following session.
