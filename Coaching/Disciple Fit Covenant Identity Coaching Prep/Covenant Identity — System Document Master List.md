## 1. Model Foundation
*The documents that define what Covenant Identity Coaching is. These are not phase-specific — they are the conceptual and theological ground the entire system stands on.*

---

**✅ Covenant Identity Coaching — Practice Definition.md**
Governing definition document. All other model documents, tools, and session protocols answer to this one when they conflict — it defines the practice with precision across five dimensions (telos, mechanism, container, practitioner role, client role), distinguishes it from its three contributing disciplines (Christian life coaching, biblical counseling, spiritual formation), and states the definition at three levels of compression (internal, practitioner, client-facing). Built 2026-05-14.

**✅ Covenant Identity Coaching — Practice Descriptions (Three Forms).md**
The three compression levels named by the Practice Definition, written out: a one-sentence description, a few-sentences description, and a one-minute spoken pitch — all authored in Dallas Willard's voice. Spine held constant across all three: the false self governing from below, covenant truth meeting it, the Spirit forming the true self into being. Built 2026-06-25.

**✅ Disciple Fit Covenant Identity Coaching — White Paper.md**
High-level practitioner-facing overview of the Covenant Identity Coaching model. States the core claim and distinguishes the model from adjacent approaches. The compressed version of the model's thesis — read before the Full Model Reference to orient the high-altitude frame before going deep.

**✅ What Is Covenant Identity Coaching — Client-Facing.md**
Plain-language explanation of the model for prospective clients. No jargon. Describes the problem being addressed, what the coaching does, what to expect, and who it is for. The first document a prospective client sees.

**✅ Covenant Identity Coaching — Manifesto.md**
Practitioner's declaration of convictions: what the practice exists to do, its anthropology, the transformation mechanism, its telos (the nine interior qualities, matching the Interior Life as Telos document), what the practice is and is not, the practitioner formation requirement, and a closing conviction statement. Built 2026-05-25; updated 2026-07-08 to add "available" to the telos list to match Interior Life as Telos.

**✅ Biblical Case for Inner Healing as Foundation of Intimacy and Discipleship.md**
Argues — from biblical characters and canon-wide scriptural evidence, not proof-texted from a single passage — that removing maladaptive internal structures (shame, insecure attachment, distorted God-representation, codependency, unfinished grief) is not supplemental to discipleship and intimacy with God but the deepening condition for both. Exegetical grounding for the model; foundational theological case for authorship use. Built 2026-05-09.

**✅ The Purpose and Limits of the Mosaic Law — Biblical Evidence.md**
*(Located in 01 — Model Foundation/Support Documents for Model Foundation/)*
Supporting research for the Biblical Case document's 2 Corinthians 3 footnote. Traces the biblical evidence — Exodus through the Prophets to Paul — for the argument that the Mosaic law exposes human inability to keep it and points forward to the need for inner transformation; the explicit theological argument is Pauline, the OT supplies the narrative pattern and prophetic anticipation. Built 2026-05-09.

**✅ Developing a Biblical Understanding of Covenantal Relationship with God.md**
Biblical-theological research document grounding the model's core claim. Surveys covenant relationship with God across Scripture (creation covenant through new covenant) and establishes it as the constitutive foundation of covenant identity work. The exegetical basis for why covenant relationship is the governing frame, not a thematic overlay on an independent anthropology. Informs grounded declaration language and prevents the nine identity layers from functioning as slogans rather than rooted truth.

**✅ Covenant Identity Coaching — Full Model Reference.md**
Comprehensive 16-section practitioner reference. All frameworks defined with jargon explained inline. The coach's complete model document — theological architecture, psychological integration, how the model differs from therapy and generic coaching. The canonical source for all model language.

**✅ Covenant Identity Coaching — Theory of Change.md**
Eight-node causal chain from presenting condition (disordered affections) to transformed interior life: entry, diagnostic precision, felt inadequacy, receptive posture, covenant truth spoken into activated belief, the Spirit's witness, between-session consolidation, telos. Every tool in the system is anchored to a node; mechanism gaps are named explicitly. Built 2026-06-23.

**✅ Covenant Identity Coaching — The CIC Promise.md**
Practitioner-facing synthesis answering "what is the CIC promise and how does it make a difference in a life?" Weaves the Manifesto's diagnosis/telos, the Theory of Change's eight-node mechanism, the Interior Life as Telos's nine qualities, and the one-sentence Practice Description into one continuous articulation. Built 2026-07-08.

**✅ Covenant Identity Coaching — The Promise (Client-Facing).md**
Client-facing companion to The CIC Promise: the promise stated directly to the client in second person, the nine interior-life qualities translated into felt/accessible language, and an explicit "What I'm Not Promising" honesty section (pace, the Spirit's agency rather than the coach's, not a substitute for church/community, not a substitute for clinical care). Built 2026-07-08.

**✅ Biblically Integrated Covenant Identity Coaching — Framework.md**
*(Located in Coaching Business folder)*
Original 14-section framework document. The conceptual origin of the model. Predates the Full Model Reference. Overlap is intentional — this is the development record.

**🔲 Biblical Foundation Reference — The 10 Layers**
Each of the ten covenant identity layers mapped to their specific biblical texts with exegetical depth. Not a devotional — a practitioner reference giving the coach textual and theological substance behind each layer. Supports grounded declaration work and prevents the layers from functioning as slogans rather than rooted truth. *Substantially fulfilled by the Biblical Foundations Index (built 2026-06-12, Section 10 below), which includes a ten-layer architecture anchor table — remains open only if deeper per-layer exegesis beyond that table's scope is still wanted.*

## 4. Intake System
*Completed before and around session 1. Establishes the documented baseline against which all growth is measured.*

---

**✅ Covenant Identity — Two-Tier Intake Protocol.md**
Overview document describing the two-tier structure of the Covenant Identity intake process. Tier 1: the written Tally form completed before session 1. Tier 2: verbal follow-up conducted within session 1. Explains the protocol logic, what each tier captures and why, and how they work together to establish the diagnostic baseline. Read before the individual intake documents (Design Basis, Annotation Reference, Intake Addendum, Build Guide).

**✅ Covenant Identity Intake — Design Basis & Annotation.md**
Medium-agnostic master reference for the intake form. Covers the form's primary job, design principles, tensions and resolutions, diagnostic target map, complete question set with full coach annotations, session 1 integration notes, and medium adaptation notes. The permanent rationale document — use this when adapting the form to a new medium.

**✅ Covenant Identity Intake — Tally Build Guide.md**
Step-by-step implementation guide for building the intake form in Tally.so. Exact question text, question types, section structure, welcome/thank you page copy, Airtable integration settings, and in-session verbal follow-up reference table.

**✅ Covenant Identity Intake — Coach Annotation Reference.md**
Standalone quick-reference for reviewing a completed intake form before session 1. Clean per-question format: diagnostic target, how to read answers, follow-up questions to prepare, referral watch signals. Working hypothesis template at the end.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Intake Addendum (Coach).md**
Coach-completed document (not client-facing). Part A: 12 verbatim language fields pulled from the Tally form. Part B: 4 supplemental fields gathered verbally in session 1 — felt-sense description, present-story statement, covenant identity self-assessment, coach synthesis statement. Summary Baseline table. The foundational "before" record for the entire engagement.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Pre-Discovery Screen — Tally Build Guide.md**
Step-by-step implementation guide for building the pre-discovery call screening form in Tally.so. A shorter screen designed to run before the discovery call, not a substitute for the full intake. Screens for basic fit indicators (faith context, presenting concern, relevant history flags) without the full diagnostic depth of the main intake form. Allows the coach to enter the discovery call with basic context already in hand.

**✅ Covenant Identity — Airtable Schema Spec.md**
Field-level schema for the Airtable base backing the two-tier intake system: Clients (master pipeline), Pre-Discovery Responses, and Intake Responses tables. Read after both Tally build guides — this is the infrastructure that receives what those forms produce. Includes automation notes: native Tally→Airtable plus Airtable native Automations cover alpha-phase needs; Make.com not required yet. Built 2026-07-06.
