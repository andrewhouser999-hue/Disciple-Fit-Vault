# Welcome to Your Covenant Identity Coaching Engagement
**Disciple Fit Coaching | Andrew Houser**

---

*You've decided to do something that takes courage: to look honestly at the distance between who you are declared to be in Christ and who you actually experience yourself to be — and to close that distance with intention and support.*

*This document is your orientation. It tells you what to expect, how the engagement works, and what will be asked of you. Read it before our first session. Keep it.*

---

## What This Coaching Is Built On

Most growth efforts — sermon series, Bible studies, accountability groups, self-help books — address what you know or what you do. They work at the level of information or behavior. This coaching works at a different level: identity.

The organizing principle of this engagement is that who you are in Christ is already declared. The covenant identity — the ten layers of what Scripture says is true about you right now, not after you improve — is already in place. The coaching works in the Gap between that declaration and your lived experience of it. The Gap is real. It is maintained by specific formation material: early messages you received and believed, vows you made to protect yourself, competing commitments that make change feel dangerous even when you genuinely want it.

Closing the Gap is not a performance project. It is a formation process. That distinction is the whole thing.

---

## How the Engagement Is Structured

Our work together moves through four phases. Each has a specific job.

**Phase 1 — Covenant Orientation (Sessions 1–2)**
We establish the framework, name the Gap, and gather the baseline — the specific language you use right now to describe yourself, God, and your situation. This is the "before" record. We will return to it.

**Phase 2 — Wound Mapping (Sessions 2–4)**
We go back before we go forward. Not to live in the past — to understand what formed the Gap. What did you decide about yourself, about God, about what is safe? What obligation is quietly making change feel dangerous? This phase produces the map the rest of the engagement uses.

**Phase 3 — Identity Installation (Sessions 4–8)**
This is the heart of the work. Using the map from Phase 2, we do targeted work at the specific points where the Gap lives — declaration practice, interrupting the schema before it runs, re-authoring the story, bringing the wound honestly to God. This is where formation happens.

**Phase 4 — Integration & Close (Sessions 8–10)**
We consolidate what was built, design the forward structure that will hold it, and close the engagement with a Growth Report — a document built from your own words, before and after, that becomes a record you keep.

The phase boundaries are guides, not rigid lines. Your engagement will have its own pace and its own shape.

## What Sessions Are Like

Sessions run 60–75 minutes. We will meet [via video / in person — specify medium].

I will ask direct questions. Some of them will be difficult. The quality of what happens in our sessions is directly proportional to your willingness to be honest — with yourself as much as with me. I will not ask anything I don't believe serves the work.

I take notes during and after sessions. Those notes feed three documents I prepare for you during the engagement: a mid-point Growth Snapshot (presented around session 4–5) and a final Growth Report (presented at or after our last session). Both are built from your own verbatim language — what you said at intake and what you said after months of work. The before/after is the evidence.

Between sessions, I will give you a specific commitment to carry — not a to-do list, but one small act connected to what we worked on. What you do with it matters. What you discover when you do it matters more.

## What the Faith Dimension Looks Like

This coaching is explicitly Christian. The framework is theological. The covenant identity architecture is drawn directly from Scripture. Prayer may be a part of our sessions. Scripture may be referenced. I operate from the conviction that formation is ultimately God's work, that the coaching relationship is a temporary human container for something the Holy Spirit is doing, and that what we are working toward is the renovation of the whole person — not just adjusted behavior or updated beliefs.

If you have theological questions about the framework, bring them. If something in the coaching conflicts with your understanding of Scripture, name it. This is not a place for performed agreement.

I am not a pastor, ordained minister, or licensed therapist. I am a coach operating from a biblically-grounded model. If pastoral care or clinical treatment becomes appropriate during our engagement, I will name that directly and help you connect with the right resource.

## What Will Be Asked of You

**Show up.** To sessions, and to the between-session work. Partial engagement produces partial results.

**Be honest.** The coaching holds whatever you bring. What it cannot hold is the performance of growth without the substance of it.

**Trust the process when it's slow.** Formation is not linear. There will be sessions that feel like nothing moved. There will be sessions that land in ways that surprise you. The arc across the full engagement is what matters, not any single session.

**Bring the real thing.** Not the theological version. Not the cleaned-up version. What is actually happening in you. That is what we work with.

## What the Growth Documents Are

I maintain records of our work together for one reason: clients forget where they started. Growth amnesia is real — it is a documented feature of human psychology. By the time something has changed, it is hard to remember what it felt like before it changed. The growth documents exist to make your own growth undeniable.

**Intake Addendum** — completed by me after your intake form and session 1. Captures your verbatim language at baseline. You will not see this document — it feeds the others.

**Session Growth Notes** — completed by me after every session. Tracks specific growth moments, language shifts, identity votes, schema activity, and formation markers. You will not see these — they feed the others.

**Mid-Engagement Snapshot** — presented to you around session 4–5. We read it together in session. It shows your own words, then and now.

**Growth Report** — presented at or after our final session. A 3–5 page document built entirely from your own language. The most significant before/after evidence of the engagement. Yours to keep.

## A Word About What This Is Not

This coaching is not therapy, counseling, or psychiatric treatment. If clinical material surfaces during our engagement — depression, anxiety, trauma requiring clinical processing, or anything that exceeds what coaching can responsibly hold — I will name it directly and help you connect with the appropriate care. That is not a failure of the coaching or of you. It is precision about what each kind of care is for.

If you are in crisis, coaching sessions are not the right resource. Please contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), your physician, or emergency services. I will give you my contact hours and response time at the start of our engagement.
