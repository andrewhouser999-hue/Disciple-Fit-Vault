## Purpose

This document maps the current provider landscape for management training grounded in Self-Determination Theory (SDT) — specifically training that targets needs-supportive or autonomy-supportive manager behavior. Relevant to advisory engagements where manager development is a core lever (e.g., Anant Operations).

---

## Research Foundation: Does It Work?

The evidence base is strong. The following studies are directly relevant and citable:

| Study | Finding |
|---|---|
| Deci, Connell & Ryan (1989) — Xerox | Trained managers showed significantly more autonomy-supportive style; employees reported higher trust and job satisfaction vs. controls |
| Baard, Deci & Ryan (2004) — Corporate | Need satisfaction from managers predicted performance ratings, well-being, and job satisfaction |
| Hardré & Reeve (2009) | Manager training in needs-supportive style produced measurable motivation and engagement gains within weeks |
| Su & Reeve (2011) — Meta-analysis | Consistent positive effects of autonomy-support training across work and educational settings |
| Slemp et al. (2018) — Meta-analysis | Leader autonomy support strongly predicted employee vitality, engagement, and job satisfaction (moderate-to-large effect sizes) |
| Jungert et al. (2022) | One-day training workshop produced measurable improvement in subordinate-rated needs-thwarting behavior reduction within six weeks |
| ImmersyveHealth (healthcare RCT) | 83% reduction in burnout; 92% improvement in retention among trained nurse managers |
| Fortune 500 RCT (reported by motivationWorks) | Training managers in SDT-consistent style produced significant increases in employee need satisfaction, autonomous motivation, and work engagement at five-week follow-up |

**Bottom line:** SDT-based manager training produces measurable improvement in employee trust, satisfaction, and work attitudes. The mechanism is established, the effect sizes are consistent, and the results hold across industries including service and healthcare — both directly relevant to hospitality.

## What Needs-Supportive Behavior Looks Like (Trainable Behaviors)

Managers are trained in three clusters:

**Autonomy support**
- Providing a meaningful rationale for directives
- Offering choices within constraints
- Using non-controlling language
- Acknowledging employees' perspectives and feelings

**Competence support**
- Providing clear, informational (not evaluative) feedback
- Offering training and optimal challenges
- Setting achievable goals with structured support

**Relatedness support**
- Regular one-on-one contact
- Supporting cooperation and team cohesion
- Sharing information and personal experience appropriately

These are specific, observable, trainable behaviors — not personality traits. That distinction is critical for a training program rationale.

## Provider Landscape

### Tier 1 — Directly SDT-Targeting (Theory Named in Marketing)

**motivationWorks** (product of Immersyve Inc.)
- Founded by Deci and Ryan — the SDT theory creators
- Offers a standalone online manager training program built explicitly on SDT
- Also sells an employee engagement survey/analytics platform
- Marketing language: names the theory prominently; frames around "three core experiences essential for employees to thrive"
- Pricing: $40–$229/user for manager training (sliding group rate); engagement platform $3–$54/user/year scaling by org size
- Will take <500 employees: **Yes** — pricing tiers are designed for it; online product does not require enterprise contract
- Verdict: The only true off-the-shelf SDT manager training product with public pricing and small-employer accessibility

**Melioscope** (Dr. Anna Sverdlik, founder/CEO)
- Boutique organizational consulting firm
- Services: program development, employee engagement protocols, education; Masterclasses, university presentations
- Marketing language: uses SDT terminology directly — psychological needs, self-determined motivation
- Will take <500 employees: **Likely yes** — boutique size means no enterprise-scale filter
- Verdict: SDT-explicit; custom consulting work rather than packaged program

**Center for Self-Determination Theory (CSDT)** — selfdeterminationtheory.org
- Non-profit hub for SDT research and dissemination; Deci and Ryan's academic institution
- Can custom-design training sessions; travels nationally and internationally
- Marketing language: research dissemination framing, not commercial sales language
- Will take <500 employees: **Structurally yes** — not set up as a commercial vendor; engagement feels like hiring a researcher
- Verdict: Source institution, not a training firm. Best for credibility anchoring or locating certified practitioners

**Jacques Forest** (UQAM, Montreal — organizational psychologist)
- Individual academic practitioner; among the most-published SDT-in-organizations researchers globally
- Develops and delivers workplace training programs directly; bilingual (French/English)
- Will take <500 employees: **Probably yes** — individual practitioner with no minimum scale requirement
- Verdict: SDT-primary, custom engagements; you are hiring a person, not a firm

### Tier 2 — SDT-Informed but Adjacent (Marketing Around Outcomes, Not Theory)

**ImmersyveHealth** (Immersyve's healthcare vertical)
- Nurse manager training only; ANCC-accredited for CE credit
- Marketing: burnout, retention, nurse-specific — SDT is the mechanism but not the headline
- Will take <500 employees: Only if healthcare employer — vertically locked
- Verdict: Adjacent to hospitality context; demonstrates what SDT training produces in a service/people-intensive industry

**Proaction International** (now a dss+ company — Canadian, 3 continents)
- Leadership development + operational excellence; uses SDT inside a broader management coaching framework
- Has a proprietary digital management platform (UTrakk)
- Marketing: "People-centric performance," "management maturity" — SDT embedded, not named
- Will take <500 employees: Possible, but enterprise positioning skews large
- Verdict: SDT-informed; one tool in a broader toolkit

**BCL Training** (Durham, NC)
- Full-service L&D and consulting agency; SDT in their learning library but not a selling point
- Case studies show large enterprise clients (46,000-employee multinationals)
- Will take <500 employees: Unlikely to be their preference
- Verdict: SDT-aware; not SDT-positioned

**Work Unlocked** (Stavy Papasotiriou — UK organizational psychologist)
- HR consultancy; SDT-informed framing around engagement, performance, retention
- UK-based; US availability unclear
- Verdict: Adjacent; UK-centric

## Market Prevalence Assessment

**The market is thin.** This is the clearest finding from the landscape survey.

The number of providers that directly market needs-supportive or SDT-based manager training as their primary product is very small — essentially motivationWorks as the only scaled commercial product, a handful of individual academic consultants (Forest, Sverdlik), and the CSDT itself.

The overwhelming majority of the L&D and leadership development industry uses SDT-adjacent language incidentally. Autonomy, belonging, and mastery show up across BetterUp, Gallup, Korn Ferry, and similar firms — but the theory is not named, the mechanism is not taught, and managers are not being trained in the specific behaviors the research actually tested.

**Market gap:** There is no well-known mid-market firm (50–500 employees) that packages SDT-based manager training as a named, branded, accessible service. The mid-market — including multi-unit hospitality operations — is largely unserved by direct providers.

## Implications for Advisory Practice

For engagements like Anant Operations (~400 employees, multi-property hospitality):

1. **No direct competitor** is positioned in this space for mid-size employers. An advisor who can translate the SDT evidence base into a practical manager development program fills a genuine gap.

2. **motivationWorks** is the closest off-the-shelf option — could be incorporated as a platform component within a broader advisory engagement rather than competing with it.

3. **The research evidence is strong enough to cite with confidence** in any proposal framing. The mechanism (trained manager behavior → employee need satisfaction → trust, satisfaction, retention) is well-established, with multi-industry replication.

4. **Hospitality-specific application:** ImmersyveHealth's results in a service/people-intensive industry (83% burnout reduction, 92% retention improvement) are directly analogous to hotel operations and are usable in ROI framing even though the setting differs.

5. **Training content is teachable and auditable.** The three behavior clusters (autonomy, competence, relatedness support) translate directly into manager coaching frameworks, observation rubrics, and feedback structures — all deliverable within an organizational consulting engagement.
