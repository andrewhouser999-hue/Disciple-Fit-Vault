## Stage 1: Narrative Interview

### Human Skills Required — Practice-Based, Claude Cannot Develop These Alone

- **Holding silence after a specificity press.** Asking "give me a specific example" and going fully quiet for 10-15 seconds — no filler. Sales background trained the opposite reflex. Single highest interference risk.
  - *Claude support: Low. Can roleplay and debrief transcripts. Cannot train the nervous system.*

- **Self-regulation tracking while attending to the leader.** Tracking two states simultaneously — the leader's Window of Tolerance position and Andy's own regulation level. Degrades under pressure.
  - *Claude support: Cannot.*

- **Distinguishing shows from tells in real time.** The leader's verbal self-description is noise. Account brevity, affect shifts, perspective-taking failure — these are signal. Requires internalization through practice.
  - *Claude support: Moderate. Can teach taxonomy, can review transcripts after the fact.*

- **Backward probing without telegraphing skepticism.** "What did the preceding days look like before you made that decision?" requires genuinely neutral tone. If the leader senses skepticism, data becomes managed.
  - *Claude support: Moderate. Can roleplay extensively.*

- **Managing flooding without redirecting prematurely.** When a leader escalates emotionally, the correct move is to slow and stay present — not rescue. Emotionally demanding. Requires practice with real activation.
  - *Claude support: Low. Can script specific language. Cannot produce the experience.*

- **Reading the uncontaminated opening baseline.** The 60-90 second window before probing requires specific attentional training — observing presenting affect, SCARF activation signals, initial posture.
  - *Claude support: Low. Can teach what to look for.*

- **D3 loyalty triangulation management.** Owner hired Andy. Andy is asking the manager about their most difficult supervisor relationship — which may be the owner. Maintaining neutrality, no ownership signals, in real time under conversational pressure.
  - *Claude support: Moderate. Can script exact language, can roleplay D3 specifically.*

### Knowledge Required — Claude Can Teach Fully

- Narrative coherence theory (Main & Goldwyn AAI) — why incoherence is diagnostic
- Window of Tolerance: above/below window behavioral indicators, what to do at each position
- MI principles: OARS, rolling with resistance, evaluation contamination
- SCARF framework: all five domains, interview-specific activation signals
- 9 incoherence signals, 4 window positions, 3 self-other designations, 4 behavioral patterns — cold
- Prochaska stages: change talk vs. sustain talk, how to observe in narrative
- Kegan developmental stage indicators in narrative structure
- Episodic memory vs. rehearsed narrative — the distinction and how to test it
- *Claude support: Full.*

### Practice Requirements — Non-Negotiable

Minimum 5 practice interviews before first live engagement:
- 1 with someone who generalizes constantly (tests specificity press)
- 1 with a high-status person who manages conversations (tests backward probe and silence)
- 1 with someone who tells a coherent-sounding but structurally avoidant story (tests shows vs. tells)
- 2 open-ended with full protocol

Complete 30-minute post-interview synthesis immediately after each practice. *Claude role: Mock interviewee, post-session debrief from notes, failure mode flagging.*

Most AI-supportable component. Primary human practice: delivering the rater confidentiality statement and rater selection conversation with confidence. Secondary: reading open-text for absence — develops over multiple administrations.

*Claude role: All analytical work from raw data Andy submits. Scoring, divergence flagging, interpretation scaffolding, open-text synthesis. This is the highest-leverage backend function.*

## Stage 1: Facilitated Observation Session

### Human Skills Required

- **Running two roles simultaneously — facilitator and observer.** Facilitating a group conversation toward developmental outcomes while conducting structured behavioral observation of each individual. These roles pull against each other. Cannot be developed remotely.
  - *Claude support: Cannot.*

- **Delivering the authority challenge genuinely.** The challenge only produces diagnostic data if leaders experience it as real. Andy must find something in the consensus he actually disagrees with and push from that place. A manufactured challenge signals a test.
  - *Claude support: Low. Can help Andy prepare genuine challenges for likely discussion positions before the session.*

- **Holding the peer challenge without mediating.** When two leaders engage in genuine disagreement, the instinct is to mediate or redirect. The protocol says: let it run. Requires tolerance for interpersonal tension.
  - *Claude support: Low. Can roleplay the scenario.*

- **Minimal real-time notetaking while maintaining facilitation presence.** Capturing verbatim phrases at peak activation moments while tracking facilitation. Note-taking cannot dominate attention.
  - *Claude support: Cannot.*

- **Calibrating productive vs. dysregulating activation.** The session creates productive pressure. Pushing too hard moves leaders outside the Window; pulling back too fast loses diagnostic data. In-group context, harder than one-on-one.
  - *Claude support: Low. Can teach theory fully; calibration in real time is Andy's.*

- **Individual debrief within one week.** Holding what was observed versus what the leader self-reports, working with the gap, introducing a divergence finding as useful data rather than surveillance.
  - *Claude support: High. Can build the individual debrief script for each leader from Andy's observation notes.*

### Knowledge Required — Claude Can Teach Fully

- SCARF activation sequence design — why peer challenge precedes authority challenge
- Experiential learning cycle (Kolb) — why observed behavior produces data self-report cannot
- Window of Tolerance in group contexts
- Pattern behavioral indicators in a group setting
- Prediction vs. observation tracking — when divergence is significant
- *Claude support: Full.*

### Practice Requirements

Cannot be practiced in isolation. Requires a group. Minimum: observe 1-2 management team meetings as observer (not facilitator) before running the session — builds dual-attention skill in lower-stakes context. Option: run with a volunteer group before first engagement.

*Claude role: Pre-session preparation (per-leader observation targets, scenario customization, challenge language). Post-session synthesis. Individual debrief scripts.*

## What Clinical Supervision Provides That Andy Doesn't Have

Seven specific functions no AI can provide:

**1. A second set of trained eyes on case readings.** Supervisor reviews Andy's pattern interpretations and catches misreadings before they become delivered profiles. Provides calibration that no amount of self-monitoring can substitute for.

**2. Real-time consultation when something unexpected surfaces.** When a leader discloses clinical-adjacent material mid-session, Andy needs someone to consult within 24 hours. Currently has no one to call.

**3. Identification of Andy's own pattern interference — with evidence.** Across multiple cases over time, a supervisor will say: "In your last four narrative interviews you moved off D3 faster than protocol calls for — here's what your contamination looks like." This feedback is only available from someone who can observe Andy across cases.

**4. Scope boundary calls — the most critical function.** "What you're describing crosses the referral threshold — here's how to make the referral without breaking the engagement." Without supervision, Andy makes that call alone with a CPCC from 2013 and a referral protocol in a document. Not the same as trained clinical judgment.

**5. Ongoing calibration — preventing drift.** Pattern identification drifts without external correction. Andy will begin over-identifying patterns he's most sensitive to and under-identifying others. Supervision catches drift across cases.

**6. Professional accountability.** A supervisor takes partial professional responsibility for the supervisee's practice. Without supervision, Andy carries 100% liability with no institutional backing.

**7. Calibrated confidence.** Supervision builds accurate self-knowledge: where Andy's judgment is reliable, where he tends to miss. Without it, confidence is either too high or too low.

**What Claude can and cannot replace here:** Claude provides framework knowledge, case conceptualization support, profile drafting, session design, and protocol review. Claude cannot observe Andy's actual practice, calibrate his pattern reading accuracy across cases, make scope boundary calls in real time, catch his contamination in the room, or take professional responsibility for his work.
