# Relational Performance Advisory — Practitioner Development Landscape

**Purpose:** Practitioner reference for Andrew's own professional development in service of RPA delivery. Answers three questions: why management behavioral training doesn't produce the practitioner skill RPA requires; where that skill actually develops; and what a sequenced acquisition path looks like.

**Audience:** Andrew only. Not client-facing.

---

## Why Management Training Doesn't Reach the Implicit Level

Standard management behavioral training — Crucial Conversations, DISC debrief certification, 360 delivery training, leadership communication workshops — teaches framing as an explicit skill: a script, a step in a model, a checklist item. "Say this before you hand over the profile." The practitioner retrieves and applies the rule consciously.

**Implicit framing is different.** It means reading the relational field in real time and adjusting the container before content delivery — without conscious retrieval. Sensing that the leader is already in threat response, or that the room shifted, and modifying approach accordingly. Neurobiologically this is co-regulation before information transfer. It cannot be scripted under pressure.

Management training almost never produces implicit skill because it lacks:
- Enough repetitions with quality feedback on real interactions (not role-play)
- Supervised live work where a more developed practitioner observes and responds
- Time — implicit competence requires procedural memory, which consolidates through extended practice

**The consequence for RPA delivery:** A practitioner trained only at the explicit level will apply the framing script in calm conditions and abandon it under pressure — exactly when it matters most. The AOI engagement will produce charged interactions with managers and the owner. Implicit skill is a delivery requirement at depth, not a luxury.

---

## Where Implicit Relational Skill Actually Develops

**Clinical supervision** — psychotherapy training (psychodynamic, person-centered, attachment-informed) builds implicit attunement through supervised live work and case consultation over years. The gold standard. Practitioners learn to sense the relational field before intervening.

**MI certification at fidelity level** — not the workshop, but extended practice with MITI coding and supervision. The *spirit* of MI (partnership, acceptance, evocation) is meant to be internalized, not applied as technique.

**ICF executive coaching supervision** (PCC/MCC level) — supervised hours with mentor coaching are designed to develop implicit presence and pacing. Quality varies enormously in practice.

**Improv training** — directly trains reading the room and adjusting in real time without a script. Some executive development programs use this for relational attunement development specifically.

**High-repetition clinical fields** — palliative care, psychiatry residency — thousands of charged conversations with supervision build implicit calibration that cannot be accelerated through shorter exposure.

**Common mechanism across all of these:**

> Explicit instruction → deliberate practice on real interactions → quality feedback → enough repetitions to build procedural memory.

The critical variable is *quality feedback on real interactions*, not role-play frequency. Without feedback on live work, repetitions entrench existing patterns rather than reshaping them.

## Full Qualification Landscape

Beyond PCC/MCC, the following domains each contribute something the others don't for coaching managers at the implicit level, supporting self-awareness development, and building sustainable psychological safety practices.

| Domain | What It Builds for RPA | Primary Entry Point |
|---|---|---|
| **Kegan/Lahey ITC Certification** | Implicit meaning-making awareness; Immunity to Change methodology; supports managers developing self-authoring capacity — surfaces competing commitments and big assumptions operating below behavioral level | Minds at Work practitioner certification |
| **IPNB — Siegel/Schore** | Neurobiological grounding for attunement under pressure; explains *why* framing works; reads dysregulation cues before they surface verbally; makes psychological safety mechanistically coherent rather than culturally aspirational | Mindsight Institute; Schore reading + workshops |
| **NTL Human Interaction Lab** | Implicit group process reading — what's operating below the content level in teams and rooms; cannot be acquired through reading alone | NTL residential T-group (5–7 days) |
| **Gestalt OSD** | Implicit relational field awareness in organizational contexts; practitioner presence work under conditions of group charge | Gestalt Institute of Cleveland — 12–18 month cohort program |
| **Process Consultation (Schein)** | Relational consulting stance: being present to the system's process, not just delivering content; fundamental posture for organizational work | *Humble Inquiry*, *Helping*, *Process Consultation* Vol. 1–2 |
| **Trauma-Informed OD** | Organizational threat-response literacy; psychological safety as nervous system condition, not cultural norm; what's actually happening when safety breaks down under pressure | Bloom/Sanctuary Model; Strozzi Institute; SAMHSA-aligned foundational reading |
| **Ongoing supervision** | Integrates everything above; prevents all other development from stalling at explicit; the single most underrated qualifier in the list | Supervisor profile: OD + developmental + IPNB depth — not standard ICF mentor coaching |

## Sequenced Acquisition Plan

Sequenced by leverage and timing relative to RPA delivery phases. The full stack takes 5–10 years at depth. The sequence below matches investment to delivery need rather than attempting everything at once.

---

### Tier 1 — Before active coaching delivery begins

**ITC Certification (Minds at Work)**
- Format: Multi-day intensive + supervised application with real clients; cohort-based
- Cost: ~$2,000–$4,000
- Timeline: 6–12 months to certification; competence deepens through continued client application
- What it unlocks for RPA: The Immunity to Change methodology is the primary tool for working with managers' self-awareness at the meaning-making level — competing commitments, big assumptions operating below their conscious awareness. Essential before running the 12-session coaching arc with any reliability.

**Find a supervisor**
- Profile: OD background + developmental framework + somatic or IPNB depth. Not standard ICF mentor coaching. Licensed psychologist or counselor with organizational consulting experience is the target profile.
- Cost: ~$150–$300/hour; monthly minimum, biweekly to accelerate development
- Lead time: longest of anything on this list — start the search now, concurrent with ITC
- What it unlocks: Integration of everything else; real-time support when sessions exceed current training; prevents implicit skill from stalling

---

### Tier 2 — During or after Phase 1 of AOI delivery

**NTL Human Interaction Lab**
- Format: 5–7 day residential; experiential (you live the group dynamics you're studying)
- Cost: ~$3,000–$5,000
- What it unlocks: Seeds implicit group process awareness — what cannot be acquired through reading or role-play. Directly relevant to facilitated observation sessions and team facilitation work. One residential experience plus follow-up programs deepens it over time.

**Schore — *The Science of the Art of Psychotherapy***
- Format: Self-directed reading; substantial but accessible at practitioner level
- Cost: Book
- What it unlocks: Neurobiological basis for right-brain-to-right-brain attunement — the mechanism beneath implicit framing. Makes sense of what already happens in well-run sessions and makes it reproducible under pressure.

---

### Tier 3 — Post-Phase 4 / entering Option B or A scope

**Gestalt OSD** (Gestalt Institute of Cleveland)
- Format: 12–18 month cohort program; multiple residential modules
- Cost: ~$8,000–$15,000
- What it unlocks: Most comprehensive OD pathway for developing implicit relational skill in sustained organizational work. Appropriate when the practice is running ongoing engagements — premature before that stage.

**Somatic literacy foundations**
- Format: Somatic Experiencing introductory training or Strozzi Institute program — not full 3-year SE certification
- What it unlocks: Reads physiological cues in the room in real time. Relevant for Phase 5–6 coaching depth and for working with managers whose regulatory patterns show up somatically before cognitively.

## Honest Current-State Assessment

The development path above is a parallel track — it runs alongside delivery and expands capacity as it matures. It is not a prerequisite for starting.

**What Andrew can deliver now** with the existing knowledge base (Perry, Siegel, Rock, Kegan/Lahey, MI, Prochaska, CPCC/CTI): assessment design and administration, narrative interview facilitation, 360 administration and scoring, behavioral pattern interpretation, individual and sponsor debrief delivery, pre-intervention training facilitation.

**What requires investment before it's fully ready at depth:** The 12-session coaching arc at the implicit meaning-making level — ITC methodology closes this gap. Sustained team facilitation in high-charge rooms — NTL + supervision closes this gap.

**What Andrew does when a session exceeds current training:** Stop coaching, name what's happening without clinical language, acknowledge the edge, and bring the question to supervision before the next session. Do not improvise past the scope boundary.

**The differentiator this stack produces:** An owner or manager who has worked with practitioners trained only at the explicit level has received scripts and models. A practitioner trained at the implicit level reads the relational field, adjusts the container before content delivery, and sustains that capacity under pressure. That gap is where the RPA service's credibility lives — and where the development investment pays.
