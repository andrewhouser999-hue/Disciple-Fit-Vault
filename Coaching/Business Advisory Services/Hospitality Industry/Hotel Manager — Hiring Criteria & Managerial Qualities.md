# Hotel Manager — Hiring Criteria & Managerial Qualities

*Director's perspective. What I'm actually hiring for.*

---

## The Foundational Question

**How can you make people around you better if you cannot make your inward self better?**

Every quality below depends on this. A manager who is not growing inwardly — who has not developed the self-awareness, emotional regulation, and personal accountability to lead themselves — cannot reliably lead others. They can perform management. They cannot build culture. The interview process is, at its core, an attempt to detect which one is true of the candidate.

---

## What I Am Hiring For

**1. Guest obsession, not guest satisfaction.**
Satisfaction is the floor. I want someone who loses sleep over a guest who left without saying anything — because that's the one we failed silently. I need managers who build cultures where the team *notices* things before guests have to ask.

**2. Floor presence.**
I don't hire managers to sit behind a desk and read reports. If you're not on the floor during peak hours, you don't know what's actually happening. Period.

**3. Accountability without blame.**
When something breaks down, I want a manager who asks "what in our system allowed this?" before they ask "who did this?" That distinction tells me everything about whether they'll build a team or burn through one.

**4. The ability to hold standards under pressure.**
Anyone can hold standards when it's easy. I watch how a candidate talks about the moment a VIP guest pushed back on a policy. Did they fold? Did they handle it with grace *and* backbone?

**5. Staff development as a non-negotiable.**
Turnover in this industry is a manager problem before it's an HR problem. I want someone who can name three people on their current team they are actively developing. If they can't name them, I'm done with the interview.

**6. Composure in chaos.**
Opening night, a sold-out weekend with two callouts and a banquet going sideways — I need someone whose presence *calms* the room, not one who adds to the noise.

**7. Financial literacy tied to the guest experience.**
Not just reading a P&L — understanding *why* the numbers move and how service decisions drive them. Labor cost isn't just a budget line; it's a staffing philosophy.

---

## What Disqualifies Someone Fast

- Talks about guests in the third person, like they're a category to manage.
- Can't articulate a specific failure and what they changed because of it.
- References "my team" only when things went well.
- No evidence of personal growth — can't name what they are working on in themselves.

---

## Notes

The technical skills are verifiable. The interview is really about character under pressure and whether this person makes the people around them better — and whether they understand that capacity flows from the inside out.
