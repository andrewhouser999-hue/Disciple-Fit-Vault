# A001 — Presentation Deck 3
## Variant: 1-1 Conversational Backup

*Six slides. Non-linear. Deploy when verbal explanation needs a visual anchor — not to open the meeting, not as an agenda, not as a pitch. Each slide is independent and can be shown in any order. Build in LibreOffice Impress using the layout specs below.*

*Pairs with: [[A001 — Conversation Readiness Guide]] (pivot triggers) · [[A001 — Initial Meeting Field Form]] (when in the meeting to consider a slide)*

---

## How to Use This Deck

**The rule:** Conversation first. Slide second. If verbal is landing, skip the slide.

**The move:** "Let me show you something." → open slide → speak to it (don't read it) → close slide → continue talking.

**The timing:** 60–90 seconds on screen. No longer. Close it before the client stops processing it.

**The index:** Know which slide number to jump to for each use case. Practice navigating in under 5 seconds.

| # | Slide | Deploy When |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Gap | Owner references training that didn't stick / asks why this keeps happening |
| 2 | The Chain | Explaining the mechanism — how environment produces behavior |
| 3 | What It Costs | Cost / ROI conversation; owner asks about the investment |
| 4 | What Phase 1 Produces | "What do I actually get?" |
| 5 | Timeline | "How long before I see a difference?" |
| 6 | What Your Managers Will Experience | Preparing the owner to introduce Andrew to OMs |

---

## LibreOffice Impress — Build Specs

**Format:** 16:9 widescreen
**Background:** White
**Primary text color:** Dark navy (#1a2744 or similar)
**Accent color:** One only — medium teal (#2e7d87 or similar) for boxes, arrows, key numbers
**Fonts:** Calibri or Liberation Sans — clean, no serifs
**Heading size:** 36pt minimum
**Body/visual text:** 24pt minimum
**No bullet points anywhere.** Use boxes, arrows, columns, and diagrams only.
**No title slide. No agenda slide. No closing slide.**

## Slide 1 — The Gap

### Visual Layout
Two columns, equal width, separated by a vertical line or gap. One accent-color arrow pointing right from Column 1 to Column 2.

**Column 1 heading:** STANDARD APPROACH
**Column 1 content (center of column, 24pt):**
Changes knowledge
↓
Doesn't reach what produces the behavior
↓
Reverts in 30–90 days

**Column 2 heading:** WHAT'S DIFFERENT HERE
**Column 2 content (center of column, 24pt):**
Changes conditions
↓
Addresses what produces the behavior
↓
Holds

**Bottom of slide, centered, 20pt italics:**
*The pattern is produced by the environment. Standard training doesn't touch the environment.*

---

### Speaker Notes
"You've already named this — you identified at [coffee shop] that the behavior is coming from the environment, not the character of your people. This is why training doesn't stick. It changes what people know. It doesn't change the conditions that activated the pattern in the first place. When the manager walks back into the same environment, the same conditions produce the same behavior. What's different here is that we work at the level where the pattern is actually produced."

**Deploy when:** Owner mentions prior training that failed, OR asks "why does this keep happening?"
**Duration:** 60–75 seconds
**Put away when:** Owner acknowledges the distinction — any verbal signal that it landed ("yeah, that makes sense," "that's exactly what happened")

## Slide 2 — The Chain

### Visual Layout
Horizontal flow diagram. Five boxes connected by arrows left to right. Full width of slide. Each box same size, teal border, white interior. Arrows between boxes in teal.

**Box 1:** Structural Conditions
**Box 2:** Manager Experience
**Box 3:** Employee Experience
**Box 4:** What You See
**Box 5:** What It Costs

**Below Box 4, smaller text (20pt):** conflict · avoidance · turnover · inconsistency
**Below Box 5, smaller text (20pt):** $840K–$1.6M/year

**Heading above diagram (36pt, left-aligned):** Why the same patterns keep showing up

**Bottom of slide, centered, italics 18pt:**
*Change the first three boxes. The last two change with them.*

---

### Speaker Notes
"This is the chain. Structural conditions in the operation — how authority works, how mistakes get handled, how much certainty people have about what's expected — shape how managers experience their environment day to day. That shapes how managers show up with their teams. That shapes how employees experience their environment. And what you see is in the fourth box: the conflict, the avoidance, the turnover. The fifth box is what it costs you. You're watching the last two boxes. The work is in the first three."

**Deploy when:** Owner asks HOW environment produces behavior — wants the mechanism explained; OR after describing a specific incident and you want to show where it fits in the chain
**Duration:** 75–90 seconds
**Put away when:** Owner can place what he described in the chain — "so where does the OM piece fit?" is the signal he's inside it

## Slide 3 — What It Costs

### Visual Layout
Clean, centered. Large numbers. Minimal text.

**Top, 28pt centered:**
400 employees &nbsp;&nbsp;×&nbsp;&nbsp; ~75% annual turnover &nbsp;&nbsp;=&nbsp;&nbsp; ~300 replacements / year

**Middle, very large (64pt), accent color, centered:**
$1,200,000

**Below large number, 22pt centered:**
in annual replacement cost — before productivity loss, training drag, and guest experience

**Dividing line**

**Bottom section, 24pt centered:**
Phase 1 investment: $28,000
Recovered at a fraction of a percentage point of turnover reduction.

---

### Speaker Notes
"At your scale, you're replacing roughly 300 people a year. At $3,000 to $5,000 per replacement — and those are conservative numbers for hospitality — that's $1.2 million annually, just in replacement cost. That doesn't count what it costs while the position is vacant, what it costs to ramp a new hire, or what it costs to your guest experience when your team is in constant turnover. The Phase 1 investment is $28,000. That pays for itself before you'd notice the savings."

**Deploy when:** Owner raises cost or asks about pricing; OR you're making the investment case before introducing Phase 1
**Duration:** 60 seconds
**Put away when:** Owner has reacted to the numbers — don't leave cost on screen while discussing it further

## Slide 4 — What Phase 1 Produces

### Visual Layout
Three equal boxes, horizontal, teal border, white interior. Clear heading above.

**Heading (36pt):** At the end of Phase 1, you have three things.

**Box 1:**
**BEHAVIORAL PROFILE**
Per leader assessed
What their patterns are · What activates them · Their development path

**Box 2:**
**TEAM MAP**
How the management team
functions collectively
What structural conditions are contributing

**Box 3:**
**STRUCTURAL RECOMMENDATIONS**
Specific changes to how
the environment is designed
Connected to what the assessment found

**Below all three boxes, centered, 20pt italics:**
*You see the picture. You decide whether to continue.*

---

### Speaker Notes
"Three things come out of Phase 1. First, a behavioral profile for each leader — what their patterns are, what activates them, and what their specific development path looks like. Second, a team map — how the management team functions collectively, and what in the structure is contributing to the patterns. Third, structural recommendations — specific things in how you're running the environment that are driving what you're seeing. Each one connects to a specific decision. At the end of Phase 1 you decide whether you want to move to implementation."

**Deploy when:** Owner asks "what do I actually get?" or "what are the deliverables?"
**Duration:** 75 seconds
**Put away when:** Owner has processed all three boxes — "so what's included in the implementation piece?" signals he's past the Phase 1 question

## Slide 5 — Timeline

### Visual Layout
Horizontal timeline across full width of slide. Timeline bar in accent color. Four labeled points above or below the bar.

**Heading (36pt, left-aligned):** When you see what.

**Timeline points (left to right):**

Point 1 — Week 13
PHASE 1 COMPLETE
Assessment findings + structural recommendations delivered

Point 2 — 60–90 Days
FIRST BEHAVIORAL SIGNALS
Managers functioning differently under pressure
(if implementation proceeds)

Point 3 — 6–12 Months
TURNOVER SHIFT
Measurable reduction in departure rate

Point 4 — 18+ Months
CULTURE THAT HOLDS
Norms self-transmitting to new employees
without you maintaining it manually

**Bottom of slide, 20pt italics, centered:**
*This isn't fast. What's faster is not doing it — and you've already seen what that costs.*

---

### Speaker Notes
"Phase 1 is 13 weeks — assessment, interpretation, debrief. The first things you'll notice if we proceed to implementation are behavioral — your managers showing up differently in difficult situations. That typically happens within 60 to 90 days of well-implemented development work. The turnover shift follows, at 6 to 12 months. A culture that holds without you having to maintain it manually — that's 18 months or more. I'm not going to tell you this is fast. What I will tell you is that you've already seen what not doing it costs."

**Deploy when:** Owner asks "how long before I see a difference?" or expresses timeline pressure
**Duration:** 75 seconds
**Put away when:** Owner acknowledges the timeline — any sign of processing ("so Phase 1 is essentially this year...")

## Slide 6 — What Your Managers Will Experience

### Visual Layout
Two columns, clear dividing line or space between them.

**Heading (36pt, left-aligned):** Before you introduce this to your team.

**Column 1 heading (accent color, 24pt):** What this is NOT
**Column 1 content (22pt, each on its own line):**
An evaluation of how they manage
An HR audit or compliance review
Individual data going to you
Supervision by an outside person

**Column 2 heading (accent color, 24pt):** What this IS
**Column 2 content (22pt, each on its own line):**
Private development — their data stays with them
A picture of how the team functions collectively
Tools to do their jobs better
Built with them, not done to them

**Bottom, centered, 20pt italics:**
*Your managers are the most important part of what we build.*

---

### Speaker Notes
"Before you meet with your managers about this — here's the frame that will set things up well for you. The thing your OMs will want to know first is what this means for them — whether they're being evaluated, whether you'll have access to what they share, whether this is being done to them or with them. The confidentiality structure means their individual data stays with them — what you receive is the aggregate picture. That protection is what makes honest assessment possible. Your managers are not the problem here. They're the lever."

**Deploy when:** Close of the meeting — owner surfaces the OM question; OR Pivot 2 ("what are you doing with my managers?") is raised and the verbal answer needs reinforcement
**Duration:** 75–90 seconds
**Put away when:** Owner has language for how to introduce Andrew to OMs — "so I'd say something like..." signals the frame has landed
