## Program Summary

**Relational Performance Advisory (RPA)** is a non-clinical organizational consulting service that addresses the relational and behavioral patterns driving turnover, culture instability, and management inconsistency in multi-property hospitality operations.

**The core finding it's built on:** Employee behavior that looks like a character problem — entitlement, conflict, absenteeism, disengagement — is almost always an environmental problem. The relational conditions of the workplace produce predictable behavioral adaptations. Standard management training addresses behavior at the surface level without reaching the structural conditions that produce it. Training reverts within 30–90 days. Culture programs produce marginal or temporary results. The pattern continues.

**What RPA does differently:** Works at the level where the patterns actually operate — the relational environment of the operation and the management behavior that either creates or resolves activation in the workforce. Assessment identifies the specific patterns present, what activates them, and what structural conditions are contributing. Development work is pattern-specific, stage-matched, and grounded in the mechanism — not generic skill training.

**What it produces:** A clear diagnostic picture, a specific development path for each assessed leader, and structural recommendations the owner can act on. At the implementation level: measurable behavioral change in management, culture conditions that reduce chronic workforce activation, and a turnover trajectory that changes.

## Advisory and Orchestration Role

Andrew's role in every engagement is advisory and orchestration — not operational management, not training delivery, not clinical intervention.

**What this means in practice:**

*Andrew designs.* He builds the assessment architecture, the coaching framework, and the structural recommendations. He does not execute the owner's management decisions or stand in for the owner's leadership.

*Andrew guides.* He facilitates the coaching arc, the team facilitation sessions, and the sponsor debrief. He does not run programs on behalf of the organization or deliver content to groups in place of internal leadership.

*Andrew identifies.* He surfaces what the assessment data shows, connects findings to operational costs, and recommends structural changes. He does not implement structural changes — the owner and management team implement.

*The owner remains accountable.* RPA does not transfer organizational ownership from the owner to the consultant. It provides the intelligence and development support that makes the owner's decisions more effective.

## Scope Boundary

Andrew is not a licensed clinician. This is not a limitation of RPA — it is a design feature.

**Non-clinical throughout:**
- All language is behavioral and observable, not diagnostic or therapeutic
- Assessment produces behavioral profiles, not clinical assessments
- Coaching targets behavioral change, not psychological treatment
- Structural recommendations address organizational conditions, not individual pathology

**When clinical referral applies:**
If assessment or coaching surfaces indicators that a leader's functioning is being significantly impaired by clinical-level dysregulation, trauma activation, or other conditions that exceed the scope of organizational coaching, Andrew refers to a licensed clinician. The engagement continues with remaining participants. This threshold is clearly defined and applied consistently.

**What this boundary protects:**
The owner, the leaders in the engagement, and Andrew. It ensures that work stays in the domain where Andrew has standing, the interventions are appropriate to the level they're addressing, and the leaders receive the right kind of support for what they're actually dealing with.
