# A001 — Initial Meeting Field Form

*During-meeting reference. Check off, note briefly. Full debrief → Engagement Intake after the meeting.*

---

## Pre-Meeting

- [ ] Two or three specific things noted from the coffee shop exchange — written down
- [ ] Three open questions prepared and held loosely
- [ ] Role boundary language is yours, not improvised
- [ ] Decided what you will NOT accomplish today
- [ ] ★ Next-step offer ready: specific ask, specific day

---

## Opening

- [ ] Reference the prior exchange briefly — attribute significance to *his* insight, not yours

- [ ] ★ Ask before adding anything: **"Where has your thinking gone since then?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] Let him set the pace — no agenda-driving in the first five minutes

---

## Diagnostic Deepening

*Goal: expand his picture of the operation. Stay in the problem space.*

### Layer 1 — Pain Picture

- [ ] ★ **"Do you know what turnover is actually costing you — across all your properties, annually?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"If I walked into three of your properties unannounced and asked employees what it's like to work here — would I hear the same answer at all three?"**

  *Notes:*

### Layer 2 — Management Behavior Gap

- [ ] **"Walk me through the last time a supervisor had to address a recurring performance problem. How did that play out?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"When someone gets promoted into a supervisory role — what does their first 90 days look like?"**

  *Notes:*

### Layer 3 — Structural Contributors

- [ ] **"What does a new employee experience in their first two weeks — beyond the job training itself?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"How do you hold your GMs accountable to a culture standard — not a performance standard, but how the place actually feels to work in?"**

  *Notes:*

### Layer 4 — Personal Experience

- [ ] **"What's it like for you when you see this playing out at a property?"**

  *Notes:*

### Reflect and Hold

- [ ] ★ Reflect back before adding anything: **"Here's what I'm hearing — [X, Y, Z]. Does that capture the shape of it, or is there more?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] Hold the solution space — do not introduce until problem space is fully mapped

---

## Role & Scope

- [ ] Introduce role clarity here — after deepening, not before

- [ ] ★ Frame as care for him, not disclaimer: *"I want to be clear about how I work — mostly because it matters when you're preparing your managers. I'm an advisor and implementation orchestrator, not operational management, not running programs. Your managers need to know they're not being supervised by an outside person."*

- [ ] State what you need from him: *"I need candid access to what's actually happening — not the polished version. And I need you willing to hear findings that might be uncomfortable."*

- [ ] Do not define full engagement scope today

---

## Transition

- [ ] Wait for him to surface "what now" before answering it

- [ ] ★ Summarize the diagnostic picture first: **"Here's what I've heard — does this capture it?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] Offer the next step, not a full engagement: *"I'll send you a short summary and a proposed next step — not a full proposal, just the shape of how I'd approach this. You review it and we talk again."*

- [ ] **Pricing:** Hold unless he raises it.
  If he raises it → *"Typically [X–Y] depending on scope. I'll give you a specific number once we know what we're actually building."*

---

## Close

- [ ] Summarize in two or three sentences: **"Have I captured that?"**

- [ ] ★ Specific next step, specific day: *"I'll send you a one-pager by [day]. Take a look and let's find time to talk before you meet with your managers."*

- [ ] ★ **OM Framing — do not skip:** *"When you talk to your managers about what we're doing, how are you thinking about framing it? I want to make sure they hear it in a way that sets us up well."*

  *Notes:*

- [ ] Close in the same relational register you opened

---

*After the meeting: complete [[A001 — Engagement Intake]] from memory.*

## Staffing & Manager Development — Supplementary Diagnostic

*Deploy after the core diagnostic deepening arc, or as a focused follow-on sequence. A → B → C is the natural arc — follow what opens.*

### Pre-Meeting Prep

- [ ] Know the behavioral/structural distinction cold — behavioral = what employees do; structural = what the operation produces
- [ ] Expect development investment to be thinner than he believes — hold space for the gap between what was attempted and what changed
- [ ] Section C only earns its weight after A and B — don't lead with manager development
- [ ] Walking away knowing: where behaviors concentrate, which structural gaps are generating them, and how much real development his managers have had

---

### Section A — Behavioral Problems

- [ ] **"Walk me through a bad week. What behaviors are you seeing that you shouldn't have to deal with?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Which ones cost you the most — financially, operationally, or just managerially?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Are these spread evenly, or do they concentrate somewhere — specific properties, departments, shifts, tenure brackets?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Do problem employees show up that way from day one, or does something change? When does it usually start?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"When a behavior blows up, is there usually a story behind it — or does it come out of nowhere?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Are your best employees and your worst behaving differently from each other, or are you seeing the same patterns across the board?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Are guests seeing it? What's showing up in reviews or complaints that traces back to staff behavior?"**

  *Notes:*

---

### Section B — Structural Problems

- [ ] **"What does someone experience in their first 30 days? Walk me through what actually happens — not the policy, what actually happens."**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"When a supervisor handles a behavior problem, what does that look like? Is there a consistent approach, or does it depend on who's managing that day?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"How consistent are your managers across properties? Same problem, same response — or is it all over the map?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Do your employees know what's expected of them beyond the job tasks? Not the handbook — do they actually know?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"When someone crosses a line, what's the real response? Walk me through what happens from the moment you find out."**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Is there a visible path forward for someone who wants to grow with you — or does advancement feel random to them?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"How do you find out when something's going wrong — does it come to you, or are you hunting for it?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] ★ **Bridge:** *"When you see the same behavior repeated across multiple people in the same role — do you read that as a people problem, a system problem, or something else?"*

  *Notes:*

---

### Section C — Manager Development & Investment

- [ ] **"When you promote someone to a management role, what prepares them for it? Is there a defined process, or do they mostly learn on the job?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Have you ever brought in outside training for your managers — leadership, communication, conflict, anything? What have you tried?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Is there a management style or philosophy you've tried to instill — something you'd say: 'This is how we lead here?'"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] ★ **"What does a good manager look like in your operation? If you picture your best one — what are they doing that the others aren't?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"When a manager struggles, what do you do with that? Is there a development path, or does it usually end in a personnel decision?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Do your managers know what to do when an employee's personal life is bleeding into their work? Not policy — do they have any actual tools for that conversation?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"Have you invested differently in managers at different properties, or has development been consistent across the chain?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"After any training you've done — did behavior change, or did people go back to their defaults within a few weeks?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] ★ **Bridge (if training didn't stick):** *"When it didn't hold, where did it break down — the managers not applying it, or the environment not supporting it?"*

  *Notes:*

## If He Asks "What Do You Recommend as Next Steps?"

*The ask is an invitation, not a close. He's asking what's next — not asking for a full proposal. Match the ask.*

- [ ] ★ **Say this:**

  > "Here's what I'd recommend — and I'll put this in writing for you before we meet again. Before either of us commits to a full engagement, I want to give you an accurate picture of what your management environment is actually producing. Not a guess — a real diagnostic. Narrative interviews with your key leaders, a 360 from their teams, and a direct observation session. At the end of that, you get a clear picture of the patterns at work, what's driving them, and a specific set of structural recommendations. You then decide whether you want to move into implementation or handle it internally.
  >
  > That phase runs 13 weeks. I'll send you a one-page summary of exactly what it includes and what it costs. Look it over, and let's talk again before you have any conversation with your managers about what we're doing."

- [ ] Then stop. Do not pivot to Options B or A. Do not give the $28K number unprompted unless he asks.

- [ ] **If he asks the price immediately:**

  > "That phase is $28,000. And based on what you've described — even at conservative turnover numbers — it pays for itself if it moves the needle by a fraction of a percent. I'll put that math in what I send you."
  > 
  > *"Before we wrap up — how are you thinking about bringing your operations managers into this conversation? I want to make sure they hear it in a way that sets things up well."*
