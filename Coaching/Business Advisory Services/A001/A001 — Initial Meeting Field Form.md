# A001 — Initial Meeting Field Form

*During-meeting reference. Check off, note briefly. Full debrief → Engagement Intake after the meeting.*

---

## Pre-Meeting

- [ ] Two or three specific things noted from the coffee shop exchange — written down
- [ ] Three open questions prepared and held loosely
- [ ] Role boundary language is yours, not improvised
- [ ] Decided what you will NOT accomplish today
- [ ] ★ Next-step offer ready: specific ask, specific day

---

## Opening

- [ ] Reference the prior exchange briefly — attribute significance to *his* insight, not yours

- [ ] ★ Ask before adding anything: **"Where has your thinking gone since then?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] Let him set the pace — no agenda-driving in the first five minutes

---

## Diagnostic Deepening

*Goal: expand his picture of the operation. Stay in the problem space.*

### Layer 1 — Pain Picture

- [ ] ★ **"Do you know what turnover is actually costing you — across all your properties, annually?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"If I walked into three of your properties unannounced and asked employees what it's like to work here — would I hear the same answer at all three?"**

  *Notes:*

### Layer 2 — Management Behavior Gap

- [ ] **"Walk me through the last time a supervisor had to address a recurring performance problem. How did that play out?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"When someone gets promoted into a supervisory role — what does their first 90 days look like?"**

  *Notes:*

### Layer 3 — Structural Contributors

- [ ] **"What does a new employee experience in their first two weeks — beyond the job training itself?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] **"How do you hold your GMs accountable to a culture standard — not a performance standard, but how the place actually feels to work in?"**

  *Notes:*

### Layer 4 — Personal Experience

- [ ] **"What's it like for you when you see this playing out at a property?"**

  *Notes:*

### Reflect and Hold

- [ ] ★ Reflect back before adding anything: **"Here's what I'm hearing — [X, Y, Z]. Does that capture the shape of it, or is there more?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] Hold the solution space — do not introduce until problem space is fully mapped

---

## Role & Scope

- [ ] Introduce role clarity here — after deepening, not before

- [ ] ★ Frame as care for him, not disclaimer: *"I want to be clear about how I work — mostly because it matters when you're preparing your managers. I'm an advisor and implementation orchestrator, not operational management, not running programs. Your managers need to know they're not being supervised by an outside person."*

- [ ] State what you need from him: *"I need candid access to what's actually happening — not the polished version. And I need you willing to hear findings that might be uncomfortable."*

- [ ] Do not define full engagement scope today

---

## Transition

- [ ] Wait for him to surface "what now" before answering it

- [ ] ★ Summarize the diagnostic picture first: **"Here's what I've heard — does this capture it?"**

  *Notes:*

- [ ] Offer the next step, not a full engagement: *"I'll send you a short summary and a proposed next step — not a full proposal, just the shape of how I'd approach this. You review it and we talk again."*

- [ ] **Pricing:** Hold unless he raises it.
  If he raises it → *"Typically [X–Y] depending on scope. I'll give you a specific number once we know what we're actually building."*

---

## Close

- [ ] Summarize in two or three sentences: **"Have I captured that?"**

- [ ] ★ Specific next step, specific day: *"I'll send you a one-pager by [day]. Take a look and let's find time to talk before you meet with your managers."*

- [ ] ★ **OM Framing — do not skip:** *"When you talk to your managers about what we're doing, how are you thinking about framing it? I want to make sure they hear it in a way that sets us up well."*

  *Notes:*

- [ ] Close in the same relational register you opened

---

*After the meeting: complete [[A001 — Engagement Intake]] from memory.*
