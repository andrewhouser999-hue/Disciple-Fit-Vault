# A001 — Internalization Cards

*Pre-meeting internalization for the 8 load-bearing verbal moves. The Field Form stays in the room — these are what need to come out naturally, without looking down.*

**How to use:** Look at the front. Say your version aloud. Flip. Compare. If it accomplishes the same move in your words, it's internalized. Verbatim required only on Card 1 (quoting his email) and Card 4 (the whole move is the brevity).

---

## CARD 1 — OPENING

**Cue:** He's settled. The moment opens. What do you say?

---

*"Something you wrote back stayed with me — 'create a generation that can carry these values forward as future leaders.' That's not the language of someone trying to fix a turnover problem. I want to understand what that actually means to you."*

> Must be near-verbatim. You're quoting his words — any paraphrase loses the specific credibility.

---

## CARD 2 — MIRROR

**Cue:** He uses a specific phrase about his vision or his people. What's your move?

---

Reflect his exact words back. Not a paraphrase. If he says *"I want these people to actually become something"* — say: *"Become something — say more about that."* His words, not yours.

---

## CARD 3 — BRIDGE

**Cue:** He's expressed the generational vision. There's a natural breath. What do you say?

---

*"What you're describing isn't what most organizations build. Most build training. Training reaches what people can consciously process and decide to change. What you're describing has to reach the level where these patterns actually live — below that. That's a different design. And it's buildable. But it requires starting in a specific place."*

[Pause.]

*"In your operation, it starts with your OMs. What you build into them is what reaches the floor. They're the transmission point for everything you're describing."*

---

## CARD 4 — THE HOLD

**Cue:** Phase 5. He pauses. He trails off. He starts *"I suppose I..."*

---

*"Say more about that."*

Full stop. Do not finish his thought. Do not explain what you're seeing. Do not help him get there faster.

---

## CARD 5 — CURIOSITY BRIDGE

**Cue:** Pattern reflected. He's confirmed it. Now what?

---

*"What do you think would need to be true for this to actually change — not improve slightly, actually change?"*

---

## CARD 6 — OM PLANT

**Cue:** After curiosity bridge and role framing. How do you create the condition for him to propose his OMs?

---

*"What do you think your OMs would say if I asked them the same question you just answered — about what it costs them personally?"*

Then, after he speculates:

*"That's exactly what I'd want to know before designing anything. Because the pattern you're describing isn't just a management behavior problem — it's a system. Your OMs are the transmission point in that system. What they're experiencing matters as much as what you're observing."*

Stop. Let him propose the OMs. Not you.

---

## CARD 7 — CLOSE

**Cue:** The conversation has run its arc. He's ready for "what now." Three beats.

---

*"Here's what I'd recommend. Before either of us commits to anything, let me give you a real picture of where your operation actually is — not a guess, a genuine diagnostic. From there, you decide whether you want to move into building what you're describing or take it another direction."*

*"That starts with a structured conversation — with you, and then with your OMs. I'll tell you what I'm seeing as we go. Within 13 weeks you have a clear picture of exactly what's running beneath the surface and what would need to shift to build what you're describing."*

*"Before we wrap — how are you thinking about bringing your OMs into this conversation? I want to make sure they hear about it in a way that sets things up well."*

---

## CARD 8 — PRICE

**Cue:** He asks the price.

---

*"That phase is $28,000. And based on what you've described — even at conservative turnover numbers — it pays for itself if it moves the needle by a fraction of a percent. I'll put that math in what I send you."*

Move immediately back to: *"Before we wrap — how are you thinking about bringing your OMs into this conversation?"* Don't linger on the number.
