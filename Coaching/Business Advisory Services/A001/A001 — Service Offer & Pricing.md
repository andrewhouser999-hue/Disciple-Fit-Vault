## Consulting Role Definition

**What Andrew is:**
Organizational consultant. Advisory and implementation orchestration role. Works with owners and organizational leaders on the relational and behavioral patterns that drive operational outcomes — specifically turnover, culture instability, and management inconsistency.

**What Andrew is not:**
- Not operational management — does not run the operation or make personnel decisions
- Not a training delivery service — does not stand up and deliver content to groups on the owner's behalf
- Not an HR consultant — does not manage compliance, documentation, or personnel process
- Not a clinical provider — non-clinical scope throughout; behavioral and organizational language only; licensed referral when clinical threshold is crossed

**The role in plain language for owner conversations:**
"I help you understand what your environment is actually producing and build a specific path to change it. I design the work, guide the leaders through it, and tell you what structural changes need to happen. Your team implements."

## Four-Component Model

All three packages draw from the same four components. Options vary by which components are included and how many leaders are in scope.

| Component | What It Is | Phases |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Assessment** | Narrative interview, multi-rater 360, facilitated observation session → Behavioral Pattern Profile per leader + Team Aggregate Pattern Map | Phases 1–4 |
| **2. Individual Development** | 12-session bi-weekly coaching arc per assessed leader; pattern-specific, stage-matched, MI-spirit throughout | Phase 5 |
| **3. Team Facilitation** | 3-session monthly facilitation arc; shared behavioral language, collective pattern work, relational agreements | Phase 5 |
| **4. Structural Recommendations** | Written report to sponsor: systemic conditions contributing to patterns + specific structural changes recommended; implementation support as needed | Phase 4/5 |

## Option C → Full Engagement Transition Language

*The Option C debrief (Phase 4 sponsor meeting) is the natural transition point to full engagement. By that meeting, the owner has seen the aggregate pattern map and structural recommendations. He knows what his management team is dealing with and has a specific path forward. The question "so what do we do about it?" is either already present or surfaces immediately.*

**The transition is not a sales conversation. It is the logical continuation of the work.**

### The Setup in the Sponsor Debrief

Before closing the Phase 4 meeting, after the owner has processed the aggregate findings:

*"Now that you have this picture — what do you want to do with it?"*

Let him answer. His answer tells you his readiness stage and his priority. Respond to what he says, not to a prepared pitch.

If he asks what the options are:

*"There are two ways we go from here. One is you take these findings and the structural recommendations and implement them with your team — I can be available on an advisory basis for questions. The other is a full implementation phase: individual development work with each leader, team facilitation, and hands-on support as the structural changes go in. I'll send you a summary of both with the investment for each so you can look at them alongside what we just covered."*

### If He Asks for a Number Before You've Sent the Summary

*"Full implementation for your scope runs between $87K and $136K depending on how many leaders we bring into the coaching work and how much implementation support you want on the structural side. I'll give you a specific number once we've looked at the Phase 1 findings together and defined what makes sense for your operation."*

### If He Wants to Move Immediately

*"I'd like a week to look at the assessment data and build a scope recommendation that's specific to what we found — rather than a generic package. You'll have something concrete to respond to, not a brochure. When do you want to talk again?"*

### If He's Not Ready

If the owner needs time or wants to share findings with OMs before deciding:

*"That makes sense. Take what you need. What I'd suggest is that before you share anything with your management team, we talk briefly — even 30 minutes — so I can give you language that sets the right frame. What you share and how you share it will affect what's possible in Phase 5."*

## Scoping Adjustments

*Use when standard package scope doesn't fit the engagement — more leaders, different phase inclusion, add-ons, or custom requests.*

| Scenario | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| More than 6 leaders in Option C assessment | Add ~$5,000 per additional leader |
| More than 4 leaders in Option B coaching | Add ~$10,500 per additional leader |
| Owner wants Month 12 checkpoint added to Option C | Add $6,500–$9,000 depending on leader count |
| Owner wants implementation support only (no assessment) | Not recommended — assessment data is the prerequisite for pattern-specific coaching; coaching without assessment produces generic development |
| Owner wants team facilitation only | Can be scoped as a standalone team session series; 3-session arc at $8,500 without assessment component (note: facilitation without pattern data is less targeted) |
| One property only (subset of portfolio) | No discount — assessment hours are leader-count driven, not property-count driven; same architecture applies |
| Accelerated timeline | Not recommended for Phase 5 coaching arc — 12 sessions over 6 months is minimum for meaningful behavioral change; assessment phases can compress modestly if scheduling allows |
| Owner wants ongoing advisory retainer after engagement closes | Scoped separately; typically $2,500–$4,000/month for ad hoc advisory availability + monthly owner check-in |
