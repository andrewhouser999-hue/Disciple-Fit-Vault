# A001 — Credibility Talking Points

*Internalize, don't recite. Use the short version in conversation. The expanded version is what you reach for when you need to develop the point.*

*Sequence: establish the foundation first (Domains 1–5), then pull back honestly (Domain 6). Competence before vulnerability — not the other way.*

---

## Domain 1 — I Know How This Works

**Short:**
"I know how this works at the level where it actually operates — not the management book version. I've been deep in the primary research."

**Developed:**
"What you put together in that conversation — environment driving behavior, not character — that's exactly what the neuroscience says. I can tell you precisely how it operates, why standard training misses it, and what it costs an operation when nothing reaches that level."

---

## Domain 2 — I've Done This Work on Myself

**Short:**
"I've done this work on myself. Not in a seminar — in my actual life, over years."

**Developed:**
"I know what it costs to reorganize how you respond under pressure because I've been through it. I regulate now in situations that used to shut me down. I know what that change actually requires — and how long it takes."

*Why this matters in the room: most consultants have studied this. Few have lived it. That's a distinction the owner will feel, even if he can't name it.*

---

## Domain 3 — Multi-Modal, Not Single-Framework

**Short:**
"I've been trained across trauma-informed work, cognitive-behavioral approaches, and behavioral change methodology. Real people don't fit one model."

**Developed:**
"I'm a certified professional coach — behavioral, organizational, non-clinical. I know exactly where my scope ends and I stay behind that line. What that means practically is that I work at the level of environment and behavior, not clinical intervention."

---

## Domain 4 — I've Built Training That Actually Changed Outcomes

**Short:**
"I've built training from scratch that moved the needle — not scored well on a survey, changed measurable outcomes."

**Developed:**
"Across 75 new hires, the program I designed produced 30% more top-rated performance than anything the organization had run before. The module ran four additional cohorts after I left. The thing I figured out building it: the gap wasn't skill — it was identity. People couldn't perform consistently until they knew who they were in the role. That's the same insight you had."

*This last line is a bridge. Use it deliberately — it connects Andrew's training discovery to the owner's coffee shop moment.*

---

## Domain 5 — I've Moved Teams Through Questions, Not Arguments

**Short:**
"I've moved teams — not by presenting better arguments, by asking better questions."

**Developed:**
"I've facilitated a siloed senior team into peer-driven collaboration. 40% fewer specialist escalations in 90 days. Not by training them in collaboration — by changing what was possible in the room through how I worked the conversation."

---

## Domain 6 — The Honest Pull-Back (Use After Foundation Is Set)

**Short:**
"I haven't run this in a hotel before. I won't pretend I have."

**Developed:**
"What I know — the mechanism, the research synthesis, how behavioral change actually happens in practice — I know that deeply. Your operation is the specific context I'm here to learn. The research I'm working from was developed in hospitality. The integration I'm bringing to a multi-property operation at this scale — that's new. That's what I'm building with you. And honestly, that's part of what you're getting: someone who's bringing the right framework to your specific situation, not a canned program."

*The pratfall principle: this disclosure, made after the foundation is set, increases credibility — it signals self-awareness. Made before the foundation, it produces doubt. Sequence matters.*

---

## The Through-Line (Coherent Narrative)

If the owner asks a version of "what makes you qualified for this?" — this is the one-paragraph version that connects everything:

"My whole career has been studying one thing: what makes people function or fail under pressure, and how to build things that actually change that. I've done it in sales, in training design, in individual coaching, and in my own life. I've been in the primary research on the mechanism you're seeing in your operation. And I've synthesized eleven evidence-based practices that are load-bearing for creating the kind of environment where your people can actually perform — not because they're forced to, but because the conditions make it possible. I haven't run this in a hotel chain before. The framework I'm drawing from was built there. What I'm doing is bringing it to your specific operation."
