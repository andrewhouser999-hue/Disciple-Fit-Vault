Raised emotionally neglected by a lovely but mechanical mother; under the threat of a dysregulated father. 
I had no idea about urban modern life, neglected at launch.
Worked exceptionally hard for success to prove personal value. 
Identity distortion impoverished my soul
Underlying lies about God, me, and others lived as truth
Internal hooks held me to perfectionism
God was an authoritarian ready to punish
I was desperate for connection and belonging, unable to obtain it.
Misaligned from purpose and calling
I found Workaholism had no limits, until my body revolted. 
I wanted belonging in a world that has narrow social interests, immature social needs, and restricts its relational commitments. 
I was kicked out, asked to leave and marginalized by all the churches I served. 
I was blind to the legalism of leadership


As a result 
I saw myself as a human doing
I saw profiles not people. I saw people through conditions of value not relationally, all the while desperately seeking connection.
I worked jobs I wasn't entirely well suited enough to keep longer term.
Success felt like my ticket to worth as employment increasingly lost its luster. I felt lost, relationally isolated while losing trust in humanity. 
At times I preferred death over having no emotions that came with PTSD 
I was used to social acceptance, then for decades I entirely experienced rejection trauma from churches, employment, family of origin and extended family, and short term friendships. 
I identified with portraying an ideal image more than relating with others free from a posture.  
Relationships last as long as the value of superficial connection.
I felt lonely surrounded by many.
I couldn't show up as regulated husband
I felt a chilling connection to this decaying, empty professional and relationally dead culture. It was destroying me. I became concerningly guarded when someone was friendly. 
The vivacious, life is a candy store, self was left completely behind.

One day I said "No more!"
I did lie work choosing truthful perspectives. Found a process to believe a new identity.  The cost of staying the same on the inside was too great. I needed to take extreme ownership of the scripts that were running my life. It was time to get free!  


**My Personhood**
Using Holland Codes: I am almost certainly Social-Investigative-Artistic.

Using SDT (Self-Determination Theory): you need autonomy, relatedness, and meaning to sustain engagement.

Using career identity theory: you've been in vocational moratorium — a prolonged exploration phase without commitment. 

 I am a high-meaning, relationally-gifted, conceptually-driven person who has spent most of his career in wrong-fit environments — and has used circumstance as the explanation when identity ambiguity was the root cause.
