# Market Segment — The Wilderness Christian

**Status:** Defined
**Market Role:** Secondary Target
**Last updated:** April 2026

---

## One-Line Definition

> A sincere, spiritually serious Christian who had a genuine encounter with God, tried to do it right, was failed or wounded by the institutional church, and is still pursuing God — isolated, carrying wounds the church either caused or couldn't touch, stuck in a way that spiritual effort alone has not fixed.

---

## Who They Are

**Demographic:**
- Primary range: 25–45
- Economic range varies — not uniformly well-off; church trauma and life trauma often correlate with economic instability
- Single, married, or in a relationally damaged marriage
- Not consistently churchgoing — between churches, church-avoidant, or attending while fundamentally disconnected
- Working in available employment, not necessarily a chosen career

**Psychographic:**
- Genuine faith — not inherited religion, a real experience with God that preceded or survived the institutional wound
- Has been burned by institutional Christianity: excommunication, legalism, spiritual abuse, failure to disciple, or quiet marginalization
- Still pursuing God privately, even when corporate faith feels empty or dangerous
- Self-aware about their patterns — can name them, cannot move them alone
- Carries real wounds: rejection trauma, family of origin patterns, possibly narcissistic abuse in marriage or church authority
- Suspicious of authority and institutions — not paranoid; earned
- Feels like they don't fit anywhere: too wounded for the happy churchgoer, too faithful to walk away

Drawn from the Disciple Fit 17. They rarely present with just one.

**Primary — the entry point:**
- **#7 — Spiritually stuck, flat, don't know what's in the way** — the persistent flatness they cannot explain or fix
- **#6 — Isolated, known by no one** — excluded from or left behind by the community that should know them

**Secondary — what drives the stuck:**
- **#2 — Theology that won't translate** — they know what's true; they cannot make it embodied
- **#3 — Anxiety and shame faith keeps failing to touch** — the emotional substrate underneath the theology

**Tertiary — the cost accumulating:**
- **#8 — Burned out on ministry or leadership** — for those who served faithfully before the wound
- **#14 — Unlived calling** — the sense of purpose they've been carrying with nowhere to deploy it
- **#15 — Life not adding up to anything** — years of motion without the formation that should accompany it

## The Internal Experience

**Isolation** — not the comfortable numbness of the high-functioning Christian, but the raw ache of genuine exclusion. They have been rejected by the community that was supposed to receive them. The loneliness has a wound quality.

**Anger** — often unnamed. Anger at the church, at leaders who failed them, at God for allowing it — followed immediately by shame about the anger at God. The cycle runs privately.

**Persistent shame** — they've been told the problem is their rebellion, their sin, their attitude, their unwillingness to submit. They've internalized some of that verdict even when it was unjust. The shame compounds the wound.

**Confusion** — they've had real encounters with God. The institutional community that was supposed to verify and extend those encounters is gone or hostile. Without relational rootedness, the experiences sometimes feel unreal. Faith built on private encounter, without community, gets brittle under pressure.

## Cognitive Architecture (CBT Lens)

**Core Schemas:**

| Schema | Manifestation |
|---|---|
| Mistrust / Abuse | "Institutions and leaders will use me and discard me when I no longer serve them." |
| Defectiveness / Shame | "Something about me provokes rejection. If people really knew me, they'd leave." |
| Social Isolation / Alienation | "I don't belong anywhere. I'm fundamentally different from normal Christians." |
| Abandonment | "The people who should have stayed didn't. This pattern will repeat." |
| Emotional Deprivation | "My real interior life has never been known or met by anyone." |

**Key Distortions:**
- Overgeneralizing from institutional wounds to all spiritual authority — difficulty trusting any guide
- Mind-reading — anticipating rejection before giving the relationship a chance
- Personalization — internalizing institutional dysfunction as personal failure ("I was kicked out because something is wrong with me")
- Emotional reasoning — *"I feel rejected by God's people, therefore God has rejected me"*
- Intermittent spiritual bypass — using theological certainty to avoid the grief of the wound

---

## Spiritual Architecture

**Stated theology:** "God is faithful. His people failed me — He didn't. I still believe."

**Operative theology:** "The community I was supposed to grow in doesn't want me. The disciplines that were supposed to work aren't working. Something is broken between me and God and I don't know what it is or if it can be fixed."

**The gap:** They believe in God's faithfulness. Their experience confirms that the structures through which God normally works — church, community, discipleship — have failed or wounded them. They are trying to grow without the ecosystem growth requires. The faith is real. The conditions are inhospitable. They are spiritually malnourished inside a genuine relationship with God, and cannot reconcile those two facts.

**Result:** Private pursuit becomes the only safe option. Spiritual disciplines are inconsistent — driven by shame cycles, unaddressed spiritual attack, and the absence of anyone to be accountable to. Church attendance, when it happens, is managed and guarded rather than open. They have learned to protect the inner life because exposure got them burned.

## What They've Already Tried (And Why It Failed)

| What They Tried | Why It Failed |
|---|---|
| Church | Has repeatedly failed to disciple, genuinely love, or walk with them; often added wounds |
| Therapy | Doesn't integrate the faith; sometimes pathologizes or dismisses spiritual experience |
| Pastoral counseling | Theologically fluent, but the pastor is often part of the institutional system that wounded them |
| Self-study (Word, books, podcasts) | Growth without relationship — information without formation; adds knowledge to an already-knowledge-saturated person |
| Accountability relationships | Short-lived or lost with the church wound; the relational infrastructure for accountability no longer exists |
| Spiritual disciplines alone | Increasingly empty without relational rootedness; disciplines practiced in isolation tend to drift or collapse under attack |

They fall through every gap. Too theologically serious for casual Christian community. Too wounded to trust institutional structures. Too self-aware to accept surface-level answers. Too faithful to walk away.

---

## Why They Don't Need More Information

This client has often studied harder than the average churchgoer precisely because they've had to survive without community. They've built a private knowledge base out of necessity. Theological information is not what's missing. What is missing is a person — someone who knows both the Word and the inner world, who has walked where they've walked, and who will not require performance in exchange for relationship. The coaching relationship itself becomes part of the intervention.

- The realization that years have passed without real movement or real relationship
- A relationship failure that strips their remaining support structure
- A season of spiritual re-engagement after a dry period, with the recognition that they cannot do this alone

- NOT church networks — they don't have those relationships; pastoral referrals are not a reliable acquisition channel for this group

---

## What Earns Their Trust

*What they need to see, hear, or experience before they will invest.*

> TO FILL IN — based on discovery call patterns and client feedback.

Likely trust signals:
- **The coach's own story told publicly** — they need to know you've been failed by the institution and stayed faithful to God. Story precedes method.
- **Language that names the wound without abandoning God** — they will not follow someone who defends the institution blindly or who uses the wound as an excuse to deconstruct faith
- **No performance requirements** — they've been judged by hidden standards. The coaching relationship must feel categorically different from the beginning.
- **Theological credibility** — they know their Bible; shallow faith will not earn sustained respect
- **Patience over urgency** — they do not trust quickly. Consistency over time is what builds the bridge.
- **Psychological vocabulary that explains without pathologizing** — they want to understand their inner experience, not be diagnosed or diminished

---

## What They Need

1. A coach who has been where they have been and can speak from earned authority — not theory
2. A framework for understanding why the wounds happened, without excusing the institution or abandoning faith in God's design for community
3. Identity work rooted in God's verdict rather than community verification — their identity cannot depend on a community that has proven unreliable
4. Wound awareness and the space to name what the church either caused or failed to address
5. A relational context that is itself a corrective experience — safe, consistent, honest, and not contingent on performance

---

## Relationship to Disciple Fit

**Primary service fit:** 1:1 coaching and structured program. Group coaching is a future option — once sufficient individual trust is built. This population's relational trauma makes group entry higher risk initially; group becomes powerful only after the client has experienced safety in a 1:1 context.

**Core value proposition:** A coach who has lived the wound, held the faith, and built a framework for both — the only intervention that is simultaneously theologically honest, psychologically rigorous, and relationally real, without requiring institutional belonging as a prerequisite.

**Offer entry point:** Story-driven content on YouTube that names the church wound without departing from God; discovery call that begins with the client's story before presenting any framework.
