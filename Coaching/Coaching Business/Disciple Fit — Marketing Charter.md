# Disciple Fit — Marketing Charter

*What this is: a two-page governing reference for every marketing, content, and intake decision Disciple Fit makes. It states the rules the underlying research produced — it does not re-argue the research. When a piece of copy, a segment choice, or a discovery-call approach needs a gut-check, this is the document to open first.*

*What this is not: a replacement for the source research. Every rule below compresses a specific document's findings. Where you need the full picture — psychographics, exact trigger language, channel-by-channel detail — the pointers at the bottom take you there.*

*Governing relationship: this Charter sits above [[Branding & Core Competency]] and [[Disciple Fit — Differentiation Document]]. Those documents handle voice, tagline, and competitive positioning. If anything in them ever conflicts with a rule stated here, this document governs — update the other document, not this one.*

---

## The Governing Rule

**People pay when they feel the cost of a problem — not when they see its root.**

A client does not walk in already understanding that their marriage flatness or their looping shame cycle is a false-identity problem. They walk in knowing only that something specific in their life is bleeding. The spiritual or identity root — what Disciple Fit actually treats — is something the coach reveals *after* the felt cost has been recognized and named, never before.

**Implication for every piece of marketing copy:** lead with the felt, concrete cost. Never lead with the diagnostic or theological root. "I'm not growing spiritually" is not, by itself, a reason anyone pays — it reads as a problem a devotional or a pastor already solves for free. "My marriage feels like coexistence, not partnership, and I don't know why" is a reason someone pays. The root is the destination of the engagement, not the opening line of the pitch.

## The Buyer's Journey

Every client moves through the same five stages, in the same order, regardless of which segment they belong to. No stage can be skipped — skipping from Stage 1 straight to Stage 4 (introducing the root before recognition has been earned) is the single most common way trust is lost before a discovery call even happens.

| Stage | What's happening | What Disciple Fit must do |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Felt cost, no root language | Client experiences a specific, bleeding cost (flatness, anxiety, isolation, burnout) but has no framework for why | Nothing yet — this stage happens without Disciple Fit's involvement |
| 2. Recognition | Client encounters content or conversation that names their exact interior experience more precisely than they could name it themselves | Say their exact experience back to them before asking anything of them. This is the trust-opening moment. |
| 3. Curiosity | Having felt truly seen, the client becomes willing to consider there's something underneath the felt cost | Stay curious, not diagnostic — invite, don't announce, the deeper pattern |
| 4. Root revealed | The spiritual/identity root is introduced — in content, on the discovery call, or early in coaching | Only after recognition (Stage 2) has already landed. Introducing this earlier reads as being sold a framework rather than being understood. |
| 5. Willing | Having been seen, understood, and shown a credible deeper account, the client is ready to invest | Make the ask now — not before |

## Two Motivation Channels

Every presenting problem reduces to one of two channels. Both are equally strategic — aspiration is not a lesser or secondary lead type.

- **Pain Away (escape):** something is breaking, drifting, or stuck. Marriage flatness, repeated failure, isolation, spiritual stagnation.
- **Gain Toward (aspiration):** something is beginning. Marriage prep, a calling coming into focus, a first leadership role, launching something.

**Why aspiration is not secondary:** aspiration clients are frequently *more* motivated and resourced than pain clients, precisely because they are not in survival mode — they have the clarity and capacity to invest that crisis narrows. Treat a "gain toward" lead with the same seriousness as a "pain away" lead. Marketing that only speaks escape-language will miss a genuinely stronger buyer.

## Current Segment Registry

Full profiles live in their own documents (linked below). This registry states only what a marketing decision needs at a glance.

| Segment | Status | How they understand their own problem |
|---|---|---|
| **Sunday-Monday Christian** | Target Market | Does **not** self-identify as broken. Searches for growth, formation, and leadership content — not "help" content. Their felt cost is a widening gap between a Sunday self and a Monday self; the trigger is usually a role or scaffolding shifting (empty nest, career change, a marriage that stops running on autopilot), not an injury. |
| **Wilderness Christian** | Secondary | **Does** self-identify as wounded — specifically by the institutional church. Genuine faith survives an institutional injury; the felt cost is exclusion, unprocessed anger, and isolation alongside real, private pursuit of God. |

**Governing rule on status:** only one segment holds Target Market status at a time. New segments enter as Hypothetical and must be fully profiled (persona, problems, trigger, reach, trust) before they can be promoted. Do not split marketing weight evenly across segments — the Target Market gets the resourcing; others are served, not chased. See [[_Segment Index]] for how a segment's status changes.

## Trust: What Earns It, Universally

These hold across both segments and should show up in content before any ask is made.

1. **Precision of language naming the interior experience** — the single strongest trust signal available. Generic Christian growth language cannot do this; it takes a sentence that names what the prospect feels with more accuracy than they'd manage themselves.
2. **Theological depth, not theological volume** — a skeptical, literate buyer can tell the difference between someone who has genuinely gone deep with Scripture and someone who has accumulated Christian vocabulary. One thin moment is detectable and costly.
3. **Psychological competence beyond "pray harder, try harder"** — showing you understand *why* prior efforts (sermons, accountability groups, willpower) didn't work is itself a trust signal, because it proves you're not offering more of what already failed.
4. **Integrated testimony, not performed vulnerability** — a resolved account of having lived through and worked out of the same gap lands as credibility. Manufactured relatability, deployed for likability, does the opposite.
5. **Precise audience specificity** — naming exactly who this is for makes a prospect feel *found*, not *targeted*. Broad, generic positioning reads as a marketing move; narrow, accurate positioning reads as evidence you know who you're talking to.
6. **Peer proof of change** — a transformation story from someone who resembles the prospect (in role, church involvement, life stage) outweighs any description of methodology or credentials.

---

## Trust: What Kills It, Universally

1. **Pressure or urgency** — registers immediately as manipulation to a self-aware buyer with good pattern recognition.
2. **Shallow theological content** — undoes prior credibility in a single instance; there is no partial credit.
3. **Demanding performance from the prospect** — this buyer is already exhausted from having to present well; anything that requires them to perform, even subtly, recreates the exhaustion instead of relieving it.
4. **Generic Christian growth language** ("become who God made you to be") — every coach in the space says this; it reads as noise, not differentiation.
5. **Overselling transformation** — a buyer who has already read the books that promised change is more persuaded by precision about what the process does *and does not* do than by a bigger promise.

## Brand Type Rule — Covenant Identity Coaching Naming

CIC (Covenant Identity Coaching) is in an active rename exploration; leading candidate is **Word Within**. This rule is scoped to CIC's own name only — it does not apply to Disciple Fit's name, which is a separate, later decision with its own logic when that work happens.

**The Rule: any CIC rename must be a functional/authority brand, not an identity or cause brand.**

The practice has no capacity to sustain a continuous publishing cadence, and that constraint is decisive between brand types, not a minor preference:
- **Identity brands** (aspirational, "who you become") require constant narrative reinforcement — the aspirational self has to be re-proven in every piece of content or the brand reads as vague and hollow.
- **Cause brands** (recruit against a named status quo) require even more — a movement has to keep proving itself against that status quo continuously or it reads as abandoned.
- **Functional/authority brands do their work once.** The name describes the mechanism; trust is earned through referral relationships and demonstrated rigor (a sourced, precise system) rather than through ongoing storytelling.

This also reinforces two rules already established above rather than adding a new risk: identity and cause brands drift toward **generic Christian growth language** over time as they run out of new things to say — already named as a universal trust-killer. **Precision of language** and **peer proof of change** — the two strongest trust-builders — are both native to a functional/authority brand and don't depend on content velocity to keep working.

**Standing guardrails for any CIC rename, non-negotiable:**
- **No Word-of-Faith adjacency** — never implies verbal declaration alone produces change. Any "word/truth spoken" language must sit next to diagnosis and relationship, never stand alone as technique.
- **No institutional critique** — church, sermons, and pastoral teaching are framed as companion, never as insufficient. This directly enforces the Governing Rule above: the felt cost stays personal and internal, never externalized onto an institution.
- **No clinical language** — CIC is non-clinical; scope stays behavioral and operational.

Full messaging architecture (positioning statement, message pillars, audience-specific translation, voice/tone, draft homepage copy) lives in [[Branding & Core Competency]] under "Word Within — CIC Messaging Architecture" — that document executes voice and positioning; this rule is what it must not violate.

## Standing Rules

- **Any CIC rename must choose a functional/authority brand type, never identity or cause** — see Brand Type Rule above; low content-capacity makes sustained-narrative brand types unsustainable.
- **Only one segment holds Target Market status at a time** (see Segment Registry above and [[_Segment Index]]).
- **This Charter governs [[Branding & Core Competency]] and [[Disciple Fit — Differentiation Document]]** — those documents execute voice and positioning; this one sets the rules they must not violate.
- **A spiritual problem is only marketing-ready once it's anchored to a named felt cost.** "Not growing spiritually" never leads; the felt cost always leads, and the spiritual root follows once trust is earned (see The Governing Rule, above).
