# How to Start an LLC in Douglas County, NE

## 1. Choose a Name

- Must include "LLC," "L.L.C.," or "Limited Liability Company"
- Check availability at the Nebraska Secretary of State name search
- Optionally reserve the name for 120 days ($30 fee)

## 2. File Articles of Organization

- File with the **Nebraska Secretary of State** (not the county)
- Can file online at sos.nebraska.gov or by mail
- Filing fee: **$100** (online) or **$110** (mail)
- Include: LLC name, registered agent name/address, organizer signature

## 3. Designate a Registered Agent

- Must be a Nebraska resident or a company authorized to do business in Nebraska
- Must have a physical street address in Nebraska (not a P.O. Box)

## 4. Publish a Notice (Nebraska-specific requirement)

- Nebraska requires publishing a **notice of organization** for **3 consecutive weeks** in a legal newspaper in Douglas County
- For Douglas County, use the **Daily Record** or another approved publication
- File proof of publication with the Secretary of State � fee: **$25**
- This step is unique to Nebraska and is commonly missed

## 5. Get an EIN

- Apply free at irs.gov � needed for taxes and opening a business bank account

## 6. Operating Agreement

- Not required by Nebraska law, but strongly recommended for multi-member LLCs

## 7. Business Licenses

- No general state business license in Nebraska
- Check with the **City of Omaha** (if operating in Omaha) for city/industry-specific licenses
- Douglas County does not issue a general business license
