## Attributes of Christ — Guide for Outcome-Based Selling Points

### The Critical Theological Distinction
The attributes of Christ are not virtues to be imitated through effort. They are the character of the indwelling Spirit — already present — being *released* as barriers are removed.

> *"Christ in you, the hope of glory." — Colossians 1:27*

- Sanctification is not addition — it's subtraction of what blocks
- The soil work removes what prevents His character from expressing
- The client isn't manufacturing love, peace, patience — they're clearing ground for what's already there to grow

**This is why the mechanism is distinct from behavior change.** Behavior change adds new actions. Sanctification removes old blockages. The psychological modalities do the clearing — the Spirit does the forming.

### Attributes Organized by Category

**Character (who He is internally):**
Humility, Holiness, Truth, Faithfulness, Self-control, Courage

**Relational (how He moves toward others):**
Love, Compassion, Mercy, Grace, Forgiveness, Presence

**Fruit of the Spirit — Galatians 5:22-23:**
Love, Joy, Peace, Patience, Kindness, Goodness, Faithfulness, Gentleness, Self-control

**Functional (how He operated):**
Wisdom, Authority, Obedience, Generosity, Justice

## Core Message — Wipe Off Before Wipe On

The key to life success has been in front of us the entire time. We glimpse it, then the current of life pulls us back before it can take root.

Most people spend their energy wiping on — adding knowledge, strategy, habits, truth. In youth, that is how we learn. As we season, wiping off becomes the path forward. What needs to be removed grows increasingly important, because it is the scaffolding of every ceiling we hit in life.

**The biblical anchor:** Matthew 6:33 — seek first the kingdom of heaven. What enables that ability is not more input. It is clearing the inner man so affections are freed to hold Heaven First. When that is settled, everything else follows.

**How unique is this message?**
The core principle — subtraction before addition — is not new:
- Lao Tzu (ancient): *"To attain wisdom, remove things every day."*
- Richard Rohr (*Falling Upward*): second half of life is letting go, not adding
- Dallas Willard (*Renovation of the Heart*): inner world transformation precedes external change
- Leidy Klotz (*Subtract*, 2021): humans systematically overlook subtraction as a solution
- Greg McKeown (*Essentialism*, 2014): the disciplined pursuit of less

**What is genuinely distinctive:**
- **"The scaffolding of every ceiling you hit"** — concrete, diagnostic, applies across all life domains simultaneously. No existing framework uses this language.
- **Kingdom-first as the organizing principle of all-domains flourishing** — not just spiritual growth, but the master key behind everything.
- **The "glimpse and miss" dynamic** — people catch this insight and the current sweeps them back before it can take root. No framework addresses why the insight doesn't stick.
- **Affections as the mechanism** — clearing the inner man so affections are freed to hold Heaven First. Augustinian love-ordering applied practically. No one at the popular level owns this combination.
- **A coaching system that implements it** — Rohr and Willard teach the concept. No one has built the accessible, practical methodology that actually gets people there.

**Competitive Forces**
AI coaching is a real competitive force for generic, low-touch life coaching. It undercuts on price ($20–50/mo vs. $200–500/session) and is always available.

Where it can't compete: relational depth, spiritual discernment, and covenantal accountability — the exact things your model is built on. A chatbot can reflect a client's words back. It can't bear witness to someone's covenant identity before God.

So AI is a threat to commodity coaching and an argument for differentiation — which is the direction you're already heading.
