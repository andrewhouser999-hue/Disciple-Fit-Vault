# Disciple Fit Coaching — Scope of Practice Definition

**What this is:** A one-page professional scope document defining what Disciple Fit coaching is, what it is not, and when to refer out. For use in ethics compliance, referral conversations, and credentialing review.
**Who it's for:** Andy (internal reference), referral partners, credentialing reviewers
**Date:** April 2026

---

## What Disciple Fit Coaching Is

Disciple Fit is a **Christian life coaching practice** operating within the framework of the International Coaching Federation (ICF), which defines coaching as:

> *"Partnering with clients in a thought-provoking and creative process that inspires them to maximize their personal and professional potential."* — ICF Official Definition

Disciple Fit applies this within a **CBC (Cognitive Behavioral Coaching with Biblical Integration)** framework — combining evidence-based coaching modalities (CBT-informed tools, GROW, Solution-Focused, ACT, SDT) with biblical truth as the structural foundation.

Disciple Fit coaching is:
- **Forward-focused** — oriented toward growth, change, and goal attainment
- **Client-directed** — the client sets the agenda; the coach facilitates clarity and movement
- **Facilitative, not authoritative** — biblical truth is offered as framework, not prescribed as doctrine
- **Skill-based** — deploying evidence-based coaching and CBT-informed tools
- **Appropriate for functional individuals** — people who are capable of normal daily functioning and motivated toward growth

## What Disciple Fit Coaching Is Not

### Not Therapy or Clinical Counseling

| Dimension | Coaching (Disciple Fit) | Clinical Therapy |
|---|---|---|
| Focus | Present → future; growth and goals | Past → present; symptom reduction |
| Client assumption | Whole and functional | May have clinical impairment |
| Diagnosis | None — outside scope | DSM-based assessment |
| Licensing | Not required for coaching | Required by law |
| Method | Facilitative, skill-based | Therapeutic intervention |
| Scope | Normal life challenges | Clinical disorders |

Disciple Fit does **not** treat, diagnose, or provide clinical intervention for mental health disorders.

### Not Pastoral Care

| Dimension | Coaching (Disciple Fit) | Pastoral Care |
|---|---|---|
| Authority | Facilitative — client-led | Spiritually authoritative — pastor-led |
| Goal | Client-defined growth | Spiritual guidance, doctrine, community |
| Method | Evidence-based tools + biblical framework | Scripture, prayer, sacrament, community |
| Relationship | Professional coaching agreement | Shepherd/congregant relationship |
| Cost | Professional fee | Free or congregational |
| Scope | Individual growth and change | Spiritual oversight and care |

Disciple Fit **uses** biblical framework — it does **not** function as pastoral oversight, spiritual authority, or doctrinal direction. Coaches are not pastors. Pastoral referral and coaching are **complementary, not competing**.

## Who Disciple Fit Serves

Ideal client: A **functional Christian** experiencing a growth gap — the distance between who they currently are and who they sense God is calling them to become. They are:
- Capable of daily functioning (work, relationships, basic self-care)
- Motivated toward change
- Willing to engage honestly with their patterns, beliefs, and identity
- Seeking more than information — they need a structured, relational process

---

## Referral Criteria — When to Refer Out

Refer to a licensed therapist or clinical counselor when a client presents with:

- Active suicidal ideation or self-harm
- Clinical depression (inability to function, extended duration)
- Diagnosed or suspected PTSD or complex trauma requiring processing
- Active addiction or substance dependence
- Psychosis, delusional thinking, or break from reality
- Active abuse situation requiring safety planning
- Personality disorder presentation
- Presenting issues requiring diagnosis or medication management

**Protocol (per ICF Standard 3.7):** When a client's needs exceed coaching scope, the coach is ethically obligated to name it clearly, explain the distinction, and actively assist the client in identifying appropriate clinical resources — without abandoning the coaching relationship unless clinical need fully displaces it.

---

## Summary Statement

> Disciple Fit operates at the intersection of professional life coaching, evidence-based cognitive tools, and biblical framework. It serves functional Christians navigating real growth challenges — not clinical disorders, not doctrinal formation. It is professionally aligned with ICF standards, theologically grounded without being pastorally authoritative, and clinically humble without being clinically limited.
