## 8. Honest Risk Assessment

**Most likely reason she stalls in the first 90 days:**
She positions herself as a generalist ("CS operations and project management") and gets lost. UpWork's algorithm does not reward broad positioning; it rewards clarity. If she applies to every CS-adjacent job, she wins none of them. She underprices to get first reviews, gets stuck doing low-value documentation or VA-adjacent work, and never breaks into the strategic consulting category she's actually qualified for.

**Second most likely reason:**
Tool fluency gaps surface during proposals. A client asks about Zendesk experience, she has none, and the conversation ends. This is fixable before launch.

**What to do in the first 30 days to de-risk both:**
1. Get Zendesk certified. Non-negotiable.
2. Build the one case study document. It becomes her primary portfolio piece.
3. Define the 4–8 week startup CS foundation offer with a clear scope, deliverables list, and fixed price. Having a packaged offer separates her from freelancers who only do hourly work.
4. Apply to 5–8 jobs at $10–15/hr below her target rate to build initial reviews. Treat this as a marketing cost, not her real rate.
5. Join Support Driven and answer one substantive question per week about CS ops or knowledge management.
