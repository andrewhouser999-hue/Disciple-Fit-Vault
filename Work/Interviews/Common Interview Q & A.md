## Why did you leave Schwab?

**Draft — approved for use**

Schwab went through significant restructuring after the TD Ameritrade merger. My department was identified as a duplication. I saw it coming, left on my terms, and was intentional about what I moved toward next. This is one of the roles that made the short list.

**Notes:**

- End with the short list line — it's a subtle power move that redirects to the present and signals you're evaluating them too.

## Q16 — Tell me about yourself

**Draft — approved for use**

The throughline across everything I have done is one discipline: understanding how people think, communicate, and decide, and using that to drive results.

I started on the front lines in sales. Became nationally ranked with a 48.6% close rate. At a second company I was producing at 3x my peers. What separated me was not effort. It was diagnosis. I was identifying where decisions were breaking down for clients and addressing that directly, which led to larger deals, stronger relationships, and more consistent outcomes.

That led me to get certified as a professional coach and pursue cognitive behavioral training. Not as a career detour. As a sharpening of the same skill — the ability to find what is actually blocking performance, in an individual or a team, and build a path to close that gap.

Leadership pulled me in to lead onboarding. I built a performance development system. I shifted the focus from skill transfer to identity and decision-making first. New hire satisfaction scores went up 30%.

I am looking for a revenue leadership role where I drive results and build the people and systems that sustain them. That is the work I am built for.
