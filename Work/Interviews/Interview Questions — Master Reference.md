## Resume-Specific Questions

### Performance & Results

**Q1. You were ranked #13 nationally with a 48.6% closing ratio. Walk me through how you got there.**

**Q2. Your perfect-score call rate was nearly 3x the company average. What were you doing differently than everyone else?**
- Answer lives in article: *The Most Underrated Skill in Every High-Performing Person's Life*
- Core: treated communication as a precision discipline, not a soft skill. Listened for the person, not just the content. Treated every conversation as a live diagnostic.
- Stats: 12.12% perfect scores vs. 4.37% company average. Quarterly peak 14%. Manager recognized as highest relational and emotional intelligence of any hire in his tenure.

**Q3. You drove 50-100% annual revenue growth against a 5% industry average. What was the mechanism?**
- Context: Grace University Advancement Officer role. Team of four reporting to Executive VP.
- Andrew's specific contribution: built a valued authentic relationship with a major donor being recognized, executed event details with excellence.
- VP was the primary strategist. This was a team effort. Andrew was an individual contributor, not the lead.
- Key differentiator: Andrew was better at building authentic relationships than the executive staff, including his team. Not transactional, not socially awkward. This separated him.
- He was offered the VP role but did not accept. Had he, he would have led with a completely different relational approach — more frequent, more authentic engagement across the full major donor pipeline.
- Article: not yet written. Hold for future.

**Q4. Your personal production was roughly 50% of total local market sales. How do you sustain that without burning out?**
- Answer partially lives in: *The Real Reason High Performers Keep Going When Everyone Else Stops*
- Core: intrinsic motivation, identity-based performance, meaning as the fuel source.

### Leadership & Team Development

**Q5. You were selected to build onboarding training from scratch. Why you, and what did you build?**
- Correction: was appointed as onboarding trainer and mentor. The training deck was already set. Found a slot and added a deck focused on "becoming World Class" — the identity the CEO championed.
- Worked with level one managers to identify the gap, built the case, got their buy-in to reinforce the identity.
- Results: 30% more calls scoring 100% client satisfaction vs. all prior classes. Deck remained for duration of tenure.
- Answer lives in article: *You're Not Stuck — You're Protecting Something*

**Q6. You identified that the core onboarding barrier was identity, not skill. Explain that diagnosis and what you did with it.**
- Answer lives in article: *You're Not Stuck — You're Protecting Something*
- Core: new hires had the content but not the belief. Skill training landed on an identity that didn't support using it. The deck addressed the belief first.

### Coaching & Human Development

**Q9. You are a certified coach with a sales career. How do those two things connect?**
- The work is the same: identify what's blocking performance, close the gap, build something that lasts. Coaching and sales both require surfacing the real issue, earning trust, and moving someone from where they are to where they are capable of being.

**Q10. What does it mean to surface the real issue beneath the stated problem? Give me an example.**
- Answer partially lives in: *You're Not Stuck — You're Protecting Something*
- Core: most people present the surface version of their concern. The real issue is usually a belief or protection strategy underneath. Sitting with the stated problem long enough to ask whether it is the actual problem.

**Q11. You say you treat communication as a precision discipline. What does that mean practically?**
- Answer lives in article: *The Most Underrated Skill in Every High-Performing Person's Life*
- Three components: genuine presence, surfacing what is underneath, adaptive delivery.
