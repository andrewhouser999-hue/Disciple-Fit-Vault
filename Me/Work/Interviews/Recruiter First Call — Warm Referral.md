# Recruiter First Call — Warm Referral

**Scenario:** Referred by a close family member to a recruiter who owns a staffing agency (temp + full-time). Never spoken before. Do not qualify for the specific role that prompted the referral.

---

## Before the Call
- Know what kinds of roles the agency places (temp, contract, direct hire, industries)
- Have your 30-second professional summary ready — who you are, what you do, what you're targeting
- Know your availability and what you're open to (full-time, contract, temp-to-hire)

---

## Opening the Call
Lead with the referral immediately — it's your credibility anchor:

> *"Hi [Name], this is Andrew. [Family member] mentioned you'd be open to connecting — I really appreciate you taking the time."*

Short. Warm. Doesn't over-explain.

---

## Address the Elephant Early
Don't let them wonder if you think you're applying for the role discussed with your family member:

> *"I know the role you were discussing with [family member] wasn't a fit for my background, but I'd love to learn more about what your agency typically places and share where I'm at in my search — in case there's a future match."*

This shows self-awareness and respects their time.

---

## What to Communicate
1. **What you do** — clear, jargon-free, one sentence
2. **What you're looking for** — specific enough to be useful, not so narrow you eliminate options
3. **Your availability** — actively searching or passively open?
4. **What makes you easy to place** — reliability, flexibility, transferable skills

---

## Goal for This Call
Not a job. **A relationship.**

End with:
> *"Would it make sense to keep in touch as you see roles come through? I'm happy to send over my resume so you have it on file."*

---

## What NOT to Do
- Don't oversell or sound desperate
- Don't ask "do you have anything for me?" right away — let the conversation develop
- Don't bad-mouth current or past employers
- Don't talk salary in the first call unless they ask

---

**Bottom line:** You're auditioning to be someone they want to call. Be easy to talk to, clear about what you offer, and low-pressure. Recruiters remember candidates who make their job easier.
