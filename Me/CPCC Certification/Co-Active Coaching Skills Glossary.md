# Co-Active Coaching Skills Glossary

Comprehensive reference of coaching skills from the [[The Co-Active Model|Co-Active Model]]. See also [[Three Levels of Listening]].

---

## Core Skills

### Accountability
Three questions: **What will you do? By when? How will I know?** No blame or judgment attached.

### Acknowledgment
Addresses **WHO the client had to BE** to take the action they took. Deeper than praise - it's a deep knowing of the person.

### Articulate What's Going On / Name It
Tell the client what you observe. Use Level 2/3 Listening to speak what has not been said.

### Asking Permission
Grants access to intimate or impolite areas of focus. Shows respect for boundaries while enabling depth.

### Authenticity & Range
Three attributes: **Connection, Aliveness, Fierce Courage.**

### Bottom-lining
Brevity and succinctness. Getting to the essence. Cutting through story to the core.

### Challenge
Requesting the client stretch beyond self-imposed limits. Shakes up self-image. Three responses: **Yes, No, Counter-offer.**

### Championing
Standing up for clients when they doubt themselves. Future-focused - holding the vision of who they can be.

### Clearing
Active listening while the client vents. Also used by the coach to clear their own preoccupations before a session.

### Curiosity
Provocative, open-ended questions. Playful, full of wonder. Non-leading. Lowers risk of judgment.

### Designed Alliance
The custom-designed coaching relationship. Revisited regularly. Empowers the client as co-creator of the relationship.

### Embodiment
Knowledge owned from the inside out through the body. Not just intellectual understanding.

### Enrollment
Authentically engaging clients in possibilities of their biggest self.

### Geography
Conscious use of **body position, space, voice tone, and environment** in coaching. Physical movement to shift perspective.

### Goal Setting (SMART - CTI Version)
- **S**pecific
- **M**easurable
- **A**ccountable
- **R**esonant
- **T**hrilling

### Hold the Client's Agenda
The heart of Co-Active Coaching. Let go of own opinions; follow the client's lead. The client's agenda, not the coach's.

### Holding the Focus
Keep the client on track when distracted by life events or [[Captain Crew and Saboteur|Saboteur]].

### Inquiry
Powerful questions given as homework for reflection between sessions. Multiple answers; none "right."

### Intrude
Interrupt when needed for the client's agenda. Tell hard truths. Requires courage and self-management.

### Intuition
Direct knowing, non-linear. Trust your gut. Remain unattached to accuracy - offer it as a gift, not a fact.

### Making Distinctions
Separating blended concepts. Examples: failing vs. failure, money vs. success, busy vs. productive.

### Metaphor
Verbal pictures to illustrate a point. Brings abstract concepts to life.

### Metaview
Big-picture perspective. A "helicopter ride" above the client's life to see patterns and themes.

### Powerful Questions
Open-ended questions that evoke **clarity, action, discovery, insight, or commitment.**

### Reframing
Interpreting original data in a different way. Same facts, new meaning.

### Request
Specified action + conditions of satisfaction + deadline. Three responses: **Yes, No, Counter-offer.**

### Self-Management
Getting out of the way to hold the client's agenda. Setting aside opinions, preferences, judgments.

### Structures
Devices that remind clients of their vision, goals, purpose. Examples: collages, calendars, alarms, visual reminders.

### Take Charge
Directing the coaching path when clients lose their way. Using authority in service of the client.

### Values
Intrinsic principles, as distinct as a thumbprint. Not chosen; not morals. See [[Three Principles - Fulfillment Balance Process|Fulfillment]] for ways to clarify values.

### Vision / Visioning
Multi-faceted mental image that inspires action. Should be **sensuous, exciting, magnetic.**

### Witnessing / Be With
Fully engaged presence beyond thinking. Creates a spacious place for the client's full experience.
