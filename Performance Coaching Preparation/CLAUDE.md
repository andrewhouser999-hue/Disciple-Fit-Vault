## Research Standards

- Prioritize peer-reviewed sources, published practitioners, and clinical evidence
- Always note source quality — distinguish well-established from emerging from practitioner model
- Flag when something is contested or when the pop version overstates the evidence
- Life and professional performance focus — not sports
- Match the format of existing notes (see CBT & Cognitive Reframing as the style reference)

## Note Format

Match this structure:
1. **The Honest Assessment** — what's real, what's oversold, what the gap is
2. **Tier 1: Empirical Foundation** — key researchers, must-read sources, anchor studies
3. **Tier 2: Thought Leaders Worth the Time** — table format
4. **What to Ignore** — explicitly call out what to avoid and why
5. **Coaching Application** — practical, session-usable, specific
6. **Bottom Line** — 1-2 sentences
7. **Sources** — citations
8. **Related** — [[wikilinks]] to connected notes

Keep notes under 100 lines. Dense reference material goes in a separate deep-dive note if needed.

## Workflow

When asked to research a topic:
1. Produce a formatted note matching the style above
2. Flag evidence quality throughout
3. Keep it practical — this is a coaching reference, not an academic paper
4. Suggest 2-3 related topics to research next
5. Ask before saving — confirm the note is ready
