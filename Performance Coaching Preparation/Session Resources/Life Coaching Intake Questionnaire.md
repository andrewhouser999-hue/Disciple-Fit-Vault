# Life Coaching Intake Questionnaire

## Framework Overview

The most reliable starting point is the **Wheel of Life** (Paul J. Meyer, 1960s) — a visual map of client satisfaction across all life domains completed before any goal-setting begins.

---

## Wheel of Life Assessment

Have the client rate each area 1–10 (1 = total dissatisfaction, 10 = complete satisfaction). The gaps between areas are as revealing as the scores themselves.

| Life Area | Score (1–10) |
|---|---|
| Career / Work | |

| Relationships | |
| Personal Growth | |
| Fun / Recreation | |
| Physical Environment | |
| Spirituality / Meaning | |
| Mental / Emotional Wellbeing | |
| Social / Community | |

## Section 1: Life Context & Responsibilities

1. What are the major roles and responsibilities you carry right now? (parent, caregiver, professional, etc.)
2. Describe your current daily life — what does a typical week look like?
3. What life domains feel most demanding of your time and energy?
4. What obligations feel chosen vs. ones that feel imposed?
5. What has recently changed in your life that brought you here?

---

## Section 2: Goals & Vision

6. What specific outcome are you hoping coaching will produce?
7. What would your life look like in 1 year if things went well? 3 years? 5?
8. What does success mean to you — in your own words, not anyone else's?
9. What do you most want but have been unable to get on your own?

---

## Section 3: Thinking Patterns & Mindset

This section surfaces *how* the client thinks, not just what they want.

**Limiting beliefs:**

10. What beliefs do you hold about yourself that might be getting in your way?
11. What do you believe you cannot do or be? Where did that belief come from?
12. What would be possible if that belief weren't true?

**Self-sabotage patterns:**

13. Where in your life do you know what to do but consistently don't do it?
14. What triggers cause you to react in ways you later regret?
15. Describe a pattern you keep repeating despite wanting to stop.

**Assumptions & blind spots:**

16. What assumptions are you making about this situation that you haven't examined?
17. How has your personal history shaped the way you see this problem?
18. What would someone who disagrees with your approach say about it?
19. What are you not willing to look at or change?

**Locus of control:**

20. When things go wrong in your life, where does responsibility typically land in your mind?
21. What feels within your control? What feels outside of it?

---

## Section 4: Values, Identity & Meaning

22. What matters most to you in life?
23. Who do you want to *become*, not just what do you want to achieve?
24. Where are you currently living out of alignment with what you say you value?
25. What does integrity look like for you?

---

## Section 5: Resources, Strengths & Prior Attempts

26. What strengths have helped you through hard times in the past?
27. What have you already tried to solve this problem? What happened?
28. What support systems do you have around you?
29. What tends to give you energy vs. drain you?

---

## Section 6: Motivation & Readiness

30. On a scale of 1–10, how ready are you to take action — even uncomfortable action?
31. What would make you stop or quit coaching partway through?
32. What does commitment look like for you right now given your circumstances?

---

## Open Close

*"Is there anything else you want me to know before we begin?"*

---

## What Coaches Look For (Beyond the Answers)

1. **Metacognition gaps** — Can the client observe their own thinking, or are they fused with it?
2. **Fixed vs. growth language** — "I'm just not good at..." vs. "I haven't learned yet..."
3. **Victim vs. agency framing** — How they describe what happens *to* them vs. what they choose
4. **Avoidance of specific domains** — Which Wheel areas do they rush past or deflect?
5. **Incongruence** — When stated values don't match described behaviors
6. **Urgency mismanagement** — Whether they live in reactive/crisis mode
7. **Identity threat** — Whether their goals require them to change who they *are*, not just what they *do*

---

## Format Notes

- **23–25 questions** is the professional consensus for a comprehensive intake
- Mix open text fields (qualitative depth) with 1–10 scales (Wheel of Life)
- Send before the first session — arrive knowing the map
- Always include a final open field for anything the client wants to add
