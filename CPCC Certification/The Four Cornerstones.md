# The Four Cornerstones of Co-Active Coaching

The cornerstones form the philosophical foundation of the [[The Co-Active Model|Co-Active Model]]. Every coaching interaction rests on these beliefs.

---

## 1. People are Naturally Creative, Resourceful and Whole

Nothing is broken or needs fixing. The coach has the questions; the client has the answers.

- The coach must **fiercely hold** this belief even when the client cannot see it themselves
- Problem-solving, leading questions, and trying to "fix" are subtle violations of this cornerstone
- This is the most fundamental belief - it shapes everything else

## 2. Dance in This Moment

About the coach's agility and complete presence in real-time.

- The paradox of **surrendering while holding authority**
- Responding (not reacting) to what arises
- Deep connection with [[Three Levels of Listening|Level 3 Listening]]
- No specific destination - only the **intention of transformation**
- Requires letting go of agenda and trusting the process

## 3. Focus on the Whole Person

Coaching addresses all aspects of a person - not just their presenting problem.

- Includes **emotions, mind, body, and spirit**
- Distinguishes the **person** from their **circumstances**
- Keeps attention on the human being, not just their situation
- Rejects the idea that coaching is only about goals and action items

## 4. Evoke Transformation

Everything a Co-Active Coach does is geared toward the client's transformation.

- Points coaching **away** from [[Captain Crew and Saboteur|Saboteur]] conversations
- Points coaching **toward** the client's authentic, magnificent self
- Transformation is not the same as change - it is fundamental shift in who the person is being
- The coach holds the vision of the client's highest potential
