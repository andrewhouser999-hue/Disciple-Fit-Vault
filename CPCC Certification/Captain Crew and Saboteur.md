# Captain, Crew, and Saboteur

Core frameworks in Co-Active Coaching for working with the client's inner landscape. Part of the [[The Co-Active Model|Co-Active Model]].

---

## The Captain

The client's inner leader - clear, knowing, always in command.

- Holds the paradox of **accepting life as it is** while **wanting more**
- Knows strengths, gifts, and limitations
- Holds the client with gentleness, compassion, and infinite patience
- Connected to the client's **Life Purpose**

### Guided Imaging Exercise
The Captain visualization takes the client to:
1. A safe place in nature
2. Meeting their Captain
3. Asking questions:
   - What is the Captain's job description?
   - What does it love?
   - What does it want for the client?
   - What is the client's Life Purpose?
4. The Captain provides a **gift** and a **special name**

## The Crew

Supporting inner voices and resources that serve the client's growth. The Captain leads the crew.

- Specific crew members can be called on for strength, courage, wisdom, etc.
- Can be personified and named for easier access during coaching

---

## The Saboteur

The inner voice of fear, doubt, and self-limitation that undermines the client.

### Eight Ways to Move Aside Saboteur Voices
1. **Bring in the client's Life Purpose** - reconnect to what matters most
2. **Engage the Captain** or a powerful crew member
3. **Acknowledge/name** the Saboteur - bring it into the light
4. **Ignore** the Saboteur Voice entirely
5. **Redirect attention** away from the Saboteur
6. **Personify** the Saboteur (give it a name, a character)
7. **Talk about values** being honored or dishonored
8. **Ask about the client's vision** - refocus on the future

### Key Distinction
- **Resonance** = honoring values, feeling alive, Captain speaking
- **Dissonance** = Saboteur presence, misalignment with values
