# Three Levels of Listening

A foundational skill in the [[The Co-Active Model|Co-Active Model]]. The coach's ability to listen at deeper levels determines the quality of the coaching.

---

## Level 1: Internal Listening

Focus is on **yourself** - your own thoughts, opinions, judgments, feelings, and conclusions.

- This is where most people listen most of the time
- Useful for the client (they need to be in Level 1 to process)
- **Not useful** for the coach during a session - the coach should rarely be here

## Level 2: Focused Listening

Sharp, laser focus on **the client** - words, emotions, what's said and unsaid.

- Hearing tone, pace, energy shifts
- Noticing resonance vs. dissonance
- Picking up on what the client is **not** saying
- The foundation of good coaching
- You are fully focused on the other person and their experience

## Level 3: Global Listening

**360-degree awareness** - everything seen, heard, felt in the entire environment.

- Awareness of the energy in the room or on the call
- Sensing shifts, tension, openness, resistance
- Includes the coach's own physical sensations as data
- Connected to [[The Four Cornerstones|"Dance in This Moment"]]
- Aware of impact and able to dance with it
- The most powerful level - includes intuition and environmental awareness

---

## Key Practice Notes

- The coach should primarily operate at **Levels 2 and 3**
- Level 1 awareness is used briefly for [[Co-Active Coaching Skills Glossary#Self-Management|self-management]] - noticing your own reactions, then returning attention to the client
- Moving between levels is fluid and natural with practice
- Level 3 is where intuitive hits and [[Co-Active Coaching Skills Glossary#Intuition|intuition]] live
