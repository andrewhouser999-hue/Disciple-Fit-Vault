# Three Principles: Fulfillment, Balance, Process

The three principles sit at the center of the [[The Co-Active Model|Co-Active Model]] and represent three distinct coaching approaches.

---

## Principle of Fulfillment

Coaching toward a life aligned with the client's deepest purpose and values.

### Six Ways to Clarify Values
1. **Captain** - Identify values from the [[Captain Crew and Saboteur|Captain visualization]]
2. **Peak Experience** - Listen for values in stories of peak experiences
3. **Suppressed Values** - Upsets indicate suppressed high values (flip the frustration)
4. **Invisible Values** - Values honored so naturally the client doesn't notice them
5. **Must Haves** - What must be present for fulfillment beyond basics
6. **Obsessive Expression** - Values taken to the extreme

> Values are intrinsic principles, as distinct as a thumbprint. They are not chosen and they are not morals.

---

## Principle of Balance

A structured approach for generating new perspectives and making empowered choices.

### The Balance Formula
1. **Identify the Topic** - Clear topic that matters, separated from embedded perspectives
2. **Generate Perspectives** - Name current perspective, brainstorm others, use geography to embody each
3. **Resonant Choice** - Client chooses a perspective from their own resonance (connected to Captain/Life Purpose), not just what sounds good
4. **Co-Active Strategy** - Brainstorm both "Co" (being) and "Active" (doing) actions; narrow with yes/no; create specific timed actions
5. **Commitment** - Conscious, witnessed declaration
6. **Action** - Client acts from the plan after the session

> "Overwhelm is not real. It is a conversation about circumstances. When truly present in the moment, overwhelm is impossible."

### Key Insight
The distinction between "Co" and "Active" strategies:
- **Co (Being):** Who do I need to be? What quality do I embody?
- **Active (Doing):** What specific actions will I take? By when?

---

## Principle of Process

Being fully present with what is happening in the moment - the emotional, physical, and energetic reality.

- Dancing with whatever arises rather than trying to fix or change it
- Trusting that the client's process holds wisdom
- Allowing emotions to be fully expressed without rushing to resolution
- Connected to the cornerstone of [[The Four Cornerstones|"Dance in This Moment"]]
