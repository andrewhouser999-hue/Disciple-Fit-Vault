# Witnessing to Jehovah's Witnesses — Strategies & Research

## I. Understand What You're Actually Dealing With

### It's a High-Control Organization, Not Just a Different Denomination
The Jehovah's Witness structure is what cult researchers call a **totalistic thought system**. Steven Hassan's BITE model (Behavior, Information, Thought, Emotional control) — JWs score high on all four.

What this means for witnessing:
- They are **trained to deliver presentations, not have conversations**
- They are taught that **independent thinking is spiritually dangerous** (literally called "apostasy")
- Their doctrines are **Watchtower-dependent** — they believe the Governing Body is God's sole channel of truth on earth
- **Fear of disfellowshipping** (complete social and family severance) is the primary psychological control mechanism

The implication: most JWs who leave do so over **years**, through accumulated seeds. Don't expect a single conversation to convert. Expect to plant.

## II. Target the Authority Structure Before the Doctrines

This is the strategic key that most Christian apologists get wrong. If you argue Trinity doctrine against someone whose entire interpretive grid is Watchtower-controlled, you're fighting on their terrain.

**First, collapse the organization's authority. Then the doctrines lose their backing.**

### The False Prophet Test — Deuteronomy 18:20-22
This is from *their own Bible* and cannot be dismissed.

> "If the prophet speaks in the name of Jehovah and the word does not occur or come true, that is the word that Jehovah did not speak." — Deut. 18:22 (NWT)

The Watchtower has set specific dates for Armageddon **multiple times**:
- **1914** — Russell predicted the end of Gentile times and the end of the world as we know it
- **1925** — Rutherford predicted Abraham, Isaac, Jacob would be resurrected and live in a house in San Diego (they actually built the house — "Beth-Sarim" — it still exists)
- **1975** — The organization explicitly taught Armageddon would arrive. Membership dropped sharply when it didn't

Ask: *"By the standard Jehovah himself gave in Deuteronomy 18, what does it mean that the Watchtower predicted these dates and was wrong?"*

They cannot answer this from their own Bible without special pleading.

## III. The Deity of Christ — The Central Doctrinal Battleground

Everything in JW theology depends on Jesus being a created being. If Jesus is God, the entire structure falls. This is where to plant your flag.

### John 1:1 — Their Translation Is Indefensible

The NWT renders: *"the Word was **a** god"* — inserting the indefinite article "a" with zero manuscript support.

The Greek: *kai theos ēn ho logos*

**Colwell's Rule** (established Greek grammar, 1933): A predicate nominative noun that *precedes* the verb typically lacks the article without becoming indefinite — it becomes **qualitative** ("the Word was fully divine in nature"). Every credible Greek scholar — including non-Christians — rejects the NWT rendering here.

**The Watchtower's own weapon against them:** The *Kingdom Interlinear Translation* is a Watchtower publication showing Greek word-for-word. It renders John 1:1 as *"god was the Word"* — and then in the right-hand column, the NWT says "a god." Their own Greek interlinear contradicts their own translation. Use this.

Ask: *"Can you show me from the Greek text — including your own Kingdom Interlinear — the basis for inserting 'a'?"*

### John 8:58 — Jesus Takes the Divine Name

> "Before Abraham came into existence, I have been." — NWT

The Greek is *ego eimi* — present tense, the exact construction of Exodus 3:14 (LXX: *ego eimi ho ōn*). The NWT rendering "I have been" is grammatically impossible for the tense used.

The Jews who heard it immediately tried to stone him for blasphemy. They understood the claim.

Ask: *"Why did they try to stone him in verse 59 if he only said he existed before Abraham? What did they understand that we're missing?"*

### John 20:28 — Thomas's Direct Address

> "Thomas said to him: 'My Lord and my God!'"

This is a direct address to Jesus, using both articles in Greek (*ho Kyrios mou kai ho Theos mou*) — definite, direct, unambiguous. Jesus does not correct him.

In JW theology, Jesus should have said "don't call me God." He doesn't.

### Colossians 1:15-17 — The NWT Inserts "Other" Four Times

NWT: *"By means of him all **other** things were created... all **other** things have been created through him and for him... all **other** things... all **other** things."*

The word "other" does not appear in the Greek at all — not once. The Watchtower inserted it four times to make Jesus appear to be a created being who then created everything *else*. Their own Kingdom Interlinear shows no "other" in the Greek column.

Ask: *"Can you show me the Greek word for 'other' in your Kingdom Interlinear for Colossians 1:16?"*

### Hebrews 1:8 — The Father Calls Jesus "God"

> "But about the Son he says: 'God is your throne forever.'"

The Father directly addresses the Son as *ho Theos* — with the article. This is God calling Jesus "God."

## IV. The Holy Spirit Is a Person, Not a Force

JWs teach the Holy Spirit is "God's active force" — impersonal.

- **Acts 5:3-4**: "You have lied to the Holy Spirit... you have not lied to men but to God." Lying *to* a force is grammatically and conceptually incoherent. Peter equates the Holy Spirit with God.
- **John 16:13**: "He will guide you... he will speak... he will declare." Impersonal forces don't speak, guide, or declare.
- **Romans 8:26-27**: "The Spirit himself intercedes... he who searches hearts knows the **mind** of the Spirit." Impersonal forces don't have minds.
- **Ephesians 4:30**: "Do not grieve the Holy Spirit." You cannot grieve a force. Grief requires a personal relationship.

---

## V. The Resurrection — Jesus Rose Bodily

JWs teach Jesus rose as a spirit creature, not in a physical body.

- **Luke 24:39**: Jesus says, *"A spirit does not have flesh and bones as you see that I have."* Jesus himself denies being a spirit.
- **John 2:19-21**: *"Destroy this temple and I will raise it up in three days... he was speaking about the temple of his body."* What gets raised is explicitly his body.
- **John 20:24-27**: Thomas touches the nail wounds. Spirit bodies don't have physical wounds you can probe with your finger.

## VI. Failed Watchtower Translations — Pattern, Not Accident

The NWT was produced by a translation committee. The Watchtower has never disclosed who was on it. Scholars who have investigated have found the primary translator was Frederick Franz, who claimed knowledge of Greek and Hebrew — but under oath in a Scottish court trial (1954, *Douglas Walsh v. the Right Honourable James Latham Clyde*), he failed basic Hebrew tests.

The pattern in NWT changes:
- Every text supporting the Trinity is softened, reworded, or has words inserted
- "Jehovah" appears 237 times in the NT — it appears in **no Greek manuscript of the NT**. They inserted it to control how readers understand references to "Lord" (*Kyrios*), so that passages about Jesus become about Jehovah

This is not translation. It's reverse-engineering a text to fit a predetermined doctrine.

## VII. Tactical Principles for the Conversation

### Stay on One Issue
JWs are trained to keep moving. When pressed, they redirect. Pick **one topic** — Jesus' deity is best — and don't leave it.

*"I want to understand your view completely before we move on. Can we stay on this one for a few more minutes?"*

### Ask Questions, Don't Assert
Assertions trigger trained defenses. Questions create cognitive openings.

- *"What would it take to convince you the Watchtower had gotten something wrong?"*
- *"If the Governing Body changed a doctrine they previously said was from God, what would that mean to you?"*
- *"Who translated the NWT, and what were their credentials?"*
- *"Can you show me the Greek support for that rendering?"*

### Use Their Own Materials
- **Kingdom Interlinear Translation** — Watchtower-published Greek interlinear that contradicts NWT in key places
- **Watchtower and Awake! archives** — contain the false prophecies in their own words
- **Aid to Bible Understanding** (old Watchtower publication) — admits uncertainty on doctrines they now present as settled

### Don't Fight on NWT Terrain
Always go back to the Greek. Carry an interlinear. The NWT is designed to neutralize every key text — don't let the conversation be a NWT-versus-KJV fight.

### Name What's at Stake Emotionally
Many JWs have never heard the real gospel — grace without performance, security without organization approval. At some point, let the conversation go there:

*"What does your relationship with God feel like day to day? Is it restful, or is it mostly about staying in good standing?"*

This is often the crack. Not argument — the felt absence of peace.

## VIII. What Research Says About JW Deconversion

Studies on those who leave high-control religious groups (Marlene Winell, Steven Hassan, Steven Martin) consistently show:

- Intellectual arguments alone rarely cause departure
- **The combination that works**: intellectual seeds + emotional dissonance (sensing something is wrong) + a relationship outside the organization
- Most leave because they *felt* something was wrong before they could articulate it theologically
- The most common trigger: **disfellowshipping of someone they loved**, or **a failed prophecy they couldn't rationalize**

You are most effective when you are both **theologically sharp** (so they can't dismiss you) and **relationally warm** (so you're not a threat to be shut down). Arguments close JWs. Questions open them. Love keeps them at the table.
