## Probing Questions

*Use conversationally. Watch for hesitation, deflection, or answers that come too fast.*

### Belonging & Identity
- When do you feel most like yourself?
- Are there moments — even now — where you feel like you don't fully belong?
- How would you describe yourself to someone who knows nothing about you or your story?
- Is there a version of you that only comes out around certain people?

### His Story & Origin
- What's the earliest memory you have of feeling genuinely safe?
- Is there anything about where you grew up that you find you don't talk about much?
- What do you miss about your childhood, if anything?
- What did "family" mean to you growing up, versus what it means to you now?

### Relationships & Trust
- Who in your life do you feel completely yourself around?
- When someone gets close to you, what do you tend to do?
- Have you ever pulled back from a relationship and couldn't fully explain why?
- When someone lets you down, how long does it stay with you?

### Emotional Patterns
- When you're under real pressure, what does that look like for you internally?
- What emotions are hardest for you to feel or express?
- When something goes wrong, where does your mind go first — what's the default narrative?
- Is there anything you feel that you don't have words for yet?

### Self-Perception
- Do you feel like you deserve the good things that are in your life right now?
- What do you think people misunderstand about you most?
- Is there something you know about yourself that most people don't see?
- What's the thing about yourself you'd most want to change?

### Patterns He May Not Have Connected
- Is there a pattern that keeps showing up in your life that you can't fully explain?
- When relationships get hard, do you tend to fight for them, go quiet, or walk away?
- Do you find it easier to help other people than to let people help you?
- What does it take for you to actually ask for what you need?

### Faith & Meaning
- How do you understand who God is in relation to who *you* are — not in doctrine, but personally?
- Is there anything about your faith that feels more like an obligation than a desire?
- Do you feel like God sees you the way your adoptive family sees you — or differently?
- What do you think God wants *for* you, not just *from* you?

### Forward & Future
- What does a good life look like to you — specifically, not generally?
- What are you most afraid won't happen for you?
- Is there anything you want that you feel like you're not allowed to want?

 A note on how to use these: *The most important questions are often the ones he hesitates on,*
  *deflects from, or answers too quickly. The fast answer is often the rehearsed one. The pause is*
  *where the real material lives.*

### The Three-Stage Recovery Model (Herman)

*Not a linear path — people cycle back through stages, especially under stress or relational disruption.*

**Stage 1: Safety** — The non-negotiable foundation. Nothing else works without it.
- Safety in the **body** — symptoms manageable, nervous system not in constant crisis
- Safety in the **environment** — physical circumstances stable
- Safety in **relationships** — at least one trustworthy relationship exists
- *Coaching note:* Samuel has environmental safety through the new family. Internal and relational safety — trusting he won't be rejected again — is a different question. Don't assume good circumstances mean Stage 1 is complete.

**Stage 2: Remembrance & Mourning** — Where actual processing happens.
- Constructing a coherent narrative of what happened
- Integrating fragmented or avoided memories
- Grieving the losses — not just events, but the self that was lost, the childhood that should have existed
- *Coaching note:* This stage is most likely to have been skipped. The adoption looked like a resolution. If he has never genuinely mourned his original family, the parents he deserved and didn't get, the years he lost — Stage 2 is likely incomplete regardless of how functional he appears.

**Stage 3: Reconnection** — Rebuilding a life forward.
- Developing a new, integrated sense of self
- Forming healthy relationships
- Finding meaning and purpose
- Many survivors find a *survivor mission* — a drive to use their experience to help others
- *Coaching note:* At 19, Samuel is likely entering this stage. This is where coaching belongs — identity, purpose, vocation, meaningful contribution. But Stage 2 grief may surface here, and knowing when to refer out matters.
