## Context
Andrew asked for a comprehensive overview of positive psychology in the context of his Disciple Fit faith-based coaching business. The question had two parts: (1) who are the primary contributors — thought leaders, researchers, research organizations — and (2) a list of essential source material with ~500-word summaries for each. This is reference material for integrating positive psychology into the Disciple Fit CBC (CBT + Coaching + Biblical framework) model.

## Key Decisions & Notes
- Positive psychology framed as providing *mechanism and vocabulary*, not direction or foundation — that is the Biblical framework's job
- Key tension flagged: positive psychology is anthropocentric; flourishing in Seligman's model is self-defined and self-driven, which conflicts with Disciple Fit's received-not-constructed model
- Three risk areas identified: (1) PERMA has no category for repentance or brokenness, (2) it can create therapeutic deism, (3) toxic positivity risk with Fredrickson's positivity ratio (note: the specific 3:1 ratio math was retracted; directional claim survives)
- Self-compassion flagged as theologically contested but practically essential for shame-based stuck clients — framed as accepting the posture toward yourself that God already holds toward you
