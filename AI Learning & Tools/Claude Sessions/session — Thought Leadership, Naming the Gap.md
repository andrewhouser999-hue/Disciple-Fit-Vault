## Context
Andrew is building a faith-based coaching business (Disciple Fit Covenant Identity Coaching) using a CBC framework — CBT + Coaching + Biblical grounding. The core niche is stuck Christians. This session explores the thought leadership opportunity of naming the gap between what Christians declare about their identity and what they actually feel and live from — a problem that is widespread, felt, but currently unnamed in a memorable or actionable way.

## Current State
The framework has been articulated as a three-layer identity model:
1. **Declared identity** — What they say they believe about who they are ("I am a new creation")
2. **Cognitive-emotional identity** — What their automatic thoughts and emotional reactions reveal they actually believe (shame, fear, performance anxiety still running the OS)
3. **Behavioral identity** — What their habits and choices show they're actually living from

Stuck Christians typically have high declared identity, low cognitive-emotional identity, and inconsistent behavioral identity. The CBC framework is positioned to close that gap — CBT addresses the cognitive-emotional layer that theology and habits-based formation both skip.

Key market observation: no one has cleanly named this three-layer gap with a coaching/formation intervention attached to each layer. Adjacent work exists (Curt Thompson — neuroscience + shame + attachment; John Mark Comer — formation/practices; Neil Anderson — identity in Christ declarations) but none integrate all three layers with a practical coaching system.

## Next Steps
- Name the gap — find the phrase that makes people say "finally, someone said it." Currently unnamed. Needs to be short, theological enough for Christians, psychologically credible, and repeatable.
- Position against Curt Thompson (most direct adjacent work) and the formation movement (Comer, etc.) — Andrew must read Thompson before building thought leadership to know exactly where his lane differs
- Validate demand — the niche is latent/unvalidated. Articles are the right first move, but only if structured to name the problem in a way that generates "finally, someone said it" response
- Determine publication target for first article

## Key Decisions & Notes
- The naming opportunity is real but the bottleneck is the phrase itself — framework quality is not the issue
- Brené Brown analogy: she didn't invent shame, she named and framed it. That's the model for this play.
- Curt Thompson is the most important competitor to study: *The Soul of Shame*, *The Anatomy of the Soul* — psychiatry + neuroscience + Christian identity. His gap: therapy frame, not coaching; diagnoses well but no practical formation-coaching system.
- Three bottlenecks identified: (1) naming, (2) platform/distribution, (3) evidence/case stories
- The article pipeline Andrew already has is the right first move — but articles must be written to *name the problem*, not just explore it
