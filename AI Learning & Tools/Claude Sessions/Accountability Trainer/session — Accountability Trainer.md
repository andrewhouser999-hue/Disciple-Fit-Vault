## Current State

**Level: FOUNDATIONAL.** No signal to move to Intermediate yet.

**Client population: Secular only.** No faith-based scenarios unlocked.

**6 of 8 scenario types covered in session 1:**

1. **Commitment-Building** — Client says "I think I'm going to try to have that conversation with my manager this week." Skill: Flores promise structure (no specific action or time = no promise exists); Gollwitzer if-then anchoring; distinguishing sincere from performative commitment. Best response: anchor the missing promise elements — "When specifically will you have that conversation — what day, and what will you say to get it on the calendar?"

2. **Opening Check-In** — Client sent 1 of 3 committed applications, leads with the win to soften the landing. Skill: Clean four-step check-in sequence; naming the gap without rescuing; not softening the question. Best response: "You committed to three. One went out. What do you make of that?"

3. **Surface Non-Completion** — Client missed 10:30 bedtime every night; "work kept pulling me in." Skill: Diagnosing which type of breakdown (goal quality, design/ability, or motivation) before responding — not assuming and solving. Best response: "Zero nights out of seven. What do you think was actually happening there?"

4. **The Story** — Client gives elaborate, multi-layered explanation for not reaching out to former colleagues (framing concern, wife/rough week, headspace). Skill: Bottom-lining (Co-Active); Reynolds' move of stepping outside the story rather than engaging its content. Best response: "What does that story do for you?"

5. **Rescuing Temptation** — Client froze when dad called, didn't initiate the planned conversation, lands with "I don't know what's wrong with me." Skill: Holding space without rescuing (Brown); staying present in the consequence rather than normalizing, redirecting, or pivoting to next steps. Best response: "Stay with that for a second. What's true for you right now?"

6. **Repeated Non-Completion** — Third week in a row not job searching, different reason each week. Skill: Naming the pattern as the diagnostic unit (Kegan/Lahey); not increasing pressure; pivoting to the competing commitment underneath. Best response: "Three weeks. Different reason each time, same outcome. What does that pattern tell you?"
