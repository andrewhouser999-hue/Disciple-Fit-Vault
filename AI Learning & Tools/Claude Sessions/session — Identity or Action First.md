## Context
A motivational psychology question with direct implications for Andrew's CBC coaching framework (CBT + Coaching + Biblical grounding). The question: do experts say identity or action comes first in motivation and behavior change?

## Current State
The dominant modern view leans identity-first, but the honest answer is that they form a loop — and entry point matters based on where the person is stuck.

**Identity-first camp:** James Clear (Atomic Habits), Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan) — identity as a new creation is declared before behavior changes; intrinsic motivation outlasts outcome-chasing.

**Action-first camp:** BJ Fogg, B.F. Skinner, Amy Cuddy, William James — small wins create evidence that reshapes self-perception; behavior precedes belief.

**Key diagnostic principle:**
- Starting with identity works better for people stuck in their self-concept — they can't imagine being different, so action never starts.
- Starting with action works better for people who overthink and under-execute — identity talk becomes a substitute for doing anything.

**Coaching context application:**
The CBC framework maps directly to this. Stuck Christians often have a theological identity that hasn't been behaviorally integrated. Identity is declared but not embodied. That's the gap coaching targets. Most Christians Andrew works with have been given identity-first theology their whole lives — and are still stuck. The missing piece is the cognitive-emotional integration layer (CBT) that bridges declared identity to felt and lived identity. Declaration without integration produces high theological identity scores and unchanged behavior.

## Next Steps
- This insight feeds directly into the Thought Leadership session — the three-layer identity model (declared / cognitive-emotional / behavioral) is the mechanism that explains WHY identity-first fails for stuck Christians despite correct theology
- Consider how to use this diagnostic (identity-stuck vs. action-stuck) as a coaching intake differentiator

## Key Decisions & Notes
- Identity and action form a loop — the coaching question is which entry point fits *this client*
- The CBC framework is differentiated precisely because it addresses the cognitive-emotional layer that both pure identity-declaration approaches and pure habit/discipline approaches skip
- Dallas Willard's "trying vs. training" distinction is relevant: training restructures the interior life so right action becomes natural — CBC is a training system, not a willpower system
