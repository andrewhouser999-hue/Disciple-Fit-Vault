## Context
Andrew asked for questions to use with coaching clients for deep shadow work in the context of Disciple Fit Coaching — a faith-based coaching business using a CBC framework (CBT + Coaching + Biblical). The full question set was generated, then put through /scrutinize across four categories. All four categories returned Fixable. The core finding: the questions are real, but the framework around them is not built yet.

## Current State
A 22-question shadow work set was produced across six categories. The /scrutinize analysis identified it as valid raw material with serious structural gaps. Nothing has been built beyond the question set itself. No session tool, no tiered system, no integration pathway, no theological resolution.

## Next Steps
1. Decide the theological frame: *sin-exposure/self-deception work* (clearly biblical) vs. *Jungian shadow integration* (contested) — must be resolved before building
2. Build a tiered depth system — label each question Tier 1/2/3 with trust threshold and session prerequisites
3. Triage scope: flag which questions require mandatory referral language if certain disclosures emerge
4. Build an integration pathway — map each tier to a landing tool (Lie Eliminator, prayer, behavioral declaration, referral)
5. Write a closing/grounding protocol for each depth tier
6. Connect shadow work to existing system: Soils diagnostic, intake form, Discipleship Markers

## Key Decisions & Notes
- The word "shadow" is Jungian, not biblical. The framework underneath the questions needs to be declared and resolved.
- Projection questions (Q1–2) are clinical hypotheses, not biblical principles — cannot be treated as diagnostic truth universally.
- Q6, Q12, Q16 are therapy-grade questions. They require containment protocols and mandatory referral language before use.
- Q21–22 (spiritualized shadow) require prior theological grounding and deep relational trust — cannot be used universally.
- The question set is detection-heavy and response-light. The integration pathway is the missing piece.
- No closing protocol exists. Shadow work sessions need a regulated landing before the client leaves.

## Full Conversation Output

### Shadow Work Question Set

**Projection/Detection**
1. What do you despise most in other people?
2. What behaviors in others make you most angry or disgusted — and when did you first feel that?
3. What would the people who know you best say is your worst trait — the one you resist hearing?
4. What part of yourself do you never want anyone to see?
5. Where do you perform goodness rather than live it?

**Shame/Hiddenness**
6. What have you done that, if others knew, would change how they see you?
7. Where do you manage others' perceptions of you most carefully?
8. What sin do you confess most rarely — and why?
9. What do you secretly believe about yourself that you've never said out loud?

**Identity Distortion**
10. What lie about yourself do you most want to be true — because it protects you?
11. Where do you feel like a fraud, even in areas where you're genuinely competent?
12. What version of yourself have you buried to survive — and what did burying it cost you?

**Compensation/Defense**
13. What strength of yours has become a weapon — against yourself or others?
14. Where does your greatest gift become your greatest liability?
15. What are you helping others with that you refuse to receive yourself?

**Relational Pattern**
16. What relational pattern keeps repeating no matter how much you want it to stop?
17. Who do you consistently diminish or dismiss — and what does that protect you from seeing?
18. Where do you make yourself small to avoid threatening someone you love?

**Spiritualized Shadow**
19. Where do you use spiritual language to avoid genuine accountability?
20. What virtue have you turned into a weapon — using "grace" to dodge confrontation, "patience" to avoid honesty?
21. Where does your theology protect you from intimacy with God rather than create it?
22. What have you attributed to "God's will" that is actually your own fear or avoidance?

### /Scrutinize Analysis

**Most Dangerous Finding:** Therapy-grade questions with no containment protocol, no sequencing logic, and an unresolved Jungian framework underneath. Several questions will open floors an untrained coach cannot hold.

---

#### 1. Theological Accuracy — VERDICT: Fixable

**Steelman:** Biblical basis is legitimate. Psalm 51, Jeremiah 17:9, Romans 7, Matthew 7, Ephesians 5:13, James 5:16 all support confronting hidden, self-deceived, unacknowledged material as a serious spiritual problem.

**Fatal gap:** "Shadow" and the framework underneath it are Jungian, not biblical. Jung's goal is integration of all parts to achieve wholeness. The Christian goal is death to self (Galatians 2:20), mortification (Colossians 3:5), transformation (Romans 12:2). These overlap but are not the same project. Unreflected Jungian framing will teach clients that integration = acceptance, when the biblical answer to certain shadow content is repentance and mortification.

**Specific overreach:**
- Q1–2 (projection questions) rest on a Jungian clinical hypothesis, not a biblical principle. Treating it as diagnostic truth is overreach.
- Q21 ("where does your theology protect you from intimacy with God") can be weaponized to dismantle sound doctrine and call the resistance "shadow."

**Fix:** Declare whether this is sin-exposure/self-deception work (biblical, defensible) or Jungian shadow integration (theologically contested). Name which one and resolve the conflict in writing before building.

---

#### 2. Clinical Accuracy — VERDICT: Fixable (Serious)

**Steelman:** Q3, Q7, Q10, Q13, Q14, Q15 are strong, accessible, non-traumatizing, within coaching scope.

**What will get someone hurt:**
- Q6 ("what have you done that if others knew...") — will surface abuse, criminal history, suicidal ideation, hidden trauma. Without clinical training and a mandatory referral protocol, the coach is in over their head.
- Q12 ("what version of yourself have you buried to survive") — structural trauma language. "To survive" implies survival-level threat. Therapy territory.
- Q16 (repeating relational patterns) — can surface personality disorder features, dissociation, complex trauma responses.
- Q1–2 (projection) — contraindicated for clients with paranoid features, narcissistic wounding, or trauma-driven hypervigilance.

**Structural problem:** No containment protocol. Shadow work without containment is an open wound with no bandage. What do you do when the client dissociates? Discloses abuse? Can't re-regulate before the session ends?

**Fix:** Triage the question set. Label which questions are entry-level, intermediate, and clinical-threshold. Add mandatory referral language for threshold questions.

---

#### 3. Structural Integrity — VERDICT: Fixable

**Steelman:** Taxonomy is reasonably complete — projection, shame, compensation, identity distortion, relational patterns, spiritualized avoidance.

**What's broken:**
- No sequencing. Depth-10 questions mixed with depth-4 questions. No trust threshold or rapport prerequisite specified.
- No integration pathway. Exposure without a landing is wound-opening without sutures. What happens after Q9? Where does the answer go?
- Q19–22 (spiritualized shadow) are a distinct category requiring different handling — cannot be asked interchangeably with psychological shadow questions.
- No connection to existing system: Soils framework, Lie Eliminator, Discipleship Markers, intake diagnostic.

**Fix:** (1) Tiered depth system with prerequisites. (2) Integration pathway mapping each tier to a landing tool. (3) Explicit connection to the existing coaching system.

---

#### 4. Practical Deployability — VERDICT: Fixable

**Steelman:** Q3, Q7, Q13, Q14, Q15 are immediately deployable in a normal coaching session.

**What won't survive contact with a real session:**
- 22 questions is a research document, not a session tool. A usable tool needs 3–5 questions per tier, decision logic for which to use, and a stopping condition.
- No intake filter. Cannot run the same questions with Samuel Cooper (19, marriage prep, 6 weeks) as with a long-term sanctification client.
- High coach skill dependency. Without a non-anxious, skilled coach, clients will deflect or open material that can't be held.
- No closing protocol. Shadow work sessions need a regulated landing before the client leaves.

**Fix:** Build a tiered card set with a prerequisite checklist, session deployment guide (which 3–5 per session, what order), and a closing protocol for each depth tier.
